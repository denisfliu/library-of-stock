When generating Anki cards:

1. **Use "Artist:" not "Painter:"** as the indicator for visual artists. Keep it general.

2. **Never leak the answer on the front.** If the card's back is "Work (Emily Carr)", don't mention "Emily Carr" or "Carr" on the front. Rewrite clues to remove the creator's name — e.g., "Lawren Harris called this work its artist's best work" not "Lawren Harris called it Carr's best work."

3. **The front must be a useful, self-contained clue.** Avoid vague clues that describe the answer rather than clue it. "Totem poles in the wilderness" is too vague as a card front — it doesn't teach you anything specific you could recall. Good clues have specific, memorable details (names, events, descriptions).

4. **Skip clues that are essentially restating the answer.** If the clue is just "Czech composer of Ma vlast" and the back is "Smetana", that's circular — skip it.

**Why:** The goal of the cards is to learn specific facts that help you recall works/topics in quizbowl. Leaking the answer or using vague descriptions defeats the purpose.

**How to apply:** During card generation, check each clue: (a) does the front text contain the answer? If so, rewrite or skip. (b) Is the clue specific enough to be useful? If it's just a category description, skip it.
