American playwright. Best known for The Little Foxes (Regina Giddens, the Hubbard family, cotton mill, stolen bonds) and The Children's Hour (Mary Tilford accuses teachers Karen and Martha of being lesbians). Also wrote Toys in the Attic, The Autumn Garden, and Another Part of the Forest. Partner of Dashiell Hammett. Refused to name names to HUAC. Feud with Mary McCarthy.

Summary of Facts

Lillian Hellman is an American playwright best known for The Little Foxes and The Children's Hour. The Little Foxes (1939) depicts the scheming Hubbard family in post-Reconstruction Alabama, centered on Regina Giddens who blackmails her brothers Ben and Oscar after nephew Leo steals Union Pacific bonds from Regina's wheelchair-bound husband Horace's safe deposit box. Horace dies of a heart attack while crawling upstairs for medicine as Regina watches without helping. The servant Addie calls the Hubbards "people who ate the earth" like "the locusts" in the Bible. Daughter Alexandra ultimately refuses to accompany Regina to Chicago. The title comes from the Song of Solomon.

The Children's Hour (1934) centers on student Mary Tilford's false accusation that her teachers Karen Wright and Martha Dobie are having a lesbian affair. Mary blackmails Rosalie Wells into corroborating her story. Martha eventually commits suicide, and Karen tells her "I loved you the way they said." Karen also breaks off her engagement to Joe Cardin. The play controversially lost the 1935 Pulitzer to Zoe Akins's The Old Maid.

Toys in the Attic depicts Julian Berniers bringing his new bride Lily home to his doting spinster sisters Anna and Carrie in New Orleans. Albertine has a relationship with her black chauffeur Henry. The Autumn Garden is set at Constance Tuckerman's summer boarding house, where Sophie blackmails Nina for $5,000 after her drunk husband Nick Denery passes out in Sophie's bed. Another Part of the Forest is a prequel to The Little Foxes depicting the Hubbard family under patriarch Marcus.

Hellman refused to name names to HUAC, declaring "I cannot and will not cut my conscience to fit this year's fashions." Her feud with Mary McCarthy became legendary when McCarthy said on The Dick Cavett Show that "every word she writes is a lie, including 'and' and 'the'," prompting Hellman to sue for libel. Her memoir Pentimento, especially the "Julia" chapter about smuggling $50,000 in a fur hat to fund the Austrian resistance, was disputed by Muriel Gardiner. She was the partner of Dashiell Hammett and wrote the original libretto for Bernstein's Candide.
