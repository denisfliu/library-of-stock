Questions: Tracey Emin

1 tossup · 3 bonuses

Tossups

T1 2023 British Novice (2023) Diff 6 · Fine Arts
A performance piece by this artist saw them incessantly scream while lying in the foetal position outside the home of Edvard Munch. In 2004, property of this artist, which included a beach hut co-owned with Sarah Lucas, was destroyed in a warehouse fire. In 1999, the Chinese artistic duo Mad For Real jumped onto an installation by this artist at the Tate Britain. The Shell Grotto in this artist’s hometown ofMargate inspired the design of a tent on which she appliquéd the names of ‘everyone [she] had ever (*) slept with’. Cigarette packets and a discarded pair of slippers were included in an installation by this artist, inspired by a four day period in which she consumed nothing but alcohol. For 10 points, the installation My Bed was created by which Young British Artist?
ANSWER: Tracey Emin (the performance piece is Homage to Edvard Munch and All My Dead Children)

Bonuses

B1 2024 NASAT (2024) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
The K Foundation used this substance for a work titled Nail to the Wall, which challenged its owner to either take it apart or wait for it to increase in value. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this substance that titles an artwork involving two blank canvases, for which Jens Haaning was sued.
ANSWER: money [or Take the Money and Run]
[10] This artist portrayed money spewing out of her splayed legs in a Polaroid shot titled I've Got It All. She exhibited her unmade bed for an installation at the Tate.
ANSWER: Tracey Emin [or Tracey Karima Emin]
[10] Hans-Peter Feldmann converted a $100,000 prize into one dollar bills to cover a room in this museum. Frank Lloyd Wright designed its spiral ramp.
ANSWER: Guggenheim Museum [or Solomon R. Guggenheim Museum; or NYC Guggenheim; do not accept "Guggenheim Bilbao"]
B2 2024 ACF Regionals (2024) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
In a 1984 book titled for a “Subversive [one of these things],” Rozsika Parker describes embroidery as “a weapon of resistance to the constraints of femininity.” For 10 points each:
[10] Name these loops that form the basis for textile arts. Examples of these loops done by hand include a “running” one, which looks like dashes of thread.
ANSWER: stitches [accept The Subversive Stitch]
[10] A new introduction to The Subversive Stitch discusses this artist’s autobiographical blankets. This Young British Artist stitched the names of family and lovers into a tent listing everyone she had ever slept with.
ANSWER: Tracey Emin [or Tracey Karima Emin]
[10] Parker chronicles female workers who used backstitches and this embroidery technique to create the Bayeux Tapestry. In this technique, string, yarn, or other material is placed against the fabric and then stitched by another thread.
ANSWER: couching [or couched filling; accept laid work]
B3 2022 ACF Winter (2022) Diff 7 · Fine Arts
Answer the following about art vandalism, for 10 points each.
[10] In 2022, the glass that covers this Leonardo da Vinci painting of a woman with an enigmatic expression was smeared with cake.
ANSWER: Mona Lisa [or La Gioconda; or La Joconde]
[10] In 1985, sulfuric acid was thrown on this artist’s painting of Danaё (“duh-NAY-ee”) before it was sliced with a knife. This artist’s wife Saskia was the original model for Danaё in that painting.
ANSWER: Rembrandt [or Rembrandt van Rijn or Rembrandt Harmenszoon van Rijn]
[10] Cài Yuán (“tsai y’wen”) and Jiàn Jūn Xī (“j’yen j’win shee”) jumped on this Tracey Emin piece while shirtless for the unlicensed performance “Two Naked Men Jump Into” this installation. Empty liquor bottles, condoms, and other detritus clutter a blue rug next to this installation’s title object.
ANSWER: My Bed [prompt on bed or Tracey Emin’s bed or similar descriptions; prompt on Two Naked Men Jump into Tracey’s Bed]
