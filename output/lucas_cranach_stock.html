General / Biographical

Court painter to Electors of Saxony (Frederick the Wise, etc.). Signed with winged snake/serpent carrying a ruby ring (coat of arms from Frederick III; later changed to single crow's wing after son Hans's death). Close friend of Luther. Shared feast day with Durer (April 6) in Lutheran/Episcopal churches. Sometimes Mayor of Wittenberg. Other works: The Judgment of Paris (Mercury with glass sphere, Paris in red cape and armor), The Stag Hunt of Elector Frederick the Wise, Venus and Cupid (skinny tall nudes), Law and Gospel (consulted Philip Melanchthon), The Fountain of Youth, Nymph of the Spring, Melancholia, Wittenberg Altarpiece, Weimar Altarpiece, Adam and Eve, Venus with Cupid and Honeycomb, The Ill-Matched Couple, Crucifixion (1503).

Summary of Facts

Lucas Cranach the Elder was a German Renaissance painter and court painter to the Electors of Saxony, most commonly identified by his many portraits of Martin Luther and his winged serpent signature.

He signed his works with a winged snake carrying a ruby ring, a coat of arms bestowed by Frederick the Wise (later changed to a single crow's wing after his son Hans's death). He was attached to Frederick the Wise's court from 1504 and was a close friend of Luther, painting him many times in his characteristic floppy black hat, as well as Luther's wife Katharina von Bora.

Key works include The Judgment of Paris (Mercury with glass sphere, Paris in red cape), The Stag Hunt of Elector Frederick the Wise (archers in bushes, deer chased across a river), many skinny tall nudes of Venus (often indistinguishable from his Eves), Venus and Cupid with Honeycomb, Law and Gospel (consulted with Philip Melanchthon), The Fountain of Youth, Melancholia (woman whittles stick while infants do precocious things), and the Wittenberg Altarpiece (self-portrait as waiter in Last Supper; Jesus dines with Germans). He shares a feast day with Durer on April 6.
