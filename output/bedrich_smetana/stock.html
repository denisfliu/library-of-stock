Czech nationalist composer, most commonly identified by the tone poem cycle Ma vlast (which includes The Moldau depicting the Vltava River) and the opera The Bartered Bride. Went deaf in 1874. Wrote the autobiographical string quartet From My Life. Associated with Prague and the Prague Spring Festival. Often clued alongside Dvorak.

String Quartet No. 1 in E minor ("From My Life")

Autobiographical chamber work. Very frequently clued.

6x Sustained high harmonic E in the first violin represents his deafness/tinnitus in the final movement mid
3x Opening viola solo: descending short-long-short motif (eighth note B, whole note B, down to E, eighth F-sharp, whole G, down to B) mid
3x Second movement is a polka (viola instructed to play "quasi Tromba" / like a trumpet on the C string) mid
1x Third movement opens with exposed cello solo descending from F above the staff to trills below the staff power
2x Dvorak played viola at the private premiere mid
1x Letter to friend: number of performers (4) purposefully set to discuss "what so obviously troubles me" power
2x Harsh, quiet tremolos underlie the high E in the final movement mid
