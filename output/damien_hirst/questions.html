Tossups

T1 2025 MRNA (2025) Diff 7 · Fine Arts
The essay Bell’s Theorem discusses this ethnicity’s role in contemporary art and posits that art from this ethnicity “is a white thing!” It’s not French, but the Utopia art colony accused an artist of appropriating an art style developed by this ethnicity for his “Veil Paintings.” In the 1970s, an art company run by members of this ethnicity named Papunya Tula popularized the use of acrylic paint over traditional ochre pigments in its country. One artist of this ethnic group, (*) Emily Kame Kngwarreye (“kar-meh ing-WAH-ray”), was known for her dot paintings. Central Desert paintings depicting this ethnic group’s mythos commonly use thick, wavy lines to represent rivers and songlines. For 10 points, paintings by members of what ethnicity often depict the Dreamtime?
ANSWER: Aboriginal Australians [or Aboriginals or accept Aborigines or Indigenous Australians or Native Australians; accept Worrorra; accept Anmatyerr; prompt on Australians] (The Utopia art colony accused Damien Hirst of appropriating the dot painting technique.)

T3 2021 GORKY II (2021) Diff 10 · Fine Arts

T4 2019 Canadian Hybrid (2019) Diff 7 · Fine Arts
A Venice art exhibition purporting to display artifacts from an ancient Greek shipwreck was in fact a solo exhibition for this artist. In another controversial act, this artist bypassed galleries and sold directly to the public with his Sotheby’s show, Beautiful Inside My Mind Forever, where this artist auctioned off The Golden Calf, a bull with gold-encrusted horns (*) suspended in liquid. The pear-shaped diamond known as the “Skull Star Diamond” is on the center of a platinum-cast human skull made by this artist and titled For the Love of God. For 10 points, name this formerly Young British Artist whose work The Physical Impossibility of Death in the Mind of Someone Living consists of a tiger-shark in a tank of formaldehyde.
ANSWER: Damien Hirst
