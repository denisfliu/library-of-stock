Tossups

T1 2023 ILLIAC (2023) Diff 7 · Literature
A character in a play by this author bets a king that if he can make the king laugh, he gets a hundred gold coins, but if he fails, his teeth will be pulled out. The antagonist of a play by this author stages a duel so his opponent can verify the beauty of a girl hiding in an attic. One play by this author opens with a woman arriving near a mountain on a “wild hippogriff.” The protagonist of a play by this author is lauded by the newly arrived (*) king after hanging the man who raped his daughter Isabel. A man in a play by this author recognizes his daughter by the sword he gave to her mother in Russia. In that play by this author, Rosaura finds the imprisoned Prince of Poland, Segismundo. For 10 points, the plays The Mayor of Zalamea and Life is a Dream are by what Spanish Golden Age playwright?
ANSWER: Pedro Calderón de la Barca (The lead-in is The Surgeon of his Honor.)

This author, who created the noblewoman Mencia in The (*) Surgeon of His Honour, wrote a play in which a farmer garrottes Don Alvaro so he can be raised to the title position. In another play by this author, Clotaldo recognises a sword belonging to Rosaura. In that play by this author, Basilio imprisons his son in a tower in fear that the Polish throne will be taken by that son, Sigismundo. For 10 points, name this Spanish playwright of The Mayor of Zalamea and Life is a Dream.

T4 2014 Penn Bowl (2014) Diff 8 · Literature
One of the most prominent translators of this author was the Irish poet Denis Florence MacCarthy. In the first act of a play by this man, a marching song that begins many lines with the phrase “Titiri tiri” is sung by a soldier’s mistress known as “La Chispa”. One of his plays includes comic relief from a jester named Clarin, while another features the roulette-related schemes of Rebolledo. At the end of one of this man’s plays, (*) Astolfo is impelled to marry Rosaura after a grateful Basilio abdicates the throne of Poland in favor of his once-imprisoned son Segismundo. In another of his plays, Don Alvaro is anonymously garotted in his jail cell, leading Philip II to appoint Pedro Crespo to the title municipal position. For 10 points, name this late Spanish Golden Age playwright of The Mayor of Zalamea and Life Is a Dream.
ANSWER: Pedro Calderon de la Barca [or Pedro Calderón de la Barca y Barreda González de Henao Ruiz de Blasco y Riaño]
T5 2014 CLEAR (2014) Diff 10 · Literature
In one work by this writer, a stranger’s arrival distracts Guillen, causing him to misunderstand a flower his friend receives from Violante; that work ends with the title event, when Blanca, Don Lope, and Don Mendo find the last’s son garroted by order of the king. In another work by this author of Three Judgments in One, Coquin fears the loss of his teeth, after he is forced to make a deal with King Pedro to make the latter laugh; that work by this author ends with Leonor as the new wife of Guttiere, after he had a bloodletter bleed his first wife, Mencia, to death on suspicion of her having an affair with Enrique. Another play by this author of The Surgeon of His Honor ends after Clarion is shot while trying to hide behind a rock, revealing to Basilio that fate cannot be changed, though he had sought to do just that by keeping his son Segismundo imprisoned in a tower. For 10 points, name this Spanish playwright of Life Is a Dream.

T8 2012 QUARK (2012) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
This playwright retold the Faustian story of Saints Cyprian and Justina in one play. He wrote a play whose title character, Diego, claims to know about Juan and María’s trysts through supernatural means. He is also known for his wife-murder tragedies, including Secret Vengeance for Secret Insult and a play in which Gutierre suspects his wife Mencía of being unfaithful with Prince Enrique. He wrote around seventy plays in the genre of the (*) auto sacramental, one-act allegorical plays, including The Great Theater of the World. He wrote a play in which King Philip II approves of the title character’s decision to execute Don Alvaro for raping Isabel, as well as one in which a prophecy about the violence of Prince Segismundo is initially confirmed after he is released from his lifelong imprisonment. For 10 points, name this playwright of The Mayor of Zalamea and Life Is a Dream.
ANSWER: Pedro Calderón de la Barca
T9 2012 BARGE (2012) Diff 8 · Literature
A traveling painter murders Serafina and her kidnapper in a characteristic work of this author's later period, which is filled with plays about husbands revenge-murdering their wives. This man's play about Amnon's rape of Tamar, The Hair of Absalom, heavily plagiarizes and drastically improves a work by Tirso de Molina. He told of King Edward’s love for the Countess of Salveric in Love, Honor, and Power, and wrote about Doña Ángela’s attempt to woo Don Manuel in The (*) Phantom Lady. A dispute between an army leader and a rural farm owner is central to a play by this author that sees Don Alvaro de Ataide rape the daughter of Pedro Crespo, who is permanently installed by Philip II in the titular post. In the most famous play by this man, King Basilio imprisons Segismundo, who is unsure whether or not he is awake. For 10 points, name this author of The Mayor of Zalamea and Life is a Dream, who succeeded Lope de Vega as the leading Spanish Golden Age dramatist.
ANSWER: Pedro Calderón de la Barca y Barreda González de Henao Ruiz de Blasco y Riaño

B6 2017 Oxford Open (2017) Diff 8 · Literature
