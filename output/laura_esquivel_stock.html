Summary of Facts

Laura Esquivel is a Mexican author best known for Like Water for Chocolate, a magical realist novel about Tita de la Garza. The novel's chapters each begin with a recipe (structured as 'monthly installments'), and it opens by urging the reader to place an onion on their head to keep from crying. Mama Elena forbids her youngest daughter Tita from marrying. The novel features the Native American grandmother Morning Light. A woman dies 'sighing out her last flatulent breath' and few attend her funeral due to the smell. The novel ends with the ghosts of Tita and Pedro setting fire to a ranch.

Esquivel also wrote Malinche, a historical novel about La Malinche that gives her the name Malinalli. In it, Malinalli falls in love with Hernan Cortes after identifying him with the Aztec god Quetzalcoatl, and a character recalls her grandmother saying 'if words are not used to water the memory of others then they are useless' while learning to work with clay.

Other works include Swift as Desire (about telegraph operator Jubilo Chi, 'good at communicating and at loving Lucha') and The Law of Love (about the romance between Rodrigo Sanchez and astrologist Azucena Martinez).
