Summary of Facts

Yves Klein was a French experimental artist and co-founder of Nouveau Realisme (New Realism) alongside the art critic Pierre Restany, who coined the term. The movement's manifesto was signed in Klein's apartment. Nouveau Realisme is often described as a French manifestation of Pop Art. Klein was an avid judo practitioner and had a fascination with Rosicrucianism that led to his obsession with emptiness and the void.

His most frequently clued works are the Anthropometries, in which nude female models ('living brushes') covered themselves in his signature International Klein Blue (IKB) paint and rolled or pressed on canvases. A 20-minute making-of film is accompanied by the 'Monotone Silence Symphony,' a single D-flat chord. Klein's The Void (Le Vide) exhibition at the Iris Clert Gallery in 1958 consisted of an empty white gallery with only an empty display cabinet; 3,000 visitors were served blue cocktails, and 1,001 balloons were released. Arman later filled the same gallery with trash in response. Klein's Leap into the Void is a doctored photograph by Harry Shunk and Jean Kender showing him apparently jumping from a second-story rooftop, collected in his artbook Dimanche.

International Klein Blue is his namesake shade of synthetic ultramarine, used across hundreds of monochrome paintings. Eleven identical, differently priced blue canvases inspired Piero Manzoni's white Achromes. Derek Jarman's experimental 1993 AIDS film Blue approximated IKB. Klein also applied the pigment to plaster casts of the Venus de Milo. In his Zone of Immaterial Pictorial Sensibility, he sold certificates of ownership of empty space for gold, then threw the gold dust into the Seine and burned the receipt. Other works include fire paintings (asbestos cardboard burned with a blowtorch) and the Cosmogonies series (canvases fastened to his Citroen car and exposed to the elements). He theorized the void in his Chelsea Hotel Manifesto. Klein died of a heart attack the night he watched Mondo Cane, which featured a parodic recreation of his Anthropometries.
