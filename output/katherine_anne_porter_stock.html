Summary of Facts

Katherine Anne Porter is an American author best known for her short fiction. The title novella of Pale Horse, Pale Rider follows theater critic Miranda Gay and soldier Adam Barclay during the 1918 Spanish flu pandemic. Adam dies while caring for Miranda, who survives. Miranda shouts "Dr. Hildesheim is a Boche!" during a hallucination and is coaxed into buying Liberty Bonds with colleague Towney.

"Noon Wine" follows dairy farmer Royal Earle Thompson, who hires escaped mental patient Olaf Helton (who plays an enigmatic Swedish folk song on his harmonica and nearly strangles a child for touching it). Thompson kills bounty hunter Homer T. Hatch and later commits suicide after touring neighboring farms to explain himself.

"Flowering Judas" features Laura, disillusioned with the Mexican Revolution, and Braggioni as "a symbol of her many disillusions." Laura ferries money between agitators and delivers narcotics to political prisoners; in a dream, Eugenio calls her a murderer and cannibal after she eats flowers from the Judas tree.

Ship of Fools follows passengers aboard the Vera sailing from Mexico to Germany, with a title from Sebastian Brant's satire. Mary Treadwell beats up William Denny. "Maria Concepcion" involves Juan covering up his wife's murder of beekeeper Maria Rosa.
