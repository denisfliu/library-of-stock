Kimberly Akimbo

A 2021 musical with music by Jeanine Tesori and book and lyrics by David Lindsay-Abaire, based on Lindsay-Abaire's 2000 play. Won the 2023 Tony Award for Best Musical. The story follows Kimberly Levaco, a teenager in New Jersey who has a condition that causes her to age rapidly. Victoria Clark won a Tony for originating the role of Kimberly. The musical features her con-artist aunt Debra, a love interest who brainstorms anagrams of her name, and a number framed as a letter to the Make-A-Wish Foundation.
Plot and Characters

Kimberly Levaco is a teenager who ages rapidly due to a medical condition. She is repeatedly perplexed by references to 'what happened in Secaucus.' Her aunt Debra is a con artist who once lived in a library after a coyote set fire to her tent; Kimberly listens to Debra dragging a USPS collection box down the stairs. Kimberly has a birthday party at a place called Skater Planet in the number 'Better.' Her love interest brainstorms anagrams of her name, eventually landing on one that uses the word 'Akimbo.' She is told 'you gotta hustle hard, you gotta muscle through' by a character who announces a show choir's sexual orientations. Wikipedia

General / Identification

The musical is composed by Jeanine Tesori (who also composed Fun Home and Shrek the Musical) with book and lyrics by David Lindsay-Abaire. Won the 2023 Tony Award for Best Musical. Tesori and Lindsay-Abaire shared the 2023 Tony for Best Original Score. Victoria Clark was nominated for (and won) a Tony for playing the title role of a rapidly-aging teenager. Wikipedia
