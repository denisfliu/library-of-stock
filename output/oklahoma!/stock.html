The first collaboration between Richard Rodgers and Oscar Hammerstein II (1943). A landmark musical about cowboy Curly courting Laurey in the Oklahoma territory, while the farmhand Jud Fry also vies for her affections. Famous for Agnes de Mille's dream ballet, the songs 'Oh, What a Beautiful Mornin'' and 'The Surrey with the Fringe on Top,' and its 2019 Daniel Fish revival. Credited with popularizing the dream ballet in Broadway musicals. Orchestrated by Robert Russell Bennett.
Dream Ballet ("Laurey Makes Up Her Mind")

Agnes de Mille's choreographed dream ballet sequence, in which Laurey drinks the Elixir of Egypt and dreams about choosing between Jud and Curly. Begins after the song 'Out of My Dreams.' This sequence is approximately fifteen minutes long and is considered one of the most famous features of the musical. Oklahoma! is credited with popularizing dream ballets in Broadway, though it did not invent them. In the 2019 Daniel Fish revival, John Heginbotham choreographed the dream sequence with performers dancing barefoot in a dark setting. Wikipedia

"The Surrey with the Fringe on Top"

A song in which Curly describes the surrey he will take Laurey out in. Contains the famous lyric 'Chicks and ducks and geese better scurry.' The melody was based on the repetition of one note with a 'clip-clop' rhythm, according to Bob Craig. Adapted into a jazz standard by Ahmad Jamal, which inspired a popular Miles Davis recording (on the album Steamin').

2019 Daniel Fish Revival

Daniel Fish directed the 2019 Broadway revival, which served cornbread and chili to audience members during intermission. Ali Stroker (also spelled Stoker in some sources), who is paralyzed from the waist down and uses a wheelchair, played Ado Annie and became the first wheelchair-using performer to win a Tony. John Heginbotham choreographed the dream sequence with performers dancing barefoot. Aunt Eller makes cornbread instead of churning butter in the opening.

Summary of Facts

Oklahoma! is the first collaboration between composer Richard Rodgers and lyricist Oscar Hammerstein II, premiering in 1943. It was Rodgers' first musical after switching from his previous lyricist Lorenz Hart. The show tells the story of the cowboy Curly courting Laurey in the Oklahoma territory, while the farmhand Jud Fry also vies for her affections. The title state is described as a place 'where the wind comes sweepin' down the plain.'

The musical is most famous for its dream ballet, choreographed by Agnes de Mille, in which Laurey 'makes up her mind' about Jud and Curly. This fifteen-minute sequence begins after Laurey drinks the Elixir of Egypt and features the song 'Out of My Dreams.' Oklahoma! is widely credited with popularizing the dream ballet in Broadway musicals, though it did not invent the form. Rodgers and Hammerstein hired de Mille after watching her receive 22 curtain calls at the premiere of her ballet Rodeo (scored by Aaron Copland). De Mille also choreographed dream ballets for their subsequent musical Carousel.

The musical's signature songs include 'Oh, What a Beautiful Mornin',' the opening number in which Curly proclaims 'I've got a beautiful feeling / everything's going my way' (the musical both opens and closes with this song, and its melody features prominently in the dream ballet); 'The Surrey with the Fringe on Top,' in which Curly sings 'chicks and ducks and geese better scurry' and describes 'the wheels are yeller, the upholstery's brown, the dashboard's genuine leather' (this song was adapted into a jazz standard by Ahmad Jamal, inspiring a Miles Davis recording that opens the album Steamin'; the melody was based on repetition of one note with a 'clip-clop' rhythm per Bob Craig); 'Lonely Room,' Jud's solo about imagining 'long tangled hair' which 'falls across my face' while vowing to win a bride; 'The Farmer and the Cowboy,' which features a square dance; and 'It's a Scandal! It's a Outrage!'

The original orchestration was by Robert Russell Bennett, and John Wilson's 2023 recording was the first complete recording of this orchestration.

Daniel Fish directed a notable 2019 Broadway revival that served cornbread and chili to audience members during intermission. In this revival, Ali Stroker played the promiscuous Ado Annie; Stroker, who is paralyzed from the waist down and uses a wheelchair, became the first wheelchair-using performer to win a Tony Award. John Heginbotham choreographed the dream sequence in this revival with performers dancing barefoot in a dark setting. In the revival's opening, Aunt Eller makes cornbread instead of churning butter. One character sings about the surrey while playing an acoustic guitar, with a slowed-down section where he sings 'don't you wish it would go on forever?'
