English Catholic novelist and MI6 agent. Known for The Power and the Glory (whiskey priest in Mexico), Brighton Rock (Pinkie Brown), The End of the Affair (Bendrix and Sarah Miles during the Blitz), The Quiet American (Pyle and Fowler in Vietnam), The Heart of the Matter (Scobie in Sierra Leone), Our Man in Havana (Wormold the vacuum-cleaner spy). Wrote screenplay for The Third Man. Key stories include 'The Destructors' and 'The End of the Party.' Converted to Catholicism in 1926; classified his novels into 'Catholic novels' and 'entertainments.'

Summary of Facts

Graham Greene (1904-1991) is clued as an English Catholic novelist who was also an MI6 agent. He converted to Catholicism in 1926 to marry Vivien Dayrell-Browning, and classified his own novels into 'Catholic novels' and 'entertainments.' He played Russian roulette as a teenager, a fact that impressed Fidel Castro.

The Power and the Glory is his most frequently clued novel. It follows an unnamed 'whiskey priest' hunted by an anti-clerical lieutenant in the Mexican state of Tabasco, with the title referencing the doxology. The priest is betrayed by a yellow-toothed mestizo, sheltered by the German couple the Lehrs, and taught Morse code by Coral Fellows (daughter of a banana plantation owner who refuses to let the lieutenant search their property). The priest has an illegitimate daughter Brigida, fights a dog over a bone before reading 'Lord Ullin's Daughter,' and steals a bone from a crippled dog and sugar cube from a child's grave. The novel opens with English dentist Mr. Tench walking to a wharf. Stories of the boy martyr Juan are interspersed throughout, and the boy Luis spits on the gun used to kill the protagonist at the end. A five-year-old is buried in the Garden of God marked 'Silencio.' The fat priest Padre Jose ignores pleas to hear confession; the protagonist crosses the border again to hear a dying man's confession and is then captured.

Brighton Rock features teenage gang leader Pinkie Brown, investigated by Ida Arnold after the murder of newspaper contest participant Fred Hale ('Kolley Kibber'). The title refers to a type of candy and is used as a metaphor for human nature. Pinkie marries the waitress Rose at Snow's so she cannot testify against him, and tries to trick her into a suicide pact. He records 'God damn you...why can't you go back home forever and let me be' on a gramophone record. Pinkie quotes Camden's 'Betwixt the stirrup and the ground,' and his lawyer Prewitt quotes Marlowe's Doctor Faustus. Pinkie accidentally splashes vitriol on his own face and falls off a cliff. Gang members include Spicer, Dallow, and Cubit; Pinkie kills Spicer by pushing him down stairs. Ida conducts a seance with Old Crow. The novel ends with a priest declaring 'the appalling strangeness of the mercy of God.'

The End of the Affair is about Maurice Bendrix's affair with Sarah Miles during the London Blitz, and is the last of Greene's 'Catholic novels,' inspired by his relationship with Lady Catherine Walston. Sarah stops seeing Bendrix after a V-1 rocket nearly kills him, having made a desperate deal with God. The narrator calls the novel 'a record of hate far more than of love.' Private detective Alfred Parkis is hired to follow Sarah and recovers her diary, discovering she has been meeting the militant atheist Richard Smythe. Bendrix and Sarah eat steak and onions at Rules to indicate interest. Sarah dies of pneumonia after walking across Clapham Common in the rain; posthumous miracles include the healing of a strawberry birthmark on Smythe's face and a boy's recovery from appendicitis. Sarah writes 'I've caught belief like a disease.' The novel ends with Bendrix begging God to leave him alone.

The Quiet American is set in Vietnam and features CIA agent Alden Pyle versus journalist Thomas Fowler, with Phuong as the love interest. Pyle is inspired by York Harding's theory of a 'Third Force' and funds a plastics company producing explosive bicycle pumps. Fowler plays the dice game 421 with investigator Vigot and collaborates with Mr. Heng to kill Pyle. Fowler lies to Phuong that his Catholic wife has granted a divorce. Burdick and Lederer's The Ugly American was titled in reference to this novel.

The Heart of the Matter follows Major Henry Scobie, a Catholic police commissioner in Freetown, Sierra Leone, who commits suicide. Scobie has an affair with shipwreck survivor Helen Rolt (who washes ashore with a stamp album) and is blackmailed by the Syrian diamond smuggler Yusef. His wife Louise is sent to South Africa; his pet name is 'Ticki.' He searches the Portuguese ship Esperanca for diamonds. Agent Wilson despises Scobie, and servant Ali is killed due to Scobie's dealings with Yusuf. Scobie pretends to be the father of a dying girl after the shipwreck and reads 'A Bishop among the Bantus' to a sick boy, pretending it is about pirates. In Chinua Achebe's No Longer at Ease, Obi says this novel was ruined by its 'happy ending.' In Adichie's Americanah, Ifemelu and Obinze discuss it.

Our Man in Havana is a spy comedy about vacuum cleaner salesman James Wormold, recruited by Hawthorne for MI6 in Cuba. He fabricates intelligence reports using vacuum cleaner schematics, passing off drawings of the 'Atomic Pile' as military installations in Oriente. He plays checkers with Captain Segura using whiskey bottles as pieces, gets Segura drunk, and steals his gun to kill Carter in revenge for the death of Dr. Hasselbacher. Segura owns a cigarette case made of human skin and opines about a 'torturable class.' Wormold's daughter Milly set her classmate Thomas Earl Parkman Jr. on fire. A dachshund named Max drinks poisoned whiskey at a European Traders Association banquet and dies. Secretary Beatrice Severn assists Wormold; they use Lamb's Tales from Shakespeare as a book code.

'The Destructors' is Greene's most clued short story: the Wormsley Common Gang, led by T. (Mr. Thomas), destroys Old Misery's house, which survived the Blitz. Old Misery is locked in an outhouse and a mattress full of money is burned. 'The End of the Party' features Francis Morton, who dies of fright during hide-and-seek when his twin brother touches him. 'The Basement Room' was adapted as The Fallen Idol by director Carol Reed (who also directed The Third Man). Other stories include 'A Hint of an Explanation' (baker offers boy an electric train for stolen communion wafers), 'A Visit to Morin,' 'The Invisible Japanese Gentlemen,' and 'Alas, Poor Maling.'

Greene wrote the screenplay for The Third Man (directed by Carol Reed), though the famous 'cuckoo clock' line was not in his screenplay. He helped R.K. Narayan get published, calling him 'the Indian Chekhov,' and helped publish Flann O'Brien's At Swim-Two-Birds. He sued Yambo Ouologuem for plagiarizing It's a Battlefield. Other works include Travels with My Aunt (Henry Pulling becomes a smuggler in Paraguay through Aunt Augusta; Brighton is the first destination), Stamboul Train (dancer Coral Musker and merchant Carleton Myatt on the Orient Express), The Ministry of Fear (Arthur Rowe given cake with secret film during the Blitz), A Burnt-Out Case (architect Querry in a Congolese leper colony), and The Honorary Consul (Dr. Eduardo Plarr dies). He was a friend of Omar Torrijos and commended Patrick Hamilton's The West Pier as the best book about Brighton.
