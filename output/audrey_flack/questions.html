B1 2022 ACF Nationals (2022) Diff 9 · Fine Arts
Mary Beth Edelson’s collage Some Living American Women Artists replaces the faces of people in this painting with those of people like Alma Thomas and Helen Frankenthaler. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this painting on which Marisol based a sculpture group that shows her looking at its scene. Renee Cox replaced many of this work’s figures with black men in a photographic montage.
ANSWER: The Last Supper (by Leonardo da Vinci)
[10] Edelson’s Last Supper replaces Christ with this artist, whose paintings of animal skulls like Ram’s Head, White Hollyhock-Hills were inspired by the New Mexico landscape.
ANSWER: Georgia O’Keeffe [or Georgia Totto O’Keeffe]
[10] One of the few color photographs in Edelson’s collage is of this photorealist, whose vanitas paintings include Wheel of Fortune and Marilyn.
ANSWER: Audrey Flack

T1 2024 ACF Fall (2024) Diff 6 · Fine Arts
An open book containing a portrait of this person is included in Audrey Flack’s series Vanitas. Several paintings of this person had a bullet shot through them by Dorothy Podber. An artist’s “Death and Disaster” series includes a “White” portrait of this person that sold for $41 million in 2014. A publicity photograph from the film Niagara was used for a series of silkscreen portraits depicting this person in highly saturated, bright colors. In the film The Seven Year Itch, this person’s white dress is blown upwards by a train passing underneath a subway grate. For 10 points, several Andy Warhol portraits depicted which cultural icon, who starred in films like Gentlemen Prefer Blondes?
ANSWER: Marilyn Monroe [or Marilyn or Norma Jeane Mortenson; accept Shot Marilyns or Marilyn (Vanitas) or White Marilyn or Marilyn Diptych or Untitled from Marilyn Monroe]
T2 2023 "Questions Based on Wikipedia Links Ophir Sent Me" (2023) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
A Jana Sterbak (“YA-na SHTAIR-bahk”) work titled for this genre, which featured slabs of beef sewn together over a tailor’s dummy and was subtitled “Flesh Dress for an Albino Anorectic,” caused an uproar when it was displayed at the National Gallery of Canada. A Charles Allan Gilbert drawing titled in reference to this genre features a double image that, when viewed up close, depicts a young woman seated at a table. David Bailly’s (“DAH-vit BYE-yee’s”) paintings in this genre partly inspired Audrey Flack’s photorealistic series titled for this genre, one entry of which depicts photographs of (*) Marilyn Monroe. Flowers, insects, bubbles, and rotting food were among the frequent subjects of this genre during the Dutch Golden Age. For 10 points, name this genre of symbolic still life that depicts the motif of memento mori.
ANSWER: vanitas (“VAN-ih-toss”) [prompt on still-life; prompt on memento mori; prompt on All Is Vanity]
T3 2020 FLopen (2020) Diff 9 · Fine Arts
Photographers David Cicero and John Florea both took pictures of this person reading Walt Whitman's Leaves of Grass within a year of each other. Eve Arnold captured this person on a rusty piece of playground equipment reading Molly Bloom’s soliloquy from Ulysses. This person is surrounded by “the telephone dial-like ring of her destiny” in a nude portrait titled for her Metamorphosis by Robert Indiana. Another painting titled for this woman contains a candlestick that reflects the image of (*) James Dean; that Vanitas was painted by photorealist Audrey Flack. William Travilla designed a dress worn by this woman in a scene where her character has just seen the film Creature from the Black Lagoon. An iconic film still depicts this woman above a subway grate while on the set of The Seven Year Itch. For 10 points, name this actress who starred in films like Gentlemen Prefer Blondes.
ANSWER: Marilyn Monroe [or Norma Jean Mortenson]
T4 2017 ACF Regionals (2017) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
Several photographs of this smiling person feature in a photorealistic “vanitas” still life by Audrey Flack. Dorothy Podber used a revolver to shoot a hole in a stack of four paintings of this person. A paste-up by Richard Hamilton consisting of crossed-out negatives of this person is titled “my” this person. Another painting features a small image of this person at the center of a six-foot-tall canvas covered in metallic gold paint. This person’s face is depicted in 25 colorful images and 25 black-and-white images composing a 1962 artwork; like similar pieces featuring Jackie Kennedy and Mao Zedong, that silkscreen “diptych” depicting this woman was made in “The Factory.” For 10 points, name this actress whose image was repeatedly reproduced by Andy Warhol after her 1962 suicide.
ANSWER: Marilyn Monroe [accept either underlined portion; or Norma Jeane Baker; or Norma Jean Mortenson; accept My Marilyn or Marilyn Diptych]
T5 2015 ACF Nationals (2015) Diff 9 · Fine Arts
Members of this movement produced paintings capturing their impressions as tourists in the “Prague Project.” The desert, sea waves and lunar craters are the subject of texture paintings by this movement’s Latvian member, Vija Celmins. A female artist from this movement created vanitas paintings, such as one that juxtaposes an hourglass, a candle, and a calendar titled Wheel of Fortune. Another artist from this movement captured the activity of a city in a painting in which the title structures reflect the action, called Telephone Booths. An artist from this movement worked in a wheelchair with a paintbrush strapped to his wrist, after he became quadriplegic. This movement included Audrey Flack, Richard Estes, and an artist who painted grids of tiles filled with shapes which, viewed from afar, look like faces. For 10 points, name this movement to which Chuck Close belonged, which made paintings that resembled camera images.
ANSWER: photorealism [or hyper-realism; or super-realism]
