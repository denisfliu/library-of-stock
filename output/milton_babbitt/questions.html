T1 2022 Penn Bowl (2022) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
Flute, violin, percussion, and a singer with this voice type are featured in Kate Soper’s chamber opera Ipsa Dixit. This voice type is sampled over a synthesizer in an Ovid-inspired piece by Milton Babbitt. In an orchestral piece, a singer with this voice type echoes the instrumental motif of E - G-sharp - F-sharp in the “Lento e largo—Tranquillissimo” second movement. Gepopo from Ligeti’s (“LIG-uh-tee’s”) Le Grand Macabre is a difficult role for singers of this voice type, who often play conceited (*) soubrette roles in traditional operas. A singer with this voice type sings a medieval Polish lament for Mary and a text from a concentration camp wall in Górecki’s (“goo-RET-skee’s”) Symphony of Sorrowful Songs. Singers with this voice type have a range between C4 and C6, and can be classified as spinto, dramatic, or coloratura. For 10 points, what highest standard voice part is represented by the “S” in SATB choirs?
ANSWER: soprano [accept dramatic, coloratura, or spinto soprano] (The Babbitt piece is Philomel.)

Bonuses

B1 2022 WORKSHOP (2022) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
In twelve-tone and serialist theory, two complementary sets of these musical series combine to form combinatorial aggregates. For 10 points each:
[10] Name these series that share their name with a mnemonic device created by the medieval music theorist Guido of Arezzo. A “magic” one of these series was used by Arnold Schoenberg in his piece Ode to Napoleon Buonaparte.
ANSWER: hexachords [or hexachordon; accept magic hexachord; prompt on hexad or hexatonic]
[10] The magic hexachord is one of the six all-combinatorial hexachord sets formulated as part of this composer’s serialist theory. This composer attracted controversy for his article “The Composer as Specialist.”
ANSWER: Milton Babbitt [or Milton Byron Babbitt]
[10] Hexachords are exhibited in the twelve-tone rows popularized by this school of composition based in an Austrian city. Along with Schoenberg, Alban Berg and Anton Webern were leading composers from this school.
ANSWER: Second Viennese School [or Zweite Wiener Schule; or New Viennese School or Neue Wiener Schule; prompt on Viennese School; prompt on Vienna or Wien]
