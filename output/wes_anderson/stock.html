American filmmaker known for symmetrical flat compositions, pastel color palettes, and use of Futura typeface. Major films include The Grand Budapest Hotel (2014), Moonrise Kingdom (2012), The Life Aquatic with Steve Zissou (2004), and two stop-motion features, Fantastic Mr. Fox and Isle of Dogs. Frequent collaborators include Owen Wilson, Jason Schwartzman, Adrien Brody, and Tilda Swinton.

Summary of Facts

Wes Anderson (born 1969) is an American filmmaker known for his highly distinctive visual style: flat, symmetrical 'planimetric' compositions, vibrant pastel color palettes, and consistent use of the Futura typeface. His recurring collaborators include Owen Wilson (famously known for saying 'wow' on screen; Bottle Rocket has the first recorded instance), Jason Schwartzman, Adrien Brody, and Tilda Swinton. His earlier films were scored by Mark Mothersbaugh of DEVO. He has designed Bar Luce, a café, for the Prada Foundation.

The Grand Budapest Hotel (2014) is Anderson's most acclaimed and clued film. Set in the fictional Republic of Zubrowka (named after Budapest, Hungary) and based on stories by Stefan Zweig, it stars Ralph Fiennes as concierge Gustave H and his bellhop Zero Moustafa. The plot revolves around the painting 'Boy with Apple,' bequeathed by Madame D. Deputy Kovacs (Jeff Goldblum) is found stuffed in a sarcophagus with four fingers missing after being chased through an art museum by the villain J.G. Jopling (Willem Dafoe). Items are smuggled in pink, ribboned Mendl's pastry boxes — graphic designer Annie Atkins misspelled 'patisserie' with two T's on the boxes, requiring VFX alterations to nearly 3,000 of them. The film features a first-person tandem sled chase from a monastery to a luge track. The interior set was built in a mall lobby in Görlitz, Germany; Adam Stockhausen won the Oscar for production design.

Moonrise Kingdom (2012) follows 12-year-old Khaki Scout Sam Shakusky (Jared Gilman) and Suzy Bishop as they run away together from a New England island. They had met during a performance of Benjamin Britten's opera Noye's Fludde (Anderson himself had performed in Noye's Fludde as a child). The film is filled with references to Britten; Alexandre Desplat's score was Britten-inspired. Captain Sharp is played by Bruce Willis; Scout Master Ward by Edward Norton. Cousin Ben (Jason Schwartzman) officiates the protagonists' wedding while complaining about $75 in nickels. A lead actress injured her hand punching a mirror; another character is stabbed in the back with left-handed scissors.

The Life Aquatic with Steve Zissou (2004) was inspired by the real life of Jacques Cousteau. Steve Zissou (Bill Murray) seeks revenge on a jaguar shark aboard his ship the Belafonte. Brazilian musician Seu Jorge performs Portuguese-language David Bowie covers diegetically throughout. Fantastic Mr. Fox (2009) was a stop-motion adaptation of Roald Dahl's novel, voiced by George Clooney as Mr. Fox; Anderson used real animal fur rather than synthetic material. Isle of Dogs (2018) is his second stop-motion film, set in Japan during a canine flu outbreak.

The French Dispatch (2021) is an anthology about the Liberty, Kansas Evening Sun magazine's final issue. In the 'Revisions to a Manifesto' / 'Chessboard Revolution' segment, a journalist played by Frances McDormand helps a teenage revolutionary (Timothée Chalamet) write his manifesto, which he obsesses over in the bath. Bottle Rocket (1996), Anderson's debut feature, starred the real-life brothers Luke and Owen Wilson; the protagonist robs a bookstore with Dignan and pursues a Paraguayan maid at a Texas motel; minor characters include 'Apple Jack' and 'Future Man.'
