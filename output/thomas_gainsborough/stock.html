Summary of Facts

Thomas Gainsborough was an 18th-century English portraitist and landscape painter, best known as the rival of Sir Joshua Reynolds, who was the first president of the Royal Academy. His most famous work, The Blue Boy (c. 1770), is a portrait of Jonathan Buttall dressed in blue, now at the Huntington Library where it hangs opposite Thomas Lawrence's Pinkie (portrait of Sarah Moulton). In 2021, a Kehinde Wiley painting replaced Pinkie. A persistent myth claims The Blue Boy was painted to refute Reynolds's Eighth Discourse, which argued for warm colors. Gainsborough's nephew Gainsborough Dupont may have modeled for the painting.

Mr. and Mrs. Andrews (c. 1750) is a double portrait showing a man with a rifle in the crook of his arm and his wife in a blue dress seated on a green bench, with a famous unfinished/blank patch in the wife's lap, a brown and white dog looking up, and a vast pastoral estate with Sudbury Hill and rows of stubble suggesting a seed drill. Yinka Shonibare recreated it 'without their heads' using headless mannequins in Dutch wax fabric. Gillian Rose described the woman as 'part of the landscape.'

The Morning Walk (1785) depicts the newlywed Halletts walking in a park with a Pomeranian/Spitz dog tugging at the lady's dress. Other frequently clued works include Girl with Pigs (a girl watching pigs drink milk, praised by Reynolds despite his calling Gainsborough's late output 'fancy pictures'), The Painter's Daughters Chasing a Butterfly (two girls in gold and silver), and The Harvest Wagon (figures modeled on Rubens's Descent from the Cross). Notable portraits include Carl Friedrich Abel with a viola da gamba, Georgiana Duchess of Devonshire with a large black 'Gainsborough hat,' Sarah Siddons, and Ignatius Sancho (done in one hour and forty minutes).

Gainsborough broke with the Royal Academy after his Three Eldest Princesses was hung 'above the line' and began exhibiting at Schomberg House. He was a student of Hubert Gravelot and studied realistic children through William Hogarth. He claimed to be 'sick of portraits' and wanted to play the viol.
