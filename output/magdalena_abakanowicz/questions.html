Questions: Magdalena Abakanowicz

1 tossup · 1 bonus

Tossups

T1 2023 ACF Nationals (2023) Diff 9 · Fine Arts
A painting from this country shows a bestial humanoid with black fur and a bandaged head scuttling across the ground in front of a burning city. An artist from this country names a form of large-scale hanging woven artworks with many folds, like those she completed in the 1970s. The Swiss artist H.R. Giger is often compared with a “nightmare” landscape painter from this country known for his thousands of untitled images of hellish environments. A sculptor from this country was inspired by her fear of crowds to sculpt large groups of rough-textured, headless humanoids, such as the pieces Unrecognized and Agora. An 1862 painting from this country shows a party in the right background as a seated man’s frowning expression contrasts with his silly red jester’s outfit. For 10 points, name this home country of Jan Matejko (“yahn mah-TAY-koh”), Magdalena Abakanowicz (“ah-bah-kah-NO-veech”), and Zdzisław Beksiński (“zuh-jee-SWOFF beck-SHIN-skee”).
ANSWER: Poland [or Republic of Poland; accept Rzeczpospolita Polska; accept Polish People’s Republic or Polska Rzeczpospolita Ludowa] (The term for the artworks in the second sentence is abakans, named after Magdalena Abakanowicz.)

B1 2023 MRNA (2023) Diff 7 · Fine Arts
Billie Zangewa cuts out empty sections while making collages of South African life on these artworks. For 10 points each:
[10] Name these woven artworks which hung in place of paintings in castles. These artworks include the Cloisters’ Hunt of the Unicorn and an embroidered chronicle of the Norman Conquest held at Bayeux Cathedral.
ANSWER: tapestries [accept the Bayeux Tapestry]
[10] This country is home to El Anatsui (el ah-NAH-ch-wee), an artist who weaves recycled aluminum artworks similar to cloth tapestries. Messages called adweneasa are weaved into a textile from this country commonly found on graduation stoles at HBCUs.
ANSWER: Ghana
[10] This artist created abstract fiber tapestries titled for the first six letters of her name. This Polish sculptor used burlap to create hollow statues like her Androgyne and Seated Figures series that evoke the horrors of World War II.
ANSWER: Magdalena Abakanowicz

T1 2023 TELEOLOGY (2023) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
Bird-like glass feet attached to blue fabric were displayed in this manner in Luciano Fabro’s Feet series. A Percy Sledge song accompanies a mechanical ventriloquist dummy-like robot being physically abused as it is displayed in this general way in Manic Love by Jordan Wolfson. Large artworks displayed in this way make up most of the oeuvre of Argentine sculptor Tomas Saraceno. Antony Gormley’s Learning to Think consists of headless nude sculptures displayed in this way. A type of folk sculpture displayed in this manner and ubiquitous in northeastern Europe is called Himmeli in Finland and Sweden. Early Soviet artworks displayed in this manner include Vladimir Tatlin’s Letatlin and an “oval construction” by Aleksandr Rodchenko. (*) Magdalena Abakanowicz’s abakans are displayed in this manner. The work Red Polygons is displayed in this manner, as are the woven wire sculptures of Ruth Asawa. For 10 points, name this manner of display used for Alexander Calder’s mobiles
ANSWER: attached to the ceiling [or hanging from the ceiling or suspended from the ceiling; accept hung; accept attached to chains or strings; reject answers that mention “walls” or “hanging on the walls”]

T3 2022 ARCADIA (2022) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
A gray steel lattice sculpture in this city modeled after a giant baseball bat is Claes Oldenburg’s Batcolumn. An installation in this city consists of 106 headless and armless bodies and is Magdalena Abakanowicz’s (“ah-bah-kuh-NO-vich’s”) Agora. Man Enters the Cosmos, a sundial outside this city’s planetarium, was designed by Henry (*) Moore. Either an Afghan Hound or Anubis inspired a sculpture in this city named for Picasso. This city, the location of the sculpture Nuclear Energy, is also home to a stabile (“stay-beel”) that coined the name for its sculptor’s eponymous shade of red. A stainless steel statue in this city was inspired by a droplet of mercury. For 10 points, name this city home to Alexander Calder’s Flamingo and Anish Kapoor’s Cloud Gate, nicknamed “The Bean.”
ANSWER: Chicago

An artist from this country is known for sculpting large crowds of headless figures, such as Chicago’s Agora, as well as woven installations called “abakans.” For 10 points each:
[10] Name this country. Leonardo DiCaprio has popularized a sculptor from this country who originated Zermatism, the theory that historical figures like Machiavelli and Churchill were actually evil half-yeti monsters.
ANSWER: Poland (The second artist is Stanisław Szukalski.)

ANSWER: Washington, D.C. [accept either underlined part]
