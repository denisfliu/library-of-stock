British-Zimbabwean (born in Iran) novelist and 2007 Nobel laureate. Best known for The Golden Notebook, in which Anna Wulf keeps four color-coded notebooks (black, red, yellow, blue) framed by the narrative 'Free Women.' Other major works include The Grass is Singing (Mary Turner murdered by servant Moses; title from The Waste Land), the semi-autobiographical Children of Violence quintet following Martha Quest, The Good Terrorist (Alice Mellings and IRA-linked squatters), and the Canopus in Argos soft sci-fi series. Lessing was influenced by Sufi mysticism and wrote 'inner space fiction' including Briefing for a Descent into Hell and Memoirs of a Survivor. Notable short stories include 'Through the Tunnel,' 'To Room Nineteen,' and 'The Old Chief Mshlanga.' She was a defender of Sufi teacher Idries Shah.
The Golden Notebook

1962 novel about writer Anna Wulf's four color-coded notebooks (black, red, yellow, blue) interspersed with the frame story 'Free Women.' The black notebook details Anna's time in Africa; the red describes her year in the British Communist Party; the yellow develops her alter ego Ella through the autobiographical novel The Shadow of the Third; the blue records her personal life. The golden notebook of the title is the synthesis of all four. Wikipedia

Summary of Facts

Doris Lessing (1919-2013) is a British-Zimbabwean novelist born in Iran (Persia) and raised in Southern Rhodesia. She won the 2007 Nobel Prize in Literature. Her best-known work is The Golden Notebook (1962), in which writer Anna Wulf keeps four color-coded notebooks: black (her time in Africa), red (her year in the British Communist Party), yellow (developing her alter ego Ella through the autobiographical novel The Shadow of the Third), and blue (personal life). The golden notebook of the title represents the synthesis of all four. The novel's frame story, 'Free Women,' concerns Anna and her friend Molly Jacobs. Anna's German psychoanalyst is nicknamed 'Mother Sugar.' Tommy Portmain, Molly's son by the business tycoon Richard, blinds himself in a failed suicide attempt. Anna's boarder Saul Green figures prominently in the final section. The novel engages with Cold War anxieties including the hydrogen bomb test and the execution of the Rosenbergs.

Her debut novel The Grass is Singing (1950) depicts Mary Turner, a white farmer's wife in Southern Rhodesia, who is murdered by her servant Moses after she strikes him across the face with a sambok (whip). The neighbor Charlie Slatter is disgusted to see Moses dressing Mary. The title comes from T.S. Eliot's The Waste Land.

The Children of Violence is a semi-autobiographical quintet following Martha Quest from the fictional country of Zambesia to London after World War II. The last novel, The Four-Gated City, places Martha in a dystopian London where she witnesses World War III.

The Good Terrorist (1985) follows Alice Mellings, who plays house mother to a group of squatters linked to the IRA through the Communist Centre Union (CCU). They eventually set off a car bomb.

Canopus in Argos: Archives is a five-book 'soft' science fiction series about the advanced alien civilization from the star Canopus. It includes Shikasta (featuring emissary Johor), The Sirian Experiments, and The Sentimental Agents in the Volyen Empire. It emphasizes culture and society rather than futuristic technology, and Lessing noted that younger readers of the series rarely engaged with her more traditionally literary output.

Lessing was influenced by Sufi mysticism and wrote 'inner space fiction' including Briefing for a Descent into Hell and Memoirs of a Survivor (the latter partially based on her time living with teenager Jenny Diski). She was a staunch defender of her friend and Sufi teacher Idries Shah, who published a controversial translation of the Rubaiyat of Omar Khayyam. She also wrote an introduction to the Penguin edition of D.H. Lawrence's Lady Chatterley's Lover arguing it should be read as a powerful statement against World War I.

Her short stories include 'Through the Tunnel' (boy Jerry swims under a rock formation), 'To Room Nineteen' (Susan commits suicide by gas in a hotel room), 'The Old Chief Mshlanga' (drawn from her early life in Rhodesia), 'Mrs. Fortescue' (a boy places a revolver under his sister's pillow), 'Report on the Threatened City' (aliens deliver an earthquake warning), and 'Homage for Isaac Babel' (the child Catherine is confused by Babel's imagery).
