Italian verismo opera composer (1858–1924). Major operas include La Bohème (1896), Tosca (1900), Madama Butterfly (1904), La fanciulla del West (1910), Il trittico (1918, comprising Il tabarro, Suor Angelica, and Gianni Schicchi), and the unfinished Turandot. His frequent librettists were Luigi Illica and Giuseppe Giacosa. Close collaborator with conductor Arturo Toscanini. Known for 'Nessun dorma,' 'O mio babbino caro,' 'Un bel dì vedremo,' 'Vissi d'arte,' 'Che gelida manina,' and 'Musetta's Waltz.'
General / Biographical

Giacomo Puccini (1858–1924), Italian verismo opera composer. His frequent librettists were Luigi Illica (who also wrote for Giordano's Andrea Chénier and for Catalani's La Wally) and Giuseppe Giacosa. Close collaborator with conductor Arturo Toscanini, who conducted the premieres of La Bohème, La fanciulla del West, and Turandot. Puccini died of throat cancer before completing Turandot — his deathbed plea to Toscanini was 'don't let [the opera] die.' The musical Rent (Jonathan Larson) is based on La bohème. Musicologist Joseph Kerman called Tosca a 'shabby little shocker.' Puccini's publisher Ricordi rejected La Rondine as 'bad Lehár.' La fanciulla del West (The Girl of the Golden West)

Opera (1910), world premiere at the Met with Emmy Destinn as Minnie. Set during the California Gold Rush. Minnie runs a saloon, is courted by Sheriff Jack Rance and miner Sonora, but loves the bandit Dick Johnson (real name Ramerrez). She cheats in a poker game against Rance to save Dick's life ('una partita a poker'). Dick Johnson sings 'Ch'ella mi creda' ('Let her believe me') before being nearly hanged. The opera was later an inspiration for John Adams's Girls of the Golden West (2017). Wikipedia

Summary of Facts

Giacomo Puccini (1858–1924) is the preeminent Italian opera composer of the verismo era, best known for La Bohème (1896), Tosca (1900), Madama Butterfly (1904), La fanciulla del West (1910), the triptych Il trittico (1918), and the posthumously completed Turandot. His chief librettists were Luigi Illica and Giuseppe Giacosa. He had a close relationship with conductor Arturo Toscanini, who conducted the premieres of La bohème, La fanciulla del West, and Turandot. Puccini died of throat cancer in 1924 before completing Turandot, begging Toscanini on his deathbed 'don't let it die.'

La Bohème follows bohemian artists in Paris. Rodolfo (poet) hides Mimi's key to keep her by candlelight, singing 'Che gelida manina' ('What a cold little hand'); she responds with 'Sì, mi chiamano Mimì.' Musetta's waltz ('Quando me'n vo'') wins back Marcello as she sends Alcindoro on a fake errand. Colline sells his overcoat ('Vecchia zimarra') to pay for medicine; Mimi dies in Rodolfo's arms. Jonathan Larson's musical Rent is based on this opera.

Tosca is a political thriller set in Rome. The painter Cavaradossi hides a fugitive and sings 'Recondita armonia' comparing his painting to Tosca, while Baron Scarpia suspects him. Tosca sings 'Vissi d'arte' (beginning E-flat, D-flat, B-flat, A-flat) in Act II before stabbing Scarpia with his own knife; Scarpia's safe-conduct letter is fake and Cavaradossi is shot. Cavaradossi sings 'E lucevan le stelle' (introduced by a clarinet solo; reprised triple-forte by brass as Tosca leaps from Castel Sant'Angelo). Act I ends with Scarpia joining the chorus singing the Te Deum. Musicologist Joseph Kerman called it a 'shabby little shocker.'

Madama Butterfly is set in Nagasaki. Butterfly sings 'Un bel dì vedremo' ('One Beautiful Day') waiting for Lieutenant Pinkerton's return. Pinkerton's theme uses motifs from 'The Star-Spangled Banner' (not yet the national anthem). A humming chorus (with solo viola d'amore) bridges the acts; it begins with cellos playing pizzicato F, A, B-flat before flutes answer with arpeggiated B-flat major. Butterfly stabs herself ('Con onor muore').

Turandot features Calaf solving three riddles to win the icy princess Turandot; he sings 'Nessun dorma' ('None shall sleep') in Act III. Toscanini conducted the premiere.

Il trittico (1918) comprises three one-act operas: Il tabarro (Michele hides Luigi's body under his cloak), Suor Angelica (a nun's secret child), and Gianni Schicchi (Dante-based comedy in which Buoso's greedy relatives scheme over his estate, including his mule, house, and mill). Lauretta sings 'O mio babbino caro' (opening A-flat, A-flat, A-flat, long C, G, long F, E-flat with harp arpeggios), threatening to throw herself in the Arno if she can't marry Rinuccio.

La fanciulla del West (1910) premiered at the Met with Emmy Destinn as Minnie; it is set during the California Gold Rush and features Minnie cheating at poker against Sheriff Rance ('una partita a poker') to save the bandit Dick Johnson, who sings 'Ch'ella mi creda' before near-hanging. This opera inspired John Adams's Girls of the Golden West (2017).

La Rondine ('The Swallow'): Magda is named 'La Rondine' by the poet Prunier; she and her lover Ruggero write their names on a tablecloth; Puccini's publisher Ricordi dismissed the opera as 'bad Lehár.' Manon Lescaut (1893): Manon dies in Louisiana singing 'Sola, perduta, abbandonata!'
