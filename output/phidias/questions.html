Tossups

T1 2022 NASAT (2022) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
To reconstruct a sculpture by this artist, Adolf Furtwängler ("FYOORT-vain-glur") attached a head from the Pelagio Palagi to a body kept in Dresden. An inscription calling a wrestler beautiful appears on the little finger of a statue by this artist. Although this artist likely didn't use the number, Mark Barr referenced this sculptor's name when he symbolized the golden ratio. A Lawrence Alma-Tadema painting depicts this artist presenting a frieze of the Panathenaic ("pan-uh-thuh-NAY-ic") games to his friends. Alan LeQuire recreated a sculpture by this artist in the naos of a William Crawford Smith project in Centennial Park, Nashville. This artist and his patron Pericles appear on the shield of a chryselephantine ("chris-ell-uh-FAN-tin") sculpture by him wearing gold and holding a small Nike. For 10 points, name this Athenian sculptor of the Statue of Zeus at Olympia and of the Athena in the Parthenon.
ANSWER: Phidias [or Pheidias]
T2 2019 Delta Burke (2019) Diff 6 · Science
If the smallest edge of a Kepler triangle is one, then the hypotenuse equals the square of this number. A continued fraction comprised only of ones can be used to express this number. A regular pentagon whose side length is one will have diagonals with a length of this irrational number, which is also a positive solution to the equation x-squared minus x minus one equals zero. Consecutive numbers in the (*) Fibonacci sequence converge to this value, which is approximately 1.618 and symbolized phi. For 10 points, name this number that is equal to one plus the square root of five, all divided by two.
ANSWER: golden ratio [or golden mean; or golden section; or golden proportion; or mean of Phidias; accept divine in place of golden; accept phi or numerical equivalents before mentioned]

ANSWER: Jean-Baptiste Dominique Ingres (“ANG-ruh”)
[10] In the painting, Napoleon holds a scepter that has a statuette of this ruler at the end. This ruler inaugurated an artistic period that produced the illuminated Ebbo Gospels and the Palatine Chapel designed by Odo of Metz.

[10] In Ingres’s painting, Napoleon’s pose is modeled on descriptions of this sculpture. Pausanias claims that this sculpture wore a glass robe and held a figurine of Nike.
