Athena Parthenos

Colossal chryselephantine statue of Athena housed in the naos of the Parthenon in Athens. About 41 feet tall. The artist and his patron Pericles appear on her circular shield. A coiled snake resided between the shield and her feet. She holds a Nike in her right hand. Her triple-crested helmet features a sphinx flanked by two pegasi. The Amazonomachy appears on her shield. Alan LeQuire recreated it in plaster for the naos of the Parthenon replica in Nashville. The Varvakeion marble is a surviving small-scale copy. Evidence of its original bright colors was erased when the British Museum cleaned the Elgin Marbles. Wikipedia

Elgin Marbles / Parthenon Sculptures

The Elgin Marbles were removed by Lord Elgin and are now at the British Museum. Lawrence Alma-Tadema painted Phidias presenting the Panathenaic frieze to his friends. Wikipedia

Summary of Facts

Phidias (c. 480–430 BC) was the preeminent sculptor of ancient Athens, closely associated with Pericles and the Classical period. He is most commonly identified by two chryselephantine (gold and ivory) colossi: the Statue of Zeus at Olympia and the Athena Parthenos in the Parthenon.

The Statue of Zeus at Olympia was commissioned by the Eleans for the Temple of Zeus and ranked among the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. The god was depicted seated and enthroned, holding a scepter. Pausanias reported the statue wore a glass robe and held a figurine of Nike in one hand. Ingres modeled Napoleon's pose in Napoleon I on His Imperial Throne on ancient descriptions of this work. Phidias was apocryphally murdered after completing it, and the statue required repeated application of olive oil due to the marsh conditions in the Altis Grove.

The Athena Parthenos stood about 41 feet tall in the naos of the Parthenon. Her circular shield depicted Phidias himself (as a bald old man hurling a stone) alongside Pericles, as well as a full Amazonomachy scene. A sacred coiled snake resided between the shield and her feet. Her triple-crested helmet featured a central sphinx flanked by two pegasi. The Varvakeion marble is a surviving small-scale copy. Pericles' political enemies accused Phidias of embezzling gold intended for the statue. When the British Museum 'cleaned' the Elgin Marbles, it erased evidence of the original bright coloring.

Phidias also supervised the broader sculptural program of the Parthenon—including the Panathenaic frieze, pediment groups, and metopes. Lawrence Alma-Tadema depicted him presenting the Panathenaic frieze to his friends. He created the Athena Promachos, a giant bronze Athena on the Acropolis, and a statue of Apollo Parnopios to commemorate the god's driving locusts from Attica. In the contest to sculpt a wounded Amazon for the Temple of Artemis, Phidias came second to Polykleitos. Mark Barr used the Greek letter phi — chosen to honor Phidias's name — to symbolize the golden ratio.
