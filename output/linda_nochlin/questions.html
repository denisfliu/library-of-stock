Tossups

T1 2023 COLONIAL (2023) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
A painting by this artist is analyzed in an essay that describes ten “resolutely alienated” men who watch with “childish, trancelike concentration,” while the audience is given a “beguiling rear view.” A painting by this artist bought by William Randolph Hearst depicts a leader on horseback with his army in the far background and was originally known as Oedipus. This artist’s trips to Egypt inspired a painting of evening prayer in Cairo and (*) Bonaparte Before the Sphinx. This artist’s son-in-law Aimé Morot helped create studies for a painting described as an “insistent, sexually charged mystery” in a Linda Nochlin essay about its “imaginary” subject matter. The Clark Art Institute holds that painting by this artist, as well as another in which a woman’s teeth are examined in a (*) slave market. In a painting by this artist, armed men sit against a blue-tiled wall and watch a nude boy with a reptile wrapped around his body. That painting was used for the front cover of Edward Said’s Orientalism. For 10 points, name this French academic painter of The Snake Charmer.
ANSWER: Jean-Léon Gérôme (the Linda Nochlin essay is “The Imaginary Orient”)
T2 2019 The Unanswered Question (2019) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
In one essay, this author points out that many great artists throughout history had fathers who were artists. In another essay, this thinker discusses the painting The Meeting as “A Portrait of the Artist as a Wandering Jew.” This author used the example of Cimabue [“chee-ma-BOO-ey”] discovering Giotto as a shepherd boy to illustrate one concept. This author of the book Courbet wrote about the misleadingly realistic style of The Snake Charmer in “The Imaginary Orient.” She wrote that the title (*) phrase of her most famous essay is rooted in the belief that great artists have an inherent genius when, in actuality, making art is determined by social, cultural, and psychological factors. That 1971 essay concludes by considering the career of the painter of The Horse Fair, Rosa Bonheur. Often called the first feminist art historian, for 10 points, name this author of “Why Have There Been No Great Women Artists?”
ANSWER: Linda Nochlin

ANSWER: Linda Nochlin

This artist also painted The Tribuna of the Uffizi.
ANSWER: Johann Zoffany
[10] Nochlin's essay extensively discussed this artist's career to show that institutional support is necessary for achievement in art. The police granted this artist permission to crossdress in order to paint The Horse Fair.

B5 2017 Chicago Open (2017) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
Daniel Fink advanced an early form of the theory created by these two men by identifying 10 points, such as “halation of highlights” and “precise treatment of reflections.” For 10 points each:
[10] Name this art historian and physicist, who collaborated on a 2001 book that argued realism in western art was achieved with the aid of the camera obscura and lenses.
ANSWER: David Hockney and Charles M. Falco [accept Hockney-Falco thesis; accept the names in either order]
[10] The Hockney-Falco thesis claims that this Dutch artist created many of his paintings, such as Allegory of Painting, with the aid of visual tools.
ANSWER: Johannes Vermeer [or Jan Vermeer or Johan Vermeer]
[10] This thinker argued against the Hockney-Falco thesis by pointing out the realistic texture of wedding gowns and cloth in general. This academic also wrote about the “death of the heroic landscape” in Bathers, Bodies, Beauty.
ANSWER: Linda Nochlin [or Linda Weinberg]
