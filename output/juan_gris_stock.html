Spanish Cubist painter. Most commonly identified by his Portrait of Picasso and Still Life with Checkered Tablecloth. Third major Cubist after Picasso and Braque. Pioneer of Synthetic and Crystal Cubism. Died at 40 of renal failure. Also known for The Sunblind, Newspaper and Fruit Dish.

Summary of Facts

Juan Gris was a Spanish Cubist painter, the third major Cubist alongside Picasso and Braque. His Portrait of Picasso (1912) shows Picasso holding a palette in front of a brown background dominated by diagonal lines, painted at the end of the Analytic Cubism period (a term Gris himself coined). His Still Life with Checkered Tablecloth punningly turns wine into water by showing only 'E-A-U' on a bottle of Beaujolais; its objects form the shape of a bull.

Gris was a major exponent of Crystal Cubism and pioneered Synthetic Cubism around 1913. He called painting 'a sort of flat, coloured architecture' in a Sorbonne lecture. He often used newspapers in his still lifes, with Le Journal appearing frequently. Other works include The Sunblind, Fantomas, The Mountain Le Canigou, The Painter's Window, and Newspaper and Fruit Dish. He designed sets for Diaghilev's Ballets Russes. Modigliani painted a portrait of him. He died at age 40 of renal failure in 1927.
