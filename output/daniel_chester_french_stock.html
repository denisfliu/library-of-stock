Lincoln Memorial Statue (1920)

Massive seated statue of Abraham Lincoln in a Henry Bacon-designed presidential memorial on the National Mall. Carved by the Piccirilli brothers from marble. Lincoln sits with one hand in a fist and the other open, on a chair decorated with fasces. Located in a Greek Doric temple with 36 columns (representing the 36 states at the time of Lincoln's death), flanked by the Gettysburg Address and his second inaugural address. South Hall contains Jules Guerin murals. Wikipedia

The Minute Man (1874)

Summary of Facts

Daniel Chester French was an American sculptor best known for the massive seated statue of Abraham Lincoln at the Lincoln Memorial in Washington, D.C. The statue was carved by the Piccirilli brothers from marble and is located in a Henry Bacon-designed Greek Doric temple with 36 columns (representing the 36 states at the time of Lincoln's death) on the National Mall next to the Reflecting Pool. Lincoln sits with one hand in a fist and the other open, on a chair decorated with fasces. The memorial's walls are inscribed with the Gettysburg Address and Lincoln's second inaugural address, and the South Hall contains Jules Guerin murals depicting Justice with golden wings. An urban legend holds that Lincoln's hands spell his initials in American Sign Language, inspired by French's earlier sculpture of Alice Cogswell at Gallaudet University.

French's career began when, as an unknown 23-year-old vouched for by Louisa May Alcott's sister, he received the commission to create The Minute Man at Old North Bridge in Concord, Massachusetts, commemorating the Battles of Lexington and Concord. The bronze sculpture depicts a man with rolled-up sleeves who has discarded his coat and plow to take up arms, carrying a gun in one hand and holding a plow in the other, in a pose resembling the Apollo Belvedere. It was made from ten melted cannons donated by Congress.

His Alma Mater at Columbia University is a seated Athena with outstretched arms, with an owl hidden in the folds of her robes, located on the steps leading to Low Memorial Library. He created the 65-foot-tall Statue of the Republic for the 1893 World's Columbian Exposition in Chicago, with a later version placed in Jackson Park. He allegorized the continents, depicting Africa as a woman between a lion and a sphinx. A badger and cluster of grapes adorn the helmet of his statue atop the Wisconsin State Capitol, for which Audrey Munson ('America's First Supermodel') modeled.

French collaborated extensively with architect Henry Bacon on the Lincoln Memorial, a Lafayette sculpture in Prospect Park, and the Dupont Circle Fountain (which contains allegorical representations of the sea and the stars, honoring Samuel Francis Dupont). He collaborated with sculptor Edward Clark Potter on the gold leaf-covered quadriga Progress of the State at the Minnesota State Capitol. Potter trained in French's studio and later created the Patience and Fortitude lions at the New York Public Library. French also created a medallion depicting Benjamin Franklin on the obverse and a printer on the reverse for the Pulitzer Committee, produced The Sons of God Saw the Daughters of Men That They Were Fair (a surprisingly erotic marble group), a bronze relief monument to the Milmore brothers, and a sculpture in San Francisco's Golden Gate Park. He was a neighbor of Gertrude Vanderbilt Whitney when she created the Titanic memorial.
