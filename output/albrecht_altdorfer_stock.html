Summary of Facts

Albrecht Altdorfer was a German Renaissance painter and leader of the Danube School. His most famous work is The Battle of Alexander at Issus (1529), a massive battle scene commissioned by Duke William IV of Bavaria. It features a giant floating tablet inscription, the sun setting over the Nile, and a crescent moon, in the 'world landscape' style pioneered by Joachim Patinir. The Persians are anachronistically dressed in red turbans.

His Landscape with Footbridge (1520) is considered one of the earliest pure landscapes in Western oil painting. St. George Overcoming the Dragon shows the saint and dragon dwarfed by a gigantic forest.

Altdorfer was a pupil of Lucas Cranach the Elder. He painted scenes for St. Florian Abbey, his Rest on the Flight into Egypt, and an unusual Nativity of the Virgin set inside a medieval cathedral. His engravings influenced the Little Masters. He collaborated with Durer on the monumental woodcut Triumphal Arch and celebrated Regensburg's expulsion of the Jews in 1519.
