T1 2025 LITANY (2025) Diff 8 · Literature
A poem titled for this author that laments, “Make one song and Heaven takes it,” opens and closes with the line “Ah, how poets sing and die!” That Anne Spencer poem memorializes this author, who created a metaphor extended in another poet’s book Shaker, Why Don’t You Sing? with the image of a creature who “stands on the grave of dreams.” William Deans Howells popularized this poet’s (*) “minors” in a review of his book Majors and Minors.
