Summary of Facts

Li Bai (701-762, also known as Li Po) was a Tang dynasty Chinese poet, one of the greatest in Chinese literary history. He is often paired with his friend and counterpart Du Fu. Known as the 'Banished Immortal,' he was associated with wine, moonlight, and nature.

'Drinking Alone by Moonlight' features the speaker drinking alone, inviting the moon and his shadow as companions to make a party of three. 'Quiet Night Thought' begins 'Bright moonlight before My Bed' and ends with the speaker bowing his head thinking of home. 'The River-Merchant's Wife: A Letter' was famously translated by Ezra Pound.

Legend holds that he drowned trying to embrace the moon's reflection in water. He served briefly at the court of Emperor Xuanzong before being exiled.
