Tossups

T1 2025 MRNA (2025) Diff 7 · Literature
This author wrote, “Love me when I am dead / And do not let me die,” about a narrator suffering from AIDS in a work used for a Jonathan Dove song cycle. A work by this author of the collection All You Who Sleep Tonight described a man who declares, “The Dow-Jones of my heart is depressed” to a drummer from Liquid Sheep. This author wrote a work divided into 19 rhyming couplets and a verse novel written in (*) iambic tetrameter, borrowing a style developed by a 19th-century poet. A 1986 novel by this author in Onegin stanzas depicts a group of yuppies in San Francisco. In this author’s longest work set in Brahmpur, Kabir doesn’t attend Lata’s marriage to Haresh. For 10 points, name this author of The Golden Gate and A Suitable Boy.
ANSWER: Vikram Seth
T2 2024 Booster Shot (2024) Diff 7 · Literature

An author with this first name included a violent scene at a Ramlila procession in a novel in which Pran and Savita undergo an arranged marriage. In a novel by an author with this first name, Haresh is chosen over Kabir as the right husband for Lata by Rupa Mehra. For 10 points, give this first name of the Indian author who wrote The Golden Gate and A Suitable Boy.
ANSWER: Vikram [or Vikram Seth; or Vikram Chandra]

Cuddles, a lapdog who enjoys biting characters in this novel, is based on a real dog named Cuddles belonging to a friend of the author. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this novel that ends with Haresh marrying Lata in a wedding that Kabir decides not to attend.
ANSWER: A Suitable Boy
[10] This Indian novelist wrote the 1,400-page novel A Suitable Boy. This author later returned his advance payment to Penguin after missing several deadlines for the sequel, A Suitable Girl.
ANSWER: Vikram Seth (“sayt”, but accept phonetic pronunciations)

B3 2021 Penn Bowl (2021) Diff 8 · Literature

[10] The closely related New Narrative movement focuses on clearly-plotted narrative poems like this poet’s verse-novel The Golden Gate. This Indian-American author also wrote the massive novel A Suitable Boy.
ANSWER: Vikram Seth
[10] Seth’s The Golden Gate is written in Pushkin stanzas, which each consist of this many lines. Edna St. Vincent Millay’s “Euclid alone has looked on Beauty bare” contains this many lines, as it is a sonnet.
