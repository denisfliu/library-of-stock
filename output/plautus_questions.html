Questions: Plautus

7 tossups · 10 bonuses

Tossups

T1 2024 Chicago Open (2024) Diff 10 · Literature
Macilente claims “I will not do as” this author did in the last line of Every Man out of His Humor, while the first lines of Volpone imitate the “acrostic arguments” attached to this author’s works. In an adaptation of this author, a kitchen maid is compared to a “globe” with France on her forehead, Spain on her breath, and Ireland’s bogs in her buttocks. A character inspired by one of this author’s characters is fended off by “Amazon” maids wielding distaffs, pokers, and brooms after Merrygreeke goads him into (*) attacking the widow Dame Custance’s house. One of this author’s title characters inspired the title man of Nicholas Udall’s play Ralph Roister Doister. An adaptation of one of this author’s plays is set in Ephesus and complicates the plot by adding two servants named Dromio. For 10 points, what author of Miles Gloriosus inspired The Comedy of Errors with his play Menaechmi?
ANSWER: Plautus [or Titus Maccius Plautus]
T2 2023 MRNA (2023) Diff 7 · Literature
In a play by this author, a prostitute borrows a baby to convince a returning soldier that he had impregnated her. Completeness of this author’s plays varies by letter of the alphabet because the monk who produced the Ambrosian palimpsest got tired of scrubbing midway through. This author used a comparatively low proportion of iambic verse, meaning more of this author’s dialogue was meant to be sung than that of a later contemporary. In a play by this author, the (*) slave Palaestrio tricks his master, who is introduced with several servants carrying his comically large shield. A play by this author about two separated twins in Epidamnus was adapted by Shakespeare for The Comedy of Errors. For 10 points, Miles Gloriosus (MEE-lez glory-OH-sus) and The Menaechmi are by which Roman playwright?
ANSWER: Plautus [or Titus Maccius Plautus] (The first play is Truculentus.)
T3 2023 BHSU (2023) Diff 9 · Literature
A maxim from this author about how men deal with strangers is the oldest extant source for the Latin saying “homo homini lupus.” A work by this author is the only extant instance of Latin literature preserving text in Carthaginian Punic. This author often compounded words to create neologisms like dentifrangibula (DEN-tee-fran-GEE-boo-lah) for “fists” and Suavisaviatio (SWA-vis-ah-vee-AH-tee-oh) for a love god. This author’s corpus preserves the most uses of unfamiliar (*) Latin forms like the exclamations ecastor (ay-CAST-or) and hercle (HAIR-clay). This author invented character names with Greek etymologies like “Conqueror of Many Towers” for a work based on the alazon (uh-LOZ-doan) trope. This archaic writer gave characters names like “Peniculus” and “Pyrgopolynices” (PEER-go-pol-ee-“NICE”-eez). For 10 points, name this Roman comic playwright who authored the Menaechmi and The Braggart Soldier.
ANSWER: Titus Maccius Plautus (The play from the second sentence is Poenulus.)
T4 2013 Western Invitational Tournament (2013) Diff 8 · Literature
In one play by this author, Gripus questions the ability of a play to affect change in its viewers and Daemones regains control of his daughter from her abductor Labrax. The first reference to a “tragicomedy” occurs in one play by this man, in which Alcumena gives birth to Hercules after Zeus seduces her as the title character. This author of Amphitryon and The Rope wrote about a slave’s rescue of Calidorus’s lover from the pimp Ballio. Pleusicles rescues his wife and his slave from (*) Pyrgopolynices, a “braggart soldier.”In his most famous play, Phaedria and Lyconides receive the title object as dowry from the miserly Euclio after it is stolen by a greedy slave. For 10 points, name this Roman playwright of Pseudolus, Miles Gloriosus, and The Pot of Gold.
ANSWER: Titus Maccius Plautus
T5 2013 Schindler's Lit (2013) Diff 10 · Literature
In one of this author’s plays, a servant caught peeking in roofs through a skylight claims to be searching for a monkey, though in reality he has spotted Philocomasium. That play’s title character has a ($) shield so big multiple servants must carry it, and is followed by the flatterer Artorogus, and is the arrogant soldier Pyrgopolynices. One of this author’s characters is mistakenly given his (*) brother’s wife’s mantle by the prostitute Erotium, who cooked dinner for that brother and Peniculus. In that work, Sosicles changes his name after his twin brother is kidnapped by a man from Epidamnus. For ten points, identify this Roman playwright of Miles Glorious, who inspired The Comedy of Errors with his play The Menaechmi.
ANSWER: Titus Maccius Plautus
T6 2012 Penn-ance (2012) Diff 8 · Literature
This author created a man who says that he doesn’t like how two characters are “soldering” their lips together, in reference to a man disguised as a sailor and that man’s lover who had earlier been kidnapped from him. Another of this author’s works includes a scene in which the chef Congrio is complaining about being beaten and then sees the main character holding a cudgel; that act also includes a discussion between (*) Megadorus and the protagonist about a planned marriage for the protagonist’s daughter, Phaedria. This man wrote a play in which Pleusicles is reunited with his lover, Philocomasium, thanks to the trickery of Palaestrio. This author of a play about Pyrgopolynices also wrote about how the title object of another play was found in the house of the miserly Euclio. For 10 points, name this Roman playwright of Miles Gloriosus and The Pot of Gold.
ANSWER: Titus Maccius Plautus
T7 2012 Michigan KABO (2012) Diff 8 · Literature
Eduard Fraenkel analyzed the works of this playwright by noting his use of comparative idioms, claiming that many speeches that he wrote compare mortals favorably to deities with no mythological basis. In one of this man’s plays, the slave Palaestrio invents the character of Honoria as part of a plan to trick Pyrgopolynices into returning Palaestrio to his former master. The title character of one of this man’s plays hires someone to impersonate the messenger, Harpax, as part of his plot to rescue (*) Phoenicium for his master, Calidorus. The creator of an egotistical soldier in Miles Gloriosus, he wrote about the bestowing of a mantle to the prostitute Erotium as part of the confusion in a play about twins that inspired A Comedy of Errors. For 10 points, name this Roman author of Pseudolus and Menaechmi.
ANSWER: Titus Maccius Plautus
