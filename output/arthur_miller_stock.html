Arthur Miller

← Home · View source questions · Make cards
American playwright known for Death of a Salesman, The Crucible, A View from the Bridge, All My Sons, and After the Fall. His failed marriage to Marilyn Monroe inspired After the Fall. His plays explore themes of moral responsibility, the American Dream, and McCarthyism.
Death of a Salesman

A play about the decline of Willy Loman, a traveling salesman who kills himself so his son Biff can use the life insurance money. Biff was a former high school football star who dropped out of school after discovering his father's infidelity with a mistress in a Boston hotel room. Biff steals Bill Oliver's fountain pen after Oliver fails to remember him. Willy exclaims 'You can't eat the orange and throw the peel away - a man is not a piece of fruit!' when his boss Howard Wagner denies him a desk job. Willy ignores the studious Bernard to spend time with Biff playing football. Willy's brother Ben struck it rich in Alaska. A character describes life as a 'casting off' while her husband insists 'some people achieve something.' His son Happy also appears. Jo Mielziner's set design incorporated semi-transparent walls for interior and exterior scenes.

A View from the Bridge

Set in Red Hook, Brooklyn, and narrated by the Italian lawyer Alfieri. The longshoreman Eddie Carbone takes in his wife Beatrice's Italian cousins Marco and Rodolpho. Eddie becomes obsessively protective of his niece Catherine and objects to her relationship with Rodolpho. The first act ends when Marco lifts a chair over Eddie's head with one hand after Eddie hits Rodolpho in a mock boxing match. Eddie reports Marco and Rodolpho to immigration services, and Marco kills Eddie in a knife fight. Eddie suggests oranges are painted orange because they grow green on the tree. Beatrice tells Eddie he can 'never have' Catherine. Alfieri advises characters to 'settle for half.' The Brooklyn Bridge dominates the backdrop of the set design.

The Crucible

A play about the Salem Witch Trials. John Proctor is described in the narration as 'a farmer in his middle thirties' troubled by the hypocrisy of his Puritan town. Proctor is hanged after declaring 'I have given you my soul! Leave me my name.' George Burroughs was imprisoned for debt by Thomas Putnam's brother-in-law. The narrations describe a group with 'a predilection for minding other people's business' and note 'We cannot look to superstition in this.' A pregnant woman is given a 'poppet' stuck with a needle. Miller called for artificial large trees to create a gloomy atmosphere. The play is often read as an allegory for McCarthyism.

All My Sons

A play about Joe Keller, who blames his partner Steve Deever for shipping faulty cylinder heads that caused the deaths of twenty-one pilots. Joe claimed he had pneumonia the day the factory produced the parts. Joe's son Larry dies in the war; a letter he sends before dying reveals he suspected his father knew about the cylinder heads and purposely crashed his plane. A tree planted to mark Larry's death blows down. Anne Deever gives Kate the letter revealing Larry's suicide. Joe blasts his brains out at the end. Jim Bayliss is the neighbor who gave up medical research to earn money as a doctor.

After the Fall

A play that takes place in the mind of Quentin, who interacts with his former wives. The set consists of a single chair in front of the stone tower of a German concentration camp. Quentin visits a concentration camp with a German archaeologist. Lou commits suicide after Mickey 'names names.' Maggie, a fictionalization of Marilyn Monroe, is portrayed as neurotic and suicidal. The play was inspired by Miller's failed marriage to Monroe, for whom he also wrote the screenplay for The Misfits.

Other Plays

Miller wrote several other plays. Incident at Vichy is set in 1940s France where the psychoanalyst Leduc rallies prisoners; a white pass allows Leduc to escape the Nazi regime. Resurrection Blues features a dictator forcing Skip L. Cheeseboro to make a TV special about a crucifixion. Broken Glass features a character who becomes paralyzed from the waist down after reading about Kristallnacht. The Price involves Victor and Walter Franz debating over furniture from their dead father. The Man Who Had All the Luck involves David saving sick mink and Amos's failed baseball career.

Summary of Facts

Arthur Miller was one of America's most important playwrights, known for exploring moral responsibility, the American Dream, and McCarthyism. His plays remain central to quizbowl literature.

Death of a Salesman depicts the decline of the traveling salesman Willy Loman, who kills himself so his son Biff can use the insurance money to start a business. Biff was a former high school football star who dropped out after discovering his father's infidelity in a Boston hotel. Biff steals Bill Oliver's fountain pen. Willy complains 'You can't eat the orange and throw the peel away.' Jo Mielziner's set design used semi-transparent walls.

A View from the Bridge, set in Red Hook and narrated by Alfieri, follows the longshoreman Eddie Carbone, who takes in Italian immigrants Marco and Rodolpho. Eddie becomes obsessed with his niece Catherine. The first act ends with Marco lifting a chair over Eddie's head with one hand. Eddie reports Marco and Rodolpho to immigration and is killed by Marco in a knife fight.

The Crucible dramatizes the Salem Witch Trials, with John Proctor declaring 'I have given you my soul! Leave me my name' before being hanged. The play's narrations provide historical context and are a distinctive formal feature. All My Sons reveals that Joe Keller shipped faulty cylinder heads that killed twenty-one pilots while blaming his partner Steve Deever. Joe's son Larry's letter reveals he knew the truth and crashed his plane.

After the Fall takes place in Quentin's mind, with a set consisting of a chair before a concentration camp tower. The character Maggie is a fictionalization of Marilyn Monroe, inspired by Miller's marriage to her. Miller also wrote Incident at Vichy (about prisoners in Nazi France), Resurrection Blues, Broken Glass, and The Price. He wrote the screenplay for The Misfits starring Monroe.
