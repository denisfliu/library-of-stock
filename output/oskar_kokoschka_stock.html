Summary of Facts

Oskar Kokoschka was an Austrian Expressionist painter and playwright. His most famous painting, The Bride of the Wind (also called The Tempest, 1914), shows him lying wide awake with dark waves swirling around him as sleeping Alma Mahler rests in his arms. Their tempestuous affair produced many works and incidents: he commissioned a life-size sex doll of Alma, which he drunkenly decapitated at a party. He kept a blood-stained cotton pad from Alma's abortion until his death. When Alma was 80, he proposed making a wooden puppet of himself with an erect penis. A series of seven Chinese fans documented their Italian journey, the last thrown into fire in jealousy.

His Self-Portrait as Knight Errant features a sphinx in a stormy landscape, inspired by Alma's abortion. The Dreaming Boys is a lithograph series of sexual awakening. He painted Queen Victoria holding a frog and riding a shark while British sailors drown. He was a student of Klimt and painted portraits for architect Adolf Loos's clients. He was a Vienna Secession member whose art was deemed 'degenerate.'

As a playwright, Kokoschka wrote Murderer, Hope of Women (in which 'The Man' kills 'The Woman' with a single touch) and Hiob/Job (adapted from Sphinx and Strawman). His play about Orpheus and Eurydice was converted into an opera by Ernst Krenek.
