Summary of Facts

Max Ernst (1891-1976) was a German-born Surrealist painter and sculptor. His most frequently clued work is Two Children Are Threatened by a Nightingale (1924), a painting/collage hybrid where an open gate and a knob protrude from the frame, a girl holds a knife at a bird, and a man reaches for a knob on a roof. He called it 'a kind of farewell' to his collage work.

He created collage novels by cutting up Victorian pulp fiction: A Week of Kindness (Une Semaine de Bonté) is split into seven sections using Easter Island moai and lion heads, and La Femme 100 Têtes features his alter ego Loplop, 'Superior of the Birds.' He invented frottage (rubbing pencil on uneven surfaces) and grattage (scratching paint off canvas). Joan Miró helped him develop grattage.

Other works include The Elephant Celebes, Ubu Imperator (with a main figure modeled on a Konkomba corn bin), Fireside Angel (monster forms a swastika), The Petrified City, and Murdering Airplane (collage with airplane with human arms). He married Dorothea Tanning and earlier had a ménage à trois with Gala and Paul Éluard. He collected Kachina dolls while living in Arizona.
