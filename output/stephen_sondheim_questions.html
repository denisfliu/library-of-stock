Questions: Stephen Sondheim

5 tossups · 6 bonuses

Tossups

T1 2025 Absolute Cinema (2025) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
This writer declares Vertigo to be the “most overrated movie ever” in one of the “late conversations” included in Finale, a 2022 book on this writer by D. T. Max. At the time of his death, this writer was working with David Ives on Here We Are, a work based on two Luis Buñuel (“boon-yoo-EL”) films. Anthony Perkins and this writer co-wrote the whodunit movie The Last of Sheila. John Mulaney parodied this writer in the Documentary Now! episode “Co-Op,” which spoofs D. A. Pennebaker’s film about one of his works, (*) Original Cast Album. He’s not a basketball player, but this writer cameoed on a Zoom call with Benoit Blanc (“BEN-wah blonk”) and Angela Lansbury in Glass Onion. This writer won an Oscar for composing Madonna’s song “Sooner or Later” from Dick Tracy. For 10 points, what lyricist of West Side Story wrote “Send in the Clowns” for the Bergman-inspired musical A Little Night Music?
ANSWER: Stephen Sondheim
T2 2023 ARCADIA (2023) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
This writer’s surprise quadruple rhyme of “he goes / she goes / egos / amigos” delighted the despondent writer of “It’s De-Lovely.” This writer rhymed “personable” with “coercin’ a bull” in a work that Marianne Elliott staged in a genderbent revival. The triple homonym “withers wither with her” and the rhyme “ask it / basket” appear in an 1987 “Prologue” by this writer, who scorned his early “purple prose” such as “It’s (*) alarming how charming I feel” in annotations to a volume of his collected works titled Finishing the Hat. “Shepherd’s pie peppered / With actual shepherd on top” is one of many imagined delicacies that this writer listed in “A Little Priest.” This man wrote lyrics to the musical Company and the song “I Feel Pretty” for a collaboration with Leonard Bernstein. For 10 points, name this lyricist of West Side Story and Sweeney Todd.
ANSWER: Stephen Sondheim [or Stephen Joshua Sondheim] (The “despondent writer” was Cole Porter.)
T3 2021 Penn Bowl (2021) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
A work by this composer features a Stravinsky-inspired “madness motif” repeating the eighth-note pattern “D, C, D, C, E-flat, C, F, C.” The quick descending minor third “A-flat, F” is used to repeatedly call the protagonist’s name in a work by this composer featuring the Act II dance “Tick-Tock.” Ravel inspired the many “Night Waltzes” of a work by this composer orchestrated by Jonathan Tunick. This student of Milton Babbitt included many pauses in a melody to fit Glynis Johns’s voice, as described in his book Finishing the (*) Hat. This frequent collaborator of Bernadette Peters worked with Hal Prince until the failure of Merrily We Roll Along. Bobby celebrates his 35th birthday and sings “Being Alive” in this composer’s musical Company. For 10 points, name this Broadway composer who wrote of a “Demon Barber of Fleet Street” in Sweeney Todd.
ANSWER: Stephen Sondheim (The fourth clue refers to “Send in the Clowns.”)
T4 2017 WAO (2017) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
This composer, who is not Mies van der Rohe, wrote that his three artistic principles are “Less is more,” “Content dictates form,” and “God is in the details,” all in service of “CLARITY”. In a failed work by this man, Gussie has an affair with the composer Frank Shepard, whose life since adolescence is shown in reverse chronology. In another of his works, Amy lambasts “a prehistoric ritual where everybody promises fidelity forever” in a rapid patter as she panics on the morning of her (*) wedding. This composer of Merrily We Roll Along, a frequent collaborator with James Lapine, reflected on his stage adaptation of a Victorian penny-dreadful and a 1970 show about the unmarried 35-year-old Bobby in his first annotated lyrics anthology, Finishing the Hat. For 10 points, name this protégé of Oscar Hammerstein, the lyricist and composer of the musicals Company and Sweeney Todd.
ANSWER: Stephen Sondheim
T5 2016 Early Fall Tournament (EFT) (2016) Diff 7 · Fine Arts
A musical by this composer begins with an ominous organ solo that is cut off by the shrill sound of a whistle. In another of this composer’s musicals, the protagonist begs “Somebody, hold me too close” and “Somebody, hurt me too deep” after seeing multiple scenes of his friends’ dysfunctional married lives on his thirty-fifth birthday party. In one of his songs, Desirée moans “Isn’t it (*) rich? Are we a pair? Me here at last on the ground and you in midair.” This composer wrote a musical in which the baker Mrs. Lovett wonders whether priests or poets would taste better in her cannibalistic pies in a conversation with the title serial killing barber. He included “Send in the Clowns” in his musical A Little Night Music. For 10 points, name this composer of Company and Sweeney Todd.
ANSWER: Stephen Sondheim [or Stephen Joshua Sondheim]

Bonuses

B1 2025 MRNA (2025) Diff 7 · Fine Arts
The mistress Dot sings “a trickle of sweat, the back of the head” in a number that begins with this character saying “White. A blank page or canvas. The challenge: bring order to the whole.” For 10 points each:
[10] Name this character originally debuted by Mandy Patinkin, who sings numbers like “Move On” and “Finish the Hat” while working on his masterpiece, which is displayed in 1984 with Chromolume #7.
ANSWER: George Seurat [accept either part individually]
[10] Sunday in the Park with George was written by this titan of Broadway. This composer also wrote Sweeney Todd, Into the Woods, Company, and A Little Night Music.
ANSWER: Stephen Sondheim [or Stephen Joshua Sondheim]
[10] A man with bone disease recreates Luncheon of the Boating Party in this non-Sondheim musical. In this musical adapted from a 2001 film, the title character, who was played by Phillipa Soo in its Los Angeles staging, finds a mysterious box on the night of Princess Diana’s death.
ANSWER: Amélie
B2 2024 PLAYTIME! (2024) Diff 7 · Fine Arts
Note to players: Description acceptable. In a musical built on this plot device, the chorus sings about “see[ing] the pretty countryside” and “bursting with dreams” during scene changes. For 10 points each:
[10] Name or describe this plot structure used to follow the disintegrating friendship of Mary Flynn, Charley Kringas, and Franklin Shepard in a 1981 musical.
ANSWER: reverse chronology [accept descriptions of events that happen backwards in time; prompt on descriptions of events being presented out of order or non-chronologically]
[10] Merrily We Roll Along has been described as this composer’s “biggest flop.” This composer of Sweeney Todd and Into the Woods started work on another reverse chronology musical, All Together Now, before his 2021 death.
ANSWER: Stephen Sondheim [or Stephen Joshua Sondheim]
[10] In the 2023 revival of Merrily We Roll Along, this actor starred as Frank alongside Daniel Radcliffe and Lindsay Mendez. This actor replaced Brian d’Arcy James as King George for the Broadway run of Hamilton.
ANSWER: Jonathan Groff [or Jonathan Drew Groff]
B3 2024 NASAT (2024) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
This actress won a Tony Award for her role as Emma in Andrew Lloyd Webber's Song and Dance. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this actress who succeeded Catherine Zeta-Jones in the role of Desiree Armfeldt in the Broadway revival of A Little Night Music. She frequently collaborated with the composer of Follies and Into the Woods.
ANSWER: Bernadette Peters [or Bernadette Lazzara]
[10] Peters often starred in musicals by this composer, who wrote the musicals Sweeney Todd and Company.
ANSWER: Stephen Sondheim [Stephen Joshua Sondheim]
[10] Peters was nominated for a Tony for starring as Dot and Marie in this Sondheim musical, which follows a French pointillist painter in the months leading up to his most famous work.
ANSWER: Sunday in the Park with George
B4 2022 Chicago Open (2022) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
In a musical, a group of people repeat this character’s name over and over again while saying lines like “your line was busy” and “what have you been up to, kiddo?” For 10 points each:
[10] Name this character who interrupts his friends singing his name at the beginning of a number by saying, “stop! What do you get?” During that song, another character tells him to “want something, want something.”
ANSWER: Bobby [or Robert or Robby]
[10] The musical Company, written by this composer, takes place during Bobby’s 35th birthday. This composer wrote the song “Send In the Clowns” for his 1973 adaptation of an Ingmar Bergman film, A Little Night Music.
ANSWER: Stephen Sondheim [or Stephen Joshua Sondheim]
[10] In another Sondheim musical, Anthony Hope sings “I feel you, and one day, I’ll steal you” and “I was half convinced I’d waken, satisfied enough to dream you” to this character, who Judge Turpin intends to marry.
ANSWER: Johanna {The musical is Sweeney Todd.}
B5 2016 MYSTERIUM (2016) Diff 7 · Fine Arts
This man sings, “There’s a part of you always standing by, Mapping out the sky” in the song “Finishing the Hat.” For 10 points each:
[10] Name this character whose work is criticized in the song “No Life” and who rapidly sings “Red red red red Red red orange Red red orange” in the song “Color and Light.”
ANSWER: Georges Seurat [accept either name]
[10] Seurat appears in Sunday in the Park with George, a musical whose songs were composed by this man. He later wrote the lyrics for West Side Story and the lyrics and score for Sweeney Todd.
ANSWER: Stephen Sondheim
[10] Sondheim also composed and wrote the lyrics for this musical, which mishmashes fairy tales like Jack and the Beanstalk and Little Red Riding Hood. It includes the songs “Agony,” “It Takes Two,” and “No One is Alone.”
ANSWER: Into the Woods
B6 2013 Chicago Open (2013) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
The song “Could I leave you?” is part of a sequence in this musical that reveal the loveless marriage of two main characters, Phyllis Rogers and Ben Stone, whose late song “Loveland” mocks the characters for their lives’ disappointment. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this musical about the 30th reunion of the title vaudeville act, whose late torch song “Losing My Mind” reveals Sally Durant’s lifelong obsession with the aforementioned Ben.
ANSWER: Follies
[10] Follies is a work by this titan of American musical theater, the composer and lyricist of A Funny Thing Happened on the Way to the Forum and Sunday in the Park with George.
ANSWER: Stephen Sondheim
[10] In a song from Follies titled by this three-word sentence, the singer says she’s “been through Herbert and J. Edgar Hoover” and recounts the ups and downs of show business.
ANSWER: “I’m still here”
