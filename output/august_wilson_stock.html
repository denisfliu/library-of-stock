African-American playwright, author of the ten-play Pittsburgh Cycle (Century Cycle) depicting Black life in each decade of the 20th century. Most commonly identified by Fences (Troy Maxson, garbage collector, Rose, Cory, Gabriel's trumpet) and The Piano Lesson (Boy Willie, Berniece, Ghosts of the Yellow Dog, Sutter's ghost). Other plays include Ma Rainey's Black Bottom, Joe Turner's Come and Gone, Two Trains Running, Jitney, Seven Guitars, King Hedley II, Gem of the Ocean, and Radio Golf.
Fences

Wilson's most famous play, set in 1950s Pittsburgh. Troy Maxson is a garbage collector (the first Black garbage truck driver in Pittsburgh) and former baseball player who couldn't break into the MLB due to the color barrier. He prevents his son Cory from playing college football, forcing him to work at A&P. Troy warns Cory he has 'strike two' and is 'living with a full count'; he eventually chases Cory from the house with a baseball bat. Troy has an affair with Alberta, who dies in childbirth; Rose adopts their daughter Raynell. Troy's brother Gabriel has a metal plate in his head from WWII and believes he's an archangel. The play ends at Troy's funeral, where Gabriel fails to blow his broken trumpet—he instead performs an 'atavistic' dance and the 'gates of Heaven stand open as wide as God's closet.' Troy tells Rose he 'planted a seed' but 'the soil was hard and rocky.' Troy refers to death as 'nothing but a fastball on the outside corner.' Troy taunts Death while building the fence: 'I ain't afraid of you!' Bono bets buying his wife a refrigerator on Troy's finishing the fence. Characters sing a song about the dog Blue who 'treed a possum in Noah's Ark.' Rose tells Troy 'I planted myself inside you and waited to bloom.' James Earl Jones premiered as Troy on Broadway. Denzel Washington has pledged to produce films of all the Pittsburgh Cycle plays. The play was adapted into a 2016 film directed by Denzel Washington.

The Piano Lesson

Wilson's 1990 Pulitzer Prize-winning play, set in 1936. Boy Willie wants to sell the family piano (which has the family's history carved into it by the slave carpenter Willie Boy) to buy Sutter's land. His sister Berniece refuses. Sutter's ghost appears and is exorcised when Berniece plays the piano and sings 'I want you to help me' eighteen times, invoking her ancestors. The Ghosts of the Yellow Dog (Boy Charles and four hobos burned alive in a boxcar by a lynch mob) are blamed for mysterious deaths including Sutter falling down a well. Wining Boy sells a silk 'magic suit' that supposedly attracts women to Lymon. Boy Willie and Lymon arrive from Mississippi with a truck full of watermelons. Doaker is the uncle who manages the household. Berniece's daughter Maretha is being taught piano. Reverend Avery wants to marry Berniece. The play's working title was Mill Hand's Lunch Bucket, inspired by a Romare Bearden painting. Song: 'When you marry, marry a railroad man, every day is dollar day.' Mama Ola rubbed the piano until her hands bled. Samuel L. Jackson appeared as Boy Willie in the first live performance; a 2024 adaptation was directed by Malcolm Washington.

Summary of Facts

August Wilson (1945–2005), born Frederick August Kittel Jr., is the most frequently clued African-American playwright in quizbowl. He is best known for his ten-play Pittsburgh Cycle (also called the Century Cycle), which depicts Black life in the Hill District of Pittsburgh across each decade of the twentieth century. His 'four B's' influences were Jorge Luis Borges, Amiri Baraka, Romare Bearden, and the blues. His plays characteristically feature 'spectacle characters'—figures with mental disabilities who provide spiritual or biblical wisdom—and he publicly criticized Robert Brustein in his speech 'The Ground on Which I Stand.'

Fences (set in the 1950s) is by far the most heavily clued play. Troy Maxson is the first Black garbage truck driver in Pittsburgh and a former baseball player who couldn't enter the MLB due to the color barrier. He prevents his son Cory from accepting a football scholarship, forcing him to work at A&P. Troy warns Cory he has 'strike two' and eventually chases him from the house with a baseball bat. Troy has an affair with Alberta, who dies in childbirth; Rose ('I planted myself inside you and waited to bloom') adopts their daughter Raynell. Troy taunts Death while building the fence, calling it 'nothing but a fastball on the outside corner.' Characters sing about the dog Blue who 'treed a possum in Noah's Ark.' Bono bets buying his wife a refrigerator on the fence getting finished. Troy's brother Gabriel has a metal plate in his head from WWII; at Troy's funeral, Gabriel's broken trumpet fails to sound and he instead performs an 'atavistic' dance, opening 'the gates of Heaven as wide as God's closet.' James Earl Jones originated the role. Denzel Washington has pledged to film all the Pittsburgh Cycle plays.

The Piano Lesson (1990 Pulitzer) is set in 1936 and revolves around the conflict between Boy Willie, who wants to sell the family piano to buy Sutter's land, and his sister Berniece, who refuses. The slave carpenter Willie Boy carved the family's history into the piano. Sutter's ghost appears and is exorcised when Berniece plays the piano and sings 'I want you to help me' eighteen times, invoking her ancestors' names. The Ghosts of the Yellow Dog—Boy Charles and four hobos burned alive in a boxcar by a lynch mob—are blamed for mysterious deaths including Sutter falling down a well. Wining Boy (a washed-up musician) sells a silk 'magic suit' to Lymon. Boy Willie and Lymon arrive from Mississippi in a truck of watermelons. Doaker manages the household. Reverend Avery courts Berniece. The play's working title was Mill Hand's Lunch Bucket, inspired by a Romare Bearden painting. Samuel L. Jackson appeared as Boy Willie at the first live performance.

Ma Rainey's Black Bottom is the only Pittsburgh Cycle play not set in Pittsburgh; it takes place in a 1920s Chicago recording studio. Ma Rainey refuses to begin recording without a Coca-Cola (and accuses police of mistreating her). The trumpeter Levee stabs the pianist Toledo for stepping on his expensive Florsheim shoes after Ma Rainey fires Levee for improvising the title song. The stuttering Sylvester mars the intro. The play was adapted into a 2020 Netflix film starring Viola Davis as Ma Rainey and Chadwick Boseman as Levee.

Joe Turner's Come and Gone (set in the 1910s) takes place at Seth Holly's boarding house. Herald Loomis searches for his wife Martha Pentecost after being enslaved by Joe Turner for seven years. He slashes his chest and rubs blood on his face, finding his 'song of self-sufficiency'; Bynum says Loomis is now 'shining like new money.' The conjure man Bynum sings a song about Joe Turner. The 'People Finder' Selig—a sheet metal seller—is hired to find the 'shiny man.' The play's working title was Mill Hand's Lunch Bucket, inspired by Bearden's painting.

Two Trains Running (set in 1969) takes place in Memphis Lee's diner. Risa purposely scars her legs to deflect male attention. Hambone can only say two phrases about ham (he demanded a ham for a job well done); after Hambone's death, Sterling steals a ham to put in his coffin. Jitney (1970s) depicts an unlicensed taxi cab station run by Becker; his son Booster returns from jail (for killing a woman who accused him of rape) and takes over after Becker dies. Youngblood buys a house to surprise his girlfriend Rena.

Seven Guitars (1940s) follows Floyd 'Schoolboy' Barton, who achieves posthumous success. King Hedley (Sr.) kills Floyd while mistaking him for Buddy Bolden. King Hedley II (1980s) features King Hedley II, an ex-convict who sells stolen refrigerators and is killed by his own mother Ruby. Stool Pigeon is a prophetic wise man. Aunt Ester dies at age 366 in this play. Gem of the Ocean (1904) centers on Aunt Ester at 1839 Wylie Avenue (age 285); Citizen Barlow is healed; Solly Two Kings burns down a steel mill and is shot by Caesar Wilks. Radio Golf (2000s) features Harmond Wilks campaigning for mayor and discovering that 1839 Wylie Avenue was illegally acquired. Aunt Ester is the recurring clairvoyant 'washer of souls' who ages from 285 to 366 across the cycle; Stool Pigeon / Canewell is a recurring harmonica player.
