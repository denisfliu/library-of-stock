T1 2025 LITANY (2025) Diff 8 · Literature
The speaker of a sonnet claims to stand within the walls of this place “with not a shred of terror, malice, not a word of jeer” though this place “sinks into my throat her tiger’s tooth” and “feeds me bread of bitterness.” Another speaker asks this place such questions as “When will you be worthy of your million Trotskyites?” That speaker tells this place “I’m putting my (*) “queer shoulder to the wheel” after earlier telling it “I’ve given you all and now I’m nothing.” A third speaker says he is this place at the end of a poem since “They’ll see how beautiful I am and be ashamed” tomorrow when he does not “eat in the kitchen when company comes.” For 10 points, the speaker of that Langston Hughes poem says, “I, too, sing” what place?
ANSWER: America [reject putative synonyms] (the poems are Claude McKay’s “America,” Allan Ginsberg’s “America,” and Langston Hughes’s “I, Too.”)
