Summary of Facts

Tracey Emin is a Young British Artist from Margate, England, best known for provocative autobiographical works. My Bed (1998) is a disheveled, unmade bed installation with cigarette packets, slippers, bloody underwear, condoms, and empty liquor bottles, inspired by four days consuming only alcohol. The Chinese duo Mad for Real (Yuan Chai and Jian Jun Xi) jumped onto it at the Tate Britain.

Everyone I Have Ever Slept With 1963-1995 is a blue tent with names appliqued inside, including Billy Childish. Inspired by the Shell Grotto in Margate, it was destroyed in a 2004 warehouse fire (along with a beach hut co-owned with Sarah Lucas).

She performed Homage to Edvard Munch and All My Dead Children by screaming in the fetal position outside Munch's home. She creates neon text works and I've Got It All, a Polaroid of money spewing from her splayed legs. She creates autobiographical blankets and chose to hang Egon Schiele's works alongside hers at the Leopold Museum.
