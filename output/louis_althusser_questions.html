Questions: Louis Althusser

2 tossups · 3 bonuses

Tossups

T1 2023 BHSU (2023) Diff 9 · Philosophy
This philosopher advanced a simplistic “reflection theory” of perception in a book denouncing the “Machist” tendencies of the tract Empirio-Monism. A lecture titled for this thinker calls for a “new practice of philosophy” based on his claim, drawn from Joseph Dietzgen, that philosophy was “the falsest of all false paths.” That lecture on this thinker titles the collection that includes the essay (*) “Ideology and Ideological State Apparatuses.” This author of Materialism and Empirio-Criticism originated the concept of “monopoly capitalism,” which he claimed extracted “super-profits” from undeveloped economies. The practice of “democratic centralism” was advocated by, for 10 points, what author of Imperialism, the Highest Stage of Capitalism and leader of the Russian Revolution?
ANSWER: Vladimir Ilyich Lenin [or Vladimir Ilyich Ulyanov] (The lecture “Lenin and Philosophy” was given by Louis Althusser.)
T2 2021 ACF Winter (2021) Diff 7 · Philosophy
This book’s “global” procedure enables it to analyze an “endless chain” involving Mr. X, Mr. Y, and Mr. Z, according to a 1970 essay subtitled “Notes Towards an Investigation.” This book’s opening line is referenced in the first thesis of Guy Debord’s The Society of the Spectacle, which describes how life “presents itself as an immense accumulation of spectacles.” Popular companions to this book include a book on “Reading” it by Louis Althusser and a lecture series by David Harvey. This book argues that technological improvements paradoxically tend to decrease the rate of profit. The first volume of this three-volume book discusses the concept of “commodity fetishism” and elucidates how workers create surplus value. For 10 points, name this “Critique of Political Economy” by Karl Marx.
ANSWER: Capital [or Das Kapital; or Capital: A Critique of Political Economy or Das Kapital: Kritik der politischen Ökonomie; accept Reading Capital] (The essay in the first sentence is “Ideology and Ideological State Apparatuses” by Louis Althusser.)

Bonuses

B1 2025 NAFTA (2025) Diff 9 · Philosophy
A 1970 essay claims that people become subjects by inserting themselves into the rituals of these institutions to explain a process that it calls “interpellation.” For 10 points each:
[10] What term from that essay denotes institutions that maintain ruling power nonviolently? Churches, schools, and the family exemplify these institutions, whose “repressive” counterpart exists as a single unified entity.
ANSWER: ideological state apparatuses [or ISAs; or state ideological apparatuses; or appareils idéologiques d'État; prompt on state apparatuses or appareils d’État]
[10] “Ideology and Ideological State Apparatuses” is an essay by this French Marxist, who was institutionalized after killing his wife Hélène Rytmann. This author of For Marx co-wrote Reading Capital with students like Étienne Balibar.
ANSWER: Louis Althusser
[10] The essay posits that no class can hold power without having this control over its ideological state apparatuses. Antonio Gramsci posited the “cultural” form of this concept, which also denotes one state’s dominance over others.
ANSWER: hegemony [or word forms like hegemonic; accept cultural hegemony]
B2 2018 ACF Nationals (2018) Diff 9 · Social Science
In “Ten Theses on Politics,” Jacques Rancière championed “dissensus” over consensus because the latter reduces politics to this institution. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this institution. A 1970 essay subtitled “Notes towards an Investigation” gives the example of a member of this institution “hailing” someone to illustrate how “interpellation” turns an individual into a subject.
ANSWER: the police [or law enforcement; accept answers mentioning the idea of a police force or law enforcement institution; prompt on “state” or “ideological state apparatus”]
[10] The example of the police officer appears in the essay “Ideology and Ideological State Apparatuses” by this French Marxist, who broke with his student Rancière over the 1968 student uprisings.
ANSWER: Louis Althusser [or Louis Pierre Althusser]
[10] Rancière contributed to Althusser’s book on “reading” this unfinished Karl Marx book that presents a “critique of political economy.”
ANSWER: Capital: Critique of Political Economy [or Das Kapital: Kritik der politischen Ökonomie]
B3 2012 College History Bowl (2012) Diff 9 · History
This historian charted the transition from a paternalistic model of the economy to one based on market forces in his 1971 article “The Moral Economy of the English Crowd in the Eighteenth Century.” For 10 points each:
[10] Identify this British socialist historian who sought to rescue British workers “from the enormous condescension of posterity,” in his magnum opus, The Making of the English Working Class.
ANSWER: Edward Palmer Thompson
[10] Thompson’s works include a long essay titled The Poverty of Theory, a criticism of this French strucuturalist Marxist, who is probably best known for his Lenin and Philosophy and for killing his wife.
ANSWER: Louis Althusser
[10] Late in life, Thompson was also active in this cause, which advocated for the decommissioning of certain kinds of weapons of mass destruction.
ANSWER: nuclear disarmament [accept logical equivalents that mention getting rid of nuclear weapons]
