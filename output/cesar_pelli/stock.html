Argentine-American architect (died 2019). Best known for the Petronas Towers in Kuala Lumpur, which were the world's tallest buildings from 1998 to 2004. Dean of the Yale School of Architecture. Known for curtain wall designs, slightly curving tower sides, and postmodern skyscrapers. Other major works include the World Financial Center in New York, Wells Fargo Center in Minneapolis, Pacific Design Center in Los Angeles, and Salesforce Tower in San Francisco.

Summary of Facts

Cesar Pelli was an Argentine-American architect who died in 2019 and is best known for designing the Petronas Towers in Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia. The Petronas Towers feature a cross-section based on the Rub el Hizb (an Islamic eight-pointed star motif, possibly inspired by the Qutb Minar), a double-decker skybridge on the 41st and 42nd floors, and controversial spires that allowed them to claim the title of world's tallest buildings from 1998 until they were surpassed by Taipei 101 in 2004. Pelli served as dean of the Yale School of Architecture, and his firm was known for non-load-bearing curtain wall facades and towers with slightly curving sides. His portfolio spans the globe: the World Financial Center (now Brookfield Place) in New York, whose Winter Garden was damaged during 9/11; the Wells Fargo Center and Minneapolis Central Library in Minneapolis; the three-building Pacific Design Center in Los Angeles (nicknamed the 'Blue Whale,' 'Green Center,' and 'Red' building); the Salesforce Tower in San Francisco (his last work before death); the UniCredit Tower in Milan (tallest building in Italy); the Iberdrola Tower in Bilbao; the Key Tower in Cleveland; the Bank of America Corporate Center in Charlotte; the Cira Centre in Philadelphia; 777 Tower in Los Angeles; Herring Hall at Rice University; the National Museum of Art in Osaka; the Overture Center in Madison; Gran Torre Santiago in Chile (tallest in South America); a tower in Seville with a red frame; and 100 North Main Street in Winston-Salem. He also designed buildings in the planned town of Celebration, Florida for Disney, and the Adrienne Arsht Center for the Performing Arts in Miami.
