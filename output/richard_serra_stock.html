Summary of Facts

Richard Serra is an American minimalist and post-minimalist sculptor best known for massive works in COR-TEN steel. His most famous work is Tilted Arc (1981), a 120-foot-long plate of rusted COR-TEN steel that bisected Federal Plaza in New York City. After being declared disruptive to pedestrians, it was removed in 1989 following a federal lawsuit; Serra argued that 'to remove the work is to destroy it.' The controversy became a landmark free speech case in public art.

His other major work is The Matter of Time (1994–2005), a permanent installation at the Guggenheim Bilbao consisting of curved, rust-brown steel walls forming ellipses, spirals, and other shapes that visitors walk through. The original piece Snake was commissioned for the museum's 1997 opening. Related works include the Torqued Ellipses and Torqued Spirals.

Serra created Clara, Clara, a sculpture shaped like two inverted C's dedicated to his wife, on which the public habitually places footprints. His early-career work One Ton Prop consists of four lead antimony plates arranged into a cube. He built an 80-foot-tall heptagonal sculpture called '7' in Qatar for the Museum of Islamic Art, and was inspired by a Borromini church to create works using computer-modeled 'topological deformations.'

In other media, Serra created the video work Hand Catching Lead (showing his hand repeatedly grabbing at falling lead), collaborated with Nancy Holt on Boomerang (1974, videotaping Holt hearing herself with a one-second delay), and made a video explaining the Prisoner's Dilemma as a game show. He portrayed the Freemason figure Hiram Abiff in the third film of Matthew Barney's Cremaster Cycle.

Serra created a parody of Goya's Saturn Devouring His Son with George W. Bush's head, used in a pleasevote.com ad. William Gaddis satirized him as Mr. Szyrk in the novel A Frolic of His Own. A part of one of his sculptures once broke free and killed a rigger in Minneapolis. David Hammons created works Pissed Off and Shoe Tree using Serra's sculpture T.W.U.
