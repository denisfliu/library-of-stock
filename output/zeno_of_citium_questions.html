Questions: Zeno of Citium

2 tossups · 4 bonuses

Tossups

T1 2018 NASAT (2018) Diff 8 · Philosophy
A commentary by Proclus on a Platonic dialogue titled for a mentor of this philosopher alludes to forty logoi, or arguments, attributed to this philosopher. This philosopher used an argument about a heavy bag of millet to suggest the unreliability of the senses. Simplicius quoted a passage by this philosopher that relies on an antinomy between the existence of finitely and infinitely many things to argue that there is only one thing. This philosopher used a form of argument called epicheirema, and many of his arguments are discussed in Book VI of Aristotle’s Physics. Diogenes of Sinope countered an argument by this student of Parmenides by walking in a circle. A flying arrow and Achilles and the Tortoise figure in two of the paradoxes of, for 10 points, what Greek philosopher of the Eleatic school?
ANSWER: Zeno of Elea [do not accept “Zeno of Citium”]
T2 2017 Oxford Open (2017) Diff 8 · Philosophy
One philosopher of this name described a fourfold concept of comprehension by gradually closing his hand; that concept is katalepsis. The apparent paradox that a thousandth of a grain of millet makes no sound is attributed to a philosopher of this name. Plato’s Parmenides is framed as a dialogue between the title character and a philosopher of this name. That philosopher is attributed with several (*) paradoxes of plurality and four paradoxes which include discussions of the motion of an arrow and of Achilles. For 10 points, give this name shared by ancient Greek philosophers from Elea and Citium.
ANSWER: Zeno (accept Zeno of Elea or Zeno of Citium)

B1 2025 NAFTA (2025) Diff 9 · Philosophy
After Dionysius the Renegade legendarily asked why a philosopher with this first name didn’t correct his mistakes, that philosopher replied, “Because I mistrust you.” For 10 points each:
[10] Give this first name of that philosopher from Citium who founded Stoicism.

ANSWER: Cyrene [accept the Cyrenaics]
[10] Dionysius created a fake one of these events to prank his other former teacher Heraclides Ponticus. A treatise concerning these events describes how they should induce an effect often translated as “purification” or “purgation.”
ANSWER: plays [accept theater or drama or verse drama; accept tragedy or tragedies; accept comedy or comedies; prompt on poetry or poetics by asking “what genre is being discussed?”] (That effect, catharsis, is outlined in Aristotle’s Poetics.)
B2 2021 Chicago Open (2021) Diff 10 · Philosophy
In a paper titled for Peter van Inwage and this term, Ted Sider argued that the existence of objects described by this term would weaken compositional nihilism, the idea that there are no objects with proper parts. For 10 points each:
[10] Identify this term from mereology which refers to any object whose parts have further proper parts, or equivalently, are not made of indivisible atoms.
ANSWER: gunk
[10] This Rutgers philosopher argued that just as things may be made up of more and more “gunk,” things may also be able to be combined into ever larger wholes, or more and more “junk.” This author of “Monism: The Priority of the Whole” also popularized the contrastavist theory of knowledge in “From Contextualism to Contrastivism.”
ANSWER: Jonathan Schaffer
[10] The idea of philosophical gunk can be traced back to this Greek philosopher, who argued that if time consisted of infinitely many instants of zero length, it would be impossible for objects to move.
ANSWER: Zeno of Elea [reject “Zeno of Citium”]
