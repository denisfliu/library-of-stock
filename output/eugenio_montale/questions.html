Questions: Eugenio Montale

3 tossups · 7 bonuses

Tossups

T1 2023 ah's modern poetry packets (2023) Diff 10 · Literature
One of this author’s poems describes “angular / starched consonants” that “braid the tongue at its root” and “foreign cities where the sun's a ghost”. That poem by this author ends by stating, “and once more on our lips we'll taste Greek (+) salt”. Another poem by this author claims that “in silences / things yield and almost betray / their ultimate secrets”, and describes a “little path” that goes through “cane-tufts” and the title (*) plants. This poet wrote, “Laureate poets / seem to wander among plants / no one knows” in a poem from his collection Cuttlefish Bones. For 10 points, name this Italian poet of “The Lemon Trees”.
ANSWER: Eugenio Montale (The first poem is “Salt”)
T2 2023 CLEAN (2023) Diff 8 · Literature
A poet from this country addressed a creature as a “brief rainbow, iris, twin to the glance mounted within your lashes” and “love’s arrow on earth”. An author from this country described how “sinking is sweet in such a sea” in a 15-line sonnet addressed to infinity. A poet from this country contrasted “laureate poets” who “seem to wander” while “even the poor / know that richness” of the title plants. This country was home to the (*) hermeticist writer of the collection Cuttlefish Bones. The Earl of Surrey translated a work from this country that describes “the sun’s rays turned pale with grief” in a collection about a woman he met on Good Friday. An author from this country addressed “you who hear the sound in scattered rhymes” in a collection dedicated to Laura. For 10 points, name this home country of Eugenio Montale and Petrarch.
ANSWER: Italy [accept Italia ] (The first line refers to Eugenio Montale’s “The Eel. “The Infinite” is by Giacomo Leopardi.”)
T3 2019 PIANO (2019) Diff 9 · Literature
A poem by this author describes the Good as “the statue in the somnolence / of noon, and the cloud, and the lofty hawk.” Another poem by this author rebukes a word that “scrutinises / our shapeless soul, and in letters of fire” and ends with the claim “All that we can tell you today is this: / what we aren't, and what we don't wish for.” This poet’s first book includes poems opening “Often have I met the ill of living” and “Don't ask us for the word.” This poet described how “the heart’s ice thaws / and songs pelt / into the breast / and trumpets of gold pour forth / epiphanies of Light” when “one day, through a gate ajar, / among the (*) trees in a courtyard, / we see” the title things. This poet asked “can you not see / that she is your sister?” in “The Eel” and included “Lemon Trees” in the volume Cuttlefish Bones. Salvatore Quasimodo, Giuseppe Ungaretti, and—for 10 points—what other poet founded Hermeticism?
ANSWER: Eugenio Montale

Bonuses

B1 2022 NASAT (2022) Diff 8 · Literature
This poet asked, "Do you know the land where the lemon trees bloom?" in "Mignon," included in a bildungsroman whose protagonist joins the Tower Society. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this author whose time in Italy inspired his Italian Journey, Venetian Epigrams, and Roman Elegies.
ANSWER: Johann Wolfgang von Goethe [The novel is Wilhelm Meister's Apprenticeship.]
[10] This country's Romantic-era poem "Exile Song" responds to Goethe's question with, "My land has palm trees where the thrush sings." Giuseppe Ungaretti taught Italian at this country's São Paulo University.
ANSWER: Brazil [Federative Republic of Brazil; or República Federativa do Brasil]
[10] This poet wrote that "a glimpse of yellow lemons" causes "the golden trumpets of radiance" to "thunder their songs" in "Lemon Trees," the first poem from the collection Cuttlefish Bones. Unlike Ungaretti and Quasimodo, he denied being a member of Italian Hermeticism.
ANSWER: Eugenio Montale

[10] In a book-length treatise, Arshi Pipa compared Eugenio Montale to this earlier Italian. This poet standardized the Tuscan dialect with his La Vita Nuova, which wasinspired by his love for Beatrice.
