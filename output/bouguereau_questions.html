B1 2018 ACF Nationals (2018) Diff 9 · Fine Arts
The American-born salon painter Elizabeth Jane Gardner married this painter and adopted his typical subjects, stating that she’d “rather be known as the best imitator of [this man] than be nobody.” For 10 points each:
[10] Name this French painter, a contemporary of Alexandre Cabanel, whose 1896 work The Wave is exemplary of one of his favored genres, namely, nude women on the seashore.
ANSWER: William-Adolphe Bouguereau
[10] Bouguereau and Cabanel were prominent members of this largely French artistic movement, named for the formal schools where most of its members studied and taught..
ANSWER: academic art [or academism; or academicism; accept word forms of academic or academism or academicism such as academists]
[10] Critics savaged academism by labeling it as this kind of “art.” The use of this French term derived from the fact that certain public safety officials in France wore headgear similar to the Attic helmets shown in academic art.
ANSWER: L’art pompier [prompt on “fireman art”]
B2 2016 Penn Bowl (2016) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
This artist’s painting of Adam and Eve discovering the body of Abel, First Mourning, was painted shortly after the death of his son. For 10 points each:
[10] Identify this Academic painter, who used the same young model in his paintings The Shepherdess and The Bohemian.
ANSWER: William-Adolphe Bouguereau
[10] Bouguereau and Alexandre Cabanel refused to allow the Impressionists to exhibit their works in the Paris Salon of 1863, leading to the establishment of this alternative salon that embraced excluded artists.
ANSWER: Salon des Refusés
[10] Cabanel and Bouguereau both produced paintings on this subject. Traditionally depicting the title goddess standing on a clam shell, as in Botticelli’s version, Cabanel’s version shows her lying on a bed of foam and gazing sleepily at the viewer.
ANSWER: The Birth of Venus [or La Naissance de Vénus]

B4 2014 ACF Nationals (2014) Diff 9 · Fine Arts
This artist depicted a shawled, barefoot girl sitting on a concrete bench with a violin on her lap in a painting titled The Bohemian. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this artist who won the Prix de Rome at the age of 25 with Zenobia Found by Shepherds on the Banks of the Araxes and showed the title goddess in the middle of the ocean surrounded by a crowd of adoring nymphs and centaurs in his 1879 painting The Birth of Venus.
ANSWER: William-Adolphe Bouguereau
[10] Bouguereau was a leading practitioner of this style of art, which favored idealized, allegorical subjects and the polished style taught in formal art training. Other artists of this style include Paul Delaroche and Jean-Leon Gerome.
ANSWER: academic art [or academicism; or academism]
[10] Critics used this derisive term, literally translating “freman art,” for academic painting, whose name refers to the classical Greek helmets that characters always wear in allegories or history paintings.
