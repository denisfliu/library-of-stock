English author known for Brave New World, Point Counter Point, Crome Yellow, and The Doors of Perception. Member of the prominent Huxley family. Also wrote Island, Antic Hay, and Eyeless in Gaza.
Brave New World

Huxley's most famous novel, set in the year 632 After Ford. The 'Resident World Controller' leads a society that uses a drug called soma (named for a Vedic ritual drink) to achieve happiness. Bernard Marx works at the London hatchery. John 'the Savage,' who has only read the complete works of Shakespeare, hangs himself at a deserted lighthouse after Darwin Bonaparte photographs him. A media frenzy leads to a massive substance-induced orgy at the lighthouse. Bernard grows jealous when Lenina Crowne sleeps with other men ('everyone belongs to everyone else'). Inhabitants are sorted into castes alpha through epsilon and watch 'feelies' like 'Three Weeks in a Helicopter.' Island was written as a utopian counterpart.

Point Counter Point

A long novel featuring Philip Quarles (an alter ego for Huxley), Walter and John Bidlake, and many married upper-class characters contemplating affairs. The fascist Everard Webley is murdered by Maurice Spandrell, who was based on Baudelaire. Mark Rampion was based on D.H. Lawrence. The novel opens with Walter Bidlake's mistress complaining about her pregnancy.

Crome Yellow

Huxley's debut novel, in which the shy poet Denis Stone fails to confess his love for the older Anne Wimbush while attending a house party at the title estate. Mr. Barbecue-Smith writes 1,500 publishable words per hour by tapping into his 'subconscious.' The painter Ivor Lombard is frightened away by the psychoanalysis-obsessed Mary Bracegirdle. Priscilla uses astrology to bet on horse races. Denis shuffles around a charity fair and arranges a fake telegram to avoid committing to Anne.

The Doors of Perception and Other Works

The Doors of Perception describes Huxley's mescaline experiments, titled from a line in Blake's The Marriage of Heaven and Hell. It inspired Jim Morrison to name his band 'The Doors.' Island, set in the Kingdom of Pala, is his utopian counterpart to Brave New World, featuring Will Farnaby and a myna bird screaming 'Attention!' Antic Hay satirizes post-WWI London, its title from Christopher Marlowe's Edward II. Eyeless in Gaza features non-chronological chapters about Anthony Beavis, titled from Milton's Samson Agonistes. After Many a Summer Dies the Swan features the immortality-seeking millionaire Jo Stoyte. The Perennial Philosophy is a neo-Vedanta-inspired compilation of religious quotations. His essay 'Wordsworth in the Tropics' argued Wordsworth's pantheism couldn't survive harsh nature. Thomas Henry Huxley ('Darwin's Bulldog') debated Bishop Wilberforce in 1860; Julian Huxley was UNESCO's first Director-General; Elspeth Huxley wrote The Flame Trees of Thika.

Summary of Facts

Aldous Huxley was an English author from the prominent Huxley family (his grandfather Thomas Henry was 'Darwin's Bulldog'; his brother Julian was UNESCO's first Director-General). He is best known in quizbowl for Brave New World and his non-fiction work The Doors of Perception.

Brave New World is set in the year 632 After Ford, depicting a society organized into alpha through epsilon castes, maintained by the happiness drug soma (named for a Vedic drink). Bernard Marx works at the London hatchery. John 'the Savage,' who has only read Shakespeare, hangs himself at a lighthouse after being photographed by Darwin Bonaparte. Island was written as a utopian counterpart, set in the Kingdom of Pala.

Point Counter Point features Huxley's alter ego Philip Quarles, with Maurice Spandrell (based on Baudelaire) murdering the fascist Everard Webley, and Mark Rampion based on D.H. Lawrence. Crome Yellow, his debut, follows the shy poet Denis Stone failing to pursue Anne Wimbush at a country house. Mr. Barbecue-Smith taps his 'subconscious' to write 1,500 words per hour.

The Doors of Perception describes Huxley's mescaline experiments, titled from Blake's Marriage of Heaven and Hell, and inspired Jim Morrison to name his band. Eyeless in Gaza uses non-chronological chapters about Anthony Beavis, titled from Milton's Samson Agonistes. After Many a Summer Dies the Swan features the immortality-seeking millionaire Jo Stoyte. Antic Hay satirizes post-WWI London. The Perennial Philosophy compiles Western and Eastern religious quotations. His essay 'Wordsworth in the Tropics' argued Wordsworth's pantheism couldn't survive hostile nature.
