  Part 1 (ans: adsorption [accept temperature-swing adsorption or pressure-swing adsorption; reject "absorption"]):

    Due to their hydrophobic nature, this class of "glassy" MOFs are useful for carbon capture. Chemical "caryatids" improve the stability of these MOFs, which are topologically isomorphic to zeolites.
  Part 3 (ans: lanthanides [prompt on rare-earth elements]):
    MOFs can catalyze the conversion of CO2 to biogas when coordinated with elements from this series, which are above actinides on the periodic table.
