The Crying of Lot 49

Pynchon's short, accessible novel about Oedipa Maas, who is appointed executor of her ex-lover Pierce Inverarity's will and discovers a secret postal organization called the Trystero (or Tristero), symbolized by a muted post horn. Features the fictional corporation Yoyodyne, the Beatles-wannabe band The Paranoids, the stamp expert Genghis Cohen, and the disillusioned employee Stanley Koteks. Oedipa investigates the 'Greek Way' gay bar and learns about Inamorati Anonymous.

Gravity's Rainbow

Pynchon's encyclopedic masterwork, opening with the famous line 'A screaming comes across the sky.' The title refers to the arc-like trajectory of V-2 rockets. Features the statistician Roger Mexico and the Poisson distribution tracking a lieutenant's sexual encounters. Dedicated to Richard Farina, Pynchon's friend from Cornell who died in a motorcycle crash. Won the 1974 National Book Award. Edward Mendelson wrote 'Gravity's Encyclopedia.' Uses pigs to symbolize the 'preterite' (non-elect of Puritan theology), referencing ancestor William Pynchon's tract.

V.

Pynchon's debut novel, interleaving Herbert Stencil's search for the mysterious title figure V. with Benny Profane's aimless adventures with the Whole Sick Crew in 1950s New York. The Whole Sick Crew hunts alligators in the sewers. Benny calls himself a 'human yo-yo.' A ballerina dies impaled on a pole during a production with robot handmaidens. Kurt Mondaugen tells of witnessing the Herero uprising. Introduces Yoyodyne.

Summary of Facts

Thomas Pynchon is the quintessential postmodernist novelist in quizbowl, appearing across all difficulty levels. The Crying of Lot 49 is his most frequently clued work as a giveaway identifier: Oedipa Maas is appointed executor of Pierce Inverarity's will and uncovers the secret postal conspiracy of the Trystero, symbolized by a muted post horn. The novel features Yoyodyne corporation (inspired by Pynchon's Boeing job), the Beatles-wannabe Paranoids, and stamp expert Genghis Cohen.

Gravity's Rainbow is the most prestigious work, identified by its opening line 'A screaming comes across the sky' and the V-2 rocket trajectory. The statistician Roger Mexico and the Poisson distribution are standard mid-level clues. The novel is dedicated to Richard Farina, Pynchon's Cornell friend who died in a motorcycle crash. V. is identified through Herbert Stencil and Benny Profane's Whole Sick Crew, who hunt alligators in New York sewers.

Pynchon's reclusiveness is a defining biographical trait, most memorably expressed through his Simpsons appearances with a paper bag over his head, making puns like 'The Frying of Latke 49.' Other works that appear include Mason & Dixon (Reverend Cherrycoke, Learned English Dog), Vineland (TV-obsessed Thanatoids), Inherent Vice (Doc Sportello), and the short story 'Entropy.' The entropy theme connects Pynchon to William Gaddis and Donald Barthelme. Against the Day features a quaternion feud. The John Batchelor conspiracy theory claiming Pynchon was secretly J.D. Salinger, to which Pynchon responded 'Not bad. Keep trying,' is a recurring power clue.
