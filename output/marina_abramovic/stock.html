Marina Abramović is a Serbian performance artist widely known as the 'grandmother of performance art.' Clues most frequently reference her 2010 MoMA retrospective The Artist Is Present, in which she sat in silence across from museum visitors for a combined 736 hours, and her 1974 piece Rhythm 0, in which she placed 72 objects (including a loaded gun) on a table and invited the audience to use them on her. Her 2005 series Seven Easy Pieces at the Guggenheim, in which she recreated works by other artists, is also heavily clued. Other notable topics include her collaborative works with partner Ulay, the Spirit Cooking series, and her Rhythm series of solo performances.
The Artist Is Present

Abramović's 2010 MoMA retrospective performance is the single most frequently clued work associated with her and often serves as a giveaway. In this piece, she sat in silence at a table across from individual museum visitors for a combined 736 hours. Clues note that Lady Gaga helped popularize interest in the performance, that visitors lined up to sit opposite her, and that the Tumblr blog 'Marina Abramovic Made Me Cry' features photos by Marco Anelli captioned with the time it took visitors to cry. A famous moment occurred when her former partner and collaborator Ulay made a surprise appearance, leading to an emotional tearful reunion that onlookers applauded. Several commentators compared Shia LaBeouf's performance #IAMSORRY to this work.

Rhythm 0

Abramović's 1974 performance Rhythm 0 is her second most frequently clued work and one of the most iconic pieces in performance art history. In this piece, she stood passively for six hours while presenting the audience with 72 objects (sometimes cited as 76) on a table, including a rose, scissors, a scalpel, cake, paint, and a loaded pistol with one bullet, and invited visitors to do whatever they wanted to her. Clues describe how audience members escalated their behavior: someone cut her neck and drank her blood, her clothes were cut away with razor blades, and most dramatically, an audience member held a loaded gun to her head (or placed her finger on the trigger), which provoked a fight among audience members. The work is described as an examination of the relationship between artist and audience.

Seven Easy Pieces

In 2005, Abramović presented Seven Easy Pieces at the Solomon R. Guggenheim Museum in New York, recreating notable performance art works by other artists as well as two of her own pieces. Clues frequently identify specific works she recreated: Bruce Nauman's Body Pressure (pressing her body against a wall), Vito Acconci's Seedbed, Joseph Beuys's How to Explain Pictures to a Dead Hare, Gina Pane's The Conditioning, and Valie Export's Action Pants: Genital Panic (for which she held a gun, inspired by a poster for the original). She was denied permission to reproduce Chris Burden's Trans-Fixed. She paired Entering the Other Side with pieces by Nauman and Beuys. On the last two nights, she performed Thomas Lips and Entering the Other Side. The series was dedicated to Susan Sontag.

Spirit Cooking

Spirit Cooking is a series of etchings by Abramović that later evolved into a form of dinner party entertainment. The etchings feature recipes like 'golden ball,' supposedly sourced from Tibetan monks, and a book instructs the reader to obtain '13,000 grams of jealousy' to make 'aphrodisiac recipes.' She wrote directions with pig blood ostensibly for use in a kitchen. Clues note that she invited John Podesta to a spirit cooking dinner in an email leaked during the 2016 election, which led Alex Jones to link her and Hillary Clinton to the occult. This became tied to a conspiracy theory concerning Hillary Clinton.

Collaborations with Ulay

Abramović collaborated extensively with her partner Ulay (Frank Uwe Laysiepen) throughout the 1970s and 1980s, forming what they called a collective being known as 'the Other.' Their collaborations include Rest Energy, in which Abramović leaned backwards holding a bow while Ulay held an arrow pointed at her chest for four minutes, and Relation in Time. Their final collaboration, The Lovers (also called The Great Wall Walk), consisted of walking across the Great Wall of China from opposite ends and meeting in the middle to end their relationship. Ulay later sued Abramović over breach of contract in 2015. In another collaborative piece, they stood naked on either side of a doorway while people squeezed past them (Imponderabilia), and in Breathing In / Breathing Out, they spent nineteen minutes breathing into each other's mouths.

Other Rhythm Series Works

Beyond Rhythm 0, Abramović's Rhythm series includes several other numbered performances that appear in clues. In Rhythm 10, her debut piece performed in Edinburgh, she played the knife game (five-finger fillet) with 20 different knives, jabbing at her own fingers. In Rhythm 5, she set fire to a wooden star and lay inside it, losing consciousness during the performance. The Rhythm series is described as a numbered sequence of performances, with its first piece involving five-finger fillet with progressively larger knives.

Other Works

Several other works by Abramović appear occasionally in clues. Balkan Baroque involved scrubbing thousands of bloody cow bones over four days, inspired by a war, and she interviewed a rat-catcher for this piece. Five Stages of Maya Dance is a series of alabaster self-portraits. Cleaning the House was a series of 5-day workshops where participants refrained from eating or speaking. The Cleaner was her first European retrospective, hosted by Denmark's Louisiana Museum of Art in 2017. Her TED talk distinguished performance art from theater by saying 'the blood is the material, not ketchup.'

General / Biographical

Abramović is consistently identified in clues as a Serbian (or Yugoslav-born) performance artist and is frequently called the 'grandmother of performance art.' She is associated with MoMA in New York City, where The Artist Is Present was staged. She is known for her TED talk about performance art as being 'made of trust, vulnerability and connection,' distinguishing it from theater by saying 'the blood is the material.' She called Taiwanese-born artist Tehching Hsieh a 'master.'

Summary of Facts

Marina Abramović is one of the most heavily clued performance artists in quiz bowl, appearing across difficulties 7 through 10. The overwhelming giveaway for identifying her is The Artist Is Present, her 2010 MoMA retrospective in which she sat silently across from visitors for 736 hours; this work appears in nearly every question about her. Rhythm 0, her 1974 piece involving 72 objects on a table and audience participation that escalated to violence, is the second most common identifier and sometimes serves as a leadin or mid-level clue. Seven Easy Pieces, her 2005 Guggenheim series recreating other artists' performances, provides rich power-level material, with clues referencing specific recreated works (Nauman, Acconci, Beuys, Pane, Export) and the denial of permission for Burden's Trans-Fixed. Her collaborations with Ulay yield consistent power clues, especially Rest Energy (bow and arrow) and The Lovers (Great Wall walk). Spirit Cooking provides distinctive power material through the Podesta/Clinton conspiracy angle and the unusual recipe-based etchings. The other Rhythm series entries (5 and 10) offer deep power clues about the knife game and the flaming star. Biographical identifiers like 'Serbian,' 'grandmother of performance art,' and the connection to MoMA round out the giveaway level. At higher difficulties, clues reach for Balkan Baroque, Cleaning the House workshops, Five Stages of Maya Dance, and details about specific Seven Easy Pieces recreations.
