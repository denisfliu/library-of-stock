American novelist of the 1930s known for darkly satirical novels. Best known for Miss Lonelyhearts (about a newspaper advice columnist tormented by his editor Shrike) and The Day of the Locust (about Hollywood disillusionment, centered on painter Tod Hackett and the painting 'The Burning of Los Angeles'). Also wrote The Dream Life of Balso Snell and A Cool Million.
Miss Lonelyhearts

A short novel about a newspaper advice columnist (known only as 'Miss Lonelyhearts') who becomes religiously troubled by the desperate letters he receives from New Yorkers. He is tormented by his cynical editor Shrike, who mocks his religious views. The protagonist has an affair with Mrs. Doyle (Fay Doyle) and retreats to a Connecticut farm with his fiancee Betty, but is ultimately shot by the crippled Peter Doyle. The novel is frequently paired with The Day of the Locust as West's signature works and appears as a giveaway identifier in nearly every tossup.

The Day of the Locust

A novel set in Hollywood about the painter Tod Hackett, who becomes infatuated with the aspiring actress Faye Greener, the girlfriend of the dull Homer Simpson (a bookkeeper). Tod paints 'The Burning of Los Angeles,' a masterpiece that comments on the awfulness of Hollywood. The novel climaxes with Homer Simpson beating up the boy Adore Loomis, causing a riot outside Kahn's Persian Palace Theatre. Features the dwarf Abe Kusich, cockfighting in Homer's garage, and the cowboy Earle Shoop.

The Dream Life of Balso Snell

West's first novel, a surreal satire in which the title character enters the Trojan Horse through its anus and traverses its bowels. Inside, Balso meets Maloney the Areopagite, who is obsessed with Saint Puce, a flea that lived in Christ's armpit. The novel features a boy who tries to act like Raskolnikov to seduce his English teacher and letters from Beagle Darwin about the potential suicide of the hunchback Janey Davenport.

A Cool Million

A satirical parody of Horatio Alger stories in which the protagonist Lemuel Pitkin is literally dismantled piece by piece — losing his scalp, teeth, an eye, part of his hand, and one leg — as he tries to pursue the American Dream. His love interest is Betty Prail, who is raped by Tom Baxter.

Summary of Facts

Nathanael West (born Nathan Weinstein) was an American novelist of the 1930s whose darkly satirical works skewered American illusions. According to the clues, his most frequently referenced works are Miss Lonelyhearts and The Day of the Locust, which appear together as giveaway identifiers in virtually every tossup about him.

Miss Lonelyhearts centers on an unnamed newspaper advice columnist who becomes religiously tormented by the desperate letters he receives. His cynical editor Shrike (described as a 'still more swollen Mussolini of the soul') mocks his spiritual aspirations. The protagonist has an affair with Fay Doyle, retreats to a Connecticut farm with his fiancee Betty where he envisions himself as a rock, and is ultimately shot by the crippled Peter Doyle.

The Day of the Locust is set in Hollywood and follows the painter Tod Hackett, who is infatuated with the aspiring actress Faye Greener and works on his magnum opus, 'The Burning of Los Angeles.' The novel features the dull ex-bookkeeper Homer Simpson, the dwarf Abe Kusich, cockfighting scenes in Homer's garage involving Miguel and the cowboy Earle Shoop, and climaxes with a riot outside Kahn's Persian Palace Theatre after Homer beats up the boy Adore Loomis.

West's first novel, The Dream Life of Balso Snell, is a surreal journey through the bowels of the Trojan Horse, featuring Saint Puce (a flea in Christ's armpit) and Maloney the Areopagite. His third novel, A Cool Million, parodies Horatio Alger stories through the progressive dismemberment of Lemuel Pitkin.

Biographically, West died in a car crash at age 37, the day after the death of his friend F. Scott Fitzgerald. His brother-in-law was the humorist S.J. Perelman, who married West's sister Laura.
