Summary of Facts

W.G. Sebald (1944-2001) was a German author who lived in England and was famous for incorporating photographs into the text of his prose narratives. His works include The Rings of Saturn, Austerlitz, The Emigrants, and Vertigo.

The Rings of Saturn is a walking tour of Suffolk incorporating history, literature, and encounters with figures like the translator Michael Hamburger. Vertigo includes a chapter about Kafka titled 'Dr. K Takes the Waters at Riva.' On the Natural History of Destruction discusses how before-and-after photographs facilitated German evasion of the past about WWII bombing.
