Nigerian author and magical realist, best known for The Famished Road about the abiku (spirit child) Azaro. Won the Booker Prize for The Famished Road, the only Nigerian to win it. Also wrote sequels Songs of Enchantment and Infinite Riches, as well as The Freedom Artist, Starbook, and short story collections Stars of the New Curfew and Incidents at the Shrine. Won the Bad Sex in Fiction Award for The Age of Magic.
The Famished Road (1991)

Okri's best-known novel, winner of the Booker Prize. Follows Azaro, an abiku (spirit child) who clings to earth rather than returning to the spirit world. Azaro's father, a boxer nicknamed 'Black Tyger,' fights a man from the Party of the Rich (which opposes the Party of the Poor). Customers from the spirit world frequent Madame Koto's bar, where she tries to keep Azaro as a good luck charm. Madame Koto joins the Party of the Rich. The novel opens with 'In the beginning there was a river.' A photographer named Jeremiah is repeatedly arrested. Ghosts eat dinner at the house of the police officer responsible for their deaths. Villagers are temporarily blinded by yellow butterflies raining from the sky. A 'beautiful beggar girl with a bad eye' accompanies Azaro's father to a betting shop. Followed by sequels Songs of Enchantment and Infinite Riches. Wikipedia

Summary of Facts

Ben Okri is a Nigerian magical realist author, best known for The Famished Road (1991), which won the Booker Prize and made him the only Nigerian winner of that award. The Famished Road is overwhelmingly his most clued work, appearing in virtually every tossup and bonus about Okri.

The Famished Road follows Azaro, an abiku (spirit child) who clings to earth rather than returning to the spirit world. Azaro's father, a boxer nicknamed 'Black Tyger,' fights opponents from the Party of the Rich, which opposes the Party of the Poor. Madame Koto runs a bar frequented by customers from the spirit world and joins the Party of the Rich, trying to keep Azaro as a good luck charm. The novel opens with 'In the beginning there was a river.' Other details include: ghosts eating dinner at the house of the police officer responsible for their deaths; villagers blinded by yellow butterflies raining from the sky; a 'beautiful beggar girl with a bad eye'; the photographer Jeremiah being repeatedly arrested; and the blessing 'Anyone who thinks evil of you, may this car run them over in their sleep.' The novel was followed by the sequels Songs of Enchantment and Infinite Riches.

Beyond The Famished Road, clues reference several other works. The Freedom Artist (2019) involves Amalantis disappearing after someone paints 'Who is the prisoner?' on a wall, prompting her lover Karnak to search for her. Starbook concerns a prince journeying to find ultimate mysteries. The short story collection Stars of the New Curfew includes 'In the City of Red Dust,' where a governor scrambles fighter planes for his birthday while inhabitants starve and Emokhai sells his blood. Flowers and Shadows, his debut, follows Jeffia Okwe witnessing his corrupt father's downfall. In Arcadia features the TV host Lao on a European train tour, and Dangerous Love features Omovo longing after Ifewiya. Okri also adapted Camus's The Stranger into a play in 2018, accompanied by a short film from 'the Arab's' perspective, and won the Bad Sex in Fiction Award for The Age of Magic.
