Philip Johnson was an American architect who won the first Pritzker Architecture Prize in 1979. Best known for his Glass House in New Canaan, Connecticut, and his collaboration with Ludwig Mies van der Rohe on the Seagram Building in New York, he also designed the AT&T Building (with its Chippendale top), PPG Place in Pittsburgh, the Crystal Cathedral, and the Gates of Europe in Madrid. He defected from Modernism to Postmodernism and had early pro-Nazi views for which he later atoned.
Glass House

The Glass House (1949) in New Canaan, Connecticut was Philip Johnson's personal residence where he lived with partner David Whitney and where he died. Modeled on Mies van der Rohe's Farnsworth House, it has a brick cylinder that conceals the bathroom. Grounds include the 'Da Monsta' red-and-black gatehouse, a Painting Gallery (three circular rooms inspired by Agamemnon's tomb, with rotating 'poster racks' to view 42 artworks), and landscaping modeled on Poussin's The Burial of Phocion.

Summary of Facts

Philip Johnson (1906–2005) was an American architect who became the first recipient of the Pritzker Architecture Prize in 1979. He lived and died in his most famous creation, the Glass House in New Canaan, Connecticut (designed 1949), with his partner David Whitney. The Glass House was based on Mies van der Rohe's Farnsworth House and features a brick cylinder that conceals the bathroom — the only fully enclosed space. The surrounding grounds include the 'Da Monsta' red-and-black visitor's pavilion, a Painting Gallery inspired by Agamemnon's tomb (three circular rooms, each with a rotating 'poster rack' for viewing 42 artworks), a Monument to Lincoln Kirstein, and landscaping modeled on Nicolas Poussin's The Burial of Phocion. A cylindrical sculpture by Donald Judd stands near the house.

Johnson collaborated with Ludwig Mies van der Rohe on the Seagram Building (375 Park Avenue, New York), designing its interiors including the Four Seasons Restaurant. An urban legend says the building scored 3 out of 100 for Energy Star rating. Johnson insisted the blinds could only have three positions.

The AT&T Building (now 550 Madison Avenue) has an open pediment top that earned it the nickname 'Chippendale Building' — it became an iconic statement of Postmodernism. Johnson had defected from the Modernist 'Whites' faction to the Postmodernist 'Grays' led by Robert Venturi and Denise Scott Brown.

PPG Place in Pittsburgh features 231 glass spires. The Gates of Europe in Madrid consists of two buildings that mirror each other and are inclined at 15 degrees. The Crystal Cathedral in California emphasizes natural light. He designed the Kneses Tifereth Israel Synagogue in Port Chester, New York for free as atonement for his pro-Nazi views in the 1930s. He designed the JFK Memorial in Dallas, the Chapel of Thanksgiving in Dallas's Thanks-Giving Square (inspired by a Samarra minaret), and the 1964 New York World's Fair State Pavilion. Robert Venturi praised his Wiley House in Complexity and Contradiction in Architecture but criticized the 'bland architecture' of the Glass House.
