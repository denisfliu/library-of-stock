English Pre-Raphaelite painter (1849–1917) known for literary and mythological subjects. His most clued works are The Lady of Shalott (red-haired woman floating in a boat, inspired by Tennyson), Ophelia (sitting on a tree branch over a lily pond — painted five times), Hylas and the Nymphs (removed from Manchester Art Gallery in 2018), and Echo and Narcissus (at the Walker Art Gallery, Liverpool). Also painted La Belle Dame sans Merci and Gather Ye Rosebuds While Ye May.

Summary of Facts

John William Waterhouse (1849–1917) was an English painter associated with the Pre-Raphaelite Brotherhood, described in quiz questions as 'non-Millais Pre-Raphaelite.' He specialized in literary and mythological subjects — especially characters from Tennyson, Shakespeare, Keats, and Greek myth — painted in a detailed, romantic style.

His most frequently clued work is The Lady of Shalott, of which he made multiple versions. The most famous depicts a red-haired woman in a white dress sitting on a quilt, floating in a boat along the river to Camelot. Another version, titled 'I Am Half-Sick of Shadows' (quoting Tennyson's poem), shows the same woman weaving a tapestry in front of a circular window or mirror reflecting Camelot. The subject, a cursed Arthurian woman from Tennyson's poem, could only view the world through a mirror and died after breaking the curse to look at Sir Lancelot.

Waterhouse painted Ophelia five times, showing the Shakespearean character sitting on a tree branch over a lilypad-covered river — distinctly different from John Everett Millais's version, where Elizabeth Siddal lay face-up in a cold bath (Millais used lamps to keep her warm; she fell ill anyway). One of Waterhouse's Ophelia paintings is also titled 'Gather Flowers While Ye May.'

Hylas and the Nymphs depicts water nymphs abducting Hylas, a companion of Hercules. In early 2018, the painting was controversially removed from and later returned to the Manchester Art Gallery. Echo and Narcissus, held at the Walker Art Gallery in Liverpool, shows Narcissus bending over a pool admiring his reflection while a partly bare-chested, red-headed Echo looks on from the left.

Other notable works include Gather Ye Rosebuds While Ye May (women in pink and blue leaning over), La Belle Dame sans Merci (a maroon-clad woman wrapping a tan scarf around a man she is about to kiss, based on Keats's poem), and Saint Cecilia (the patron saint of music, shown sleeping in a chair while kneeling angels play violin and bowed lute for her).
