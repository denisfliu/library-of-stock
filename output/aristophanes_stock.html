Aristophanes

← Home · View source questions · Make cards
Ancient Greek comic playwright, the master of Old Comedy. Known for The Clouds, Lysistrata, The Frogs, The Birds, The Wasps, Thesmophoriazusae, The Acharnians, Peace, Ecclesiazusae (Assemblywomen), and Plutus. Frequently satirized Athenian politics, Socrates, and Euripides. Works performed at the City Dionysia.

Summary of Facts

Aristophanes was the foremost playwright of Athenian Old Comedy, a genre characterized by obscenity, mythological characters, political satire, and a six-part structure including the parodos, agon, and parabasis (where the chorus directly addresses the audience). He won first place for comedy at the City Dionysia in 426 BC with The Babylonians.

The Clouds satirizes Socrates and intellectualism. Strepsiades, drowning in debt from his son Pheidippides's horse purchases, enrolls at Socrates's school, the Thinkery. Socrates sits in a basket floating in the air to keep his mind from drying out like lettuce and was once defecated on by a lizard while contemplating the moon. A debate between Right Logic (Superior Argument) and Wrong Logic (Inferior Argument) results in the worse argument winning. The play ends when Strepsiades and his slave Xanthias burn down the Thinkery.

Lysistrata follows women who withhold sex from their husbands to end the Peloponnesian War. The women swear an oath on a skin of Thasian wine, vowing not to 'crouch like a lioness on all fours.' Characters include Caloniki and the Spartan Lampito. Lysistrata pours water on a magistrate who scorns her woolworking analogy for society.

The Frogs follows Dionysus traveling to the underworld to bring back Euripides but ultimately choosing Aeschylus instead. Dionysus swaps clothes with his slave Xanthias to avoid being mistaken for Heracles. The chorus of frogs chants 'brekekek koax koax.' A 1974 Yale musical adaptation used the varsity swim team as the frog chorus.

The Birds features Pisthetaerus and Euelpides creating Cloudcuckooland to blockade the gods from human offerings. Prometheus sneaks in under a parasol so Zeus can't see him. Iris is mistreated; Heracles is tricked into a treaty. The geometer Meton is chased away.

The Wasps centers on Philocleon's jury addiction and his son Anticleon's efforts to cure him, culminating in a mock trial where a household dog is accused of stealing cheese. Thesmophoriazusae is set at the Thesmophoria festival where women discuss Euripides's unflattering portrayals; it contains the first documented use of the word 'mimesis.' Peace has Trygaeus riding a giant dung beetle to rescue the goddess Peace. Ecclesiazusae (Assemblywomen) features Praxagora leading women to disguise as men, vote themselves into power, and impose communal property. The Knights pits the sausage-seller Agoracritus against Cleon for the favor of Demos.
