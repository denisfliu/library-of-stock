Summary of Facts

Elena Ferrante is a pseudonymous Italian author best known for the four Neapolitan Novels: My Brilliant Friend, The Story of a New Name, Those Who Leave and Those Who Stay, and The Story of the Lost Child. The series follows the lifelong friendship of Lenu (Elena Greco), the narrator, and her brilliant friend Lila (Raffaella Cerullo) growing up in a poor Naples neighborhood.

As children, the girls throw each other's dolls (Tina and Nu) into the basement of the loan shark Don Achille. Lila experiences 'dissolving margins' where the outlines of things disappear. She designs shoes, marries Stefano (Don Achille's son), and notices at her wedding that Marcello Solara is wearing the shoes she designed. Lila's father once threw her through a glass window for secretly taking the middle school exam. The series ends with Lenu receiving the dolls Tina and Nu in the mail.

Ferrante also wrote The Days of Abandonment about Olga's mental breakdown after her husband leaves. In 2016, Claudio Gatti 'unmasked' Ferrante as possibly being the translator Anita Raja; textual analysis has also pointed to Raja's husband Domenico Starnone. All English translations are by Ann Goldstein.
