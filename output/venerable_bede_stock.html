Summary of Facts

The Venerable Bede is an 8th-century Northumbrian monk at Wearmouth-Jarrow, called 'the father of English history.' His Ecclesiastical History of the English People chronicles Christianity's spread in Britain and is the source for Gregory the Great's 'not Angles, but Angels' pun, the Synod of Whitby's Easter controversy, and Caedmon's story. His On the Reckoning of Time deals with computus, and his writings popularized BC/AD dating. He was first to correlate tides to the moon. He died on Ascension Day 735 after finishing his translation of the Gospel of John, asking for 'pepper, napkins, and incense.' He is the only British native made a Doctor of the Church.
