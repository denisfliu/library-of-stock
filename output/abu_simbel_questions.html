Answer the following about Jean-François Champollion’s (“zhawn frawn-SWAH shawm-poll-YONES”) decipherment of Egyptian hieroglyphs, for 10 points each.
[10] Champollion built upon the work of this polymath, who correctly induced that Demotic script -- which he called “enchorial” -- included both phonetic and ideographic signs, but failed to recognize that hieroglyphic script functioned the same way. He is the subject of the biography The Last Man Who Knew Everything.
ANSWER: Thomas Young
[10] Champollion made the key breakthrough in deciphering hieroglyphs while reviewing texts from this site, which was relocated in its entirety during the construction of the Aswan High Dam. This site features two enormous rock‑cut temples that commemorate Ramesses (“RAM-zeez”) the Great’s victory at the Battle of Kadesh.
ANSWER: Abu Simbel (“ah-BOO sim-BELL”)
[10] Karl Richard Lepsius’ discovery of the Decree of Canopus in this city confirmed the accuracy of Champollion’s method. ANSWER: Tanis (“TAY-niss”) [or San al-Hagar]
