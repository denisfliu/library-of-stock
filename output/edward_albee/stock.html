The Zoo Story

Albee's first play, a one-act about the encounter between Jerry (a 'permanent transient') and the publishing executive Peter on a bench in Central Park. Jerry describes poisoning his landlady's dog with hamburger meat, describes God as 'a colored queen who wears a kimono,' spells out 'h-o-m-o-s-e-x-u-a-l,' describes an eleven-day relationship with a Greek boy, and asks 'Was trying to feed the dog an act of love?' Jerry pokes Peter, provokes a fight, and ultimately impales himself on a knife held by Peter. Peter repeats 'Oh my god.' Later expanded with the prequel Homelife as At Home at the Zoo.

Summary of Facts

Edward Albee is one of the most heavily clued American playwrights in quizbowl, with Who's Afraid of Virginia Woolf? and The Zoo Story appearing with extreme frequency. Who's Afraid of Virginia Woolf? centers on the dysfunctional couple George and Martha, who invite the younger couple Nick and Honey for drinks and play cruel games including 'Get the Guests,' 'Hump the Host,' and 'Bringing Up Baby.' The central revelation is that George and Martha's son is imaginary — George claims the son died in a car crash swerving to avoid a porcupine, and Martha shouts 'you cannot do that!' George's 'Get the Guests' game involves describing a novel about 'Blondie' and 'Mousie' in the 'Walpurgisnacht' section. Martha quotes Bette Davis's 'what a dump!' and Nick teaches biology.

The Zoo Story is Albee's first play, depicting Jerry's encounter with the publishing executive Peter on a bench in Central Park. Jerry describes poisoning his landlady's dog with hamburger meat, describes God as 'a colored queen who wears a kimono,' spells out the word 'homosexual,' and ultimately impales himself on a knife held by Peter. The play was expanded 45 years later with the prequel Homelife into Edward Albee's At Home at the Zoo.

Seascape features the human couple Charlie and Nancy meeting the lizard couple Sarah and Leslie on the beach, with offstage jet sounds and discussions of evolution. A Delicate Balance shows Harry and Edna fleeing to Agnes and Tobias's home after an unexplained fright. Three Tall Women reveals A, B, and C as the same woman at different ages. The Goat, or Who is Sylvia? climaxes with Stevie dragging a dead goat onstage. The American Dream features Mommy and Daddy mutilating an adopted child from the Bye-Bye Adoption Service.
