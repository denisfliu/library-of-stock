Pygmalion

Shaw's most famous play. Professor Henry Higgins bets Colonel Pickering he can pass the Cockney flower girl Eliza Doolittle off as a duchess. Alfred Doolittle asks for five pounds for his daughter. Mrs. Patrick Campbell debuted the role and caused the longest audience laugh in English stage history with 'Not bloody likely!' The essay 'What Happened Afterwards' explains Eliza married Freddy Eynsford-Hill, not Higgins, and opened a flower shop. Shaw was annoyed that audiences wanted a romance between Higgins and Eliza. Described as a 'Romance in Five Acts.'

Summary of Facts

George Bernard Shaw (1856-1950) was an Irish playwright and one of the most important dramatists of the modern era. In quizbowl, he appears extremely frequently, primarily through Pygmalion, Man and Superman, and Major Barbara.

Pygmalion is his most clued work, in which the phonetics professor Henry Higgins bets Colonel Pickering he can pass the Cockney flower girl Eliza Doolittle off as a duchess. Alfred Doolittle memorably asks for five pounds for his daughter. Mrs. Patrick Campbell, who debuted the role of Eliza, caused possibly the longest audience laugh in English stage history with the line 'Not bloody likely!' Shaw was annoyed by romantic readings and wrote the essay 'What Happened Afterwards' to explain that Eliza married Freddy Eynsford-Hill, not Higgins, and opened a flower shop.

In Man and Superman, Jack Tanner marries Ann Whitefield, who embodies the 'Life Force.' The third act, 'Don Juan in Hell,' is often performed separately. Tanner is the author of The Revolutionist's Handbook. The anarchist Mendoza captures Tanner in the Sierra Nevada.

Major Barbara centers on the Undershaft family of munitions manufacturers, who only bequeath wealth to foundlings. Barbara is conflicted about her father Andrew's donation to the Salvation Army. Adolphus Cusins must reveal his foundling status to inherit. Saint Joan is about Joan of Arc's canonization, called a 'tragedy without villains.' Mrs. Warren's Profession features Vivie discovering her mother runs brothels. Arms and the Man features Captain Bluntschli, the 'chocolate-cream soldier,' during the Serbo-Bulgarian War.

Shaw coined 'Bardolatry' and 'Chesterbelloc.' His will funded a phonetic alphabet. He wrote 'Quintessence of Ibsenism' and Back to Methuselah. Also wrote Heartbreak House, Candida, and The Dark Lady of the Sonnets.
