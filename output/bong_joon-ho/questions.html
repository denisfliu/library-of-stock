Questions: Bong Joon-ho

5 tossups · 5 bonuses

Tossups

T1 2025 Spec Script (2025) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
A film by this director resurrected a song from the 1987 album Because I Love You called “Gloomy Letter.” For the ending credits of a film that features Ennio Morricone’s song “In Ginocchio da Te,” this director wrote a song in which the protagonist drinks “one glass” of liquor while lamenting having to work for 564 years. The best-known song from this director’s films is titled “The Belt of Faith.” A dreamlike opening scene by this director is set in a wheat field where an old woman with a deadpan expression dances to a guitar tune. In a film by this director, a (*) policewoman discovers that a pop song played by a radio station is linked to serial femicides. This director referenced students’ use of a pop song about the Liancourt Rocks dispute in a shot of a girl ringing a doorbell after singing “Jessica, only child, Illinois, Chicago.” For 10 points, name this director of a film in which an aria from Handel’s Rodelinda plays over a montage of the Kims getting rid of the Parks’ housekeeper.
ANSWER: Bong Joon-ho [or Joon-ho Bong] (The films mentioned are Memories of Murder, Mother, and Parasite.)
T2 2025 ACF Fall (2025) Diff 6 · Fine Arts
A director from this country created an elderly woman who writes poetry while amassing money to cover up her grandson’s crime. A film from this country ends with a detective returning to a culvert in farmland before staring into the camera after a child fails to describe a killer. In a film from this country, a vegetarian actor ate a live octopus and performed an extended-take hallway fight. In a film from this country, a character comments on his driver’s “subway smell.” A film from this home country of the original Oldboy depicts a poor family using a housekeeper’s peach allergy to infiltrate a rich household. For 10 points, name this home country of Parasite’s director, Bong Joon-ho.
ANSWER: South Korea [or Republic of Korea or ROK or Hanguk or Tehan-minguk; prompt on Korea; reject “North Korea” or “Democratic People’s Republic of Korea” or “DPRK”] (The first two lines refer to Poetry by Lee Chang-dong and Memories of Murder by Bong Joon-ho. The unnamed director is Park Chan-wook.)
T3 2023 ACF Regionals (2023) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
In this director’s first film, an old woman who comes to dry vegetables prevents an unemployed professor from dropping his neighbor’s Shih Tzu off a roof. Another film by this director ends as a former detective looks down a farm drainpipe and then stares directly at the camera as if trying to find a killer among the audience. This director of Barking Dogs Never Bite depicted the search for his country’s first serial killer in Memories of Murder. In another film by this director, two siblings practice a short song about Jessica, an only child from Chicago, before they ring a doorbell. In that film by this director, members of a poor family pose as an English tutor, an art therapist, and other jobs to infiltrate the rich Park household. For 10 points, name this South Korean director of Parasite.
ANSWER: Bong Joon-ho
T4 2021 WORKSHOP (2021) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
In a film by this director, a mentally-ill man is run over by a train after being coaxed down from a telephone pole. Another film by this director repeatedly mentions its characters’ past failed attempts at opening up fried chicken restaurants and cake shops. This director created an inspector who realizes that the local radio station plays the same song each night that a nearby (*) murder is committed in a 2003 film. A man confined to an underground bunker flickers a stairway light to communicate with his wife in a film by this director in which a chauffeur dresses up as a Native American chief at a child’s birthday party. That film by this director of Memories of Murder follows a poor family’s attempts to infiltrate the wealthy Park household. For 10 points, name this South Korean director of Parasite.
ANSWER: Bong Joon-ho
T5 2019 Reel Knowledge (2019) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
In one film, this director’s camera shakes as it follows a man turn the corner of an outdoor hallway, where a woman in another scene gets decked while running by a man opening his front door. In the last shot of one of this director’s films, a detective standing in a country road looks into the camera after a young girl tells him about an ordinary man returning to a crime scene; the actor in that shot, (*) Song-Kang Ho, has been in four of this director’s films. Two films by this director with similar environmental themes include about the conflict over a massive, experimental pig and the other about the residents of the Tail Section of a train that never stops. At the start of one of this director’s most recent film, a poor high school student lies about being in university to begin a tutor job for the wealth Park family. For 10 points, identify this Korean director behind dark and peculiar films like the monster movie the Netflix film Okja, the post-apocalyptic Snowpiercer, and the 2019 Palme D’or winner Parasite.
ANSWER: Bong Joon-ho

Bonuses

B1 2024 PACE NSC (2024) Diff 5 · Fine Arts
Answer the following about the Queer Palm prize, which is awarded to films that deal with LGBTQ+ issues and themes, for 10 points each.
[10] The Queer Palm is awarded every year at this French film festival which also awards the Palme d’Or.
ANSWER: Cannes Film Festival [or Festival de Cannes]
[10] Near the end of this 2019 Queer Palm winner, the protagonist sees a former love interest at a performance of Vivaldi’s “Summer.” In this film, a woman commissioned to paint an aristocrat falls in love with her over the course of a week on a remote island in Brittany.
ANSWER: Portrait of a Lady on Fire [or Portrait de la jeune fille en feu] (Directed by Céline Sciamma.)
[10] A director from this country was nominated for the Queer Palm in 2016 for his film The Handmaiden. A film from this country was the first non–English language film to win the Oscar for Best Picture.
ANSWER: South Korea [or Republic of Korea; prompt on Korea] (The Handmaiden is by Park Chan-Wook. The unnamed film is Parasite by Bong Joon-ho.)
B2 2023 NASAT (2023) Diff 8 · Literature
In one film, a member of this profession grasps a man's hands to feel their softness, before brutally beating him. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this profession. A film ends with a former member of this profession peering under a drainage canal before looking directly into the camera.
ANSWER: police detective [prompt on police]
[10] This director depicted two police detectives failing to catch a serial killer in Memories of Murder. In another of his films, a character practices the jingle "Jessica, only child, Illinois, Chicago."
ANSWER: Bong Joon-ho
[10] Bong Joon-ho is a director from this country, whose Gwangju uprising was depicted in the 2017 film A Taxi Driver.
ANSWER: South Korea [prompt on Korea]
B3 2022 ACF Winter (2022) Diff 7 · Fine Arts
Name these directors of non-English-language films titled for mothers, for 10 points each.
[10] A woman calmly dances in a field in the opening of this director’s film Mother. This Korean director described the “one-inch tall barrier of subtitles” in an Oscar speech after winning Best Picture for Parasite.
ANSWER: Bong Joon-ho
[10] Two teenage boys go on a raunchy road trip with a middle-aged woman in this director’s film Y tu mamá también (“ee too mah-MAH tahm-b’YEN”). This director drew on his childhood for a 2018 film about an Indigenous housekeeper.
ANSWER: Alfonso Cuarón (“kwah-ROHN”) [or Alfonso Cuarón Orozco; prompt on Orozco] (The second film is Roma.)
[10] In this director’s film Mamma Roma, a former sex worker hides her profession from her son. This gay Italian director’s staunch anti-Fascist views and profane films like Salò contributed to his mysterious unsolved murder.
ANSWER: Pier Paolo Pasolini
B4 2021 HARI (2021) Diff 7 · Fine Arts
A series of crimes in one of this director’s films always affect women in red on rainy nights as a special song plays on the radio. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this diretor. This director frequently collaborates with an actor who played the lead role in his films Memories of Murder and The Host.
ANSWER: Bong Joon-ho (The actor is Song Kang-ho.)
[10] Song Kang-ho plays Ki-taek, the patriarch of the Kim family, who are hired by the Park family in this 2019 Bong Joon-ho film. This is the first non-English film to win an Academy Award for Best Picture.
ANSWER: Parasite
[10] Song played an engineer in a 2013 film in English set in one of these locations. A 2016 live-action film by Yeon Sang-ho about a zombie outbreak is titled for one of these locations.
ANSWER: trains [or locomotives] (The 2013 film is Snowpiercer; the 2016 film is Train to Busan.)
B5 2019 ACF Fall (2019) Diff 6 · Fine Arts
In a film from this country set during its Japanese occupation, Sook-hee ends up falling in love with the heiress whom she was supposed to trick. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this home country of Park Chan-wook, who directed The Handmaiden as well as Oldboy and Lady Vengeance.
ANSWER: South Korea [or Republic of Korea; or ROK; accept Hanguk or Namhan; prompt on Korea; do not accept or prompt on “Democratic People’s Republic of Korea” or “DPRK” or “North Korea”]
[10] This man became the first Korean director to win the Palme d’Or at Cannes (“can”) with his 2019 film Parasite. His other films include Okja, Snowpiercer, and The Host.
ANSWER: Bong Joon-ho
[10] Snowpiercer is set in one of these locations, which shelters the remaining population of a frozen Earth. In a 2016 South Korean film, a man and his daughter survive a zombie outbreak while in one of these locations.
ANSWER: trains [accept Train to Busan]
