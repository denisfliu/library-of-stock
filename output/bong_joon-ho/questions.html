Tossups

T1 2025 Spec Script (2025) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
A film by this director resurrected a song from the 1987 album Because I Love You called “Gloomy Letter.” For the ending credits of a film that features Ennio Morricone’s song “In Ginocchio da Te,” this director wrote a song in which the protagonist drinks “one glass” of liquor while lamenting having to work for 564 years. The best-known song from this director’s films is titled “The Belt of Faith.” A dreamlike opening scene by this director is set in a wheat field where an old woman with a deadpan expression dances to a guitar tune. In a film by this director, a (*) policewoman discovers that a pop song played by a radio station is linked to serial femicides. This director referenced students’ use of a pop song about the Liancourt Rocks dispute in a shot of a girl ringing a doorbell after singing “Jessica, only child, Illinois, Chicago.” For 10 points, name this director of a film in which an aria from Handel’s Rodelinda plays over a montage of the Kims getting rid of the Parks’ housekeeper.
ANSWER: Bong Joon-ho [or Joon-ho Bong] (The films mentioned are Memories of Murder, Mother, and Parasite.)
T2 2025 ACF Fall (2025) Diff 6 · Fine Arts
A director from this country created an elderly woman who writes poetry while amassing money to cover up her grandson’s crime. A film from this country ends with a detective returning to a culvert in farmland before staring into the camera after a child fails to describe a killer. In a film from this country, a vegetarian actor ate a live octopus and performed an extended-take hallway fight. In a film from this country, a character comments on his driver’s “subway smell.” A film from this home country of the original Oldboy depicts a poor family using a housekeeper’s peach allergy to infiltrate a rich household. For 10 points, name this home country of Parasite’s director, Bong Joon-ho.
ANSWER: South Korea [or Republic of Korea or ROK or Hanguk or Tehan-minguk; prompt on Korea; reject “North Korea” or “Democratic People’s Republic of Korea” or “DPRK”] (The first two lines refer to Poetry by Lee Chang-dong and Memories of Murder by Bong Joon-ho. The unnamed director is Park Chan-wook.)
T3 2023 ACF Regionals (2023) Diff 8 · Fine Arts

Another film by this director ends as a former detective looks down a farm drainpipe and then stares directly at the camera as if trying to find a killer among the audience. This director of Barking Dogs Never Bite depicted the search for his country’s first serial killer in Memories of Murder. In another film by this director, two siblings practice a short song about Jessica, an only child from Chicago, before they ring a doorbell. In that film by this director, members of a poor family pose as an English tutor, an art therapist, and other jobs to infiltrate the rich Park household. For 10 points, name this South Korean director of Parasite.
ANSWER: Bong Joon-ho
