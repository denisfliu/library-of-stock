The Short Story (Annie Proulx, 1997)

Annie Proulx's short story was first published in 1997 and collected in Close Range: Wyoming Stories (1999). Jack Twist (rodeo cowboy) and Ennis del Mar (rancher) meet as teenagers herding sheep for Joe Aguirre at the title Wyoming mountain. 'I wish I knew how to quit you' is the famous line. Alma Beers (Ennis's wife) divorces him after noticing he doesn't use his fishing pole on trips (inferring his time away with Jack). The story ends with Ennis finding Jack's blood-stained shirt in a closet and pinning a postcard to it — Ennis believes Jack was killed with a tire iron. The final line notes an 'open space' between what a man 'knew' and 'tried to believe' that 'nothing could be done about.' The story was published a year before the murder of Matthew Shepard in Wyoming.

The Film (Ang Lee, 2005) →

Directed by Ang Lee; screenplay by Larry McMurtry and Diana Ossana. Stars Heath Ledger (Ennis del Mar) and Jake Gyllenhaal (Jack Twist). Cinematography by Rodrigo Prieto. Won the Golden Lion at Venice 2005. Lost the Academy Award for Best Picture to Crash ('inexplicably'). Heath Ledger won the MTV Movie Award for Best Kiss with Gyllenhaal; Ledger later won a posthumous Oscar for playing the Joker in The Dark Knight. In the film's final scene, Ennis clutches Jack's old jacket in a closet and cries. B. Ruby Rich identified the film as changing the direction of New Queer Cinema. Charles Wuorinen composed an opera based on Proulx's story. Ang Lee also directed Crouching Tiger, Hidden Dragon and Sense and Sensibility.

Summary of Facts

Brokeback Mountain is both an Annie Proulx short story (1997, collected in Close Range: Wyoming Stories, 1999) and a 2005 film directed by Ang Lee. The story follows Jack Twist (rodeo cowboy) and Ennis del Mar (rancher) who fall in love as teenagers while herding sheep for Joe Aguirre near the title Wyoming mountain. The famous line 'I wish I knew how to quit you' occurs when the men discuss leaving for Mexico. Alma Beers, Ennis's wife, divorces him after noticing he doesn't use his fishing pole on trips (inferring his time with Jack). The story ends with Ennis finding Jack's blood-stained shirt in a closet and pinning a postcard to it — Ennis believes Jack was killed with a tire iron. The final line notes an 'open space' between what a man 'knew' and 'tried to believe' that 'nothing could be done about.' The story was published a year before Matthew Shepard's murder in Wyoming.

Larry McMurtry and Diana Ossana wrote the screenplay; McMurtry won an Academy Award for it. The film stars Heath Ledger (Ennis) and Jake Gyllenhaal (Jack); cinematography by Rodrigo Prieto. It won the Golden Lion at Venice 2005 but lost Best Picture at the Oscars to Crash ('inexplicably'). Heath Ledger won the MTV Movie Award for Best Kiss with Gyllenhaal; Ledger later won a posthumous Oscar for playing the Joker in The Dark Knight. The film's final scene shows Ennis clutching Jack's old jacket in a closet and crying. B. Ruby Rich identified the film as changing the direction of New Queer Cinema. Charles Wuorinen composed an opera based on Proulx's story.
