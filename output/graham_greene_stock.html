English Catholic novelist. Known for The Power and the Glory (whiskey priest in Mexico), Brighton Rock (Pinkie Brown), The End of the Affair, The Quiet American, The Heart of the Matter, Our Man in Havana. Wrote screenplay for The Third Man. Also wrote stories 'The Destructors' and 'The End of the Party.'

Summary of Facts

Graham Greene is clued as an English Catholic novelist. The Power and the Glory follows an unnamed 'whiskey priest' hunted by an anti-clerical lieutenant in Mexico; a yellow-toothed mestizo betrays the priest. Brighton Rock features teenage gang leader Pinkie Brown investigated by Ida Arnold after Fred Hale's murder. The End of the Affair is about Maurice Bendrix's affair with Sarah Miles during the Blitz. The Quiet American features CIA agent Alden Pyle vs. journalist Thomas Fowler in Vietnam, with Pyle inspired by York Harding's 'Third Force.' The Heart of the Matter follows Major Scobie's suicide in Sierra Leone. Our Man in Havana is about vacuum salesman Wormold recruited as a spy. 'The Destructors' is his most clued story — the Wormsley Common Gang destroys Old Misery's house. He wrote The Third Man screenplay and helped publish R.K. Narayan and Flann O'Brien.
