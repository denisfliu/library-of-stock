B1 2024 Penn Bowl (2024) Diff 8 · Philosophy
Two answers required. Plato supposedly added “with broad, flat nails” to a definition of man as having these two properties. For 10 points each:
[10] Name these two properties that Plato used to define man according to one story. Diogenes legendarily refuted that definition by presenting a chicken that had both of these properties.
ANSWER: featherless AND bipedal [accept in either order; accept biped or descriptions of walking on two feet for bipedal; accept descriptions of not having feathers for featherless]
[10] Although Diogenes was a featherless biped, his dog-like following of this earlier thinker may have inspired the name of Cynicism. This thinker is often considered the founder of Cynicism.
ANSWER: Antisthenes
[10] Some stories attribute Diogenes’ death to eating the raw meat of one of these animals. These animals appear in the subtitle of a 2016 book by the Australian thinker Peter Godfrey-Smith.
ANSWER: octopuses [or octopedes or octopi; accept Other Minds: The Octopus, The Sea, and the Deep Origin of Consciousness]
B2 2023 NASAT (2023) Diff 8 · Philosophy
Two members of this school, Crates of Thebes and Hipparchia of Maroneia, were married and often had sex in public. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this school of philosophy whose proponents include Diogenes of Sinope, who wandered around Athens with a lantern looking for an honest man.
ANSWER: cynicism [or the cynics]
[10] In one story, Plato's definition of a man was changed to include "with broad, flat nails" after Diogenes brought one of these things to the Academy and proclaimed "Behold! A man!".
ANSWER: plucked chicken
[10] This student of Socrates was retroactively called the founder of cynicism by Diogenes Laertius. This man's Physicus asserted that there is only one God who is not understandable by humans.
ANSWER: Antisthenes of Athens
B3 2017 NASAT (2017) Diff 8 · Philosophy
When asked why he was called a dog, this man replied "I fawn on those who give me anything, I yelp at those who refuse, and I set my teeth in rascals." For 10 points each:
[10] Name this man who engaged in such weird behavior as sleeping in a ceramic jar in the marketplace and carrying a lamp in the daytime, claiming that he was looking for an honest man.
ANSWER: Diogenes of Sinope
[10] The thought of Cynic philosophers like Diogenes was adapted by Zeno of Citium into this philosophical school, which emphasized controlling destructive emotions. The Roman Emperor Marcus Aurelius followed this philosophy.
ANSWER: Stoicism
[10] Diogenes was called the "faithful hound" of this man because, according to legend, he doggedly pursued him. This student of Socrates argued that virtue, not pleasure, was the end of existence, and he is sometimes considered the founder of Cynicism.
ANSWER: Antisthenes
