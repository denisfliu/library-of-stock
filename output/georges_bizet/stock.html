French composer (1838–1875), best known for the opera Carmen (1875). Also composed The Pearl Fishers (Les pêcheurs de perles, 1863), the incidental music for Alphonse Daudet's L'Arlésienne (arranged into two suites, both concluding with the 'March of the Kings' theme), and a precocious Symphony in C (written at 17, first performed 60 years after his death). His teacher was Charles Gounod. Ernest Guiraud arranged the second L'Arlésienne suite and wrote recitatives for Carmen.

L'Arlésienne Suites

Two orchestral suites arranged from Bizet's incidental music for Alphonse Daudet's play L'Arlésienne. Both suites feature themes from the Epiphany hymn 'March of the Kings' — in the Suite No. 1's 'Prelude' and Suite No. 2's closing 'Farandole.' The first suite's final movement, 'Carillon,' features horns imitating tolling church bells with a three-note ostinato. The second suite was arranged by Ernest Guiraud. Wikipedia

Symphony in C

Written by Bizet at age 17 while a student at the Paris Conservatory, borrowing heavily from Gounod's Symphony in D (his teacher's work). Bizet suppressed it; it was first performed 60 years after his death. The allegretto vivace scherzo opens with a fugato; the adagio second movement also features fugato development. The aria 'De mon amie' was based on its second movement. Inspired Balanchine's ballet The Crystal Palace. Also associated with his Roma Symphony. Wikipedia

Summary of Facts

Georges Bizet (1838–1875) was a French opera composer best known for Carmen (1875) and The Pearl Fishers (Les pêcheurs de perles, 1863). His teacher at the Paris Conservatory was Charles Gounod. At age 17, Bizet composed a Symphony in C that borrowed heavily from Gounod's Symphony in D — Bizet suppressed it, and it was not performed until 60 years after his death. It later inspired Balanchine's ballet The Crystal Palace.

The Pearl Fishers, set in ancient Sri Lanka (Ceylon), features the famous friendship duet 'Au fond du temple saint' sung by the baritone Zurga and the tenor Nadir, who discover they are both in love with the Brahman priestess Leila. The opera also includes Leila's aria 'Comme autrefois dans la nuit sombre' recalling happier times, and the aria 'Je crois entendre encore' in which Nadir admits betrayal. Hector Berlioz gave The Pearl Fishers a glowing review in his final feuilleton. The librettist of another Bizet opera, The Fair Maid of Perth (in which Ralph and Henry Smith duel over Catherine Glover), was Saint-George — the same person who wrote the libretto for the ballet Giselle.

Bizet wrote incidental music for Alphonse Daudet's play L'Arlésienne, which was arranged into two orchestral suites. Both suites quote the Epiphany hymn 'March of the Kings' — in Suite No. 1's 'Prelude' and Suite No. 2's concluding 'Farandole.' The first suite's final movement, 'Carillon,' features French horns imitating tolling church bells with the three-note ostinato G-sharp, E, F-sharp. Ernest Guiraud (a New Orleans-born French composer) arranged Suite No. 2 and also wrote orchestral recitatives for Carmen and Offenbach's The Tales of Hoffmann.

Carmen (1875), Bizet's final opera, is his masterpiece. The title character sings the Habanera ('L'amour est un oiseau rebelle'), comparing love to a rebellious bird — with a melody Bizet unknowingly borrowed from a Sebastián Iradier song. The soprano character Micaëla, who was not in the Mérimée novella, sings 'Je dis que rien ne m'épouvante' in Act III. The opera ends with Don José stabbing Carmen outside a bullfight arena. Bizet also set words by Charles Grandmougin to music and completed Fromental Halévy's unfinished opera Noé.
