Summary of Facts

Jenny Saville is a contemporary British painter and member of the Young British Artists (YBAs). She is known primarily for her large-scale paintings of female nudes, often depicting obese, distorted, or fleshy bodies. Clues about Saville overwhelmingly center on her 1992 painting Propped, a 7-by-6-foot nude self-portrait in which she is perched atop a wooden post with mirror-written text from feminist theorist Luce Irigaray carved directly into the paint surface. This is by far her most frequently asked-about work and appears in both tossup and bonus questions.

Her other notable work in the quiz canon is Strategy, a monumental 9-foot-by-21-foot triptych consisting of three panels titled South Face, Front Face, and North Face, depicting a woman in white lingerie staring down at the viewer from three different angles. Strategy is also notable for appearing on the cover of Manic Street Preachers' album The Holy Bible. Matrix is referenced as a work discussed by Linda Nochlin in writing about nudes.

Biographically, Saville is consistently identified as a member of the YBAs alongside figures like Tracey Emin, Rachel Whiteread, Sarah Lucas, and Damien Hirst, all of whom were featured in Charles Saatchi's 1997 exhibit Sensation. Her work is frequently compared to that of Lucian Freud, particularly for their shared interest in painting nude, fleshy bodies with thick impasto technique. She also collaborated with Martyn Atkins on a work featuring the Appiani family tomb in Genoa. Power clues tend to focus on the Irigaray text in Propped, the Strategy triptych dimensions, and the Martyn Atkins collaboration; giveaways identify her as a British painter of large nudes.
