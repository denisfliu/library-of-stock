B1 2022 NASAT (2022) Diff 8 · Literature
David Sylvester named a cultural movement for these objects in reference to John Bratby's depressing painting of one. For 10 points each:
[10] Name these objects, which describe a genre of gritty working-class drama exemplified by Shelagh (SHEE-la) Delaney's play A Taste of Honey.
ANSWER: kitchen sinks [or kitchen sink dramas; prompt on kitchens]
[10] A woman burns her arm on an iron in this pioneering kitchen sink drama, which depicts the effects of class pressures on Alison and Jimmy Porter.
ANSWER: Look Back in Anger [by John Osborne]
[10] In A Taste of Honey, a character with this first name worries about future economic pressures after getting pregnant. A member of the March family with this first name, the sister of Meg, Beth, and Amy, becomes a writer in Little Women.
ANSWER: Jo [or Josephine]
B2 2022 ACF Regionals (2022) Diff 8 · Literature
A character in this play jumps up and down and shouts “oooooh!” while making “paw gestures,” prompting another character to ask her, “What makes you think you’re happy?” For 10 points each:
[10] Name this play by the author of The Entertainer. This 1956 play focuses on the working-class Jimmy Porter and his middle-class wife Alison, who play “bears and squirrels” upon reuniting.
ANSWER: Look Back in Anger (by John Osborne)
[10] Nora is belittlingly called a “little squirrel” by her husband Torvald, whom she later leaves by symbolically slamming the door in this Henrik Ibsen play.
ANSWER: A Doll’s House [or A Doll House; or Et dukkehjem]
[10] Squirrels frequently appear as portents of misfortune for the title character of this novel. This novel’s protagonist recalls the death at Buchenwald of his former lover Mira Belochkin, whose last name means “squirrel.”
ANSWER: Pnin (“puh-NEEN”) (by Vladimir Nabokov)
