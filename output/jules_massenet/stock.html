General / Biographical

Jules Massenet (1842–1912) was a prolific French opera composer and student of Ambroise Thomas. His style was characterized by lyrical melody and emotional warmth, bridging grand opera and the lighter opera comique tradition. His use of the saxophone (e.g., in Herodiade and Le roi de Lahore) may have been influenced by Thomas's use of the instrument in Hamlet. Gabriel Fauré criticized Massenet's 'vulgar and passionate writhings.' He cast soprano Sibyl Sanderson to sing the high 'Eiffel Tower note' G6 in Esclarmonde.

Thais

Massenet's opera about the monk Athanael who attempts to convert the title courtesan Thais to Christianity, only to discover his attraction to her is of a sexual nature. The opera includes the famous 'Meditation' — a D-major entr'acte for violin and orchestra that plays as Thais contemplates conversion. At the opera's end, Thais joins a convent and is praised as a saint, while Athanael is undone by his own desire. Wikipedia

Manon

Massenet's opera based on the Abbé Prévost novel about Manon Lescaut. Manon sings 'Adieu, notre petite table' when she decides to leave her lover for Monsieur de Bretigny. She ultimately dies in the arms of Chevalier des Grieux, before being deported to Le Havre. Des Grieux's dream aria 'En fermant les yeux' is also known as the 'Dream Song.' The opera is seen as a precursor to operas with Carmen-like female leads, and Puccini also set the same novel as Manon Lescaut. Wikipedia

Werther

Massenet's adaptation of Goethe's The Sorrows of Young Werther. The title character (tenor) recites poetry by Ossian in front of his married love interest Charlotte (Lotte), and sings the aria 'Pourquoi me reveiller?' Act III opens with a letter scene in which Charlotte reads Werther's letters and sings the alto saxophone-accompanied aria 'Va! Laisse couler mes larmes.' Werther borrows two pistols from Albert (Charlotte's husband) before shooting himself. The opera begins and ends with a Christmas carol ('Noel! Jesus vient de naitre') sung by the bailiff's children. The role was adjusted from tenor to baritone for Mattia Battistini. Wikipedia

Esclarmonde

A lesser-known Massenet opera in which the title character, Esclarmonde — daughter of Phorcas, Emperor of Byzantium — falls in love with Roland. She gives him a sword to defeat her empire's enemies but never reveals her face to him. Roland refuses the hand of the King of France's daughter and wins a tournament to marry Esclarmonde. Sibyl Sanderson made her professional debut in the title role, singing the high G6 'Eiffel Tower note.' Wikipedia

Other Works

Other operas by Massenet include Le Cid (whose aria inspired Laurie Anderson's 'O Superman'), Le roi de Lahore (which featured alto saxophone solos), Herodiade (in which Rodrigue sings 'O souverain, o juge, o pere'), and the comic opera Cherubin (a sequel to Mozart's Marriage of Figaro following the page character). He also wrote the oratorio La Vierge with libretto by Charles Grandmougin.

Summary of Facts

Jules Massenet was a prolific French opera composer and student of Ambroise Thomas (who had himself used the saxophone in Hamlet). Massenet's own use of the saxophone in operas like Herodiade and Le roi de Lahore may reflect this influence. Gabriel Fauré criticized Massenet's style as exhibiting 'vulgar and passionate writhings.' He wrote over two dozen operas representing a lyric style that bridged the elaborate grandeur of French grand opera with the lighter opera comique tradition.

Massenet's best-known opera is Thais, in which the monk Athanael attempts to convert the title courtesan to Christianity but discovers his attraction to her is sexual. The opera's most famous excerpt is the 'Meditation,' a D-major entr'acte for solo violin and orchestra marked Andante Religioso, which plays as Thais contemplates conversion. At the opera's end, Thais joins a convent and is sainted. In the opera, Thais asks to spare her statue of Eros even as she agrees to destroy her other possessions.

Manon, based on the Abbé Prévost novel (the same source as Puccini's Manon Lescaut), is another major opera. The heroine sings 'Adieu, notre petite table' when she decides to leave Chevalier des Grieux for the wealthier Monsieur de Bretigny. She ultimately dies in des Grieux's arms before being deported to Le Havre. Des Grieux's 'Dream Song' ('En fermant les yeux') also appears in the opera.

Werther adapts Goethe's epistolary novel The Sorrows of Young Werther. The title tenor sings 'Pourquoi me reveiller?' while reciting Ossian's poetry to the married Charlotte (Lotte), before asking her husband Albert to lend him two pistols. Act III opens with a letter scene in which Charlotte reads Werther's letters and sings 'Va! Laisse couler mes larmes,' accompanied by a prominent alto saxophone solo. The opera famously begins and ends with the Christmas carol 'Noel! Jesus vient de naitre' sung by the bailiff's children. Massenet adjusted the tenor role to baritone for a performance by Mattia Battistini.

Esclarmonde is a lesser-known Massenet opera in which the title character, daughter of Byzantine Emperor Phorcas, loves Roland and gives him a sword, but conceals her face from him. Roland wins a tournament for the right to marry her. Soprano Sibyl Sanderson — who made her professional debut in the role — was cast specifically so Massenet could feature the high G6 'Eiffel Tower note.'

Among his other works, the aria from Le Cid inspired Laurie Anderson's performance art piece 'O Superman (For Massenet),' which opens with the looped syllable 'Ah' and addresses 'mom and dad,' a judge, and the postal service motto. Massenet also wrote Cherubin, a late comic opera that continues the story of the page character from Mozart's Marriage of Figaro, and the oratorio La Vierge with libretto by Charles Grandmougin.
