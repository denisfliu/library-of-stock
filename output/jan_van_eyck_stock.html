Summary of Facts

Jan van Eyck (c. 1390-1441) was a Netherlandish Renaissance painter credited by Vasari with inventing oil painting (along with brothers Lambert and Hubert). His most commonly clued work is the Arnolfini Portrait (1434), a double portrait often interpreted as depicting a wedding, which features the artist's signature ('Jan van Eyck was here') written on a wall in lavish calligraphy near the date 1434, above a convex mirror whose wooden frame depicts the Stations of the Cross.

The Ghent Altarpiece, created with his brother Hubert, is a massive polyptych featuring the Adoration of the Mystic Lamb in the bottom panel, St. Cecilia playing the organ, God/Mary/John the Baptist in the center, and the famous Just Judges panel (which shows both brothers as horsemen). Inscriptions say 'music in praise of God.'

His signature motto 'Als Ich Kan' ('As I can') puns on his name. He is likely 'Hand G' who worked on the Turin-Milan Hours. Man in a Red Turban is considered a possible self-portrait. Leal Souvenir is a portrait that Erwin Panofsky claimed depicts the composer Gilles Binchois. His Annunciation features 'ecce ancilla Domini' written upside-down and a Book of Wisdom quote on Mary's robe.
