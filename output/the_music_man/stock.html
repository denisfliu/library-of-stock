Characters and Plot

The musical centers on Harold Hill, a con man who arrives in River City, Iowa, and tries to scam the townspeople by convincing them to buy instruments for a boys' band he has no intention of forming. He falls in love with Marian the Librarian (who works in a library). Other characters include the lisping child Winthrop Paroo, teenage couple Tommy Djilas and Zaneeta, Mayor Shinn, the rival salesman Charlie Cowell, and Marcellus. The barbershop quartet the Buffalo Bills performs songs including 'Lida Rose' and harmonizes at an ice cream social.

Summary of Facts

The Music Man is a musical by Meredith Willson that premiered in 1957. It is set in River City, Iowa, and centers on Harold Hill, a con man who arrives in town and tries to scam the residents by convincing them to buy instruments and uniforms for a boys' band he has no intention of actually forming. He falls in love with Marian the Librarian, who works in the town library.

The musical's most recognizable song is 'Ya Got Trouble,' in which Harold Hill warns the townspeople about the moral corruption that a pool hall will bring, featuring the famous lyric 'With a capital T / that rhymes with P / that stands for Pool!' Another signature song is 'Seventy-Six Trombones,' for which Leroy Anderson wrote a popular arrangement. Other well-known songs include ''Til There Was You,' 'The Wells Fargo Wagon,' 'Iowa Stubborn' (with the lyric 'You ought to give Iowa a Try!'), 'Shipoopi' (about a girl who waits to kiss until the third date), and 'Gary, Indiana,' which is sung by the lisping child Winthrop Paroo.

The opening scene, 'Rock Island,' is set on a moving train and features the repeatedly spoken line 'but he doesn't know the territory!' The making of the musical is the subject of the book But He Doesn't Know the Territory, which takes its title from this line.

Notable characters include the teenage couple Tommy Djilas and Zaneeta, who are seeing each other secretly; Mayor Shinn; the rival salesman Charlie Cowell, whom a woman kisses to delay from delivering evidence; and Marcellus. A barbershop quartet, the Buffalo Bills, performs songs including 'how can there be any sin in sincere / where is the good in goodbye?' and harmonizes at an ice cream social while being asked to repeatedly say the word 'ice cream.' Harold Hill avoids revealing his teaching degree by instead teaching the barbershop quartet.

The Music Man is referenced in several other contexts: in the film The Apartment, the protagonist receives tickets to The Music Man; Marian working in a library has been clued; and the barbershop quartet is referenced in questions about the barbershop genre.
