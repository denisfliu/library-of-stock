Tossups

T1 2025 Spec Script (2025) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
In a 2017 article about this character, physicist Robert E. Wallace confirmed that his mother-in-law’s husband Jean Plummer, not Elliot Carpenter, recorded music used in the film to dub this character’s performance. This male character’s most famous dialogue inspired the title of a podcast about Hollywood history hosted by Karina Longworth. Until intervention by story editor Irene Diamond and screenwriters the Epstein twins, producer Hal B. Wallis considered changing this character’s sex to cast Hazel Scott or Lena (*) Horne. This character’s employer remarks, “I don’t buy or sell human beings” when he is asked about the price to get this character to work for the Blue Parrot. This character pretends not to remember the melody of a song whose lyrics assert that “a sigh is just a sigh.” Dooley Wilson’s performance as this character popularized the jazz song “As Time Goes By.” For 10 points, what Black pianist in Casablanca is never actually told to “play it again”?
ANSWER: Sam [accept “play it again, Sam”; prompt on the pianist from Casablanca until “pianist” is read] (The podcast is You Must Remember This, which is a line from the lyrics of “As Time Goes By.”)
T2 2025 ACF Fall (2025) Diff 6 · Fine Arts
An Umberto Eco essay argues that this film’s casting of Conrad Veidt (“fight”) and Peter Lorre (“LOHR-uh”) in supporting roles makes it an “anthology” of character archetypes. A declaration in this film by a policeman played by Claude Rains gave its title to the movie The Usual Suspects. In this film’s climax, a man tells his former lover that their problems “don’t amount to a hill of beans” before convincing her to board a plane to Lisbon with Victor Laszlo. The pianist Sam is told to play “As Time Goes By” in this film, in which a crowd sings “La Marsellaise” to drown out a battalion of Nazis. For 10 points, what 1942 film stars Humphrey Bogart as Rick Blaine, who owns a bar in its title Moroccan city?
ANSWER: Casablanca
T3 2024 This Set is Garbo (2024) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
Description acceptable. In an infamous foreign dub, a Norwegian nuclear physicist shows up at this place wanted by Interpol for breaking out of prison. This place titles a play deemed “sophisticated hokum” that the schoolteacher Murray Burnett wrote with Joan Alison. A man in this place is told “boss, you’ve done a beautiful thing” for telling a Bulgarian couple to try number 22. When asked, (*) “You despise me don’t you?”a man in this place replies, “If I gave you any thought, I probably would.” In this place, an official says that he is shocked to see gambling taking place before being handed his winnings. A chorus of “Die Wacht am Rhein” in this place is countered by the band playing “La Marseillaise.” For 10 points, identify this “gin joint” where Sam plays “As Time Goes By.”
ANSWER: Rick’s Café Americain [or Rick’s American Café or Café Americain; accept Everybody Comes to Rick’s; accept descriptions of the bar from Casablanca; prompt on Casablanca; prompt on the bar from Casablanca and synonyms]
T4 2024 Chicago Open (2024) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
The lawns around one of these structures contain a former part of it whose failed renovation earned it the nickname “Smith’s Folly.” Michel Pinseau included a laser in his design for one of these structures adorned with seafoam-colored tiles. According to legend, a monarch covered up his laziness by claiming that his tinkering with paper was a design for one of these structures. One of these structures inspired Philip Johnson’s 1976 design for an all-white building in Dallas’s Thanks-Giving Square. One of these structures in (*) Seville was converted into the Giralda (“hee-RALL-dah”). Indian instances of these structures include four that comprise an iconic monument of Hyderabad and a brick one in Delhi’s Qutb complex. Ziggurats may have inspired the Malwiya, a spiral-shaped sort of this structure in Samarra. For 10 points, name these towers from which muezzins call for prayer.
ANSWER: minarets [or minars; accept Charminar, Qutb Minar, or Qutub Minar; accept Malwiya or Al-Minārat al-Malwiyyah until “Malwiya” is read; prompt on mosques; prompt on towers, pillars, or bell towers] (Pinseau designed the Hassan II Mosque in Casablanca. The ruler is Ibn Tulun, the namesake of the Mosque of Ibn Tulun in Cairo. The Great Mosque of Samarra contains a spiral-shaped minaret.)
