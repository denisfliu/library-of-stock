Daniel Libeskind is a Polish-American deconstructivist architect known primarily for the Jewish Museum Berlin, with its zigzag plan, 'voids,' and Holocaust Tower; for his master plan for the rebuilding of the World Trade Center site (One World Trade Center, 1,776 feet tall); and for the Frederic C. Hamilton Building at the Denver Art Museum. He also designed the Imperial War Museum North in Manchester and the Crystal expansion of the Royal Ontario Museum.
Jewish Museum Berlin

The Jewish Museum Berlin is Libeskind's masterpiece, featuring a zigzag plan with a metal exterior perforated by slash-mark-like windows. The museum is accessible only through underground tunnels and features 'voids' representing the erasures of the Holocaust — one void contains thousands of iron cutouts resembling human faces ('Fallen Leaves'). The Garden of Exile has 49 pillars planted with oleaster. A staircase unexpectedly leads to a blank wall, and the complex includes the Holocaust Tower.

Summary of Facts

Daniel Libeskind is a Polish-American architect associated with deconstructivism, a movement connected to Jacques Derrida's theoretical ideas, characterized by a distorted and fragmented style. His most prominent work is the Jewish Museum Berlin, whose plan follows a zigzagging shape whose exterior is perforated by slash-mark-like windows. The museum is accessible only through underground tunnels and contains sections called 'voids' meant to represent the emptiness left by the erasures of the Holocaust — one void lacking air conditioning contains thousands of iron cutouts resembling human faces. One void's installation is called 'Fallen Leaves.' The museum also contains 49 pillars planted with oleaster in the Garden of Exile. A staircase in the museum unexpectedly leads to a blank wall, and the museum is connected to the Holocaust Tower.

Libeskind is also widely recognized for winning the competition to design the rebuilt World Trade Center site in Lower Manhattan after September 11, 2001. His design called for a building with a 1,776-foot-tall spire. However, developer Larry Silverstein had David Childs of Skidmore, Owings and Merrill rework the design, discarding much of Libeskind's original plans. The resulting One World Trade Center still reaches 1,776 feet. Libeskind had been inspired by the Statue of Liberty in his original design.

Other major works include the Frederic C. Hamilton Building, an extension of the Denver Art Museum, whose jagged form was inspired by the Rocky Mountains; the Imperial War Museum North in Manchester (a key work of deconstructivism); the 'Crystal' expansion of the Royal Ontario Museum in Toronto; the Westside Center shopping mall on the outskirts of Bern, Switzerland; The Shops at Crystals mall in Las Vegas (with David Rockwell); a 70-pointed crystal star for the annual Rockefeller Center tree ceremony; and a redesign of a Pittsburgh synagogue following a 2018 shooting. Three triangular towers he designed for Rome were prevented from being built near AS Roma's stadium.
