Amahl's mother tries to steal the kings' gold for her son; caught, the kings forgive her. Amahl then offers his crutch as his only gift for the Christ Child and is miraculously healed. He sings 'I Walk, Mother.' Menotti insisted the role of Amahl must be played by a child, not an adult singer (unlike the standard operatic practice of using women to play boys). Wikipedia

Summary of Facts

Amahl and the Night Visitors is a one-act Christmas opera by Italian-American composer Gian Carlo Menotti (1911–2007), premiered on NBC television on December 24, 1951 — the first opera specifically composed for television. It was commissioned by NBC and premiered with the NBC Opera Theatre (itself reformed after the NBC Symphony Orchestra was disbanded). The opera premiered the same year as Billy Budd, The Pilgrim's Progress, and The Rake's Progress.

The plot: A disabled, crutch-dependent boy named Amahl lives in poverty with his widowed mother in Bethlehem. The three Magi — Kaspar, Balthazar, and Melchior — arrive at their door, seeking shelter on their way to visit the newborn Christ Child. They carry lavish gifts of gold and other treasures. Amahl's mother, moved by her son's suffering, tries to steal some of the gold intended for Jesus (expressed in her aria 'All That Gold!'). She is caught, but the kings forgive her. Amahl then offers his crutch — his only possession — as a gift for the Christ Child. As the kings accept it, Amahl is miraculously healed and sings 'I Walk, Mother.' The page of the kings sings 'Thief! Thief!' in pursuit of the mother. Other songs include 'From Far Away We Come' and 'This is my box.'

Menotti insisted the role of Amahl be played by a real child, not by the adult women who conventionally sing travesti (trouser) roles in opera. Menotti's other operas include The Consul (Pulitzer Prize), The Saint of Bleecker Street (Pulitzer Prize), The Medium, and The Telephone. He was the long-term romantic partner of composer Samuel Barber (with whom he shared a house called Capricorn). He also founded the Spoleto Festivals.
