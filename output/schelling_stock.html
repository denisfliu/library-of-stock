German Idealist philosopher who developed Naturphilosophie and wrote System of Transcendental Idealism. Known as an 'objective' proponent of idealism, roommate and later rival of Hegel, and mentee of Fichte. His work spans early nature philosophy, the 'identity philosophy,' the Freedom Essay, and later 'positive philosophy.'
Naturphilosophie (Philosophy of Nature)

Schelling popularized the term Naturphilosophie in a sequence of works including Ideas for a Philosophy of Nature. His nature philosophy grounded reality in an original unity or primordial totality of opposites and was meant to counter Fichte's Wissenschaftslehre. Lorenz Oken was a leading figure in the broader Naturphilosophie movement. Samuel Taylor Coleridge drew heavily on Schelling's Naturphilosophie in his Biographia Literaria, introducing Schelling to the English-speaking world.

System of Transcendental Idealism

Published in 1800, this work attempts to trace the evolution of the thinking subject through the 'absolute I' coming to know itself. It begins by arguing that since every truth is as true as any other, there must be 'one mediating principle in all knowledge.' In it, Schelling claims art is the 'eternal organ and document of philosophy' and that aesthetic activity unifies the conscious and unconscious. Franz Baader ('cogitor, ergo Deus est') influenced Schelling's account of the history of self-consciousness in this work.

Freedom Essay and Later Philosophy

Schelling's Philosophical Inquiries into the Essence of Human Freedom (the 'Freedom Essay') argues that evil is the ground that teaches man the difference between good and evil and is necessary for the development of perfection. His middle period is called the Philosophy of the Ages of the World (Die Weltalter), aiming to avoid the fatalism of Spinoza. His later 'positive philosophy' moved beyond the 'merely logical relationship between God and the world.' He lectured on positive philosophy and philosophical theology (Philosophy of Mythology) at the University of Berlin.

General / Biographical

Schelling was Hegel's roommate at university and later rival. Hegel mocked his 'point of absolute indifference' as a 'night in which all cows are black' in the Phenomenology of Spirit. He was a mentee of Fichte and an 'objective' proponent of German Idealism. His 'identity philosophy' called being 'revelation' and existence 'the link of a being as One with itself as a multiplicity.' Manfred Frank argues Feuerbach and Marx drew their critique of Hegel from Schelling. Fichte criticized his 'Presentation of My System.'

Summary of Facts

Friedrich Wilhelm Joseph von Schelling appears in quizbowl as a German Idealist philosopher best known for his Naturphilosophie and System of Transcendental Idealism. His nature philosophy grounded reality in an original unity of opposites and was meant to counter Fichte's Wissenschaftslehre. Ideas for a Philosophy of Nature is a key work. Samuel Taylor Coleridge drew heavily on Schelling's Naturphilosophie in his Biographia Literaria.

The System of Transcendental Idealism (1800) traces the evolution of the thinking subject through the 'absolute I' coming to know itself and argues there must be 'one mediating principle in all knowledge.' Schelling claimed art is the 'eternal organ and document of philosophy,' unifying the conscious and unconscious.

His Philosophical Inquiries into the Essence of Human Freedom (the 'Freedom Essay') argues evil is necessary for the development of perfection. His middle period, the Philosophy of the Ages of the World, aimed to avoid Spinoza's fatalism. His later 'positive philosophy' moved beyond the merely logical relationship between God and the world, and he lectured on philosophical theology at the University of Berlin.

Biographically, Schelling was Hegel's roommate and later rival. Hegel famously mocked Schelling's 'point of absolute indifference' as a 'night in which all cows are black.' He was a mentee of Fichte and an 'objective' proponent of idealism. Manfred Frank argues Feuerbach and Marx drew their critique of Hegel from Schelling. His identity philosophy called being 'revelation' and existence 'the link of a being as One with itself as a multiplicity.'
