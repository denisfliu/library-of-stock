  Part 2 (ans: vanitas paintings):
    This subgenre of Dutch still-life paintings often depicted skulls on tables as a symbol of the emptiness of life.

    This artist and member of Les Vingt depicted two skulls playing tug-of-war over a pickled herring with their teeth in one of his skeleton paintings. He also created a large crowd scene in which Jesus visits his nation's capital, in which a large red banner reads "VIVE LE SOCIALE."
