  Part 2 (ans: Hieronymous Bosch [or Jheronimus van Aken]):
    "Speech bubbles" in the form of Gothic inscriptions appear above the heads of Pilate and the mob in this artist's first rendition of Ecce Homo. He also painted The Garden of Earthly Delights.
  Part 3 (ans: monkey [or primate; or Ecce Mono]):
    In 2012, an Ecce Homo fresco by the painter Elias Garcia Martinez gained notoriety when it was "restored" by an untrained octogenerian woman, resulting in Christ resembling this type of animal. The painting received an unofficial punning retronym.

     Leadin: His landscapes include Landscape with a Castle in the Ruins, and self portraits include one in which a skeleton holds onto him. For 10 points each:
  Part 1 (ans: Arnold Bocklin):
    Name this Swiss painter of Isle of the Dead.
  Part 2 (ans: Symbolism):
    Arnold Bocklin was a member of this late 19th century movement, which sought to revive some elements of mystical Romanticism. Other practitioners of this loosely defined movement included Fantin-Latour and James Ensor. Jean Moreas wrote its manifesto.
  Part 3 (ans: Edvard Munch):
    This other Symbolist painted The Sick Room as part of The Frieze of Life. He depicted a man holding his hands to his face with his mouth agape in his The Scream.
