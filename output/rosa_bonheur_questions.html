3 tossups · 8 bonuses

Tossups

T1 2022 Penn Bowl (2022) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
John Boultbee’s depiction of a celebrated example of these animals inspired the hilarious right angles in other images by John Porter and Henry Stafford. Paulus Potter made a monumental portrait of one of these animals that rivaled The Night Watch in popularity. A portrait by Édouard Dubufe (“doo-BOOF”) shows a French artist in black draping her arm over one of these animals; she had earlier painted two groups of them in a (*) diagonal composition found in the Musée d’Orsay. A black one of these animals is surrounded by crows in a depressing work by Van Gogh. Regionalist works titled for them include John Steuart Curry’s Ajax and Thomas Hart Benton’s Achelous and Hercules. A vacation inspired Édouard Manet to paint these animals surrounded by garishly dressed figures in a circular ring. For 10 points, name these animals that Manet painted in Spain fighting matadors.
ANSWER: cows [or cattle; accept bulls, heifers, oxen, and other synonyms for cow] (The lead-in refers to the widely celebrated Durham Ox. The power-crossing line refers to Rosa Bonheur.)
T2 2021 Penn Bowl (2021) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
Yellow and red blotches surround a lounging deer in a painting this artist did on a palette. A traveling wild west show inspired this artist’s meeting with, and portrait of, Buffalo Bill. The utopian socialism of this artist’s father is discussed in an essay that describes the stronger “pathetic fallacy” in the work of Edwin Landseer. For a large painting compared to the Parthenon frieze, this artist was assisted by Nathalie Micas. Linda Nochlin’s essay “Why Have There Been No Great (*) Women Artists?” extensively discusses this artist, who may have been inspired by George Sand for a painting depicting teams of oxen being driven in a field. This artist of Ploughing in the Nivernais donned trousers to paint a tree-lined boulevard along which dealers sell the title animals. For 10 points, name this artist of The Horse Fair.
ANSWER: Rosa Bonheur
T3 2017 Jordaens Visual Arts (2017) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
A portrait of this artist at age 76 holding a sketchpad and sitting by an easel was painted by Anna Klumpke, who as a child had played with dolls depicting this artist that were widely sold in the US. Ernest Gambart’s engravings spread the fame of a painting by this artist that was begun by Nathalie Micas and was partly inspired by the Parthenon frieze. This artist’s many exotic pets included a gazelle, a deer, and Nero and Fatma, two lions who appear in many paintings set on the savannah. In a painting by this artist, a team of twelve oxen move right along a diagonal line of soil, which is compositionally similar to the line of (*) trees in another painting by this artist. This artist of Ploughing in the Nivernais received a special dispensation from the police to wear trousers in public, allowing her to make sketches in the Boulevard de l’Hôpital of animals being led in a circle at the market. For 10 points, name this French painter of The Horse Fair.
ANSWER: Rosa Bonheur

Bonuses

B1 2024 Chicago Open (2024) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
Camille Corot used a painting of the Bas-Bréau section of this forest as the basis of his depiction of Hagar in the Wilderness. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this forest often painted by members of the Barbizon School. A letter from Théodore Rousseau resulted in this forest being made an “artistic reserve” protected in French law.
ANSWER: Forest of Fontainebleau [or Forêt de Fontainebleau; accept Forêt de Bière]
[10] Douglas Cooper successfully argued that Monet’s depiction of Chailly Road should be renamed after this oak tree in the Bas-Bréau portion of Fontainebleau. This tree is named after a Swiss painter who often painted it, but who is better known for depictions of Native Americans along the Missouri River.
ANSWER: Bodmer Oak [or Karl Bodmer]
[10] After the success of paintings such as Ploughing in the Nivernais and The Horse Fair, this lesbian painter was able to purchase the Château de By on the outskirts of Fontainebleau.
ANSWER: Rosa Bonheur
B2 2024 ARGOS (2024) Diff 9 · Fine Arts
An essay by this author criticizes the commonly held presumption that artistic achievement is "miraculous, nondetermined, and asocial." For 10 points each:
[10] Name this author who argued that despite historians' contemporary focus on artists like Artemisia Gentileschi, there is no female equivalent to Michelangelo in the essay "Why Have There Been No Great Women Artists?"
ANSWER: Linda Nochlin
[10] As an example of how women were historically banned from painting nude subjects, Nochlin cited this artist’s group portrait of the English Royal Academy, in which Angelica Kauffman and Mary Moser appear as paintings on the wall because there are nude male models in the room. This artist also painted The Tribuna of the Uffizi.
ANSWER: Johann Zoffany
[10] Nochlin's essay extensively discussed this artist's career to show that institutional support is necessary for achievement in art. The police granted this artist permission to crossdress in order to paint The Horse Fair.
ANSWER: Rosa Bonheur
B3 2022 NASAT (2022) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
This artist stated that painting "can only consist of the representation of objects which are visible and tangible" in an open letter called the "Realist Manifesto." For 10 points each:
[10] Name this French realist painter who depicted a disinterested crowd at a funeral in A Burial at Ornans.
ANSWER: (Jean Désiré) Gustave Courbet
[10] Critics shamed the central figures in this 1857 realist painting as the "Three Fates of Poverty" in reference to their red, blue, and white bonnets representing the tricolour. Women collect wheat after a harvest in this painting.
ANSWER: The Gleaners [or Des Glaneuses] (by Jean-François Millet)
[10] This other realist painting features twelve oxen in groups of six trudging across a field. This painting may have been inspired by the opening scene of George Sand's novel La Mare au Diable.
ANSWER: Ploughing in the Nivernais [or Laborage Nivernais; or Oxen Plowing in Nevers; or Ploughing in Nivernais] [by Rosa Bonheur]
B4 2019 ACF Nationals (2019) Diff 9 · Fine Arts
Camille Corot painted a reclining Mary Magdalene reading a book in one of his “historical landscapes” set in this place. For 10 points each:
[10] Identify this forest, the site of the world’s first natural preserve, which was repeatedly painted by several artists living in the adjoining village of Barbizon.
ANSWER: Forest of Fontainebleau
[10] This member of the Barbizon school repeatedly sketched old women gathering bundles of twigs from the Forest of Fontainebleau, and painted them stooping down in a field in The Gleaners.
ANSWER: Jean-François Millet
[10] The Château de By at the edge of Fontainebleau Forest housed the studio of this artist, who painted a team of oxen Ploughing in the Nivernais.
ANSWER: Rosa Bonheur [or Marie-Rosalie Bonheur]
B5 2018 Cambridge Open (2018) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
Paul Cézanne complained that this painting “was horribly like the real thing”. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this painting, said to be inspired by the opening scene of George Sand’s novel La Mare au Diable. The title action is performed by twelve Charolais oxen, who work the land in a pastoral landscape.
ANSWER: Ploughing in the Nivernais [also accept Labourage Nivernais or any translation that gets anywhere close to describing ploughing in/of/related to Nevers or the Nivernais]
[10] Ploughing in the Nivernais is a work by this French artist, best known for her realistic depictions of animals. She has come to be viewed as a pioneering feminist of the 1800s, not least for routinely wearing trousers.
ANSWER: Rosa Bonheur
[10] Another painting by Bonheur depicts a “fair” where these animals are being sold. Her depictions were inspired by George Stubbs and Théodore Géricault, who depicted these animals racing in The Derby at Epsom.
ANSWER: (Draft) horses
