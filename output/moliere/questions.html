[10] This playwright of The Pretentious Young Ladies would have played Mascarille in the first few productions. He also wrote Tartuffe and The Misanthrope.
ANSWER: Moliere [or Jean-Baptiste Poquelin]
[10] In this other play, Moliere would have played the pompous Arnolphe, who tries to raise Agnès for his wife without exposing her to the ways of the world.
ANSWER: The School for Wives [or L'École des Femmes]
