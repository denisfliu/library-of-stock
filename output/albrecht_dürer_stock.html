German Renaissance engraver and painter. Known for three 'master prints' (Melencolia I, St. Jerome in His Study, Knight Death and the Devil), the Rhinoceros woodcut, nature studies (Young Hare, Great Piece of Turf), and a Christ-like self-portrait at age 28. Also created The Four Apostles and Feast of the Rosary.

Summary of Facts

Albrecht Dürer (1471-1528) was the preeminent German Renaissance engraver and painter, based in Nuremberg where his godfather Anton Koberger ran the printing industry. He was apprenticed to Michael Wolgemut and contributed illustrations to Hartmann Schedel's Nuremberg Chronicle. He married Agnes Frey (called 'the old crow') and sent a lost self-portrait in gouache on cambric to Raphael. He added a lake in his Landauer Altarpiece as homage to Albrecht Altdorfer.

His three 'master prints' (Meisterstiche) are: Melencolia I (1514), featuring a brooding winged figure with a magic square, hourglass, truncated rhombohedron ('Dürer's solid' with faintly visible skull), emaciated dog, putto scribbling on a chipped grindstone, comet creating a rainbow, and a bat holding a banner with the title. Keys hang from the figure's belt; she wears a wreath of buttercup and watercress and holds a compass on her lap. Erwin Panofsky argued the figure represents one who can imagine Platonic forms but cannot create them, connecting it to Cornelius Agrippa's study. Caspar David Friedrich drew on it, and Gunther Grass discussed it in From the Diary of a Snail. Saint Jerome in His Study (1514) shows the haloed saint writing the Vulgate with a lion and dog sleeping on the floor and a skull on the windowsill. Knight, Death, and the Devil (1513) depicts an armored knight riding past skeletal Death (wreathed with snakes, holding an hourglass, with a plaque reading 'S. 1513') and a goat-headed Devil; a fox-tail may reference Erasmus's Handbook of a Christian Soldier.

His Self-Portrait at Twenty-Eight (1500) is a frontal portrait with Christlike iconography, flowing hair, fur-trimmed coat, and his monogram with the date 1500. The Latin inscription was composed by Conrad Celtes's secretary. Robert Mapplethorpe's photograph of Patti Smith imitates it.

His Four Horsemen of the Apocalypse woodcut, from a series on the Book of Revelation, depicts Death on an emaciated horse with a trident. He created the Feast of the Rosary (Maximilian I crowned by the Virgin Mary), the Large Triumphal Carriage and Triumphal Arch woodcuts for Maximilian I, and collaborated with Matthias Grunewald on the Heller Altarpiece. His Praying Hands drawing on blue paper may have been a study for the Heller Altarpiece — Andy Warhol made a blotted line copy that inspired Warhol's gravestone; Samuel Beckett was obsessed with the image. He created a witches engraving (skull at feet of four naked witches) and a woodcut of a witch riding a goat. His Rhinoceros (1515) woodcut is based on accounts from Lisbon — he never saw the animal, whose body resembles plate armor. His nature studies include Young Hare (Albertina), a stag beetle (Getty), and The Great Piece of Turf. The Four Apostles is a diptych with Luther's Bible translation, at the Alte Pinakothek. Milton Glaser was inspired by Dürer's watercolor of a European Roller.
