Questions: Chevalier de Saint-Georges

1 tossup · 6 bonuses

Tossups

T1 2022 DECAF (2022) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
The opening melody of this composer’s symphony number two, which they also used as an operatic overture, consists of three unison quarter notes and a half note, repeated on each note of an ascending D major chord. Other than the attributed composer, this composer is the likeliest candidate for having composed the basis for or even part of the ballet Les petits riens. This composer is the subject of the second episode of a BBC 4 radio program hosted by (+) Chi-chi Nwanoku. This successor of François-Joseph Gossec as conductor of the Concert des Amateurs had that ensemble sponsored by a Freemason lodge and rebranded as Le Concert Olympique, for which he commissioned Haydn’s (*) Paris symphonies. This composer’s only surviving opera is L’Amant anonyme. Aside from music, this composer was known as “the god of arms” and notoriously bested fencer Alexandre Picard. For 10 points, name this knighted French swordsman and early Black composer.
ANSWER: Joseph Bologne, Chevalier de Saint-Georges [accept either underlined part]

ANSWER: Chevalier de Saint-Georges [or Joseph Bologne; or Joseph Boulogne] (The biopic is Chevalier.)

ANSWER: Joseph Bologne, Chevalier de Saint-Georges [accept either underlined part]
[10] Under the baton of Saint-Georges (“san-ZHORZH”), Le Concert Olympique premiered a set of six symphonies by Haydn named for this French city. Saint-Georges was himself the director of an opera company based in this city before he was removed due to racist objections.
ANSWER: Paris [accept Paris symphonies; accept Opéra de Paris]

ANSWER: Marie Antoinette [or Marie Antoinette Josèphe Jeanne] (The symphony is subtitled “La Reine”, or “The Queen”.)

ANSWER: Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart

T1 2025 ACF Nationals (2025) Diff 9 · Fine Arts
The composer of this set of orchestral works swindled the publishers Artaria and Forster by selling exclusive rights to both companies. It is not known why the third of these works was nicknamed for the three-word Latin phrase that their composer habitually wrote on the dedication page of his scores. The first of these works derives its nickname from the bagpipe-like drone on C in its finale. These are the most performed works premiered by a European conductor of African descent, who led a Masonic orchestra called the Olympic Lodge; that conductor is Chevalier de Saint-Georges. The second of these symphonies derives its nickname from a dotted-rhythm oboe ostinato that sounds like clucking. For 10 points, “The Bear” and “The Hen” are among what group of six symphonies by Joseph Haydn, written the decade before his “London” Symphonies?
ANSWER: Joseph Haydn’s “Paris” Symphonies [or Joseph Haydn’s Symphonies Nos. 82–87; prompt on Joseph Haydn’s symphonies by asking “Which set?”]
T2 2025 ACF Fall (2025) Diff 6 · Fine Arts
A G minor piece of this type includes a dotted oboe passage in its second movement representing an animal’s jerky head movements, and is part of a set of six premiered by conductor Chevalier de Saint-Georges (“san-ZHORZH”). The final movement of one piece of this type features a five-part fugato centered around the notes [read slowly] C, D, F, E. An F sharp minor piece of this type ends with musicians blowing out their candles and leaving the stage. Joseph Haydn (“HIGH-dun”) included a sudden G major chord in the second movement of his 94th piece of this type, allegedly to wake up any sleeping audience members. For 10 points, name these orchestral pieces that include Jupiter by Mozart and Farewell and Surprise by Haydn.
ANSWER: symphony [or symphonies; accept Jupiter Symphony; accept Farewell Symphony; accept Surprise Symphony]
T3 2024 Booster Shot (2024) Diff 7 · Fine Arts
Some of the earliest pieces in this genre were written by the Milanese composer Antonio Brioschi [bree-OS-key], who wrote 26 of them. Seventy-eight of these pieces were written by Giovanni Battista Sammartini, who helped shift this genre away from an earlier “Italian” style. With an ensemble partly named the Olympic Lodge, Chevalier de Saint-Georges [che-val-YAY de sahnt george] premiered a set of six of these pieces in Paris. These pieces originally were (*) three movements and were used as overtures in opera houses prior to the developments of Mannheim School composer Johann Stamitz. Classical-era composers began writing pieces in this genre with an opening sonata, a slow movement, a minuet or scherzo, and an allegro or rondo finale. For 10 points, name this long genre for full orchestra, such as Haydn's “Farewell.”
ANSWER: symphony
T4 2024 ACF Regionals (2024) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
Composer and genre required. One of these pieces by this composer opens with the stormy ascending half notes G, B-flat, C-sharp, and D, followed by dotted figures falling to low D then a dramatic silence. In the Vivace finale of one of these pieces, a drone in the low strings accompanied by gracenotes on the downbeat evokes a Dudelsack used to accompany street performances, giving the piece its nickname. A set of six of these pieces was conducted at their premiere at the Olympic Lodge by Joseph Bologne, Chevalier de Saint-Georges, and subsequently gained nicknames like “The Hen” and “The Bear.” A set of these pieces named for London includes one in which a startling fortissimo chord interrupts its quiet Andante second movement. For 10 points, “Farewell” and “Surprise” are examples of what orchestral pieces written by an Austrian composer?
ANSWER: symphonies by Franz Joseph Haydn [prompt on symphonies or Paris symphonies or London symphonies by asking “by what composer?”]
