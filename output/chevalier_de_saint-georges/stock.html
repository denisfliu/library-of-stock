General / Biographical

Joseph Bologne, Chevalier de Saint-Georges (1745–1799), French Baroque/Classical composer, violinist, conductor, and swordsman. Born in Guadeloupe, the illegitimate son of a slave and a French colonial planter; sent to France as a child. Known as the 'Black Mozart' — though a 2020 New York Times article criticized this label. Also known as 'the god of arms' for his fencing skill; notoriously bested fencer Alexandre Picard. Succeeded François-Joseph Gossec as conductor of the Concert des Amateurs, which he had sponsored by a Freemason lodge and rebranded as Le Concert Olympique (also called the Concert de la Loge Olympique). Commissioned and conducted the premiere of Haydn's Paris Symphonies. His only surviving opera is L'Amant anonyme. Wrote eight symphonies concertantes to showcase his own violin virtuosity. Was appointed director of an opera company in Paris but removed due to racist objections. Marie Antoinette failed to advocate for him as director of the Paris Opera; he played in her private quarters instead. Mozart allegedly based the villain Monostatos in The Magic Flute on him (a 'dubious theory'). Candidate for having composed the basis of the ballet Les petits riens. Summary of Facts

Joseph Bologne, Chevalier de Saint-Georges (1745–1799) was a French composer, violinist, conductor, and swordsman of African descent. He was born in Guadeloupe as the illegitimate son of a French colonial planter and an enslaved African woman, and was sent to France as a child to be educated. He became a celebrated figure in late 18th-century Parisian musical life and was known by two sobriquets: 'the god of arms' for his fencing prowess (he famously bested the champion fencer Alexandre Picard) and 'the Black Mozart' — though a 2020 New York Times article criticized this latter label as reductive.

As a conductor, Saint-Georges succeeded François-Joseph Gossec at the Concert des Amateurs, which he reorganized under Freemason patronage and rebranded as Le Concert Olympique (also called the Concert de la Loge Olympique). In this role, he commissioned Haydn's famous Paris Symphonies (Nos. 82–87) and conducted their premiere. These are considered the most-performed works ever premiered by a European conductor of African descent. As a composer, he wrote eight symphonies concertantes to showcase his own violin virtuosity. His only surviving opera is L'Amant anonyme.

Saint-Georges faced significant racial discrimination. He was appointed director of a Paris opera company but was removed from the position due to racist objections from performers. Marie Antoinette failed to advocate for his placement as director of the Paris Opera; instead, he played violin in her private chambers. Apocryphally, Mozart based the villain Monostatos in The Magic Flute on Saint-Georges (though this theory is widely considered dubious). He is also a candidate for having composed the basis of (or even part of) the ballet Les petits riens. He was the subject of the second episode of a BBC 4 radio program hosted by Chi-chi Nwanoku, and of a 2023 biopic (Chevalier) that fictionalizes a rivalry with Christoph Gluck over the Paris Opera.
