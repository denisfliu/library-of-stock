Russian novelist, playwright, and short story writer of the 19th century. Best known for the novel Fathers and Sons, which features the nihilist medical student Bazarov. Also wrote the short story collection A Sportsman's Sketches (which contributed to the abolition of serfdom), the novellas First Love and Spring Torrents, the novels Smoke and Home of the Gentry (A Nest of Gentlefolk), and the play A Month in the Country. Popularized the term 'superfluous man' with Diary of a Superfluous Man. Gave the speech 'Hamlet and Don Quixote.' Lived in Paris with the mezzo-soprano Pauline Viardot. Had a longstanding feud with Dostoevsky, who caricatured him as the poet Karmazinov in Demons.
Fathers and Sons

Turgenev's most famous novel, in which Arkady Kirsanov brings his nihilist friend, the medical student Yevgeny Bazarov, home to his father's estate Marino. Bazarov duels with Arkady's uncle Pavel (shooting him in the leg) after being caught kissing the servant Fenichka (who has borne a child by Nikolai Kirsanov). Bazarov dies after cutting himself on his own scalpel while treating a typhus patient and failing to cauterize the wound. The novel inspired public debates over nihilism and is by far the most frequently clued Turgenev work. Wikipedia

A Sportsman's Sketches

A short story collection depicting the lives of Russian serfs and peasants, based on Turgenev's experiences at his mother's estate. It contributed to the movement to abolish serfdom and got Turgenev arrested. Published with the help of the editor Nikolay Nekrasov in the journal The Contemporary. Contains notable stories including 'Khor and Kalinych,' 'Hamlet of the Shchigrovsky District,' 'Singers,' 'Bezhin Lea,' 'Kasyan from the Beautiful Lands,' 'A Living Relic,' and others. The narrator meets the landowner Polutikin while hunting birds. Wikipedia

Hamlet and Don Quixote (essay/speech) →

A famous speech/essay by Turgenev comparing the archetypes of Hamlet and Don Quixote, arguing they form 'poles of the human axis.' Hamlet represents egocentrism and solipsism ('cannot love anyone'), while Don Quixote represents foolish altruism and self-sacrifice. Both characters were created in the first decade of the 17th century. Turgenev's interest in Don Quixote was partly inspired by his love for Pauline Viardot. Wikipedia

Summary of Facts

Ivan Turgenev is a major figure in 19th-century Russian literature, clued primarily as the author of Fathers and Sons, which features the nihilist medical student Yevgeny Bazarov. That novel is by far his most frequently referenced work: virtually every tossup on Turgenev ends with Bazarov and Fathers and Sons as the giveaway. Key plot points from Fathers and Sons include Arkady Kirsanov bringing Bazarov to his father's estate Marino, Bazarov's duel with the foppish uncle Pavel (whom he shoots in the leg after being caught kissing the servant Fenichka), and Bazarov's death from a scalpel cut sustained while treating a typhus patient.

Turgenev's second most important work in quizbowl is A Sportsman's Sketches, a short story collection depicting the lives of serfs that contributed to the abolition of serfdom and got Turgenev arrested. Individual stories from this collection appear frequently as power clues: 'Hamlet of the Shchigrovsky District' (a man at a party who identifies as Hamlet, with Vasily Vasilyevich sticking his tongue out at his reflection), 'Singers' (Yakov wins a singing contest while the Gabbler and Blinkard become emotional; the narrator hears a boy calling for 'Antropka'), and 'Khor and Kalinych' (the first story, featuring the landowner Polutikin and two contrasting peasants). Other stories include 'Bezhin Lea' (Pavlusha drives away wolves but later dies falling off a horse), 'Kasyan from the Beautiful Lands' (a dwarf who catches nightingales), and 'A Living Relic' (the immobilized serf Lukerya).

First Love is a frequently clued novella in which the teenager Vladimir falls for Princess Zinaida, only to discover his own father is her secret lover. It is autobiographical, fictionalizing Princess Catherine Shakhovskoy as Zinaida. The father is seen striking Zinaida with a riding crop. Spring Torrents features the confectioner's daughter Gemma Roselli enthralling Sanin in Frankfurt; Hemingway's parody novel took its title from this work.

Turgenev's essay/speech 'Hamlet and Don Quixote' compares the two archetypes as 'poles of the human axis,' contrasting Hamlet's egocentrism with Don Quixote's self-sacrifice. Diary of a Superfluous Man popularized the term 'superfluous man' through the dying aristocrat Tchulkaturin. A Month in the Country is a play about the tutor Aleksei Belyaev and the unhappily married Natalya Petrovna; Stanislavski first deployed his acting system for a revival of it.

The novel Smoke is set in Baden-Baden, where Litvinov finds heliotropes on his windowsill, and satirizes Alexander Herzen via the character Gubaryov. Home of the Gentry (A Nest of Gentlefolk) focuses on Lavretsky and the unfaithful wife Varvara; Goncharov accused Turgenev of plagiarizing The Precipice for it.

Biographically, Turgenev is strongly associated with his relationship with the mezzo-soprano Pauline Viardot, for whom he wrote libretti and with whom he lived in a menage a trois in Paris. His feud with Dostoevsky is notable: Dostoevsky caricatured him as the poet Karmazinov in Demons. Isaiah Berlin wrote about Turgenev's 'complacent ambivalence' and 'the liberal predicament.' He was also jailed for writing an obituary of Gogol.
