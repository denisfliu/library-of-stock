Daphne du Maurier

English Gothic author known for Rebecca, 'The Birds,' 'Don't Look Now,' Jamaica Inn, and My Cousin Rachel. Granddaughter of George du Maurier (author of Trilby). Many works set in Cornwall and adapted by Alfred Hitchcock.
Rebecca

Du Maurier's most famous novel, opening with 'Last night I dreamt I went to Manderley again.' The unnamed narrator (second wife of Maxim de Winter) meets Maxim through Mrs. Van Hopper at Monte Carlo. At the Cornwall estate Manderley, she is tormented by the sinister housekeeper Mrs. Danvers, who is obsessed with the first wife Rebecca. The narrator wears a dead woman's dress to a ball at Mrs. Danvers's suggestion, causing a commotion. Rebecca's body is found in a sunken ship. Dr. Baker reveals Rebecca had terminal cancer and was infertile, confirming she lied to Maxim the night he killed her. Mrs. Danvers burns down Manderley at the end. The narrator chases the dog Jasper, who leads her to the mentally handicapped gardener Ben. Sally Beauman wrote a sequel. Accused of plagiarizing Carolina Nabuco. Modeled on Jane Eyre. Adapted by Hitchcock in 1940.

The Birds

A short story about Cornish war veteran Nat Hocken whose family is attacked by birds that assault only at high tide. It ends with 'the tearing sound of splintering wood' as Nat smokes his 'last cigarette' and turns on a silent wireless radio. The story begins on December 3rd when 'the wind changed overnight and it was winter.' The Triggs die during an attack. Sometimes read as an allegory for the Blitz. Adapted into a film by Alfred Hitchcock.

Don't Look Now

John and Laura travel to Venice after their daughter Christine's death. Laura discovers a pair of elderly psychic twins, one of whom is a blind clairvoyant. John sees his wife on a vaporetto with the twins. His 'gift' of second sight fails to save him from being stabbed by a red pixie-hooded dwarf. The story ends with John thinking 'what a bloody silly way to die.'

Jamaica Inn and Other Works

Jamaica Inn follows Mary Yellan discovering that the vicar Francis Davey (who draws himself as a wolf preying on a congregation of sheep) leads a gang of ship-sinking 'wreckers' including her uncle Joss Merlyn, keeper of the title Cornish guesthouse. My Cousin Rachel: Philip Ashley suspects the title character of poisoning his father Ambrose, aided by the dubious medical advisor Rainaldi. The House on the Strand features Dick traveling to the 14th century and wooing Isolda; Magnus Lane develops a time-travel drug. The Scapegoat involves a lecturer named John whose identity is stolen at a Le Mans rail station. Frenchman's Creek: Dona dresses as a boy to join her lover. Du Maurier was cousin of the Davies boys who inspired J.M. Barrie's Peter Pan stories. Grandfather George du Maurier wrote Trilby (about the hypnotist Svengali and tone-deaf singer) and The Martian.

Summary of Facts

Daphne du Maurier was an English Gothic and suspense author whose works are frequently set in her adopted Cornwall. She was the granddaughter of George du Maurier, author of Trilby (featuring the manipulative hypnotist Svengali). Many of her works were adapted into films by Alfred Hitchcock.

Rebecca is her most famous novel, opening with the iconic line 'Last night I dreamt I went to Manderley again.' The unnamed narrator meets Maxim de Winter through Mrs. Van Hopper at Monte Carlo. At the Cornwall estate Manderley, she is tormented by the sinister housekeeper Mrs. Danvers, obsessed with the dead first wife Rebecca. The narrator wears Rebecca's dress to a ball at Danvers's suggestion. Rebecca's body is discovered in a sunken ship, and an inquest reveals her terminal cancer and infertility, confirming she manipulated Maxim. Mrs. Danvers burns down Manderley.

'The Birds' follows Cornish veteran Nat Hocken defending his family against birds that attack at high tide, ending as he smokes his 'last cigarette' listening to 'the tearing sound of splintering wood.' 'Don't Look Now' takes John and Laura to Venice after their daughter's death; John is killed by a knife-throwing dwarf in a red pixie hood after encountering psychic elderly twins.

Jamaica Inn features Mary Yellan discovering the vicar Francis Davey leads ship-sinking 'wreckers.' My Cousin Rachel follows Philip Ashley suspecting the title character of poisoning his father Ambrose. The House on the Strand involves time travel to the 14th century. Du Maurier was cousin of the Davies boys who inspired J.M. Barrie's Peter Pan.
