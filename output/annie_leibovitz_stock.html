Summary of Facts

Annie Leibovitz (born 1949) is an American celebrity and portrait photographer who became famous as chief photographer for Rolling Stone and later as the leading portrait photographer for Vanity Fair. Her most iconic photograph is the 1980 image of a nude John Lennon curled in a fetal position around his fully clothed wife Yoko Ono, taken hours before Lennon was shot by Mark David Chapman. This image was published on the cover of Rolling Stone and has been widely recreated and referenced (notably by Sam Taylor-Wood and Henry Bond in '26 October 1993').

For Vanity Fair, Leibovitz created a series of celebrated and controversial covers: the 1991 cover of a nude, seven-months-pregnant Demi Moore; the 1992 'Birthday Suit' cover showing Moore in body paint; a 2008 cover of LeBron James and Gisele Bundchen that controversially evoked the WWI recruitment poster 'Destroy This Mad Brute'; and the 2015 cover depicting Caitlyn Jenner in a white dress with the caption 'Call Me Caitlyn.' She also took a controversial topless photo of 15-year-old Miley Cyrus in 2008, and photographed Whoopi Goldberg submerged in a tub of milk.

Leibotvitz documented the Bosnian War while traveling to Sarajevo with her long-term partner, writer Susan Sontag. There she photographed 'Bloody Bicycle' (also called 'Fallen Bicycle of Teenage Boy Just Killed by a Sniper'), showing an overturned bicycle with a blood trail on the street. Other notable work includes a nude portrait of Keith Haring standing atop a paint-covered coffee table that blended into his similarly patterned living room, and the Born in the U.S.A. album cover for Bruce Springsteen showing a man in jeans facing an American flag.

Biographically, Leibovitz climbed Mount Fuji after purchasing her first camera — a Minolta SR-T 101 — as recounted in her memoir At Work. She emulated Margaret Bourke-White by mounting the eagle gargoyle of the Chrysler Building to photograph dancer David Parsons. She photographed the cottage of 19th-century photographer Julia Margaret Cameron in her book Pilgrimage. She also photographed Susan Sontag in a bear suit. For the 2016 Pirelli Calendar, she chose subjects like Serena Williams and Amy Schumer instead of traditional models.
