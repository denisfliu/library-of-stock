The Remains of the Day

The most frequently clued Ishiguro novel, appearing in virtually every tossup as the standard giveaway. It follows the butler Stevens on a motoring trip lent by his new American employer Mr. Farraday to visit the former housekeeper Miss Kenton (now Mrs. Benn). Stevens reflects on his time serving the Nazi sympathizer Lord Darlington at Darlington Hall. Key clues include Stevens's attempts to master 'bantering,' his concept of 'dignity' (illustrated by the tiger-under-the-table story), and the moment when Miss Kenton says there is 'no turning back the clock now' and Stevens realizes 'my heart was breaking.' Stevens defends Darlington against allegations of Nazi sympathy for hosting Herr Ribbentrop. His father drops a tray on the steps and inspects them 'as if to find a precious jewel.' Stevens is caught reading a sentimental romance novel and claims he is trying to 'develop his control of the English language.' The Hayes Society and the radio show Twice a Week or More appear. Reginald Cardinal is angry at his godfather. The novel won the Booker Prize in 1989 and the Whitbread Prize for An Artist of the Floating World in 1986.

Never Let Me Go

The second most frequently clued Ishiguro novel, a dystopian work about clones raised to be organ donors at Hailsham boarding school. Kathy H is the narrator and 'carer,' Tommy is her love interest, and Ruth drives them apart. The title comes from a fictional song by Judy Bridgewater on a cassette tape that Kathy finds. Key clues include the students' search for 'possibles' (their genetic originals), their hope for 'deferrals' by proving they are in love, the Gallery where Madame collects student art to prove clones have souls, and the division into 'carers' and 'donors.' Madame tearfully watches Kathy pretend to cradle a baby while listening to the Bridgewater song. Tommy 'completes' his organ donations. Norfolk is where lost things supposedly go, and the characters find the cassette there.

Klara and the Sun

Josie and her neighbor Rick play the 'bubble game' filling in speech bubbles in each other's drawings. Children are 'lifted' by genetic enhancement. Klara sees the world in pixelated images and believes the sun sets behind Mr. McBain's barn. The novel appears on Barack Obama's 2021 Summer Reading List. Josie unknowingly models for the likeness of an android.

An Artist of the Floating World

Ono's grandson does Lone Ranger impressions instead of imitating his own nation's heroes. Ono turns the painting 'Complacency' into propaganda titled 'Eyes on the Horizon.' He created the Migi-Hidari restaurant. His daughter's marriage is delayed because her father created propaganda posters during the 'China crisis.'

The Buried Giant

A mysterious 'mist' of forgetfulness settles over the land, caused by the dragon Querig's breath. The Saxon warrior Wistan kills Sir Gawain in a duel and slays Querig, ending the amnesia. The novel ends with a couple telling their memories to a boatman who refuses to ferry both to an island. James Wood's review called its allegory 'too literal and too vague.'

General / Biographical

Ishiguro is a Japanese-born British author who won the 2017 Nobel Prize in Literature. He writes lyrics for jazz singer Stacey Kent and collaborated on The Summer We Crossed Europe in the Rain. His daughter Naomi Ishiguro wrote the collection Escape Routes, including the novella The Rat Catcher. He wrote screenplays including The Gourmet (about eating a ghost) and an adaptation of Akira Kurosawa's Ikiru. His short story collection is Nocturnes: Five Stories of Music and Nightfall.

Summary of Facts

Kazuo Ishiguro is a Japanese-born British novelist who won the 2017 Nobel Prize in Literature and the 1989 Booker Prize for The Remains of the Day. In quizbowl, he is overwhelmingly identified by The Remains of the Day, which appears in virtually every tossup as the giveaway. This novel follows the butler Stevens on a motoring trip lent by his new American employer Mr. Farraday to visit the former housekeeper Miss Kenton (now Mrs. Benn) at Darlington Hall. Stevens reflects on his service to the Nazi sympathizer Lord Darlington, who hosted Herr Ribbentrop. Stevens's defining trait is his pursuit of 'dignity,' illustrated by his story of a man discovering a tiger under a dining table. His attempts to master 'bantering' and his emotional repression — such as continuing to work while his father dies — are frequently clued. The moment when Miss Kenton says 'no turning back the clock now' and Stevens realizes 'my heart was breaking' is a common power clue.

Never Let Me Go is the second most important work, a dystopian novel about clones Kathy, Tommy, and Ruth raised at Hailsham boarding school to become organ donors. The title comes from a fictional Judy Bridgewater song on a cassette tape. Key concepts include 'carers' and 'donors,' 'possibles' (genetic originals the clones search for), 'deferrals' (which Madame reveals never existed), and the Gallery of student art meant to prove clones have souls. Madame tearfully watches Kathy pretend to cradle a baby while listening to the Bridgewater song. Norfolk is where lost things supposedly end up.

Klara and the Sun (2021) features the solar-powered Artificial Friend Klara who companions the sickly Josie. Klara destroys a 'Cootings Machine' to cure Josie, and the children play a 'bubble game' with speech bubbles. Children are 'lifted' by genetic enhancement. The Buried Giant is set in post-Arthurian Britain where amnesia-causing mist from the dragon Querig covers the land; elderly couple Axl and Beatrice search for their son before Wistan kills Querig and Gawain. The Unconsoled follows the pianist Ryder in an unnamed European city, with the drunken conductor Brodsky and his ironing-board crutch. An Artist of the Floating World features the painter Masuji Ono in post-WWII Japan, drinking at Mrs. Kawakami's bar, reflecting on his propaganda work. When We Were Orphans centers on detective Christopher Banks investigating his parents' kidnapping in Shanghai. A Pale View of Hills is Ishiguro's debut about Etsuko and Sachiko in post-war Nagasaki.

Beyond novels, Ishiguro writes lyrics for jazz singer Stacey Kent and wrote screenplays including The Gourmet and an Ikiru adaptation. His daughter Naomi Ishiguro wrote the collection Escape Routes.
