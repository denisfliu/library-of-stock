Summary of Facts

Ford Madox Ford is clued as an English modernist novelist best known for The Good Soldier and the Parade's End tetralogy. The Good Soldier opens with the famous line "This is the saddest story I have ever heard" and is narrated by John Dowell about the entangled affairs of two couples at the German spa town of Nauheim. Florence Dowell fakes a heart ailment to conceal her affair with Edward Ashburnham and eventually commits suicide by drinking prussic acid. John Rodker called it "the finest French novel in the English language."

Parade's End follows Christopher Tietjens, called "the last Tory" and "the most brilliant man in England," who works in the Imperial Department of Statistics. His manipulative wife Sylvia torments him throughout, while he develops a relationship with the suffragette Valentine Wannop. Sylvia plots to cut down the Great Tree of Groby, a symbol of Tietjens's family. The tetralogy's third volume, A Man Could Stand Up--, is set on Armistice Day. Ford's real-life relationship with Jean Rhys partly inspired the Tietjens-Valentine dynamic; Rhys fictionalized her side of the story in her novel Quartet.

Ford collaborated extensively with Joseph Conrad on The Inheritors (a science fiction novel about Fourth Dimensionists taking over Earth) and The Nature of a Crime. He founded both The English Review and The Transatlantic Review, helping to launch careers of writers like D.H. Lawrence and Hemingway. Hemingway memorably described Ford as annoying and smelly in A Moveable Feast. Ford also championed literary impressionism, more explicitly than Conrad, and wrote The Fifth Queen trilogy about Catherine Howard.
