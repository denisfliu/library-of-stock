Clarice Lispector

Ukrainian-born Brazilian novelist and short story writer (1920-1977), known for introspective, existential fiction with stream-of-consciousness techniques. Her most frequently clued works are The Passion According to G.H. (a woman kills a cockroach and eats it) and The Hour of the Star (Macabea is hit by a yellow Mercedes). Her short story collection Family Ties includes 'The Smallest Woman in the World' and 'Love.' Her debut novel Near to the Wild Heart was published at age 23. Often compared to Virginia Woolf. The subject of Helene Cixous's essay 'The Hour of Clarice Lispector.' Also wrote the genre of cronicas (short newspaper columns).
The Passion According to G.H.

Lispector's most frequently clued novel. The title character, a wealthy sculptor, enters the room of her former Black maid Janair and finds charcoal drawings of a man, a woman, and a dog on the wall. She kills a cockroach (by slamming the wardrobe door on it) and has an existential/mystic crisis, eventually putting the cockroach's entrails in her mouth. G.H. describes the maid's empty room as 'a violation of my quotation marks' that makes her 'a citation of herself.' She compares her past abortion to squashing a cockroach. The cockroach is also the subject of Lispector's 'Five Stories on a Single Theme' and 'The Fifth Story,' which give different accounts of cockroach killings. Wikipedia

The Hour of the Star

A novella about Macabea, a poor typist from Northeast Brazil who lives on a diet of hot dogs and Coca-Cola. She learns facts from a Clock Radio. Narrated by Rodrigo, who chronicles her life and death. A European man's promise is represented by the yellow Mercedes that runs her over after she visits the fortune teller Madame Carlota, who predicts she will meet a wealthy foreigner named Hans. Contrasts Brazil's Northeast and Southeast regions. The subject of Helene Cixous's essay 'The Hour of Clarice Lispector.' Wikipedia

Near to the Wild Heart

Lispector's debut novel, published in 1943 at age 23. Narrates the inner life of Joana (also called Joan), a Spinoza-loving woman, through episodes from her childhood and her marriage to her adulterous husband Otavio. The protagonist slaps herself while bathing after her aunt calls her a 'viper' for stealing a book. Summary of Facts

Clarice Lispector (1920-1977) was a Ukrainian-born Brazilian novelist and short story writer, one of the most important figures in Brazilian and Latin American literature. She was a Ukrainian-Brazilian Jewish woman frequently compared to Virginia Woolf for her use of stream of consciousness. She wrote in Portuguese and set many of her works in Brazil.

Her most frequently clued work is The Passion According to G.H., about a wealthy sculptor who enters the room of her former Black maid Janair and finds charcoal drawings of a man, woman, and dog on the wall. G.H. kills a cockroach by slamming the wardrobe door on it and has an existential/mystic crisis, eventually putting the cockroach's entrails in her mouth. She describes the maid's empty room as 'a violation of my quotation marks' that makes her 'a citation of herself,' and compares her past abortion to squashing a cockroach. The cockroach motif also appears in Lispector's 'Five Stories on a Single Theme' (where she imagines cockroaches as statues made of Parisian plaster) and 'The Fifth Story' (five increasingly bizarre accounts of cockroach killings), both collected as cronicas in the 2018 volume Too Much of Life.

The Hour of the Star is a novella about Macabea, a poor typist from Northeast Brazil who lives on hot dogs and Coca-Cola and learns facts from a Clock Radio. Narrated by Rodrigo, it contrasts Brazil's Northeast and Southeast regions. After visiting the fortune teller Madame Carlota, who predicts she will meet a wealthy foreigner named Hans, Macabea is run over by a yellow Mercedes. A man shows off by lifting her over his head and drops her into mud. Helene Cixous wrote the essay 'The Hour of Clarice Lispector' about this author.

Family Ties is her major short story collection. 'The Smallest Woman in the World' features French explorer Marcel Pretre discovering a 45-centimeter-tall pregnant Likouala (pygmy) woman nicknamed 'Little Flower'; various readers react to the newspaper article, including an old woman who says 'God knows what He's doing' and a girl who concludes 'misfortune has no limit.' 'Love' follows Ana, who breaks a package of eggs on a tram when she sees a blind man chewing gum. The title story features Catherine awkwardly saying goodbye to her mother Severina, who obsesses over her suitcases, while Tony addresses his mother-in-law by her first name.

Near to the Wild Heart was her debut novel, published in 1943 at age 23. It narrates the inner life of Joana, a Spinoza-loving woman, through her childhood and her marriage to adulterous husband Otavio. The protagonist slaps herself while bathing after her aunt calls her a 'viper' for stealing a book. The title was suggested by Lucio Cardoso from James Joyce's A Portrait of the Artist as a Young Man, which Lispector had not read.

Other works include: The Apple in the Dark (Martim's journey after possibly killing his wife); A Breath of Life; a novel that abruptly ends with a colon as philosophy teacher Ulisses is about to speak; a story about a girl who bullies the narrator by delaying lending a book ('I was no longer a girl with a book: I was a woman with her lover'); a story where a mathematics professor buries and unburies a dead dog. A novel begins with the 'How a Man is Made' section and depicts a man abandoning language in 'The Birth of the Hero.' Lispector attended the World Congress of Sorcery. Her complete stories were translated by Katrina Dodson, who noted that most photos of Lispector actually show lookalikes like Rita Elmor, who starred in a one-woman play about the author.
