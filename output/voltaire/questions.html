Questions: Voltaire

3 tossups · 7 bonuses

Tossups

T1 2025 ACF Regionals (2025) Diff 8 · Philosophy
An essay by this thinker imagines the “obstinate pride” needed to say that there are “nine hundred million little ants” on earth but “only my anthill is beloved of God.” Mikhail Bakunin’s God and the State uses the word “abolish” in an inversion of a quote by this thinker. This thinker’s critiques of dogmatic authority include the claim that “superstition sets the world in flame,” the plea to “crush the infamous,” and his Treatise on Tolerance. This thinker wrote that “if God did not exist, it would be necessary to invent him.” This author of Letters on the English and the Philosophical Dictionary created a character who rationalizes his syphilis and the Lisbon earthquake. For 10 points, what author parodied Leibniz’s Theodicy via the character Pangloss in his novel Candide?
ANSWER: Voltaire [or François-Marie d’Arouet] (Bakunin wrote that “If God really existed, it would be necessary to abolish him.”)
T2 2021 Illinois Open (2021) Diff 10 · Philosophy
In one essay, this thinker imagined a conversation with an Imam, a Buddhist priest, and a Christian who declares "my ant-hole is the only one dear to God; all the others are cast off by Him for eternity." In another work, this thinker claimed that China had "perfected moral science" and argued for integrating Chinese social structures into Europe. This thinker criticized Descartes' "shining and strong imagination," which he negatively compared to the rational thought of (*) Isaac Newton. This thinker argued that belief in an afterlife was necessary for preventing criminal behavior in his commentary on The Three Imposters by saying "if God did not exist, it would be necessary to invent him." For 10 points, name this author of the Philosophical Letters, who satirized Leibniz's belief that we live in the "best of all possible worlds" in his novel Candide.
ANSWER: Voltaire [or François-Marie d’Arouet]
T3 2014 NASAT (2014) Diff 8 · Philosophy
This thinker used the example of Jean Calas as evidence that all men should be brothers in his Treatise on Toleration. Historiographers credit this thinker's Essay on the Universal History, the Manners, and the Spirit of the Nations for the study of social history. This man frequently used the phrase "Crush the infamy." He popularized the myth of Newton’s apple and also fought for inoculation in his Philosophical Letters on the English. He wrote that, “If God did not exist, it would be necessary to invent him.” This author of a Philosophical Dictionary satirized theodicy in a book rationalizing that we "must cultivate our garden." For 10 points, name this Enlightenment wit who wrote Candide.
ANSWER: Voltaire [or Francois-Marie Arouet]
