Summary of Facts

Vasily Vereshchagin was a Russian painter of anti-war works, a contemporary of Ilya Repin. He is most known for The Apotheosis of War (1871), depicting a pyramid of skulls in a desert landscape, dedicated 'to all conquerors, past, present and to come.' It is part of his Turkestan series documenting Russian military campaigns in Central Asia.
