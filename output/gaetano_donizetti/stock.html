Italian bel canto opera composer (1797–1848). Best known for Lucia di Lammermoor, The Elixir of Love, The Daughter of the Regiment, and Don Pasquale. Grouped with Bellini and Rossini as the three great bel canto composers. Known for prolific output — Berlioz quipped one could no longer speak of Paris's opera houses, only Donizetti's. His Tudor Queens trilogy (Anna Bolena, Maria Stuarda, Roberto Devereux) and the 'mad scene' from Lucia are staples of the soprano repertoire.
Lucia di Lammermoor →

Donizetti's most famous opera, based on Walter Scott's novel about Lucy Ashton. Contains the celebrated 'mad scene' (aria 'Il dolce suono'), the sextet 'Chi mi frena in tal momento,' and the soprano showpiece 'Ardon gli incensi.' The lead role is written for coloratura soprano. The opera begins with Normanno searching for an intruder (Edgardo) and ends with Edgardo killing himself. Lucia is tricked into signing a marriage contract to Arturo, whom she then murders. The Elixir of Love (L'elisir d'amore)

Comic opera in which the peasant Nemorino loves Adina. The charlatan Dr. Dulcamara sells Nemorino cheap Bordeaux wine disguised as a love potion. Contains the famous tenor aria 'Una furtiva lagrima,' in which Nemorino sees a tear in Adina's eye and believes she has fallen for him. Other characters include the soldier Belcore and Giannetta. The Daughter of the Regiment (La Fille du Regiment)

French-language opera comique set during the Napoleonic wars. Marie is raised as an army regiment's canteen girl. Tonio joins the regiment to marry Marie and sings 'Ah! mes amis,' which requires the tenor to hit nine high Cs — called the 'Mount Everest' of tenor arias. Also contains the aria 'Chacun le sait.' Marie gets distracted playing piano with the Marquise of Birkenfeld, who is revealed to be her mother. Ruth Bader Ginsburg had a non-singing cameo as the Duchess of Krakenthorp in a 2016 production. Don Pasquale

General / Biographical

Donizetti is one of the three great bel canto composers alongside Bellini and Rossini. He was prolific — Berlioz complained 'one can no longer speak of the opera houses of Paris, but only of Donizetti's opera houses.' His mentor was Simon Mayr. He left Naples for Paris after censors refused to allow his production of Poliuto. He also wrote Dom Sebastien (libretto by Eugene Scribe) and Marino Faliero. The bel canto style is characterized by dramatic melodic lines and smooth vocal legato (Italian for 'beautiful singing').

Summary of Facts

Gaetano Donizetti is one of the most frequently clued opera composers in quizbowl, appearing across all difficulty levels. He is almost always identified through his three most famous operas: Lucia di Lammermoor, The Elixir of Love, and The Daughter of the Regiment, with Don Pasquale as a frequent fourth.

Lucia di Lammermoor is his most iconic work and the most common giveaway identifier. The 'mad scene' (aria 'Il dolce suono') appears in nearly every tossup. Other Lucia clues include the sextet 'Chi mi frena in tal momento,' the excerpt 'Ardon gli incensi' (with its flute cadenza associated with Joan Sutherland's Met debut), the Walter Scott source novel about Lucy Ashton, the characters Normanno and Edgardo, and the use of 'Home Sweet Home' themes. The lead role is a coloratura soprano.

The Daughter of the Regiment is the second most commonly clued opera. Its signature clue is the tenor aria 'Ah! mes amis,' which requires nine high Cs and has been called the 'Mount Everest' of tenor arias. The plot involves Marie, a canteen girl raised by an army regiment, and Tonio, who joins the regiment to marry her. The aria 'Chacun le sait' and the role of the Marquise of Birkenfeld (revealed to be Marie's mother) appear at higher difficulties. A memorable bonus clue notes Ruth Bader Ginsburg's cameo as the Duchess of Krakenthorp in a 2016 production. Notably, despite Donizetti being Italian, this opera is in French.

The Elixir of Love centers on Nemorino's love for Adina and the quack Dr. Dulcamara, who sells cheap Bordeaux wine as a love potion. The aria 'Una furtiva lagrima' is the most common identifier for this opera, with Nemorino seeing a tear in Adina's eye. Power-mark clues include Dulcamara's sales pitch aria 'Udite, udite, o rustici,' and Giannetta's scene involving the Tristan and Isolde love story.

Don Pasquale appears frequently in bonuses. It is an opera buffa about an old bachelor who consults Dr. Malatesta and tries to disinherit his nephew Ernesto. Norina is identified as a classic soubrette role.

The Tudor Queens trilogy — Anna Bolena, Maria Stuarda, and Roberto Devereux — appears across multiple questions. Beverly Sills performing all three at the New York City Opera in 1971 and making the cover of Time is a key clue. Montserrat Caballe's 22-second pianissimo in Maria Stuarda and the confrontation scene ('impure daughter,' 'vile bastard') are power clues. Roberto Devereux features Elizabeth I and a blue scarf. Anna Bolena features Smeton's dropped locket.

Biographical clues include: Donizetti is grouped with Bellini and Rossini as the three bel canto masters (the 1950s bel canto revival is credited to him); Berlioz's quip about Donizetti's prolific output; his mentor Simon Mayr; his departure from Naples to Paris after censors blocked Poliuto; his less famous works Dom Sebastien (libretto by Scribe) and Marino Faliero; and his brother Giuseppe Donizetti ('Donizetti Pasha'), who composed the Ottoman Empire's first national anthem.
