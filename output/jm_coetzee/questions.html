[10] Many of this author’s writings are set in Serowe, and she wrote of the forced divisions between Bushmen and other indigenous Africans in a novel in which a child is adopted by the hypocritical Margaret Cadmore.
ANSWER: Bessie Head
[10] This South African writer drew heavily upon Dostoyevsky’s The Possessed for his The Master of Petersburg, and wrote about a woman who rewrites Ulysses from the perspective of Molly Bloom in Elizabeth Costello.
ANSWER: John Maxwell Coetzee
[10] This other South African author wrote about the corrupt policeman Pieter Vlaanderen in Too Late the Phalarope and about Stephen Kumalo’s relentless search for his son Absalom in Cry, the Beloved Country.
ANSWER: Alan Paton

For 10 points each, answer these questions about literary reworkings.
[10] Jean Rhys’ Wide Sargasso Sea was a prequel to this Charlotte Brontë novel, which ends with the narrator in love with the blind – but single – Mr. Rochester.
ANSWER: Jane Eyre
[10] This author of Disgrace rewrote Robinson Crusoe from the perspective of a woman, Susan Barton, in Foe.
ANSWER: J (ohn) M (axwell) Coetzee
[10] Alice Randall got sued for writing this 2001 novel, which tells a well-known story from the perspective of a slave named Cynara.
ANSWER: The Wind Done Gone [based on Gone with the Wind]
