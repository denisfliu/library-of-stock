Andrea Chénier

Verismo opera by Umberto Giordano (1896), libretto by Luigi Illica. Set during the French Revolution. The title character is a real historical poet who falls in love with the noblewoman Maddalena de Coigny; the revolutionary Gerard (formerly a servant in her household) also loves her. Both Andrea and Maddalena go to the guillotine together at the end. Opens with Gerard adjusting furniture while his father sings 'Son sessant'anni' about the suffering of servants. The spy 'Incredibile' sees through the maid Bersi's disguise as a revolutionary. They meet at the Café Hottot. Was once staged on a floating stage on Lake Constance. The Maria Callas recording of 'La mamma morta' was used in the film Philadelphia. Wikipedia

Summary of Facts

Andrea Chénier is a verismo opera in four acts by Umberto Giordano (1896), with a libretto by Luigi Illica. It is set during the French Revolution and is based loosely on the life of the real French poet André Chénier. The opera opens in the de Coigny household, where the young revolutionary servant Gerard (baritone) adjusts furniture while his elderly father sings 'Son sessant'anni' about a lifetime of servitude. At a party, the poet Andrea Chénier (tenor) improvises an aria contrasting the beauty of nature with the suffering of the lower classes — 'Un dì all'azzurro spazio,' also known as 'L'Improvviso,' which opens with a solo cello on G# before moving to B#-C# eighth notes. Maddalena de Coigny (soprano), a noblewoman, is moved by his words.

In Act II, the spy 'Incredibile' sees through the disguise of Maddalena's maid Bersi at the Café Hottot. In a Cyrano-esque moment, Maddalena reveals she has been sending anonymous love letters to Andrea, and the two pledge their love in the duet 'Ora soave.' In Act III, Gerard (now a powerful revolutionary official) signs a denunciation of Andrea before questioning his own motives in the baritone aria 'Nemico della Patria' ('Enemy of the Fatherland'). Maddalena then sings 'La mamma morta,' the opera's most celebrated aria, in which she describes the burning of her home, the death of her mother, and her years of poverty until love brought her back to life — and declares she is willing to give herself to Gerard to save Andrea. The aria contains the lines 'A dying body is my body / So take it. / I am already dead matter.' The Maria Callas recording of this aria was used in Jonathan Demme's 1993 film Philadelphia. Despite Gerard's regret, the Revolutionary Tribunal sentences Andrea to the guillotine. Maddalena insists on going to the scaffold with him. The opera ends with both characters being executed.

Andrea Chénier is frequently cited as a canonical example of verismo opera, alongside works by Mascagni, Leoncavallo, and Cilea. It was once memorably staged on a floating platform on Lake Constance.
