Tossups

T1 2024 ACF Winter (2024) Diff 7 · Fine Arts
Birds and smoke from a painting by this artist begin to move in the first shot of Abbas Kiarostami’s posthumous film 24 Frames. A painting by this artist returns to the subject of a drawing in which a large fish disgorges smaller fish that in turn disgorge even smaller fish. This artist’s craggy “world landscapes” in the style of Joachim Patinir (“YO-ah-keem PAH-tee-neer”) include his Parable of the Sower and a Flight Into Egypt held at London’s Courtauld Gallery. A roof is tiled with tarts in a dense, zany painting by this artist that illustrates idioms like “biting the column.” This artist’s Months of the Year series includes a winter landscape in which foreground figures carry just a single fox. For 10 points, what Flemish artist depicted peasants in genre paintings like Netherlandish Proverbs and The Hunters in the Snow?
ANSWER: Pieter Bruegel the Elder
T2 2022 ACF Winter (2022) Diff 7 · Fine Arts

In a painting by this artist, a man carrying a dead fox and his companion walk on a hill in the foreground. In that painting by this artist, people skate on a lake in the distance while dogs trail the title figures in a winter landscape. For 10 points, name this Flemish artist of The Hunters in the Snow.
ANSWER: Pieter Bruegel the Elder [or Pieter Brueghel the Elder; accept Pieter Bruegel and the Art of Laughter; reject “Pieter Brueghel the Younger” or other Brueghels]
