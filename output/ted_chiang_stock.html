Story of Your Life

Chiang's best-known work, adapted into the film Arrival. Linguist Dr. Louise Banks nicknames two heptapod aliens Flapper and Raspberry and works to learn the language Heptapod B. The story explores the Sapir-Whorf hypothesis and Fermat's principle of least time. It begins and ends with Banks telling her daughter about her husband asking 'Do you want to make a baby?' By learning Heptapod B, Banks is able to perceive her entire life at once.

Hell is the Absence of God

A story in which Janice Reilly is given legs during an angelic visit and goes on a pilgrimage with the minister Ethan Mead. A character dying in a car crash is struck by a beam of light from Heaven inspiring him to love God, but God sends him to Hell anyway. Ken Liu's 'Single-Bit Error' was written in response.

Summary of Facts

Ted Chiang is a Chinese-American science fiction author whose works appear primarily at higher difficulty levels in quizbowl. His most commonly clued story by far is 'Story of Your Life,' the basis for the film Arrival. In it, linguist Dr. Louise Banks nicknames two heptapod aliens Flapper and Raspberry and works to learn the language Heptapod B, exploring the Sapir-Whorf hypothesis and Fermat's principle of least time. The story begins and ends with Banks telling her daughter about her husband's question 'Do you want to make a baby?' and by learning Heptapod B, she perceives her entire life simultaneously.

'Tower of Babylon' is the second most frequently clued work, featuring Hillalum, a miner from Elam, who climbs the title structure with a caravan. After tunneling through what he thinks is the vault of heaven, he finds himself back in Shinar, as the universe is shaped like a seal cylinder. 'What's Expected of Us' introduces the Predictor, a device proving free will is an illusion by flashing a second before you press its button, creating 'a legion of Bartleby the Scriveners.'

The title story of the collection Exhalation features an air-powered being who dissects its own brain and discovers flowing currents of argon, with memories engraved on gold foil. 'Hell is the Absence of God' features a character who loves God after being struck by heavenly light during a car crash but is sent to Hell anyway. 'Liking What You See: A Documentary' depicts Pembleton College voting on calliagnosia technology to eliminate lookism. 'Anxiety is the Dizziness of Freedom,' titled from a Kierkegaard quote, involves a prism for communicating with alternate selves.
