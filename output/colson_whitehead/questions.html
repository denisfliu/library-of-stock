T1 2025 ACF Nationals (2025) Diff 9 · Literature
Description acceptable.While dying, a man of this profession dictates a passage about a “shining beacon… born of necessity and virtue between the hammer and the anvil,” which he calls the “American imperative.” That man of this profession grapples with finding the “Great Spirit” in his work, like his blacksmith father, and travels with the young boy Homer. After he is shot by George Harris, a man of this profession is nursed back to health at a Quaker settlement, where he renounces his past evils. Schoolteacher, his nephew, a sheriff, and a man of this profession comprise the “four horsemen” in Toni Morrison’s Beloved. The evil man Ridgeway, who has this profession for hire, pursues Caesar and Cora in a novel by Colson Whitehead. For 10 points, name this profession of Tom Loker, who is hired to find Eliza after her escape, in Uncle Tom’s Cabin.
ANSWER: slave catchers [or slave hunters; accept descriptions of people who retrieve runaway slaves; prompt on bounty hunters or fugitive huntersby asking “whom do they pursue?”; reject “enslaver,” “slaveowner,” “slave master,” or “slave trader”] (The Colson Whitehead novel is The Underground Railroad.)
