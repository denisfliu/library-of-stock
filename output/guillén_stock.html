Summary of Facts

Nicolás Guillén (full name Nicolás Cristóbal Guillén Batista) was a Cuban poet and journalist of African descent, declared Cuba's national poet by the Castro government in 1961, a position he held for over two decades. He is overwhelmingly identified in quizbowl through his poem "Sensemayá" (also known as "Chant for Killing a Snake"), which features the onomatopoeic refrain "Mayombe-bombe-mayombe" and describes a snake-killing ritual. In that poem, a figure with "eyes of glass" "coils on a stick" and "can move without feet," and the speaker instructs "Hit him with an ax and he dies." The poem inspired a musical composition of the same name by Mexican composer Silvestre Revueltas, which begins with a bass clarinet trill in 4/8 time. Eartha Kitt recorded a reading of Langston Hughes's translation of the poem.

Guillén drew on the rhythms of the musical genre son in his collections Motivos de son and El son entero. His first gained fame with eight short poems in Motivos de son based on Afro-Cuban son music; the collection's first poem addresses a boy of Afro-Cuban ethnicity, telling him "Charity's payin' yo' keep / She's givin' you all you need." Hughes translated poems from Motivos de son into Black vernacular English, including "Li'l Manuel," who is critiqued for "all dat English you used to know." His collection Songoro cosongo is noted for its onomatopoeia. In West Indies Ltd, he critiqued ongoing racial exploitation. He also wrote the collections Tengo and El son entero.

His poem "Ballad of the Two Grandfathers" ends by describing figures "of equal size" who "shout, dream, weep, sing." The poem opens by describing "Shadows that only I can see" and contrasts the title characters' cries of "I tire!" and "I am dying!" In his poem "Sometimes" (or "A Veces"), he described wanting to bring a flower to his lover.

Guillén was parodied by Guillermo Cabrera Infante in the novel Three Trapped Tigers. Nancy Morejón is an Afro-Cuban poet and scholar of Guillén. Langston Hughes translated two collections of Guillén's Selected Poems, which appeared after his work became known in the English-speaking world.
