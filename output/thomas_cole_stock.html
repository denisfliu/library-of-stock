Thomas Cole

British-American landscape painter, founder of the Hudson River School. Most commonly identified by The Oxbow, The Course of Empire (5-painting series), and The Voyage of Life (4-painting series). Patron: Luman Reed. Depicted alongside William Cullen Bryant in Asher Durand's Kindred Spirits. Also painted The Titan's Goblet and The Architect's Dream.
The Course of Empire (1833–1836)

Series of five paintings depicting the rise and fall of a civilization. Commissioned by Luman Reed. Inspired by a line from Byron's Childe Harold's Pilgrimage. Each painting depicts the same rocky outcrop with a precariously balanced boulder. Wikipedia

The Oxbow (1836)

View from Mount Holyoke after a thunderstorm, depicting a bend in the Connecticut River. Cole includes a tiny self-portrait of himself painting in the foreground. Wikipedia

The Voyage of Life (1842)

Series of four allegorical paintings depicting a man's journey down a river in a golden boat, guided by an angel. Stages: Childhood, Youth, Manhood, Old Age. Second set at the National Gallery of Art. Wikipedia

The Titan's Goblet (1833)

Fantastical painting of a gigantic drinking vessel / goblet standing on a hill, with boats sailing on water inside it and a Greek temple on the brim. Wikipedia

The Architect's Dream (1840)

Commissioned by architect Ithiel Town (who rejected it). A figure reclines on a column atop books, overlooking a fantastical landscape of Greek and Gothic architecture. Wikipedia

Kindred Spirits (by Asher Durand, 1849)

Painted by Asher Durand, not Cole — but Cole is depicted in it. Shows Cole and poet William Cullen Bryant standing on a ledge at Kaaterskill Falls in the Catskills. Wikipedia
