Questions: Chevalier de Saint-Georges

1 tossups · 6 bonuses

Tossups

T1 2022 DECAF (2022) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
The opening melody of this composer’s symphony number two, which they also used as an operatic overture, consists of three unison quarter notes and a half note, repeated on each note of an ascending D major chord. Other than the attributed composer, this composer is the likeliest candidate for having composed the basis for or even part of the ballet Les petits riens. This composer is the subject of the second episode of a BBC 4 radio program hosted by (+) Chi-chi Nwanoku. This successor of François-Joseph Gossec as conductor of the Concert des Amateurs had that ensemble sponsored by a Freemason lodge and rebranded as Le Concert Olympique, for which he commissioned Haydn’s (*) Paris symphonies. This composer’s only surviving opera is L’Amant anonyme. Aside from music, this composer was known as “the god of arms” and notoriously bested fencer Alexandre Picard. For 10 points, name this knighted French swordsman and early Black composer.
ANSWER: Joseph Bologne, Chevalier de Saint-Georges [accept either underlined part]

Bonuses

B1 2025 Chicago Open (2025) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
This composer included an onstage interracial kiss in the finale of an 1804 comic opera set in colonial Virginia. For 10 points each:
[10] The title character leads a slave revolt in what composer’s opera Tarare? In Amadeus, this composer claims to have murdered Mozart.
ANSWER: Antonio Salieri (The unnamed opera is Die Neger.)
[10] A 2023 biopic invents a feud between Christoph Gluck and this composer of L’amant Anonyme (“la-MAHNT ah-no-NEEM”) over control of the Paris Opera. This composer conducted the premiere of Haydn’s “Paris Symphonies” with the Olympic Lodge.
ANSWER: Chevalier de Saint-Georges [or Joseph Bologne; or Joseph Boulogne] (The biopic is Chevalier.)
[10] In this opera by Gioachino Rossini, the lead soprano sings “Assisa a piè d’un salice” (“ahs-SEE-zah ah pyeh doon sah-LEE-cheh”) before being stabbed by a Black man who thought he wasn’t allowed to marry her. Stendhal criticized this opera for diverging wildly from its source material.
ANSWER: Otello [or Otello, Ossia l’Africana in Venezia]
B2 2024 COOT (2024) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
Out of jealousy, Mozart allegedly based the villain Monostatos in The Magic Flute on this composer. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this French composer of eight symphony-concertantes and the opera L'Amant anonyme (“la-MONT a-noh-NEEM”). This Guadeloupe-born composer’s status as a free man of colour did not stop him from conducting Le Concert Olympique (“luh con-SEHR oh-lam-PEEK”).
ANSWER: Joseph Bologne, Chevalier de Saint-Georges [accept either underlined part]
[10] Under the baton of Saint-Georges (“san-ZHORZH”), Le Concert Olympique premiered a set of six symphonies by Haydn named for this French city. Saint-Georges was himself the director of an opera company based in this city before he was removed due to racist objections.
ANSWER: Paris [accept Paris symphonies; accept Opéra de Paris]
[10] The fourth of Haydn’s Paris symphonies is named due to it being a favourite of this student of Christoph Gluck, who was also close friends with Saint-Georges, and the female dedicatee of Luigi Cherubini’s (“ke-roo-BEE-nee”) Requiem in C Minor.
ANSWER: Marie Antoinette [or Marie Antoinette Josèphe Jeanne] (The symphony is subtitled “La Reine”, or “The Queen”.)
B3 2023 BHSU (2023) Diff 9 · Fine Arts
This composer, the illegitimate son of a slave and a colonial French planter, was sent away from Guadeloupe as a child to study in France. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this 18th-century French composer and violinist. A 2020 New York Times article criticized the common practice of referring to this man as the “Black Mozart.”
ANSWER: Joseph Boulogne [or the Chevalier de Saint-Georges]
[10] A dubious theory holds that Boulogne was the inspiration for the villainous Monostatos in this Mozart opera. Other theorists claim that Empress Maria Theresa is the basis for this opera’s “Queen of the Night.”
ANSWER: The Magic Flute [or Die Zauberflöte]
[10] As conductor of the Concert Olympique, Saint-Georges commissioned this group of six orchestral pieces. This set includes pieces nicknamed “The Queen,” “The Bear,” and “The Hen.”
ANSWER: Paris Symphonies [prompt on symphonies by Haydn]
B4 2022 WORKSHOP (2022) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
Answer the following about opera composers who crossed paths with Marie Antoinette, for 10 points each.
[10] This music teacher of Marie Antoinette composed Iphigénie en Aulide. Earlier, this composer wrote another classically themed opera, Orfeo ed Euridice (“ew-ree-DEE-cheh”).
ANSWER: Christoph Willibald Gluck [or Christoph Willibald Ritter von Gluck]
[10] After Marie Antoinette failed to advocate for his placement as director of the Paris Opera, this biracial Guadeloupean composer played in her private quarters instead. This composer’s only surviving opera is L’Amant anonyme.
ANSWER: Chevalier de Saint-Georges [or Joseph Bologne]
[10] Apocryphally, this composer promised to marry Marie Antoinette after he performed for her at age six. This man composed Così fan tutte and The Magic Flute.
ANSWER: Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart
B5 2019 PIANO (2019) Diff 9 · Fine Arts
The “Romanze” second movement from one of these pieces was a royal favorite, leading it to be nicknamed “The Queen.” For 10 points each:
[10] Name this group of pieces, one of which features the oboe in a “clucking” repeated-note theme. The bagpipe-like opening of the last movement of the first of these pieces has caused it to be nicknamed “The Bear.”
ANSWER: Paris Symphonies
[10] The Paris Symphonies were written in the 1780s by this “Father of the Symphony.”
ANSWER: (Franz) Joseph Haydn
[10] This virtuoso violinist born in Guadeloupe commissioned the Paris Symphonies. This man, sometimes called the “Black Mozart,” was a major composer of Symphonies Concertantes to show off his own skill.
ANSWER: Chevalier de Saint-Georges [or Joseph Bologne]
B6 2015 Chicago Open (2015) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
The fourth of these pieces is nicknamed “The Queen” for Marie Antoinette. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this set of six symphonies by Joseph Haydn , numbered 82 through 87 in his overall catalog.
ANSWER: Paris Symphonies
[10] The Paris Symphonies were premiered by the Concert de la Loge Olympique orchestra under the leadership of this violinist and conductor originally from Guadeloupe. The son of a slave, he became one of the first classical music stars of African descent.
ANSWER: Joseph de Boulogne, Chevalier de Saint-Georges [accept either underlined part]
[10] This earlier Haydn symphony is his only one in E minor, and one of the few to put the menuet in the second movement rather than the third.
ANSWER: Symphony No. 44 [or Trauer Symphony or Funeral Symphony or Mourning Symphony]
