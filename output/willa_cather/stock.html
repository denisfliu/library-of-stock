Summary of Facts

Willa Cather is an American novelist best known for her Great Plains trilogy and other works set in the American West. My Antonia is narrated by Jim Burden, who reminisces about his Nebraska childhood with the title Bohemian girl from the Shimerda family; the novel ends "whatever we had missed, we possessed together the precious, the incommunicable past." O Pioneers! follows Alexandra Bergson; Carl Linstrum marries her after Emil's death.

Death Comes for the Archbishop follows Jean Latour and Father Vaillant setting up a diocese in New Mexico, with Kit Carson as a character and mules named Contento and Angelica. The Song of the Lark traces Thea Kronborg's rise from Moonstone, Colorado to opera stardom; she takes lessons with Harsanyi and falls in love with Fred Ottenburg at the Flagstaff pueblos. A Lost Lady is set in Sweet Water, where narrator Niel Herbert watches the decline of Captain Forrester's wife Marian.

"Paul's Case" (from The Troll Garden) follows a boy who wears a red carnation, ushers at Carnegie Hall, steals $1,000, and jumps under a train. "A Wagner Matinee" depicts Aunt Georgiana crying "I don't want to go" after a symphony. Shadows on the Rock is set in Quebec, and Sapphira and the Slave Girl involves a woman suspecting her husband of an affair with Nancy.
