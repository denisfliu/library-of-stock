Summary of Facts

Correggio (Antonio Allegri da Correggio) was an Italian Renaissance/Mannerist painter from Parma, best known for illusionistic ceiling frescoes using the di sotto in su technique. His Assumption of the Virgin in Parma Cathedral features spiraling layers of clouds that disguise the octagonal architecture, with figures appearing to jump toward the viewer.

His Loves of Jupiter series, commissioned by Federico II Gonzaga, includes four paintings: Jupiter and Io (a stormy cloud with a humanoid face embraces a woman from behind), Danaë (an angel looks toward a shower of gold), Abduction of Ganymede (an eagle carries the youth), and Leda and the Swan. He painted Diana over a fireplace in the Camera di San Paolo (abbess's bedroom). His Vision of St. John on Patmos pairs gospel writers with Doctors of the Church. He was the teacher of Parmigianino and helped complete Mantegna's family chapel in Mantua.
