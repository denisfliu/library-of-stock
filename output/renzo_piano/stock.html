Italian modernist architect, Pritzker Prize winner. Designed the Centre Pompidou in Paris (with Richard Rogers) — famous for its inside-out, color-coded exterior pipes and tubes. Also designed The Shard (London's tallest building), Kansai International Airport (on an artificial island in Osaka Bay), the Whitney Museum of American Art (New York), the Menil Collection (Houston), the Modern Wing of the Art Institute of Chicago, and the Jean-Marie Tjibaou Cultural Centre in Noumea, New Caledonia.
Centre Pompidou (1977)

Co-designed with Richard Rogers (and engineer Peter Rice). A cultural complex in Paris featuring a modern art museum and library. Famous for its 'inside-out' design — elevators, electrical wiring, and plumbing are color-coded and displayed on the outside of the building. Named for former French President Georges Pompidou. General / Biographical

His designs consistently maximize natural light in museums. He converted an automobile manufacturing plant into a convention center and trade fair district.

Summary of Facts

Renzo Piano is an Italian architect and Pritzker Prize winner most famous in quizbowl for two works: the Centre Pompidou in Paris and The Shard in London. The Centre Pompidou was co-designed with Richard Rogers (Piano being the alphabetically-first designer) and is celebrated for its 'inside-out' aesthetic — elevators, electrical wiring, and plumbing are color-coded and displayed on the building's exterior, with external escalators. The complex contains a modern art museum and a library and was named for former French President Georges Pompidou. Rogers later went on to design the Lloyd's Building in London, while Piano designed The Shard — a glass-clad pyramidal skyscraper inspired by Canaletto's paintings of London's spires — which became the tallest building in the United Kingdom, built on the site of the demolished Southwark Towers.

Piano also designed Kansai International Airport in Osaka, Japan, built on the artificial island of Kankujima in Osaka Bay. It features the world's longest airport terminal — a mile-long departure lounge — and a wing-shaped design whose sliding joints allowed it to survive the 1995 Kobe earthquake unscathed. His collaborator on this project was Noriaki Okabe. In New York, Piano designed the new Whitney Museum of American Art in the Meatpacking District, replacing a Marcel Breuer-designed building with a terraced structure opening onto the High Line, featuring a cantilevered entrance leading to a 'largo.'

In New Caledonia, Piano designed the Jean-Marie Tjibaou Cultural Centre in Noumea to celebrate Kanak culture — ten pavilions ('cases') inspired by traditional Kanak Great Houses, with specially designed louvers called 'nacos' on the exterior to regulate wind flow. His Menil Collection museum in Houston features ferroconcrete 'leaves' on the roof that serve as both a heat source and UV protection, maximizing natural light — a consistent Piano trademark. This museum also contains a Cy Twombly Pavilion. The Modern Wing of the Art Institute of Chicago similarly maximizes natural light and is connected to Millennium Park by the Nichols Bridgeway, which Piano also designed.

Among Piano's other works: the Zentrum Paul Klee in Bern (wavy steel beams blending into surrounding hills); the California Academy of Sciences (green roof inspired by San Francisco's seven hills); a renovation of Harvard's Fogg Museum that preserved the brick facade while adding a glass pyramid roof; an expansion of the Morgan Library with three glass-and-steel pavilions; the Parliament House of Malta (Gozitan limestone, zero-emission design); the Kimbell Art Museum expansion in Fort Worth; the Biosfera glass-and-steel structure in Genoa's Porto Antico (housing 150 flora and fauna species); the Padre Pio Pilgrimage Church; the NEMO Science Museum in Amsterdam; Aurora Place in Sydney; and a redesign of Valletta City Gate in Malta, where he preserved the ruins of Edward Middleton Barry's Royal Opera House as an open-air space.
