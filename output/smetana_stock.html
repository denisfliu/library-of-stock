Bedrich Smetana

View source questions
Czech nationalist composer, most commonly identified by the tone poem cycle Ma vlast (which includes The Moldau depicting the Vltava River) and the opera The Bartered Bride. Went deaf in 1874. Wrote the autobiographical string quartet From My Life. Associated with Prague and the Prague Spring Festival. Often clued alongside Dvorak.
Ma vlast (My Country / My Fatherland)

Cycle of six symphonic/tone poems depicting Bohemia. The most commonly referenced movements are Vltava (The Moldau) and Vysehrad.

String Quartet No. 1 in E minor ("From My Life")

Autobiographical string quartet. Smetana's most commonly clued chamber work.

The Bartered Bride (Prodana nevesta)

Comic opera, Smetana's best-known operatic work.
