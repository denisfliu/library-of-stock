Questions: David Lynch

4 tossups · 11 bonuses

Tossups

T1 2025 Absolute Cinema (2025) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
This director filmed a single 13-minute shot of a head of cheese in front of power lines for a film that debuted at the Festival of Disruption. In a twenty-minute long film, this author of the book Catching the Big Fish recalls helping a European girl drink her first Coke and buying colored sugar water in the middle of nowhere in Yugoslavia. This director, who is the better known of the two co-creators of a character who often praises the merits of a “damn fine cup of (*) coffee,” was once filmed cooking broccoli and quinoa. Every day at 2:30 pm for seven years, this director ordered a chocolate milkshake at Bob’s Big Boy diner. A fast food customer inspired this director to create a character who exclaims “Heineken? Fuck that shit! Pabst! Blue! Ribbon!” For 10 points, a creepy monster emerges from behind a diner in what director’s film Mulholland Drive?
ANSWER: David Lynch [or David Keith Lynch] (The characters mentioned are Dale Cooper from Twin Peaks and Frank Booth from Blue Velvet.)
T2 2024 ARGOS (2024) Diff 9 · Other Academic
A 2019 Bonnefanten Museum retrospective on this artist displayed surrealist artworks such as Rock with Seven Eyes and Boy Lights Fire. This artist used a bottleneck to play slide guitar on an album he collaborated on with John Neff, BlueBOB. This artist narrates a story about seeing “frog-moths” rise out of the earth in a clip that shows him cooking quinoa. A David Foster Wallace essay titled “[this artist] Keeps His Head” discusses a scene in which a woman covers her entire face in (*) red lipstick. After hiring Angelo Badalamenti as a singing coach for his wife Isabella Rossellini, this artist collaborated with him on a series that opens with the theme “Falling” by Julee Cruise. A nightclub singer attempts to retrieve her son in a film by this artist that opens with the discovery of a severed ear in a field. For 10 points, what multimedia artist directed the films Wild at Heart and Blue Velvet?
ANSWER: David Lynch
T3 2022 NASAT (2022) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
Before Oliver Stone's Platoon, this director used Barber's Adagio for Strings as a John Hurt character dies in his sleep, after which Tennyson's "Nothing Will Die" is recited. In another film by this director, Cherubini's C minor Requiem plays as Leland kills his daughter in a train car, before wrapping her in plastic and throwing her in a river. In a series by this director with a theme by Angelo Badalamenti, a depiction of the Trinity test is accompanied by Penderecki's Threnody for the Victims of Hiroshima. Isabella Rossellini performs the title song of a film by this director as nightclub singer Dorothy Vallens, who is tormented by the gas-huffing Frank Booth. This director depicted the murder of Laura Palmer in a film subtitled "Fire Walk with Me." For 10 points, name this director of The Elephant Man, Blue Velvet, and Twin Peaks.
ANSWER: David Lynch [David Keith Lynch] [Clues are from The Elephant Man, Twin Peaks: Fire Walk with Me, Twin Peaks: The Return, Blue Velvet, and Twin Peaks, in that order.]
T4 2012 NASAT (2012) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
In one of this director’s films, Cherubini’s Requiem in C Minor accompanies a murder in a train car, after which the killer gives up his garmonbozia. One of his film’s opening sequences ends with an extreme close-up of beetles warring in the grass. In another of his films, the opening of a mysterious blue box causes most characters to shift identities. He created a nightclub singer who is ritualistically raped as Jeffrey Beaumont watches from the closet, and he worked with Mark Frost on a popular television series. For 10 points, name this American director of Mulholland Dr. and Blue Velvet who created Twin Peaks.
ANSWER: David Lynch

Bonuses

B1 2025 ACF Regionals (2025) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
Answer the following about French electronic composer Jean-Jacques Perrey (“peh-RAY”), for 10 points each.
[10] Perrey earned a roast hedgehog from this musician for backing him on the Ondioline. Stéphane Grappelli played violin on the song “Minor Swing” as part of a quintet led by this musician, who overcame a burnt hand.
ANSWER: Django Reinhardt [or Jean Reinhardt; prompt on Django] (The quintet is the Quintette du Hot Club de France.)
[10] The theme song of the Mexican sitcom El Chavo del Ocho is from a Perrey album whose punning title, “Moog (“mohg”) Indigo,” refers to a type of this electronic instrument that produces audio signals.
ANSWER: synthesizer [or Moog synthesizer]
[10] This composer and Gershon Kingsley co-wrote Perrey’s oft-sampled song “E.V.A.” This composer scored films like Wild at Heart for a director who used his song “Falling,” which was sung by Julee Cruise, as the theme for a TV series.
ANSWER: Angelo Badalamenti [or Angelo Daniel Badalamenti] (David Lynch wrote the lyrics for the Twin Peaks theme “Falling” and the rest of the songs on Cruise’s album Floating Into the Night.)
