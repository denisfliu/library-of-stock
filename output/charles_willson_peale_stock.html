Summary of Facts

Charles Willson Peale was an early American painter who named his sons after famous artists — Raphaelle, Rembrandt, Titian, Rubens, Linnaeus, and Franklin. His self-portrait The Artist in His Museum shows him lifting a red curtain to reveal a gallery of taxidermied objects. Exhumation of the Mastodon depicts a pulley system at Barber farm with his sons and Alexander Wilson present. The Staircase Group is a trompe l'oeil showing two of his sons. He painted many portraits of George Washington including George Washington at Princeton. He exchanged a saddle for art lessons from John Hesselius. Rachel Weeping shows a deceased child. He named a daughter Sophonisba after Sofonisba Anguissola. His slave Moses Williams became a silhouette maker.
