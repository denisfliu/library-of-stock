Tossups

T1 2025 LITANY (2025) Diff 8 · Literature
This movement was arguably launched by a poem titled for a remark about “an only daughter” in The Madness of Suibhne [“SIV-nuh”]. A train journey from Rome to Paris prompts a discussion of dwindling Catholic faith in the first poem of a collection from this movement, whose name comes from a review by M. L. Rosenthal. “Beyond the Alps,” “91 Revere Street,” and (*) “Heart’s Needle” were early pieces in this movement of a poem that declares, “White Godiva, I unpeel” after opening, “Stasis in darkness.” That poem, which ends with the image of “the red eye, the cauldron of morning,” titles a collection edited by Ted Hughes and named for the poet’s horse, Ariel. For 10 points, Robert Lowell.and Sylvia Plath belong to what movement of frequently autobiographical poetry?
ANSWER: Confessional poetry [or Confessionalism; or Confessionalists] (the first two poems mentioned are W. D. Snodgrass’s “Heart’s Needle” and Robert Lowell’s “Beyond the Alps”)
T2 2020 DECAMERON (2020) Diff 7 · Literature
A poem from this literary movement states that “I would like a simple life” and describes “all night dark wings flapping in my heart”. Besides “The Ambition Bird”, another poem from this movement tells of the “pheasant cocks that march past their dubious hens'' and ends with the claim that “You are still my daughter”. That poem was dedicated to “ Cynthia'' and is interspersed with imagery from the Korean War. One poem from this movement describes “where the graveyard shelves on the town” and a “red fox stain” that covers “Blue Hill”. A radio bleats (*) “Love, O careless Love. . .” and the narrator states that, “I myself am Hell” in a poem from this movement. For 10 points, name this movement characterized by its use of direct and colloquial style whose members included W.D Snodgrass, Robert Lowell, and Anne Sexton.
ANSWER: Confessional Poetry
T3 2016 Early Fall Tournament (EFT) (2016) Diff 7 · Literature
The term for this poetic movement was coined in a review by M.L. Rosenthal. A poem from this movement states, “A woman like that is not a woman” and declares, “I have been her kind,” while another poem from the same collection from this movement describes its author’s mental state “Part way back from Bedlam.” A poem from a movement named for this style describes construction near the Boston Aquarium disturbing a monument to (*) Colonel Shaw. A poet from this movement published the collections The Awful Rowing Toward God, while a different, male member of this movement wrote the poem “For the Union Dead.” For 10 points, Anne Sexton, Robert Lowell, and Sylvia Plath belonged to what poetic movement of the 1950s which focused on personal experience and trauma?

B1 2023 MRNA (2023) Diff 7 · Literature
The speaker states “There ought to be a law against [this character]” in a poem that begins “Filling her compact &/ delicious body / with chicken páprika, she glanced at me / twice.” For 10 points each:
[10] Name this “huffy” character with “plights and gripes as bad as Achilles.” He appears in a cycle of poems that includes His Toy, His Dream, His Rest.
ANSWER: Henry [prompt on Mr. Bones]
[10] John Berryman used Henry as an authorial stand-in in this autobiographical group of poems, which includes musings on depression and controversial “blackface” passages.
ANSWER: The Dream Songs [accept 77 Dream Songs]
[10] Berryman’s Dream Songs are often grouped with this postwar style of American poetry. Associated with Sylvia Plath and Robert Lowell, it focuses on the personal disclosure of extreme experience or trauma.
ANSWER: confessional poetry
B2 2022 Winter Closed (2022) Diff 8 · Literature
The works of Rainer Maria Rilke have spawned a variety of poetic responses. For 10 points each:
[10] John Berryman flatly asserts “Rilke was a jerk” in an entry of his Dream Songs, a landmark collection in this poetic style that emerged during the 1950s. Its name was coined in a review of Robert Lowell’s Life Studies.
ANSWER: confessional poetry [or Confessionalism]
[10] Rilke’s “thing-poems” prompted this writer to call him the “Santa Claus of loneliness.” This writer’s review “Rilke in English” was published four days after the title date of his poem about World War II, “September 1, 1939.”
ANSWER: W. H. Auden [or Wystan Hugh Auden]
[10] Denise Levertov’s many Rilke-inspired works include two poems whose title begins with this word. In a parody of William Carlos Williams whose title begins with this word, the speaker chops down a house and sprays some hollyhocks with lye.
ANSWER: variations [or variations on a theme; or theme and variations; accept “Variations on a Theme by Rilke” or “Variations on a Theme by William Carlos Williams”] (The second poem is by Kenneth Koch.)
B3 2022 NASAT (2022) Diff 8 · Literature
A poem titled for this action states that "I was stamped out like a Plymouth fender into this world" and concludes that a "story ends with me still" performing this action. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this action. The last poem in a collection titled for this "awful" action describes the speaker getting a royal flush in poker after arriving at "the island called God."
ANSWER: rowing [or An Awful Rowing Toward God; or "The Rowing Endeth"]
[10] This poet wrote An Awful Rowing Toward God. This author of "Wanting to Die" proclaimed, "I have gone out, a possessed witch" to open the poem "Her Kind," collected in To Bedlam and Part Way Back.
ANSWER: Anne Sexton [or Anne Gray Harvey]
[10] Like Sharon Olds and Robert Lowell, Sexton was a member of this movement of the 1950s and 1960s, whose members wrote poetry focused on individual experience and personal trauma.
ANSWER: Confessional poetry
B4 2022 ACF Winter (2022) Diff 7 · Literature

[10] Name this poetic movement that critics coined in reviews of the collection Life Studies, which features a poem titled for an animal who “jabs her wedge-head in a cup of sour cream.”

[10] This surname was shared by Robert, the Confessional poet who included “Skunk Hour” in Life Studies, and Amy, who wrote the Imagist poems “Lilacs” and “Patterns.”
