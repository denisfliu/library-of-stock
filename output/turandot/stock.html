Turandot

Giacomo Puccini's last opera, left unfinished at his death from throat cancer in 1924. The most commonly performed ending was completed by Franco Alfano. The premiere at La Scala in 1926 was conducted by Arturo Toscanini, who famously stopped at the point where Puccini's manuscript ended, reportedly saying 'at this point the maestro died.' Set in legendary Beijing, it follows Prince Calaf's pursuit of the icy Princess Turandot through a riddle contest. The opera is based on a play by Carlo Gozzi and incorporates Chinese folk melodies, most prominently 'Mo Li Hua' (Jasmine Flower). Luciano Berio composed an alternative ending in 2002. A chapter on it appears in Andrew C. Davis's 2010 book on Puccini's 'Late Style.' Wikipedia

"Nessun dorma"

The most famous tenor aria from Act 3 of Turandot, sung by Prince Calaf. The title translates as 'Let no one sleep.' The aria ends with three cries of 'Vincerò!' ('I will win!'), sustained on A4. It begins in G major with a melody starting on D4 in a double-dotted 'LONG, short, LONG, LONG' rhythm, repeated an octave below. Calaf sings it because he knows Turandot cannot learn his name before dawn. A staple of Luciano Pavarotti's repertoire; famously performed at multiple World Cup finals. In 2020 a video of tenor Maurizio Marchini singing it from a Florentine balcony went viral during the Italian COVID lockdown. Wikipedia

"In questa reggia"

Soprano aria from Act 2, sung by Princess Turandot herself. She explains the fate of her ancestor Princess Lo-u-Ling — who was raped and murdered — and uses this to justify her hatred of men and her deadly riddle contest. The title translates as 'In this palace.' An ostinato alternates between F-sharp sus2 sus4 and F-sharp minor add9 chords. It repeats the exclamation 'No one will ever possess me!' General / Biographical

Giacomo Puccini (1858–1924), Italian opera composer. Turandot was his final and unfinished work. His other operas include La Bohème and La fanciulla del West, both also premiered by Toscanini. He received a music box with Chinese melodies that inspired the score. Puccini died of throat cancer while composing Turandot.

Summary of Facts

Turandot is the final opera by Giacomo Puccini, left unfinished at his death from throat cancer in 1924. The most commonly performed completion was written by Franco Alfano. The opera premiered at La Scala in Milan in 1926 under conductor Arturo Toscanini, who famously stopped the performance at the point where Puccini's manuscript ended, reportedly announcing to the audience 'at this point the maestro died.' Before his death, Puccini had begged Toscanini not to let the opera die. In 2002, Luciano Berio composed a second alternative ending intended to correct dramaturgical flaws in the Alfano version.

Set in legendary Beijing, the opera follows Prince Calaf, who falls in love with the icy Princess Turandot. She challenges all suitors to answer three riddles on pain of death; Calaf succeeds and wins the right to marry her. He then issues a counter-challenge in the spirit of the Rumpelstiltskin tale: if Turandot can discover his name before dawn, he will forfeit his life. The servants Ping, Pang, and Pong torture the slave girl Liu to extract Calaf's name. Liu, declaring Turandot to be 'encircled in ice,' stabs herself rather than betray him.

The opera incorporates the Chinese folk song 'Mo Li Hua' (Jasmine Flower) — Puccini had received a music box with Chinese melodies which inspired him. The crowd sings a version of this tune. The score was discussed as the latest work in Andrew C. Davis's 2010 book on Puccini's 'Late Style.'

The opera contains several celebrated arias. 'Nessun dorma' ('Let no one sleep'), sung by Calaf in Act 3, is one of the most famous tenor arias in the repertoire. It begins in G major with a melody on D4 in a double-dotted 'LONG, short, LONG, LONG' rhythm, then repeated an octave below, and ends with three sustained cries of 'Vincerò!' ('I will win!'). Calaf sings it because he knows Turandot cannot learn his name before dawn. It became a signature piece for Luciano Pavarotti and has been performed at multiple FIFA World Cup finals. During the 2020 Italian COVID lockdown, a video of tenor Maurizio Marchini performing it from a Florentine balcony went viral. Performance tradition often holds the penultimate high B much longer than the semiquaver notated in the score.

'In questa reggia' ('In this palace') is the Act 2 aria in which Turandot herself explains her motivations: she describes the fate of her ancestor Princess Lo-u-Ling, who was raped and murdered by a foreign conqueror, and vows that no man will ever possess her. It features an ostinato alternating between F-sharp sus2 sus4 and F-sharp minor add9 chords, and repeats the exclamation 'No one will ever possess me!' A soprano aria also describes a king who 'unfurled' seven banners and conquered a rival kingdom.

'Tu che di gel sei cinta' is the aria in which the slave girl Liu describes Turandot as 'encircled in ice' before stabbing herself. Uniquely, Puccini wrote the text for this aria himself rather than leaving it to his librettists. The opera is based on a play of the same name by Carlo Gozzi. Toscanini had also conducted the premieres of other Puccini operas, including La Bohème and La fanciulla del West.
