Tossups

T1 2025 ACF Winter (2025) Diff 7 · Fine Arts
The first notes in this opera consist of four octave doubles of [read slowly] A, E-sharp, low B, C-sharp. An aria in this opera begins by alternating between a G major and a complex D9 over E-flat chord accompanying four gentle repeated notes with dotted rhythm sung on D4 which is repeated an octave lower. A slave girl describes the title character of this opera as being encircled in ice in the aria “Tu che di gel sei cinta.” The crowd of this opera sings “there on the Eastern mountains a stork sang,” with a melody taken from the folksong “Jasmine Flower.” Franco Alfano composed the most commonly performed ending of this opera, whose male lead solves three riddles and sings “Nessun Dorma.” For 10 points, name this unfinished opera about a Chinese princess by Giacomo Puccini.
ANSWER: Turandot
T2 2025 ACF Nationals (2025) Diff 9 · Fine Arts
This question can be read to any team. A chapter on this opera titled for its composer’s “Solution: Integrating the Conventional and Unconventional” appears in Andrew C. Davis’s 2010 book on its composer’s “Late Style,” in which this is the latest opera discussed. A soprano aria in this opera’s second act has an ostinato alternating between F-sharp sus2 sus4 and F-sharp minor add9 chords. That aria from this opera repeats the exclamation “No one will ever possess me!” and describes the fate of the protagonist’s ancestor. For 10 points, the aria “Nessun Dorma” (“NESS-oon DOHR-mah”) appears in what unfinished opera by Puccini, titled for a Chinese princess?

T3 2023 Halloween Hippopotamus Invitational (2023) Diff 7 · Fine Arts
This opera was completed by Franco Alfano two years after the death of its composer; at its premiere, the conductor Toscanini ended the performance where that composer had left off, with a kiss between its two main characters. This opera features three (*) riddles that a suitor must answer for the titular character’s hand in marriage, as well as a Rumpelstiltskin-like challenge posed by a successful suitor. A famous tenor aria from this opera has been performed at several World Cup finals; that aria, titled “Nessun dorma”, ends with the singer repeating the word “vincerò (“veen-CHAIR-oh”),” or “I will win,” three times. For ten points, name this Puccini opera set in China about Prince Calaf and his efforts to marry the titular princess.
ANSWER: Turandot (“tur-an-DOH”)
T4 2020 DECAMERON (2020) Diff 7 · Fine Arts
This G major work begins with four Ds, and then the same one octave lower, echoing an earlier proclamation. Earlier, the characters that deliver "Ho una casa nell’Honan" exclaim that a man’s grave is already being dug, in contrast with this work in which the singer claims that at dawn, another character described to be encircled in ice in the aria "Tu (*) che di gel sei cinta" will be his. This piece is sung because Calaf knows that the Princess cannot name him; accordingly, it ends on a sustained A4 "Vincero!" A video of this aria sung on a Florentine balcony went viral during the 2020 Italian lockdown due to coronavirus, and performed by tenor Maurizio Marchini. For 10 points, name this staple of Luciano Pavarotti’s repertoire, a tenor aria from the final act of Giacomo Puccini’s Turandot translated as "Let no one sleep."
ANSWER: “Nessun dorma” [prompt on Turandot]
T5 2015 Oxford Open (2015) Diff 8 · Fine Arts

The aria “In Questa Reggia” is sung by the title character of this opera and explains how Lo-U-Ling was raped and murdered. In this opera, a gong is struck three times, and the servants Ping, Pang and Pong torture the slave girl Liu to try to find out a man’s name. “Vincero! Vincero!” are the final words of an aria sung by Calaf in this opera, “Nessun Dorma”. For 10 points, name this opera about a Chinese princess, the final work of Giacomo Puccini.
ANSWER: Turandot
T6 2015 NASAT (2015) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
A tenor aria from this opera begins with the note D sung in a double-dotted "LONG, short, LONG, LONG" rhythm, after which the same melody is repeated an octave below. A soprano aria from this opera describes a king that "unfurled" seven banners, conquered a rival kingdom, and "stifled" the voice of its ruler. A slave girl in this opera stabs herself to death after proclaiming the title character to be "encircled in ice" in the aria "Tu che di gel sei cinta." Bonuses

B1 2024 ARCADIA (2024) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
During the 2013 Chinese New Year’s Gala, Celine Dion performed a duet of this song with Sòng Zǔyīng. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this Chinese folk song that opens with the melody [read slowly] “E, E-G-A-C-C-A-G,” as the narrator observes a “beautiful” one of the title objects.
ANSWER: “Mò Lì Huā” (“mwuh lee hwah”) [or “Jasmine Flower”]
[10] After he was gifted a music box with Chinese melodies, Giacomo Puccini based the musical theme for this character on “Mò Li Huā.” This title princess of Puccini’s last opera gives a deadly challenge of three riddles to win her hand in marriage.
ANSWER: Turandot
[10] The melody of “Mò Lì Huā” is also quoted in this contemporary Chinese composer’s Symphony 1997. This composer of the Water Concerto scored the films Hero and Crouching Tiger, Hidden Dragon.
ANSWER: Tán Dùn
B2 2023 ILLIAC (2023) Diff 7 · Fine Arts
As he was dying of throat cancer, the composer of this opera approached Arturo Toscanini and begged him “don’t let [this opera] die!” For 10 points each:
[10] Name this opera. Toscanini later conducted the premiere of this opera in which Calaf correctly answers three riddles to win the hand of the title princess.
ANSWER: Turandot
[10] In addition to Turandot, Toscanini premiered other operas by this composer like La Fanciulla del West and La Boheme.
ANSWER: Giacomo Puccini
[10] Before he ever conducted his first opera premiere, Arturo Toscanini played in the cello section at the world premiere of this opera. This opera set in Cyprus was the second collaboration between its composer and librettist Arrigo Boito.
ANSWER: Otello (by Giuseppe Verdi)
