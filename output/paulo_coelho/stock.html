Brazilian novelist writing in Portuguese, best known for the 1988 allegorical novel The Alchemist. Born 1947 in Rio de Janeiro. One of the best-selling authors in history. His works deal with spiritual journeys and self-discovery; his central concept is the 'Personal Legend' — each person's true destiny.
The Alchemist (1988)

Coelho's defining work and one of the best-selling novels of all time. An Andalusian shepherd named Santiago dreams of hidden treasure buried beneath the Egyptian pyramids and travels across the Sahara to find it — only to discover the treasure is back where he started. The King of Salem, Melchizedek, gives Santiago mystical black-and-white stones called Urim and Thummim. Santiago works for a crystal merchant in Tangier after being robbed, and joins a caravan across the Sahara where he meets the alchemist. The novel's central message: 'If you want something, all the universe conspires in helping you achieve it.' The prologue retells the myth of Narcissus, where the lake mourns because it can no longer see its own beauty reflected. The title is also shared with a Ben Jonson comedy featuring characters Face and Subtle. Wikipedia

General / Biographical

Paulo Coelho is a Brazilian author writing in Portuguese, born 1947 in Rio de Janeiro. He is frequently identified in quizbowl as a Brazilian counterpart to Jorge Amado (Gabriela, Clove and Cinnamon) and as a writer of internationally bestselling Portuguese-language fiction. His concept of the 'Personal Legend' — each person's individual spiritual calling — is central to his work. Wikipedia

Summary of Facts

Paulo Coelho is a Brazilian novelist born in 1947 in Rio de Janeiro who writes in Portuguese and is one of the best-selling authors of all time. In quizbowl, he is almost exclusively represented by a single landmark work: The Alchemist (1988), an allegorical novel whose shepherd protagonist Santiago dreams of treasure buried under the Egyptian pyramids and embarks on a journey across North Africa to find it, ultimately discovering the treasure was back where he started — near a sycamore tree outside an abandoned church in Andalusia. The Alchemist is dense with quizbowl-ready details: the King of Salem, Melchizedek, gives Santiago two divination stones called Urim and Thummim; Santiago works for a crystal merchant in Tangier after being robbed of his savings; he joins a desert caravan and eventually meets the alchemist who helps him fulfill his 'Personal Legend.' The prologue features an unusual retelling of the Narcissus myth in which the lake grieves not for Narcissus but for its inability to see its own reflection in his eyes. Santiago's climactic transformation into the wind while held by desert militants is another frequently cited detail. The novel shares its title with a Ben Jonson comedy — which has appeared in comparative questions — featuring the con men Face and Subtle and the dupe Dapper, who is told he must consult the Queen of Fairy. Coelho's only other quizbowl-relevant title is Veronika Decides to Die (1998), about a depressed Slovenian woman. In geography and language questions, Coelho is invoked as a representative Brazilian Portuguese-language author alongside Jorge Amado, Clarice Lispector, and Machado de Assis.
