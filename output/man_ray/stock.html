American Dadaist and Surrealist photographer born Emmanuel Radnitzky. Known for Le Violon d'Ingres (Kiki de Montparnasse's back with f-holes), photograms called 'rayographs,' solarization technique, and photographing Marcel Duchamp as cross-dressing alter-ego Rrose Selavy. Also made readymades including The Gift (iron with tacks) and Object to Be Destroyed (metronome with an eye). Spent most of his career in Paris.
Le Violon d'Ingres (1924)

Man Ray's most famous photograph. Depicts his model and lover Kiki de Montparnasse (Alice Prin) nude from behind, with two f-holes painted on her back to evoke a violin. Sold in May 2022 for $12.4 million, then the most expensive photograph ever sold at auction. Wikipedia

General / Biographical

Summary of Facts

Man Ray (1890–1976), born Emmanuel Radnitzky in Philadelphia, was one of the most inventive and wide-ranging artists associated with both Dada and Surrealism. He spent the most significant decades of his career in Paris, where he became embedded in the avant-garde milieu alongside Marcel Duchamp, with whom he co-founded the Société Anonyme.

His most iconic photograph, Le Violon d'Ingres (1924), depicts his muse and lover Kiki de Montparnasse (Alice Ernestine Prin) — the 'Queen of Montparnasse' — nude from the back, with two f-holes painted on her skin to transform her body into a visual pun on a violin. In May 2022, it sold for the record price of $12.4 million. Another famous photograph, Noir et Blanche (1926), shows Kiki's white face resting horizontally next to an upright African mask, a juxtaposition of Western and African forms typical of Surrealist primitivism.

Man Ray was instrumental in developing cameraless photography. He named his version of photograms 'rayographs' after himself, creating images by placing objects such as springs, rolls of film, and a slinky directly on photosensitive paper. He also developed the solarization (Sabattier effect) technique together with his lover Lee Miller — partially reversing tonal values by exposing a developing image to a sudden flash of light. Lee Miller's torso appears in one of his photograms. Man Ray also photographed Meret Oppenheim nude with ink on her palm and forearm at an etching press, one of his celebrated series of photographs of that Surrealist artist.

As a Dadaist, Man Ray created several celebrated readymades and assemblages. The Gift (1921) is an iron with a row of metal tacks glued to the bottom, making it useless. The Enigma of Isidore Ducasse (1920) is a sewing machine wrapped in an army blanket and tied with yellow string, referencing Lautréamont's famous Surrealist image of an umbrella and a sewing machine meeting on a dissecting table. Object to Be Destroyed (1923) combined a metronome with a photograph of an eye; in 1957, student protesters actually destroyed the original in his Paris studio, after which Man Ray created an edition titled Indestructible Object.

Among Man Ray's collaborative and documentary work, his photographs of Marcel Duchamp dressed as the cross-dressing female alter-ego Rrose Selavy are iconic; one appeared on the label of the Belle Haleine perfume bottle. Man Ray helped discover and champion the Parisian street photographer Eugène Atget alongside Berenice Abbott, who worked as his studio assistant. Bill Brandt also trained in his studio. Man Ray was a passionate chess player and designed the first modern chess set composed entirely of abstract geometrical forms. His aerographs — paintings made with an airbrush — date from 1919, with Seguidilla as the first.
