Questions: Andrea del Verrocchio

1 tossup · 5 bonuses

Tossups

T1 2023 ARCHIVE (2023) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
A sculpture by this artist in the Frick Collection is attributed to the wrong family due to a mistake over the testicle motif in that sculpture. The National Gallery once claimed to own 6 sculptures by this artist, although Andrew Butterfield's recent book on this artist cites only one as possibly authentic, a marble relief of Alexander the Great. This sculptor made La Piagnona, the bell used by (+) Savonarola that was subsequently put on trial and flogged. Vasari relates how this man collaborated with the wax-painting specialist Orsino Benintendi to create three life-size figures of the targets of the Pazzi Conspiracy. A narrative bronze sculpture this artist created for a niche in (*) Orsanmichele depicts Christ raising his right hand above Saint Thomas. Lorenzo di Credi and Alessandro Leopardi completed the casting of this man's unfinished equestrian statue of Bartolomeo Colleoni. For 10 points, name this Renaissance sculptor and teacher of Leonardo.
ANSWER: Andrea del Verrocchio [prompt on Andrea]

B1 2025 MRNA (2025) Diff 7 · Fine Arts
Lively descriptions of the Apostles and Doctors of the Church comprise 10 low-relief panels decorating one of these structures constructed for the Old Sacristy. For 10 points each:
[10] What kind of structure designed by Andrea Pisano contains scenes detailing John the Baptist’s life? Before leaving for Rome, Filippo Brunelleschi (“broo-nuh-LESS-kee”) was slated to co-design one of these structures with Lorenzo Ghiberti (“gee-BAIR-tee”).
ANSWER: bronze doors [accept gates; accept South Doors or North Doors or East Doors; accept Gates of Paradise]
[10] Pisano and Ghiberti built bronze doors for this city’s octagonal Baptistry. This city’s Uffizi gallery houses many artworks commissioned by the Medici family.
ANSWER: Florence [or Firenze]
[10] This sculptor collaborated with Michelozzo (“mee-kell-LOT-soh”) to create the Florence Baptistry’s silver altar of San Giovanni. Alessandro Leopardi completed this sculptor’s equestrian statue of Bartolomeo Colleoni.
ANSWER: Andrea del Verrocchio (“vuh-ROH-kee-yoh”) [or Andrea di Michele di Francesco de' Cioni]
B2 2024 IQBT Undergraduate Championship (2024) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
With his student Lorenzo di Credi (“dee KRAY-dee”), this artist introduced the presentation of the Madonna, child, and saints framed by a loggia (“LOH-juh”) and landscape views in an altarpiece for Pistoia Cathedral. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this artist whose Lady with Flowers was the first marble bust to include the subject’s hands. He made an equestrian statue of a condottiere (“condo-t’YAY-ray”) whose herald contains three testicles.
ANSWER: Andrea del Verrocchio (“vair-OKE-yoh”)
[10] Before turning to sculpture and painting, Verrochio was trained as a smith of this metal. Influenced by Byzantine art, early Renaissance Italian paintings often used the “leaf” of this precious metal.
ANSWER: gold [accept gold leaf]
[10] Verrochio sculpted a bronze statue of Saint Thomas reaching towards Jesus for this building. Nanni di Banco (“NAH-nee dee BAHN-koh”) sculpted the Four Crowned Martyrs in a semi-circular arrangement for this building.
ANSWER: Orsanmichele (“or-sahm-mee-KAY-lay”)
B3 2022 ACF Winter (2022) Diff 7 · Fine Arts
A marble sculpture of this figure by Michelangelo was first installed at the entrance of the Palazzo Vecchio (“pah-LOT-zoh VECK-yoh”), while another artist’s bronze version of this figure was first displayed inside the building. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this figure whom a bronze Donatello sculpture depicts atop the head of Goliath.
ANSWER: David [or Davide; or King David]
[10] The Palazzo Vecchio houses this other artist’s bronze statue of David wearing a leather tunic and standing on Goliath’s head. This artist also sculpted Christ and St. Thomas for the Orsanmichele (“or-sahm-mee-KEH-lay”).
ANSWER: Andrea del Verrocchio (“veh-ROH-kyoh”) [or Andrea di Michele di Francesco de’ Cioni]
[10] Donatello, Verrocchio, and Michelangelo’s statues of David all make use of this pose with an Italian name. In this pose, a figure’s weight rests on one foot, which tilts the hips and creates a relaxed appearance.
ANSWER: contrapposto
B4 2020 Terrapin (2020) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
This sculptor destroyed the head of one of his statues in a fit of rage, but when threatened with beheading by its commissioner, replied that he could return a head to a statue but that his head couldn’t be returned to his body. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this artist who started that equestrian statue, which was finished by Alessandro Leopardi. The long sides of the pedestal for that statue have three pillars each, referencing a rumor that its subject, Bartolomeo Colleoni, had three testicles.
ANSWER: Andrea del Verrocchio (“vair-ROKE-yo”) (All clues refer to his Equestrian Statue of Bartolomeo Colleoni.)
[10] Vasari claimed that Verrocchio gave up painting after this apprentice of his drew an angel in The Baptism of Christ more beautifully than any of Verrocchio’s prior works. This artist demonstrated ideal proportions in Vitruvian Man.
ANSWER: Leonardo da Vinci [or Leonardo]
[10] Leonardo likely modeled for Verrocchio’s statue of David, which is housed in this building. Since it was too tall for the Florence Cathedral, Michelangelo’s David was placed outside this building, but the version currently there is only a copy.
ANSWER: Palazzo Vecchio (“pah-LOTS-oh VEK-yo”) [or Palazzo della Signoria; accept Palazzo del Popolo or Palazzo dei Priori or Palazzo Ducale or Piazza della Signoria; prompt on Old Palace]
B5 2014 College History Bowl (2014) Diff 9 · Fine Arts
Identify the following about materials used in Renaissance sculpture, for 10 points each.
[10] To sculpt his famous David, Michelangelo needed this material, which he obtained from a quarry in Carrara.
ANSWER: marble
[10] Legend had it that the Romans lost their ability to work in this material, extremely prized in Renaissance sculptures, after Diocletian executed the Four Crowned Saints. Purplish in color, this extremely hard igneous rock could not be cut by Renaissance artists and was obtained by taking it from Greek and Roman columns.
ANSWER: porphyry
[10] This Renaissance sculptor created a statue of marble, porphyry and bronze to commemorate Piero and Giovanni de Medici. He taught both Perugino and Leonardo da Vinci.
ANSWER: Andrea del Verrocchio [or Andrea di Michele di Francesco de' Cioni]

T1 2024 Penn Bowl (2024) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
It’s not the Thinker but a 2018 reimagining of a statue of this figure showed him atop a washing machine as part of an advertisement by Samsung. A statue of this figure that was moved into the “Hall of the Clock” was originally intended to be accompanied by a statue by Nanni di Banco. Another depiction of this figure shows flowers covering his chest and pseudo-Arabic script on his tunic is by (*) Andrea del Verrocchio. A sculpture of this figure is at risk of collapsing due to the sculpted tree stump supporting its weak ankles. That statue of this figure was given as a commission to Agostino di Duccio and looked toward Rome in its original placement at the Palazzo Vecchio. For 10 points, name this sling-wielding biblical character sculpted in marble by Michelangelo, the slayer of Goliath.
ANSWER: David
T2 2024 NASAT (2024) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
A Madonna nicknamed after this location is unusually set in front of an open window facing a seascape and features two angels. This location, with a notably long internal courtyard, holds an Annunciation by a student of Andrea del Verrocchio in which Mary reads from a book on a veil on the right. Filippo Lippi's most famous painting is held at this museum, which also contains a tondo Madonna featuring a pomegranate. Another painting at this Giorgio Vasari-designed museum depicts an impossible pudica pose of a goddess identified as Aura. That painting at this museum shows Zephyrus and Chloris next to the title goddess standing on a sea shell. For 10 points, name this Florentine art museum home to The Birth of Venus.
ANSWER: Uffizi Gallery
T3 2023 ACF Winter (2023) Diff 7 · Fine Arts
Workers under this artist used a terracotta pot to create peposo stew during a project for which this artist created a marble transportation ship that sank on its maiden voyage. This artist died before a lantern he designed could be topped by a golden ball and cross designed by Andrea del Verrocchio. Andrea della Robbia created a series of blue tondi decorated with babies for this artist’s construction of the Ospedale degli Innocenti. This artist used stone and chain hoops to connect the inner and outer walls of a herringbone-patterned brick building. After losing a contest to design a city’s baptistery doors to Lorenzo Ghiberti, this artist constructed an octagonal building without flying buttresses. For 10 points, name this Renaissance architect who created the dome for the Florence Cathedral.
ANSWER: Filippo Brunelleschi
T4 2019 RULFO (2019) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
According to Vasari, an artist lost much of his reputation after his colossal “nine braccia and a half”-tall sculpture outside this building did not live up to his boasts. That sculpture depicts Hercules holding a club next to the head of Cacus and was sculpted by Baccio Bandinelli. A handsome young man pins an old bearded man to the ground with his knee in a sculpture in this building, Michelangelo’s Genius of Victory . The title man hangs his feet over a triangular cushion in a bronze rendition of (*) Judith and Holofernes that is displayed in a room in this building whose coffered ceiling is decorated with molds of fleur-de-lys . A putto raises a leg in the air and holds a fork-tailed dolphin in a bronze sculpture in this building by Andrea del Verrocchio. The frontispiece of this building places two statues of lions in front of a blue background. A copy of Michelangelo’s David stands to the left of this building’s entrance on the Piazza della Signoria. For 10 points, name this palace in Florence.
ANSWER: Palazzo Vecchio (accept Palazzo della Signoria , Palazzo del Popolo , Palazzo dei Priori , or Palazzo Ducale )
T5 2016 Penn Bowl (2016) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
Works by this artist confused with those of Il Sodoma include a painting bought by the Marquess of Lansdowne in which an infant Christ plays with a sewing implement. Raphael’s Madonna of the Pinks is probably based on a work by this artist “rediscovered” by Leon Benois. This artist of two versions of the Madonna of the Yarnwinder created a painting with two musician angels on its side panels and another angel by the side of baby Christ identified as either Gabriel or Uriel - that painting’s background features a curved road amidst a (*) craggy landscape. This artist painted the angel at left of the version of The Baptism of Christ executed by his teacher, Andrea del Verrocchio. His other Biblical scenes include the Madonna of the Rocks and a painting in which Judas’ face is cast in shadow at a feast. For 10 points, name this artist of the Mona Lisa.

T6 2016 ACF Nationals (2016) Diff 9 · Fine Arts
This artist drew a pair of intertwined spiral rings to draw a study of an octagonal hat that resembles a torus. This illustrator often drew a “vacuus,” or “empty” version of an object before following up with their “solidus” or “solid” counterparts in a group of works that illustrates the Platonic solids as elements, which culminates with a depiction of a 72-sided polyhedron. A manuscript discovered in 2006 called On the Game of Chess is thought to contain illustrations of chess pieces by this artist, since his mentor Luca Pacioli is the author of that work. Many of this artist’s illustrations are collected in the Codex Arundel and the Codex Atlanticus, and scholars think that this man’s portrait of a man drawn in red chalk is a self-portrait. This artist drew many pictures of tendons and muscles while studying under Andrea del Verrocchio. For 10 points, name this Italian artist of The Virgin of the Rocks whose notebooks contain text written in mirror script.
ANSWER: Leonardoda Vinci [accept either underlined answer]
T7 2013 VCU Closed (2013) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
One of this man's polyptychs shows the nativity flanked on one side by John the Baptist and Saint Michael, and on the other by Jerome and George. In addition to the Albani Torlonia Altarpiece, one of this man's paintings shows at its center Mary being given a wedding ring by Joseph in front of an octagonal building. According to Vasari, this man apprenticed for Andrea del Verrocchio and he took his name from the most important city in (*) Umbria. His most famous work depicts three separate buildings in the background of an overcast painting, at the front of which is St. Peter receiving the title objects, and symbol of the Vatican, from Jesus. For 10 points, identify this Italian Renaissance painter of The Delivery of the Keys, the teacher of Raphael.
ANSWER: Pietro Perugino [or Pietro Vanucci]

An octagonal-shaped building inspired by Andrea del Verrocchio appears in the background of several of this artist’s works, as seen in Marriage of the Virgin. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this artist from the Umbrian school. This teacher of Raphael portrayed St. Peter in front of the Temple of Solomon in the painting Delivery of the Keys.

ANSWER: The Crossing of the Red Sea
