The Yiddish Policemen's Union

An alternate history novel set in Sitka, Alaska, where Jewish refugees settled during the Holocaust and the state of Israel didn't survive. Detective Meyer Landsman and his partner Berko Shemets investigate a chess prodigy named Emanuel Lasker found dead in the Hotel Zamenhof next to a cardboard chess set. Mendel Shpilman was considered the Tzaddik Ha-Dor. A conspiracy to start a war by destroying the Dome of the Rock is uncovered. A rehab facility in Peril Strait serves as a front for raising red heifers. The Federal District of Sitka is about to revert to Alaska.

The Amazing Adventures of Kavalier & Clay

A novel about the cousins Sammy (Clay) and Joe (Kavalier) who create the superhero The Escapist for Empire Comics during the Golden Age of comics. Joe escapes from Prague with the help of magician Bernard Kornblum, hiding in a coffin with a golem. Joe beats up Carl Ebling after breaking into the Aryan American League office. Joe uses a plane patched with dog skin to escape a carbon monoxide leak at a naval base in Antarctica. He bases the character Luna Moth on the Surrealist Rosa Saks. An eleven-page sentence takes up the entire third section.

General / Biographical

Chabon wrote the children's novel Summerland about baseball player Ethan Feld on Clam Island. He wrote the serial novel Gentlemen of the Road set in Khazaria about Amram and Zelikman. Recurring characters in his works include Eli Drinkwater and horror writer August Van Zorn. He contributed lyrics to Mark Ronson's album Uptown Special. He edited Kingdom of Olives and Ash about the Israeli occupation. He wrote the essay 'Trickster in a Suit of Lights' complaining about plotless modern short stories. He and his wife Ayelet Waldman are a prominent literary couple.

Summary of Facts

Michael Chabon is a contemporary American novelist whose work frequently explores Jewish-American identity, comic book culture, and alternate history. His two most frequently clued novels are The Yiddish Policemen's Union and The Amazing Adventures of Kavalier & Clay.

The Yiddish Policemen's Union is an alternate history set in Sitka, Alaska, where Jewish refugees settled during the Holocaust. Detective Meyer Landsman and partner Berko Shemets investigate a chess prodigy (Emanuel Lasker) found dead at the Hotel Zamenhof and uncover a conspiracy to destroy the Dome of the Rock.

The Amazing Adventures of Kavalier & Clay follows cousins Sammy and Joe who create the superhero The Escapist for Empire Comics. Joe escapes Prague by hiding in a coffin with a golem, aided by magician Bernard Kornblum. The novel spans the Golden Age of comics and includes an eleven-page sentence.

Telegraph Avenue is set in Oakland/Berkeley and centers on Brokeland Records, threatened by an NFL player's superstore. The Mysteries of Pittsburgh, Chabon's debut (submitted as his Master's thesis at UC Irvine), follows Art Bechstein's post-college summer exploring bisexuality.

Chabon also wrote the children's novel Summerland about baseball, the serial novel Gentlemen of the Road, and contributed lyrics to Mark Ronson's Uptown Special.
