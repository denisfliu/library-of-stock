Summary of Facts

Jaune Quick-to-See Smith (born 1940) is a Native American artist of Salish-Kootenai heritage. She is best known in quiz bowl for her 1992 works created in response to the Columbus quincentenary. Her most frequently clued work is Trade (Gifts for Trading Land with White People), an eleven-foot-long collage featuring a painting of a canoe with a clothesline of sports merchandise and caps dangling above it. This work is often referenced in questions about Columbus, the quincentenary, and Indigenous responses to colonialism. Her assemblage I See Red: Target, which shows historic representations of Native Americans and features a target inspired by Jasper Johns's series, is also commonly referenced. The Red Mean: Self-Portrait sketches an outline of Leonardo da Vinci's Vitruvian Man over newsprint with a red circle and X. Smith's works are frequently cross-referenced with other artists who responded to the Columbus quincentenary, such as Coco Fusco and Guillermo Gomez-Pena (Couple in a Cage), Shelley Niro (500 Year Itch), and James Luna.
