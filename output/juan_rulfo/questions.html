Tossups

T1 2025 ACF Nationals (2025) Diff 9 · Literature
In a novel titled for this type of character, a writer narrates his father’s resistance during the Dirty War after finding articles about the janitor Alberto José Burdisso’s murder. At the end of a novel, a girl leaps “into the void” after some of these characters invite her to the Big Midnight Feast. That novel titled for these characters is set on New Year’s Eve at an apartment complex construction site and is by César Aira (“SAY-sar EYE-rah”). These characters’ murmurs fill a town at “the very mouth of hell” in a novel in which the hostess Eduviges Dyada (“ay-doo-VEE-hess dee-YAH-duh”), the cook Damiana Cisneros, and the deaf mule-driver Abundio Martínez are revealed to be this type of character. Juan Preciado visits a town inhabited by these characters in Pedro Páramo. For 10 points, Clara del Valle (“VAH-yay”) can communicate with what beings in an Isabel Allende (“ah-YEN-day”) novel partly titled for them?
ANSWER: ghosts [or fantasmas; or dead people; or spirits; or specters; accept The House of the Spirits or La casa de los espíritus; accept My Father’s Ghost Is Climbing in the Rain or El espíritu de mis padres sigue subiendo en la lluvia] (My Father’s Ghost is Climbing in the Rain is by Patricio Pron. Pedro Páramo is by Juan Rulfo.)
T2 2023 Halloween Hippopotamus Invitational (2023) Diff 7 · Literature

That story, Can’t You Hear the Dogs Barking, was included along with Tell Them Not to Kill Me in this author’s collection “The Plain in Flames”. This author’s most famous novel is about Juan’s promise to his dying mother and his journey to visit his father in the town of Colama. That novel by this author was described by Gabriel Garcia Marquez as a life-changing discovery. For 10 points, name this Mexican author of Pedro Páramo.

Bonuses

B1 2024 ESPN (2024) Diff 9 · Literature
An author with this first name wrote a pioneering magical realist novel in which time stops as Hurtado and Julia escape from the bloodthirsty General Rosas. For 10 points each:
[10] Give this first name, shared by the author of Memories of the Future and an author with the surname Poniatowska who wrote an oral history of a massacre of Mexican students in The Night of Tlatelolco.
ANSWER: Elena [accept Elena Poniatowska; accept Elena Garro; accept Hélène Poniatowska]
[10] Elena Poniatowska wrote a biography of this Mexican author, who was also the husband of Elena Garro. This author wrote The Labyrinth of Solitude.
ANSWER: Octavio Paz
[10] The protagonists of a short story by Elena Garro wake up one day transformed into these animals. A man carries his wounded son, the bandit Ignacio, in a short story titled for a sound made by these animals.
ANSWER: dogs [accept perros] (The Garro story is “The day we were dogs,” and the second story is Juan Rulfo’s“Don’t you hear the dogs barking?”)
B2 2023 BHSU (2023) Diff 9 · Literature
In this story, a foreman and an administrator are among a group of people executed in a mock bullfighting game. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this story that ends as the main character meets his son after spending years in jail and working to derail a train. This is the title story of a collection that also includes “North Pass” and “They Have Given Us the Land.”
ANSWER: “The Burning Plain” [or “El Llano en llamas”; accept “The Plain in Flames” or “The Llano in Flames”] (by Juan Rulfo)
[10] “The Burning Plain” begins with the epigraph “Now they killed [one of these animals] / But [her offspring] remain,” taken from a popular ballad. Demetrio Macías joins a revolution after government soldiers kill one of these animals in a novel whose 1929 English translation is titled for a word derived from the name of these animals.
ANSWER: dogs [or perros or perras or perritos; accept The Underdogs] (The Underdogs is the English title of Mariano Azuela’s Los de abajo.)
[10] The Burning Plain and The Underdogs both depict the bleakness of the 1910s revolution in this country, which is the birthplace of both Juan Rulfo and Mariano Azuela.
ANSWER: Mexico [or United Mexican States; or Estados Unidos Mexicanos]
B3 2023 ACF Nationals (2023) Diff 9 · Literature
In a story from Sergio Pitol’s (“SAIR-hee-oh pee-TOLL’s”) collection Mephisto’s Waltz, this animal gives the narrator a message of “twelve enlightening words.” For 10 points each:
[10] A novel by a different author opens with a man’s description of a young woman from the Carpathians painting what type of animal at a zoo?
ANSWER: panthers [accept black panthers; prompt on big cats or felines or Cat People; prompt on black leopards] (The novel is Manuel Puig’s Kiss of the Spider Woman.)
[10] Pitol’s story “The Panther” is included in Sun, Stone, and Shadows, an anthology of stories from this country. This country is home to the author of the poem “Sunstone.”
ANSWER: Mexico [or United Mexican States; or Estados Unidos Mexicanos; reject “Estados Unidos”] (Octavio Paz wrote “Sunstone.”)
[10] Sun, Stone, and Shadows also includes this author’s story “Tell Them Not to Kill Me!”, which is part of his collection The Burning Plain. In a novel by this author, the title tyrant lets the town die of hunger out of spite while he is mourning Susana’s death.
ANSWER: Juan Rulfo [or Juan Nepomuceno Carlos Pérez Rulfo Vizcaíno; prompt on Vizcaíno] (The novel is Pedro Páramo.)
B4 2019 ACF Regionals (2019) Diff 8 · Literature
Foreign-language answer acceptable. A book named after one of these places includes the story “It’s Because We’re So Poor,” which is about a family who loses a cow in a flood. For 10 points each:

[10] This Colombian author allegedly read a gifted copy of Pedro Páramo twice in one night and claimed that he could recite it forwards and backwards. Pedro Páramo influenced his novel One Hundred Years of Solitude.

B5 2017 Letras (2017) Diff 10 · Literature

[10] This Uruguayan author wrote La tregua in the style of the personal diary of the protagonist, Martín Santomé. His El cumpleaños de Juan Ángel is a novel written in verse, while this author explored other forms of poetry in his Rincón de Haikus.
