Questions: Nikos Kazantzakis

4 tossups · 6 bonuses

Tossups

T1 2025 Chicago Open (2025) Diff 10 · Literature
In a novel from this country, village women make sexual rhymes about the large feet of the title shepherd from the mountains. A Penguin Classic from this country collects several “sea stories” and an allegory about a pair of brothers, one obsessed with recovering artifacts from their estate. Naturalist war novels like Life in the Tomb and Number 31328 are characteristic of this country’s Generation of the ‘30s. In a novel sometimes titled for a (*) revolutionary slogan from this country, a man shouts it while clutching the severed head of his nephew, who died defending a monastery destroyed by a powder keg explosion. In that novel from this country, Eminé, the Muslim wife of his friend Nuri, is the beloved of Captain Michalis. For 10 points, name this country where the author of Freedom or Death also wrote about a cook for a lignite mine.
ANSWER: Greece [or the Hellenic Republic] (The first sentence refers to Patouchas by Ioannis Kondylakis. The second sentence is about The Archeologist and Selected Sea Stories by Andreas Karkavitsas. Life in the Tomb is by Stratis Myrivilis. Number 31328 is by Elias Venezis. Death or Freedom and Zorba the Greek are by Nikos Kazantzakis.)
T2 2023 Penn Bowl (2023) Diff 8 · Literature
This author was bedridden with eczema during his stay in Vienna, which sped up his work on a spiritual book containing sections like “The Preparation” and “The Silence.” In a poem written by this author, the soul of the main character separates from his body after his sailboat collides with an Antarctic iceberg. This author’s frequent translator Kimon Friar argued that the philosophy of this author’s book Saviors of God was echoed in a (*) 33,333-line poem by this author, which bills itself as a continuation of an earlier poem. Two lovers in a novel by this author give each other the pen-names of Canavaro and Bouboulina; the title character of that novel by this author reopens a lignite mine on the island of Crete. For 10 points, name this author of The Odyssey: A Modern Sequel and Zorba the Greek.
ANSWER: Nikos Kazantzakis
T3 2023 ACF Regionals (2023) Diff 8 · Literature
A novel by this author opens with a young man dreaming of a village that cries “How long? How long?” That novel by this author ends with its title character repeatedly being called a “coward, deserter, traitor” before dying with the triumphant cry “IT IS ACCOMPLISHED!” A character created by this author uses the pet name “Canavaro” and peers through a café door to watch the narrator reading Dante. In a novel by this author, Satan disguises himself as a guardian angel and persuades the protagonist to live a long life rather than being crucified. In another novel by this author, the title character seduces the hotel-owner Madame Hortense and becomes the foreman on a disused lignite mine in Crete. For 10 points, name this author of The Last Temptation of Christ and Zorba the Greek.
ANSWER: Nikos Kazantzakis
T4 2012 WELD (2012) Diff 10 · Literature
This author wrote a “tragedy in one act” ironically called Comedy. In an epic poem by this author, the protagonist is killed by an iceberg in Antarctica after meeting Motherth and Kapetan Enas. The protagonist of that poem becomes an ascetic, introducing a theme which would be developed in his book of spiritual exercises, The Saviors of God. His minor novels include The Fratricides and Serpent and Lily. In one of his novels, Manolios plays the central character of a play which is staged every seven years in the village of Lycovrissi. He also wrote a novel about Captain Michalis which is usually called Freedom or Death in English. This author of a 33,333-verse sequel to The Odyssey wrote about a vivacious santuri player who seduces Madame Hortense in one novel. For 10 points, name this author of The Last Temptation of Christ and Zorba the Greek.
ANSWER: Nikos Kazantzakis
