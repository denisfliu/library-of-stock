Summary of Facts

William Dean Howells is an American realist novelist known as the "Dean of American Letters." His best-known novel, The Rise of Silas Lapham, follows a paint mogul whose daughter Penelope marries Tom Corey. Silas employs typist Zerilla Dewey and thwarts a sale planned by Milton Rogers. A Hazard of New Fortunes centers on the magazine Every Other Week, run by Basil March and bankrolled by Mr. Dryfoos. The climax features Conrad Dryfoos's death during a streetcar strike inspired by the Haymarket affair. A Modern Instance traces the declining marriage of Marcia Gaylord and Bartley Hubbard. Howells served as editor of The Atlantic Monthly, championing Chesnutt and Dunbar. His Criticism and Fiction discusses the "smiling aspects of life" in American literature.
