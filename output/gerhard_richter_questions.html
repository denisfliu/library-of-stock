Tossups

T1 2021 The World As It Is (2021) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
In 1994, this artist used a carpet knife to divide a painting into 64 squares that were each then sold under the title Souvenir. A fictionalized version of this artist was the subject of Florian Henckel von Donnersmarck's film Never Look Away. The last-ever exhibition at the Met Breuer was this artist's COVID-shortened retrospective called Painting After All, which featured four abstract works titled Birkenau. Curator Hans Ulrich Obrist edited a book about a (*) grid of five-by-five paintings by this artist arranged by chance; he also used chance for his design for the stained-glass windows in the Cologne Cathedral. Some works by this artist were made by applying multiple layers of paint and then wiping off portions with a squeegee, including a series named for John Cage. For 10 points, name this German artist known for his blurry photo-paintings.
ANSWER: Gerhard Richter
