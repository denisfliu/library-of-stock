T1 2018 Sun God Invitational (2018) Diff 7 · Literature
A woman of this first name writes an advice column under the name “Mrs. Green Thumb” and is born despite her mother never knowing she was pregnant; that woman is the protagonist of Carol Shields’s The Stone Diaries. In an opening scene set in Switzerland, a woman with this first name remarks on the number of hotels nearby and dismisses complaints about being unable to get “American candy” made by her younger brother Randolph, who later falls asleep and causes a (*) boat ride to be cancelled. Another woman with this first name repeats “You always look so cool” to confess her love to a man who attended St. Olaf College, and says of her daughter that “the best thing a girl can be” is a “beautiful little fool.” For 10 points, give this first name shared by a Henry James title character who dies of “Roman Fever” and the wife of Tom Buchanan in The Great Gatsby.
ANSWER: Daisy [accept Daisy Goodwill Flett; accept Daisy Miller; accept Daisy Buchanan; prompt on Annie (but roll your eyes because the novella is literally called Daisy Miller)]

B1 2024 PLAYTIME! (2024) Diff 7 · Literature
In this story, a woman recounts how her grandmother died after being sent into the “deadly cold” to collect flowers for her sister Harriet. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this story in which Mrs. Ansley recounts the death of her grandmother from the title disease. This story ends with the revelation to Mrs. Slade that a rendezvous in a certain city resulted in the birth of her daughter Barbara.
ANSWER: “Roman Fever”
[10] This author of “Roman Fever” wrote stories such as “Autres Temps” and “The Other Two,” as well as the novels The House of Mirth and The Age of Innocence.
ANSWER: Edith Wharton [or Edith Newbold Jones]
[10] This title character is warned of Roman Fever by Frederick Winterbourne in a novella by a different author where she visits the Colosseum with Giovanelli.
ANSWER: Daisy Miller [or Daisy Miller] (Daisy Miller is by Henry James.)
B2 2016 BASQT (2016) Diff 3 · Literature
This author wrote of Basil Miller and Olive Chancellor in The Bostonians. For ten points each:
[10] Name this American-British author who used his heritage to contrast the two sides of the Atlantic in works such as The Portrait of A Lady.
ANSWER: Henry James
[10] James often told his stories through these characters whose descriptions cannot be trusted by the reader. Other examples of these characters include Humbert Humbert and Holden Caulfield.
ANSWER: unreliable narrators
[10] In this Henry James novella, the title character is courted by Winterbourne, who finds her sitting in the center of the Colosseum with Giovanelli.
ANSWER: Daisy Miller
B3 2012 LIST (2012) Diff 3 · Literature
The protagonist catches malaria after a trip to the Colosseum with Giovanelli. For 10 points each:
[10] Identify this novella, in which the title character meets Winterbourne and eventually dies of “Roman Fever.”
ANSWER: Daisy Miller
[10] This author of Daisy Miller wrote about Isabel Archer in The Portrait of a Lady and about Christopher Newman’s failed attempt to woo Claire in The American.
ANSWER: Henry James
[10] In this Henry James novella, Mrs. Grose tells the tale of siblings Miles and Flora and their encounter with ghosts of the former housekeeper Miss Jessel and her lover Peter Quint.
ANSWER: The Turn of the Screw
B4 2012 GSAC (2012) Diff 3 · Literature
An unnamed governess cares for Miles and Flora in one of his works. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this author of The Turn of the Screw.
ANSWER: Henry James
[10] Chadwick Newsome leaves Madame Marie de Vionnet, and Lewis Strether returns to America at the end of this Henry James novel.
ANSWER: The Ambassadors
[10] In this Henry James novella, Winterbourne is rejected by the title character, who dies of “Roman fever” after Giovanelli takes her to the Colosseum.
ANSWER: Daisy Miller

T1 2024 PLAYTIME! (2024) Diff 7 · Literature
A novel ends by noting how an audience in this city “is not ungenerous” as a character sheds “not the last” tears of her union. Another novel set in this city inspired “Nationalist Clubs” that promoted a socialist utopia. Julian West wakes up in this city in the year 2000 in the Edward Bellamy novel Looking Backward. Tom Corey courts Penelope while a spot in the newspaper series “Solid Men of [this city]” is sought by a (*) paint mogul. A novel that ends in this city’s Music Hall at a feminist convention led by Olive Chancellor inspired a term for two wealthy women living together in a “marriage” in this city. This city is the setting for a novel in which Verena Tarrant elopes with Basil Ransom, as well as The Rise of Silas Lapham. For 10 points, a political Henry James novel is titled for residents of what New England city?
ANSWER: Boston [accept The Bostonians] (The Rise of Silas Lapham is by William Dean Howells)

B1 2023 ILLIAC (2023) Diff 7 · Literature
The title lovelorn sculptor dies after falling off of a cliff in Switzerland in this author’s early novel Roderick Hudson. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this prolific author who in his New York Edition added prefaces to novels like The Portrait of a Lady.
ANSWER: Henry James
[10] Roderick dies lovelorn after this woman marries an Italian prince. In a later James novel titled for her new title, she meets Hyacinth Robinson, who kills himself instead of carrying out an assassination.
ANSWER: Christina Light [or The Princess Casamassima; accept any underlined portion]
[10] Another recurring character first introduced in Roderick Hudson is the sculptor Gloriani, who in this later James novel, owns a garden in Paris where Lambert Strether tells Little Bilham to “live all you can.”
ANSWER: The Ambassadors

B1 2020 IKEA (2020) Diff 7 · Literature
For 10 points each, name these authors who contributed to developing the notion of the "Great American Novel" in the 19th century:
[10] Many critics cite a novel about a raft trip down the Mississippi by this author from Hannibal, Missouri as the first Great American Novel.
ANSWER: Mark Twain [or Samuel Langhorn Clemens]
[10] This author introduced the abbreviation "GAN" for the Great American Novel. A park in Manhattan titles this author's novel about the heiress Catherine Sloper, who eventually rejects the gold-digging Morris Townsend.
ANSWER: Henry James [prompt on James] (The novel is Washington Square.)
[10] This author of the Civil War novel Miss Ravenel's Conversion from Secession to Loyalty coined the term "Great American Novel" in an essay for The Nation magazine.
ANSWER: John William De Forest
B2 2017 POMMSS (2017) Diff 3 · Literature
This author of The Golden Bowl created a novel where a governess thinks she is haunted by two orphaned children. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this late 19th century American author of The Turn of the Screw and The Ambassadors.
ANSWER: Henry James (prompt on James)
[10] In this James novel, Catherine Sloper lives in a eponymous New York area with her aunt and father. The father tries to prevent Catherine from marrying partially because he lost his own wife.
ANSWER: Washington Square
[10] In this perhaps more famous James novel, Isabel Archer falls for Gilbert Osmond. Osmond turns out to not be an ideal husband because he is having an affair with Madame Merle.
ANSWER: The Portrait of a Lady
B3 2014 Prison Bowl (2014) Diff 3 · Literature
In this novel, Isabel meets her disabled uncle, Ralph Touchett, after the death of her father. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this novel in which Isabel Archer rejects the marriage proposals of Lord Warburton and Casper Goodwood, and discovers that her husband Gilbert had an affair with Madame Merle.
ANSWER: The Portrait of a Lady
[10] This early modernist author of Portrait of a Lady wrote a novella about a governess who believes she sees the ghost of Miss Jessel, The Turn of the Screw.
ANSWER: Henry James
[10] In this novel by Henry James, Catherine Sloper is forced to choose between her love for Morris Townsend and her devotion to her father, Dr. Austin Sloper.
ANSWER: Washington Square
