Summary of Facts

Rosso Fiorentino was an Italian Mannerist painter named for his red hair (il Rosso) and home city of Florence. His best-known work is the Volterra Deposition at the Duomo of Volterra, featuring men in brightly colored robes climbing ladders at unusual perspectives and an oddly green-shaded Jesus.

He was a student of Andrea del Sarto and co-founded the First School of Fontainebleau with Francesco Primaticcio at the behest of Francis I of France. He also painted Moses Defending the Daughters of Jethro (title figure knocking out nude men) and Dead Christ with Angels. Supposedly he and his student Barristino owned an ape that broke roof tiles by dancing on them.
