[B21] 2012 Minnesota Open (2012) | Diff: 10 | Fine Arts
     Leadin: Humor quizbowl's number one Scipione Borghese fan by naming the following artists of paintings now located in the Galleria Borghese, for 10 points each.
  Part 1 (ans: Titian [accept Tiziano Vecelli or Teziano Vecellio]):
    Aphrodite and Cupid sit beside Laura Bagarotto on an open sarcophagus in this artist's Sacred and Profane Love, which is now housed at the Galleria Borghese. This Venetian artist also painted The Rape of Europa.
  Part 2 (ans: Lucas Cranach the Elder [accept no other Cranachs]):
    The Gallery also houses this artist's Venus and Cupid With a Honeycomb. Judith calmly rests her arm on Holofernes' head in the depiction of that scene by this court painter to Frederick the Wise.
  Part 3 (ans: Antonia da Correggio):
    This artist's Danae, in which Cupid and Danae receive Jupiter in the form of a golden shower, is part of the Borghese collection. This man painted the Assumption of the Virgin for the dome of the Parma cathedral.

[B22] 2012 ACF Nationals (2012) | Diff: 9 | Fine Arts
     Leadin: Located in the Santa Maria Gloriosa dei Frari, this work shows two putti holding a cross atop a dark cloud, while below them a blue and gold-clad St. Peter kneels in front of the Virgin Mary. For 10 points each:
  Part 1 (ans: Pesaro Altarpiece [or Pesaro Madonna; or Madonna of the Pesaro Family; or Pala Pesaro]):
    Name this painting which shows members of the title family kneeling below St. Francis of Assisi and the Virgin Mary. It shares its name with a work by its painter's teacher Giovanni Bellini.
  Part 2 (ans: Titian):
    The Pesaro Altarpiece was painted by this Venetian artist, who may be better known for his Venus of Urbino.
  Part 3 (ans: Allegory of Prudence):
    This painting by Titian depicts the heads of a dog, a lion, and a wolf under the heads of a youth, a mature man, and an old man. It is thought to be a triple portrait of Titian, his son Orazio and his cousin Marco, and represents one of the four cardinal virtues.
