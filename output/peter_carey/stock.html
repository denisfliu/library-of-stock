Australian novelist, considered perhaps his country's best candidate for the Nobel Prize in Literature. Won the Miles Franklin Award three times (for Bliss, Jack Maggs, and Oscar and Lucinda). Best known for Oscar and Lucinda (a bet to transport a glass church across Australia), My Life as a Fake (inspired by the Ern Malley hoax), Illywhacker (narrated by the 139-year-old con man Herbert Badgery), and True History of the Kelly Gang.
Oscar and Lucinda

Carey's most frequently referenced novel. An Anglican priest (Oscar/Theophilus Hopkins's son) and an industrial heiress (Lucinda Leplastrier) who are both gambling addicts make a bet to transport a glass church 400 kilometers across New South Wales to Bellingen, to be delivered to Reverend Dennis Hasset. Oscar drowns after delivering the church. Oscar's father Theophilus Hopkins beats his son for eating Christmas pudding (a detail inspired by Edmund Gosse's Father and Son). Oscar receives a Prince Rupert's drop, and Miriam Chadwick assaults him. Won the Miles Franklin Award in 1989 and the Booker Prize. Wikipedia

Summary of Facts

Peter Carey is an Australian novelist frequently identified through his novel Oscar and Lucinda, which is by far his most referenced work. Oscar and Lucinda centers on a bet between two gambling addicts — an Anglican priest and an industrial heiress named Lucinda Leplastrier — to transport a glass church 400 kilometers across New South Wales to Bellingen, where it is to be delivered to Reverend Dennis Hasset. One of the title characters drowns after delivering the church. Oscar's father Theophilus Hopkins beats his son for eating Christmas pudding, a detail inspired by Edmund Gosse's Father and Son. Fanny Drabble bakes the offending pudding. Oscar receives a Prince Rupert's drop, and Miriam Chadwick assaults him. The novel won the 1989 Miles Franklin Literary Award and the Booker Prize.

Carey's second most-referenced novel is My Life as a Fake, a 2003 work inspired by the real-life Ern Malley literary hoax, in which James McAuley and Harold Stewart submitted deliberately bad poems to the magazine Angry Penguins. In the novel, Christopher Chubb creates the fictional working-class poet Bob McCorkle, a seven-foot giant dressed in black, who may or may not have become a real person. The novel is narrated by Sarah Wode-Douglass. Chubb runs a bicycle shop in Kuala Lumpur. A Tamil man recounts killing a Japanese officer by poisoning half of a melon during the invasion of Malaya. Sarah discovers that Chubb's own literary creation may have kidnapped his daughter Tina.

Illywhacker is narrated by Herbert Badgery, a con man who claims to be 139 years old, making it a work of magical realism. Badgery is taught the art of disappearance by Goon Tse Ying, who warns him that a dragon appears every time he uses the trick. The finger of Goon Tse Ying provides visions to Badgery. Badgery was earlier imprisoned for assaulting Goon Tse Ying, and his son Charles runs a pet emporium. The snake-dancer Leah Goldstein has a relationship with Badgery. Phoebe runs away to continue her affair with Annette Davidson after her father Jack McGrath marries her off to Badgery. The novel ends with Hissao accidentally killing a rare bird he is smuggling.

True History of the Kelly Gang is about the outlaw Ned Kelly, who leads a band of outlaws constructing homemade armor in the Australian outback. Carey drew heavily on the style of the Jerilderie Letter. Kelly is betrayed by Thomas Curnow before a shootout in Glenrowan. Mary is given money to emigrate to San Francisco from crimes committed by her lover, who stays behind to await his mother Ellen's release from jail.

Jack Maggs is a reworking of Dickens's Great Expectations from Abel Magwitch's perspective, fictionalizing Charles Dickens as Tobias Oates. It won the Miles Franklin Award. The Chemistry of Tears features a character mourning her colleague Matthew who discovers the Victorian magnate Henry Brandling's diaries while rebuilding an automaton; Catherine is tasked with assembling a mechanical duck that is revealed to be a swan.

Other works include His Illegal Self (the protagonist and Dial settle into a commune after she tries to reunite him with his mother killed by a bomb), The Unusual Life of Tristan Smith (the fictional Efica is dominated by 'Reds' and 'Blues'; a disfigured child is raised by the acting troupe Feu Follet), The Tax Inspector (ends with gelignite destroying a family car business; centers on the self-styled angel Benny Catchprice in a basement of preserved reptiles), Theft: A Love Story (Michael Boone becomes involved in the theft of a painting by Jacques Leibovitz), A Long Way From Home (engages with colonialism; Irene finds a baby's skull 'as fragile and powdery as an emu egg'), and Parrot and Olivier in America (Olivier de Garmont and his servant Parrot comment on each other's memoirs).

Carey won the Miles Franklin Award three times, for Bliss, Jack Maggs, and Oscar and Lucinda. He has been lauded as perhaps Australia's best candidate for the Nobel Prize in Literature. He is frequently clued alongside other Australian authors like Patrick White and Miles Franklin.
