[B1] 2016 ACF Nationals (2016) | Diff: 9 | History
     Leadin: Answer the following about monks who wrote British history in the early Middle Ages, for 10 points each.
  Part 1 (ans: The Ecclesiastical History of the English People [or Historia Ecclesiastica Gentis Anglorum or History of the English Church]):
    The Venerable Bede is best remembered for this five-book work completed around 731 CE that praises Edwin of Northumbria as the ideal Christian king.
  Part 2 (ans: Asser):
    This Welsh monk from St. David's Abbey is best known for writing a Life of King Alfred the Great in 893 CE, after he was invited to Alfred's court and given the monasteries of Congresbury and Banwell.
  Part 3 (ans: Gildas [or St. Gildas Bandonicus or Gildas the Wise or Gildas Sapiens]):
    This Celtic monk from Rhuys is a key source for Britain during the 5th and 6th centuries. He blasts rulers like the tyrant king Vortigern for inviting in the Saxons and sealing the doom of the nation in his work De Excidio Britanniae, or On the Ruin of Britain.
