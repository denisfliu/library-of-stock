Sophocles

← Home · View source questions · Make cards
Ancient Greek tragedian, author of the Theban Trilogy: Oedipus Rex, Oedipus at Colonus, and Antigone. Also wrote Ajax, Philoctetes, Women of Trachis, and Electra. Most commonly identified by 'call no man happy until he is dead' (quoting Solon) and by Oedipus blinding himself upon discovering he killed his father and married his mother.

Summary of Facts

Sophocles was an ancient Greek tragedian, author of the Theban Trilogy (Oedipus Rex, Oedipus at Colonus, Antigone) as well as Ajax, Philoctetes, Women of Trachis, and Electra.

Oedipus Rex is his most famous play. Oedipus investigates a plague in Thebes, is told by the blind seer Tiresias that he himself caused it, and discovers he killed his father Laius at a crossroad and married his mother Jocasta. He gouges out his eyes upon finding her hanged body. The play ends with the chorus quoting Solon: 'Count no man happy until he is dead.' Sigmund Freud drew on the play for his theory of the Oedipus complex. E.R. Dodds wrote about common misunderstandings of the play, and Jonathan Lear analyzed the 'knowingness' of Oedipus.

Antigone depicts the title character burying her brother Polynices against Creon's orders. She sprinkles dust and wine on his corpse, is entombed alive, and hangs herself. Her fiancé Haimon (Creon's son) tries to stab his father and then kills himself. The play contains the famous ode 'Numberless are the world's wonders, but none more wonderful than man.'

In Oedipus at Colonus, the aged Oedipus dies at Colonus, and his tomb in the Grove of the Eumenides protects Athens. Theseus thwarts a kidnapping attempt. Ajax depicts the title hero's madness (Athena reveals he massacred cattle in a frenzy) and his suicide by impaling himself on his sword, followed by a debate over his burial. His 'deception speech' states 'great, unfathomable time brings dark things into the light.'

Philoctetes features a snakebitten archer on Lemnos, convinced by deified Heracles (deus ex machina) to return to Troy with Neoptolemus. Women of Trachis depicts Heracles's death from accidental poisoning by Deianeira; he orders Hyllus to marry Iole on his deathbed. In Electra, the title character cradles a funeral urn and laments 'No mother did that for you. No nurse. No slave. I.' while an old tutor falsely reports Orestes's death in a chariot race.
