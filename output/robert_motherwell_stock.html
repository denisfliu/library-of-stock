Summary of Facts

Robert Motherwell (1915-1991) was an American Abstract Expressionist known primarily for his Elegy to the Spanish Republic series — over 100 paintings featuring alternating black ovals and rectangular forms on white backgrounds. The first entry was titled At Five in the Afternoon, from a Federico Garcia Lorca poem. He described the series as 'a funeral for something one cared about.'

He also created the nearly monochromatic Open series, the Je t'aime series with cursive French text, and paintings inspired by poetry (Mallarmé's Swan, The Voyage from Baudelaire). He edited the Documents of Modern Art series and co-edited the art journal Possibilities with Harold Rosenberg. His third wife was Helen Frankenthaler ('the golden couple'). He was encouraged by critic Meyer Schapiro.
