[B1] 2021 ACF Nationals (2021) | Diff: 9 | History
     Leadin: A failed "Great" one of these campaigns in 1373 led by John of Gaunt saw English forces lose 15,000 people while moving from Calais to Gascony. For 10 points each:
  Part 1 (ans: chevauchee ("shuh-vo-SHAY") [or cabalgada; or cavalgada]):
    Name this type of short raiding campaign, commonly launched by the English cavalry in the Hundred Years' War. This kind of raid focused on pillaging farms and industry while avoiding major cities.
  Part 2 (ans: Edward the Black Prince [or Edward of Woodstock; prompt on Edward]):
    This English prince and eldest son of King Edward III led many chevauchees in the 1350s, including one that ended in the Battle of Poitiers and the capture of John II. He was also the brother of John of Gaunt.
  Part 3 (ans: Battle of Najera):
    In a later campaign, the Black Prince defeated Henry of Trastamara at this 1367 battle named for a small Spanish town, fighting on behalf of Peter the Cruel during the Civil War of Castile.

[B2] 2020 ACF Winter (2020) | Diff: 7 | History
     Leadin: Michael Jones argues that this man was not responsible for the sack of Limoges ("lee-MUZH"), positing that the French carried out the massacre as punishment for letting this man into the city. For 10 points each:
  Part 1 (ans: Edward the Black Prince [accept Edward Of Woodstock, Prince d'Aquitaine, Prince of Wales, Duke of Cornwall, Earl of Chester]):
    Name this English soldier and the first Prince of Wales, nicknamed "the Black Prince."
  Part 2 (ans: condottieri [accept condottiero or condottiere]):
    Edward the Black Prince legendarily knighted John Hawkwood, one of these Italian mercenaries. These soldiers contracted out their private armies to various city-states vying for control in 14th-century Italy.
  Part 3 (ans: Castile [accept Castilian Civil War]):
    Edward the Black Prince fought at the Battle of Najera ("NAH-hay-rah") during a civil war in this kingdom. Another celebrated mercenary, Bertrand du Guesclin ("bair-TRAWN due gay-CLAN"), or the "Black Dog of Broceliande," fought in that civil war in this kingdom.

[B4] 2013 NASAT (2013) | Diff: 8 | History

[B5] 2012 BARGE (2012) | Diff: 8 | History
     Leadin: Political squabbling between the victorious commanders at this battle gave an opening for the defeated pretender, Henry of Trastamara, to assassinate his brother at Montiel two years later. For 10 points each:
  Part 1 (ans: Battle of Najera [or Navarette]):
    Identify this 1367 clash during the Castilian Civil War, won by Pedro the Cruel thanks to English support led by a pair of brothers including John of Gaunt.
  Part 2 (ans: Edward, the Black Prince [or Edward of Woodstock; prompt on partial answer]):
    This man was that brother of John, who in the wake of Najera led his troops on a disastrous, dysentery-plagued retreat through France. His prolific military career included winning the naval battle of Winchelsea, massacring the populace of Limoges, and capturing John the Good and Philip the Bold in 1356.
  Part 3 (ans: Jews [accept equivalents]):
    Henry was able to foment unrest against Pedro by using the latter's support of this faith, which Henry began slaughtering wholesale during the war. Things again got dicey for this group in Spain after the Alhambra decree, which led many to become "Marranos," practicing their faith in secret.
