American expatriate modernist author based in Paris, known for experimental prose and poetry. Life partner of Alice B. Toklas. Art collector and salon host at 27 rue de Fleurus with brother Leo. Coined the phrase 'Lost Generation' and wrote the famous line 'Rose is a rose is a rose is a rose.' Author of Tender Buttons, The Autobiography of Alice B. Toklas, Three Lives, and The Making of Americans. Collaborated with Virgil Thomson on operas. Subject of a famous Picasso portrait.
Tender Buttons

Stein's Cubist poetry collection divided into three sections: 'Objects,' 'Food,' and 'Rooms.' Individual poems include 'A Carafe, That Is a Blind Glass,' 'Glazed Glitter,' and 'A Piece of Coffee.' The collection may be a coded account of her lesbian relationship with Alice B. Toklas. A 1971 letter to the New York Review of Books noted its title 'obviously refers to women's nipples.' Ben Marcus pointed out that Jonathan Franzen's vocabulary metrics fail to contend with the 'associative leaps and logical pirouettes' between Stein's sentences in this book. Language poets took inspiration from it. Aram Saroyan cited it as inspiration for his one-word poems.

The Autobiography of Alice B. Toklas

Stein's memoir narrated in the voice of her life partner Alice B. Toklas. The narrator is described as 'the wife of a genius' and imagines writing a book titled 'The Wives of Geniuses I Have Sat With.' It describes assisting Stein in typing her Cubist poetry manuscripts and stitching 'a rose is a rose is a rose' into Stein's pillowcases. The book recounts the poodle Basket, Stein's maid's hatred for Matisse, and Stein's claim of telling William James she didn't feel like taking a psychology exam. Stein describes sitting for the Picasso portrait. Opens with a parable about a man yelling 'Stop! I did not drag my father beyond this tree.' The truck 'Auntie' appears in the WWI sections. Criticized by artists like Matisse for its lack of realism. Elaine Showalter called Stein's prose 'unreadable, incomprehensible, self-indulgent.'

Three Lives

Stein's first published work, set in the fictional town of Bridgepoint (based on Baltimore). Consists of three stories: 'The Good Anna,' 'Melanctha,' and 'The Gentle Lena.' The Good Anna and The Gentle Lena are both German immigrants. 'Melanctha,' the longest story, is about a mixed-race woman who dies alone after a series of love affairs. James Weldon Johnson declared that 'Melanctha' made Stein the first white writer to write a love story about Black people treating them as 'normal members of the human family.' Edith Sitwell arranged the Oxford lecture 'Composition as Explanation,' in which Stein discusses her process of writing Three Lives.

Sacred Emily and Poetry

Stein's 367-line poem 'Sacred Emily' contains the famous line 'Rose is a rose is a rose is a rose.' The poem opens 'Compose compose beds' and includes seven lines consisting only of the word 'pale.' The poem 'If I Told Him: A Completed Portrait of Picasso' repeats 'shutters shut and open so do queens' and ends with 'Let me recite what history teaches. History teaches.' Her long poem Stanzas in Meditation includes the one-line stanza 'I wish that I had spoken only of it all' and ends 'Certainly I come having come / These stanzas are done.' Her lover forced a change of 'may' to 'can' throughout due to jealousy over an ex named May Bookstaver. The 'word portrait' 'Ada' describes her lover as happier 'than any one else who ever could.' She also wrote the book To Do: Alphabets and Birthdays.

Operas with Virgil Thomson

Stein collaborated with composer Virgil Thomson on two operas. Four Saints in Three Acts features the line 'pigeons on the grass alas' and has Saint Ignatius wondering 'if a magpie in the sky on the sky can not cry.' The Mother of Us All is about Susan B. Anthony, with characters including Daniel Webster, Lillian Russell, and Indiana Elliot; it ends with a ghostly voice declaiming 'my long life, my long life' after a statue is unveiled at the US Capitol. Stein also wrote the play Doctor Faustus Lights the Lights, implementing her theory of 'play as landscape.'

The Making of Americans

General / Biographical

Stein was an American expatriate in Paris, living with her partner Alice B. Toklas at 27 rue de Fleurus, where they hosted a famous salon frequented by Hemingway, Matisse, and Picasso. Her brother Leo was an art critic who co-collected with her. They purchased Matisse's Woman with a Hat, and Pablo Picasso painted her famous angular portrait during his Rose Period (requiring 80+ sittings, finished from memory after studying Iberian sculpture). Stein coined the phrase 'Lost Generation,' used as an epigraph for Hemingway's The Sun Also Rises. She wrote that Oakland has 'no there there' (used as the title of Tommy Orange's novel There There). Her essay 'Miss Furr and Miss Skeene' may be the first literary use of 'gay' for same-sex relationships. Hemingway described hearing her say 'Don't, please don't' through a door. She was controversial for collaborating with the Vichy Regime. The Vietnamese cook Binh works at her house in Monique Truong's The Book of Salt.

Summary of Facts

Gertrude Stein is one of the most frequently clued modernist authors in quizbowl, appearing across both literature and fine arts categories due to her dual roles as a writer and art collector/patron. Based in Paris at 27 rue de Fleurus with her life partner Alice B. Toklas, she hosted a famous salon frequented by Hemingway, Matisse, and Picasso.

Her poetry collection Tender Buttons, divided into 'Objects,' 'Food,' and 'Rooms,' is her most distinctive literary work, featuring poems like 'A Carafe, That Is a Blind Glass' and 'Glazed Glitter.' The collection may be a coded account of her lesbian relationship with Toklas and applied the ideas of Cubism to literature. Her poem 'Sacred Emily' contains the famous line 'Rose is a rose is a rose is a rose.' Other important poems include 'If I Told Him: A Completed Portrait of Picasso' ('shutters shut and open so do queens'; 'Let me recite what history teaches') and Stanzas in Meditation, whose composition was affected when Toklas forced a change of 'may' to 'can' throughout due to jealousy over May Bookstaver.

The Autobiography of Alice B. Toklas, her most readable work, is narrated in the voice of her partner. It describes their life together in Paris, Stein's salon, sitting for the Picasso portrait, and their experiences during WWI. Three Lives, set in the fictional Bridgepoint (based on Baltimore), includes the stories 'The Good Anna,' 'Melanctha,' and 'The Gentle Lena.' The Making of Americans is her thousand-page novel exemplifying her repetitive vocabulary technique.

Stein collaborated with Virgil Thomson on the operas Four Saints in Three Acts ('pigeons on the grass alas') and The Mother of Us All (about Susan B. Anthony). She coined the phrase 'Lost Generation,' famously used as an epigraph for Hemingway's The Sun Also Rises. Her remark about Oakland—'there is no there there'—inspired the title of Tommy Orange's novel There There.

Picasso's famous portrait of Stein, painted during his Rose Period, required 80+ sittings and was finished from memory after the artist studied Iberian sculpture in Gosol. The portrait's angular, mask-like face provoked Picasso's famous remark: 'everybody says that she does not look like it, but that does not make any difference—she will.'
