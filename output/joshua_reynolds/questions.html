Tossups

T1 2024 ARCADIA (2024) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
This institution first hosted a show controversially displaying the portrait of a child murderer made from handprints, Marcus Harvey’s Myra. In a painting targeting leaders of this institution, a young woman leans over the knee of an old man pointing his wand at prints on a wall; that work is Nathaniel Hone’s The Conjurer. A group portrait for this institution by Johann Zoffany incorporates two of its members as portraits on a wall. Thor Battling the Midgard Serpent was a diploma work submitted to this institution by Henry (*) Fuseli. In his Discourses on Art, one lecturer taught students of this institution about his “Grand Manner” and painted Sarah Siddons as the Tragic Muse. For 10 points, Mary Moser and Angelica Kauffman were other founders of what institution, whose first president was Joshua Reynolds?
ANSWER: Royal Academy of Arts [or the RA; reject the “Academy” or the “British Academy”] (The first line refers to Sensation, a YBA exhibition. Nathaniel Hone’s Conjurer makes fun of Joshua Reynolds and Angelica Kauffman. The third line refers to Mary Moser and Angelica Kauffman, as it would have been improper for them to be featured in person for a group portrait that also includes nude male models.)
T2 2024 ACF Nationals (2024) Diff 9 · Fine Arts
Two answers required. One of these artists broke with the other and began exhibiting at Schomberg House after his portrait Three Eldest Princesses was hung “above the line.” The artistic quotation used by one of these artists was mocked by an artist who supported the other in a painting where prints are burned by the title Conjuror. One of these artists called the other’s late work “fancy pictures” but praised his Girl with Pigs. One of these artists said “Confound the nose, there’s no end to it!” of a model the other painted between figures holding a dagger and poisoned chalice. A myth claims that the color theory in one of these artist’s Eighth Discourse caused the other to paint a portrait of Jonathan Buttall. For 10 points, name these 18th-century British rivals, one of whom painted The Blue Boy while the other was the first president of the Royal Academy.
ANSWER: Joshua Reynolds AND Thomas Gainsborough [accept answers in either order] (The Conjuror was painted by Nathaniel Hone as an attack on Joshua Reynolds.)
