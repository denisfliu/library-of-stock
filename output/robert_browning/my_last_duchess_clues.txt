[T1] 2019 Penn Bowl (2019) | Diff: 8 | Literature
     Answer: "My Last Duchess"
  - In an Atwood story named for this poem, Bill breaks up with the narrator because she empathizes with this poem's villain. [PWR]
  - Another response to this poem describes "How walls / rise out of water yet appear to recede / identically / into it" and recommends paying a dowry off slowly. [PWR]
  - That poem is Richard Howard's zigzag-formatted "Nikolaus Mardruz to his Master Ferdinand." This poem describes a figure with "depth and passion of its earnest glance" and a "faint half-flush that dies along her throat." The narrator of this poem refuses to say "Just this or that in you disgusts me," claiming "I never (*) stoop." This poem's subject, who smiles at a white mule or a bough of cherries, has "a heart...too soon made glad" and is painted by Fra Pandolf.
  - For 10 points, name this Robert Browning monologue in which the Duke reminisces about his wife he had killed. [GIVE]
