T1 2024 Notes on the Underground (2024) Diff 10 · Literature
In a story by an author with this first name, the tiger skin rug-enthusiast Frank is trapped in a bottle by a djinn and the most beautiful girl in the world that he had wished for. In another story by that author with this first name, the free verse poet Mr. Snell renounces the world and begins living in a lavish department store only to find an entire society of people already living there, including the nude mannequin larper Ella. This was the first name of an author whose many urban fantasy stories, like “Bottle Party” and “Evening Primrose,” appeared in The New Yorker from the 1930s through the 1950s; that author of the collection Fancies and Goodnights had the surname Collier. In a story by a different author with this first name, the title object first mixes in the hums of (*) electronics to Mozart’s music before allowing a couple to listen to their neighbors. For 10 points, give the first name of an author nicknamed the “Chekhov of the Suburbs” for writing stories like “The Enormous Radio.”
ANSWER: John [accept John Collier or John Cheever]
T2 2022 ACF Regionals (2022) Diff 8 · Literature

Another story by this author ends after a man sees constellations and smells an “autumnal fragrance” as he finds his home abandoned.
