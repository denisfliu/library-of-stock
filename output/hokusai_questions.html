Questions: Hokusai

9 tossups · 10 bonuses

Tossups

T1 2025 ACF Nationals (2025) Diff 9 · Fine Arts
A set of 103 postcard-sized drawings by this artist, rediscovered in 2019, was commissioned for a never-published encyclopedia called The Great Picture Book of Everything. This artist used eight diagonal gray bands to represent a stream in which two mallards search for food. Late in life, this artist engaged in “daily exorcisms” involving painting a lion every day, and painted other big cats in late works like Tiger in the Snow. This artist predicted that his art would finally achieve a divine state at age 110 in the colophon to a series of books in which he called himself an “old man mad about painting.” That series includes over 100 depictions of a site that this artist also depicted in prints like Fine Wind, Clear Morning and Thunderstorm Beneath the Summit. For 10 points, name this artist of Thirty-Six Views of Mount Fuji.
ANSWER: Katsushika Hokusai [or Tokitarō]
T2 2024 ESPN (2024) Diff 9 · Fine Arts
An artist from this country showed a general in a red dress standing on his boat and staring towards a gray mountain in one entry in a 100-painting series whose entries all depict the moon. A witch unfurls a scroll at the left side of a triptych from this country that depicts two warriors preparing to battle a gigantic skeleton. In another triptych, that artist from this country showed cats in various states of being awake and sleeping. The teal-colored bottom half of a work from this country is obscured by a blossoming (*) plum tree in the foreground. A work from this country which uses fine black lines to represent rain over a bridge was copied by Vincent van Gogh. A sky colored using Prussian Blue surrounds the central object of a work from this country, Fine Wind, Clear Morning. For 10 points, what country was home to the artist of the 53 Stations of the Tokaido?
ANSWER: Japan [or Nihon; or Nippon] (The clues are all about Utagawa school artists except for Fine Wind, Clear Morning, which was painted by Hokusai.)
T3 2023 ILLIAC (2023) Diff 7 · Fine Arts
This artist details how to break figures into elementary shapes, colors, and strokes in each of the three parts of his manual Quick Lessons in Simplified Drawings. Towards the end of his life, this artist painted an animal resembling a shishi featuring oddly connecting stripes “in the Snow.” This artist painted scenes of men at a stream “Fishing by Torchlight” and another depicting a whaling party in his Oceans of Wisdom series. Rippling clouds against a prussian blue sky are depicted in this artist’s (*) Fine Wind, Clear Morning. This artist used five yellow lines to depict a natural event in Thunderstorm Beneath the Summit. Three boats are shown traveling through a stormy sea in the best known work by this artist. For 10 points, name this ukiyo-e artist whose Thirty-six Views of Mount Fuji includes The Great Wave off Kanagawa.
ANSWER: Katsushika Hokusai
T4 2023 ACF Winter (2023) Diff 7 · Fine Arts
A technique manual of “Quick Lessons” by this artist emphasizes his method that combined contour and gesture drawing. A work by this artist depicting peasants working at a water wheel shows a young child with a turtle on a leash. A known admirer of this artist, Vincent van Gogh, included many of his common subjects in the background of his Portrait of Père Tanguy (“pair tawn-GHEE”). This artist often used imported Prussian blue in a series that includes his painting Fine Wind, Clear Morning. An erotic work by this artist titled Dream of the Fisherman’s Wife depicts a diver having sex with two octopi. For 10 points, name this Japanese ukiyo-e (“oo-kee-yo-EH”) painter whose Thirty-Six Views of Mount Fuji includes The Great Wave off Kanagawa.
ANSWER: Katsushika Hokusai
T5 2022 Eminent Victorians (2022) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
This artist contrasted a floating leaf representing a peaceful mind and a snake wrapped around a tablet representing obsession beyond death in the last completed work of one series. In one work by this artist, a stack of blue plates form the neck of a spirit exiting a well; in another, this artist depicts a mutilated spirit as a paper lantern with a gaping mouth. Near the end of their life, this artist painted a tiger every day as a “daily exorcism” for good luck, such as in their hanging (+) scroll Tiger in the Snow. Memorial Anniversary and The Plate Mansion are part of this artist’s One Hundred Ghost Stories. Western painting may have influenced this artist’s use of black as a color in their series Oceans of Wisdom. This artist’s erotic series Young Pines includes an artwork with very gross dialogue from a woman being pleasured by two (*) octopi. For 10 points, name this ukiyo-e artist of The Dream of the Fisherman’s Wife.
ANSWER: Katsushika Hokusai
T6 2020 Longhorn Invitational Tournament (2020) Diff 7 · Fine Arts
One painting by this artist inspired a photograph in which leaves from two dead trees and paper thrown into the air are caught by the title Sudden Gust of Wind. In that original painting by this artist, two men clutch their hats while a third man watches his trilby blow away. In another work by this artist, three men camp alongside the banks of a waterfall emerging from a circular cliffside opening. According to legend, this artist chased a (*) chicken with red paint on its feet across a canvas to create one landscape. An elderly cooper constructs an unusually large barrel in the foreground of another painting by this artist. Small boats are threatened by the title force of nature with Mount Fuji in the background in this artist’s best-known work. For 10 points, name this Japanese ukiyo-e (“YOO-kee-OH-ey”) printmaker of Great Wave of Kanagawa.
ANSWER: Katsushika Hokusai (the unnamed Hokusai works in power are Travellers Caught in a Sudden Breeze at Ejiri and The Waterfall of Amida behind the Kiso Road)
T7 2019 RULFO (2019) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
This artist uncharacteristically signed his name sideways and drew clouds with a solid black outline in a series of privately commissioned prints whose cover depicts a microscope analyzing a city. The Boston Museum of Fine Arts holds a painting by this artist in which a woman with her back to the viewer looks down at a mirror on a short black dresser. Possibly because his father was a mirror-maker, this artist often experimented with optics, including in a print in which a (*) mountain has no snow but its reflection does. A young boy leads a turtle on a leash in a print by this artist that depicts peasants working at a waterwheel. That print is part of a series that also depicts the title object in the distance seen through an unfinished barrel. This artist took advantage of his country’s recent import of the pigment Prussian blue for a print of a tiny mountain beneath the crest of an enormous wave. For 10 points, name this Japanese printmaker of The Great Wave off Kanagawa .
ANSWER: Katsushika Hokusai
T8 2015 Penn Bowl (2015) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
This artist supposedly lived in over hundred houses, since he would never clean them, instead moving to a new home until after it became too dirty. At the conclusion of one of the books, this artist predicts that when he lives to the age of 140, every stroke he paints will actually come to life. This artist legendarily painted red maple leaves on a canvas by dipping a chicken in paint and chasing it across his canvas. In one of his paintings, a peasant assembles a large (*) barrel while working in the title province. In another of his works, titled Fine Wind, Clear Morning, the title landform is shown with red hues. This artist, who specialized in a genre of woodblock painting whose name means “pictures of the floating world,” showed a large rogue wave in The Great Wave off Kanagawa. For 10 points, name this ukiyo-e artist who created “Thirty-Six Views of Mount Fuji.”
ANSWER: Katsushika Hokusai
T9 2012 NASAT (2012) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
One of this artist's works depicts a gust of wind that blows away a stream of papers and hats, while another shows a craftsman sitting in a wheel-like object in the process of constructing a barrel. He once won a commission by dipping a chicken's feet in red paint and chasing it across a canvas. In one work, he painted a sunrise hitting a mountain in such a way that it turns red. More notoriously, this artist painted two octopi enveloping a diver in The Dream of the Fisherman's Wife. He incorporated various random sketches into a book called his namesake "manga." For 10 points, name this Japanese print maker who included The Great Wave Off Kanagawa in his 36 Views of Mount Fuji.
ANSWER: Katsushika Hokusai [do not accept "Katsushika"]

Bonuses

B1 2025 WAO (2025) Diff 9 · Fine Arts
The ukiyo-e artist Sharaku primarily painted people in this profession, including a widely-memed portrait of Ōtani Oniji III. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this profession of most people depicted in the yakusha-e genre, which typically showed them in distorted poses with highly caricatured faces.
ANSWER: actors [accept kabuki performers or noh performers; prompt on performers]
[10] This artist started out mostly depicting kabuki actors but later shifted towards depicting everyday people and landscapes, such as in his Thirty-Six Views of Mount Fuji.
ANSWER: Katsushika Hokusai
[10] Utagawa Kunisada painted actors and everyday people as characters from this work in his “modern,” or kinsei, series titled for it. Utagawa Kuniyoshi gained fame through his paintings of this work’s “heroes.”
ANSWER: Suikoden [or Water Margin; or Outlaws of the Marsh; or Shuǐhǔ zhuàn; accept Modern Suikoden or Kinsei Suikoden or 108 Heroes of Suikoden]
B2 2025 COOT (2025) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
According to the preface to its third volume, this series contains “images of everything [the artist] had seen or that his mind had conceived”. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this series of woodblock prints, published in fifteen volumes between 1814 and 1878. The title of this series refers to a style of drawing that is contrasted with utsushi and zu.
ANSWER: Manga [or Mangwa; accept, but DO NOT REVEAL, Hokusai Manga or Hokusai’s Sketches; prompt on sketches] (Utsushi, zu, and ga can be translated as ‘copies,’ ‘pictures,’ and ‘sketches,’ respectively.)
[10] This artist, who began work on the Manga during a stay in Nagoya with the painter Bokusen, later elaborated on a recurring theme of its landscape prints in his Thirty-Six Views of Mount Fuji.
ANSWER: Katsushika Hokusai
[10] Also frequently depicted in Hokusai’s landscapes are these objects, including a “hanging” one between Hi and Etsu. Another of Hokusai’s prints depicts “One Hundred [of these objects] in a Single View.”
ANSWER: bridges [or hashi]

B4 2022 Winter Closed (2022) Diff 8 · Other Academic
