Russian Romantic composer (1839–1881) and member of the Mighty Five (Mighty Handful). Best known for the piano suite Pictures at an Exhibition (orchestrated by Ravel), the tone poem Night on Bald Mountain, and the opera Boris Godunov. Also composed the song cycles Songs and Dances of Death and Sunless. Khovanshchina was left unfinished. He died of alcohol-related illness.
Pictures at an Exhibition

Piano suite (1874) inspired by drawings and watercolors by Viktor Hartmann, exhibited after his death. Memoralized by Mussorgsky as a tribute to his friend. Orchestrated by Maurice Ravel in 1922 (commissioned by Serge Koussevitzky). Henry Wood had also orchestrated it in 1915 but deferred to Ravel's version. Leopold Stokowski made his own orchestration, removing 'Tuileries' and 'Limoges' and also the fanfare from Night on Bald Mountain. The suite includes multiple 'Promenade' interludes depicting the composer walking between paintings. The opening Promenade alternates between 5/4 and 6/4 time in B-flat. In Ravel's orchestration, an alto saxophone plays the melody of 'The Old Castle'; piccolo and high strings represent 'Ballet of Chicks in Their Shells'; a tuba plays the lumbering 'Bydlo' (ox-cart) movement. Vladimir Stasov provided annotations. The suite ends with 'The Great Gate of Kiev.' Wikipedia

Night on Bald Mountain

Tone poem depicting a witches' sabbath on St. John's Eve (Midsummer Night). Assembled posthumously by Nikolai Rimsky-Korsakov from fragments. Depicts the worship of the dark god Chernobog (the 'spirits of darkness' disperse when a distant church bell rings). Leopold Stokowski's 1940 arrangement removed the 'fanfare' depicting the dark god. Made famous by its appearance in Disney's Fantasia (1940). Wikipedia

Boris Godunov

Mussorgsky's most famous opera, based on Pushkin's play about Tsar Boris Godunov during the Time of Troubles. The only complete opera by Mussorgsky. The original version was rejected for having no female leads; Mussorgsky revised it to add the 'Polish Act' and expanded the roles of Xenia and Marina. Heavily reworked by Rimsky-Korsakov (the version normally heard). The Coronation Scene opens with 38 bars of alternating A-flat dominant seventh and D dominant seventh chords, representing tolling bells; the people sing a 'Slava' chorus. Boris has a hallucinatory clock scene where he imagines seeing the ghost of the murdered Tsarevich Dmitry. The vagabond Varlaam has an aria. The 'Farewell' scene (Boris's farewell to his son) is considered the most celebrated aria in Russian opera. Feodor Chaliapin was particularly known for his performances as Boris. The title role was also a signature role of Mark Reizen and Boris Christoff. Wikipedia

Khovanshchina

Mussorgsky's second major opera, left unfinished at his death from alcoholism; first completed by Rimsky-Korsakov. A later completion by Shostakovich restored cuts from Acts 2 and 5 and is the most-performed version today. Depicts a rebellion against Peter the Great by the Streltsy and Old Believers. The Streltsy leader Khovansky is stabbed to death; a group of Old Believers commit suicide by immolation at the end. Features an oboe solo in 'Dance of the Persian Slaves.' The Prelude depicts dawn on the Moskva River. In an 1872 letter to Vladimir Stasov, Mussorgsky wrote 'I am pregnant with something and I am giving birth,' referring to this opera. Wikipedia

Songs and Dances of Death

Song cycle for voice and piano, based on poems by Arseny Golenishchev-Kutuzov. Four songs partly sung by Death as a character: 'Lullaby' (Death sings to a dying baby as the mother defends it), 'Serenade,' 'Trepak' (a peasant dreams of summer while freezing in a blizzard), and 'The Field Marshal' (Death boasts of trampling over mass graves; the piano borrows the Polish chorale 'With the smoke of fires'). Dmitri Shostakovich orchestrated the cycle for soprano Galina Vishnevskaya and responded to it in his Symphony No. 14. Wikipedia

General / Biographical

Modest Mussorgsky (1839–1881), Russian Romantic composer and member of the Mighty Five (or Mighty Handful / Kuchka). Motto: 'to new shores.' Died from alcohol-related illness. His works were heavily edited/completed by Nikolai Rimsky-Korsakov after his death. Other works include the song cycles The Nursery (inspired Debussy's Children's Corner) and Sunless (also based on Golenishchev-Kutuzov); his opera Marriage (an 'experiment in dramatic music in prose,' influenced by Dargomyzhsky's The Stone Guest); Sorochyntsi Fair (unfinished opera including a Gopak); and piano pieces inspired by a trip to Gurzuf. Feodor Chaliapin was a key interpreter of his music.

Summary of Facts

Modest Mussorgsky (1839–1881) was a Russian Romantic composer and member of the Mighty Five (also called the Mighty Handful or Kuchka), alongside Rimsky-Korsakov, Borodin, Balakirev, and Cui. His motto was 'to new shores.' He died from alcohol-related illness, leaving several major works unfinished, which were completed or heavily reworked by Nikolai Rimsky-Korsakov.

Pictures at an Exhibition (1874) is a piano suite inspired by drawings and watercolors by Viktor Hartmann, exhibited after his death. The suite opens with a 'Promenade' that alternates between 5/4 and 6/4 time in B-flat, representing the composer walking among the paintings; this theme recurs between movements. Movements include 'The Gnome,' 'The Old Castle,' 'Bydlo' (an ox-cart), 'Ballet of Chicks in Their Shells,' 'Two Jews, Rich and Poor,' 'Limoges marketplace,' 'Catacombs,' 'The Hut on Fowl's Legs (Baba Yaga),' and 'The Great Gate of Kiev.' The Hut on Fowl's Legs opens with a dissonant falling major seventh from F-sharp to G. Vladimir Stasov provided program notes ('a little gnome, clumsily running'). Maurice Ravel's orchestration (commissioned by Koussevitzky) is most famous; it uses an alto saxophone for 'The Old Castle,' a tuba for 'Bydlo,' and piccolo and high strings for 'Chicks.' Henry Wood orchestrated it in 1915 but deferred to Ravel's; Stokowski made yet another arrangement, removing 'Tuileries' and 'Limoges.'

Night on Bald Mountain is a tone poem depicting a witches' sabbath on St. John's Eve, depicting the worship of the dark god Chernobog; the spirits disperse when a distant church bell rings at the end. Assembled posthumously from fragments by Rimsky-Korsakov. Made famous by Disney's Fantasia (1940). Stokowski's arrangement removed the opening fanfare.

Boris Godunov is Mussorgsky's only complete opera, based on Pushkin's play about Tsar Boris Godunov during the Time of Troubles. The original was rejected for lacking female leads; Mussorgsky revised it adding the 'Polish Act' and expanding the roles of Marina and Xenia. The Coronation Scene begins with 38 bars of alternating A-flat dominant seventh and D dominant seventh chords representing tolling bells, as the people sing a 'Slava' chorus. Act II ends with a tritone ostinato between A and E-flat as Boris hallucinates the bloody body of the murdered Tsarevich Dmitry. Boris's farewell to his son ('Farewell' scene) is considered the most celebrated aria in Russian opera. Feodor Chaliapin and Boris Christoff were famous in the title role. The standard version is Rimsky-Korsakov's edition.

Khovanshchina depicts the Streltsy rebellion against Peter the Great; completed by Rimsky-Korsakov, with Shostakovich's later version being more faithful. Features 'Dance of the Persian Slaves' (with oboe solo) and a Prelude depicting dawn on the Moskva River.

Songs and Dances of Death is a song cycle based on poems by Arseny Golenishchev-Kutuzov. Four songs feature Death as a character: 'Lullaby' (Death sings as a mother defends her dying baby), 'Serenade,' 'Trepak' (a peasant dreams of summer while freezing), and 'The Field Marshal' (Death boasts over mass graves, with a piano borrowing from the Polish chorale 'With the smoke of fires'). Shostakovich orchestrated the cycle for Galina Vishnevskaya and responded to it in his Symphony No. 14. Other works: The Nursery song cycle inspired Debussy's Children's Corner; Sunless (Bez Solntsa) is another Golenishchev-Kutuzov cycle; Marriage was an unfinished 'experiment in dramatic music in prose' influenced by Dargomyzhsky's The Stone Guest; Sorochyntsi Fair was an unfinished opera with a Gopak finale.
