[10] Name this city where one might find Palace Walk.
ANSWER: Cairo [or al-Qahira]
[10] Palace Walk is the first novel of the Cairo Trilogy by this Nobel laureate from Egypt.
ANSWER: Naguib Mahfouz
[10] Another novel set in Cairo is this Alaa al-Aswany novel about the residents of the title structure, who include a gay man named Hatim Rasheed.
ANSWER: The Yacoubian Building [or ‘Imarat ya‘qubiyan]
B24 2012 ACF Regionals (2012) Diff 8 · Literature
One character in this novel is hit by a car on the one occasion that she does leave her house at the urging of her children and in direct defiance of her husband. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this novel about the family of Al-Sayyid Ahmad Abd al-Jawad, who in its opening pages returns at an ungodly hour partying in a decidedly un-Islamic fashion to his wife Amina, who stays up waiting for him.
ANSWER: Palace Walk [or Between the Two Palaces; or Bayn al-qasrayn]
[10] Palace Walk is the first entry of the Cairo Trilogy written by this Egyptian Nobel Laureate, who wrote about the historic assassination of Sadat in The Day the Leader Was Killed.
ANSWER: Naguib Mahfouz
[10] Mahfouz represented himself in the Cairo Trilogy as this character, whose struggle to become a writer is the focus of much of Palace of Desire. He pines after Aida, gets a degree in philosophy, and eventually becomes a schoolteacher.
ANSWER: Kamal
