Summary of Facts

Edward Lear (1812-1888) was a Victorian nonsense poet, illustrator, and composer. His most famous poem, 'The Owl and the Pussycat,' features the title characters going to sea in a 'pea-green boat' and eating 'mince and slices of quince' with a 'runcible spoon.' 'The Jumblies' describes creatures who 'went to sea in a Sieve' despite their friends' reservations. 'The Dong with a Luminous Nose' tells of a character who gathers bark of the Tangum tree to construct an apparatus with a lamp inside after being spurned by a girl with sky-blue hands and green hair. He also wrote A Book of Nonsense and a poem about the King and Queen of the pelicans hosting a party on the Nile for their daughter Nell.

Lear maintained a multi-decade correspondence with Emily Tennyson, named his villa in Sanremo after her, and illustrated and composed music for many of Alfred Lord Tennyson's poems, including a lullaby-like setting of 'Sweet and Low.' John Ashbery wrote a cento named after a Lear poem in his collection Wakefulness.
