     Leadin: In 2019, The Guardian reviewed this pianist's performance of his album Seven Days Walking at the Barbican Center and claimed that he was "as cliched and shameless as a Simon Cowell No. 1." For 10 points each:
  Part 1 (ans: Ludovico Einaudi ("ay-NOW-dee")):
    Name this Italian composer whose piece "Nuvole Bianche" ("noo-VOH-lay BYAHN-kay") has been featured in many films and TV shows. He accompanied his playing with synthesized sounds on his album Nightbook.
  Part 2 (ans: ambient music [accept Ambient 1: Music for Airports]):
    Many of Einaudi's compositions are considered part of this genre of slow and repetitive music meant to evoke or accompany particular backgrounds. Brian Eno's album Music for Airports is partly titled for this genre.
  Part 3 (ans: harp [or pedal harp]):
    Einaudi's second album, Stanze ("STAHNT-seh"), was written for Cecilia Chailly ("cheh-CHEEL-ya shy-EE"), a player of this instrument. Yolanda Kondonassis plays this large instrument with 7 pedals and 47 strings.
