Cormac McCarthy

View source questions · Make cards
American novelist. Best known for Blood Meridian (the Kid, Judge Holden, Glanton Gang), All the Pretty Horses (John Grady Cole, Alejandra, Border Trilogy), and his Appalachian novels (Child of God, Outer Dark). Also wrote The Passenger and Stella Maris (2022). First four novels set in Appalachia; later work set in the American Southwest/Mexico.

Summary of Facts

Cormac McCarthy is an American novelist known for violent, lyrical fiction. Blood Meridian (1985), subtitled "The Evening Redness of the West," follows the Kid as he joins the Glanton Gang under the terrifying Judge Holden, a giant hairless man who dances under a meteor shower proclaiming he will never die and murders a man in the jakes. All the Pretty Horses, the first of the Border Trilogy, follows John Grady Cole who runs away to Mexico with friend Rawlins and has an affair with Alejandra. The Crossing begins with returning a wolf to the mountains, and Cities of the Plain ends with the protagonist killed in a knife fight. Child of God follows Lester Ballard, a serial killer who keeps bodies in a cave. Outer Dark features three unnamed figures who burn a child. The Passenger and Stella Maris (2022) follow mathematical prodigies whose father worked on the atomic bomb.
