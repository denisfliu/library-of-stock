American novelist (1933–2023) known for violent, lyrical fiction set in the American South and Southwest. Major works include Blood Meridian (Judge Holden, the Kid, Glanton Gang), All the Pretty Horses (Border Trilogy, John Grady Cole), No Country for Old Men (Anton Chigurh, Llewelyn Moss), The Road (post-apocalyptic father and son), and the Appalachian novels Child of God (Lester Ballard) and Suttree. First four novels set in Appalachia; later work set in the American Southwest and Mexico. The Road won the Pulitzer Prize.
Blood Meridian

Western epic (1985) subtitled "The Evening Redness in the West." The Kid, born in Tennessee during a meteor shower, first meets Judge Holden when the Judge crashes a religious revival by accusing the preacher of pedophilia and bestiality. The Kid joins Captain White's filibustering militia into Mexico; after that group is massacred, he is imprisoned in Chihuahua. With the earless Louis Toadvine (who has 'HT' branded on his forehead), the Kid joins the Glanton Gang of scalp hunters. Judge Holden is a giant 7-foot hairless polymath who catalogs wildlife: 'Whatever in creation exists without my knowledge exists without my consent.' He tells the ex-priest Tobin 'War is God' and claims he will never die. He is explicitly associated with the devil. The Kid and Tobin escape; they make improvised gunpowder from urine. The Kid is presumably murdered by the Judge in an outhouse during a meteor shower at the end. The Judge then dances naked with strippers. The world is called 'a hat trick in a medicine show.'

No Country for Old Men

Novel (2005), originally conceived as a screenplay. Llewelyn Moss finds $2.4 million in heroin money near the Mexican border (after meeting a dying Mexican man). The killer Anton Chigurh pursues him; Chigurh carries an air-powered captive bolt stunner ('cattle gun') and flips coins to decide victims' fates. He kills Carla Jean saying 'You didn't do anything. It was bad luck.' Bounty hunter Carson Wells offers Moss protection while he recovers in a Mexican hospital. Sheriff Ed Bell narrates; the novel ends with Bell recounting a dream where his dead father carried a moon-colored horn lit with fire. Title from the Yeats poem 'Sailing to Byzantium.' Javier Bardem played Chigurh in the Coen brothers' acclaimed film adaptation. Harold Bloom compared Chigurh unfavorably to Judge Holden.

The Road

Post-apocalyptic novel (2006), winner of the Pulitzer Prize. An unnamed father and son travel south through a devastated American landscape, surviving on scavenged food. The father has only two bullets; uses one to kill a cannibal. He tells his son they are 'carrying the fire' and are 'the good guys.' They discover a wall of human heads and human infants roasted on spits. They find a bunker full of food (peaches, pears, biscuits) and thank 'the people.' An old man who claims his name is Ely appears. The mother had walked into the wilderness and killed herself with a piece of obsidian after an argument about the father's two bullets. The father is shot by an arrow and uses a flare gun; he is also shot by a diesel truck driver and later whittles wooden bullets. The father dies telling his son to 'carry the fire'; the son is taken in by a family. The novel ends with an intricate description of brook trout with 'vermiculate patterns that were maps of the world in its becoming' on their backs. McCarthy was inspired by seeing 'fires on the hill' while visiting El Paso with his son. Set about ten years after an unnamed apocalyptic event.

General / Biographical

Cormac McCarthy (1933–2023) was an American novelist. His first novel was The Orchard Keeper; his first four novels were set in Appalachia, later work in the American Southwest and Mexico. He moved to El Paso, Texas in 1976. He died in June 2023. The Coen brothers adapted No Country for Old Men and reportedly modeled Barton Fink on him. The Tennessee River park Suttree Landing is dedicated to him in Knoxville.

Summary of Facts

Cormac McCarthy (1933–2023) was an American novelist known for violent, lyrical fiction. His first four novels were set in Appalachia; later work shifted to the American Southwest and Mexico. He moved to El Paso, Texas in 1976 and died in June 2023.

Blood Meridian (1985), subtitled 'The Evening Redness in the West,' follows the Kid, who is born in Tennessee during a meteor shower. The Kid first encounters Judge Holden when the Judge crashes a religious revival by accusing the preacher of pedophilia and bestiality. The Kid joins Captain White's filibustering militia into Mexico before joining the Glanton Gang of scalp hunters. Judge Holden is a giant, 7-foot, hairless polymath who extensively catalogs wildlife ('Whatever in creation exists without my knowledge exists without my consent') and tells the ex-priest Tobin 'War is God.' He is explicitly associated with the devil and claims he will never die. The Kid and Tobin escape by making improvised gunpowder from urine. The Kid is presumably murdered by the Judge in an outhouse during a meteor shower; the Judge then dances naked with strippers.

All the Pretty Horses, the first of the Border Trilogy, follows John Grady Cole from San Angelo, Texas, who runs away to Mexico with friend Lacey Rawlins after his grandfather's death. They meet Jimmy Blevins, a 13-year-old who claims lightning deafened him in one ear and who kills a man while retrieving his Colt pistol. John Grady has an affair with Alejandra, whose ranch-owner father betrays them to the authorities. John Grady stabs a cuchillero to death in a prison knife fight and cauterizes his escape wound with a heated pistol barrel. The Crossing begins with Billy Parham taking a pregnant wolf back to Mexico and ends with him crying after failing to call back an injured dog. Cities of the Plain ends with John Grady falling in love with an epileptic prostitute at The White Lake; her owner kills him in a knife fight.

No Country for Old Men (2005), originally conceived as a screenplay, follows Llewelyn Moss who finds $2.4 million in drug money near the Mexican border. The killer Anton Chigurh, who carries an air-powered captive bolt stunner and flips coins to decide victims' fates, pursues him. Bounty hunter Carson Wells offers Moss protection in a Mexican hospital. Chigurh kills Carla Jean saying 'You didn't do anything. It was bad luck.' Sheriff Ed Bell narrates; the novel ends with his dream of his dead father carrying a moon-colored horn lit with fire. Title from Yeats's 'Sailing to Byzantium.' Javier Bardem played Chigurh in the acclaimed Coen brothers adaptation.

The Road (2006), winner of the Pulitzer Prize, follows an unnamed father and son traveling south through post-apocalyptic America. The father tells his son they are 'carrying the fire' and are 'the good guys.' They have only two bullets. They encounter a wall of human heads, infants roasted on spits, and a caravan of cannibals keeping slaves. They find a bunker full of food and thank 'the people.' An old man claims his name is Ely. The mother earlier walked into the wilderness and killed herself with a piece of obsidian. The father dies and the son is taken in by a family. The novel ends with an intricate description of brook trout with 'vermiculate patterns that were maps of the world in its becoming.' McCarthy was inspired by seeing 'fires on the hill' while visiting El Paso with his son.

Suttree is a semi-autobiographical novel in which Cornelius Suttree abandons his luxurious family life to live as a catfish-seller on a houseboat in McAnally Flats on Knoxville's Tennessee River. His friend Gene Harrogate ('the Moonlight Melonmounter') was sent to a work camp for violating watermelons. Suttree Landing park in Knoxville is dedicated to McCarthy.

Child of God (third novel) follows Lester Ballard, a dim-witted serial killer and necrophile in Sevier County, Tennessee, who moves into caves and murders women, keeping their bodies; the novel ends with his body dissected by medical students. Outer Dark features siblings Culla and Rinthy; three unnamed characters burn a child and hang the Tinker. The Passenger and Stella Maris (2022) follow mathematical prodigies whose father worked on the atomic bomb; the sister hallucinates the Thalidomide Kid.

The Coen brothers adapted No Country for Old Men and reportedly modeled Barton Fink on McCarthy.
