Tossups

T1 2024 Untitled Film Set (2024) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
The upbeat score of a scene in this film is briefly interrupted by a man saying “There I was. Staring square in the face of death.” In this film, the camera tracks two newspapers fluttering in the wind after a couple have sex out of joy for a poem one of them wrote. A still from this film appears on the print cover of Mark Harris’s book Pictures at a Revolution. The telegram “I’m talking cinema, you’re talking weather. Au revoir” signalled the end of Jean-Luc Godard’s interest in directing this film. Twice in this film, a man tells a joke about a man who replaced his mother’s (*) milk with brandy. In honor of 50 years of this film, its two leads announced the 2017 Best Picture winner. A car chase in this film popularized Earl Scruggs’s bluegrass track “Foggy Mountain Breakdown.” The New York Times fired Bosley Crowther due to his hate for this 1967 Arthur Penn film. For 10 points, name this New Hollywood film starring Warren Beatty and Faye Dunaway as two bank robbers.
ANSWER: Bonnie and Clyde
T2 2024 TELEOLOGY (2024) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
A review of this film claimed that we cannot blame movies for the fate of the world any more than we can blame Medea for instances of infanticide. A scathing review of this film by Charles Samuels called it “a bunch of decaying cabbage leaves smeared with catsup” before asserting that according to it “society [only represents] convention and force.” The BFI Film Classic book on this film by Lester Friedman identifies a generational divide in its reception led on the negative side by Bosley Crowther and Page Cook and by Kathleen Carroll and Pauline Kael on the other; the latter’s 9000-word review of this film catapulted her to notoriety. Theadora van Runkle’s costume design for this film included a mid-calf skirt and blouse combo with an (*) angled beret and colorful kerchief for the female lead, inspiring a real-world fashion fad. The screenplay of this film was written by the untested Esquire magazine writers David Newman and Robert Benton, who were inspired by Godard’s Breathless to initially offer it to Francois Truffaut. Along with Easy Rider and The Graduate, this film is considered the start of the New American Cinema. For 10 points, name this Arthur Penn film starring Faye Dunaway and Warren Beaty as bank robbers.
ANSWER: Bonnie and Clyde
T3 2024 PLAYTIME! (2024) Diff 7 · Fine Arts
Note to players: Two answers required. After these two characters hug in a wheat field, a hazy filter is used as they visit one of their mothers. A film ends after several quick cuts between the faces of these two characters near a fake flat tire. As these two characters meet and drink Coke, one of them sexually strokes the other’s gun along his thigh and dares him to use it. Bosley Crowther resigned as a film critic after bashing a film featuring these two characters, which Pauline Kael loved. Dede Allen edited and (*) Arthur Penn directed a 1967 film titled for these two characters, whose violence helped kick off the New Hollywood era. These two characters go on a crime spree with C. W. Moss and end up riddled with bullets by the police. For 10 points, name this duo played by Warren Beatty and Faye Dunaway, who were real-life bank robbers.
ANSWER: Bonnie AND Clyde [accept Clyde Chestnut “Champion” Barrow in place of “Clyde”; accept Bonnie Elizabeth Parker in place of “Bonnie”]

B2 2021 ACF Winter (2021) Diff 7 · Fine Arts
