Brideshead Revisited

Waugh's best-known novel about the painter Charles Ryder's friendship with the wealthy Catholic Flyte/Marchmain family at Oxford. Sebastian Flyte carries the teddy bear Aloysius (inspired by John Betjeman's Archibald Ormsby-Gore). Sebastian becomes an alcoholic and retires to a Tunisian monastery. Ryder has an affair with Julia Flyte. Lord Marchmain makes the sign of the cross on his deathbed. Rex Mottram is Julia's politically ambitious Canadian husband. Anthony Blanche shouts passages from The Waste Land from a window. Begins during Eights Week rowing races. Ryder's epilogue use of 'an ancient, newly learned form of words' implies his conversion to Catholicism. Waugh's affair with Alastair Graham at Oxford inspired Sebastian. Paul Pennyfeather from Decline and Fall and Ryder both attended Oxford.

A Handful of Dust

Tony Last leaves Hetton Abbey after divorcing Brenda (who has an affair with John Beaver). He travels to South America where he is captured by Mr. Todd and forced to read Dickens novels aloud indefinitely. Brenda reacts with relief when learning her son (not her lover with the same first name) was killed in a riding accident. The title comes from a T. S. Eliot line in The Waste Land: 'I will show you fear in a handful of dust.' Tony plays 'animal snap' with his son. Dr. Messinger falls over a waterfall.

Scoop

A novel inspired by Waugh's time covering the Second Italo-Abyssinian War for the Daily Mail. The Daily Beast sends the wrong man named Boot (nature columnist William Boot instead of novelist John Boot) to cover a civil war in the fictional country of Ishmaelia. Boot's columns feature purple prose like 'Feather-footed through the plashy fen passes the questing vole.' Boot buys rocks from a woman at the Pension Dressler and gives her a collapsible canoe. The editor Mr. Salter says 'Up to a point' to Lord Copper. Boot convinces his uncle to impersonate him at a banquet. The novel is said to be based on future Conservative Lord William Deedes.

Vile Bodies

Waugh's second and 'most overtly modernist' novel, satirizing the 'Bright Young Things.' Adam Fenwick-Symes seeks to marry Nina Blount and works as gossip columnist Mr. Chatterbox. Agatha Runcible dies after a car racing accident. The novel was Waugh's follow-up to Decline and Fall.

Other Works and Biographical

Decline and Fall features Paul Pennyfeather expelled from fictional Scone College after the Bollinger Club dares him to run without pants. The Loved One satirizes Hollywood funeral culture with Mr. Joyboy and Dennis Barlow. Black Mischief features the African nation of Azania ruined by Oxford-educated Emperor Seth. The Sword of Honour trilogy (including Unconditional Surrender and Men at Arms) follows Guy Crouchback in WWII; Cyril Connolly is satirized as Everard Spruce. The Ordeal of Gilbert Pinfold recounts Waugh's hallucinatory voyage. Love Among the Ruins is a dystopian novella with Miles Plastic. Waugh's first name caused gender confusion (a reviewer assumed his Rossetti biography was by a woman); he married a woman with the same name. His brother Alec wrote The Loom of Youth. Waugh quipped about removing 'the one part of Randolph [Churchill] which was not malignant.' He was a Catholic convert and close to Nancy Mitford.

Summary of Facts

Evelyn Waugh is an English satirical novelist whose quizbowl giveaway is invariably Brideshead Revisited, the novel about painter Charles Ryder's friendship with the wealthy Catholic Marchmain/Flyte family at Oxford. Sebastian Flyte carries the teddy bear Aloysius (inspired by John Betjeman's bear Archibald Ormsby-Gore), becomes an alcoholic, and retires to a Tunisian monastery. Lord Marchmain makes the sign of the cross on his deathbed. Julia Flyte marries the politically ambitious Canadian Rex Mottram.

A Handful of Dust is the second most important work: Tony Last leaves Hetton Abbey after his wife Brenda's affair with John Beaver, travels to South America, and is captured by Mr. Todd, who forces him to read Dickens aloud indefinitely. The title comes from T. S. Eliot's Waste Land. Brenda's relief that her dead son (not her same-named lover) was killed is a key power clue.

Scoop satirizes war journalism: the Daily Beast sends the wrong Boot (nature columnist William, not novelist John) to cover Ishmaelia. 'Feather-footed through the plashy fen passes the questing vole' is Boot's purple prose. Vile Bodies satirizes the 'Bright Young Things' with Adam Fenwick-Symes and Agatha Runcible.

Other works include Decline and Fall (Paul Pennyfeather expelled from Scone College), The Loved One (Hollywood funeral satire), Black Mischief (Azania), and the Sword of Honour trilogy. Waugh was a Catholic convert whose Oxford affair with Alastair Graham inspired Sebastian. His ambiguous first name caused gender confusion, and he married a woman also named Evelyn.
