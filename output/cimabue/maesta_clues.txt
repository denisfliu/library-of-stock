  Part 2 (ans: Florence [or Firenze]):

[B2] 2019 Oxford Open (2019) | Diff: 8 | Fine Arts
     Leadin: In the centre of one fresco by this artist, ten people are dancing in a circle, something which was against their city's laws at the time. For 10 points each:
  Part 1 (ans: Ambrogio Lorenzetti):
    Name this artist of the frescoes of Good and Bad Government in the Sala di Nove, two walls of which depict cities in prosperity and decay, while on the third the reclining figure of Peace sits on a bench with other personified virtues.
  Part 2 (ans: Siena):
    Those frescoes are in this Tuscan city's Palazzo Pubblico, the focus of its shell-shaped Piazza del Campo where the Palio horse-race is held. Its black-and-white-striped cathedral was meant to be the transept of a much larger building.
  Part 3 (ans: Duccio's Maesta):
    Siena Cathedral was home to this huge altarpiece, with the enthroned Virgin Mary holding Jesus on its front panels. Its back panels depict forty-three scenes from the life of Mary and the life of Jesus, but it was sawn up in the eighteenth century and many of the pieces are now dispersed around the world.
