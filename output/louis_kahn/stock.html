Louis Kahn (1901–1974) was an Estonian-American architect based in Philadelphia whose work blended Modernist geometry with monumental brick and concrete. His celebrated buildings include the Salk Institute in La Jolla, the National Parliament House of Bangladesh in Dhaka, the Kimbell Art Museum in Fort Worth, the Phillips Exeter Academy Library, and the Richards Medical Research Laboratories at Penn. He died alone in a restroom at Penn Station and was the subject of the documentary My Architect by his son Nathaniel.
Salk Institute for Biological Studies

Summary of Facts

Louis Kahn (1901–1974) was an Estonian-American architect based in Philadelphia who developed one of the twentieth century's most distinctive architectural philosophies, centered on the opposition between 'served' and 'servant' spaces, an obsession with natural light, and the expressive use of brick and concrete. He famously told his students to 'talk to brick,' envisioning the material as saying 'I like an arch.' Wendy Lesser's biography of him is titled You Say to Brick.

The Salk Institute for Biological Studies in La Jolla, California (completed 1965) is perhaps his most celebrated work. Two streams of water separate three rows of buildings made of teak and varieties of concrete, framing a view of the Pacific Ocean along a plaza. Each office space has a view of the Pacific. The design was a collaboration with engineer August E. Komendant. The campus also includes a citrus grove courtyard.

The Kimbell Art Museum in Fort Worth, Texas consists of sixteen parallel cycloid (barrel vault) shapes with light entering through skylights at the apex of each vault. Michelangelo's first painting — The Torment of Saint Anthony — is a highlight of the collection. A later expansion was designed by Renzo Piano.

The National Parliament House (Jatiya Sangsad Bhaban) in Dhaka, Bangladesh was Kahn's last project. Eight peripheral blocks surround a central octagonal block, and the building is surrounded by a symbolic artificial lake. Multistory cutouts in a repeated pattern of triangle on top of square next to circle mark the outer walls. The assembly chamber is lit by large semicircular windows.

The Phillips Exeter Academy Library is designed in three concentric square rings: the outer ring is load-bearing red brick; the innermost ring is a central atrium with four large circular holes in its concrete walls revealing different floors of the middle ring. A large X-shaped beam of concrete spreads light through it.

The Richards Medical Research Laboratories at the University of Pennsylvania exemplify his 'served' and 'servant' spaces theory, grouping stairwells and elevators in large concrete brick-clad shafts separate from the lab spaces. His Trenton Bath House was an earlier application of this theory. The Indian Institute of Management in Ahmedabad also used red brick for its facade.

Kahn designed the Yale University Art Gallery with a triangular coffered concrete ceiling — one of his earliest major commissions. He also designed the Yale Center for British Art using an innovative mix of concrete and light-colored wood. He designed the First Unitarian Church in Rochester, NY, organized around the shape of a question mark. He collaborated with Isamu Noguchi on an unrealized playground design. His long-uncredited collaborator was Anne Tyng, and he taught Robert Venturi. His body was not identified for two days after he died alone in a men's room at Penn Station in 1974. His son Nathaniel directed the documentary My Architect about his life.
