Summary of Facts

Sofonisba Anguissola was an Italian Renaissance painter from Cremona, considered the most famous female painter of the Italian Renaissance. Her best-known work, The Chess Game (1555), depicts her sisters Lucia, Minerva, and Europa playing chess while a maid observes. Europa laughs as Minerva raises her hand because Lucia is winning. It is signed 'virgin daughter of Amilcare' on a board.

Her chalk drawing Boy Bitten by a Crawfish shows her brother Asdrubale crying after being bitten by a crustacean. Michelangelo was reportedly impressed by it, and according to Roberto Longhi, it influenced Caravaggio's Boy Bitten by a Lizard. She was a student of Bernardino Campi and created a self-portrait showing Campi painting her.

Anguissola served as court painter to Philip II of Spain, arriving via recommendation of the Duke of Alba and tutoring Elizabeth of Valois. Anthony van Dyck visited her when she was around 92 years old and painted her portrait. Her self-portrait miniature features a green medallion with a monogram of her sisters' initials. A Joseph Cornell shadowbox incorporates a copy of one of her portraits.
