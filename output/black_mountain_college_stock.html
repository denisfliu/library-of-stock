Summary of Facts

Black Mountain College was an experimental liberal arts college in North Carolina (1933-1957), founded by Josef and Anni Albers after the Nazis shut down the Bauhaus (Philip Johnson arranged their positions). Josef Albers ran it for a decade and created studies for his Homage to the Square series there.

The college was a crucible for interdisciplinary art. John Cage performed Theater Piece No. 1, an early 'happening.' Robert Rauschenberg studied there and was introduced to photograms by Susan Weil. Ruth Asawa created her signature looped-wire hangings after watching basket makers in Toluca. Merce Cunningham founded his dance company at the school. M.C. Richards translated Erik Satie's The Ruse of Medusa for a performance.

In literature, it was the home of the Black Mountain Poets, including Charles Olson (advocate of 'projective verse'), Robert Creeley, and Denise Levertov. Anni Albers taught textiles (first textile designer with a MoMA solo show in 1949) and wrote On Weaving, inspired by Peruvian weavers. Tom Wolfe mocked Albers's paper exercises in his book about the school.
