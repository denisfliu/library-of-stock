American playwright, Nobel laureate (1936). Most commonly identified by Long Day's Journey into Night (Tyrone family, Mary's morphine addiction, Edmund's tuberculosis, fog motif), The Iceman Cometh (Harry Hope's saloon, Hickey, pipe dreams), The Emperor Jones (Brutus Jones, silver bullet, drums), The Hairy Ape (Yank, Mildred Douglas, 'filthy beast,' gorilla), and Anna Christie (Mat Burke, Chris Christopherson, prostitution). Founded with Provincetown Players by Susan Glaspell. Father James O'Neill starred in Count of Monte Cristo 6,000+ times. Daughter Oona married Charlie Chaplin.
Long Day's Journey into Night

O'Neill's autobiographical masterwork about the dysfunctional Tyrone family over one long day. Mary Tyrone is addicted to morphine; Edmund has tuberculosis; the actor James is dissatisfied with having played one role (Count of Monte Cristo) throughout his career; Jamie is an alcoholic. Fog and foghorn are recurring motifs. Mary complains about the foghorn and reminisces about her youth in a convent, holding her wedding dress at the end. The father and son argue about keeping a lightbulb on. Edmund quotes Swinburne and wishes he'd been born a 'seagull or a fish.' Mary plays piano at the very end despite maimed fingers. Leon Edel biography volumes.

Hugo declaims 'The days grow hot, O Babylon!' Rocky denies being a pimp. Joe Mott rails against racial abuse. The play marked O'Neill's return to Broadway after 12 years.

Jones stumbles through a forest seeing Little Formless Fears, a slave auction, and the ghost of the murdered Jeff. Smithers advises him to flee. Lem leads a rebellion. Paul Robeson starred.

The Hairy Ape

Yank (Bob Smith), a ship's coal stoker, is called 'oh, the filthy beast!' by the steel heiress Mildred Douglas. He adopts the pose of Rodin's The Thinker and tries to join the IWW but is kicked out for proposing to blow up a steel factory. He passes rich pedestrians wearing masks on Fifth Avenue. Ends when a gorilla crushes Yank at the zoo; stage direction notes he 'at last belongs.'

Anna Christie

First act set in Johnny the Priest's saloon. The 1930 film had the tagline 'Garbo talks!' S.S. Glencairn plays are related one-act works.

Summary of Facts

Eugene O'Neill is the foundational American playwright in quizbowl, appearing across all difficulty levels. Long Day's Journey into Night is his most heavily clued work, centering on the autobiographical Tyrone family: Mary (morphine addict), James (actor who played Count of Monte Cristo 6,000+ times), Jamie (alcoholic), and Edmund (tuberculosis). Fog serves as a central motif. Mary ends the play holding her wedding dress and playing piano despite maimed fingers.

The Iceman Cometh is set in Harry Hope's saloon, where the salesman Hickey confesses to murdering his wife Evelyn and urges regulars to give up their 'pipe dreams.' Don Parritt jumps off a fire escape. The Emperor Jones features Brutus Jones, a former Pullman porter ruling a Caribbean island who claims he can only be killed by a silver bullet, with drums steadily accelerating throughout. Paul Robeson starred. The Hairy Ape features the stoker Yank, called 'filthy beast' by Mildred Douglas, who adopts the pose of Rodin's Thinker and is ultimately crushed by a gorilla at the zoo.

Anna Christie depicts a former prostitute who defies her father Chris Christopherson to marry the shipwreck survivor Mat Burke. Strange Interlude features Nina Leeds and nine acts of lengthy soliloquies. A Moon for the Misbegotten is a sequel to Long Day's Journey featuring Jamie Tyrone. Ah, Wilderness! is O'Neill's only comedy. The Provincetown Players, co-founded with Susan Glaspell, premiered many early works. O'Neill's daughter Oona married Charlie Chaplin. His Nobel speech acknowledged a debt to Strindberg.
