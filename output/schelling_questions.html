Tossups

T1 2024 ARCADIA (2024) Diff 8 · Philosophy
A thinker from this country used the Greek name Aenesidemus in a dialogue refuting a theory from Philosophy of the Elements, written by another thinker from this country. Reality is grounded in an original unity or a primordial totality of opposites according to a thinker from this country who wrote Ideas for a Philosophy of Nature. A thinker from this country grounded their thought in a “pure I” to avoid dogmatism and sought a doctrine of (*) science. Attempts to understand reality through reason can lead to antinomies according to a thinker from this country. An Attempt at a Critique of All Revelation was written by a thinker from this country who developed the concepts of thesis, antithesis, and synthesis. For 10 points, name this country, home of the author of The Phenomenology of Spirit, G. W. F. Hegel.
ANSWER: Germany [or Deutschland] (The thinkers in the first line are Gottlob Ernst Schulze and Karl Leonard Reinhold. Ideas for a Philosophy of Nature is by Friedrich Wilhelm Joseph Schelling.)
T2 2019 ACF Nationals (2019) Diff 9 · Philosophy
This philosopher claimed the Arabic copula is analogous to “can” and characterized the “is” relation as transitive, not attributive. He called existence the “link of a being as One, with itself as a multiplicity” and called being “revelation” as part of his identity philosophy. This non-Comte philosopher characterized his early work as a “negative” project to explain the logic of change and sought a “positive philosophy” that moved beyond the “merely logical relationship between God and the world” in Die Weltalter. This philosopher’s notion of a “point of absolute indifference” was mocked by another philosopher as a “night in which all cows are black.” Manfred Frank argues Feuerbach and Marx drew their critique of Hegel from this thinker, who was Hegel’s former roommate. For 10 points, name this German idealist who developed his early Naturphilosophie in works like System of Transcendental Idealism.
ANSWER: Friedrich (Wilhelm Joseph) von Schelling

[10] This German idealist philosopher popularized the term Naturphilosophie in a sequence of works on the concept. This thinker later wrote Ages of the World and the “Freedom Essay.”

B2 2019 Spartan Housewrite (2019) Diff 8 · Philosophy

[10] Name this 19th century philosopher whose lectures collected in his Philosophy of Mythology include a discussion of his "philosophical theology.” This philosopher also lectured on positive philosophy while holding a post at University of Berlin.
