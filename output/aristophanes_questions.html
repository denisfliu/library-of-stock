B8 2022 Chicago Open (2022) Diff 10 · Literature
For 10 points each, answer the following about mimesis (“mim-MEE-siss”) in ancient Greek literature: Note to players: word forms acceptable.
[10] In Book III (“three”) of the Republic, Socrates contrasts mimesis with this other mode of narration. Though this Greek term is often simplified to “telling” rather than the “showing” of mimesis, the distinction is imprecise in many ancient sources, as Socrates claimed that poets can accomplish this mode “by means of mimesis.”
ANSWER: diegesis [accept word forms, e.g. diegetic mode]
[10] This treatise by Aristotle claims that mimesis is the underlying principle that drives the creation of all art forms. This book of literary theory discusses tragedy and analyzes epic poetry, which it labels “diegematic mimesis,” though its section discussing comedy is lost.
ANSWER: Poetics [or Peri poietikês]
[10] This play contains the first documented use of the word mimesis in a passage in which a character states “Whatever we don't have, we must capture by mimesis.” Characters in this play include a woman who sells myrtle wreaths, and Micca, who desperately tries to save some spilled wine.
ANSWER: Thesmo·phoria·zusae [or Women Celebrating the Thesmophoria] {Thesmophoriazusae is by Aristophanes; in the passage, Agathon is talking about cross-dressing.}
