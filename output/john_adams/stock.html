General / Biographical

John Coolidge Adams (b. 1947) is an American minimalist and post-minimalist composer. He frequently collaborates with theater director Peter Sellars, who has directed and written libretti for many of his operas. His website is earbox.com. He should not be confused with John Luther Adams (an Alaskan composer best known for Become Ocean). Adams described his pieces as coming in twos — 'serious' and 'trickster.' He depicted his dreams of an oil tanker taking off from water and two limousines transforming into pianos in Harmonielehre and Grand Pianola Music. He has been associated with the San Francisco Symphony and California arts institutions. Wikipedia

Nixon in China →

Three-act opera premiered in 1987, with libretto by Alice Goodman and direction by Peter Sellars. Depicts Nixon's 1972 visit to Beijing to meet Chairman Mao. Opens with the chorus 'The people are the heroes now' as the presidential plane 'Spirit of '76' lands in Beijing airfield. Includes an aria called 'News Has a Kind of Mystery' and a scene where Nixon and Mao's wife attend a performance of The Red Detachment of Women. The work was preceded by the orchestral piece The Chairman Dances. Pat Nixon sings 'This is prophetic' in the Summer Palace. Soprano Dawn Upshaw's recording includes her singing 'This Is Prophetic.' Summary of Facts

John Coolidge Adams (b. 1947) is an American minimalist and post-minimalist composer, best known for his operas Nixon in China and The Death of Klinghoffer, his orchestral fanfare Short Ride in a Fast Machine, and his 9/11 memorial On the Transmigration of Souls. His website is earbox.com, and he is frequently associated with California — he was affiliated with the San Francisco Conservatory and the San Francisco Symphony.

His operas are closely tied to theater director Peter Sellars, who has directed and often written libretti for them. Nixon in China (1987), with libretto by Alice Goodman, depicts Richard Nixon's 1972 visit to Beijing. It opens with the chorus 'The people are the heroes now' as Air Force One (the 'Spirit of '76') lands in a Beijing airfield. Nixon and Mao's wife attend a performance of The Red Detachment of Women. Pat Nixon's aria 'This is prophetic' is sung in the Summer Palace. The opera was preceded by the orchestral piece The Chairman Dances.

The Death of Klinghoffer (1991), also with libretto by Alice Goodman and direction by Peter Sellars, dramatizes the 1985 hijacking of the cruise ship Achille Lauro. Leon Klinghoffer sings the 'Aria of the Falling Body' as he is thrown overboard. Act II opens with the chorus 'Hagar and the Angel' and ends when a character is shot by the hijacker Molqi.

Doctor Atomic (2005), with a libretto by Sellars, dramatizes J. Robert Oppenheimer and the Trinity nuclear test. Oppenheimer sings a setting of John Donne's sonnet 'Batter my heart, three-person'd God'; the opera also uses a traditional Tewa song and the Bhagavad Gita.

On the Transmigration of Souls (2002) is a 'memory space' for orchestra, children's choir, and a tape playing city sounds (traffic, footsteps, a siren) and reading the names of those who died in the September 11 attacks, drawn from the New York Times's 'Portraits of Grief.' A boy's voice repeats 'missing' in a heartbeat rhythm. It won the 2003 Pulitzer Prize.

Short Ride in a Fast Machine (1986) is a fanfare for orchestra composed for the Pittsburgh Symphony's Great Woods Festival. Inspired by the feeling of riding in a fast sports car and then regretting it, it features a woodblock ostinato that creates a 'rhythmic tunnel.' Other notable works: Harmonielehre (in which Adams depicted dreaming of an oil tanker lifting off from water and two limousines turning into pianos); Shaker Loops for strings (inner movements 'Hymning Slews' and 'Loops and Verses'); Phrygian Gates (piano; begins in the Lydian mode of A); the nativity oratorio El Niño (Dawn Upshaw sang the premiere); and the 2017 opera Girls of the Golden West (inspired by Puccini's La fanciulla del West).

Adams cited Conlon Nancarrow's player piano works as inspiration for Century Rolls (movements 'Manny's Gym' and 'Hail, Bop') and Grand Pianola Music. He should not be confused with John Luther Adams (an Alaskan composer whose piece Become Ocean won the 2014 Pulitzer Prize for Music and a Grammy).
