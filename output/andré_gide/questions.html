For 10 points, name this French author of The Counterfeiters and The Immoralist.

B1 2023 ILLIAC (2023) Diff 7 · Literature
In a novel by this author, the blind girl Gertrude regains her eyesight and falls in love with a pastor’s son. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this author of the novel The Pastoral Symphony. In a different novel by this author, the tuberculosis-suffering protagonist watches the boy Moktir steal a pair of scissors.
ANSWER: André Gide [or André Paul Guillaume Gide] (The other novel is The Immoralist.)
[10] A town suddenly regains its eyesight at the end of the novel Blindness written by this Portuguese author.
ANSWER: José Saramago [or José de Sousa Saramago]
[10] In a novel by the Spanish author Benito Pérez Galdós, a boy with this first name regains his eyesight and rejects the girl Marianela. The final section of a twelve-part poem by a poet with this first name declares, “Rise up in birth with me, my brother.”
ANSWER: Pablo [accept Pablo Neruda] (The poem is “The Heights of Macchu Picchu.”)
B2 2023 BHSU (2023) Diff 9 · Literature
John Milton’s “Lycidas” closely imitates a Latin poem by this writer, which includes the line “Love conquers all; let us then yield to love!” For 10 points each:
[10] Name this Roman poet of the Eclogues and the Georgics. Another poem by this author begins “I sing of arms and the man.”
ANSWER: Virgil [or Publius Vergilius Maro] (The unnamed poem is the Aeneid [I guess])
[10] This other poet is the primary speaker of the tenth Eclogue, in which he is given the line “Omnia vincit amor.” The Eclogue depicts him dying due to his unrequited love for Lycoris.
ANSWER: Gaius Cornelius Gallus
[10] This author of the dialogue series Corydon drew character names from Virgil’s Eclogues for several of his novels. In one such novel, Menalque challenges his friend, a Protestant academic, to embrace homosexuality.
ANSWER: André Gide [or André Paul Guillaume Gide]
B3 2022 WORKSHOP (2022) Diff 8 · Literature
In a list poem titled for this character’s “Workout,” Anne Carson notes that this character’s name occurs 2,363 times in the novel she appears in, and that “on a good 19 per cent of these pages she is asleep.” For 10 points each:
[10] Name this character who is first glimpsed on a beach in Within a Budding Grove and later dies in a riding accident. In The Prisoner, the narrator jealously imprisons this character in his house over her lesbian affairs.
ANSWER: Albertine Simonet [or Albertine Simonet; accept The Albertine Workout]
[10] The narrator, who is Albertine’s lover, struggles to sleep without his mother’s goodnight kiss in this author’s novel In Search of Lost Time.
ANSWER: Marcel Proust
[10] The Albertine Workout discusses this author’s “transposition theory” that Proust’s chauffeur inspired Albertine. In a novel by this author, Gertrude realizes that she loves the pastor’s son Jacques after gaining her eyesight.
ANSWER: André Gide (“zheed”) (The novel is The Pastoral Symphony.)
