Summary of Facts

Matthew Barney is an American contemporary artist whose quizbowl presence is dominated by the Cremaster Cycle, a five-part film series (1994-2002) named for the cremaster muscle that controls testicular descent. The films represent the male gonads in increasingly descended states and feature elaborate, surreal imagery. Cremaster 3 is the most frequently referenced installment: it was partially filmed at the Solomon R. Guggenheim Museum, where Richard Serra throws molten Vaseline at Frank Lloyd Wright's iconic spiral ramp; its prologue and epilogue reference Fingal's Cave on Staffa; and it features double amputee athlete Aimee Mullins in dual roles. Cremaster 4 depicts the Loughton Candidate, a red-haired faun tunneling beneath the Isle of Man during a motorcycle race. Cremaster 5 features pigeons tied to the genitals of a diva played by Barney himself. Norman Mailer played Harry Houdini in Cremaster 2.

Barney's other major work is River of Fundament (2014), an experimental filmed opera focused on Norman Mailer's death and reincarnation, with Mailer's body represented by various cars. His Drawing Restraint series involves creating art under physical constraints — he painted while jumping on a trampoline or attached to bungee cords. Drawing Restraint 9 was filmed on a Japanese harpoon ship with his then-partner Bjork, who plays an 'occidental guest' engaging in Shinto rituals. The OTTO Trilogy is a series of linked installations, videos, art books, and films focused on NFL player Jim Otto. Barney also collaborated with Elizabeth Peyton on a performance piece titled Blood of Two. Questions about Barney frequently appear at difficulty 9-10, and the Cremaster Cycle has appeared as its own answerline.
