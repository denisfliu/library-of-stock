Nixon in China is a 1987 opera by John Adams with libretto by Alice Goodman, depicting President Richard Nixon's historic 1972 diplomatic visit to China. One of the most important American operas of the late 20th century, it is associated with the minimalist tradition and features characters including Nixon, Pat Nixon, Mao Zedong, Jiang Qing, and Premier Zhou Enlai.

Summary of Facts

Nixon in China (1987) is the first opera by American composer John Adams, with a libretto by Alice Goodman and original stage direction by Peter Sellars. The opera fictionalizes President Richard Nixon's historic 1972 diplomatic visit to China to meet Mao Zedong. It is a landmark of American minimalist opera and draws on the political theater of the Cold War.

The opera opens with the landing of Air Force One — called here the Spirit of '76 — after which Nixon delivers the extended aria 'News has a kind of mystery,' repeating the word 'news' twelve times as he marvels at the significance of his visit. A chorus sings 'The Three Main Rules of Discipline' at the start. In Act 2, Nixon and his wife Pat attend a performance of the Chinese revolutionary ballet The Red Detachment of Women, overseen by Jiang Qing (Madame Mao). Pat Nixon, moved by what she thinks is a character being whipped to death, rushes onstage to intervene. Pat receives a small glass elephant at the Summer Palace and sings the aria 'This is prophetic,' imagining a future when luxury dissolves into the atmosphere. Jiang Qing sings 'I am the wife of Mao Tse-tung' and proclaims 'the revolution must not end.' The opera closes with Zhou Enlai asking the haunting question 'How much of what we did was good?' The orchestra is notably augmented by two pianos and four saxophones.

The orchestral intermezzo The Chairman Dances — subtitled 'Foxtrot for Orchestra' — is frequently performed as a standalone concert piece. In it, Mao descends from his portrait to dance a foxtrot with Jiang Qing. Peter Sellars, who produced the original production and collaborated with Adams on later operas (Girls of the Golden West, Doctor Atomic), wrote the libretti for several Adams works though Alice Goodman wrote the text for Nixon in China and The Death of Klinghoffer specifically. The opera's premiere featured soprano Dawn Upshaw, and the aria 'This is prophetic' has been recorded by Upshaw on The World Is So Wide.
