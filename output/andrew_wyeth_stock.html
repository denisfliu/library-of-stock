Summary of Facts

Andrew Wyeth was an American Realist painter based in Chadds Ford, Pennsylvania and Cushing, Maine, the son of illustrator N.C. Wyeth and father of painter Jamie Wyeth.

Christina's World (1948) is his most famous painting, depicting a paralyzed woman (Christina Olson, who had Charcot-Marie-Tooth disease) in a pink dress crawling through grass toward the Olson House in Cushing, Maine. The Farnsworth Art Museum operates the house.

The Helga Pictures comprise 247+ portraits of his German neighbor Helga Testorf, many nude, created secretly over 14 years in Chadds Ford. They were purchased by Leonard Andrews and became a sensation when revealed in cover stories by Time and Newsweek. Individual works include Lovers (nude woman on stool by window), Braids, Overflow, Black Velvet, and The Prussian.

Winter 1946 depicts a boy running down Kuerner's Hill, the first painting after his father was killed in a car/train accident at that site. He learned egg-tempera from brother-in-law Peter Hurd. Other works include Snow Hill, The Master Bedroom, Barracoon (painted Helga as a Black woman), Breakup (hands on ice), and Chambered Nautilus.
