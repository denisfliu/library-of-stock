Summary of Facts

Andrew Wyeth (1917-2009) was an American Realist painter based in Chadds Ford, Pennsylvania and Cushing, Maine, the son of illustrator N.C. Wyeth (who illustrated Treasure Island and Rip van Winkle for Scribner's) and father of painter Jamie Wyeth (Portrait of Pig, pumpkinhead self-portrait, posthumous JFK portrait). He learned the egg-tempera technique from his brother-in-law Peter Hurd.

Christina's World (1948) is his most iconic painting, depicting a paralyzed woman (Christina Olson, who had Charcot-Marie-Tooth disease, often misidentified as polio) in a pink dress crawling through grass toward the Olson House in Cushing, Maine. Wyeth's wife Betsy James, 30 years younger than Christina, modeled the upper body. The Farnsworth Art Museum now operates the Olson House, and the painting hangs at the top of the escalator at MoMA. Wyeth met Christina while picking blueberries and chose to be buried at the painting's real-life setting. He described it as depicting 'an extraordinary conquest of a life which most people would consider hopeless.' Related works include Wind from the Sea (fluttering curtains in Christina's house), Kitchen at Olson's, and Maga's Daughter (Betsy modeling for Christina). After Christina and her brother Alvaro died, Wyeth painted brown and blue doors representing them. The painting inspired Terence Malick's Days of Heaven, Bernard Langlais's sculpture Local Girl, and was reimagined by Kennardphillipps for a 2015 Greenpeace campaign. It also influenced Chinese 'rustic realism,' including He Duoling's Spring Breeze is Awake.

The Helga Pictures (1971-1985) comprise 247+ portraits of his German neighbor Helga Testorf, many nude, created secretly over 14 years primarily in the loft of a millhouse in Chadds Ford. They were purchased by Leonard Andrews and became a sensation when revealed on the covers of both Time ('Stunning Secret,' 1987) and Newsweek. Individual works include Lovers (nude woman on stool by open window), Braids (solemn woman in gray turtleneck), Overflow, Black Velvet, The Prussian, Letting Her Hair Down, In the Orchard, Gone (last chronological depiction), Sheepskin, Pageboy, and On Her Knees. Helga is often depicted with a medallion on a black band around her neck. She originally worked as a nurse for Karl Kuerner, and Michael Palin (of Monty Python) was the first to interview her. The catalog was the first art catalog selected by the Book-of-the-Month Club and had a VHS narrated by Charlton Heston.

Winter 1946 depicts a boy running down Kuerner's Hill, the first painting after his father N.C. Wyeth was killed in a car/train accident at that spot. Its composition is similar to Christina's World. The Kuerner Farm in Chadds Ford served as the setting for many of his works. Other notable paintings include The Master Bedroom, Trodden Weed (self-portrait wearing Howard Pyle's shoes after an operation), Snow Hill, Barracoon (painted Helga as a Black woman), Breakup (his own hands emerging from an ice floe), and Chambered Nautilus.
