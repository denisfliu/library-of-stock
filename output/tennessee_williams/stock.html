American playwright (born Thomas Lanier Williams III) whose major plays include A Streetcar Named Desire, The Glass Menagerie, Cat on a Hot Tin Roof, The Night of the Iguana, Suddenly Last Summer, Sweet Bird of Youth, The Rose Tattoo, and Orpheus Descending. His mentally ill sister Rose inspired many of his female characters. Known for his elaborate stage directions and Southern settings.
A Streetcar Named Desire

Set on Elysian Fields in New Orleans, the play follows Blanche DuBois, who visits her sister Stella after losing the family estate Belle Reve. Blanche's husband Allan Grey committed suicide when his homosexuality was discovered. She cries 'I don't want realism. I want magic!' after Stanley Kowalski rips a paper lantern to reveal her face. The Varsouviana Polka plays after she confronts her husband's affair and grows loudest during her mental breakdown. Blanche's famous last line is 'I have always depended upon the kindness of strangers.' The stage directions describe 'the warm breath of the brown river beyond the river warehouses.' The original title referenced poker, which Stanley and Mitch play at the end. Vivien Leigh played Blanche in the film. The epigraph quotes Hart Crane about 'the visionary company of love.' Manuela replaces a stoned actress in a performance of this play in Almodovar's All About My Mother.

The Glass Menagerie

A memory play in which Tom Wingfield narrates his family's story. Laura Wingfield, nicknamed 'Blue Roses' by the gentleman caller Jim, drops out of business college after having a breakdown during a typing class. Their mother Amanda recalls her youth receiving 'gentleman callers.' Tom claims he 'didn't go to the moon' but 'much further, because time is the longest distance between places.' The play's stage directions describe the fire escape as 'a structure whose name is a touch of accidental poetic truth' and state that 'memory is seated predominantly in the heart.' Williams's mentally ill sister Rose was the basis for Laura. The essay 'The Catastrophe of Success' describes Williams's discomfort after the play's enormous popularity, including staying in a first-class hotel where he poured chocolate sauce on his steak thinking it was gravy. Laura bends over to blow out candles at the end.

Cat on a Hot Tin Roof

A play about the Pollitt family. Brick drinks until he hears a 'click' in his head due to a leg injury from hurdling. He rants about 'mendacity' to his father Big Daddy when it is suggested he had a romantic relationship with his teammate Skipper. Maggie ('the Cat') calls the children 'no-neck monsters' and says 'you can't be old without money.' Gooper and Mae ('Sister Woman' and 'Brother Man') eagerly await Big Daddy's inheritance. The opening stage directions describe a bed with giant wicker cornucopias that used to belong to Jack Straw and Peter Ochello. Maggie locks away liquor until they conceive. At the end, Maggie hurls bottles off a balcony. The 1959 film starred Paul Newman and Elizabeth Taylor. Maggie carries a 'Diana Trophy' from an intercollegiate archery championship. A pro football player commits suicide, and another character has a spastic colon.

Sebastian gathers orchids in a wheelchair and keeps a bound collection of twenty poems. Gore Vidal wrote the screenplay for the film adaptation.

General / Biographical

Williams's mentally ill sister Rose was the basis for many of his female characters, including Laura Wingfield. He wrote about his fear of becoming like Rose and described their relationship in 'The Resemblance Between a Violin Case and a Coffin.' His early plays like Fugitive Kind were inspired by Clifford Odets's pro-labor Depression plays. He adapted Chekhov's The Seagull into The Notebook of Trigorin. He wrote the play Not About Nightingales about a prison hunger strike, and the play Camino Real (set in Mexico, featuring Kilroy and Lord Byron boarding a ship). His essay 'The Catastrophe of Success' described America's 'national myth' of Cinderella. He wrote the introduction for Carson McCullers's Reflections in a Golden Eye. He was a close friend of McCullers and met Hemingway in Cuba through Kenneth Tynan. He encountered the 'Kilroy was here' meme in Mexico. Carroll Baker popularized a style of nightgown in the film Baby Doll.

Summary of Facts

Tennessee Williams (born Thomas Lanier Williams III) was one of the most important American playwrights, known for his Southern Gothic dramas and elaborate stage directions. His mentally ill sister Rose inspired many of his female characters.

A Streetcar Named Desire, Williams's most famous play, is set on Elysian Fields in New Orleans. Blanche DuBois visits her sister Stella after losing Belle Reve and clashes with Stanley Kowalski. Blanche cries 'I don't want realism. I want magic!' and her famous last line is 'I have always depended upon the kindness of strangers.' The Varsouviana Polka plays during her breakdown, and her husband Allan Grey's suicide over homosexuality haunts her. The play's epigraph quotes Hart Crane. Marlon Brando originated the role of Stanley.

The Glass Menagerie is a memory play in which Tom Wingfield narrates his family's story. Laura, nicknamed 'Blue Roses' by the gentleman caller Jim, drops out of business college. The stage directions state 'memory is seated predominantly in the heart.' Williams wrote 'The Catastrophe of Success' about his discomfort after the play's popularity.

Cat on a Hot Tin Roof centers on the Pollitt family. Brick drinks until he hears a 'click' and rants about 'mendacity' when Big Daddy questions his relationship with Skipper. Maggie ('the Cat') calls children 'no-neck monsters.' The Night of the Iguana is set at Maxine Faulk's Mexican hotel, where the defrocked Reverend Shannon encounters Hannah Jelkes and her poet grandfather Nonno, who recites 'How calmly does the orange branch.'

Suddenly Last Summer features Violet Venable trying to lobotomize Catherine Holly, who reveals that the homosexual poet Sebastian was devoured by cannibals after watching sea turtles slaughtered on the Galapagos. The Rose Tattoo follows the Sicilian widow Serafina who falls for Alvaro Mangiacavallo, who has a matching tattoo. Orpheus Descending, reworked from Battle of Angels, retells the Orpheus myth with Val and Lady. Summer and Smoke depicts the failed romance between Alma Winemiller and John Buchanan. Sweet Bird of Youth features an aging actress called 'Princess Kosmonopolis' and Boss Finley.

Williams also wrote Not About Nightingales (a prison hunger strike play), Camino Real (featuring Kilroy and Lord Byron in Mexico), and adapted Chekhov's The Seagull as The Notebook of Trigorin. He was close friends with Carson McCullers and wrote the introduction for her Reflections in a Golden Eye.
