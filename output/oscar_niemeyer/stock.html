Brazilian modernist architect (1907–2012), Pritzker Prize winner, and the primary designer of Brasilia — Brazil's planned capital. Worked closely with urban planner Lucio Costa, and contributed to the UN Headquarters design with Le Corbusier. General / Biographical

Oscar Niemeyer (full name: Oscar Ribeiro de Almeida Niemeyer Soares Filho) was a Brazilian modernist architect, Pritzker Prize winner, and the chief designer behind Brasilia. He collaborated with urban planner Lucio Costa on the capital project, with Costa handling layout and Niemeyer designing the buildings. The city's buildings lie along the Monumental Axis of an airplane-shaped city plan. Niemeyer also contributed to the design of the United Nations Headquarters in New York (based on a joint submission with Le Corbusier; Niemeyer submitted Plan 32). He went into exile in Paris and designed the headquarters of the French Communist Party (with a massive underground 'Worker's Hall'). He teamed up with Converse in 2012 to release Chuck Taylor trainers printed with a poem about his longing for the 'free and sensual curve.' He also designed a pavilion at the New York World Fair with Lucio Costa. Wikipedia

Cathedral of Brasilia

The Metropolitan Cathedral of Our Lady Aparecida (Cathedral of Brasilia), one of Niemeyer's most iconic works. Built from sixteen hyperbolic (hyperboloid) concrete columns that curve outward and support a glass roof. Often described as crown-shaped. Three angels are suspended by steel cables inside. The cathedral is a key landmark along Brasilia's Monumental Axis. Wikipedia

Church of Saint Francis of Assisi (Pampulha)

Part of Niemeyer's Pampulha Project — a suburb complex built around a man-made lagoon in Belo Horizonte (Pampulha). The church is made of four concrete parabolas and an inverted obelisk. The exterior features blue mosaics and murals by artist Candido Portinari (who did quasi-cubist work), mostly depicting Saint Francis of Assisi. Its hanger-inspired style scandalized the clergy and delayed use by a decade; the local bishop reportedly called it 'the devil's bomb shelter.' Summary of Facts

Oscar Niemeyer (full name Oscar Ribeiro de Almeida Niemeyer Soares Filho) was a Brazilian modernist architect and Pritzker Prize winner, most famous as the chief architect of Brasilia — Brazil's planned capital city. His collaboration with urban planner Lucio Costa defined Brasilia: Costa designed the airplane-shaped city layout, with buildings arranged along a central Monumental Axis, while Niemeyer designed the principal structures. Niemeyer's work is characterized by bold uses of curved reinforced concrete, hyperboloid forms, and parabolic vaults.

The Cathedral of Brasilia (Metropolitan Cathedral of Our Lady Aparecida) is among his most celebrated works, built from sixteen hyperbolic concrete columns that curve outward and support a glass roof in a crown-like form. Three angels are suspended by steel cables inside. The National Congress of Brazil is another Brasilia landmark, topped by two white domes — one facing up and one facing down — symbolizing the bicameral legislature (Senate and Chamber of Deputies). President Juscelino Kubitschek commissioned Niemeyer to design these buildings.

Niemeyer's earlier Pampulha Project (in Belo Horizonte) established his international reputation. The Church of Saint Francis of Assisi there is made of four concrete parabolas and an inverted obelisk, with blue mosaic walls and exterior murals by Candido Portinari depicting the saint — work that scandalized the clergy and delayed the church's use for a decade; the local bishop reportedly called it 'the devil's bomb shelter.' A tapestry copy of Picasso's Guernica is displayed inside.

The Niteroi Contemporary Art Museum, set on a cliffside in Niteroi, is saucer-shaped (frequently compared to a UFO), surrounded by a reflecting pool, and reached by a winding red-carpeted ramp leading up to a white cupola. The Copan Building (Edificio Copan), a sprawling residential tower in São Paulo, features a distinctive S-shaped facade made of 72 million exterior tiles, now under renovation due to the tiles falling off. The Oscar Niemeyer Museum in Curitiba (self-designed) features a wide eye-shaped structure (nicknamed 'the Eye') perched atop a yellow rectangular tiled pillar.

During political exile in Paris, Niemeyer designed the French Communist Party Headquarters, featuring a massive underground 'Worker's Hall.' He also contributed to the United Nations Headquarters in New York through a joint submission (Plan 23/32) with Le Corbusier. Other works include 'The Volcano' cultural center in Le Havre, France; the University of Haifa (all buildings in one integrated complex); a memorial with a 'Hall of Acts' and 'Creativity Pavilion' whose main portion depicts a hand dripping blood in a map shape; and the Brasilia Palace hotel. In 2012, he collaborated with Converse to release Chuck Taylor trainers printed with a poem about his longing for the 'free and sensual curve.'
