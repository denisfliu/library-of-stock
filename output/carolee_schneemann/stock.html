American feminist performance artist. Best known for Interior Scroll (1975), in which she pulled a scroll from her vagina and read from it, and Meat Joy (1964), an orgy-like performance involving raw fish, chicken, and sausages. Her film Fuses documents her having sex with partner James Tenney. Took Polaroids of kissing her cats in Infinity Kisses. Wrote the book Cezanne, She Was a Great Painter. Suspended from Bard College for painting a nude self-portrait. Based in New York City.

Summary of Facts

Carolee Schneemann (1939-2019) was an American feminist performance artist, one of the most frequently asked figures in quiz bowl Fine Arts. Her most iconic work, Interior Scroll (1975), appears in virtually every question about her: she read from her book Cezanne, She Was a Great Painter, then pulled a long scroll from her vagina and read aloud from it. The scroll text describes a dismissive encounter with a 'structuralist filmmaker.' Her other signature work, Meat Joy (1964), is an orgy-like performance in which eight performers ecstatically rolled around with raw fish, chicken, and sausages. Questions often pair these two works as giveaway clues. Her film Fuses (1967) silently documents her having sex with composer James Tenney while her cat Kitch looks on; this was made after a bad experience acting in a Stan Brakhage film. The photo series Infinity Kisses consists of out-of-focus Polaroids of her morning ritual of kissing her cats. Earlier biographical clues include her suspension from Bard College for 'moral turpitude' after painting a nude self-portrait, her 1963 series Eye Body involving 36 'transformative actions' inspired by Cretan snake goddesses, and her role posing as Manet's Olympia in Robert Morris's performance Site at the Judson Dance Theater. Harder clues reference her 1999 exchange with MacArthur Foundation director Daniel Socolow about health insurance, her unrealized piece involving Syrian refugees described in an interview with Pipilotti Rist, and her 1984 piece Mortal Coils dedicated to 15 friends including John Cage.
