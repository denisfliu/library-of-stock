Ojibwe American novelist and poet. Best known for Love Medicine (1984), an episodic novel set on a Chippewa (Ojibwe) reservation in North Dakota, following interlinked families including the Kashpaws and Lazarres. Also wrote The Round House, The Night Watchman (2020 Pulitzer), The Painted Drum, The Sentence, and Future Home of the Living God. Owns Birchbark Books in Minneapolis. Her sister Heid E. Erdrich is also a poet.
Love Medicine

Erdrich's debut novel (1984), an episodic work set on a Chippewa (Ojibwe) reservation in North Dakota. It opens with June Kashpaw (or June Morrissey) dying as she walks home in a snowstorm, freezing to death. Key characters include the love triangle between Marie Lazarre, Lulu Lamartine, and Nector Kashpaw — Marie marries Nector despite his assault of her. Lipsha plans to use goose hearts to make the title 'love medicine' to cement Marie and Nector's relationship, since geese mate for life. The first chapter was originally a short story called 'The World's Greatest Fisherman.' Stories like 'The Red Convertible' and 'Saint Marie' were incorporated into the novel. In 'Saint Marie,' Marie Lazarre has scalding water poured in her ear by a Satan-obsessed nun (Sister Leopoldina).

"The Red Convertible"

A story about brothers Lyman and Henry. After Henry returns from the Vietnam War deeply changed, he jumps into a river and tells Lyman 'my boots are filling' before drowning. Lyman then drives the title red convertible into the river after his brother's death. The story was incorporated into Love Medicine.

General / Biographical

Louise Erdrich (Karen Louise Erdrich) is an Ojibwe (Chippewa) American author who is half-German-American. She owns Birchbark Books, a bookshop in Minneapolis that supports writing workshops to preserve Native American writing. Her sister Heid E. Erdrich is a poet who wrote the cookbook Original Local and the poem 'The Theft Outright' (which inverts Robert Frost's 'The Gift Outright'). Erdrich's work is centrally concerned with Native American reservation life, particularly in North Dakota.

Summary of Facts

Louise Erdrich (Karen Louise Erdrich) is an Ojibwe (Chippewa) American novelist who is one of the most prominent Native American authors in quizbowl. She owns Birchbark Books, a bookshop in Minneapolis, and is half-German-American, half-Chippewa. Her sister Heid E. Erdrich is a poet who wrote the cookbook Original Local and the poem 'The Theft Outright' (which inverts Robert Frost's 'The Gift Outright').

Her debut and most frequently clued novel is Love Medicine (1984), an episodic work set on a Chippewa reservation in North Dakota. It opens with June Kashpaw freezing to death while walking home in a snowstorm, which is the single most common clue about Erdrich. The novel follows interlinked families: Marie Lazarre marries Nector Kashpaw despite his assault of her, forming a love triangle with Lulu Lamartine. Lipsha tries to use goose hearts (since geese mate for life) to make the title 'love medicine' for Marie and Nector. Stories like 'The Red Convertible' (about brothers Henry and Lyman — Henry drowns after Vietnam, telling Lyman 'my boots are filling') and 'Saint Marie' (scalding water poured in Marie's ear by a nun) were incorporated into the novel.

The Round House is part of Erdrich's 'Justice Trilogy' about sexual violence on reservations, following Joe Coutts as he investigates the assault of his mother Geraldine by Linden Lark, aided by his friend Cappy. The Night Watchman (2020 Pulitzer) is inspired by Erdrich's grandfather's resistance to Native termination policies at the Turtle Mountain Indian Reservation. The Painted Drum features an unfaithful wife who sacrifices her daughter to wolves on a frozen lake. The Sentence features Tookie's incarceration and a ghost exorcised by reading Proust. Erdrich also co-wrote The Crown of Columbus with her husband Michael Dorris and studied creative writing under John Barth.
