  Part 2 (ans: Ghent [or Gent; or Gand; accept Ghent Altarpiece]):

[B2] 2017 WAO (2017) | Diff: 8 | Religion
     Leadin: This saint is often portrayed standing in a tower with three windows, making reference to how her father Dioscorus kept her in a tower. For 10 points each:
  Part 1 (ans: Saint Barbara):
    Dioscorus was struck by lightning for beheading which saint, who thus became the patron saint of explosives and artillerymen, among other things?
  Part 2 (ans: Saint George):
    Saint Barbara was venerated as one of the Fourteen Holy Helpers, another member of whom was this patron saint of England. He is well-known for slaying a dragon.
  Part 3 (ans: Saint Catherine):
    Another of the Fourteen Holy Helpers is this saint, who protected against sudden death. Since she consecrated her virginity to Jesus Christ, she refused the advances of the emperor Maxentius, who had her beheaded after her touch shattered the instrument meant to torture her.

[B3] 2015 George Oppen (2015) | Diff: 9 | Religion
     Leadin: For 10 points each, name these saints whose crazy life stories were popularized by the hagiographies of Jacobus de Voragine's Golden Legend.
  Part 1 (ans: Saint Eustace [or Eustachius; or Eustathius]):
    The Golden Legend claims that this patron saint of hunters and firefighters was once a Roman general who converted upon seeing a crucifix growing between a stag's antlers. Once he returned to Rome, he refused to make a pagan sacrifice and was condemned by Trajan to be burned alive inside a brass bull.
  Part 2 (ans: Saint Barbara):
    This saint and member of the Holy Helpers somehow, according to the Golden Legend, managed to pray her way through the stone walls of a fortified tower that her father had imprisoned him in. Because her father was punished for beheading her by being struck by lightning, she became the patron saint of explosives and artillerymen.
  Part 3 (ans: Mary Magdalene [or Mary of Magdala; or The Magdalene; prompt on Mary]):
    The Golden Legend states that this woman atoned for her sins in a desert near Provence where angels lifted her up every hour to feed her, and that she washed Jesus's feet by crying penitent tears over them, both of which outdo the boring suggestion that she conceived Christ's child.

[B4] 2014 ACF Regionals (2014) | Diff: 8 | Religion
     Leadin: This ethnicity has believed in a life force called ashe, which stems from their supreme deity, Olodumare. For 10 points each:
  Part 1 (ans: Yoruba people):
    Name this West African ethnicity whose pre-Christian religion included a divination ritual using palm nuts and the drum-accompanied "mounting" ritual, in which spirits called orishas could possess a person.
  Part 2 (ans: Cuba):
    On this island, slaves of Yoruba descent blended their beliefs with Spanish Catholicism to create Santeria.
  Part 3 (ans: Saint Barbara):
    In Cuba, this female saint and martyr is syncretized with Chango, the male fire and lightning orisha. This patron saint of artillery has a feast on December 4. Her father was struck by lightning for beheading her.

[B5] 2012 NASAT (2012) | Diff: 8 | Religion
     Leadin: One of these beings named Babalu Aye is connected to disease and healing, and one named Eshu represents death. For 10 points each:
  Part 1 (ans: Orishas):
    Name these manifestations of the Yoruban deity Olorun in a syncretic religion.

  Part 3 (ans: Saint Barbara):
    The Orisha Shango is often conflated with this Catholic saint. She was tortured and killed by her father Dioscurus, who was subsequently struck by lightning.
