Questions: W.D. Snodgrass

2 tossups · 1 bonus

Tossups

T1 2025 LITANY (2025) Diff 8 · Literature
This movement was arguably launched by a poem titled for a remark about “an only daughter” in The Madness of Suibhne [“SIV-nuh”]. A train journey from Rome to Paris prompts a discussion of dwindling Catholic faith in the first poem of a collection from this movement, whose name comes from a review by M. L. Rosenthal. “Beyond the Alps,” “91 Revere Street,” and (*) “Heart’s Needle” were early pieces in this movement of a poem that declares, “White Godiva, I unpeel” after opening, “Stasis in darkness.” That poem, which ends with the image of “the red eye, the cauldron of morning,” titles a collection edited by Ted Hughes and named for the poet’s horse, Ariel. For 10 points, Robert Lowell.and Sylvia Plath belong to what movement of frequently autobiographical poetry?
ANSWER: Confessional poetry [or Confessionalism; or Confessionalists] (the first two poems mentioned are W. D. Snodgrass’s “Heart’s Needle” and Robert Lowell’s “Beyond the Alps”)
T2 2021 HARI (2021) Diff 7 · Literature
A "cell of concrete" where "liars face up to blank truth" is described by a member of this group in a poem that ends by stating "all things shall be equal." The final days of several members of this group are chronicled in a collection of dramatic monologues by confessional poet W. D. Snodgrass. The narrator's skin is described as "Bright as" a "lampshade" belonging to a figure from this group in the poem (*) "Lady Lazarus". A figure implied to be part of this group is told "There's a stake in your fat black heart" before the narrator declares "You bastard, I'm through." For 10 points, name these figures which Sylvia Plath compares the title figure of "Daddy" to by referring to him as "A man in black with a Meinkampf look."
ANSWER: Nazis [prompt on Fascists; prompt on Sylvia Plath’s family] (The Snodgrass collection is The Führer Bunker; the first poem is from that collection, "Dr. Joseph Goebbels")

B1 2012 Chicago Open (2012) Diff 10 · Literature
This collection takes its epigraph and title from The Madness of Suibhne. FTPE:
[10] Name this ten-poem sequence, inspired by the author’s separation from his daughter, Cynthia Jean, whom he refers to as “Child of my winter”. It won its author the 1960 Pulitzer Prize for Poetry.
ANSWER: Heart’s Needle
[10] Heart’s Needle is by this American poet, who wrote a series of poetic monologues from the perspective of Hitler during the last days of WWII in The Führer Bunker.
ANSWER: W(illiam) D(eWitt) Snodgrass
[10] Heart’s Needle was one of the most influential works in this movement of poetry, which focuses on intimate and often dark details of the poet’s life. Other collections in this movement include Sylvia Plath’s Ariel and Anne Sexton's work.
ANSWER: confessional poetry
