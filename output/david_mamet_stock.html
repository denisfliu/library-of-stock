Glengarry Glen Ross

Mamet's most famous play about down-and-out real estate salesmen competing for 'leads.' The first act is set in a Chinese restaurant where conversations include Aaronow and Moss mocking the Patels. Ricky Roma is the most successful salesman but loses a sale to James Lingk. Shelley 'The Machine' Levene begs John Williamson for good leads; at the end, detective Baylen takes Levene in for questioning after he admits to stealing the leads. In the film adaptation, Alec Baldwin's added character Blake yells 'Coffee's for closers!' and shows off brass balls. Dave Moss coerces a colleague to steal leads between the two acts. Roma threatens to go 'downtown' to Mitch and Murray to get Williamson fired. A controversial scene features characters disdainfully commenting on selling to someone named Patel.

Oleanna

A two-person play inspired by the Clarence Thomas Supreme Court confirmation hearings, taking its title from a Norwegian folk song. Carol accuses her professor John of sexual harassment, which escalates to assault. John eventually violently beats Carol with a chair in his office as his life unravels, losing his chance at tenure.

General / Biographical

Mamet is known for terse, profane dialogue with characters talking over each other. He developed the 'practical aesthetics' approach to acting with William H. Macy at Goddard College. Joe Mantegna starred in his films Homicide and House of Games. He used the pseudonym 'Richard Weisz' as script doctor on Ronin. He wrote screenplays for Wag the Dog (adapted from American Hero), The Untouchables, and The Verdict. His play The Cryptogram, inspired by the divorce of his parents, features Donny discovering her cheating husband has left. Boston Marriage is about two women in New England. The play Race premiered at the Ethel Barrymore Theater. He wrote On Directing Film exhorting students to 'think visually.' He also wrote the play November about a president. Bill Burr made his Broadway debut in a 2025 revival.

Summary of Facts

David Mamet is a prominent American playwright and screenwriter known for his terse, profane dialogue. His most famous play, Glengarry Glen Ross, follows down-and-out real estate salesmen competing for leads in a cutthroat environment. The first act is set in a Chinese restaurant; Shelley 'The Machine' Levene begs Williamson for good leads, while Ricky Roma is the top salesman. At the end, detective Baylen questions Levene for stealing leads. The film adaptation added Alec Baldwin's iconic Blake monologue with 'Coffee's for closers!'

American Buffalo is set at Don's Resale Shop where Don and Teach plan to rob a customer who got a good deal on a rare coin. Oleanna, inspired by the Clarence Thomas hearings, features student Carol accusing professor John of sexual harassment, culminating in John violently beating her. Speed-the-Plow satirizes the film industry.

Mamet developed the 'practical aesthetics' approach to acting with William H. Macy at Goddard College. His screenwriting credits include Wag the Dog, The Untouchables, and The Verdict. Other plays include The Cryptogram (inspired by his parents' divorce), Boston Marriage, and Race.
