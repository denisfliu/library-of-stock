Spheres Trilogy (Bubbles, Globes, Foam)

Sloterdijk's best-known work, divided into three volumes: Bubbles, Globes, and Foam. The trilogy analyzes relations of space and was partly intended as a follow-up to Heidegger's Being and Time. It also develops Sloterdijk's theory of how humans interact with an inhospitable world through three 'immune systems' — biological, societal, and psychological.

Critique of Cynical Reason

One of the best-selling philosophical books in Germany after World War II. The Critique of Cynical Reason defines cynicism as 'enlightened false consciousness' and distinguishes between 'cheeky' (ancient, Kynicism) and non-cheeky (modern, Cynicism) incarnations. It is widely read as a response to the postmodern thought of the 1960s and 1970s.

You Must Change Your Life and Anthropotechnics

You Must Change Your Life draws its title from Rilke. The book documents the history of what Sloterdijk calls 'anthropotechnics,' which '(re-)merges entities that were already inseparable in primitive times.' Sloterdijk argues that de-spiritualization in the 19th century led to the rise of movements such as the modern Olympic Games. He also discusses the concept of 'immune systems' as ways humans cope with the world.

General / Biographical and Debates

Sloterdijk is a contemporary German philosopher. His controversial advocacy for genetic technologies resulted in Jürgen Habermas calling him a Nazi. He deeply influenced Bruno Latour. He wrote about Nietzsche in Thinker on Stage: Nietzsche's Materialism, attacking the notion of literary 'debts' and critiquing Nietzsche's 'megalomania.' Other works include God's Zeal: The Battle of the Three Monotheisms.

Summary of Facts

Peter Sloterdijk is a contemporary German philosopher known for a distinctive body of work that engages with space, technology, and post-Enlightenment culture. Sloterdijk's best-known work is the Spheres trilogy, divided into three volumes: Bubbles, Globes, and Foam. The trilogy analyzes relations of space and was partly intended as a follow-up to Heidegger's Being and Time. Related to this is his theory that humans interact with an inhospitable world through three 'immune systems' — biological, societal, and psychological.

His Critique of Cynical Reason is one of the best-selling philosophical books in Germany after World War II. It defines cynicism as 'enlightened false consciousness' and uses two spellings to distinguish 'cheeky' ancient Kynicism from modern, non-cheeky Cynicism. It is widely read as a response to the postmodern thought of the 1960s and 1970s.

You Must Change Your Life, whose title is drawn from Rilke, documents the history of 'anthropotechnics' — practices that '(re-)merge entities that were already inseparable in primitive times.' Sloterdijk argues that de-spiritualization in the 19th century led to the rise of movements like the modern Olympic Games.
