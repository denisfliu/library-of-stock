Summary of Facts

Francis Picabia was a French Dadaist and Cubist painter. His most frequently clued work is Machine Turn Quickly, which depicts two interlocking gears labeled 'femme' (smaller) and 'homme' (larger). He created 'mechanomorph' portraits, including one of Alfred Stieglitz depicted as a bellows-style camera with a gear shift under the word 'Ideal' (titled Here, This is Stieglitz Here). He founded the magazine 391, inspired by Stieglitz's 291 gallery.

His Cubist painting Dances at the Spring was shown at both the 1912 Section d'Or exhibition and the Armory Show. He placed a stuffed toy monkey on cardboard as a Portrait of Cézanne / Renoir / Rembrandt. His Transparencies were a late-career series of mythological works with interwoven dark outlines, painted while living in Mougins. Caoutchouc (1909) is a candidate for the first abstract painting, though it 'looks a little bit like a bowl of fruit.'

Star Dancer and Her School of Dance was inspired by Stacia Napierkowska's rehearsals on a transatlantic voyage. He collaborated with Tristan Tzara on the Dadaglobe project. In René Clair's film Entr'acte, he fires a cannon alongside Erik Satie. His Optophone I may be a precursor to Op Art. I See Again in Memory My Dear Udnie uses automobile parts to evoke genitals. He painted portraits of Suzy Solidor and Gertrude Stein.
