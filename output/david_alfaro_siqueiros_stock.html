The March of Humanity (Polyforum Cultural Siqueiros)

The largest mural in the world, housed at the dodecagonal/octagonal Polyforum Cultural Center in Mexico City. Divided into sections including 'Science and Technology' and 'to the Revolution of the Future.' Viewed from a rotating platform. Depicts the space race, revolutions, and a search for 'a better society.'

                                      Summary of Facts

                                      David Alfaro Siqueiros was a Mexican muralist and one of 'los tres grandes' alongside Diego Rivera and Jose Clemente Orozco. His largest and most frequently clued work is The March of Humanity, the largest mural in the world, housed at his namesake dodecagonal Polyforum Cultural Center in Mexico City. The mural is divided into sections including 'Science and Technology' and depicts the space race, revolutions, and a search for 'a better society,' viewable from a rotating platform.

                                      Echo of a Scream (1937) depicts a red-robed crying baby emerging from the mouth of an identical, larger baby above a field of metal scraps. Tropical America (1932) was painted at the Plaza Art Center in Los Angeles and shows a crucifixion scene with a gold-brown eagle atop a double cross in the jungle around a Mayan ruin; inspired by Masaccio frescoes, it was restored by the Getty in 2012. Portrait of the Bourgeoisie features generals and businessmen in gas masks, a metal falcon, a machine feeding blood to an octopus, and a temple inscribed 'Liberté, Egalité, Fraternité' on fire. It was created using pyroxylin automobile paint and spearheaded by Joseph Renau.

                                      Other works include The Future Triumph of Medical Science over Cancer (painted for Mexico's Social Security Oncology Hospital), The Devil in the Church (a monster crashing through the roof), La nueva democracia (bare-breasted woman holding chains), and Cain in the United States (black figure torn apart by white ones). His self-portrait shows him in a red toga throwing a fist forward.

                                      Siqueiros was a committed Stalinist who participated in a failed assassination attempt on Trotsky in 1940. He hosted an Experimental Workshop in Union Square where 'controlled accidents' and 'accidental painting' techniques of letting paint freely mix influenced a young Jackson Pollock's drip technique and later Abstract Expressionists and fluid dynamicists. He toured Italy with Diego Rivera in the early 1920s. Morris Louis also studied under Siqueiros.
