Iraqi-born British architect (1950–2016) and the first woman to win the Pritzker Prize (2004). Known as the 'queen of the curve,' her work defines Deconstructivism and Parametricism — the latter term coined by her firm's partner Patrik Schumacher. Major works: the Guangzhou Opera House (twin-boulder Pearl River design), the London Aquatics Centre (2012 Olympics), Rome's MAXXI Museum (built on a former military barracks), the Al-Janoub Stadium (Qatar 2022 World Cup, dhow-inspired), the Dongdaemun Design Plaza (Seoul), the Phaeno Science Centre (Wolfsburg, on stilts), the Vitra Fire Station (her first building), the Heydar Aliyev Center (Baku), and Daxing International Airport near Beijing ('The Starfish'). A proposed 2020 Tokyo Olympic Stadium was scrapped amid comparisons to a bicycle helmet.

Summary of Facts

Zaha Hadid was an Iraqi-born British architect who died in 2016. She was the first woman to win the Pritzker Prize, receiving it in 2004. (SANAA's Kazuyo Sejima later became the second woman to win it.) She studied mathematics before moving to London to study architecture, where she studied under Rem Koolhaas and Elia Zenghelis; Koolhaas later co-founded the Office of Metropolitan Architecture. Her work is associated with Deconstructivism and Parametricism — the latter term coined by her firm's partner Patrik Schumacher, who also titled his book The Autopoiesis of Architecture using a term for self-generating systems. She became known as the 'queen of the curve.'

Her most frequently clued work is the Guangzhou Opera House in China, designed around the concept of two rocks/pebbles being smoothed by the Pearl River (the 'twin boulders' or 'double pebble' design), largely made of glass and using reinforced gypsum. For the 2012 Summer Olympics she designed the London Aquatics Centre — notable for its wave-shaped/undulating roof, a concrete ceiling sloping toward the pool between two walls of windows, and spectator wings that were later removed. Rome's MAXXI (Italy's National Museum of 21st Century Arts) is built on a former military barracks and features floating/weaving staircases, flowing concrete beams, louvered skylights, a sharply angular concrete facade overhang inspired by Constructivist art, and fluid pathways designed to contrast with Rome's 'static' nature; Hadid described it as moving away from 'the museum as an object' toward 'a field of buildings.'

Hadid designed the Al-Wakrah / Al-Janoub Stadium for the 2022 FIFA World Cup in Qatar, inspired by the shape of a dhow fishing boat (also described by critics as resembling a vagina). She sued the New York Review of Books for defamation after criticism of her dismissal of concerns over dying migrant construction workers during its construction. She won the commission for the 2020 Tokyo Olympic Stadium, but the design — described by Arata Isozaki as 'a turtle waiting for Japan to sink so that it can swim away' and criticized as looking like a bicycle helmet — was scrapped. For the Dongdaemun Design Plaza in Seoul, she used the concept of a 'Metonymic Landscape'; the neofuturistic complex boasts a flat, fluid body with 45,000 uniquely curved gray panels. This building is often cited in connection with Schumacher's parametricism.

The Phaeno Science Centre in Wolfsburg rests on huge concrete stilts allowing visitors and vehicles to pass underneath, and inspired her Z.Scape furniture collection. Her first constructed building was the Vitra Fire Station, built after a factory fire on the Vitra campus in Weil am Rhein, Germany. The Heydar Aliyev Center in Baku, Azerbaijan (named for the country's former president) showcases her flowing style and is featured on the 200 manat banknote. In Abu Dhabi, she built the Sheikh Zayed Bridge (named for the former UAE leader), characterized by dramatic curved arches. In Innsbruck she designed both the Bergisel Ski Jump tower and the glacier-inspired Hungerburg Funicular stations serving Nordkette Mountain. Daxing International Airport near Beijing, nicknamed 'The Starfish' and claimed to be the world's largest airport, was completed posthumously by Zaha Hadid Architects. The Opus Office Tower in Dubai is a cube-shaped building distinguished by a curving, amorphous hole through it, also completed posthumously. In Milan, her CityLife tower is known as 'The Curved One,' alongside Daniel Libeskind's 'Twisted One' and Arata Isozaki's 'Straight One.'

Other notable works include: the Cardiff Bay Opera House (unrealized), the BMW Central Building in Leipzig (bringing blue- and white-collar workers together), the Rosenthal Center / Contemporary Arts Center in Cincinnati (Hadid's first American building; the museum involved in the 1990 Robert Mapplethorpe obscenity trial moved here in 2003), the Innovation Tower in Hong Kong, the Broad Art Museum at Michigan State University (exterior of perpendicular/parallel steel rods above glass), the Evelyn Grace Academy (Z-shaped secondary school in London), the King Abdullah Petroleum Research Center in Riyadh (white honeycomb exterior), and a proposed but unrealized museum addition to Frank Lloyd Wright's Price Tower in Bartlesville. She designed the first Serpentine Gallery annual pavilion in 2000, initiating the yearly tradition. As a furniture/product designer she created the Aqua Table (a single piece of polyurethane with three wave-like undulations), 3D-printed shoes for Rem Koolhaas's company United Nude, and boots for Lacoste. She mentored Ma Yansong at Yale. Before architecture she created paintings including The Peak Project, depicting an unrealized project for the hills of Kowloon.
