[T1] 2024 Notes on the Underground (2024) | Diff: 10 | Literature
     Answer: Pseudolus
  - When asked why he doesn't cry in a sordid situation, a man in this play replies that his eyes are like pumice stones shortly after misunderstanding a letter about silver and wood.
  - Several loose ends in the plot of this play include Callipho being asked to "stand by," before he disappears entirely from the plot. [PWR]
  - A character in this play is introduced with a monologue in which he drives a group of slaves into the street and demands that they prepare him a lavish birthday dinner. [PWR]
  - In this play, a wine-loving man devises a series of impersonations and (*) 2,000 drachma bets, allowing him to free the slave-courtesan Phoenicium from the vile pimp Ballio at the behest of her beloved, Calidorus. [PWR]
  - This play's title character shares his name with the main character of Sondheim's A Funny Thing Happened on the Way to the Forum, which loosely adapted it and its author's other plays Curculio, Miles Gloriosus, and Mostellaria.
  - For 10 points, name this Plautus play titled for a clever slave. [GIVE]

--- BONUS ANSWERLINE CLUES ---

[B1] 2012 ACF Nationals (2012) | Diff: 9 | Literature
     Leadin: For 10 points each, name these comedies by Plautus.
  Part 1 (ans: Pseudolus):
    The title character of this play helps rescue Phoenicium, the prostitute with whom his master Calidorus has fallen in love. The title character fools the pimp Ballio by disguising himself as the messenger Harpax.
  Part 2 (ans: Miles Gloriosus):
    In this play, Acroteletium schemes with Palaestrio to reunite Pleusicles with his lover Philocomasium, who has been kidnapped by the title character, Pyrgopolynices. It inspired the character of Capitano in the commedia dell'arte.
  Part 3 (ans: Menaechmi):
    Moschus's son Sosicles becomes separated from his twin brother and then adopts his name in this play of mistaken identities, which inspired Shakespeare's Comedy of Errors.
