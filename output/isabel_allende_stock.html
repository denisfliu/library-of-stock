Chilean novelist, known for The House of the Spirits (about the Trueba family, the clairvoyant Clara del Valle, Esteban Trueba, and Rosa the Beautiful), Eva Luna (title storyteller, Rolf Carle, Riad Halabi, the transgender actress Mimi), and Daughter of Fortune (Eliza Sommers in the California Gold Rush, Joaquin Murieta, Tao Chi'en). Also wrote City of the Beasts (Alexander Cold, People of the Mist), the short story collections Stories of Eva Luna ('Two Words,' 'And of Clay Are We Created'), and the memoir Paula. Fled Chile after the 1973 coup.
The House of the Spirits

Allende's debut and most famous novel, about the Trueba family. The clairvoyant Clara del Valle goes mute for nine years after the death of her green-haired sister Rosa the Beautiful by poisoning (intended for their father Severo). Clara breaks her silence to announce she will marry Esteban Trueba. Set at the hacienda Las Tres Marias. Blanca has an affair with Pedro Tercero Garcia against her father's wishes. Count Jean de Satigny reveals Blanca's relationship. Alba (Esteban's granddaughter) is rescued from the sadistic Colonel Esteban Garcia with the help of the prostitute Transito Soto. Blanca and Pedro Tercero flee to Canada after a military coup. Clara recovers the decapitated head of her mother Nivea from a car wreck. A mummy-collecting nobleman has sex with servants in his photography studio. Esteban dies in Alba's arms, escaping Ferula's prophecy that he would 'die like a dog.' Opens with 'Barrabas came to us by sea.' Wikipedia

Eva Luna

A novel about a storyteller conceived when her mother Consuelo sleeps with a dying Indian gardener bitten by a snake. Eva becomes homeless after the 'Revolt of the Whores' and works at the Pearl of the Orient with Riad Halabi. She makes fake grenades from 'Universal Matter' to help the guerilla fighter Huberto Naranjo. The transgender telenovela actress Mimi encourages Eva to write down her stories. Eva eventually falls in love with the journalist Rolf Carle. A servant works for a minister who sits in a chair with a hole in it to defecate. Wikipedia

General / Biographical

Chilean author. Fled Chile after the 1973 coup (like Ariel Dorfman). Wrote the memoir Paula about her daughter's coma from porphyria complications. Wrote Aphrodite: A Memoir of the Senses (cookbook about aphrodisiacs). Wrote Soul of a Woman (2021 memoir). Also wrote The Japanese Lover, In the Midst of Winter, The Wind Knows My Name, The Infinite Plan, Zorro, and Island Beneath the Sea. Wrote a foreword for Eduardo Galeano's Open Veins of Latin America.

Summary of Facts

Isabel Allende is a Chilean novelist best known for The House of the Spirits, her debut novel about the Trueba family. The most frequently clued elements are: the clairvoyant Clara del Valle going mute for nine years after her green-haired sister Rosa the Beautiful dies from poisoning (intended for their father Severo); Clara later breaking her silence to announce she will marry Esteban Trueba; the hacienda Las Tres Marias; Blanca's affair with Pedro Tercero Garcia; the prostitute Transito Soto rescuing Alba from the sadistic Colonel Esteban Garcia; and the military coup that sends Blanca and Pedro to Canada. Other notable details include Clara recovering her mother Nivea's decapitated head, a mummy-collecting nobleman, Count Jean de Satigny, and the opening line 'Barrabas came to us by sea.'

Eva Luna is her second most important work, about a storyteller conceived when her mother sleeps with a dying gardener bitten by a snake. Eva works at the Pearl of the Orient with Riad Halabi after becoming homeless during the 'Revolt of the Whores.' She makes fake grenades from 'Universal Matter,' is encouraged to write by the transgender telenovela actress Mimi, and falls in love with journalist Rolf Carle. The Stories of Eva Luna collection includes 'Two Words' (Belisa Crepusculario sells words and makes a Colonel sick with a secret phrase) and 'And of Clay Are We Created' (a girl stuck in mudpit after an avalanche).

Daughter of Fortune follows Eliza Sommers, who disguises herself as a man and travels to the California Gold Rush searching for her lover Joaquin Murieta. She works as a cook and pianist for traveling prostitutes and settles with the physician Tao Chi'en.

City of the Beasts is a YA novel in which Alexander Cold and his grandmother Kate travel to the Amazon, meet the People of the Mist, and Alexander transforms into a jaguar.

Allende also wrote the memoir Paula (about her daughter's porphyria-related coma), The Japanese Lover, In the Midst of Winter, The Wind Knows My Name, Island Beneath the Sea, The Infinite Plan, Zorro, and Soul of a Woman.
