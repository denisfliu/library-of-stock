Summary of Facts

Peter Sellars is a contemporary American theater and opera director known for politically charged, anachronistic stagings of classical works and for his prolific collaboration with composer John Adams. The soprano Elizabeth Schwarzkopf famously compared his directorial choices to 'spray[ing] graffiti on the Mona Lisa,' reflecting the controversy his work often generates.

His most enduring collaboration with John Adams encompasses both direction and libretto writing. Sellars directed the original productions of Nixon in China (Adams's first opera), The Death of Klinghoffer (based on the 1985 Achille Lauro hijacking), and Girls of the Golden West. His most celebrated original libretto is for Doctor Atomic, which dramatizes Robert Oppenheimer and the Trinity nuclear test. That libretto draws on diverse sources: John Donne's Holy Sonnet 'Batter my heart, three person'd God,' excerpts from the Bhagavad Gita, government documents including the Smyth Report, and Tewa traditional songs sung by the Tewa housemaid Pasqualita (a repeated refrain describes how 'the cloud-flower blossoms' in each cardinal direction). The opera opens with the chorus declaring 'We believed that matter can be neither created nor destroyed.' The making-of documentary is titled Wonders are Many. Sellars also wrote the libretto for Girls of the Golden West, an opera about the California Gold Rush inspired by letters from that period, whose title references Puccini's La fanciulla del West.

For Nixon in China, his original staging famously placed the presidential airplane Spirit of '76 and a giant bomb on stage. His direction of The Death of Klinghoffer was so controversial that the opening scene was later removed from the score.

Sellars is also celebrated for his radical relocations of Mozart. He staged Don Giovanni as a blaxploitation story in the South Bronx, replacing the wind ensemble with a boombox. His 1988 production of The Marriage of Figaro — influenced by Wye Jamison Allanbrook's analysis of dance rhythms and social class in the opera — was set in a penthouse on the 52nd floor of Trump Tower. Earlier, in 1980, he set a production of Handel's Orlando in outer space.

Among his other notable stagings: his Chernobyl-inspired production of Ligeti's Le Grand Macabre had the chorus in contamination suits lit by a huge lightbulb, which infuriated Ligeti; in 1992, his staging of Messiaen's Saint François d'Assise (which filled the stage with televisions, with an angel portrayed by soprano Dawn Upshaw in a gray business suit) inspired Finnish composer Kaija Saariaho to write her first opera, L'Amour de loin, which Sellars also directed. His 1995 Covent Garden staging of Hindemith's Mathis der Maler anachronistically included a machine gun and TVs. He also collaborated with Bill Viola on a co-production of Tristan und Isolde, and with Toni Morrison and Rokia Traore on a 2011/2012 work centered on Desdemona from Othello. He created a staged version of Bach's St. Matthew Passion in 2010 for the Berlin Philharmonic. Among his visual collaborators is the Chicano artist Gronk, who has created hand-painted backdrops for productions including The Indian Queen and Ainadamar.
