Taiwanese director known for genre-spanning films. Best known for Crouching Tiger, Hidden Dragon (2000, Green Destiny sword; scored by Tan Dun; Michelle Yeoh and Chow Yun-Fat), Brokeback Mountain (2005, gay cowboys Jack Twist and Ennis del Mar from Annie Proulx's story), and the 'Father Knows Best' trilogy (Eat Drink Man Woman, The Wedding Banquet, Pushing Hands). Also directed Life of Pi, Sense and Sensibility, Lust, Caution, and The Ice Storm. Considered part of the second generation of Taiwanese New Wave.
Crouching Tiger, Hidden Dragon (2000) →

Wuxia film credited with reviving the wuxia genre. Based on a Chinese novel by Wang Dulu. Stars Michelle Yeoh as Yu Shu Lien and Chow Yun-Fat as Li Mu Bai. The 400-year-old Green Destiny sword is stolen by Jen, an apprentice of Jade Fox. Jade Fox's poison dart kills Li Mu Bai as he confesses his love to Yu Shu Lien. In the final scene, Jen jumps off a bridge and glides into fog. Scored by Tan Dun. The cinematographer was Rodrigo Prieto. Includes a wire-work sword duel in a bamboo forest (homage to King Hu). Brokeback Mountain (2005) →

Film about the homosexual love affair between cowboys Jack Twist (Jake Gyllenhaal) and Ennis del Mar (Heath Ledger) in 1960s Wyoming. Based on Annie Proulx's short story from Close Range: Wyoming Stories (1999). Alma Beers divorces Ennis after noticing he doesn't use his pole on fishing trips. After discussing leaving for Mexico, Jack says 'I wish I knew how to quit you.' Larry McMurtry co-wrote the screenplay. Ennis pins a blood-stained shirt in Jack's honor after learning Jack was killed with a tire iron. Identified by B. Ruby Rich as changing the direction of New Queer Cinema. Lost the 2005 Best Picture Oscar to Crash. Wikipedia

Summary of Facts

Ang Lee (Li An, born 1954) is a Taiwanese director considered part of the second generation of the Taiwanese New Wave. He is known for working across widely varied genres. His early 'Father Knows Best' trilogy — Pushing Hands (1992), The Wedding Banquet (1993), and Eat Drink Man Woman (1994) — examined Taiwanese family life and modernity. The Wedding Banquet depicts a gay Taiwanese man in a fake wedding arrangement; it was remade in 2025 with Lily Gladstone and Bowen Yang. Eat Drink Man Woman follows a widower chef who cooks Sunday feasts for his three unmarried daughters.

Crouching Tiger, Hidden Dragon (2000), based on a novel by Wang Dulu and scored by Tan Dun, is credited with reviving the wuxia genre. Michelle Yeoh plays Yu Shu Lien and Chow Yun-Fat plays Li Mu Bai. The 400-year-old Green Destiny sword is stolen by Jen, an apprentice of the villain Jade Fox; Jade Fox's poison dart kills Li Mu Bai as he confesses his love to Yu Shu Lien. In the final scene, Jen jumps off a bridge and glides into a sea of fog. The film includes a wire-work sword duel in a bamboo forest (homage to King Hu's A Touch of Zen). Rodrigo Prieto was the cinematographer.

Brokeback Mountain (2005) is based on Annie Proulx's short story from Close Range: Wyoming Stories (1999). Heath Ledger plays Ennis del Mar and Jake Gyllenhaal plays Jack Twist, who share a homosexual love affair across decades in Wyoming. Jack says the iconic line 'I wish I knew how to quit you.' Alma Beers divorces Ennis after noticing he doesn't use his pole on fishing trips. Ennis later learns Jack was killed with a tire iron. Larry McMurtry co-wrote the screenplay. The film was identified by critic B. Ruby Rich as changing the direction of New Queer Cinema; it controversially lost the 2005 Best Picture Oscar to Crash. Life of Pi (2012), adapted from Yann Martel's novel, follows Pi and the CGI Bengal tiger Richard Parker adrift for 227 days; Rhythm & Hues won an Oscar for their work on Richard Parker just after going bankrupt. Sense and Sensibility (1995) was a major commercial success. Lust, Caution (2007) starred Tang Wei (in her debut) with cinematography by Rodrigo Prieto. Other films include The Ice Storm (1997, from Rick Moody's novel), Hulk (2003), Ride with the Devil (from Daniel Woodrell's Woe to Live On), and Billy Lynn's Long Halftime Walk (2016, shot at 120 fps).
