Norman Mailer

American novelist, journalist, and public intellectual known for The Naked and the Dead, The Armies of the Night, The Executioner's Song, and the essay 'The White Negro.' A central figure of New Journalism, known for his pugnacious personality, feuds with Gore Vidal and Kate Millett, and the stabbing of his wife Adele Morales.
The Naked and the Dead

Mailer's first novel, based on his experience fighting in the Philippines in World War II. Depicts an American regiment's invasion of the fictional Filipino island Anopopei. Uses the euphemism 'fug' instead of the actual word, prompting Dorothy Parker to call Mailer 'the man who can't spell fuck.' Sergeant Croft orders an attack on Mount Anaka, crushes a baby bird, and kills a prisoner-of-war. A character poops his pants during the opening action scene and is later killed by a hand grenade.

The Armies of the Night

A nonfiction novel about Mailer's attendance at the 1967 March on the Pentagon. Split into two sections titled 'History as a Novel' and 'The Novel as History.' Mailer joins his friend Mitch Goodman at the event and marches alongside luminaries like Dwight Macdonald and Robert Lowell. The book includes a lengthy analysis of anti-Vietnam War protests.

The Executioner's Song

A Pulitzer Prize-winning nonfiction novel about the capital punishment of Gary Gilmore in Utah. Gilmore murders two men and then demands his death penalty be delivered as soon as possible. Nicole Baker has a brief affair with Gilmore. The novel begins with Toni and Brenda helping Gilmore get back on his feet in Utah.

"The White Negro"

An essay with the subtitle 'Superficial Reflections on the Hipster.' It discusses how existentialist hipsters ('philosophical psychopaths') 'absorbed the existentialist synapses of black men' after World War II. Opens with a discussion of the 'psychic havoc' of WWII. Describes jazz as the 'music of orgasm.' Popularized the term 'hipster' for white jazz aficionados. Posits a choice between death in state-sponsored violence (atomic bomb) and the slow death of conformity. CIA records show the agency kept a copy on file. Eventually collected in Advertisements for Myself. James Baldwin criticized Mailer's use of the language of 'deprived Negroes.'

Ancient Evenings

A historical fiction novel set in ancient Egypt about Menenhetet, a man who gets reincarnated three times. On the first occasion, he tells of his time serving as charioteer for Ramses II at the Battle of Kadesh. During the 'Night of the Pig,' he tells his life story. He is undone by the High Priest Khem-Usha. The novel discusses the indigestible phallus of Osiris and incorporates the Egyptian Book of the Dead. Orhan Pamuk was accused of plagiarizing this novel for My Name is Red.

General / Biographical

Mailer was known for his pugnacious personality. He stabbed his wife Adele Morales with a penknife at a party to launch his mayoral campaign, after she supposedly told him he wasn't as good as Dostoevsky. James Baldwin compared this event to 'burning down the house in order to be free of it.' His feud with Gore Vidal culminated in an iconic appearance on The Dick Cavett Show, where Cavett told him to 'fold it five ways and put it where the moon don't shine' and Mailer punched Vidal (told 'once again, words have failed' him). Kate Millett mocked him as 'a prisoner of the virility cult' in Sexual Politics, to which he responded with The Prisoner of Sex. He called Michiko Kakutani 'a one-woman kamikaze.' He wrote the book The Fight about the 'Rumble in the Jungle' (covered in the documentary When We Were Kings). He also wrote The Faith of Graffiti with photographer John Naar, The Castle in the Forest (won the Bad Sex in Fiction Award), The Gospel According to the Son (framed as autobiography of Jesus), Harlot's Ghost (about the CIA), and 'Superman Comes to the Supermarket' about JFK's rise. He bit off a chunk of Rip Torn's ear during a fight on the set of Maidstone. Letters addressed to him make up Jack Henry Abbott's In the Belly of the Beast. Town Bloody Hall (Pennebaker documentary) depicts his debates with Germaine Greer.

Summary of Facts

Norman Mailer was an American novelist, journalist, and public intellectual, a central figure of New Journalism known for his pugnacious personality and prolific output.

His first novel, The Naked and the Dead, draws on his WWII experience in the Philippines and depicts an American regiment's invasion of the fictional island Anopopei. It uses the euphemism 'fug,' prompting Dorothy Parker to call Mailer 'the man who can't spell fuck.' Sergeant Croft crushes a baby bird, kills a POW, and orders an attack on Mount Anaka.

The Armies of the Night is a nonfiction novel about the 1967 March on the Pentagon, split into 'History as a Novel' and 'The Novel as History.' Mailer joins friend Mitch Goodman and marches alongside Dwight Macdonald and Robert Lowell.

The Executioner's Song, winner of the Pulitzer Prize, chronicles the capital punishment of Gary Gilmore in Utah, who murders two men and demands his death penalty. Nicole Baker has a brief affair with him.

'The White Negro: Superficial Reflections on the Hipster' discusses how existentialist 'philosophical psychopaths' absorbed black culture after WWII, describes jazz as 'the music of orgasm,' and was collected in Advertisements for Myself. The CIA kept a copy on file. James Baldwin criticized Mailer's appropriation of black language.

An American Dream features Stephen Rojack murdering his wife and having sex with her maid. Ancient Evenings is set in Egypt about the thrice-reincarnated Menenhetet, charioteer for Ramses II.

Mailer stabbed his wife Adele Morales at a party launching his mayoral campaign after being told he wasn't as good as Dostoevsky. His feud with Gore Vidal culminated on The Dick Cavett Show, where he punched Vidal and was told 'once again, words have failed you.' Kate Millett attacked him as 'a prisoner of the virility cult' in Sexual Politics; he responded with The Prisoner of Sex. He wrote The Fight about the Rumble in the Jungle, bit Rip Torn's ear on the set of Maidstone, and wrote 'Superman Comes to the Supermarket' about JFK. Letters to him comprise Jack Henry Abbott's In the Belly of the Beast. The Pennebaker documentary Town Bloody Hall depicts his debates with Germaine Greer. He won the Bad Sex in Fiction Award for The Castle in the Forest.
