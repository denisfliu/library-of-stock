The Musical (2017)

A 2017 Broadway musical adapted from the 2001 French romantic comedy film by Jean-Pierre Jeunet. The title character is a shy waitress from Montmartre who carries out elaborate schemes to make other people happy. The musical features a neighbor with bone disease (known as 'The Glass Man' in the film) who repeatedly copies Renoir's Luncheon of the Boating Party. Amelie finds a mysterious box on the night of Princess Diana's death. Songs include 'Times are Hard for Dreamers,' in which Amelie describes nearby happenings to a blind beggar and sings 'this is how my world gets made.' Phillipa Soo originated the title role on Broadway (and played it in the Los Angeles staging). Wikipedia

Summary of Facts

The musical Amelie appears infrequently in quizbowl as its own answerline, appearing mainly as a bonus part rather than a tossup answer. It is most commonly clued through its connection to Phillipa Soo (who is far more often asked about for Hamilton), the adaptation from the 2001 French film, and the distinctive plot detail of the Glass Man neighbor who copies Renoir's Luncheon of the Boating Party.

The film version of Amelie appears much more frequently in quizbowl than the musical — as a clue for other answers like Paris, cathedrals, Renoir, and photographs. Key film details that cross over into musical clues include: the waitress premise, the Glass Man and Luncheon of the Boating Party, the mother's death by a tourist jumping off a cathedral, and Nino's photo booth photograph collection.

Players should be able to identify the musical from the Phillipa Soo connection, the Luncheon of the Boating Party detail, and the song 'Times are Hard for Dreamers.' The musical is described as 'non-Sondheim' in one bonus, distinguishing it from Sunday in the Park with George which also involves painting.
