Canadian short story writer, winner of the 2013 Nobel Prize in Literature. Most commonly identified by 'Dance of the Happy Shades' (Miss Marsalles's piano recital, disabled student plays Gluck), 'The Moons of Jupiter' (Janet, planetarium, dying father), 'The Bear Came Over the Mountain' (Grant, Fiona, Alzheimer's), and 'Boys and Girls' (narrator dismissed as 'only a girl'). Collections include Dear Life, Runaway, Hateship Friendship Courtship Loveship Marriage, and Lives of Girls and Women.
The Moons of Jupiter

The most frequently clued Munro story. Janet visits a planetarium between visits to her hospitalized father, who is awaiting heart surgery. She reflects on her strained relationship with her estranged daughter Nichola and the leukemia scare that may have altered her love. Her father remembers the phrase 'shoreless seas' from a Joaquin Miller poem.

The Bear Came Over the Mountain

Summary of Facts

Alice Munro is one of the most frequently clued short story writers in quizbowl, consistently identifiable through a handful of signature stories. 'The Moons of Jupiter' is her most heavily clued work, appearing in nearly every tossup: Janet visits a planetarium between visits to her dying father in the hospital, reflects on her estranged daughter Nichola and a leukemia scare, and her father remembers the phrase 'shoreless seas' from a Joaquin Miller poem. 'Dance of the Happy Shades' is the second most common giveaway, always identified through the piano recital at Miss Marsalles's house where a disabled girl from the Greenhill School plays the title Gluck piece beautifully.

'The Bear Came Over the Mountain' receives extensive treatment as a standalone bonus and tossup subject. Grant's wife Fiona develops Alzheimer's and falls in love with Aubrey at a nursing home; Grant seduces Aubrey's wife Marian to ensure Fiona can stay with Aubrey. Fiona's connection to her Icelandic heritage through reading Letters from Iceland is a recurring power clue. 'Boys and Girls,' the story of a tomboy on a fox farm who lets the horse Flora escape and is dismissed as 'only a girl,' is a standard mid-level clue.

'Hateship, Friendship, Courtship, Loveship, Marriage' appears regularly, identified through the forged love letters that Sabitha and Edith write from Sabitha's father to the housekeeper Johanna. 'The Love of a Good Woman' features the boys finding the dead optometrist Mr. Willens in a submerged car. Less common stories include 'Too Much Happiness' (Sofia Kovalevskaya), 'Miles City, Montana,' 'Free Radicals,' 'Chance,' and 'The Progress of Love.'

Munro is always identified as Canadian and as the 2013 Nobel laureate. Her collections Lives of Girls and Women, Runaway, Dear Life, and The View from Castle Rock appear as giveaway identifiers. She is frequently linked to Margaret Atwood and other Canadian authors.
