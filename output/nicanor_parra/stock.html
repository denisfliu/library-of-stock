Roberto Bolano admired his work, saying 'it will endure.'

Summary of Facts

Nicanor Parra was a Chilean poet and theoretical physicist/mathematician known as the creator of 'anti-poetry,' a movement opposing the lyrical conventions of poets like Pablo Neruda. He lived to age 103 and was a perennial Nobel Prize contender. Thomas Merton called him the 'poet of the sneeze.'

His landmark 1954 collection Poems and Antipoems established the concept. It includes 'The Soliloquy of the Individual,' in which the speaker describes advancing from fire to cities and highways, then concludes he should go back 'to that rock that was home, and start scratching all over again.' The collection was translated by Lawrence Ferlinghetti and Allen Ginsberg.

His visual poem series Artefactos consists of hundreds of postcards with pithy phrases such as 'Bargain crucifix, for sale' and 'U.S.A., where liberty is a statue.' It includes 'Lyric Poet Pithecantropos Erectus,' which satirizes Neruda's poem 'Walking Around.'

Parra was famous for saying 'I take back everything I've said' after poetry readings. One of his antipoems, 'I Take Back Everything I've Said,' ends by urging 'generous reader, burn this book' as the speaker leaves with a 'forced and sad smile.' His poem 'Manifesto' declares 'The poets have come down from Olympus,' and 'News Bulletin 1930' opens with 'A new era begins now, with the burning of the dirigible R101 as it crashes to earth.' Other collections include Emergency Poems, Versos de salon, and After Dinner Declarations.

Roberto Bolano admired Parra greatly, writing in 'Eight Seconds with Nicanor Parra' that his poetry 'will endure.' Parra jokingly listed Chile's 'four great poets' as 'three: Alonso de Ercilla and Ruben Dario' — the latter being Nicaraguan. He gave an academic speech in front of Neruda noting the contradiction with his status as an 'anti-poet.' Filmmaker Alejandro Jodorowsky met his girlfriend Stella through Parra, as depicted in the film Endless Poetry. Parra's sister Violeta Parra was a leading light of nueva cancion Chilena, known for 'Gracias a la vida.'
