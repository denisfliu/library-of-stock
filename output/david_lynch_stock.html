American surrealist filmmaker and multimedia artist (1946–2025). Known for dreamlike, disturbing imagery and nonlinear narratives. Major works include Blue Velvet, Twin Peaks (with Mark Frost), Mulholland Drive, The Elephant Man, and Eraserhead. Longtime collaborator with composer Angelo Badalamenti. Also a painter, musician, and writer (book: Catching the Big Fish).

General / Biographical

American filmmaker and multimedia artist David Lynch (1946–2025). Longtime music collaborator: Angelo Badalamenti (first met through Blue Velvet). Lynch wrote the book Catching the Big Fish about creativity and Transcendental Meditation. He also played slide guitar on BlueBOB (with John Neff) and made a painting called Rock with Seven Eyes. A 2019 Bonnefanten Museum retrospective displayed his surrealist artworks. He filmed a 13-minute shot of a cheese head in front of power lines for a film at the Festival of Disruption. David Foster Wallace wrote an essay titled '[Lynch] Keeps His Head.' Lynch ordered a chocolate milkshake at Bob's Big Boy every day at 2:30 pm for seven years. He and Mark Frost co-created Twin Peaks. Kyle MacLachlan frequently starred in his work. Wikipedia

Summary of Facts

David Lynch (1946–2025) was an American surrealist filmmaker and multimedia artist known for dreamlike, disturbing imagery and nonlinear narratives. He also painted (a 2019 Bonnefanten Museum retrospective displayed surrealist works like Rock with Seven Eyes and Boy Lights Fire), played slide guitar on an album (BlueBOB with John Neff), and wrote the book Catching the Big Fish about creativity and Transcendental Meditation. David Foster Wallace wrote an essay titled '[Lynch] Keeps His Head' about him. Lynch ordered a chocolate milkshake at Bob's Big Boy every day at 2:30 pm for seven years.

Blue Velvet (1986) opens with an extreme close-up of beetles warring in the grass and the discovery of a severed ear in a field. Dorothy Vallens (Isabella Rossellini) is a nightclub singer ritualistically raped by gas-huffing Frank Booth (Dennis Hopper) while Jeffrey Beaumont watches from a closet. Frank exclaims 'Heineken? Fuck that shit! Pabst! Blue! Ribbon!' — a line inspired by a fast food customer Lynch observed. Angelo Badalamenti was originally hired as Rossellini's singing coach for this film, beginning a lasting creative partnership with Lynch.

Twin Peaks (1990–91, 2017), co-created with Mark Frost, follows the murder of Laura Palmer. The theme 'Falling' was sung by Julee Cruise (lyrics by Lynch) and scored by Badalamenti. Agent Dale Cooper is known for praising 'a damn fine cup of coffee.' In the prequel film Twin Peaks: Fire Walk with Me, Cherubini's Requiem in C Minor plays as Leland kills his daughter in a train car and wraps her in plastic. Twin Peaks: The Return (2017) features a depiction of the Trinity nuclear test accompanied by Penderecki's Threnody for the Victims of Hiroshima. Dougie Jones, a 'tulpa' of Agent Cooper trapped in the Black Lodge and played by Kyle MacLachlan, was inspired by Peter Sellers's performance in Being There.

Mulholland Drive (2001) is a surrealist Hollywood mystery starring Naomi Watts and Laura Harring, whose characters shift identities when a mysterious blue box is opened. A creepy monster emerges from behind a diner. A woman believing herself to be Rita Hayworth discovers the body of Diane Selwyn. Lynch paid tribute to the doubling theme of Bergman's Persona.

The Elephant Man (1980) starred John Hurt, who spent eight hours a day in prosthetics to portray Joseph Merrick. Barber's Adagio for Strings plays as his character dies in his sleep, followed by a recitation of Tennyson's 'Nothing Will Die' — this predated Oliver Stone's more famous use of the same piece in Platoon.

Eraserhead (1977), Lynch's debut feature, opens with a giant floating sperm emerging from the protagonist Henry Spencer's mouth; Henry and Mary X struggle to care for and ultimately kill a horrifying skinless baby.
