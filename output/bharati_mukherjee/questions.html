Questions: Bharati Mukherjee

3 tossups · 7 bonuses

Tossups

T1 2025 LITANY (2025) Diff 8 · Literature
An author with this surname wrote a pentalogy of crime novels starring Captain Wyndham and Sergeant Banerjee. Another author with this surname created an unsuccessful farmer who kills his starving wife and children in the prologue of his novel The Lives of Others. An author with surname name chronicled the history of genes and genetic research in an “intimate history” and wrote a (*) “biography of cancer” titled The Emperor of All Maladies. A Canadian author with this surname fictionalized a plane bombing in her story “The Management of Grief.” For 10 points, the novels Wife and Jasmine were written by an author with what surname and first name Bharati?
ANSWER: Mukherjee [accept Abir Mukherjee, Neel Mukherjee, Siddhartha Mukherjee, or Bharati Mukherjee]
T2 2021 ACF Nationals (2021) Diff 9 · Literature
In a story by this author, the narrator’s neighbor tells her only “modern young girls have to say it” when she reveals she never told her husband that she loved him. The arms smuggler Alfred Judah lusts after Clovis Ransome’s wife in the title story of a collection by this author. This author wrote the book The Sorrow and the Terror with Clark Blaise, the basis of a story where a woman wades into a bay in Ireland and speaks to a couple on behalf of the social worker Judith Templeton. In a novel by this author, a girl kills the ship captain Half-Face before settling down in Iowa with the wheelchair-bound banker Bud Ripplemayer. That novel by this author is based on a story about a young maid from her collection The Middleman and Other Stories. For 10 points, name this Canadian author of Bengali origin who wrote the oft-anthologized story “The Management of Grief” and the novel Jasmine.
ANSWER: Bharati Mukherjee
T3 2012 Michigan KABO (2012) Diff 8 · Literature
This author described her own “twin halves” when living in a foreign country where she faced sentiments of racism in the essay “An Invisible Woman,” which parallels the protagonist of one of her novels who stabs her husband seven times. The narrator concludes that “I think I can only envy her” when thinking of Kusum, a woman who lost her daughter, in this author’s story “The (*) Management of Grief.” In one of this author’s novels, the title character lives on a farm in Iowa with an adopted child, Du, and the crippled Bud Ripplemeyer after the death of her husband at a young age. Often focusing on themes of displacement and immigration, this author wrote the collections Darkness and The Middleman and Other Stories. For 10 points, name this author of the novels Wife and Jasmine who hails from India.
ANSWER: Bharati Mukherjee [prompt on partial answer]

B1 2025 COOT (2025) Diff 8 · Literature
This novel’s title character lives in Iowa with the banker Bud Ripplemayer who repeatedly refuses Darrel Lutz’s loan applications for a new farm. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this novel. The protagonist of this novel immigrates to Florida after her husband is killed in a bombing attack while shopping for saris.
ANSWER: Jasmine

B2 2023 NASAT (2023) Diff 8 · Literature
The protagonist of a play in this language is introduced as "and Indrajit," since he is seen as a member of society instead of an individual with his own identity. For 10 points each:
[10] Give this language of the plays of Badal Sarkar. Amal is mocked for fantasizing about receiving a letter from the king in The Post Office, a play in this language.
ANSWER: Bengali [or Bangla]
[10] The Post Office is a Bengali play by this author of the poetry collection Gitanjali.
ANSWER: Rabindranath Tagore
[10] A Bengali playwright with this surname wrote the plays Mareech Sambad and Jagannath. An author of Bengali origin with this surname was inspired by the Air India Flight 182 bombing for a story centering on Shaila Bhave.
ANSWER: Mukherjee [or Bharati Mukherjee; or Arun Mukherjee]
B3 2023 ACF Regionals (2023) Diff 8 · Literature
In a novel set in this city, Murugan learns about a mysterious cult that tries to guide scientific research on malaria. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this city that partially titles a genetics-based historical fiction thriller by Amitav Ghosh. Supratik mobilizes an agrarian uprising in another novel set in this city, The Lives of Others.
ANSWER: Kolkata [or Calcutta; accept The Calcutta Chromosome]
[10] The Lives of Others is by an author with this surname and first name Neel. Another author with this surname and first name Bharati co-authored the memoir Days and Nights in Calcutta.
ANSWER: Mukherjee [accept Bharati Mukherjee; accept Neel Mukherjee]
[10] Anuradha, an author with this surname, depicts three generations of a family from Kolkata in An Atlas of Impossible Longing. The author of The God of Small Things has this surname and first name Arundhati.
ANSWER: Roy [accept Arundhati Roy; accept Anuradha Roy]

B5 2018 CMST (2018) Diff 9 · Literature

[10] In the first story in The Interpreter of Maladies, “A Temporary Matter,” a series of these events help rekindle the relationship of Shoba and Shukumar after the stillbirth of their son.
