American film director (1928–1999), one of cinema's most technically innovative auteurs. Known for meticulous cinematography, use of existing music, and a wide range of genres. Major works include 2001: A Space Odyssey, A Clockwork Orange, Barry Lyndon, The Shining, Dr. Strangelove, and Eyes Wide Shut. Started as a photographer for Look magazine.
2001: A Space Odyssey (1968)

Kubrick's landmark science fiction film. The opening popularized Richard Strauss's 'Thus Spoke Zarathustra.' 'The Blue Danube' plays during a space station docking sequence. Kubrick originally hired Alex North to write a score but rejected the entire score without telling North and used existing music by Gyorgy Ligeti and Richard Strauss instead. President-elect JFK saw Kubrick's earlier film Spartacus over protests from the American Legion for employing Dalton Trumbo as screenwriter. Wikipedia

General / Biographical

Stanley Kubrick (1928–1999) started his career as a teenager when he sold a staged photo of a newsstand employee looking sad over FDR's death to Look magazine. He then made his first film, the documentary Day of the Fight, adapted from a photoessay on boxer Walter Cartier. The Seafarers International Union commissioned his first color film, which drew on Eisenstein's Strike. His debut feature was The Killing (based on Lionel White's novel Clean). His family lived at Childwickbury Manor in Hertfordshire. He popularized the tracking shot across films. Wikipedia

Summary of Facts

Stanley Kubrick (1928–1999) began his career in photography, selling a staged photo of a newsstand employee reacting to FDR's death to Look magazine as a teenager. His first film was the documentary Day of the Fight, based on a photoessay on boxer Walter Cartier; the Seafarers International Union commissioned his first color film, which drew on motifs from Eisenstein's Strike. His debut narrative feature was The Killing (based on Lionel White's novel Clean), which involves sharpshooter Nikki Arcane taking out a horse to distract a crowd during a heist.

2001: A Space Odyssey (1968) opened with Richard Strauss's 'Thus Spoke Zarathustra' and features 'The Blue Danube' during a space station docking sequence. Kubrick commissioned Alex North to write a score but rejected it entirely without informing North, using pre-existing pieces by Gyorgy Ligeti and Richard Strauss instead.

A Clockwork Orange (1971) features protagonist Alex and his gang beating up writer F. Alexander and raping his wife to 'Singin' in the Rain'; Alex is then subjected to the Ludovico conditioning process to cure him of 'ultraviolence,' inadvertently making him unable to tolerate Beethoven. The Korova bar is plastered with the Russian word for milk ('moloko'); its furniture was inspired by Allen Jones's artworks (Hatstand, Table, Chair). The set features Herman Makkink sculptures (Rocking Machine; four Christs dancing arm-in-arm) and a painting by Kubrick's wife. The main theme is Purcell's Music for the Funeral of Queen Mary arranged for Moog synthesizer by Wendy Carlos; the William Tell Overture accompanies a three-minute timelapse of a threesome.

Barry Lyndon (1975) is a period drama in which the title character climbs the English social ladder but loses his left leg in a duel. Kubrick used NASA Zeiss lenses (f-stop 0.7) to shoot entirely by candlelight, inspired by Hogarth paintings; the camera slowly zooms outward throughout to mimic the framing of 18th-century paintings. The final shot recreates the last piece in Hogarth's Marriage-a-la-Mode series. The film grew out of Kubrick's scrapped Napoleon biopic idea.

The Shining (1980) ends with Kubrick personally airbrushing Jack Nicholson into a 1921 New Year's Eve party photograph, played to 'Midnight, the Stars, and You.' The Grady twins were inspired by Diane Arbus's photograph Identical Twins. Danny's tricycle scenes were filmed using the newly invented Steadicam by Garrett Brown.

Dr. Strangelove (1964) depicts General Jack D. Ripper ordering a nuclear attack on the Soviet Union; Major Kong waves his hat while riding an atomic bomb. The film ends with Vera Lynn's 'We'll Meet Again' as a character frets about a 'mineshaft gap.' It was inspired in part by RAND Corporation analyst Herman Kahn's On Thermonuclear War.

Eyes Wide Shut (1999), Kubrick's final film, was adapted from Arthur Schnitzler's Dream Story. Tom Cruise infiltrates a masked orgy at a former Rothschild home by saying the password 'fidelio.' The orgy's score features Jocelyn Pook's reversed Romanian liturgy and originally included Sri Lankan Manickam Yogeswaran reciting the Bhagavad Gita (removed after protests). Cate Blanchett voiced a character's lines uncredited. A blindfolded pianist was played by director Todd Field.

Paths of Glory (1957) depicts WWI trench warfare and ends with a German prisoner singing 'The Faithful Hussar.' Kirk Douglas starred before reuniting with Kubrick on Spartacus (1960); Kubrick met his wife Christiane on the Paths of Glory set. Spartacus inspired the famous 'I'm Spartacus' solidarity line, later parodied in the Lolita adaptation and Monty Python's Life of Brian.
