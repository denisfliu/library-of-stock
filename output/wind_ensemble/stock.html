What is the Wind Ensemble

The wind ensemble (also called concert band, symphonic band, military band, or wind band) consists of woodwinds, brass, and percussion — no strings. It is different from the orchestra and the marching band. Conductor Frederick Fennell founded the Eastman Wind Ensemble at the Eastman School of Music, which became a landmark for interpreting the genre. John Philip Sousa ('The March King') led a famous wind ensemble for decades. The Leeds Music Corporation commissioned works for this ensemble, including Milhaud's Suite Française. The genre includes both 'concert' settings (with symphonies, suites, and tone poems) and marching band formats.

Gustav Holst — Suites for Military Band

Holst wrote two suites for military/wind band — the First Suite in E-flat and the Second Suite in F. The Second Suite incorporates seven English folk songs including 'I'll Love My Love,' 'Greensleeves,' and 'Fantasia on the Dargason' (the final movement). The First Suite opens with a chaconne. The 'Song of the Blacksmith' in one suite calls for an anvil. Holst originally composed the Fantasia on the Dargason for the second suite. Wikipedia

Percy Grainger — Lincolnshire Posy and Other Works

Percy Grainger composed Lincolnshire Posy for wind band — a six-movement piece based on English folk tunes, including 'Rufford Park Poachers' and 'The Lost Lady Found' (final movement). He also composed Irish Tune and Colonial Song for band. Lincolnshire Posy features unusual time signatures including 1.5/4 and 2.5/4 in the 'Lord Melbourne' movement. Wikipedia

Summary of Facts

The wind ensemble (also called concert band, symphonic band, military band, or wind band) is an instrumental ensemble consisting of woodwinds, brass, and percussion without a string section. It differs from the orchestra (which has strings) and the marching band (which is mobile and primarily ceremonial). The conductor Frederick Fennell founded the landmark Eastman Wind Ensemble at the Eastman School of Music. John Philip Sousa, 'The March King,' led a famous wind ensemble for decades and composed iconic marches such as 'The Stars and Stripes Forever,' 'The Thunderer,' and 'Semper Fidelis.'

Gustav Holst wrote two suites for military band: the First Suite in E-flat (which opens with a chaconne) and the Second Suite in F (which incorporates seven English folk songs including 'Greensleeves' and concludes with the 'Fantasia on the Dargason'). The 'Song of the Blacksmith' movement calls for an anvil. Percy Grainger's Lincolnshire Posy is a six-movement suite based on English folk tunes including 'Rufford Park Poachers' and 'The Lost Lady Found'; it features unusual time signatures (1.5/4 and 2.5/4) in the 'Lord Melbourne' movement. Grainger also wrote Irish Tune and Colonial Song for band.

Hector Berlioz wrote the Grande Symphonie funèbre et triomphale for wind ensemble to commemorate the tenth anniversary of the July Revolution; the martial third movement 'Apotheosis' can optionally include chorus. Paul Hindemith's Symphony in B-flat is his only work for the medium; its third movement is a fugue marked 'Rather broad.' Ralph Vaughan Williams's English Folk Song Suite (originally for military band, published by Boosey & Hawkes) quotes 'Dives and Lazarus' and 'Seventeen Come Sunday' in its first movement. Handel's Music for the Royal Fireworks was originally written for wind ensemble to accompany fireworks.

Other significant works include: Frank Ticheli's Angels in the Architecture (unusual for the genre in beginning and ending with a soprano singing 'I am an angel of light'); John Mackey's Wine-Dark Sea (three movements inspired by the Odyssey); David Maslanka's A Child's Garden of Dreams and Give Us This Day; William Schuman's George Washington Bridge and Chester Overture; Alfred Reed's Armenian Dances; and Johan de Meij's 'Lord of the Rings' Symphony. The Leeds Music Corporation commissioned Darius Milhaud's Suite Française for this ensemble.
