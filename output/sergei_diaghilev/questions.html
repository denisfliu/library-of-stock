Tossups

T1 2014 ACF Nationals (2014) Diff 9 · Fine Arts
This man praised a contemporary for adhering to the model of a “producer-autocrat” in his article “More About Julius Cae-sar.” This man went through the painstaking process of choosing twenty sonatas out of Domenico Scarlatti’s 555 to score an adaptation of Carlo Goldoni’s The Good-Humored Ladies, the frst of what Constant Lambert termed his “time-travelling” works. He expressed many of his ideas in the periodical he co-founded, World of Art. This man’s diferences with Prince Volkonsky led to the cancellation of their planned collaborative staging of Sylvia. This man’s collaborator on the choreogra-phy of Les Noces was the younger sister of his lover, who broke up with him in an acrimonious dispute and was diagnosed with schizophrenia. His friends Alexandre Benois and Leon Bakst designed his costumes and sets, and he launched another collab-orator’s career by hiring him to compose music for The Firebird. For 10 points, name this collaborator with Igor Stravinsky, the founder of Ballet Russes.
ANSWER: Sergei Pavlovich Diaghilev

[10] Name this action done in a pas de chat (“pah duh SHAH”) or assemblé. Dancers will often begin or end in a demi-plié (“dem-EE-plee-AY”) while doing this action.
ANSWER: jumping [accept leaping or hopping; accept sauté; prompt on being in the air]
[10] Monday is about strengthening the aplomb, or center of gravity, through repeated exercise on this handrail used for warmups. It is phonetically identical to a long piece of wood or metal.
ANSWER: barre (“bar”)
[10] Dancers for this ballet company like Anna Pavlova and Vaslav Nijinsky strictly adhered to Cecchetti’s weekly schedule. Sergei Diaghilev founded this company, whose members were largely recruited from the Saint Petersburg ballet.
ANSWER: Ballets Russes (“roose”) [or Les Ballet Russes de Serge Diaghileff; accept the Russian Ballet or Diaghilev’s Russian Ballet]

ANSWER: en pointe

ANSWER: The Rite of Spring [or Le sacre du printemps]

[10] Name this neoclassical ballet whose music is based on compositions spuriously attributed to Giovanni Pergolesi. Florindo and Cloviello beat up its commedia dell'arte-inspired title character after he is caught dancing with Rosetta.
ANSWER: Pulcinella
[10] The first collaboration between Sergei Diaghilev and the composer of Pulcinella was this 1910 ballet, whose protagonist Prince Ivan was originally played by choreographer Michel Fokine. The title character bewitches the servants of Koschei the Immortal, forcing them to perform an “Infernal Dance”.
ANSWER: The Firebird [or L'Oiseau de feu; or Zhar-ptitsa]
[10] The music for both Pulcinella and The Firebird was composed by this Russian, whose collaboration with the Ballets Russes also produced the famously controversial The Rite of Spring.
ANSWER: Igor Stravinsky [or Igor Fyodorovich Stravinsky]
