Summary of Facts

W.D. Snodgrass is an American Confessional poet best known for Heart's Needle, a ten-poem sequence about his divorce and separation from his daughter Cynthia Jean, whom he calls "Child of my winter." The collection's title and epigraph come from The Madness of Suibhne, an Irish text containing a remark about "an only daughter." Heart's Needle won the 1960 Pulitzer Prize and is considered one of the most influential works of confessional poetry, arguably launching the movement. The sequence alternates personal imagery of the separation with scenes from the Korean War, and concludes "You are still my daughter."

Snodgrass also wrote The Fuhrer Bunker, a collection of dramatic monologues from the perspective of Hitler and other Nazis during the last days of World War II. One poem, "Dr. Joseph Goebbels," describes "a cell of concrete" where "liars face up to blank truth" and ends "all things shall be equal."
