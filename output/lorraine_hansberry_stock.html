Lorraine Hansberry

← Home · View source questions · Make cards
African-American playwright best known for A Raisin in the Sun, the first play by a black woman staged on Broadway. Also wrote The Sign in Sidney Brustein's Window and the unfinished Les Blancs. Her ex-husband Robert Nemiroff compiled her writings into To Be Young, Gifted, and Black. Died of pancreatic cancer at age 34.
A Raisin in the Sun

Hansberry's most famous play, the first by a black woman staged on Broadway, about the Younger family in a Chicago apartment. Inspired by the real court case Hansberry v. Lee, which barred her father from buying property due to racially restrictive covenants. Walter Lee, son of a railroad porter, frequently argues with his sister Beneatha. Their mother Mama receives an insurance payout and plans to move the family to the all-white neighborhood of Clybourne Park. Karl Lindner, a representative of the Clybourne Park Improvement Association, tries to bribe/buy out the Youngers. Walter drunkenly jumps on a table and shouts 'OCOMOGOSIAY!' and is described as coming into his manhood 'like a rainbow after the rain' when he rejects Lindner's offer. Beneatha dreams of being a doctor and must choose between the Nigerian suitor Joseph Asagai (who calls her hair straightening 'mutilation' and asks her to move to Nigeria) and the older, assimilated George Murchison. Beneatha's plant is called a 'raggedy-looking old thing,' and she shouts 'It expresses ME.' The play's title comes from Langston Hughes's poem 'Harlem' ('What happens to a dream deferred?'). Bruce Norris's Clybourne Park is a sequel, and Kwame Kwei-Armah's Beneatha's Place completes the Raisin Cycle.

The Sign in Sidney Brustein's Window

A play centering on Sidney Brustein, a Jewish intellectual in Greenwich Village during an election. A sign in his window bearing slogans 'Clean Up Community Politics' and 'Wipe Out Bossism' supports the politician Wally O'Hara, whose campaign is pushed by the activist Alton Scales. David Ragin is told to 'believe in independent men.' Sidney's wife Iris has the maiden name Parodus (which her sister Mavis points out is associated with the ancient Greek chorus). Alton, who pushes Sidney to endorse O'Hara, withdraws his marriage proposal to Gloria after learning she is a prostitute, leading Gloria to commit suicide. Sidney states the old refrain 'It wasn't supposed to be a nightclub' about his failed venture at creating Walden Pond. The title character buys the Village Crier newspaper from Harvey Wyatt after moving to Majorca.

To Be Young, Gifted, and Black

A play compiled from Hansberry's letters, interviews, and unpublished autobiographical writings by her ex-husband Robert Nemiroff after her death. The play inspired Nina Simone's song about the 'precious dream' of having the three title attributes. It begins with the claim 'I have been possessed of the desire to put down the stuff of my life.' In it, Hansberry describes her father's Supreme Court case (Hansberry v. Lee) about racially restrictive covenants, and she recalls being allowed to take 'the most desired of all — one GIANT step' in children's games.

Les Blancs and General

Les Blancs was left unfinished at Hansberry's death. In it, the European intellectual Tshembe returns to Africa for his father's funeral. Hansberry criticized Jean Genet's The Blacks in this play. She died of pancreatic cancer at age 34. Her ex-husband Robert Nemiroff withheld her personal papers, concealing her lesbianism for years after her death. She was working on an unfinished 'Musical Drama in 7 Scenes' about Toussaint L'Ouverture at the time of her death. A biography by Imani Perry examines her 'radical life.' Beneatha in A Raisin in the Sun is considered a semi-autobiographical portrayal.

Summary of Facts

Lorraine Hansberry was an African-American playwright best known for A Raisin in the Sun, the first play by a black woman staged on Broadway. Inspired by the real court case Hansberry v. Lee about racially restrictive covenants in Chicago, the play follows the Younger family in their apartment. Mama receives an insurance payout and plans to move to the all-white neighborhood of Clybourne Park. Karl Lindner tries to bribe the family not to move. Walter Lee drunkenly shouts 'OCOMOGOSIAY!' and comes into his manhood 'like a rainbow after the rain' when rejecting Lindner. Beneatha dreams of being a doctor and chooses between Nigerian suitor Joseph Asagai (who calls her hair straightening 'mutilation') and the assimilated George Murchison. The title comes from Langston Hughes's poem 'Harlem.' Bruce Norris's Clybourne Park and Kwame Kwei-Armah's Beneatha's Place continue the Raisin Cycle.

The Sign in Sidney Brustein's Window centers on a Jewish intellectual in Greenwich Village during an election. Sidney supports the politician Wally O'Hara via a sign reading 'Clean Up Community Politics' and 'Wipe Out Bossism.' The activist Alton Scales withdraws his proposal to Gloria after learning she is a prostitute, leading to her suicide. Iris's maiden name Parodus recalls the ancient Greek chorus.

Hansberry died of pancreatic cancer at age 34. Her ex-husband Robert Nemiroff compiled her autobiographical writings into To Be Young, Gifted, and Black, which inspired a Nina Simone song. He withheld her personal papers, concealing her lesbianism. Her unfinished play Les Blancs features Tshembe returning to Africa for his father's funeral and criticizes Genet's The Blacks. She was also working on an unfinished musical about Toussaint L'Ouverture.
