American Broadway playwright, one of the most commercially successful dramatists of the 20th century. Best known for The Odd Couple (Felix Unger and Oscar Madison), Barefoot in the Park (newlyweds Paul and Corie Bratter), and the Eugene Jerome trilogy (Brighton Beach Memoirs, Biloxi Blues, Broadway Bound). Also wrote Lost in Yonkers, Rumors, The Sunshine Boys, The Last of the Red-Hot Lovers, and the musical Sweet Charity.
The Odd Couple

Simon's most famous play about the mismatched roommates Felix Unger (a fastidious neat-freak hypochondriac) and Oscar Madison (a slovenly sportswriter). Felix moves in with Oscar after his wife kicks him out. The play opens during a poker game; the players realize Felix has disinfected the playing cards. Felix leaves a note on Oscar's pillow saying 'We're all out of Corn Flakes. F.U.' Oscar throws a plate of linguini at the wall. The two attempt to double-date the British Pigeon sisters. Felix takes green pills and sends 'suicide telegrams.' Adapted twice as a TV comedy and into films starring Walter Matthau and Jack Lemmon.

Eugene Jerome Trilogy (Brighton Beach Memoirs / Biloxi Blues / Broadway Bound)

Simon's semi-autobiographical trilogy following Eugene Jerome, a Brooklyn teenager, through adolescence and early adulthood. In Brighton Beach Memoirs, 15-year-old Eugene grows up with his brother Stanley, widowed Aunt Blanche, and cousins Nora and Laurie Morton, whispering disease names to explain how Aunt Blanche became a widow. In Biloxi Blues, Eugene is a WWII private training under Sergeant Toomey in Mississippi, where Private Arnold Epstein forces the abusive sergeant to do two hundred push-ups. Broadway Bound ends with Jack and Kate separating.

Barefoot in the Park

A comedy about newlyweds Paul and Corie Bratter. Corie is impulsive and tries to get her uptight lawyer husband Paul to unwind. Their apartment has a leaking skylight and they deal with intrusions from their eccentric upstairs neighbor Victor Velasco. Corie complains that Paul won't walk barefoot in Washington Square Park; Paul protests it was seventeen degrees. The play kicked off Mike Nichols's Broadway directing career. Gene Saks adapted it into a film with Robert Redford and Jane Fonda, who hear an Albanian folk song at a restaurant.

General / Biographical

Neil Simon (full name Marvin Neil Simon) was an immensely prolific American playwright and screenwriter. He had five marriages, which inspired The Dinner Party. Mike Nichols directed several of his plays, winning four Tony Awards. Simon's plays were frequently adapted into films and TV shows, with The Odd Couple adapted twice as a television comedy.

Summary of Facts

Neil Simon (born Marvin Neil Simon) was one of the most commercially successful American playwrights of the 20th century. The clues paint a picture of a prolific comedic dramatist whose works center on New York City life, family dynamics, and mismatched relationships.

His most iconic work is The Odd Couple, which appears as a giveaway in virtually every tossup. The play centers on the fastidious Felix Unger, who moves in with the slovenly sportswriter Oscar Madison after being kicked out by his wife. Key clues include Felix's 'We're all out of Corn Flakes. F.U.' note, Felix's green pills, the disinfected poker cards, Oscar throwing linguini at the wall, and the pair's ill-fated double date with the British Pigeon sisters.

Simon's semi-autobiographical Eugene Jerome trilogy (Brighton Beach Memoirs, Biloxi Blues, Broadway Bound) follows a Brooklyn teenager through adolescence and WWII military service. The most frequently clued scene is from Biloxi Blues, where Private Arnold Epstein forces the abusive Sergeant Toomey to do 200 push-ups. Brighton Beach Memoirs features Eugene whispering disease names (cancer, diphtheria, asthma) to explain family secrets.

Barefoot in the Park follows the newlyweds Paul and Corie Bratter, with Corie's famous complaint about Paul refusing to walk barefoot in Washington Square Park (his defense: it was 17 degrees). The play launched Mike Nichols's Broadway directing career and was adapted into a film starring Robert Redford and Jane Fonda.

Other frequently referenced Simon works include Lost in Yonkers (Jay and Arty staying with Aunt Bella), The Last of the Red-Hot Lovers (Barney Cashman's failed seductions), Rumors (Deputy Mayor Charlie shooting himself in the earlobe), The Sunshine Boys (reuniting vaudeville team), Sweet Charity (based on Fellini's Nights of Cabiria with Fosse's 'Big Spender'), and his screenplays for The Goodbye Girl and The Heartbreak Kid.
