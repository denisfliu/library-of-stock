Tossups

T1 2025 COOT (2025) Diff 8 · Literature
In Olive Kitteridge, the depressed Kevin Coulson remembers a poem by this author that mentions “Hemingway in despair, Hemingway at the end”. This author observes that “we [...] flash and yearn” like the sky and sea in a poem ending with a dog leaving behind the “wag” of its tail. This 20th-century poet started twelve consecutive lines with the words ‘I am’ in a poem labelling himself “the blackt-out man” and “the king of the pool”. A plea to “save us from shotguns & fathers’ (*) suicides” was written by this poet, who called life “a handkerchiefsandwich” in a ‘Confession’ featuring the character ‘Mr. Bones’. This poet remembered being told “you have no Inner Resources” in a poem that opens “Life, friends, is boring”. The character ‘Huffy’ Henry appears in, for 10 points, which Confessional poet’s Dream Songs?
ANSWER: John Berryman [or John Allyn Smith, Jr]

(*) This is not for tears” before concluding “Nobody is ever missing.” Each of the four season finale episodes of Succession draws its episode title from that poem by this poet. The Hold Steady’s song “Stuck Between Stations” describes the suicide of this poet by jumping off the Washington Avenue Bridge in Minneapolis. T4 2016 Penn Bowl (2016) Diff 8 · Literature
This poet claimed that “Death is a German expert” and asked what a little boy who dropped his ball should do, since he is “learning … the epistemology of loss.” This poet of Love and Fame and Delusions, Etc. wrote “I spit upon this dreadful bankers grave” and wrote about a “huffy” man who “hid the day” and “sulked” while “empty grows every bed.” This poet concluded he had “no inner resources” and claimed “Life, friends, is boring” in a collection inspired by the suicide of his father. He asked a woman (*) “moved you not, restless, waiting for [your husband]?” in a work addressed to the author of The Tenth Muse Lately Sprung Up in America, and expanded his most famous work in His Toy, His Dream, His Rest. For 10 points, name this Confessional poet with the alter ego Henry, who wrote Homage to Mistress Bradstreet and The Dream Songs.
