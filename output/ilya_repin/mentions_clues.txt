[BM8] 2017 Penn Bowl (2017) | Diff: 8 | Science
     Leadin: Name some things about fight or flight responses, for 10 points each:
  Part 1 (ans: sympathetic nervous system):
    Fight or flight is stimulated by this part of the autonomic nervous system, contrasted with the parasympathetic nervous system. Its neurons release acetylcholine to trigger the release of norepinephrine.
  Part 2 (ans: catecholamines):
    Many hormones released by the sympathetic nervous system, including epinephrine, norepinephrine, and dopamine, belong to this class of hormones derived from tyrosine.
  Part 3 (ans: chromaffin cells [do NOT accept enterochromaffin cells]):
    These cells in the medulla of the adrenal glands are activated by the sympathetic nervous system's acetylcholine release from the splanchnic nerve to release epinephrine. They are also found in the organ of Zuckerlandl.

[BM9] 2016 Chicago Open (2016) | Diff: 10 | Science
     Leadin: At this point in the day, you're probably reflecting on how nice it would be if humans could biosynthesize caffeine. Sadly, no. For 10 points each:
  Part 1 (ans: S-adenosyl-methionine [or SAM]):
    The last enzyme in the caffeine biosynthesis pathway, which is disappointingly expressed only in plants, converts theobromine to caffeine using this ubiquitous methylation cofactor. In humans, it is also required to convert norepinephrine to epinephrine.
  Part 2 (ans: uric acid [or urate]):
    The starting material, xanthosine, is a purine native to humans, but rather than metabolizing it to stimulants, we oxidize it to this worthless compound, which accumulates in gout. Birds excrete nitrogen in the form of this compound.
  Part 3 (ans: salvage pathways):
    Humans would probably make more caffeine than plants since we express the enzyme HGPRT, an easy route to xanthine from degraded nucleotides through one of these purine biosynthesis pathways. They are contrasted with de novo routes.
