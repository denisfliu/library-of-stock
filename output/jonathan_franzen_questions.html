Tossups

T1 2025 LITANY (2025) Diff 8 · Philosophy
This text’s name is shared by a projected trilogy about “the fundamentally irrational basis for everything we think and do and espouse” whose first volume details the failing marriage of the New Prospect, Illinois couple Russ and Marion Hildebrant. Writing pamphlets called “parerga” distracts the author of this text from completing it. Research for this text, which is considered a “tomb” by the author’s wife, is conducted at the Vatican during the author’s (*) honeymoon. Instructions to complete this text are found at Lowick by a character who offers to learn Latin or Greek to help work on it. This text is revealed to be doomed to be out of date by Will Ladislaw due to its author’s inability to read German. For 10 points, name this scholarly text meant to link all belief systems to Christianity, which is left unfinished by Edward Causabon in Middlemarch.
ANSWER: The Key to All Mythologies (the first line refers to Jonathan Franzen’s trilogy A Key to All Mythologies)
T2 2022 NASAT (2022) Diff 8 · Literature
In a 2021 novel by this author, Frances Cottrell is flirted with by the associate minister Russ Hildebrandt. This author cites Shirley Brice Heath's work on "forced transition zones" in an essay that recounts promoting a novel about St. Louis police chief S. Jammu, The Twenty-Seventh City. This author, who is currently writing the trilogy A Key To All Mythologies, described struggling to read The Recognitions by William Gaddis in the essay "Mr. Difficult." This author of a literary manifesto titled either "Perchance to Dream" or "Why Bother?" wrote a novel in which the drug Corecktall (correct-all) is used by the retired railway engineer Alfred to treat his Parkinson's disease. That novel by this author is set in St. Jude after Enid reunites the Lambert family for "one last Christmas." For 10 points, name this contemporary author of The Corrections.
ANSWER: Jonathan Franzen [Jonathan Earl Franzen]
T3 2017 Eisenhower Memorial Tournament (EMT) (2017) Diff 7 · Literature

This author was criticized for elitism after saying that his goal of (*) “actually reaching a male audience” made him uncomfortable about one of his novels’ selection for Oprah’s Book Club. That novel by this author centers on Enid’s plans to have her family, including her dementia-suffering husband Alfred, return to St. Jude for Christmas together. For 10 points, name this American author of Freedom and The Corrections.

T4 2014 PADAWAN (2014) Diff 7 · Literature
