Thomas Middleton

Jacobean-era English playwright. Most commonly identified by Women Beware Women, A Chaste Maid in Cheapside, The Changeling (with William Rowley), and The Revenger's Tragedy (now attributed to Middleton rather than Cyril Tourneur). Collaborated with Shakespeare on Timon of Athens and possibly Macbeth and All's Well That Ends Well. Also co-wrote The Roaring Girl and The Honest Whore with Thomas Dekker.

Summary of Facts

Thomas Middleton was a Jacobean-era English playwright, most commonly identified by the plays Women Beware Women and A Chaste Maid in Cheapside, and by his collaborations with William Rowley on The Changeling. He is now generally attributed as the author of The Revenger's Tragedy, previously credited to Cyril Tourneur.

The Changeling, co-written with William Rowley, is Middleton's most frequently clued individual work. Middleton wrote the main tragic plot set in Vermandero's castle, while Rowley wrote the comic subplot set in a madhouse. In the main plot, Beatrice-Joanna induces the servant De Flores to murder her fiance Alonzo de Piracquo by showing him secret passages, so that she might marry Alsemero instead. The play features a bed trick in which De Flores murders the substitute under cover of a fire when Beatrice worries that daylight will reveal the deception. Beatrice-Joanna and De Flores ultimately die together after their affair is discovered by Alsemero, while Tomazo seeks revenge for his brother Alonzo. In the comic subplot, Alibius runs a madhouse where he confines his wife Isabella to prevent adultery; Antonio and Franciscus feign foolishness and madness in order to seduce Isabella, and they are originally accused of the central murder. An insane Welshman memorably cries "Cat-whore, cat-whore! Her Parmesan, her Parmesan!" about a cat that stole his cheese.

A Chaste Maid in Cheapside is a city comedy set in Cheapside, a London district whose bawdy reputation contributes to the play's humor. The play centers on Moll Yellowhammer and involves the virile Touchwood Senior, who must leave his wife for fear of having yet another child. Allwit and his wife expel Sir Walter after news that he slew Touchwood, the lover of his fiancee. Touchwood and Moll are ultimately wed after their double funeral is revealed as a ruse. In subplots, a woman hides a baby under mutton to trick the promoters into taking her child, and the foolish university graduate Tim speaks to his fiancee in Latin, only to mistake her Welsh for Hebrew.

Women Beware Women centers on Bianca and Leantio in a love quadrangle that ends with most characters dead. The play opens with Leantio bragging to his mother of his "unvalued'st purchase" of a wife. A famous scene features a chess match between Mother and Livia that mirrors a rape carried out above and offstage. The fool Ward swears not to eat "eggs in moonshine" as they give him erections. The play ends with a bloody masque in which Hippolito and Isabella's incest is punished, Leantio's death is avenged, and the Duke and Bianca's marriage ends in poisoned death.

The Revenger's Tragedy follows the character Vindice in his quest for revenge. It features a bed trick. Contemporary scholarship now attributes the play to Middleton rather than Cyril Tourneur. A Game of Chess is a satirical play allegorically themed around chess, depicting the relationship between Spain and Great Britain.

Middleton had significant connections to Shakespeare. He may have co-written Timon of Athens with Shakespeare — the play about an overly generous Greek who declares himself an enemy of mankind after realizing his friends are parasites. Scholar Gary Taylor concluded that Middleton wrote approximately eleven percent of Macbeth, particularly the scenes with Hecate (Act 3 Scene 5 and Act 4 Scene 1). Middleton may also have written part of All's Well That Ends Well. He co-wrote The Roaring Girl with Thomas Dekker, dramatizing the life of Mary Firth as Moll (both authors shared the first name Thomas). He also co-wrote The Honest Whore with Dekker.
