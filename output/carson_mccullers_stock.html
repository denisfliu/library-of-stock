American Southern Gothic author known for The Heart Is a Lonely Hunter, The Member of the Wedding, The Ballad of the Sad Cafe, and Reflections in a Golden Eye. Her works frequently feature tomboyish young women, deaf-mute characters, and themes of loneliness and isolation in the American South.
The Heart Is a Lonely Hunter

McCullers's debut and best-known novel, originally titled The Mute, about the deaf-mute John Singer who works as a jewelry repairman. Four characters confide in Singer: Dr. Copeland (a Black physician dying of tuberculosis who delivers a Christmas speech about Karl Marx), Jake Blount (an alcoholic who beats his head against bricks and advocates for workers' rights), Mick Kelly (a young tomboy who hears the Eroica Symphony on a neighbor's radio and tries to build a homemade violin), and Biff Brannon (who runs the New York Cafe). Singer's best friend Spiros Antonapoulos becomes a kleptomaniac and is committed to a mental asylum, where he dies; Singer commits suicide upon learning of his death. Richard Wright praised McCullers for handling Negro characters 'with as much ease and justice as those of her own race.'

The Member of the Wedding

A novel about the tomboyish twelve-year-old Frankie Addams who obsesses over the wedding of her brother Jarvis and his bride Janice, dreaming of joining them on their honeymoon to Alaska. She renames herself 'F. Jasmine' and wants to become part of 'the we of me.' The novel features the housekeeper Berenice Sadie Brown, and Frankie's cousin John Henry West. A drunken red-haired soldier makes a date with Frankie, and she later hits a soldier over the head with a pitcher during a sexual assault. McCullers adapted the novel into a successful Broadway play. Composed while at February House.

The Ballad of the Sad Cafe

A novella about Miss Amelia Evans, who loses a fight with her former husband of ten days, Marvin Macy, after the intervention of the hunchbacked Cousin Lymon. Marvin Macy returns from prison and ransacks the cafe with Cousin Lymon, and absconds with her money and her best friend. The story examines themes of unrequited love and loneliness in a small Southern town.

Reflections in a Golden Eye

A novel set on a military base about the homosexual Captain Weldon Penderton, whose wife Leonora has an affair with Major Langdon. Private Ellgee Williams is attracted to Leonora and spies on her after clearing tree branches. The novel features the Filipino houseboy Anacleto, who helps Alison Langdon around the house after she cuts off her nipples. Tennessee Williams wrote the introduction defending its portrayal of homosexuality. Made into a film with Elizabeth Taylor and Marlon Brando.

General / Biographical

McCullers was a Southern Gothic author from Georgia. She co-founded the queer literary commune February House with W.H. Auden, where they lived with Benjamin Britten, Klaus Mann, and Gypsy Rose Lee. Her close friend Tennessee Williams wrote the introduction to Reflections in a Golden Eye. Suzanne Vega explored her relationship with photographer Annemarie Schwarzenbach in an album. Jenn Shapland wrote about her 'retroactive closeting by peers and biographers.' Carlos Dews is working on a book of her letters. Her unfinished autobiography is Illumination and Night Glare.

Summary of Facts

Carson McCullers is a frequently clued Southern Gothic author known for works exploring loneliness, isolation, and unrequited love in the American South. Her debut novel The Heart Is a Lonely Hunter centers on the deaf-mute John Singer, who serves as an unwitting confidant for four characters: the tomboy Mick Kelly (who hears the Eroica Symphony on a neighbor's radio and tries to build a violin), the Black physician Dr. Copeland (dying of tuberculosis, delivering a Marx speech), the alcoholic Jake Blount, and the cafe owner Biff Brannon. Singer's best friend Spiros Antonapoulos is committed to an asylum and dies there, prompting Singer's suicide.

The Member of the Wedding follows twelve-year-old Frankie Addams, who obsesses over her brother Jarvis's wedding and dreams of joining the honeymoon to Alaska, renaming herself 'F. Jasmine.' The novel features the housekeeper Berenice Sadie Brown and was adapted into a Broadway play. It was composed at February House, the queer literary commune McCullers co-founded with W.H. Auden.

The Ballad of the Sad Cafe centers on Miss Amelia Evans, who is defeated by her former ten-day husband Marvin Macy with the help of the hunchbacked Cousin Lymon. Reflections in a Golden Eye, set on a military base, features the homosexual Captain Penderton, his wife Leonora's affair with Major Langdon, and Private Williams's obsessive spying. Tennessee Williams wrote its introduction defending the portrayal of homosexuality.

McCullers's works are characterized by tomboyish heroines (Mick Kelly, Frankie Addams), themes of deafness and isolation, and the Southern Gothic tradition.
