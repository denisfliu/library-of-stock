A 1987 Stephen Sondheim musical (lyrics and music) with a book by James Lapine, their second collaboration. Interweaves fairy tale characters — Cinderella, Jack (and the Beanstalk), Little Red Riding Hood, Rapunzel, and the Baker and his Wife — who venture into the woods to fulfill their wishes. The Witch drives the plot by demanding the Baker collect four items (a cow as white as milk, a cape as red as blood, hair as yellow as corn, a slipper as pure as gold) to reverse a curse. Act II subverts fairy-tale endings as characters face consequences. Joanna Gleason originated the role of the Baker's Wife. Bernadette Peters originated the Witch. The musical opens and closes with the word 'I wish.'

A comedic duet performed by Cinderella's Prince and Rapunzel's Prince, in which they try to one-up each other over their respective loves. Musical Motifs and Structure

The musical features distinctive leitmotifs. A recurring motif begins with a descending fifth (A down to D, E, C-sharp, B) associated with the warning 'Don't you know what's out there in the world?' The motif 'short D, long E' represents the closing words 'I wish.' The show was Sondheim's second collaboration with librettist James Lapine (after Sunday in the Park with George). Wikipedia

Summary of Facts

Into the Woods is one of the most frequently referenced Stephen Sondheim musicals in quizbowl, appearing both as a direct answerline and as one of the standard identifiers for Sondheim (alongside Sweeney Todd, Company, and Sunday in the Park with George). The musical premiered in 1987 with a book by James Lapine, making it their second collaboration after Sunday in the Park with George.

The most commonly clued elements are the songs "Agony" (the two princes' duet), "No One is Alone" (Cinderella's consolation number), and "On the Steps of the Palace" (Cinderella's deliberation about leaving her slipper). The Baker and Baker's Wife are central characters, with Joanna Gleason's Tony-winning origination of the Baker's Wife being a frequent clue. The Witch's four-item curse (cow as white as milk, cape as red as blood, hair as yellow as corn, slipper as pure as gold) appears regularly.

Harder clues focus on musical motifs — particularly the descending fifth motif (A-D-E-C#-B) and the "short D, long E" motif representing "I wish." The narrator's sacrifice to the giant is a stage-specific detail that appears at higher difficulties. The magic beans have been the subject of their own standalone tossup. Cinderella is the character most frequently asked about in connection with this show, particularly her opening/closing "I wish" and her Act II decision to leave the Prince.

In mention-based clues, Into the Woods most often appears as a Sondheim identifier, typically listed with Sweeney Todd. Bernadette Peters and the 2014 film adaptation (with Anna Kendrick as Cinderella) also generate mentions.
