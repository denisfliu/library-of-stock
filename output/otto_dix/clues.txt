[B1] 2022 C++ (2022) | Diff: 7 | Fine Arts
     Leadin: Practitioners of an art style that developed during this political period were often divided into "verists" and "classicists." For 10 points each:
  Part 1 (ans: Weimar Republic [or German Reich; accept Weimar period; accept German Republic; prompt on Germany]):
    Name this period, during which the New Objectivity movement developed. In the period following it, many of its artworks were classified as "degenerate art" by its political successors.
  Part 2 (ans: expressionism):
    The New Objectivity movement formed as a reaction to this earlier artistic style. This movement's focus on exaggerated emotions is exemplified by Edvard Munch's depiction of a man clutching his face on a bridge in The Scream.
  Part 3 (ans: Metropolis [or Großstadt] (by Otto Dix)):
    This New Objectivity painting depicts crippled veterans of World War I on its left and right being ignored by prostitutes wearing fur. The central panel of this painting shows Alfred Schulze playing saxophone in a band at a nightclub.

     Leadin: In a portrait by this artist, a female journalist with bobbed hair, a red-and-black checkered dress, and a monocle smokes while crossing her legs at a cafe table. For 10 points each:
  Part 1 (ans: Otto Dix):
    Name this artist of that unflattering Portrait of Sylvia von Harden, whose other portrait subjects include the nude dancer Anita Berber, the dermatologist Hans Koch, and the painter Adolf Uzarski.

    Otto Dix is also known for this macabre series of woodcuts showing subjects like stormtroopers advancing under a gas attack and a skull infested with maggots.
