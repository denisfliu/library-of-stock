    This Abbasid caliph established the House of Wisdom and allegedly sent a water clock and an elephant to Charlemagne's court.
  Part 3 (ans: Barmakids):
    Harun also destroyed the power of this family after one of its members impregnated his sister Abassa. This family patronized scholars such as Geber and was influential during the early years of the caliphate.

[B18] 2012 Penn Bowl (2012) | Diff: 8 | History
     Leadin: This ruler sent Charlemagne a series of gifts, including an elephant named Abbul-Abbas, a water clock, perfume, and ivory chessmen. For 10 points each:
  Part 1 (ans: Harun Al-Rashid):
    Identify this caliph who was advised by the Barmakid family. Among his more famous actions was creating the House of Wisdom in Baghdad.
  Part 2 (ans: Abbasid caliphate):
    Harun Al-Rashid was the fifth caliph of this empire, which succeeded the Umayyad caliphate after winning the Battle of the Zab River. It lost a great deal of power after Hulagu Khan's sack of Baghdad.
  Part 3 (ans: Muhammed ibn Musa al-Khwarizmi or Algoritmi):
    Among the members of the House of Wisdom is this early mathematician, who is probably most famous for deriving the quadratic formula and basically inventing algebra.

[B19] 2012 ACF Regionals (2012) | Diff: 8 | History
     Leadin: Harun al-Rashid ruled this dynasty that came to power after defeating the Umayyads. For 10 points each:
  Part 1 (ans: Abbasid Caliphate or Dynasty):
    Name this Islamic dynasty that ruled from 750 to 1258. Avicenna lived and worked during this dynasty.
  Part 2 (ans: Baghdad):
    The Abbasid Caliphate ended when Hulagu Khan sacked this city in 1258. The sacking allegedly caused this city's rivers to run black with ink because of all the great scholarship that occurred here.
  Part 3 (ans: Sultanate of Rum [prompt on "Seljuk Turks"]):
    Like the Abbasids, this entity in Asia Minor was subjugated by the Mongols, becoming a vassal state in 1243 and finally collapsing in 1308. Its victories over the Byzantines included the capture of Antioch, which was soon lost to Crusaders, and the Battle of Myriocephalum.
