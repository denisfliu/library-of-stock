Tossups

T1 2025 Chicago Open (2025) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
An artist with this first name showed a nude woman stepping out from a wolf in the sculpture Rapture, one of her many artworks using woman and wolf motifs. A model with this first name appears in Nude on Tapestry by Sanyu and several portraits by Moïse Kisling. A wax sculptor with this first name, who painted internal organs and put bodily fluids in water jugs during the AIDS crisis, has the last name (*) Smith. A bronze sculpture by Pablo Gargallo depicts the trademark garçon bob of a woman who used this first name, which she also wears in an erotic photo series by Julien Mandel. That woman with this first name bares her nude back with f-holes painted on it in Man Ray’s photo Le Violon d’Ingres. For 10 points, what first name was used by the surrealist model Alice Prin, the “Queen of Montparnasse?”
ANSWER: Kiki [accept Kiki de Montparnasse or Kiki from Montparnasse; accept Kiki Smith; prompt on Alice Prin by asking “what first name did she use as a model?”]
T2 2016 ACF Nationals (2016) Diff 9 · Fine Arts
An artist with this surname created a row of twelve glass water-cooler bottles with labels such as “blood” and “pus,” and has used beeswax to create many life-sized sculptures of naked humans excreting various body fluids. An artist with this surname used found tools and scraps he dubbed “chopped clouds” to create twenty-seven pieces while working in an abandoned factory in Voltri, Italy. The best-known of the two-dimensional “drawings in space” created by that artist with this surname is the Hudson River Landscape. A sculptor with this surname created a 6-by-6-by-6 foot object entitled Die, one of his pioneering minimalist sculptures. Another sculptor with this surname created series such as Tanktotem and Agricola, as well as several sculptures consisting of various combinations of highly polished, stainless steel hexahedrons. For 10 points, give this surname of artists Kiki, Tony, and David, the creator of the Cubi series.
ANSWER: Smith [accept Kiki Smith, Tony Smith, or David Smith]

B1 2024 PACE NSC (2024) Diff 5 · Fine Arts
A German artist with this first name depicted a woman stepping out of the stomach of a wolf in her sculpture Rapture. For 10 points each:
[10] Give this first name, which appeared with “de Montparnasse” in the stage name of a model who had violin holes painted on her back for photographs by Man Ray.
ANSWER: Kiki [accept Kiki Smith or Kiki de Montparnasse; prompt on Alice or Alice Prin with “what was her stage name?”] (The photograph is Le Violon d’Ingres.)
[10] Kiki Smith created a silicon bronze sculpture of this woman with flayed skin and dragging a chain. Donatello created a realist sculpture of this “penitent” woman made of wood.

T1 2020 Longhorn Invitational Tournament (2020) Diff 7 · Fine Arts
A 2017 MOMA exhibition displayed a series of geometric works in this medium by Brazilian artist Hercules Barsotti. This substance was used to depict a nude woman lying with a wolf in a Kiki Smith work. It’s not fabric, but recyclable metal is sewn together with copper wire in a work titled for a “splash” of this substance by the Ghanaian sculptor, El Anatsui. In 2012, this substance was used to (*) vandalize one of Mark Rothko’s nine Seagram Murals at the Tate Modern. Sumi-e “wash painting” is a technique using this substance, which Singaporean artist Chen Chong Swee is best-known for adapting to landscape painting. It's not water, but Wang Xizhi was known for his mastery of the “running” type of an art form using this substance. For 10 points, name this substance used to write calligraphy.
ANSWER: ink [accept pen or ink on paper/parchment; prompt on “calligraphy” or “drawing/sketch” with “created using what substance?”; do not accept or prompt on pencil or graphite/lead]
