Summary of Facts

Kiki Smith is a German-born American sculptor and printmaker whose work centers on the human body, bodily fluids, and feminist themes. In quiz bowl, she appears most often through her sculpture Rapture (2001), which depicts a nude woman stepping out from the stomach of a wolf — a signature motif of wolves and women that recurs throughout her oeuvre. Her AIDS-crisis-era works are also frequently clued: a row of twelve glass water-cooler bottles labeled with bodily fluids like 'blood' and 'pus,' as well as life-sized beeswax sculptures of naked humans excreting body fluids. She painted internal organs and put bodily fluids in water jugs during this period. Her silicon bronze Mary Magdalene, shown with flayed skin dragging a chain, is compared alongside Donatello's wooden Penitent Magdalene. Smith is often identified as German and as a wax sculptor, and her surname connects her to Tony Smith (minimalist sculptor of Die) and David Smith (creator of the Cubi series). She also created an ink work of a nude woman lying with a wolf. Power marks tend to be her specific works (Rapture, water-cooler bottles), while giveaways reference the Smith surname shared with Tony and David.
