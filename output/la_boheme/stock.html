Opera by Giacomo Puccini, premiered in 1896 and conducted at its premiere by Arturo Toscanini. Set in the Latin Quarter of Paris, it follows a group of bohemian artists — especially the poet Rodolfo and his love Mimi, a seamstress who dies of tuberculosis. Also features the flirtatious Musetta and her lover Marcello. The Metropolitan Opera's most-performed opera. Basis for Jonathan Larson's musical Rent.

Summary of Facts

La Bohème is a verismo opera by Giacomo Puccini, premiered February 1, 1896, with Arturo Toscanini conducting. It is based on Henri Murger's novel Scènes de la vie de bohème, and is the Metropolitan Opera's most-performed opera. Jonathan Larson's musical Rent is based on it, with the female lead of Rent also named Mimi and 'Musetta's Waltz' specifically referenced in the musical.

The opera is set in the Latin Quarter of Paris and follows a group of impoverished bohemian artists: the poet Rodolfo, the painter Marcello, the philosopher Colline, and the musician Schaunard. In Act I, set in their attic garret, Rodolfo burns his play for warmth and Schaunard arrives having been paid by an Englishman to play violin until his parrot died. The landlord Benoit arrives to collect rent on Christmas Eve. Mimi, a seamstress from downstairs, knocks at the door — her candle has gone out. Rodolfo hides her key while relighting her candle, and sings 'Che gelida manina' (Your Tiny Hand is Frozen). Mimi introduces herself in 'Mi chiamano Mimì.' The act closes with the love duet 'O soave fanciulla,' opened by three descending chords; Rodolfo, seeing Mimi's face bathed in moonlight, sings a unison perdendosi high C at the duet's close.

At the Café Momus in Act II, the group meets Musetta, who arrives with her rich admirer Alcindoro. Musetta sings her waltz 'Quando me'n vo'' in 3/4 time, boasting that people stare at her beauty in the street, intending to make Marcello jealous. She pretends her shoe hurts to send Alcindoro away. When a military procession passes, the group slips out without paying, leaving Alcindoro with the bill.

In Act III, Mimi and Rodolfo part; Mimi receives a pink bonnet and laments the separation in 'Donde lieta uscì.' In Act IV, Mimi returns dying of tuberculosis. Colline sings 'Vecchia zimarra' while pawning his beloved overcoat (originally bought with Schaunard's horn money and a bonnet) to buy medicine. Mimi dies and Rodolfo is overcome with grief.

Puccini and librettist Giuseppe Giacosa (who resigned three times) were accused of stealing the libretto by Ruggero Leoncavallo, who wrote a competing La Bohème rewritten to focus on Marcello and Musetta. Puccini reportedly replied, 'Let him compose, I will compose. The audience will decide.'
