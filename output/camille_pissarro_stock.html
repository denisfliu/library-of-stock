Summary of Facts

Camille Pissarro (1830-1903) was a Danish-born (St. Thomas, Danish West Indies) Impressionist painter, the oldest Impressionist and the only artist to exhibit at all eight Impressionist exhibitions. He is often called the 'father' of the Impressionists and Neo-Impressionists, and Cezanne referred to him as 'le bon dieu' after they lived together in the countryside. He studied under Camille Corot and Anton Melbye.

His most frequently clued work is the Boulevard Montmartre series, comprising 14 paintings showing the boulevard from above at different times and seasons, including day and night versions and a Mardi Gras parade scene. He painted this series after a similar series of the Rue St. Lazare. Van Gogh referred to the streets painted by Pissarro, Degas, and Monet as 'grand boulevards,' in contrast to the 'petit boulevards' painted by himself and Toulouse-Lautrec.

He painted many landscapes of Pontoise and Louveciennes, including Jalais Hill (two women walking around a bend). In 1896 he created a series of Rouen cityscapes featuring smokestacks and an iron bridge, including Morning, An Overcast Day showing steam ships in the harbor. He also painted Rouen Cathedral, the same subject as Monet's famous series.

His pointillist works include Apple Harvest at Eragny, one of four pointillist paintings displayed separately from his other work at the Eighth Impressionist Exhibition. He later reverted from Neo-Impressionism to Impressionism. He was a crucial mentor to Cezanne, whose House of the Hanged-Man was inspired by Pissarro, and he urged Van Gogh to live with Dr. Gachet after his release from the asylum at Saint-Remy.

Pissarro created Social Turpitudes (Turpitudes sociales), a series of 28 drawings with anti-Semitic content, and circulated political drawings including one where an old man watches the word 'anarchy' rise with the sun. A self-portrait shows his infected eye drooping, painted while hiding in a hotel room from anti-Dreyfusard rioters. He painted two peasants and a hay cart directly onto a wooden palette. Mary Cassatt introduced the American artist Lucy Bacon to him. His paintings were sold by art dealer Theo van Gogh. Derek Walcott's Tiepolo's Hound draws parallels between Walcott and Pissarro, including a narrative of Pissarro's move to Paris, illustrated with 26 of Walcott's own paintings. While in England he painted views of Sydenham. His son Lucien befriended Walter Sickert and helped found the Camden Town Group.
