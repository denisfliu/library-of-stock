Summary of Facts

Black Mountain College was an experimental liberal arts college near Asheville, North Carolina, founded in 1933 and existing for 23 years. John Andrew Rice was its first rector. It was known for integrating art into everyday life and interdisciplinary studies.

Josef and Anni Albers came to the college after the Nazis shut down the Bauhaus, with Philip Johnson offering Josef the head of painting position. Josef taught a Werklehre class emphasizing 'fingertip touch for materials' with paper exercises (Tom Wolfe mockingly described these). He created the Homage to the Square series (1949-1976), consisting of three or four nested quadrangles painted with a palette knife on Masonite, with entries including Ascending, Joy, Glow, Precinct, and Skyward, inspired by trips to Mexico to study pre-Columbian art. He wrote Interaction of Color, a textbook on color theory discussing the Bezout Effect and distinguishing 'factual fact' from 'actual fact.' At Yale he taught Eva Hesse. His Graphic Tectonics portfolio is included in Formulation: Articulation. Anni forbade Josef from selling paintings using red. Walter Gropius had him create a mural called Manhattan for the Pan Am Building lobby. Anni Albers was the first textile designer with a solo MoMA exhibition (1949), wrote On Designing and On Weaving, established the weaving workshop, and created Six Prayers to memorialize Holocaust victims.

Robert Rauschenberg was a student known for combines. His White Paintings were installed on the ceiling for John Cage's Theater Piece No. 1, and he sat onstage painting during performances of Merce Cunningham's dance Story. Susan Weil introduced Rauschenberg to photograms at the college. John Cage was on faculty and created Theater Piece No. 1 (a happening). He also rediscovered Erik Satie's Vexations at BMC and organized its 840-repetition premiere with David Tudor. Merce Cunningham founded his dance company at BMC in 1953 and premiered the role of the Preacher in Appalachian Spring. Willem de Kooning taught there and painted Asheville and Excavation, ending his 'black-and-white abstractions' period. Cy Twombly attended and later said it was impossible not to love Rilke after BMC. Ruth Asawa created looped-wire hangings after watching basket makers in Toluca (15 sculptures at the de Young Museum). William Carlos Williams was a noteworthy lecturer. M.C. Richards translated Satie's The Ruse of Medusa. The college also attracted Walter Gropius.

The Black Mountain poets were a significant literary movement centered at the college. Charles Olson pioneered 'projective verse' and 'composition by field' in his 1950 essay, and also wrote Call Me Ishmael and The Maximus Poems. Robert Creeley taught there and wrote 'I Know a Man.' Denise Levertov, Robert Duncan, and Ed Dorn were also associated. They were sometimes called 'projectivists' and influenced the British Poetry Revival.
