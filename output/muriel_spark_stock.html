The Prime of Miss Jean Brodie

Spark's best-known novel, set at the Marcia Blaine School for Girls. The title schoolteacher selects six bright girls as her chosen 'set,' calling them the 'creme de la creme.' Sandy Stranger has an affair with the art teacher Teddy Lloyd. Sandy becomes a nun (Sister Helena) after helping Miss Mackay get Brodie fired for having fascist sympathies. Sandy writes Transfiguration of the Commonplace. Joyce Emily Hammond is inspired by Brodie to fight in the Spanish Civil War and dies. Miss Brodie is accused of 'thinking she is the God of Calvin.'

The Girls of Slender Means

Set at the May of Teck Club. The women share a Schiaparelli dress as a good-luck token. Nicholas Farringdon becomes a missionary and is executed in Haiti after being inspired by another character's piety. Selina rushes back into a crumbling house to retrieve a dress instead of helping the elocution instructor Joanna Childe, who dies due to an unexploded bomb. The title refers to both the economic and physical stature of the characters. Jane Wright investigates Farringdon's death.

The Comforters and Other Works

Spark suffered a breakdown early in her career in which she believed T.S. Eliot's play The Confidential Clerk contained secret messages written to her in Greek. This, along with her conversion to Catholicism and abuse of Dexedrine, inspired The Comforters, in which Caroline Rose is haunted by a 'Typing Ghost' who tells her she is a fictional character. The Ballad of Peckham Rye features Dougal Douglas, who is associated with the devil due to lumps from surgically removed horns, and opens with Humphrey Place leaving his fiancee at the altar. The Mandelbaum Gate follows Barbara Vaughan traveling through an Israeli checkpoint to marry an archaeologist in Jerusalem. Anthony Burgess included The Mandelbaum Gate among his favorite English novels. 'The Seraph and the Zambesi' is one of her short stories. Aiding and Abetting features two characters who believe they are Lord Lucan. She was Scottish.

Summary of Facts

Muriel Spark was a Scottish author whose conversion to Catholicism and early mental breakdown profoundly influenced her fiction. She believed T.S. Eliot's play The Confidential Clerk contained secret messages to her in Greek, an experience that, combined with her Dexedrine abuse, inspired her first novel The Comforters, in which Caroline Rose is haunted by a 'Typing Ghost.'

Her most famous work is The Prime of Miss Jean Brodie, set at the Marcia Blaine School for Girls, where the charismatic title teacher selects six girls as her 'set' and calls them the 'creme de la creme.' Sandy Stranger has an affair with art teacher Teddy Lloyd, later becomes the nun Sister Helena (writing Transfiguration of the Commonplace), and helps Miss Mackay get Brodie fired for fascist sympathies. Joyce Emily Hammond is inspired by Brodie to fight and die in the Spanish Civil War.

Memento Mori features elderly characters receiving mysterious phone calls saying 'Remember you must die,' with Charmian forgetting her maid Jean Taylor lives in a nursing home. The Girls of Slender Means is set at the May of Teck Club, where women share a Schiaparelli dress. Joanna Childe dies from an unexploded bomb, Nicholas Farringdon is later executed as a missionary in Haiti, and Selina prioritizes retrieving a dress over helping Joanna.

The Ballad of Peckham Rye features Dougal Douglas, associated with the devil due to lumps from surgically removed horns. The Mandelbaum Gate follows Barbara Vaughan traveling through an Israeli checkpoint. Aiding and Abetting features two characters who believe they are Lord Lucan.
