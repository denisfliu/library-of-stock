[10] This man’s autobiography describes his visit to the Paris EXPO of 1900 and his bewilderment at the advances of technology in a chapter titled “The Dynamo and the Virgin.” He also lamented how education often results accumulated ignorance in “the form of inert facts.”
ANSWER: Henry Adams (The Education of Henry Adams is the book)
[10] Something Like An Autobiography is a book by this film director. One chapter relates the struggle of his crew to get their equipment to the summit of Mt. Fuji, only for the light to fail, requiring them to take it all back down again and come back the next day.
ANSWER: Akira Kurosawa (accept names in either order)
[10] This author chronicled the time he spent in hiding after the publication of his novel Satanic Verses in his autobiography Joseph Anton: A Memoir.
ANSWER: Salman Rushdie
