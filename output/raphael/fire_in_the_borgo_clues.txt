[B1] 2024 Chicago Open (2024) | Diff: 10 | Fine Arts
     Leadin: In this painting, an old man wearing a Santa-hat-like cap is carried to safety in a scene inspired by Aeneas bearing Anchises away from Troy. For 10 points each:
  Part 1 (ans: The Fire in the Borgo [or L'Incendio di Borgo]):
    Name this fresco depicting an episode from the Liber Pontificalis centering on Pope Leo IV, who is seen on a balcony in the background.
  Part 2 (ans: Giulio Romano [or Giulio Romano or Giulio Pippi]):
    Sydney Freedberg attributed The Fire in the Borgo to this student of Raphael. The Fall of the Giants is among the Mannerist frescoes that this artist created for the Palazzo del Te in Mantua.
  Part 3 (ans: Titian [or Tiziano Vecellio or Tiziano Vecellio; or Titianus]):
    Giulio designed the Palazzo del Te for Federico II Gonzaga, who was also a patron of this artist of Sacred and Profane Love.

[B2] 2016 Chicago Open (2016) | Diff: 10 | Fine Arts
     Leadin: This painting, finally completed during the reign of Pope Clement VII, contains a man in a yellow sash grabbing a column with a sign reading "IAM TANDEM CHRISTUM LIBERE PRO FITERI LICET". For 10 points each:
  Part 1 (ans: The Donation of Constantine [or The Donation of Rome]):
    Identify this painting, completed by Giulio Romano and Gianfrancesco Penni. This painting is located in a room which also depicts its title figure's baptism and vision.
  Part 2 (ans: Raphael [or Raffaello Sanzio da Urbino]):
    The Donation of Constantine and The Donation of Rome were among the paintings finished by the assistants of this Renaissance master, who also painted The Parnassus for the Stanza della Segnatura.
  Part 3 (ans: The Fire in the Borgo):
    This painting, mostly the work of Romano, lends its name to another of the Raphael Rooms. A nude man carries another nude on his back away from the title conflagration on the left of this canvas.
