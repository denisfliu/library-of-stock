[T1] 2023 IQBT Undergraduate Championship (2023) | Diff: 8 | Fine Arts
     Answer: Sistine Madonna [or Madonna di San Sisto]
  - A Hubert Grimme study about the "Riddle" of this painting claims that the molding at the bottom depicts the lid of a coffin.
  - Johann Joachim Winckelmann called this painting the best modern imitation of Greek "noble simplicity." In the background of this Dresden-based painting, dozens of blue cherub faces emerge from the clouds. [PWR]
  - A kneeling Saint Barbara looks downward, out of the frame, on the right of this painting. [PWR]
  - At the bottom of this painting, a (*) tiara topped with an acorn, an emblem of the Della Rovere family, lies on a wooden parapet.
  - Julius II  was the likely model for a pope depicted in this painting, at the bottom of which two bored, mischievous-looking cherubs are resting.
  - For 10 points, name this Raphael painting in which the title saint looks up towards the Virgin Mary. [GIVE]

[B1] 2024 Chicago Open (2024) | Diff: 10 | Fine Arts
     Leadin: Horst Bredekamp argued that an approach to art history named for these things was a "neglected tradition." For 10 points each:
  Part 1 (ans: images [or Bilder; accept Bildwissenschaft or Bild-Anthropologie]):
    Name these things. In his "anthropology" of these things, Hans Belting described how interaction with them takes place through a medium that acts as an artificial body.
  Part 2 (ans: iconology [or Ikonologie; reject "iconography"]):
    Belting frames his Bild-Anthropologie as a new approach to this style of art history. In a book titled for Studies in this field, Erwin Panofsky separated it into three levels and contrasted it with a similarly named field with the suffix -ography.
  Part 3 (ans: Sistine Madonna [or Madonna di San Sisto]):
    In Invisible Masterpiece, Belting described how Augustus III bringing this painting to Dresden caused it to dominate the German imagination. Two bored putti appear below the Virgin Mary in this Raphael painting, which is titled for a saint rather than a building in the Vatican.

[B2] 2022 Penn Bowl (2022) | Diff: 8 | Fine Arts
     Leadin: In 1754, this painting was moved from Piacenza ("p'yah-CHENT-zah") to Dresden after it was bought by King Augustus III for a massive twelve thousand zecchini ("t'zeh-KEE-nee"), upon which he moved his throne so he could view it from a better position. For 10 points each:
  Part 1 (ans: Sistine Madonna [or Madonna di San Sisto]):
    Name this painting, whose bottom center features a pair of bored-looking putti resting on their elbows. Vibrant green curtains frame its central figures, who stand on clouds while flanked by St. Barbara and a golden-robed pope.
  Part 2 (ans: Raphael [or Raffaello Sanzio da Urbino]):
    The Sistine Madonna was painted by this Renaissance artist. The Stanza della Segnatura is home to his frescos The Parnassus and The School of Athens.
  Part 3 (ans: goldfinch [or cardellino; or puttertje; accept Madonna of the Goldfinch or The Goldfinch; prompt on finch]):
    In another Madonna by Raphael, infant versions of Christ and John the Baptist hold one of these animals in cupped hands. A thin chain ties one of these birds to his feeding container in a simple painting by Carel Fabritius.
