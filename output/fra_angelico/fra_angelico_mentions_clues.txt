  Part 3 (ans: Jan van Eyck):
    A reversed image of the portrait's subjects can be seen in the convex mirror at the rear of this artist's Arnolfini Marriage.

[BM16] 2013 Michigan Fall Tournament (2013) | Diff: 7 | Fine Arts
     Leadin: For 10 points each, name some religious subjects that appear frequently in artwork.
  Part 1 (ans: Jesus Christ [accept either name]):
    This dude is the child in various depictions of the Madonna and Child. He is seen in the house of his parents in a John Everett Millais painting, and he can be seen in his infancy in several takes on the Flight into Egypt.
  Part 2 (ans: the Annunciation):
    The central panel of the Merode Altarpiece shows this scene, usually the first scene in series of the life of Christ. Versions by Fra Angelico and Jan Van Eyck both feature Gabriel on the right and the Virgin Mary on the left.
  Part 3 (ans: Maesta):
    This term refers to the scene of an enthroned Madonna. Notable examples include one with St. Francis in Assisi by Cimabue, and one built as the altarpiece for the Siena Cathedral by Duccio.
