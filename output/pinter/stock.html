The Dumb Waiter

Two hitmen, Ben and Gus, wait in a basement for their assignment. They argue about the phrase 'light the kettle' vs. 'put on the kettle.' A speaking tube (the dumb waiter) delivers mysterious restaurant orders like 'Ormitha Macarounada.' Gus reads news about a child killing a cat and a man run over by a lorry. Wilson sends orders through the device. At the end, Ben is hired to kill his partner Gus, who returns stripped of clothing and his gun.

General / Biographical

British playwright. 'Comedies of menace' — term coined by Irving Wardle. Nobel Prize lecture 'Art, Truth & Politics' (2005) denounced U.S. invasion of Iraq. Visited imprisoned authors in Turkey with Arthur Miller. Close friend of Sarah Kane (said 'she was a poet' at her funeral). Directed a revival of Coward's Blithe Spirit. Adapted Proust's In Search of Lost Time (unproduced). Screenwriter — collaborated with Joseph Losey on The Servant; adapted The Comfort of Strangers. Also wrote Old Times, A Kind of Alaska, No Man's Land, One for the Road, Ashes to Ashes, and the sketch Applicant.

Summary of Facts

Harold Pinter (1930-2008) was a British playwright, screenwriter, and Nobel laureate known for his 'comedies of menace,' a term coined by Irving Wardle. His plays combine absurdist humor with an atmosphere of undefined threat.

The Dumb Waiter is his most frequently clued play, featuring the hitmen Ben and Gus who wait in a basement, argue about 'light the kettle' vs. 'put on the kettle,' and receive mysterious restaurant orders through the title device. At the end, Ben is hired to kill Gus, who returns stripped of clothing and his gun.

The Birthday Party features Goldberg and McCann terrorizing Stanley at Meg's boarding house. Meg gives Stanley a toy drum, and the play climaxes with a blind man's bluff game. Betrayal unfolds in reverse chronological order, revealing Jerry's affair with his friend Robert's wife Emma. The Homecoming sees philosopher Teddy bring his wife Ruth home, where she ultimately stays to work as a prostitute while Teddy returns to America. The Caretaker features Mick smashing a Buddha statue while arguing with the homeless Davies.

Pinter's Nobel lecture 'Art, Truth & Politics' denounced the Iraq invasion. He was a close friend of Sarah Kane and collaborated with Joseph Losey on screenplays.
