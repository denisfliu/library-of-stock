Summary of Facts

Miguel de Unamuno (1864-1936) was a Spanish author and philosopher, a leading member of the Generation of '98. He coined the nonsense term 'nivola' to distinguish his novels from conventional realism.

Mist (Niebla) is a metafictional nivola in which Augusto Pérez, jilted by Eugenia, confronts Unamuno himself, who tells him he cannot commit suicide because he is a fictional character. Augusto dies mysteriously, and his dog Orfeo delivers the funeral oration. The novel includes a prologue by the fictional Victor Goti and a disagreeing post-prologue by Unamuno.

Abel Sanchez retells Cain and Abel: the doctor Joaquín Monegro envies the painter Abel Sanchez throughout their lives. Joaquín kills Abel by grabbing his throat, and on his deathbed declares his wife Antonia could not cure his envy because he didn't love her.

San Manuel Bueno, Martír is set in Valverde de Lucerna on a lake said to hide a submerged city. Lazarus reveals to his sister Angela that the beloved priest Don Manuel secretly doesn't believe in God. The Tragic Sense of Life opens with 'the man of flesh and bone' and reformulates Descartes's cogito as 'sum ergo cogito.'
