American playwright (1915–2005) known for Death of a Salesman, The Crucible, A View from the Bridge, All My Sons, and After the Fall. His plays explore themes of moral responsibility, the American Dream, McCarthyism, and the immigrant experience. He was investigated by HUAC after All My Sons, which he later channeled into The Crucible. His marriage to Marilyn Monroe (for whom he wrote The Misfits) inspired After the Fall.
Death of a Salesman

A 1949 play about the decline of the traveling salesman Willy Loman, who kills himself in a car crash so his son Biff can use the life insurance money. Biff was a former high school football star who dropped out after discovering his father's infidelity with a mistress (called 'the Woman') in a Boston hotel room. Biff steals Bill Oliver's fountain pen after Oliver fails to remember him. Willy's boss Howard Wagner fires him after Willy asks to be taken off the road; Willy exclaims 'You can't eat the orange and throw the peel away - a man is not a piece of fruit!' Willy ignores the studious Bernard in favor of Biff playing football; Bernard later argues before the Supreme Court. Ben (Willy's brother) went into the jungle at 17, came out rich at 21 ('by God I was rich'). Willy idolizes Dave Singleman, who died in his green velvet slippers on a train. A recurring flute motif evokes Willy's flute-maker father. Willy plants seeds at night while imagining Ben. A rubber pipe on the gas heater reveals Willy's suicidal intent. Linda sobs 'we're free, we're free' at the end in the 'Requiem.' Mielziner's set used semi-transparent walls; Miller originally envisioned setting it inside a giant skull. The play was performed by Emad and Rana in Asghar Farhadi's Oscar-winning film. Elia Kazan directed the original 1949 production. Miller's essay 'Tragedy and the Common Man' defends the play's form. Charley tells Willy a man can be worth 'more dead than alive.' Biff burns his 'University of Virginia' sneakers.

A View from the Bridge

Set in Red Hook, Brooklyn, and narrated by the Italian lawyer Alfieri. The longshoreman Eddie Carbone takes in his wife Beatrice's Italian cousins Marco and Rodolpho. Eddie becomes obsessively protective of his niece Catherine and objects to her relationship with Rodolpho. The first act ends when Marco lifts a chair over Eddie's head with one hand after Eddie hits Rodolpho in a mock boxing match. Eddie reports Marco and Rodolpho to immigration services, and Marco kills Eddie in a knife fight. Eddie suggests oranges are painted orange because they grow green on the tree. Beatrice tells Eddie he can 'never have' Catherine. Alfieri advises characters to 'settle for half.' The Brooklyn Bridge dominates the backdrop of the set design.

The Crucible

A 1953 play about the 1692 Salem Witch Trials, widely read as an allegory for McCarthyism. John Proctor, described in a narration as 'a farmer in his middle thirties,' is hanged after declaring 'I have given you my soul! Leave me my name.' Proctor forgets 'Thou shalt not commit adultery' when asked to recite the Ten Commandments, alluding to his affair with Abigail Williams. Abigail tells a character 'I can make you wish you had never seen the sun go down.' Betty Parris falls into a catatonic state after her father Reverend Parris catches her dancing in the forest with Abigail and Tituba. Elizabeth Proctor is found with a 'poppet' stuck with a needle (sewn by Mary Warren) as evidence of witchcraft. Proctor declares 'God is Dead' to a group of girls accusing him of harboring the devil. A pregnant woman is reprieved from execution. George Burroughs was imprisoned for debt by Thomas Putnam's brother-in-law. The narrations provide historical context and note 'We cannot look to superstition in this' and describe a group with 'a predilection for minding other people's business.' Miller called for artificial large trees to create a gloomy atmosphere. Ivo van Hove's 2016 Broadway revival featured a Philip Glass score, with Saoirse Ronan as Abigail and Sophie Okonedo as Elizabeth Proctor. In the trial scene, girls repeat another character's lines after their leader sees an invisible yellow bird in the rafters.

All My Sons

A 1947 play about Joe Keller, who blames his partner Steve Deever for shipping faulty cylinder heads that caused the deaths of twenty-one pilots during World War II. Joe claimed he had pneumonia the day the factory produced the parts. Joe's son Larry dies in the war. The play opens with the destruction of a tree planted in Larry's memory. Kate uses an apple tree struck by lightning as proof that Larry is still alive. A letter Larry sent before dying reveals he suspected his father knew about the cylinder heads and purposely crashed his plane. Anne Deever gives Kate the letter revealing Larry's suicide. Joe shoots himself at the end. Jim Bayliss is the neighbor who gave up medical research to earn money as a doctor. Frank Lubey consults a horoscope and determines that November 25th was a 'favorable day' for Larry. The neighbor boy Bert annoys the family by trying to see the jail in the basement.

After the Fall

A play that takes place in the mind of Quentin, who interacts with his former wives. The set consists of a single chair in front of the stone tower of a German concentration camp. Quentin visits a concentration camp with a German archaeologist. Lou commits suicide after Mickey 'names names.' Maggie, a fictionalization of Marilyn Monroe, is portrayed as neurotic and suicidal. The play was inspired by Miller's failed marriage to Monroe, for whom he also wrote the screenplay for The Misfits.

Summary of Facts

Arthur Miller (1915–2005) was one of America's most important playwrights, known for exploring moral responsibility, the American Dream, McCarthyism, and the immigrant experience. He was investigated by HUAC (House Un-American Activities Committee) after All My Sons, an experience he channeled into The Crucible. His marriage to Marilyn Monroe, for whom he wrote the screenplay The Misfits, inspired After the Fall.

Death of a Salesman (1949) depicts the decline of the traveling salesman Willy Loman, who kills himself in a deliberate car crash so his son Biff can use the life insurance money. Biff was a former high school football star who dropped out after discovering his father's infidelity with 'the Woman' in a Boston hotel room. Biff steals Bill Oliver's fountain pen when Oliver fails to recognize him. Willy exclaims 'You can't eat the orange and throw the peel away - a man is not a piece of fruit!' when his boss Howard fires him (after playing a recording of his son reciting the state capitals). A recurring flute motif invokes Willy's flute-maker father. Willy plants seeds at night while hallucinating his brother Ben, who walked into the jungle at seventeen and came out rich at twenty-one ('by God I was rich'). A rubber pipe on the gas heater reveals Willy's suicidal intent. Linda sobs 'we're free, we're free' at Willy's poorly-attended funeral in the section titled 'Requiem,' where she reveals she made the last payment on the house that day. Willy idolizes Dave Singleman, who died peacefully in his green velvet slippers on a train. Biff burns his University of Virginia sneakers after the Boston discovery. Jo Mielziner's set design used semi-transparent walls; Miller originally planned to set the play inside a giant skull. Elia Kazan directed the original 1949 production. The play was performed by Emad and Rana in Asghar Farhadi's Oscar-winning Iranian film. Miller defended the play in his essay 'Tragedy and the Common Man.'

The Crucible (1953) dramatizes the 1692 Salem Witch Trials as an allegory for McCarthyism. John Proctor, described in a narration as 'a farmer in his middle thirties,' is hanged after declaring 'I have given you my soul! Leave me my name.' Proctor forgets 'Thou shalt not commit adultery' when asked to recite the Ten Commandments, alluding to his affair with Abigail Williams. Abigail threatens a character with 'I can make you wish you had never seen the sun go down.' Betty Parris falls into a catatonic state after her father Reverend Parris catches her dancing with Abigail and the Barbadian slave Tituba. Elizabeth Proctor is found with a 'poppet' stuck with a needle as evidence of witchcraft. At the trial, girls begin to repeat the lines of the accused after their leader sees an invisible yellow bird in the rafters. Proctor declares 'God is Dead.' The play's narrations provide historical context, noting 'We cannot look to superstition in this' and describing a group with 'a predilection for minding other people's business.' Miller called for artificial large trees to create a gloomy atmosphere. Ivo van Hove's 2016 Broadway revival featured a continuous Philip Glass score, Saoirse Ronan as Abigail, and Sophie Okonedo as Elizabeth Proctor.

A View from the Bridge is set in Red Hook, Brooklyn, and narrated by the Italian lawyer Alfieri. The longshoreman Eddie Carbone takes in his wife Beatrice's Italian cousins Marco and Rodolpho. Eddie becomes obsessively protective of his niece Catherine and objects to her relationship with Rodolpho. The first act ends when Marco lifts a chair over Eddie's head with one hand after Eddie hits Rodolpho in a mock boxing match. Eddie reports Marco and Rodolpho to immigration services, and Marco kills Eddie in a knife fight. Beatrice tells Eddie he can 'never have' Catherine. Alfieri advises characters to 'settle for half.' Eddie suggests oranges are painted orange because they grow green on the tree.

All My Sons (1947) follows Joe Keller, who shipped faulty airplane cylinder heads that caused the deaths of twenty-one pilots, blaming his partner Steve Deever. Joe claimed he had pneumonia that day. The play opens with a tree planted in memory of Joe's missing son Larry being destroyed. Kate uses an apple tree struck by lightning as proof that Larry is still alive. A letter Larry sent reveals he suspected his father and purposely crashed his plane; Anne Deever delivers this letter to Kate. Frank Lubey's horoscope says November 25th was a 'favorable day' for Larry. Jim Bayliss gave up medical research to earn money as a doctor. Joe shoots himself at the end.

After the Fall takes place in Quentin's mind, with a minimalist set of a single chair before a Nazi concentration camp tower. The character Maggie is a thinly veiled fictionalization of Marilyn Monroe; Lou commits suicide after Mickey 'names names.' Quentin visits a concentration camp with a German archaeologist. Incident at Vichy (set in Nazi-occupied France) features the psychoanalyst Leduc, who is given a white pass to escape. Broken Glass features a character paralyzed from the waist down after reading about Kristallnacht. The Price features Victor and Walter Franz debating over their dead father's furniture. The Man Who Had All the Luck features David saving sick mink. Resurrection Blues features a dictator forcing Skip L. Cheeseboro to produce a TV special about a crucifixion.
