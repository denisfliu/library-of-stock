Summary of Facts

Gotthold Ephraim Lessing (1729-1781) was a German Enlightenment playwright, critic, and aesthetician who pioneered bourgeois tragedy in Germany.

Nathan the Wise is a plea for religious tolerance. The title Jewish merchant, based on Lessing's friend Moses Mendelssohn, tells the Ring Parable to Saladin: a father gives each of his three sons a ring without telling them which is the original, showing all three Abrahamic religions deserve respect. The Parable first appeared in Boccaccio's Decameron. A Christian Templar who saved Nathan's daughter Recha from a fire is revealed to be her sibling and a nephew of Saladin. Lessing wrote the play after his freedom from censorship was revoked by the theologian Johann Goeze.

Emilia Galotti is a bourgeois tragedy in which the chamberlain Marinelli schemes on behalf of Prince Hettore Gonzaga. Odoardo kills his daughter Emilia to preserve her from the prince. The play is found open in Werther's room after his suicide.

The essay Laocoön argues that painting can only depict a single moment while poetry depicts moments in succession, disagreeing with Winckelmann. Miss Sara Sampson was the first German bourgeois tragedy. Hamburg Dramaturgy coined the term 'dramaturgy.'
