Tossups

T1 2024 ACF Winter (2024) Diff 7 · Literature
The first section of W. G. Sebald’s novel Vertigo consists of a biography of this author and discusses this author’s treatise On Love, which describes the process of “crystallization.” This author’s autobiographical novel The Life of Henry Brulard documents his fear of falling off a horse, a fate which befalls one of this author’s characters who had previously worked at a seminary in Besançon (“buh-zahn-SAWN”). This author’s travels to Florence inspired the coining of his namesake “syndrome,” which occurs when encountering artworks of great beauty. In one of this author’s novels, a severed head is kissed before being buried in a marble cave. The shooting of Madame de Renal leads to the execution of Julien Sorel in that novel by this author. For 10 points, name this French author of the novel The Red and the Black.
ANSWER: Stendhal [or Marie-Henri Beyle; accept Stendhal syndrome]
T2 2019 ACF Nationals (2019) Diff 9 · Literature
This author illustrated the idea that beauty is “the promise of happiness” with an anecdote about Alberic admiring the smallpox scar of his mistress. A photograph of the plaster cast of Metilde Dembowski’s hand kept on this writer’s desk is printed in the first section of W.G. Sebald’s Vertigo, which consists of a biography of this author. While traveling with Madame Gherardi, this author wrote of the transformation undergone by tree branches left in a Salzburg salt mine, which inspired him to describe a process with stages of “Admiration,” “Acknowledgement,” “Hope,” “Delight.” This author sketched a diagram on a playing card that depicts the “crystallization” of love as a journey from Bologna to Rome. This author of On Love wrote of nearly fainting in a Florence basilica, thus lending his name to a “syndrome” whose sufferers become overwhelmed by art. For 10 points, name this author whose experiences in the Napoleonic Wars inspired his novel The Charterhouse of Parma.
ANSWER: Stendhal [or Marie-Henri Beyle]
