B1 2024 PLAYTIME! (2024) Diff 7 · Philosophy
This institution ended during the First Mithridatic War when Philo of Larissa fled to Rome. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this Athenian institution founded by Plato which lends its name to modern schools.
ANSWER: the Academy [or Plato’s Academy or Platonic Academy; or Academic School]
[10] Philo of Larissa supported a more moderate form of this philosophical view. During the Middle Academy, Arcesilaus promulgated the “Academic” form of this philosophical view, which advocates for akatalepsia.
ANSWER: skepticism [accept Academic skepticism; accept ancient skepticism]
[10] Scholars disagree whether Arcesilaus or this leading Academic skeptic of the “New Academy” first promulgated akatalepsia. This thinker who emphasized probabilism in skepticism gave contradictory lectures on justice while visiting Rome.
ANSWER: Carneades
B2 2022 ACF Regionals (2022) Diff 8 · Philosophy
Apocryphally, upon hearing the phrase “without Chrysippus (“cry-SIP-us”), there would be no Stoa,” a member of this school responded, “And without Chrysippus, there would be no me.” For 10 points each:
[10] Name this ancient school of thought, one branch of which was founded by Pyrrho. Members of this school, like Sextus Empiricus, questioned the possibility of knowledge, leading to a modern word referring to those who doubt the truth of something.
ANSWER: ancient skepticism [accept academics; accept Academic skepticism or Pyrrhonian skepticism; accept being a skeptic]
[10] Ancient skeptics espoused epoche (“eh-po-KAY”), or the suspension of judgment, as the best way to achieve this Greek-language mental state usually translated as “freedom from disturbance.” This state was also considered the highest good in Epicureanism.
ANSWER: ataraxia (“at-uh-RACK-see-uh”) [accept ataraxy]
[10] This skeptic scholarch of the Academy argued that although there are no cognitive impressions, knowledge is still possible through probabilistic reasoning. This rival of Chrysippus (“cry-SIP-us”) names a “plank” in a thought experiment used to discuss self-defense killings.
ANSWER: Carneades (“car-NAY-uh-deez”) of Cyrene [accept Plank of Carneades]
