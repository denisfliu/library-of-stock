French Baroque composer (1683–1764), dominant figure in French opera after Lully. Best known for the opera-ballets Les Indes galantes and Les fêtes d'Hébé, and the tragedie en musique Hippolyte et Aricie and Castor et Pollux. Also a major theorist: his 1722 Treatise on Harmony proposed the concept of 'fundamental bass' and codified the theory of tonal music. Also composed important harpsichord collections (Pièces de clavecin), including the famous 'La Poule.' Rivaled by Lully's legacy and embroiled in the War of the Buffoons (Querelle des Bouffons). Debussy composed a piano homage to him.
General / Biographical

French Baroque composer (1683–1764), successor to Lully as the dominant French opera composer. Rose to fame after publishing his 1722 Treatise on Harmony. His operas were supported by one faction in the War of the Buffoons (Querelle des Bouffons) against Italian opera fans, sparked by a performance of Pergolesi's La serva padrona. Jean-Jacques Rousseau was on the opposing side. Patronized by the wealthy financier La Pouplinière. John Whitney produced a film homage to Rameau while at IBM, influenced by the Treatise on Harmony. A Vikingur Ólafsson piano album pairs Debussy and Rameau as 'painters in sound.'

Summary of Facts

Jean-Philippe Rameau (1683–1764) was the dominant French opera composer of the Baroque era, emerging after the death of Jean-Baptiste Lully. He rose to prominence in 1722 when he published his Traité de l'harmonie (Treatise on Harmony), which introduced the concept of 'fundamental bass' and proposed that chords can be understood as inversions of a root — effectively codifying the theory of tonal music. John Whitney produced a film homage to Rameau while serving as IBM's first artist-in-residence, having been deeply influenced by the Treatise. Rameau was patronized by the wealthy financier La Pouplinière.

Rameau's operas are divided into tragedies en musique and opera-ballets (ballets-héroïques). His first opera, Hippolyte et Aricie (1733), was based on a libretto drawing from Racine's Phèdre and was immediately controversial — partisans of Lully's older French style complained it destroyed everything 'with a single stroke.' Castor et Pollux features the soprano aria 'Tristes apprêts, pâles flambeaux' sung by Telaira; the opera concerns twin brothers, only one initially immortal, who ultimately share immortality by Jupiter's decree. A prologue celebrating the War of the Polish Succession was cut from the revival. Debussy's 'Homage to Rameau' in his first book of piano Images draws its melody from Castor et Pollux's aria 'Séjour de l'éternelle paix.'

Les Indes galantes is an opera-ballet in four acts set respectively in Turkey, Peru, Persia, and North America. The North American act, sometimes referred to as 'Les Sauvages,' features Zima and Adario pledging love before a peace pipe ceremony and the act 'The Generous Turk.' The Incan Peru entrée has Don Carlos and Huascar rival each other for Princess Phani while a volcano erupts. Rameau's keyboard works include three books of Pièces de clavecin: 'La Poule' is the most famous, with opening notes G-G-G-G-G-G-B-flat-D over which Rameau wrote 'co co co co co co co dai' to imitate hen clucking. 'The Three Hands' has the left hand constantly crossing the right, imitating a deformity. The best-known tambourin is the penultimate movement of the E-minor suite. Rameau also wrote Pièces de clavecin en concerts, adding viol to the harpsichord. 'La Poule' was quoted by Saint-Saëns in the 'Hens and Roosters' movement of Carnival of the Animals.

Rameau's operas were at the center of the Querelle des Bouffons (War of the Buffoons) of the 1750s, in which fans of Italian opera — led by Jean-Jacques Rousseau, who attacked Rameau's Encyclopédie articles on music — opposed the French Baroque tradition. The quarrel was sparked by a performance of Pergolesi's La serva padrona. Other Rameau opera-ballets include Les fêtes d'Hébé (Mercury and Égle dance with Terpsichore) and Les fêtes de Polymnie (Mnemosyne praises the victory at the Battle of Fontenoy). Voltaire wrote the libretto for his Le Temple de la Gloire.
