Summary of Facts

Rabindranath Tagore (1861-1941) was a Bengali poet, novelist, musician, and polymath — the first non-European Nobel laureate in Literature (1913). He composed the national anthems of both India ('Jana Gana Mana') and Bangladesh ('Amar Shonar Bangla') and founded Shantiniketan (Visva-Bharati University). He renounced his knighthood after the Jallianwala Bagh massacre.

Gitanjali is a collection of devotional prose poems whose English translation, aided by W.B. Yeats's introduction, won the Nobel Prize. It includes the famous poem 'Where the mind is without fear and the head is held high.'

The Home and the World (Ghare Baire) depicts the love triangle between Bimala, her husband Nikhil, and the charismatic nationalist Sandip during the Swadeshi movement. Satyajit Ray adapted it into film. The Post Office follows the sick boy Amal confined to his room and was performed in the Warsaw Ghetto. Gora features a character who discovers he is actually Irish-born rather than Hindu. The Broken Nest was also adapted by Ray.
