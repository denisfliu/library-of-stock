Austrian-born German Expressionist film director (1890–1976), associated with Weimar-era cinema. Best known for M (1931) and Metropolis (1927). Fled Nazi Germany after allegedly being offered control of its film division; continued career in Hollywood. His wife Thea von Harbou wrote many of his screenplays. Appeared as himself in Godard's Contempt (1963).
M (1931)

Lang's first sound film, starring Peter Lorre as Hans Beckert, a compulsive child murderer who whistles 'In the Hall of the Mountain King' (Grieg). A blind balloon vendor identifies him by the tune. A criminal underworld hunts him down because police attention is hurting their business. He is found, marked with the letter 'M' in chalk on his overcoat, and tried in an improvised kangaroo court in an abandoned distillery. The film ends with three women in black mourning, one speaking a warning to an unseen audience. Wikipedia

Silent science fiction film set in a dystopian city divided between elite rulers and underground workers. The evil scientist Rotwang transfers the likeness of labor leader Maria onto a robot to incite riots. Freder (the protagonist) has a vision of workers being fed to the machine Moloch. The Schufftan process (mirrors + miniatures) was used for special effects. The film's score has been shown with Giorgio Moroder's version. Lost reels were found in 2008 in Buenos Aires. Dr. Mabuse Trilogy

A trilogy of German Expressionist films about the villainous hypnotist-gambler Dr. Mabuse. The three films: Dr. Mabuse the Gambler (1922), The Testament of Dr. Mabuse (1933, in which he outlines criminal plans from a mental asylum), and The Thousand Eyes of Dr. Mabuse (1960). In the first film, Mabuse creates a stock market panic, seduces Countess Told, and battles detective Norbert von Wenk. The opening sequence was inspired by Feuillade's Fantomas series. Wikipedia

General / Biographical

Fritz Lang (Friedrich Christian Anton Lang, 1890–1976) was an Austrian-born director who became a key figure in Weimar German cinema. He fled Germany after allegedly being offered control of the Nazi film division, which he turned down. His wife and frequent screenwriter was Thea von Harbou. He collaborated with composer Gottfried Huppertz (who used the dies irae in Metropolis). He played himself directing the Odyssey in Jean-Luc Godard's 1963 film Contempt / Le Mépris. He directed Hangmen Also Die! (1943), an anti-Nazi wartime film about the assassination of Reinhard Heydrich.

Summary of Facts

Fritz Lang (Friedrich Christian Anton Lang, 1890–1976) was an Austrian-born German Expressionist director who became one of the key figures in Weimar cinema before fleeing Germany — allegedly after being offered (and declining) control of the Nazi film division. His wife and frequent screenwriter was Thea von Harbou.

Lang's most frequently clued film is M (1931), his first sound film, starring Peter Lorre as Hans Beckert, a compulsive child murderer who whistles Grieg's 'In the Hall of the Mountain King' before killing. A blind balloon vendor identifies him by the tune. The film opens with a girl being offered a balloon by a man whose shadow appears on her own wanted poster; a man rubs chalk on his hand and marks Beckert's overcoat with the letter 'M.' Both the police and the criminal underworld (whose operations are disrupted by police attention) hunt Beckert. He is tried in an improvised kangaroo court in an abandoned distillery, where he delivers the speech: 'I cannot help myself! I have no control over this evil thing that is inside me — the fire, the voices, the torment!' The film ends with three women in black mourning, one speaking a warning to an unseen audience.

Metropolis (1927) is a landmark silent science fiction film in which the evil scientist Rotwang transfers the likeness of labor leader Maria onto a robot to incite riots among the underground workers. Protagonist Freder has a hallucinatory vision of workers being fed to the machine Moloch. The Schufftan process (combining mirrors and miniatures) was invented for the film; it is sometimes shown with Giorgio Moroder's score. An inter-title reads 'the mediator between head and hands must be the heart.' Lost reels were found in 2008 in Buenos Aires. Janelle Monae's 'ArchAndroid' concept albums, featuring her robot alter ego Cindi Mayweather, were inspired by Metropolis.

The Dr. Mabuse trilogy spans his career: Dr. Mabuse the Gambler (1922), in which the villainous hypnotist creates a stock market panic and seduces Countess Told (the opening was inspired by Feuillade's Fantomas); The Testament of Dr. Mabuse (1933), in which Mabuse outlines criminal plans from a mental asylum; and The Thousand Eyes of Dr. Mabuse (1960), his final film after returning to West Germany.

In Hollywood, Lang made the American noir The Big Heat (1953), in which Lee Marvin throws boiling coffee in Gloria Grahame's face and fired detective Dave Bannion hunts his wife's killers; a woman coolly phones in her husband's suicide as 'Tom Duncan's widow.' Fury (1936) shows a lynch mob attempting to execute a mechanic played by Spencer Tracy; MGM forced Lang to cast a white lead. Woman in the Moon (1929) features the first onscreen countdown to a rocket launch, with rocket scientist Hermann Oberth as consultant. Hangmen Also Die! (1943) is his anti-Nazi film about the assassination of Reinhard Heydrich. Lang appeared as himself in Jean-Luc Godard's Contempt / Le Mépris (1963), directing a film of the Odyssey; producers forced Godard to add a nude scene with Brigitte Bardot to the film.
