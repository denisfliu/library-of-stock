Tossups

T1 2018 Oxford Open (2018) Diff 8 · Literature
In one of this author’s novels, a man is transfixed by the heroine’s bracelet falling down her arm and being replaced, and by the way that she used her fingers as sugar tongs. In another of this author’s works, Mr. Benson reads from Revelation 7 because he is too distraught to deliver a sermon when he remembers first meeting the dead heroine at Llan-dhu [hlan-du] with her wealthy lover Mr. Bellingham whom she had died nursing. In a letter, Charles Dickens described this writer as ‘dear Scheherazade’ and one of their works serialized in Household Works tells of Margaret Hale’s move from her hometown ‘like a village in a poem’, Helstone to the Darkshire town of Milton. For 10 points, name this Victorian novelist of Ruth, and North and South.
ANSWER: Elizabeth Gaskell [accept Mrs. Gaskell]
T2 2015 Oxford Open (2015) Diff 8 · Literature
In one of this author’s stories, a rural worker who leads a second life as a poacher has his attempts at redemption repeatedly foiled by the thought of his enemy Dick Jackson. Job Legh collects insects in a novel by this author, in which the main story follows the reconciliation between a mill-owning and a mill-working family. As well as Mary Barton, this author wrote a novel in which Margaret Hale falls in love with Mr Thornton and sheds some of her assumptions about the North. For 10 points, identify this author of Cranford and North and South.
ANSWER: Elizabeth Gaskell
T3 2013 VCU Closed (2013) Diff 8 · Literature
In a novel by this author, the entire town delights in hearing a story about a cat who swallowed a piece of lace and how the lace was later cleaned after being excreted, and Betsy Barker attempts to save a cow that had fallen into a lime‐pit by dressing it in flannel. This author wrote a novel in which the title character becomes a nurse to typhoid patients and again re‐encounters the MP Donne, who is actually the nefarious seducer (*) Bellingham. Thornton studies classics and decides to treat his workers beter in another novel by this writer, in which the Hale family moves to Milton. This author of Ruth and Wives and Daughters created Miss Matty, who must sell tea after losing her money in a bank collapse in a novel narrated by Mary Smith. For 10 points, name this author of North and South and Cranford.
ANSWER: Elizabeth Gaskell
