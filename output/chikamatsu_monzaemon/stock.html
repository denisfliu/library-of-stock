Japanese Edo-period playwright, master of bunraku (puppet theater) and kabuki. Best known for his 'love suicide' plays: The Love Suicides at Sonezaki (Tokubei and Ohatsu in the Wood of Tenjin) and The Love Suicides at Amijima (Jihei and Koharu). Also wrote The Battles of Coxinga (a historical/jidaimono play) and The Uprooted Pine (Hard-Luck Yohei ransoms the courtesan Azuma). Donald Keene published four major plays in English translation in 1961. Part of the Genroku triumvirate with Matsuo Basho and Ihara Saikaku. His domestic plays belong to the sewamono genre.
The Love Suicides at Sonezaki

Chikamatsu's most famous play. The oil merchant Tokubei and the courtesan Ohatsu commit suicide together in the Wood of Tenjin after Kuheiji steals Tokubei's money through a promissory note scam (pretending it is extortion). In the last scene, Tokubei and Ohatsu dance in the Woods of Tenjin before killing themselves. The oil merchant Kuheiji swindles Tokubei out of two pieces of silver. A pine tree and palm tree grow from the same trunk at the shrine where the suicide occurs. Wikipedia

The Love Suicides at Amijima

A domestic play (sewamono) about the paper merchant Jihei and the courtesan Koharu, who kill themselves together. Jihei's wife is Osan. The speaker laments 'my tears are now of molten iron!' after learning a woman is being courted by 'Lone Wolf.' A man has his hands tied to a window after trying to stab a woman through a lattice. The Uprooted Pine

A domestic play set on New Year's Day. The man nicknamed 'Hard-Luck' Yohei beats up a tobacco merchant (Hikosuke) to save the courtesan Azuma, whose contract he later buys/ransoms. Azuma is in love with Yojibei, who cannot marry her due to his wife Okiku. The title ambiguously refers to New Year's festivities or the buying out of a courtesan's contract. Wikipedia

The Battles of Coxinga

A wildly popular historical play (jidaimono) about a general who rebels against the Qing dynasty. Ends with Ri Toten being beheaded and Eiryaku taking the throne. The title character claims to have learned military strategy from watching a shrike attack a clam. A man performs a Caesarean section with his sword on a dead empress to remove a prince. Ends with a character having both arms and head cut off while the Great King of Tartary only suffers 500 lashes. The protagonist frees Tei Shiryu while companions Go Sankei and Kanki capture the Great King. Wikipedia

The Courier for Hell

A domestic play that starts in a post office blaming delays on rain while an employee embezzles money shipments. A character flees home after stealing 300 gold pieces from a nobleman to ransom his lover. His mother thinks he uses three packs of handkerchiefs a day to blow his nose when he actually uses them to clean up after sex.

General / Biographical

Master of bunraku (puppet theater) and kabuki. Donald Keene published Four Major Plays of Chikamatsu in 1961. Part of the Genroku triumvirate with Matsuo Basho and Ihara Saikaku. His domestic plays are sewamono (contrasted with jidaimono historical plays). The tayu narrates his works. Also born Sugimori Nobumori.

Summary of Facts

Chikamatsu Monzaemon is a Japanese Edo-period playwright and master of bunraku (puppet theater). He is most frequently clued for The Love Suicides at Sonezaki, in which the oil merchant Tokubei and the courtesan Ohatsu commit suicide together in the Wood of Tenjin after Kuheiji steals Tokubei's money through a promissory note scam. A pine tree and palm tree grow from the same trunk at the shrine. In one scene, a man hides under his lover's robes to avoid his creditor.

The Love Suicides at Amijima is another domestic (sewamono) play about the paper merchant Jihei and the courtesan Koharu. The Uprooted Pine features 'Hard-Luck' Yohei beating up a tobacco merchant to ransom the courtesan Azuma; its title ambiguously refers to New Year's festivities or buying out a courtesan's contract.

The Battles of Coxinga is his most famous historical play (jidaimono), about a general rebelling against the Qing dynasty. It includes a Caesarean section performed with a sword on a dead empress. The title character claims to have learned military strategy from watching a shrike attack a clam.

The Courier for Hell starts in a post office blaming delays on rain while an employee embezzles. A character steals 300 gold pieces to ransom his lover, while his mother thinks his handkerchief usage is for nose-blowing.

Donald Keene published Four Major Plays of Chikamatsu in 1961. Chikamatsu is part of the Genroku triumvirate with Matsuo Basho and Ihara Saikaku. His domestic plays belong to the sewamono genre. Tanizaki referenced his works in Some Prefer Nettles.
