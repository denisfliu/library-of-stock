Finnish-American modernist architect and furniture designer. Son of architect Eliel Saarinen. Best known for the Gateway Arch in St. Louis, the TWA Flight Center at JFK Airport, and Dulles International Airport. Also designed the MIT Chapel and Kresge Auditorium, the CBS Building ('Black Rock'), the Miller House in Columbus, Indiana (famous for its conversation pit), and the Ingalls Rink at Yale ('the whale'). Designed iconic furniture for Knoll including the Tulip, Womb, and Grasshopper chairs.
Gateway Arch (1965)

The most frequently clued Saarinen work. A stainless steel catenary arch in St. Louis, Missouri — the world's tallest arch — built as the Jefferson Expansion Memorial. The arch design inspired his Dulles Airport roof. Wikipedia

Summary of Facts

Eero Saarinen was a Finnish-American modernist architect and furniture designer, son of the Finnish architect Eliel Saarinen. He is most famous in quizbowl for two structures: the Gateway Arch in St. Louis — the world's tallest arch, built as the Jefferson Expansion Memorial using a catenary curve design — and the TWA Flight Center at JFK International Airport, now converted into the TWA Hotel. The TWA terminal was described by critic Vincent Scully as exemplifying 'paramilitary dandyism,' a term also applied to his CBS Building in New York City, nicknamed 'The Black Rock' for its dark granite pier facade, which Saarinen designed to be 'the simplest skyscraper in New York City.'

At MIT, Saarinen designed two adjacent buildings: the nondenominational red-brick chapel, which features a Harry Bertoia sculpture that creates a cascade of light from a skylight to a white marble altar, and the Kresge Auditorium, shaped like a leaf — a perfect eighth of a sphere. At Yale, he designed the Ingalls Rink, nicknamed 'the whale' for its dramatic sloping roof; one building from that project was previously used as an all-girls dormitory with a drawbridge to keep men out. He also designed Washington Dulles International Airport, featuring an airfoil-like concrete roof incorporating catenary curves (linking it to the Gateway Arch concept).

Saarinen's Miller House in Columbus, Indiana is credited with popularizing the 'conversation pit' — a sunken seating area that became a mid-century modern design icon. The house contains a bas relief by sculptor Jacques Lipchitz. Columbus, Indiana became renowned for its modernist architecture through multiple Saarinen commissions; the 2017 film Columbus by Kogonada was set there. Eero collaborated with his father Eliel on several projects, including the General Motors Technical Center in Warren, Michigan and the Kleinhans Music Hall in Buffalo, New York (which contains the Mary Seaton room). Eliel's 1922 runner-up design for the Chicago Tribune Tower competition influenced the American Radiator Building in New York, and Eliel also directed the Cranbrook Academy of Art, which was strongly influenced by the Bauhaus.

As a furniture designer, Saarinen created three iconic chairs for the Knoll company: the Grasshopper, the Womb, and the Tulip — the last of which is housed in the Vitra Design Museum. He also collaborated with Charles Eames on the Entenza House, a Case Study House adjacent to the famous Eames House. Saarinen designed the Vivian Beaumont Theater at Lincoln Center and corporate headquarters for John Deere, IBM, and CBS. His wife (or his father's wife) Lillian Swann Saarinen was a sculptor who created Night at the 1939 World's Fair, depicting the panther Bagheera, and designed a reflecting pool surrounded by buffalo originally paired with Eliel's catenary curve design for the Jefferson Expansion Memorial.
