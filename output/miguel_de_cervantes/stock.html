Spanish Golden Age author, best known for Don Quixote. Also wrote the Exemplary Novels (including 'The Colloquy of the Dogs,' 'The Lawyer of Glass,' and 'The Deceitful Marriage'), the pastoral La Galatea, the satirical poem Journey to Parnassus, and plays including The Siege of Numantia. Veteran of the Battle of Lepanto. Imprisoned in Algiers. Excommunicated multiple times. Called Lope de Vega 'The Phoenix of Wits' and 'Monster of Nature.' Savaged Avellaneda for writing an unauthorized Don Quixote sequel. An annual Spanish-language literary prize is named after him.
Don Quixote

Cervantes's masterpiece about an 'ingenious gentleman' (a 50-year-old hidalgo) who goes mad reading books of chivalry and becomes a knight errant. He rides the horse Rocinante alongside his squire Sancho Panza, who joins him hoping to win an island. He loves the imagined lady Dulcinea. A priest and barber burn the books in Don Quixote's library (sparing La Galatea and praising Tirant lo Blanch). He attacks windmills. He thinks a barber's basin full of curds is the helmet of Mambrino. A hunchbacked servant crawls into bed with the protagonist at an inn where Sancho is tossed in a blanket. Samson Carrasco disguises himself as the Knight of the White Moon to defeat Don Quixote. The interpolated tale 'The Impertinently Curious Man' concerns two friends from Florence (Anselmo and Lothario) and the housewife Camilla. In Part 2, Cervantes savages Avellaneda's unauthorized sequel; Alvaro Tarfe signs an affidavit that the sequel's characters are impostors. Don Quixote imagines slaying the giant Caraculiambro. The preface includes a story about a madman who inflates a dog with a pointed reed. Pierre Menard recreates this novel word-for-word in a Borges story. Miguel de Unamuno rebuked Cervantes for mistreating Don Quixote. Ruben Dario wrote 'Litany for Our Lord Don Quixote.' Wikipedia

Exemplary Novels

A collection of twelve novellas published in 1613. Includes 'The Colloquy of the Dogs,' 'The Lawyer of Glass,' 'The Deceitful Marriage,' 'Rinconete y Cortadillo,' 'The Jealous Extremaduran,' 'The Power of Blood,' and 'The Illustrious Scullery Maid.' John Ford's The Spanish Gypsy was based on stories from this collection. Wikipedia

La Galatea

Cervantes's first novel, a pastoral work set near the Tagus river. Features the shepherdess Galatea (Aurelio's daughter) loved by Elicio and Erastro. A journey to the wedding of Daranio and Silveria is a central event. Imitated Jorge de Montemayor's prose romance Diana. Spared during the burning of Don Quixote's library, judged to have 'some good invention' but noted as having 'a pretty good plot that goes nowhere.' Cervantes never wrote the promised second part. Journey to Parnassus (Viaje del Parnaso)

A long satirical poem about poetry. Lope de Vega is rained out of a cloud onto a boat of poets. Gongora is declared 'a good poet' and praised. Venus turns poets into pumpkins. Cervantes ridiculed contemporary poets in this work. Included among his Exemplary Novels the story 'Rinconete y Cortadillo.' Wikipedia

Plays (The Siege of Numantia and others)

Cervantes wrote several plays, mostly set in Algiers where he was imprisoned. The Siege of Numantia is about the Celtiberian Wars: the first act ends with a discussion between Spain and the river Duero after a speech by Scipio Africanus; the sorcerer Marquino resurrects a corpse; a young lad jumps off a tower amid the Four Horsemen of the Apocalypse. Other plays include one about the baths of Algiers (basis for Massinger's The Renegado), a farce where a divorce court judge refuses to let anybody divorce, and a play titled for a treaty (Aurelio and Silvia love each other in captivity). General / Biographical

Miguel de Cervantes Saavedra (1547-1616) was a Spanish Golden Age author. A veteran of the Battle of Lepanto. Was imprisoned in Algiers. Excommunicated multiple times. Nicknamed Lope de Vega 'The Phoenix of Wits' and called him a 'Monster of Nature.' Parodied Lope de Vega as the puppeteer Gines de Pasamonte in Don Quixote Part 2. Savaged Avellaneda for writing an unauthorized Don Quixote sequel. An annual prize for Spanish-language literature (the Cervantes Prize) is named after him. Also wrote The Travels of Persiles and Sigismunda (posthumously published; Rutilio describes men turning into wolves). Influenced by both Lazarillo de Tormes and Amadis de Gaula.

Summary of Facts

Miguel de Cervantes Saavedra (1547-1616) is the central figure of Spanish Golden Age literature in quizbowl. His masterpiece Don Quixote dominates the clue pool: the titular hidalgo who goes mad reading books of chivalry, rides Rocinante alongside Sancho Panza, attacks windmills, and loves the imagined Dulcinea. The burning of Don Quixote's library by a priest and barber is one of the most frequently clued scenes, with La Galatea being notably spared. Samson Carrasco's disguise as the Knight of the White Moon and the interpolated 'Impertinently Curious Man' tale (about Anselmo, Lothario, and Camilla) also recur. Part 2 of Don Quixote contains Cervantes's attacks on Avellaneda's unauthorized sequel, including the scene where Alvaro Tarfe signs an affidavit. The novel is the subject of Borges's 'Pierre Menard, Author of the Quixote' and Unamuno's critical essays. The Exemplary Novels (1613) form the second major cluster. 'The Colloquy of the Dogs' — in which the dogs Cipion and Berganza converse by a hospital window, overheard by the syphilitic Campuzano — is the most frequently clued story; Freud notably signed 55 letters as 'Cipion.' 'The Lawyer of Glass,' about Tomas Rodaja who eats a spiked quince and believes he is made of glass, appears almost as often. 'The Deceitful Marriage' is the framing story for the Dogs, with Campuzano robbed by his wife. Other notable stories include 'Rinconete y Cortadillo' (vagrant boys join thieves led by Monopodio in Seville), 'The Jealous Extremaduran,' 'The Power of Blood,' and 'The Illustrious Scullery Maid.' La Galatea, his first novel, is a pastoral work about the shepherdess Galatea loved by Elicio and Erastro, set near the Tagus. It imitated Montemayor's Diana and features a journey to the wedding of Daranio and Silveria. Journey to Parnassus is a satirical poem praising Gongora and mocking other contemporary poets, including raining Lope de Vega from a cloud. His plays, mostly set in Algiers, include The Siege of Numantia (featuring Scipio Africanus and the sorcerer Marquino) and a divorce court farce. Biographically, Cervantes was a veteran of the Battle of Lepanto, was imprisoned in Algiers, was excommunicated multiple times, and nicknamed Lope de Vega 'The Phoenix of Wits.' The annual Cervantes Prize for Spanish-language literature is named after him.
