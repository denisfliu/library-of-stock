American Romantic author. Most commonly identified by 'Young Goodman Brown' (Faith, serpent-shaped staff, Goody Cloyse, witches' sabbath), The Scarlet Letter (Hester Prynne, Arthur Dimmesdale, Pearl, Roger Chillingworth), and The House of the Seven Gables (Pyncheon family). Herman Melville dedicated Moby-Dick to him and wrote 'Hawthorne and His Mosses.' Changed spelling of family name (added 'w') to distance from Salem ancestry.
Young Goodman Brown

Hawthorne's most frequently clued short story. The protagonist walks through the woods with a man whose staff is shaped like a serpent, meets Goody Cloyse and Deacon Gookin, and discovers his wife Faith at a witches' sabbath/satanic ceremony. Faith's pink ribbon falls from the sky. Ends with 'no hopeful verse' on his tombstone. Published in Mosses from an Old Manse. Melville called it 'as deep as Dante.'

The Scarlet Letter

Hawthorne's most famous novel. Hester Prynne wears the title scarlet letter 'A' after her affair with Reverend Arthur Dimmesdale, who fathers the child Pearl. Roger Chillingworth torments Dimmesdale. Dimmesdale reveals a symbol seared on his chest during an Election Day sermon on a scaffold, then dies. Opens by noting all societies provide for a prison and a cemetery. Framing story: narrator finds Jonathan Pue's manuscript while working at the Salem Custom House.

Summary of Facts

Nathaniel Hawthorne is one of the most canonical American authors in quizbowl. 'Young Goodman Brown' is his most heavily clued short story, appearing with remarkable frequency: Goodman Brown walks through the woods with a man whose staff resembles a serpent, encounters Goody Cloyse and Deacon Gookin, and finds his wife Faith at a witches' sabbath. Faith's pink ribbon falling from the sky and the 'no hopeful verse' tombstone ending are standard clues. The story appears in Mosses from an Old Manse, which Melville reviewed, calling 'Young Goodman Brown' 'as deep as Dante.'

The Scarlet Letter serves as the primary giveaway identifier through Hester Prynne, Arthur Dimmesdale, Pearl, and Roger Chillingworth. Dimmesdale's Election Day sermon on the scaffold, the Custom House framing story, and the opening note about societies providing prisons and cemeteries are all recurring clues. The House of the Seven Gables appears through the Pyncheon family curse, Judge Pyncheon choking on blood, and the romance/novel preface distinction.

'Rappaccini's Daughter' (Beatrice, poisonous garden, Professor Baglioni's fatal antidote) and 'The Birthmark' (Aylmer, Georgiana, Aminadab) are both frequently clued stories about science gone wrong. Other commonly clued works include 'My Kinsman, Major Molineux' (tarring-and-feathering), 'Ethan Brand' (Unpardonable Sin, lime kiln), 'The Celestial Railroad' (Mr. Smooth-it-away), 'Feathertop' (Mother Rigby's scarecrow), and 'Dr. Heidegger's Experiment' (Fountain of Youth). The Blithedale Romance fictionalizes Brook Farm.

Biographically, Hawthorne is defined by his relationship with Melville (Moby-Dick dedication, 'Hawthorne and His Mosses'), his Salem ancestry (adding 'w' to the family name), and his complaint about 'a damned mob of scribbling women.' He wrote the campaign biography Life of Franklin Pierce and invited Rebecca Harding Davis to Concord after reading 'Life in the Iron Mills.'
