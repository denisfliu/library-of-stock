Summary of Facts

Richard Hamilton was a British Pop artist and member of the Independent Group, best known for the collage Just what is it that makes today's homes so different, so appealing? (1956), created for the 'This is Tomorrow' exhibition. The collage features a bodybuilder (based on Irvin Koszewski from Tomorrow's Man magazine, representing Adam) holding a giant Tootsie Pop with 'Pop' on it, a nude woman on a grey couch wearing a lampshade, a maid cleaning stairs with an extraordinarily long vacuum cleaner with an arrow stating 'ordinary cleaners reach only this far,' a poster for Young Romance comic, a cinema playing The Jazz Singer through a window, and the planet Jupiter at the top. A later adaptation made with the Quantel Paintbox system replaced the bodybuilder with an accountant at a computer, added a bust of Thatcher captioned 'Jeff Koons,' and arranged the word AIDS like Robert Indiana's LOVE.

Hamilton created three diptychs about the Northern Ireland Troubles: The Subject (an Orangeman), The State (a soldier), and The Citizen (Bobby Sands depicted as a Christ-like hunger striker, with abstract swirls representing feces smeared on the walls of the Maze prison). His painting Hommage a Chrysler Corp. combines a woman's outline with red lips blending into the hood of a car, while $he depicts a woman as disconnected plastic shapes with an eye above a refrigerator and toaster.

Other works include Treatment Room (an installation with an orange blanket, hygienic bed, and X-ray machine showing Thatcher), six relief sculptures of the Guggenheim Museum in various shades, Fashion Plate (collage of Sophia Loren's features), The Critic Laughs (dentures on an electric toothbrush), the unfinished Le Chef-d'oeuvre inconnu (based on Balzac, with Poussin, Courbet, and Titian), a series on James Joyce's Ulysses, a Polaroid self-portrait series with Warhol, Man Ray, Beuys, Bacon, and Jarman, late maps of Israel's West Bank occupation, and the cover design for the Beatles' White Album. He was formerly a commercial artist at EMI.
