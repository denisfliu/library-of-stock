Questions: Patrick White

4 tossups · 5 bonuses

Tossups

T1 2023 ILLIAC (2023) Diff 7 · Literature
One author with this surname described his struggles of learning falconry in the book The Goshawk (“goss-hawk”). In a novel by this author with this surname, a Holocaust survivor dies after being subjected to a mock crucifixion outside a bicycle lamp factory. That novel, partly set at the Xanadu estate, was by an author with this surname who also wrote a novel based on the naturalist Ludwig Leichhardt. In a novel by an author with this surname, the protagonist, referred to as (*) “Wart,” befriends his tutor’s owl Archimedes. An author with this surname wrote about Laura Trevelyan’s love for the explorer Voss. A book series by an author with this surname that begins with The Sword in the Stone follows King Arthur. For 10 points, give this surname shared by the Australian author Patrick and the British author of The Once and Future King, T.H.
ANSWER: White [accept Patrick White or Patrick Victor Martindale White; accept T.H. White or Terence Hanbury White]
T2 2022 NASAT (2022) Diff 8 · Literature
In a novel by this author, the shipping merchant Angelos leaves a Paris hotel after Joan Golson meets his mistress. The last novel this author published concerns a non-gender conforming protagonist who uses the names Eudoxia, Eddie, and Eadith. Mordecai is subjected to a mock crucifixion in a novel by this author about four people who share a vision from the book of Ezekiel, set in his fictional suburb of Sarsaparilla. Rhoda Courtenay and Kathy Volkov befriend the protagonist of a novel by this author, who daubs excrement on a painting showing the eye of God. In another novel, this author of The Twyborn Affair and Riders in the Chariot depicted Laura Trevelyan's time with the title German explorer, who leads an expedition across a continent before dying in the Outback. For 10 points, name this Australian author of The Vivisector and Voss.
ANSWER: Patrick White [Patrick Victor Martindale White]
T3 2015 Oxford Open (2015) Diff 8 · Literature
One novel by this author details the life of an artist and painter who seeks to complete his magnum opus after suffering a stroke. The protagonist of that work is theorized by some critics to be based off of Sidney Nolan. Another of his works chronicles the attempt of the Parker family to adapt a traditional way of life with their new life on the frontier. Those novels are The Vivisector and The Tree of Man. This author’s best known work centers of the connection that Laura, an orphan from New South Wales, has with a Prussian explorer. For 10 points, name this Nobel winning Australian novelist, the author of Voss.
ANSWER: Patrick White
T4 2012 QUARK (2012) Diff 8 · Literature
This author’s last novel was purported to be edited from the papers of one Alex Xenophon Demirjian Gray, but an unfinished novel by him was published in 2012. In a novel reminiscent of Orlando, he wrote about a single soul which inhabits three different identities, two female and one male, all of whom have names starting with E. One of his characters is inspired by characters like Kathy (*) Volkov, Hero Pavloussi, and the hunchbacked Rhoda Courtney. That character is a painter who creates the title painting after being partially paralyzed by a stroke. In another of his novels, a journey backed by Edmund Bonner is undertaken by Frank Le Mesurier, Palfreyman and other companions of the title explorer, who has a deep connection with Laura Trevelyan. For 10 points, name this author of The Vivisector and Voss.
ANSWER: Patrick White

Bonuses

B1 2024 Penn Bowl (2024) Diff 8 · Literature
An author from this country wrote about Daphne Withers, who undergoes a lobotomy, in her novel Owls Do Cry. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this country home to the author of The Carpathians, whose mother was a housemaid to another author from this country who wrote “Bliss” and “The Garden Party,” Katherine Mansfield.
ANSWER: New Zealand [or Aotearoa]
[10] This author of The Carpathians depicted her return to New Zealand and her diagnosis of schizophrenia in the autobiographical books To the Is-Land (“IS land”) and An Angel at My Table.
ANSWER: Janet Frame [or Janet Paterson Frame]
[10] This novelist praised Frame's autobiography as “amongst the wonders of the world.” This author created the spinster Mary Hare, who lives in Xanadu near the painter Alf Dubbo, in Riders in the Chariot.
ANSWER: Patrick White [or Patrick Victor Martindale White]
B2 2024 ACF Nationals (2024) Diff 9 · Literature
In a fairy tale-like novel by Murray Bail, Ellen Holland’s father promises her hand to any suitor who can name every species of this plant on his estate. For 10 points each:
[10] What type of plant is addressed as a “fellow citizen” in an Oodgeroo Noonuccal poem? In an 1895 ballad, a man sitting under one of these plants cries “You’ll never catch me alive!”
ANSWER: eucalyptus trees [or gum trees; accept coolibah trees or coolabah trees; prompt on trees] (The poems are “Municipal Gum” and Banjo Paterson’s “Waltzing Matilda.”)
[10] A poet with this surname wrote of peeling a eucalyptus’s bark in “Scribbly Gum.” Oblivia is rescued from a eucalyptus tree at the start of The Swan Book, a novel by a Waanyi author with this surname who wrote Carpentaria.
ANSWER: Wright [accept Alexis Wright; accept Judith Wright or Judith Arundell Wright]
[10] This author portrayed a kitchen maid touching a gum tree for the first time during a bushfire in The Tree of Man and described the flora of the outback in Voss.
ANSWER: Patrick White [or Patrick Victor Martindale White]
B3 2021 ACF Nationals (2021) Diff 9 · Literature
This novel’s title was partially inspired by an Odilon Redon painting that its author saw as a child, and is referenced in a scene where the wine merchant Norbert poses a question to his young daughter. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this novel in which the eccentric heiress Mary Hare lives on the decaying estate of Xanadu outside of the suburb of Sarsaparilla, home to the devout mother Ruth Godbold and the artist Alf Dubbo.
ANSWER: Riders in the Chariot
[10] Riders in the Chariot was written by this Australian writer who wrote The Tree of Man and a novel about a German explorer’s attempt to traverse Australia, Voss.
ANSWER: Patrick White
[10] After winning the 1973 Nobel Prize in literature, White set up a namesake literary award in Australia, the first of which went to this writer of The Puzzleheaded Girl as well as a novel about the dysfunctional Pollit family.
ANSWER: Christina Stead (That novel is The Man Who Loved Children.)
B4 2019 ACF Nationals (2019) Diff 9 · Literature
This book closes with the protagonist suffering a stroke while writing the word “indigo,” an anagram for “god in I.” For 10 points each:
[10] Name this novel about the painter Hurtle Duffield, who smears shit onto the canvas of his work showing the eye of god, whose image had haunted him since he grew up poor in Sydney.
ANSWER: The Vivisector
[10] The Vivisector is by this Australian Nobel laureate, who also wrote about the artist Alf Dubbo in Riders in the Chariot.
ANSWER: Patrick White
[10] This other Australian author included a detailed description of a painting of soil entitled Decline and Fall of the Empire of Grass in his novel The Plains. This perennial Nobel candidate’s short fiction is collected in the volume Stream System.
