Tossups

T1 2025 ACF Nationals (2025) Diff 9 · Literature
This place is contrasted with a “world inhabited by civilized Cartesian phantoms” in a book’s opening paragraph. A collection set in this place, meant to be worthy of a “world, chiseled out of stone,” features the author bidding “Farewell” to his girlfriend Maria. A story’s narrator says, “I am not a Count of Monte Cristo” upon recognizing a doctor from this place based on how he spells “naphthenate” (“NAFF-thuh-nate”) in a letter about faulty varnish. Autobiographical stories about Tadek set in this place are quoted in the chapter “Beta, the Disappointed Lover” from The Captive Mind. To teach Italian to Jean, a man in this place recites Inferno’s “Canto of Ulysses” from memory. A man trades goods stolen in this place for bread in “Cerium,” a story in The Periodic Table. For 10 points, Primo Levi’s memoir If This is a Man depicts the horrors of what place?
ANSWER: Auschwitz [accept Oświęcim or Auschwitz II-Birkenau or Auschwitz III-Monowitz; accept Survival in Auschwitz; prompt on concentration camps or death camps or Konzentrationslager] (The third sentence is from “Vanadium.” The Captive Mind is by Czesław Miłosz. The author is Tadeusz Borowski.)
T2 2022 Penn Bowl (2022) Diff 8 · Literature
This poet asserted “it’s possible to conjecture that mankind exists / To provision and populate Hell” in an ekphrastic poem describing Bosch’s Garden of Earthly Delights. This poet wrote of “trees outside the window, which probably exist” in a poem opening “We are not so badly off, if we can / Admire Dutch painting.” Another poem by this author of the poem “Realism” mentions a fisherman mending “a glimmering net” and a bee circling a clover before closing with the repeated line “There will be no other (*) end of the world.” This author’s 1995 collection Facing the River was translated into English by his colleague Robert Hass. An anti-communist book by this author discusses the ideologies of men nicknamed Alpha and Beta. For 10 points, name this dissident Polish poet who wrote The Captive Mind.
ANSWER: Czesław Miłosz (“CHESS-wahf MEE-wosh”)
T3 2021 ARCADIA (2021) Diff 7 · Literature
A poem in this language notes that a “white-haired old man” who “binds his tomatoes” is the only one who believes that the title event “is happening now.” Another poem in this language describes those who “push the rubble to the side of the road” and “get mired in scum and ashes.” A “Treatise on Poetry” in this language was translated into English by Robert Hass. A poem in this language reflects that “after every war, someone has to (*) clean up” and is titled “The End and The Beginning.” “Delta, the Troubadour” appears in a book about this language’s literature titled The Captive Mind. A novel in this language follows a patrician who falls in love with Lygia and is set during “the time of Nero.” For 10 points, Czesław Miłosz (“CHESS-wahf MEE-wosh”) and the author of Quo Vadis, Henryk Sienkiewicz (“shen-KYEH-vich”), wrote in what language?
ANSWER: Polish [or Polski] (The first poem is “Song on the End of the World” by Miłosz; “The End and the Beginning” is by Wisława Szymborska.)
T4 2016 ACF Nationals (2016) Diff 9 · Literature
This author’s viewing of the frescoes of Luca Signorelli led him to examine the apocalyptic nature of Vladimir Solovyov’s writings in his essay “Science Fiction and the Coming of Antichrist.” In another essay, this author examined the philosopher Lev Shestov’s “purity of despair.” This man, who examined “modes of eccentric vision” in Emperor of the Earth, wrote a book that examines a writer’s novel about the dictator Murti Bing, who gives everyone a special pill to strip people of their resistance.
