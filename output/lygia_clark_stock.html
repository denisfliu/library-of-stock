Summary of Facts

Lygia Clark was a Brazilian Neo-Concretist artist best known for her Bichos series — hinged aluminum sculptures that viewers can fold, turn, and orient in various combinations. The title means 'critters' in Portuguese. She also created Walking (Caminhando), a participatory work in which viewers cut a Mobius strip lengthwise until too thin to continue. Clark wrote the essay 'Nostalgia of the Body,' which defined Neo-Concretism as treating art not as a 'machine' or 'object' but as an 'almost-body.' She shares the first name Lygia with another Brazilian artist, Lygia Pape, who created Divisor. Clark appears infrequently in quizbowl, mostly as bonus answers.
