Summary of Facts

Fernando Botero was a Colombian artist known for his distinctive exaggerated, rotund figures, which he said emphasized 'volume' and 'sensuality of form.' His Abu Ghraib series (2005) is a 56-piece response to the torture scandal, showing guards and dogs violently humiliating blindfolded prisoners, inspired by a Seymour Hersh article in The New Yorker and donated to the Berkeley Museum of Art. The Death of Pablo Escobar shows a giant man on rooftops riddled with bullets.

His Mona Lisa, Age 12 parodies the original with a face filling a quarter of the canvas; it hangs in his namesake museum in Bogota's Candelaria neighborhood. The Presidential Family alludes to Las Meninas. He created the Dove of Peace sculpture from the remains of his Bird sculpture after it was destroyed in a terrorist bombing. He was expelled from Jesuit high school for praising Picasso. His Violence series depicts car bombings and kidnappings in Colombia. He died in September 2023.
