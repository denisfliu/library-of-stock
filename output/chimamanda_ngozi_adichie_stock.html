Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie

← Home · View source questions · Make cards
Nigerian author known for novels Americanah, Half of a Yellow Sun, and Purple Hibiscus. Also wrote the short story collection The Thing Around Your Neck and the essays/talks 'We Should All Be Feminists' and 'The Danger of a Single Story.' Her epistolary book Dear Ijeawele offers feminist parenting advice. Her most recent novel is Dream Count. Public feud with Akwaeke Emezi over transphobic comments and withdrawal of endorsement of Freshwater.
Americanah (2013)

Adichie's best-known novel, about the Nigerian couple Ifemelu and Obinze. Ifemelu immigrates to the U.S., studies at Princeton on a fellowship, and writes the blog 'Raceteenth or Various Observations About American Blacks (Those Formerly Known as Negroes) by a Non-American Black.' Obinze goes to London and works illegally cleaning toilets, tries to arrange a green card marriage with Cleotilde, and is eventually deported. They reunite in Lagos after Ifemelu shuts down her blog and returns to Nigeria. Wikipedia

The Danger of a Single Story (2009 TED Talk)

One of the most popular TED talks ever. Adichie discusses how single narratives about people create stereotypes. She recalls writing stories as a child with blue-eyed white characters who ate apples, and writing characters who drank ginger beer despite not knowing what it was. Cites John Lok's description of Africans as 'beasts with no houses' and Chinua Achebe's concept of a 'balance of stories.' Recounts how her college roommate assumed she couldn't use a stove, and how she herself had a 'single story' of Mexicans as 'abject immigrants.' Uses the Igbo word 'nkali.' Wikipedia

Summary of Facts

Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie is a Nigerian author whose works are among the most frequently asked about in quizbowl among contemporary African writers. Her three novels form the backbone of most tossups: Americanah (2013) is by far the most clued, with the blog 'Raceteenth,' the Ifemelu-Obinze romance, Obinze's green card marriage attempt with Cleotilde, and nicknames like 'Ceiling' and 'The Zed' appearing repeatedly. Half of a Yellow Sun (2006), set during the Biafran War, features the twins Olanna and Kainene, the intellectual Odenigbo, the Englishman Richard Churchill, and the houseboy Ugwu who writes 'The World Was Silent When We Died.' Purple Hibiscus (2003), her debut, centers on the teenager Kambili and her abusive Catholic father Eugene, whom his wife Beatrice eventually poisons.

Adichie's nonfiction is nearly as prominent. 'We Should All Be Feminists,' adapted from her TED talk, was famously sampled by Beyonce in 'Flawless' and is cited for its definition of feminism and its description of masculinity as 'a hard, small cage.' 'The Danger of a Single Story,' one of the most-viewed TED talks, draws on her childhood experience writing about blue-eyed characters who ate apples and drank ginger beer, and her roommate's assumption she couldn't use a stove. Dear Ijeawele offers 15 tips on raising a feminist daughter for the mother of Chizalum.

Her short story collection The Thing Around Your Neck generates several clues across individual stories: 'Jumping Monkey Hill' (writers' retreat with the sexist Edward Campbell), 'The Headstrong Historian' (Nwamgba's granddaughter Grace/Afamefuna, an extension of Things Fall Apart), 'Cell One' (brother arrested after cult members steal a red Mercedes), 'The American Embassy' (woman in line after burying son Ugonna), the title story (second-person narrator wins a visa lottery), 'A Private Experience' (two women in an abandoned shop during a riot), and 'Birdsong' (affair remembered while stuck in traffic).

Controversy clues focus on her feud with Akwaeke Emezi and withdrawal of endorsement from Freshwater, her transphobic comments, and defense of J. K. Rowling. Her most recent novel, Dream Count (2025), about four immigrant women during the Covid-19 pandemic, has begun appearing in bonuses. She also wrote the play For Love of Biafra and the short story 'The Arrangements' about Donald Trump's wife.
