[B1] 2020 IKEA (2020) | Diff: 7 | Fine Arts
     Leadin: This artist's late black-and-white depictions of Biblical scenes include The Vision of Death and The Confusion of Tongues. For 10 points each:
  Part 1 (ans: Gustave Dore (Those are from his Divine Comedy series.)):
    Name this 19th century illustrator whose best-known series includes scenes of a couple gazing into the Empyrean and Virgil watching a man rise from a grave.
  Part 2 (ans: Eugene Delacroix (Those paintings are The Barque of Dante and The Women of Algiers.)):
    Like his contemporary Dore, this artist drew on the Divine Comedy for his first major work, in which Dante stands on a boat and looks over his shoulder at a burning city. In another of his paintings, a black servant at right attends to three women in a harem.
  Part 3 (ans: Auguste Rodin):
    This later French artist was inspired by Dante to sculpt his masterpiece The Gates of Hell.

[B2] 2017 Early Fall Tournament (EFT) (2017) | Diff: 7 | Fine Arts
     Leadin: Answer the following about Orientalism in 19th-century French art, for 10 points each.
  Part 1 (ans: Jean-Baptiste Auguste Dominique Ingres [ANG]):
    This Neoclassical French painter depicted a sensuous turbaned woman from a harem with far too many vertebrae in his iconic La Grande Odalisque.
  Part 2 (ans: The Women of Algiers in their Apartment):
    This painting by Eugene Delacroix shows a trio of figures sitting around a hookah with a black servant woman attending to them at right.
  Part 3 (ans: The Snake Charmer):
    This Jean-Leon Gerome painting, which is on the original cover of Edward Said's Orientalism, shows a nude woman with her back to the viewer holding an animal, with a blue wall forming the background.

[B3] 2015 NASAT (2015) | Diff: 8 | Fine Arts
     Leadin: A rendition of this painting sold for a record-breaking 179 million dollars at an auction in May 2015. For 10 points each:

    This artist of The Women of Algiers series painted five nude prostitutes, some of which have faces resembling African masks, in Les Demoiselles d'Avignon.

    Picasso also reinterpreted a painting of these people by Jacques-Louis David set in the backdrop of the Tarpeian Rock. In that painting, these people place their bodies in between two groups of shield- and spear-wielding soldiers.
