Stoner

Williams's most famous novel (1965) about William Stoner, a University of Missouri English professor who is inspired to study literature by Shakespeare's Sonnet 73. The protagonist has an affair with Kathrine Driscoll and feuds with the crippled colleague Hollis Lomax (aka Willy Lomax) for 20 years after savagely interrogating a blowhard student during a thesis defense. A 2018 Charles Shields biography called Williams 'The man who wrote the perfect novel.' The novel saw a dramatic rise in popularity in 2013 after being reissued by NYRB Classics, and Anna Gavalda's 2011 French translation helped make it a surprise bestseller in Europe.

Summary of Facts

John Williams was an American novelist and academic best known for Stoner (1965), a novel about William Stoner, a University of Missouri English professor. The clues identify it as one of the great rediscovered novels of American literature: a 2018 Charles Shields biography called Williams 'The man who wrote the perfect novel,' and the book saw a dramatic rise in popularity after its 2013 reissue by NYRB Classics. Anna Gavalda's French translation helped make it a surprise bestseller in Europe.

In Stoner, the protagonist is inspired to study literature by Shakespeare's Sonnet 73. He has an affair with Kathrine Driscoll and feuds with his crippled colleague Hollis Lomax (Willy Lomax) for twenty years after aggressively interrogating a student during Lomax's thesis defense.

Williams also wrote Butcher's Crossing, a frontier novel in which the protagonist returns to the title locale and hears McDonald say 'You get born, and you nurse on lies, and you wean on lies.' He sees a skinned buffalo that reminds him of a prostitute. His epistolary novel Augustus reconstructs the life of the Roman emperor through letters, with only the final few written by Octavian himself. His first novel, Nothing but the Night, was later disowned by the author.

Note: Many clue sets that mention 'John Williams' also reference the famous American film composer (Star Wars, Superman, Jaws) and the Australian classical guitarist, who are entirely different people.
