Hans Memling

← Home · View source questions · Make cards
German-born Flemish Renaissance painter based in Bruges, student of Rogier van der Weyden. Best known for The Last Judgment triptych (featuring gold-armored Archangel Michael weighing souls, stolen by pirates and taken to Gdansk), the Shrine of St. Ursula, and the Mystic Marriage of St. Catherine. Also painted the Donne Triptych, Scenes from the Passion of Christ, and the Triptych of Earthly Vanity and Divine Salvation.
The Last Judgment

Triptych depicting the Last Judgment. Commissioned by Angelo Tani, stolen at sea by the pirate Paul Beneke and taken to Gdansk. Center panel features gold-armored Archangel Michael weighing souls beneath a rainbow-seated Christ. Left panel: souls led up a crystal staircase to heaven. Right panel: sinners tumble into the inferno.

        Summary of Facts

        Hans Memling (c. 1430-1494) was a German-born painter of the Flemish Renaissance, based in Bruges. He was a student of Rogier van der Weyden and succeeded Petrus Christus as Bruges' most prominent painter.

        His most frequently clued work is The Last Judgment triptych, originally commissioned by Angelo Tani but stolen at sea by the pirate Paul Beneke and taken to Gdansk (Danzig), where it remains. The center panel features a gold-armored Archangel Michael weighing naked souls on a balance beneath Christ seated on a rainbow in red robes with feet on a sphere of gold, with a lily of mercy and red sword of justice above his head. The left panel shows angels on a Gothic facade leading saved souls up a crystal staircase, while the right depicts sinners tumbling into the inferno. Tommaso Portinari appears in the painting (carried by an angel with peacock wings or kneeling in a golden pan). When closed, it shows kneeling portraits of Tani and Caterina Tanagli with grisaille statues. Portinari may have later commissioned the Portinari Altarpiece from Hugo van der Goes because of this painting.

        The Shrine of St. Ursula is a miniature gilt reliquary shaped like a Gothic chapel, housed in Bruges, painted with six harbor scenes depicting the life of the Breton princess St. Ursula. His Mystic Marriage of St. Catherine (owned by the Met) shows the crowned saint kneeling next to a wheel and spike as Jesus places a ring on her finger. His Scenes from the Passion of Christ features 23 scenes set against a Jerusalem cityscape, with Tommaso Portinari and his wife flanking the bottom corners.

        Other works include the Donne Triptych (Sir John Donne of Kidwelly with musical angels and a red-clothed Virgin), the Triptych of Earthly Vanity and Divine Salvation (dead body, vain woman, and horned demon), the St. John Altarpiece (unusually using realistic color instead of grisaille), his namesake 'Memling carpets' (Orientalist rugs with hook patterns), and the Moreel Triptych. His Virgin and Child and Two Angels landscape was quoted by Fra Bartolomeo.
