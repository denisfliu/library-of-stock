Summary of Facts

Clifford Odets is an American playwright and member of the Group Theatre, best known for Waiting for Lefty and Awake and Sing! Waiting for Lefty (1935) consists of seven vignettes about cab drivers separated by blackouts, ending with Agate shouting "WE'RE STORMBIRDS OF THE WORKING-CLASS" as cab drivers chant "STRIKE!" after Lefty's body is found. Awake and Sing! depicts the Berger family in the Bronx, where grandfather Jacob jumps off the roof after his daughter Bessie smashes his Caruso records, and Hennie runs off with Moe Axelrod. Jacob advises his grandson to "fight so life shouldn't be printed on dollar bills." Golden Boy follows boxer Joe Bonaparte, who breaks his hand and ruins his violin career. Rocket to the Moon ends with Cleo Singer seeking "a whole full world, with all the trimmings." Odets named names to HUAC alongside Elia Kazan, and the Coen brothers modeled Barton Fink on him.
