Summary of Facts

Arno Schmidt (1914-1979) was a German experimental novelist best known for Bottom's Dream (Zettels Traum), a massive 1,334-page novel written using a unique orthography based on 'etyms' — units of words that supposedly reveal their author's subconscious thoughts. The novel follows three scholars and translators walking through fields, discussing the alcoholism of authors, with text divided between three columns. It draws heavily on Joyce, Poe, and Shakespeare. The main character is an aging translator of Edgar Allan Poe.
