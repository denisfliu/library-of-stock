Tossups

T1 2023 ah's modern poetry packets (2023) Diff 10 · Literature
This poet ended one poem by describing figures “of equal size” who “shout, dream, weep, sing”. That poem by this poet opens by describing “Shadows that only I can see”, and contrasts the title characters’ cries of (+) “I tire!” and “I am dying!”. In another poem, this poet described a figure with “eyes of glass”, who “coils on a stick” and “can move without feet” before instructing, “Hit him with an ax and he dies”. This poet repeatedly exclaimed (*) “Mayombe—bombe—mayombé” in a “Chant for Killing a Snake”. For 10 points, name this Cuban poet of “Ballad of the Two Grandfathers” and “Sensemayá”.
ANSWER: Nicolás Guillén [or Nicolás Cristóbal Guillén Batista]
T2 2023 Chicago Open (2023) Diff 10 · Other Academic
This thinker wrote an allegorical criticism of neoliberalism featuring a knight-errant beetle named Don Durito. Following backlash, the US National Endowment for the Arts canceled funding for a children’s book about the Mayan creation myth by this thinker entitled The Story of the Colors. In 2006, this thinker went on a rare public tour of his country as part of The Other Campaign, during which he called himself (*) Delegate Zero. To honor a murdered teacher, this thinker announced that he was “ceasing to exist” in 2014, adopting the new pseudonym “Galeano.” Born Rafael Sebastián Guillén Vicente, this thinker was a philosophy professor at UAM before debuting as his most famous persona during a 1994 uprising against NAFTA. For 10 points, a black ski mask and a pipe are the trademarks of what pseudonymous figurehead of the Zapatistas?
ANSWER: Subcomandante Marcos [accept Rafael Sebastián Guillén Vicente until read; accept Subcomandante Galeano until “Galeano” is read]
T3 2021 Illinois Open (2021) Diff 10 · Literature
In a poem by an author from this group, the speaker sees an old man being “slowly borne off in the sun’s last rays, like so many other invisible things in the world.” A poet from this group wrote of a man who “wants to storm the bank, steal clouds, stars, golden comets, and to buy what is most difficult: the sky” in his poem “The Covetous Angel,” from his collection Concerning the Angels. A member of this group wrote “the horseman came nearer drumming across the plain” in the opening poem of a collection, which warns: “Run, (*) moon, moon, moon!” The author of Destruction or Love invoked his memory of being in this group upon being told he had won the 1977 Nobel Prize. A poet wrote a collection of ballads inspired by Roma people a year after convening with this group on the 300th anniversary of Luis de Gongora’s death. For 10 points, Rafael Alberti, Vicente Aleixandre, and Federico Garcia Lorca belonged to what circle of Spanish poets named for a year?
ANSWER: Generation of ‘27 [or Generation of 1927; or Generación del 27; or Generation of 1925; or Generación del 25; or the Generation of the Dictatorship; or Generation of Avant-Gardes; or Generation of Friendship; accept Generation Guillén-Lorca before “Lorca”]
T4 2015 Chicago Open History (2015) Diff 10 · History
In 2005, this man invited Inter Milan to play a game of soccer against members of his organization, refereed by Diego Maradona, but the game didn’t end up happening. A toucan narrates the story of the gods’ invention of colors in a folktale for children written by this man. He considers the Cold War to be the Third World War and the struggle between capitalism and socialism to be the Fourth World War. He was referred to as “Delegate Zero” during a campaign based on the ideals set forth in the Sixth Declaration of the (*) Lacandon Jungle. This man traveled around his country in an anti-capitalist program called the Other Campaign. In 2014, this pipe-smoking, mask-wearing figure announced that he no longer existed. For 10 points, name this former spokesman of the Zapatista National Liberation Army.
ANSWER: Subcomandante (Insurgente) Marcos (or Subcomandante Insurgente Galeano; prompt on “Rafael Sebastián Guillén Vicente”)

Bonuses

B1 2025 Chicago Open (2025) Diff 10 · Literature
A boy of this ethnicity is told, “Charity’s payin’ yo’ keep, / She’s givin’ you all you need” in the first poem of a collection titled for motifs in this ethnic group’s music. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this ethnic group. A poet from this ethnic minority imitated its music with onomatopoeia exemplified by the collection title Sóngoro cosongo and the line “Mayombe-bombe-mayombé” from a “chant to kill a snake.”
ANSWER: Afro-Cubans [or Black Cubans or afrocubanos; prompt on Black or Cuban or negro; reject “Yoruba”] (The poems are “Negro bembón,” from the collection Motivos de son, and “Sensemayá.” Both are by Nicolás Guillén.)
[10] This Afro-Cuban poet and scholar of Nicolás Guillén (“ghee-YEN”) began successive stanzas “I rebelled,” “I walked,” and “I rose up” in her poem “Mujer negra,” or “Black Woman.”
ANSWER: Nancy Morejón
[10] In her poem “Cimarrones,” Morejón describes how it “dignifies our struggle / to go out and see us … sweeping shit / birthing [these things].” “Guantanamera” was adapted from a José Martí collection titled [these things] Sencillos (“sen-SEE-yohs”).
ANSWER: verses [accept versos; accept Simple Verses or Versos sencillos]
B2 2024 NASAT (2024) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
Note to players: Two answers required. A form popular in these two countries is based on a rhythm called the baqueteo. For 10 points each:
[10] Name these two countries. A poem from one of these two countries about killing a snake inspired a composer from the other to write a piece beginning with a bass clarinet trill in 4/8 time.
ANSWER: Mexico AND Cuba [or United Mexican States AND Republic of Cuba; or Estados Unidos Mexicanos AND República de Cuba] [The poem is Nicolás Guillén's Sensemayá, which inspired a piece of the same title by Silvestre Revueltas.]
[10] The baqueteo forms the basis for this musical form popular in Cuba and Mexico, which developed from the contradanza. Gustavo Dudamel popularized a piece called "[this musical form] No. 2" by Mexican composer Arturo Marquez.
ANSWER: danzón [or Danzón No. 2; prompt on dance]
[10] This American composer wrote a piece for two pianos called Danzón Cubano and imitated the jarabe dance in El Salón México. He also wrote Appalachian Spring.
ANSWER: Aaron Copland
B3 2022 CMST (2022) Diff 9 · Literature
Two collections of Selected Poems, respectively translated by Doris Dana and Langston Hughes, appeared after this poet’s 1957 death. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this romantic partner of Dana. Hughes’s book opens with “Cradle Songs” drawn from this poet’s Desolación.
ANSWER: Gabriela Mistral [or Lucila Godoy Alcayaga]
[10] “Li’l Manuel” is critiqued for “all data English you used to know” in Hughes’s Black vernacular translation of a poem from this author’s Motives de son. Eartha Kitt recorded a reading of Hughes’s translation of this other Black poet’s “Chant for Killing a Snake.”
ANSWER: Nicolas Guillen [or Nicolas Cristobal Guillen Batista] (The latter poem is Sensemaya.)
[10] Hughes translated Masters of the Dew after the death of author Jacques Roumain, a Marxist from this home country of Edwidge Danticat (“dah-tee-KAH”). A revolution in this non-Cuban country is the setting for Alejo Carpentier’s The Kingdom of This World.
ANSWER: (Republic of) Haiti [or République d’Haïti; or Repiblik d Ayiti]
