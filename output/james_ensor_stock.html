Summary of Facts

James Ensor was a Belgian painter known for grotesque imagery featuring masks and skeletons. His masterpiece, Christ's Entry Into Brussels in 1889, shows a procession of mask wearers under a banner reading 'Vive La Sociale' largely ignoring Christ on a donkey. It was kept rolled up for 30 years and is now at the Getty Museum. A roundel depicting the crucifixion hangs above figures with bloody hands, and frogs jump from a black cauldron. Ensor wears a red feathered hat in the crowd.

Skeletons Fighting Over a Pickled Herring shows two skeletons in a tug-of-war over a herring; Ensor represents himself as the fish, mocking his critics. He was a member of Les XX (Les Vingt), formed partly due to rejection of his Oyster Eaters by the L'Essor group. His mother Marie owned a curio shop whose oriental vases and dolls appear in his paintings. Other works include The Dangerous Cooks (depicting Octave Maus and Edmond Picard) and The Seven Deadly Sins Dominated by Death. He painted a self-portrait imagining himself at age 100, menaced by a spider.
