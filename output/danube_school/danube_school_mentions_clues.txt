[BM1] 2019 NASAT (2019) | Diff: 8 | Fine Arts
     Leadin: A Renaissance painting reimagines this artist's lost work The Calumny, which shows King Midas extending his hand towards Slander as an innocent man is dragged towards him. For 10 points each:
  Part 1 (ans: Apelles of Kos):
    Name this artist who, along with Zeuxis, was the foremost painter of Ancient Greece. Raphael may have painted himself as this artist in The School of Athens, and this artist's few known works include the Aphrodite Anadyomene.
  Part 2 (ans: Sandro Botticelli [or Alessandro di Mariano di Vanni Filipepi]):
    This Renaissance artist painted that Calumny of Apelles, which reuses a similar figure to the female nude at the centre of his Birth of Venus. He also painted La Primavera.
  Part 3 (ans: Alexander the Great [or Alexander III of Macedon]):
    In one work, Giovanni Tiepolo painted this man and Campaspe in the studio of Apelles. A large banner floats in the air in a Danube School painting titled for this man.

[BM2] 2015 Minnesota Undergraduate Tournament (MUT) (2015) | Diff: 6 | Fine Arts
     Leadin: The Heidelberg School in Australia produced many impressionist works in this genre, which was also the favored subject matter of the Four Wangs and the rest of the Chinese shan shui tradition. For 10 points each:
  Part 1 (ans: landscape paintings [or landscape art]):
    Identify this genre of painting which was popularized in England by, among others, John Constable, whose works in the form include Wivenhoe Park, Dedham Vale, and many other depictions of the Suffolk area.
  Part 2 (ans: Frederic Edwin Church):
    This member of the Hudson River School made many trips to the Arctic and South America, resulting in such landscapes as Aurora Borealis, The Icebergs, Cotopaxi, and The Heart of the Andes.
  Part 3 (ans: Albrecht Altdorfer):
    This member of the Danube school created one of the first pure landscapes in oil, his 1520 Landscape with Footbridge. The contemporaneous "world landscape" style influenced his famous Battle of Alexander at Issus.

[BM3] 2012 College History Bowl (2012) | Diff: 9 | Fine Arts
     Leadin: This member of the Danube School painted several works celebrating Regensburg's expulsion of the Jews in 1519. For 10 points each:
  Part 1 (ans: Albrecht Altdorfer):
    Identify this artist best known for a battle scene commissioned by Wilhelm IV of Bavaria where the sun sets over the Nile on the right and a crescent moon is about to disappear in the top left.
  Part 2 (ans: Alexander the Great [or Alexander III]):
    That Altdorfer painting showed this general's victory over Darius at the Battle of Issus. This military leader founded a city in Egypt and also laid siege to Tyre.
  Part 3 (ans: Lucas Cranach the Elder [or Lucas Cranach der Altere]):
    Altdorfer made copies of some paintings by this German artist, best known for his depiction of The Judgment of Paris and his portraits of Martin Luther.
