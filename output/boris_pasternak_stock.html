Summary of Facts

Boris Pasternak is a 20th-century Russian poet and novelist, overwhelmingly identified in quizbowl by his novel Doctor Zhivago and the poetry collection My Sister, Life.

Doctor Zhivago is the most frequently clued work. It tells the story of the physician and poet Yuri Zhivago and his love for the nurse Lara during the Russian Revolution. Lara was tormented by a sexual relationship she was pressured into by the corrupt lawyer Viktor Komarovsky, who once worked for Yuri's father Andrei; Lara shot Komarovsky at a Christmas party. Lara is married to Pasha Antipov, and Yuri goes to Yuriatin to meet her. For eighteen months, Zhivago is held captive by Red Partisans from the Forest Brotherhood, led by the Bolshevik Liberius. In the epilogue, Nika and Mischa (fighting in the Red Army) meet Tania, Yuri and Lara's daughter, who is a day laborer protected by Yuri's half-brother Evgraf. Twenty-five poems are placed at the end of the novel, framed as Yuri's work, including 'Hamlet' (which ends with 'to live a life is not to cross a field'), 'Winter Night' (which repeats 'The candle burns upon the desk, the candle burns'), 'Miracle,' and 'The Garden of Gethsemane.' The novel was the first full-length novel shared via samizdat, and the CIA bought and distributed thousands of copies to make Pasternak a Nobel Prize contender. The Soviet Union forced Pasternak to decline the 1958 Nobel Prize in Literature, and Pablo Neruda was widely criticized for failing to condemn this.

My Sister, Life (Sestra moya — zhizn) is Pasternak's most famous poetry collection. Its title poem describes life 'overflowing today, / spring rain shattering itself like glass' and compares 'the timetable on the Kamyshin line' to the Bible. The collection opens with 'In Memory of the Demon' and closes with 'The End,' which asks 'Is it all real? Is it time now to drift away?' It includes the garden and mirror cycles.

Other frequently clued poems include 'February' ('February. Take ink and weep'), 'In the Wood' / 'In the Woods' (meadows 'blurred by a lilac heat' where 'cathedral shadows swirled'), and his inaugural collection Twin in the Clouds, published through the Moscow Futurist group Tsentrifuga. He also wrote On Early Trains and The Last Summer, and produced translations of Shakespeare plays into contemporary Russian.

Biographically, Pasternak was a correspondent of Marina Tsvetaeva (who wanted to name her son after him) and exchanged letters with Rainer Maria Rilke in the summer of 1926. Osip Mandelstam recited his famous 'Stalin Epigram' at Pasternak's house. Pasternak was associated with the Russian Futurist group Tsentrifuga early in his career.
