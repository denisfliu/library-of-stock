Questions: Nam June Paik

3 tossups · 8 bonuses

Tossups

T1 2025 Penn Bowl (2025) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
One of this artist’s Playable Pieces, which instructs the performer to "cut your left forearm a distance of ten centimeters” was performed by composer Joseph Byrd. One of this visual artist’s installations was displayed during New Year’s Day 1984 and featured music from Peter Gabriel, Laurie Anderson, and John Cage. In addition to creating Good Morning, Mr. Orwell, this man collaborated with Christine Moorman on (*) TV Brafor Living Sculpture. This performance artist depicted Buddhist themes and religious iconography in his installations Portable God and TV Buddha. This man created a map of the United States with neon tubing, TV sets, and DVD players in his installation Electronic Superhighway. For 10 points, name this South Korean Fluxus member and video art founder.
ANSWER: Nam June Paik
T2 2018 Human Use of Human Beings (2018) Diff 10 · Other Academic
This man imagined a world 26 years from now where "audio cassettes, telex, data pooling" and other technologies would contribute to a "broadband communication network". Problems with a satellite link to France disrupted a "space yodel" this man planned for an event hosted by George Plimpton on New Year's Day in 1984. Charlotte Moorman was an assistant to this man. He placed a magnet on top of his signature devices to generate abstract forms. In his report "Media Planning for the Postindustrial Society" he coined the term "electronic (*) super highway". He had a breakthrough moment during Pope Paul VI's arrival in New York City, aided by his recently purchased Sony Portapak. Scenes from The Wizard of Oz play in the Kansas screen of one of his works, which consists of a map of the US made from some 300 TV screens. For 10 points, name this Korean-American video artist.
ANSWER: Nam June Paik
T3 2016 ACF Nationals (2016) Diff 9 · Fine Arts
This artist proposed that an “acoustic space” could be created if you put “all the politicians from the world ... inside a circle” and activated “23 machine guns at the same time.” A piece of “danger music” by this artist instructs the player to “climb inside the vagina of a live female whale.” The journalist George Plimpton appeared as a host in a work by this artist in which John Cage’s “prepared cactus” was broadcast. Another work by this one-time member of Fluxus features shots from The Wizard of Oz and uses neon lights to depict Alaska, Hawaii, and the continental US. This artist of Good Morning, Mr. Orwell often collaborated with Charlotte Moorman, for whom he designed a “cello bra” whose “cups” are screens. A 1974 proposal he wrote contained an early use of the phrase "electronic super highway". The idea that televisions would link the world together drove the work of, for 10 points, what South Korean artist?
ANSWER: Nam June Paik [or Baek Namjun; or Paek Namjun]
