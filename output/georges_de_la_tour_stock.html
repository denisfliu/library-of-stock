Summary of Facts

Georges de la Tour was a 17th-century French Baroque painter based in Luneville, patronized by Louis XIII, known for chiaroscuro interior scenes lit by a single candle. His works were largely forgotten until a 1915 article by Herman Voss.

The Penitent Magdalene shows a woman with a skull on her lap staring into a mirror reflecting candlelight (the 'Smoking Flame' version appeared in The Little Mermaid). The Cheat exists in two versions (Ace of Diamonds and Ace of Clubs), showing three people colluding against a foppish youth as a card sharp retrieves a card from his belt.

The Fortune Teller shows a boy having his purse and watch stolen while his fortune is read (center of a forgery controversy at the Met). The Musician's Brawl features hurdy-gurdy players fighting, with one squeezing lemon juice into a beggar's eye. Other works include Joseph the Carpenter, St. Sebastian Tended by St. Irene, The Newborn Christ, and The Flea Catcher. He was heavily influenced by Caravaggio and the Caravaggisti, especially Gerrit van Honthorst.
