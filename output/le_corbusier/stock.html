Swiss-French architect (born Charles-Edouard Jeanneret), pioneer of the International Style and modern architecture. Best known for the Villa Savoye, planning the city of Chandigarh with cousin Pierre Jeanneret, and his treatise Towards a New Architecture. Developed the Five Points of Architecture (pilotis, roof gardens, free plan, free facade, ribbon windows), the Modulor proportional system, and the Ville Radieuse urban concept. Co-founded Purism with Amedee Ozenfant. Called a house 'a machine for living in.'
Villa Savoye (1931)

Modernist house in Poissy, outside Paris, exemplifying Le Corbusier's Five Points of Architecture. A concrete 'machine for living' raised on pilotis with roof gardens, free facade, and ribbon windows. Ground floor curved to fit a 1927 Citroen's turning radius. Wikipedia

Summary of Facts

Le Corbusier (Charles-Edouard Jeanneret) is arguably the single most frequently asked architect in quiz bowl. He appears as both a direct answerline and as a common mention across architecture, urban planning, and art movement questions. His career spans from early Purist painting to monumental late-career brutalist works.

The Villa Savoye (1931) in Poissy, outside Paris, is Le Corbusier's signature building and the default giveaway clue. It exemplifies his Five Points of Architecture: pilotis (concrete stilts raising the building off the ground), roof gardens, free plan, free facade, and ribbon windows. Quiz clues consistently describe it as a concrete 'machine for living' raised on pilotis, with a ground floor curved to accommodate a car's turning radius (specifically a 1927 Citroen). A darker 'Southern Hemisphere shadow' version in Canberra was designed by Ashton Raggatt MacDougall.

Chandigarh, the planned capital of Punjab and Haryana in India, is Le Corbusier's second most-clued topic. He designed the city with cousin Pierre Jeanneret, along with Maxwell Fry and Jane Drew, based on his Ville Radieuse (Radiant City) concept and CIAM planning principles. The Capitol Complex includes the Palace of Assembly (featuring a truncated hyperboloid roof), the High Court (with brise soleils), the Secretariat Building, and the 85-foot-tall Open Hand Monument (a multi-colored sheet metal sculpture that rotates in the wind, intended as 'a sign of peace and reconciliation'). He also designed the Tower of Shadows, a three-sided cubic structure with perforated walls demonstrating sun control.

Towards a New Architecture (Vers une architecture, 1923) is his foundational treatise, containing the Five Points of Architecture and the famous declaration that 'a house is a machine for living in.' The book includes a chapter titled 'Eyes that do not See' and identifies the Parthenon as a 'machine for stirring emotion.' The authorship was disputed by Amedee Ozenfant, who claimed credit since its constituent essays were published under a shared pseudonym in the journal L'Esprit Nouveau.

The Modulor is Le Corbusier's proportional system based on the height of a six-foot-tall man with a raised hand, incorporating the golden ratio. It appears in mid-to-late clues and was applied to furniture design (LC series chairs appear in Spy x Family volume covers).

The Ville Radieuse (Radiant City) was his unrealized ideal city plan featuring identical housing blocks, green spaces on a Cartesian grid, and cross-shaped skyscrapers connected by greenery. Its principles influenced Lucio Costa and Oscar Niemeyer's design of Brasilia. The related Plan Voisin (named after an automobile maker) would have demolished central Paris to install 18 cruciform towers.

Le Corbusier co-founded Purism with Amedee Ozenfant, writing the manifesto Apres le Cubisme. Purism was a less-decorative offshoot of Cubism that sought to depict objects in simplified form.

The Notre Dame du Haut chapel at Ronchamp features a curved roof resembling an airplane wing that lets in a crack of light. The Philips Pavilion, designed with Iannis Xenakis for the 1958 World Expo, featured nine hyperbolic paraboloids and the electronic composition Concret PH. He also collaborated with Xenakis on the Convent of Sainte Marie de La Tourette.

Other notable works include the Unite d'Habitation (concrete housing block incorporating terrace gardens and pilotis), the League of Nations design (rejected for not being in Indian ink), the Tsentrosoyuz building in Moscow, the National Museum of Western Art in Tokyo (his easternmost building), the Mill Owners' Building and Sarabhai House (designed with mentee Balkrishna Doshi), and co-designing the UN Headquarters with Oscar Niemeyer. He oversaw the Ministry of Education and Health in Rio de Janeiro.

Le Corbusier is consistently identified as Swiss-French (or Swiss-born), and his real name Charles-Edouard Jeanneret is accepted on answerlines. He was a member of CIAM (International Congress of Modern Architecture) and the Athens Charter was based on his Radiant City concept. Jane Jacobs criticized his urban planning theories. He reportedly carried his wife's vertebrae around after she died.
