T1 2025 The Pyramid (2025) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
The unassuming white facade of a house named for this family distinguishes it from style sapin buildings in La Chaux-de-Fonds like the Villa Fallet. One member of this family designed teakwood chairs with “horned” legs that fill a university whose library features red brick on a facade with concrete brise soleil. Two designers from this family won the jury prize to design the Palace of Nations but were passed up for a team of more conservative architects. A house named for this family is conjoined to the Villa La Roche and now hosts a foundation for one member. Two members of this family collaborated on the Pavilion of the Esprit Nouveau with Amedee Ozenfant as part of their theory of Purism and later reunited to plan and build the city of Chandigarh. For 10 points, name this family of architects that includes cousins Pierre and Charles-Edouard, who usually went by Le Corbusier.
ANSWER: Jeanneret [or Jeanneret-Gris; or Jeanneret-Perret; accept Charles-Edouard Jeanneret, Pierre Jeanneret, Albert Jeanneret, Georges Edouard Jeanneret, Marie Jeanneret-Perret; accept Villa Jeanneret-Perret or Villa Jeanneret; prompt on Le Corbusier before “Le Corbusier”]

T7 2013 DRAGOON (2013) Diff 7 · Fine Arts
This theorist identified the Parthenon as an example of selection applied to an established standard, and argued that standards were necessary to face the ‘Problem of Perfection.’ Along with Amedee Ozenfant, this theorist co-wrote the Purist manifesto Apres le Cubisme. He emphasized the use of pilotis to suspend buildings off the ground as a hallmark of his style, and the largest of his Open Hand sculptures is in the (*) Indian city of Chandigarh. He also designed a system of proportions based on the human body which he called the Modulor. ANSWER: Le Corbusier [or Charles Edouard Jeanneret]
