Tossups

T1 2025 Spec Script (2025) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
Early in his career, this theorist drew on commedia dell’arte tropes to stage the play Enough Stupidity in Every Wise Man. In a lecture series, this theorist insisted that Paul Robeson should wield a candelabrum to star in a film about Jean-Jacques Dessalines (“day-sah-LEEN”). This theorist remarked that “movies were boiling” in the kettle described in the opening line of The Cricket in the Hearth, in an essay positing that Charles Dickens was the strongest influence on D. W. Griffith. In an essay on cinema’s “fourth dimension,” this theorist analyzed the emotions induced by haiku and (*) kabuki. This theorist directed a film that inspired the name of an arts journal founded by Rosalind Krauss and Annette Michelson. This theorist used the terms “intellectual,” “overtonal,” “tonal,” “rhythmic,” and “metric” to describe the five types of a concept he developed with peers like Vsevolod Pudovkin. For 10 points, what theorist edited the “Odessa steps” montage in his film Battleship Potemkin?
ANSWER: Sergei Eisenstein [or Sergei Mikhailovich Eisenstein] (The journal October is named after Eisenstein’s film October: Ten Days That Shook the World.)
T2 2025 MRNA (2025) Diff 7 · Fine Arts
To end a film, this director created a shot in which a ruler’s bearded head appears to tower over a parade demanding his return in the background. To end another film, this director cut between graphic footage of cows being slaughtered with soldiers firing on crowds of people in a muddy field. In 1946, this director died of a heart attack after he chose not to revise the second part of a trilogy deemed “anti-historical” by the Central Committee. A bishop of the (*) Teutonic Order wears a hat with a swastika in a film by this director that restored his political reputation by dramatizing the Battle of the Ice. In a film by this director, a baby in a carriage is let loose during the Odessa Steps sequence. For 10 points, name this Soviet pioneer of montage who directed Alexander Nevsky and Battleship Potemkin.
ANSWER: Sergei Eisenstein

In a scene by a director from this country that inspired a Boogie Nights shot, the camera follows a bikini contest participant underwater in a rooftop swimming pool. Charlie Chaplin helped a director from this country meet Upton and Mary Sinclair to make the unfinished epic film ¡Que viva México! T4 2022 Chicago Open (2022) Diff 10 · Religion
A man with this surname used both Hebrew and Ladino sources to produce the first English translation of the Me’am Lo’ez (meh-AM lo-EZ) and included many maps and diagrams in a 1981 English translation of the Pentateuch titled The Living Torah. A man with this surname defined God as “the power that makes for salvation” as part of his theology of transnaturalism and coined the phrase “the past has a vote, but not a veto.” Together with Eugene Kohn, that man with this surname edited a Sabbath prayer book that used the word “drawn” instead of “chosen,” prompting his (*) excommunication by the Union of Orthodox Rabbis. That man with this surname held the first American bat mitzvah for his daughter Judith, who later married his student Ira Eisenstein, and argued that Jews needed to adapt their practices to modern society in Judaism as a Civilization. For 10 points, give the surname of the rabbi who founded the Reconstructionist movement.
ANSWER: Kaplan [accept Aryeh Moshe Eliyahu Kaplan or Leonard Kaplan or Judith Kaplan Eisenstein or Mordecai Menahem Kaplan or Mottel Kaplan]
T5 2017 NASAT (2017) Diff 8 · Science
A sieve (siv) with this name introduced by Pomerance outperforms the general number field sieve in factoring integers with fewer than 100 digits. The Eisenstein integers are part of this type of field extension over the rationals; the elements of that extension take the form a-plus-b-omega. Functions described by this adjective correspond to the shaded cells on diagonal lines in the prime number-depicting Ulam (OO-lom) spiral; one of those functions described by this adjective outputs a prime for its first 40 positive integer inputs. Al-Khwarizmi found the general solution to this type of equation by completing the square, and the solutions are real when the discriminant is positive. For 10 points, identify this term for polynomials of degree two.
ANSWER: quadratic [prompt on polynomial until "Eisenstein" is read]

B2 2024 PLAYTIME! (2024) Diff 7 · Science
Newton’s identities give rearrangement formulae for the elementary symmetric variety of these functions. For 10 points each:

ANSWER: polynomials
[10] By Gauss’s lemma, a primitive polynomial is irreducible over the integers if and only if it is irreducible over these numbers. A namesake theorem constrains roots in this set in terms of a polynomial’s first and last coefficients.
ANSWER: rational numbers [or rationals; accept Q]
[10] Another sufficient criterion for the irreducibility of polynomials over the rational numbers is given by this German mathematician. Gauss apocryphally rated this mathematician on par with Newton and Archimedes.
ANSWER: Gotthold Eisenstein [or Ferdinand Gotthold Max Eisenstein]

B4 2024 COOT (2024) Diff 8 · Social Science
This film theorist discussed Kabuki theatre in his essay The Filmic Fourth Dimension. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this film theorist who developed five ‘methods of montage’. In their collection of essays Film Form, this theorist stated that ‘montage is conflict’.
ANSWER: Sergei Eisenstein
[10] Eisenstein (“EYE-zuhn-shtyne”) was a pioneer of Soviet montage theory, which emphasised this general part of post-production. Basic techniques in this field include shot/reverse shot, cuts, and transitions.
ANSWER: editing
[10] The theoretical basis of Soviet montage cinema is this effect, whose namesake invented ‘creative geography’. In this effect, viewers derive more meaning from two sequential shots interacting than a single isolated one.
ANSWER: Kuleshov effect
