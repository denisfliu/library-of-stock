Art movement of contemporary provocateurs from the United Kingdom, including Damien Hirst, Tracey Emin, Chris Ofili, Yinka Shonibare, Sarah Lucas, Jenny Saville, the Chapman Brothers, Rachel Whiteread, Sam Taylor-Johnson, Michael Landy, Gary Hume. Patronized by Charles Saatchi. Key exhibitions: Freeze (1988, curated by Hirst) and Sensation (1997). Opposed by the Stuckist movement (Billy Childish). Jay Jopling founded the White Cube gallery.

Summary of Facts

The Young British Artists (YBA) are a movement of provocative contemporary British artists who emerged in the late 1980s, patronized by Charles Saatchi and centered around key exhibitions: Freeze (1988, curated by Damien Hirst, also featuring Fiona Rae and Sarah Lucas) and Sensation (1997 at the Royal Academy). Jay Jopling founded the White Cube gallery to host their shows. The Stuckist movement, created by Billy Childish, arose in opposition. Multiple members were controversially nominated for or won the Turner Prize.

Damien Hirst is the most prominent member, famous for preserving a shark in formaldehyde (The Physical Impossibility of Death in the Mind of Someone Living), covering a skull in diamonds (For the Love of God), his polka dot paintings (some burned for an NFT project in 2022), and butterfly works including In and Out of Love. Tracey Emin is known for My Bed (disheveled bed with bloody underwear and used condoms, jumped on by the performance duo Mad for Real), Everyone I Have Ever Slept With 1963-1995 (blue tent with 102 names including Billy Childish and her grandmother), and neon text works.

Chris Ofili's Holy Virgin Mary used elephant dung and collaged female genitalia in a depiction of a bare-breasted Black Madonna that infuriated Rudy Giuliani. His The Upper Room features 13 paintings of rhesus macaque monkeys in a walnut-paneled room designed by David Adjaye, controversially purchased by the Tate in 2005. Yinka Shonibare uses Dutch wax/Ankara fabric for headless mannequins, recreating works like Fragonard's The Swing and Gainsborough's Mr. and Mrs. Andrews; he also created Nelson's Ship in a Bottle for the Fourth Plinth of Trafalgar Square.

Other members include Sarah Lucas (food-themed self-portraits: Eating a Banana, Self-Portrait with Fried Eggs), Jenny Saville (large paintings of obese nude women; Propped with mirror-written Luce Irigaray text), the Chapman Brothers (Exquisite Corpse series), Sam Taylor-Johnson (time-lapse still life decay videos), Michael Landy (who had workers destroy all his possessions), Gary Hume (Snowman), and Rachel Whiteread.
