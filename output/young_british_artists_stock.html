General

Movement of provocative contemporary British artists. Key members: Damien Hirst (shark in formaldehyde, diamond skull For the Love of God), Tracey Emin (My Bed, Everyone I Have Ever Slept With tent), Chris Ofili (Holy Virgin Mary with elephant dung, The Upper Room), Yinka Shonibare (Nelson's Ship in a Bottle), Sarah Lucas (food self-portraits), Jenny Saville (large nudes of obese women), Sam Taylor-Johnson, the Chapman Brothers, Rachel Whiteread, Michael Landy, Gary Hume. Patronized by Charles Saatchi. Exhibitions: Freeze (1988, curated by Hirst), Sensation (1997, at Royal Academy). Jay Jopling founded White Cube gallery. Opposed by Stuckist movement (Billy Childish). Summary of Facts

The Young British Artists (YBA) are a movement of provocative contemporary British artists who emerged in the late 1980s, patronized by Charles Saatchi and centered around exhibitions like Freeze (1988, curated by Damien Hirst) and Sensation (1997).

Damien Hirst is the most well-known member, famous for preserving a shark in formaldehyde (The Physical Impossibility of Death in the Mind of Someone Living), covering a skull in diamonds (For the Love of God), and his polka dot paintings. Tracey Emin is known for My Bed (disheveled bed with bloody underwear, jumped on by the duo Mad for Real) and Everyone I Have Ever Slept With 1963-1995 (tent with names appliqued inside). Chris Ofili's Holy Virgin Mary used elephant dung and angered Rudy Giuliani; his The Upper Room features 13 paintings of rhesus macaques.

Other members include Yinka Shonibare (headless mannequins in Ankara fabric), Sarah Lucas (food-themed self-portraits), Jenny Saville (large nudes of obese women, Propped with mirror-written Luce Irigaray text), the Chapman Brothers, Rachel Whiteread, Sam Taylor-Johnson, Michael Landy, and Gary Hume. Jay Jopling founded the White Cube gallery. The Stuckist movement (Billy Childish) was created to oppose them. Multiple members were controversially nominated for or won the Turner Prize.
