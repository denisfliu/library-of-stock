Tossups

T1 2022 MUSES III (2022) Diff 10 · Literature
In Muhammad and the Empires of Faith, Sean Anthony argued that the story of this poet’s life was based directly on accounts of Muhammad laid out by Ibn Ishaq. The use of the word “scōp” in a work by this poet has led to debates over whether the central figure is creating the world analogously to poetry. This poet’s most famous work ends by describing how the (+) Lord created “Heaven as a roof” and “the middle-earth, the Guardian of mankind” for humanity, and it opens by declaring “Now we ought to praise the Guardian of the heavenly kingdom.” According to tradition, this poet composed his most famous work in a (*) dream vision while sleeping in a stable at Hild of Whitby’s monastery. In the Ecclesiastical History of the English People, the Venerable Bede claimed that this poet was an illiterate and musically untrained cow-herd who was then blessed with divine inspiration. For 10 points, name this Anglo-Saxon poet, the author of a hymn that is the oldest extant piece of Old English poetry.
ANSWER: Caedmon
