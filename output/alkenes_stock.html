Alkenes

← Home · View source questions · Make cards
Unsaturated hydrocarbons containing at least one carbon-carbon double bond (also called olefins). Simplest example is ethylene. General formula CnH2n. Contain sp2-hybridized carbons.

Summary of Facts

Alkenes (olefins) are unsaturated hydrocarbons containing at least one carbon-carbon double bond with sp2-hybridized carbons. The most commonly clued aspects are: (1) Named reactions — Grubbs/Schrock metathesis is the most frequent, followed by Zaitsev's rule for elimination selectivity, McMurry coupling, and Wittig/HWE/Peterson olefinations. (2) Addition reactions — Markovnikov's rule (HX/water), anti-Markovnikov hydroboration-oxidation, bromine decolorization test, Sharpless dihydroxylation with OsO4, oxymercuration, and epoxidation. (3) Organometallic chemistry — the Dewar-Chatt-Duncanson model describes alkene-metal bonding, Zeise's salt is the classic example, and Ziegler-Natta catalysts polymerize alkenes. (4) Diels-Alder — alkenes act as dienophiles. (5) E-Z isomerism from restricted rotation. (6) Industrial — ethylene as plant hormone, hydroformylation, Wacker process, conductive polymers (2000 Nobel). Power clues tend toward specific named reactions (Peterson, Julia, Still-Gennari), the Wacker process, and applications like the 2000 Nobel.
