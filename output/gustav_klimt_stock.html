Summary of Facts

Gustav Klimt was an Austrian Symbolist painter and the founding member of the Vienna Secession, most commonly identified by The Kiss and the Beethoven Frieze. His 'golden phase' featured extensive use of gold leaf.

The Kiss depicts a couple in golden robes embracing on a flowered cliff, with the man's garment decorated with rectangles and the woman's with circles. The Beethoven Frieze is a massive work (7 feet tall, 112 feet wide) covering three walls of the Secession Building, depicting Beethoven's Ninth Symphony. It features the ape-like giant Typhoeus and his three Gorgon daughters as hostile forces, floating Genii, and a knight in gold armor. It was created for the 14th Vienna Secessionist exhibition alongside Max Klinger's bare-chested Beethoven sculpture.

Klimt painted two portraits of Adele Bloch-Bauer. The first (1907), also called 'Woman in Gold,' exemplifies his gold phase with a dress decorated with eyes. It was stolen by the Nazis and reclaimed by Maria Altmann in Republic of Austria vs. Altmann, briefly selling for $135 million (then a record). It is now at the Neue Galerie; the second portrait is at MoMA. His Danae shows a nude red-haired woman curled in the fetal position, impregnated by Zeus's golden rain, and is his only mythological subject besides Pallas Athene.

The Stoclet Frieze for the Palais Stoclet in Brussels (Josef Hoffmann's most famous building) features the Tree of Life with spiral motifs and panels The Expectation and The Embrace. His University of Vienna ceiling paintings (Philosophy, Medicine, Jurisprudence) caused scandal and were destroyed by fire at Schloss Immendorf. Schubert at the Piano, commissioned by Nikolaus Dumba, was also destroyed.

Klimt mentored Egon Schiele and Oskar Kokoschka. He painted square-shaped landscapes of Lake Attersee, Birch Forest, The Park (canopy of colored dots), Hope I and II (pregnant model Herma), Judith (Adele Bloch-Bauer as model), Pallas Athene, Three Ages of Woman, and a portrait of partner Emilie Floge. Ver Sacrum was the Secession's magazine. The Secession Building was designed by Joseph Maria Olbrich, nicknamed the 'Golden Cabbage,' with owl decorations by Koloman Moser. Roses Under the Trees was restituted to the Stiasny family in 2022.
