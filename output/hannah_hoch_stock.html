Summary of Facts

Hannah Höch was a German Dada artist and pioneer of photomontage. Her most famous work is Cut with the Kitchen Knife Dada through the Last Weimar Beer-Belly Cultural Epoch of Germany (1919), a massive photomontage using newspaper cuttings. Its top part features Friedrich Ebert and anti-modernists, while Käthe Kollwitz, John Heartfield, Marx, and Lenin appear in the bottom half. Near the center, a headless dancer throws a woman's head toward an elephant. It includes cutouts of the word 'Dada' and phrases like 'Invest your money in Dada!'

Her From an Ethnographic Museum series combines magazine cutouts with photographs of ethnographic artifacts; The Sweet One depicts an African statue with a flapper's legs. She overlaid a still of Renée Falconetti with a Cameroonian mask in another entry.

Höch was the lover and collaborator of Raoul Hausmann, creator of Mechanical Head (The Spirit of Our Time). Other works include High Finance (a banker with John Herschel's head threatened by a giant gun) and The Beautiful Girl (a woman with a lightbulb head amid BMW logos). She paired poems and collages in her Picture Book. The Nazis branded her work 'degenerate art.' She contributed to Retrato de la Burguesia due to anti-Nazi beliefs.
