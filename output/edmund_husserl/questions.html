T1 2023 ILLIAC (2023) Diff 7 · Philosophy
This philosopher’s idea of self awareness is called “pre-reflective self-consciousness” by Shaun Gallagher and Dan Zahavi. Borrowing an Aristotelian concept, this thinker referred to “intuitive representational content” based on the senses as “hýle” (“HE-lay”). Jürgen Habermas further developed a concept this philosopher thought of as the pregiven framework of social groups, which this thinker termed the “lifeworld.” This philosopher adopted the idea of (*) “intentionality” from his teacher Franz Brentano. In this philosopher’s book Ideas, he coined the term “eidetic reduction” for a method he developed that focuses on experiences in the first-person point of view via “epoché” (“eh-po-KAY”), or “bracketing.” For 10 points, the book Logical Investigations is by what Austrian thinker who founded phenomenology?
ANSWER: Edmund Husserl [or Edmund Gustav Albrecht Husserl]
T2 2022 NASAT (2022) Diff 8 · Philosophy
This philosopher praised Eugen Fink's addition of a sixth entry to a series in which this thinker describes how intersubjectivity constructs objective reality, as later elaborated by his student Edith Stein. Franz Brentano's concept of intentionality is extended in this thinker's difficult-to-decipher conception of "noema" and its relation to "noesis." Heidegger originally dedicated Being and Time to this thinker, who turned two Sorbonne lectures into a set of five "meditations" modeled on the Meditations on First Philosophy. This philosopher sought to found all sciences on a "Transcendental Subjectivity" developed by suspending previous beliefs about phenomena, a method he termed "epoche" or "bracketing." For 10 points, name this author of Logical Investigations and Cartesian Meditations who founded modern phenomenology.
ANSWER: Edmund Husserl [or Edmund Gustav Albrecht Husserl] (Heidegger's dedication was later rescinded on account of Husserl's Jewish ancestry)
T3 2017 Penn Bowl (2017) Diff 8 · Philosophy
The English translation of one of this author’s works was done by William Boyce Gibson, who used “sense” and “conceptual significance” for “Sinn” and “Bedeutung.” That work discusses the “principle of all principles,” the “natural standpoint,” and the noesis and noema. This philosopher identified morphology of meanings, logic of consequence, and metalogic as three increasingly abstract strata of logic. This philosopher wrote that he wanted to found “a science covering a new field of experience, exclusively its own, that of (*) Transcendental Subjectivity.” He identified a kind of “reduction” that consists of pure consciousness of the Being of some intentional object, and argued that logicians had failed to pay attention to the role of subjective consciousness. For 10 points, name this author of Cartesian Meditations and Logical Investigations who founded phenomenology.
ANSWER: Edmund Husserl
T4 2014 PADAWAN (2014) Diff 7 · Philosophy
This thinker organized formal logic into a three-part stratum whose first level is concerned only with grammar, and not with validity or truth, which he called an apophantic “morphology of meanings”. This thinker broke down intentionality into conscious intentional acts and the objects-of-intention, called “noesis” and “noema”. He introduced the concept of a pre-existing framework of beliefs and attitudes that shapes how we conceive of the shared human experience in his The (*) Crisis of European Sciences; that concept is the “lifeworld”. He referred to suspending beliefs about the material world and emphasizing mental experiences as “epoche” or “bracketing”. For 10 points, name this author of Cartesian Meditations and Logical Investigations, who founded phenomenology.
ANSWER: Edmund Husserl [or Edmund Gustav Albrecht Husserl]
T5 2012 QUARK (2012) Diff 8 · Philosophy
This philosopher was assisted in editing his magnum opus by Adolf Reinach, who also co-founded a journal edited by this man. This subject of a book-length study by Paul Ricoeur argued that “naturalism refutes itself” in his seminal paper “Philosophy as Rigorous Science.” His work “On the Concept of Number” was expanded into a work influenced by his studies of mathematics under Weierstrass, The Philosophy of (*) Arithmetic. He argued that our perception of time depends on retention and protention, and divided intentional acts into noesis and noema. This philosopher’s concept of eidetic reduction is related to a suspension of judgment he called epoché, or bracketing. His major works include Cartesian Meditations and Logical Investigations. For 10 points, name this student of Franz Brentano, a founder of phenomenology.
ANSWER: Edmund Husserl
