Summary of Facts

Honoré de Balzac (1799-1850) was a towering French realist novelist, author of The Human Comedy (La Comédie Humaine), a vast interconnected cycle of novels depicting French society across all social classes. His work is referenced in Dai Sijie's Balzac and the Little Chinese Seamstress, in which two boys borrow French literature at a reeducation camp.

Père Goriot is set in the Maison Vauquer boardinghouse in Paris. The title character is an old pasta maker whose two daughters, Anastasie and Delphine, drain his fortune and fail to attend his funeral. The criminal Vautrin (secretly the master criminal Jacques Collin / Trompe-la-Mort) meets the ambitious law student Eugène de Rastignac, who at the novel's end challenges Paris from Père Lachaise cemetery.

The Unknown Masterpiece is a story about the painter Frenhofer whose obsessive work produces only chaos from which a single foot emerges. Frenhofer dies in a fire. Sarrasine features a sculptor who falls for a castrato singer; Roland Barthes analyzed it in S/Z using "hermeneutic" and "proairetic" codes. Cousin Bette follows the scheming Lisbeth Fischer against the Hulot family. Ursule Mirouet involves a Catholic doctor whose will naming a pious girl as heir is destroyed by a postmaster.
