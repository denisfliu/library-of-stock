Summary of Facts

Adonis (born Ali Ahmad Said Esber, 1930) is a Syrian poet who adopted a mythological pen name. His best-known collection, Songs of Mihyar the Damascene, adopts the guise of a medieval Arab poet and contains prose 'Psalms.' His poem 'A Grave for New York' addresses Walt Whitman and describes the Statue of Liberty holding 'a rag called freedom' while 'choking a child named Earth.' The Book (Al Kitab) is a three-volume epic using al-Mutannabi as a guide through the Arab world. He made the first complete Arabic translation of Ovid's Metamorphoses and wrote a book examining parallels between Surrealism and Sufism.
