B1 2017 ACF Regionals (2017) Diff 8 · Literature
A novel by this author contains a chapter that consists of a numbered list describing a coffin, with statements such as “a body is not square like a crosstie” and “so I made it on the bevel.” For 10 points each:
[10] Name this author from Mississippi who also wrote a chapter that consists entirely of the statement “my mother is a fish” in his novel As I Lay Dying.
ANSWER: William Faulkner [or William Cuthbert Faulkner; or William Cuthbert Falkner]
[10] Most of the stories in Faulkner’s Go Down, Moses feature this central character, who kills his first buck in “The Old People.” In “The Bear,” he helps Boon Hogganbeck hunt Old Ben.
ANSWER: Isaac McCaslin [accept either underlined portion; accept (Uncle) Ike McCaslin]
[10] This African American writer drew from the spiritual “Go Down, Moses” for his poem “Go Down, Death,” which is described as a “funeral sermon” and collected with other poems couched as sermons in a 1927 book.
ANSWER: James Weldon Johnson (The book is God’s Trombones.)
