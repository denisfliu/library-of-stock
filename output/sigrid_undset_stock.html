Sigrid Undset

← Home · View source questions · Make cards
Norwegian Nobel Prize-winning author, best known for her historical trilogy Kristin Lavransdatter (The Wreath, The Wife, The Cross), set in medieval Norway. Also wrote the tetralogy The Master of Hestviken and early novels like Jenny and Fru Marta Oulie. Born in Denmark, grew up in Norway. A prominent anti-Nazi. Converted to Catholicism, which influenced her work.
Kristin Lavransdatter (trilogy)

A trilogy of historical novels set in 14th-century medieval Norway, consisting of The Wreath, The Wife, and The Cross. Follows Kristin, a nobleman's Catholic daughter, through her life: from her betrothal to Simon Darre, her seduction by and marriage to Erlend Nikulausson, her time at the estate Husaby, and her death of the Black Plague in a convent. Translated by Tiina Nunnally; Charles Archer's original translation cut modernist passages including stream-of-consciousness. Wikipedia

Summary of Facts

Sigrid Undset was a Norwegian Nobel Prize-winning author, born in Denmark but raised in Norway. She is best known for her historical trilogy Kristin Lavransdatter, consisting of The Wreath, The Wife, and The Cross, which depicts the life of a nobleman's Catholic daughter in 14th-century medieval Norway.

In the Kristin Lavransdatter trilogy, the protagonist Kristin is sent to the Nonneseter convent on account of a rumor involving her foster-brother and the attempted rapist Bentein. She is seduced by Erlend Nikulausson and breaks off her betrothal to Simon Darre, who ends up with her sister Ramborg. Despite being pregnant, Kristin wears the traditional golden bridal wreath at her wedding — a privilege reserved for virgin brides. She violates custom by naming one of her eight sons after her still-living husband. The second book is set at the estate of Husaby, which Kristin loses because Erlend plotted to overthrow the king. Erlend is killed in a skirmish by a spear to the groin while defending Kristin from charges of adultery. Kristin ultimately dies of the Black Plague in a convent after returning to tend to the sick. Charles Archer's original English translation cut many modernist passages, including several using stream of consciousness, which were later restored in Tiina Nunnally's translation.

Undset also wrote The Master of Hestviken, a tetralogy set in the 13th and 14th centuries centering on Olav and Ingunn at the title estate. Its volumes include The Axe and The Snake Pit. Two of its characters were betrothed as children, possibly as a joke, which they take seriously. Olav kills his wife's lover, puts the body in a cabin, and burns it down, but cannot confess his guilt.

Her early novels include Jenny, about a title artist in Rome who is impregnated by Gert (the father of her former fiance Helge Gram) and kills herself after Helge rapes her, and Fru Marta Oulie, a controversial novel that begins with the line 'I have been unfaithful to my husband.' Undset was a prominent anti-Nazi and is frequently grouped with other Norwegian authors such as Knut Hamsun, Henrik Ibsen, and Bjornstjerne Bjornson.
