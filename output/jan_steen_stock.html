Summary of Facts

Jan Steen was a 17th-century Dutch Golden Age painter known for lively, comedic genre scenes. He worked as a brewer and innkeeper, which influenced his many tavern and domestic scenes.

The Feast of St. Nicholas is his most frequently clued work, depicting a child in a gold dress delighted by a doll while an older brother cries over receiving only a birch rod. Children teach a cat to dance in another section. It hangs in the Gallery of Honour at the Rijksmuseum.

The Merry Family shows a large family drinking, smoking, and playing instruments around a table, with a man holding a fiddle and raising a glass. Beware of Luxury (In Luxury, Look Out!) shows a woman balancing wine in a man's lap, reversing the proverb about strong legs. He painted multiple scenes of doctors visiting lovesick or pregnant women and The Drawing Lesson where a teacher uses a plaster nude.

He co-founded the Guild of St. Luke in Leiden and inspired Joan Miro's Dutch Interiors series.
