Questions: Yves Klein

6 tossups · 8 bonuses

Tossups

T1 2025 NAFTA (2025) Diff 9 · Fine Arts
Months before dying, this artist burned asbestos-covered cardboard with a blowtorch to make “fire paintings.” Arman filled the Iris Clert Gallery with trash in response to an exhibition by this artist that only displayed an empty glass cabinet. In one project, this artist sold certificates of ownership of non-existent spaces for gold, then threw half of the gold into a river. Pierre Restany collaborated with this artist to found (*) New Realism. A material developed by this artist was approximated for an experimental 1993 film on the AIDS crisis by Derek Jarman. This artist had nude models cover themselves with paint and roll on canvases to create his Anthropometries. For 10 points, name this French artist of The Void who created many monochrome works using an ultramarine pigment that is his namesake “International” shade of blue.
ANSWER: Yves Klein [accept International Klein Blue or Yves Klein Blue] (Derek Jarman’s film is Blue.)
T2 2023 On Cinema at the Buzzer (2023) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
A director with this surname made a film in which a white nationalist superhero teams up with a sidekick played by Delphine Seyrig (“seh-REEG”) to fight communists. In a film by Joseph Losey, Alain Delon (“ah-LEN duh-LAHN”) plays an art dealer with this last name who is mistaken for a Jewish man with the same name during the Holocaust. Satirical films like Mr. Freedom were directed by William, a photographer and director with this last name. An artist with this surname had a heart attack the night that he watched (*) Mondo Cane (“KAH-nay”), which features a recreation of one of his “anthropometries” (“ahn-troh-poh-may-TREE”) performances. That artist with this surname inspired the visuals of a 1993 film about the director’s experience living with AIDS. For 10 points, Derek Jarman’s Blue uses a shade of blue named after an artist with what surname?
ANSWER: Klein [accept Mr. Klein or Robert Klein; accept William Klein; accept Yves Klein or International Klein Blue]
T3 2023 Nora Barnacle Memorial Tournament (2023) Diff 10 · Literature
Note to players: I apologize in advance. In a novel whose English translation's cover is this color, a "drug baron" named Marc Dutroux attempts to recruit the narrator to courier a red gym bag from Ibiza to the DJ Schütte. In a scene added to a play titled for this color, a woman explains why she always wears a thick veil when she visits happy people. The covers of English translations of Rainald Goetz' novels Insane and Rave are this color. Jacques Testard paid only 3500 pounds to publish an English translation of Second-Hand Time with a cover of this color. (*) Olga Tokarczuk and Annie Ernaux are two of four recent Nobelists whose works' first English translations have covers with this color, and are published by Fitzcarraldo Editions. A play titled for this color ends with a girl asking the audience to give back an escaped dove if they manage to catch it; in that "fairy play," whose third act includes a dialogue between "Bread" and "Night," two children are led by the cat Tylette, "Sugar," and "Fire" to the castle of the Fairy Berylune. For 10 points, Tyltyl and Mytyl search for a bird of what color in a Maurice Maeterlinck play?
ANSWER: blue [or "The Blue Bird"; accept International Klein Blue or Yves Klein Blue; reject any other shades of blue]
T4 2021 NASAT (2021) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
An artist with this first name painted a black cloud moving towards a lone cactus in Mama, Papa is Wounded! The 2012 "Double Solitaire" exhibition examined works by Kay Sage and that surrealist with this first name, her husband. An artist with this first name was photographed jumping off of a roof in Leap into the Void. An artist with this first name created a series of Anthropometries, in which women would roll around on the canvas covered in his signature ultramarine paint. A fashion designer with this first name created Mondrian-inspired dresses and the "Le Smoking" female tuxedo. For 10 points, give this first name of an artist surnamed Tanguy, the creator of the International Klein Blue shade, and of an Algerian designer surnamed Saint-Laurent (san luh-ron).
ANSWER: Yves [accept Yves Klein or Yves Tanguy or Yves Saint-Laurent]
T5 2021 ACF Regionals (2021) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
A rose of this color names a Russian symbolist movement led by Pavel Kuznetsov. Eleven identical, differently priced canvases of this color inspired white Achromes by Piero Manzoni. A heron and ibis appear in the bottom left as a standing nude grasps the title bird of this color in a painting from multiple perspectives by Jean Metzinger (“met-san-ZHAY”). In another work, a patch of this color appears on the side of a cow that possibly represents the artist’s wife. “Living Brushes” applied this color to paper with their naked bodies in Anthropometries using an “international” shade of it mixed by Yves Klein (“eev clan”). One artist found this color “astringent and spiritual,” and often painted horses of this color. For 10 points, Franz Marc and Wassily Kandinsky founded a movement named for what color of Rider?
ANSWER: blue [accept Blue Rose, Golubaya roza, International Klein Blue, The Blue Bird, L’Oiseau bleu, Blue Rider, or Der Blaue Reiter] (Piero Manzoni saw Yves Klein’s Proposte monocrome, epoca Blu; Franz Marc included a patch of blue in Yellow Cow.)
T6 2014 Chicago Open (2014) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
This artist traded one of his artworks for a bag of gold dust and a receipt, then threw the gold dust into the Seine and burnt the receipt. This artist painted canvases and fastened them to his Citroën car to subject them to rain, wind, and dirt to create artworks for his Cosmogonies series. This artist’s fascination with Rosicrucianism led to his obsession with emptiness, as demonstrated in a photograph in which he is seen jumping out of a second-story window into nothingness. In 1958, he exhibited an empty display case in the Iris Clert Gallery in an exhibition called The Void. This artist soaked nude models in paint and had them roll on a canvas to create paintings in his Anthropometries series. His series of Monochromes featured canvasses painted entirely one color, many of which were executed in a shade based on synthetic ultramarine. For 10 points, name this artist who named an “international blue” pigment after himself.
ANSWER: Yves Klein
