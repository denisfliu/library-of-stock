Summary of Facts

Carlos Fuentes (1928-2012) was a Mexican novelist and a key figure in the Latin American literary Boom alongside García Márquez, Vargas Llosa, and Cortázar.

The Death of Artemio Cruz uses three alternating narrative voices — first person (I), second person (you/tú), and third person (he) — to tell the story of the dying Mexican Revolution veteran Artemio Cruz, who betrayed his revolutionary ideals to become corrupt. Aura is a novella written entirely in second person, in which the young historian Felipe Montero is hired by elderly widow Consuelo to catalogue memoirs, and discovers that the mysterious young Aura is Consuelo's double. Terra Nostra is a massive novel spanning from the Roman era to 1999. The Old Gringo is loosely based on Ambrose Bierce's disappearance in Mexico.
