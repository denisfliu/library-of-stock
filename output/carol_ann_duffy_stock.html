Summary of Facts

Carol Ann Duffy is clued as a Scottish poet who served as British Poet Laureate from 2009 to 2019, the first woman, first Scot, and first openly LGBT person to hold the position. She was succeeded by Simon Armitage. Her most frequently clued work is The World's Wife, a collection of dramatic monologues from the perspectives of female counterparts to famous men. Key poems from this collection include "Anne Hathaway" (about Shakespeare's wife reflecting on the "second best bed" she was bequeathed), "Little Red-Cap" (a retelling where the speaker kills a wolf with an axe for the sake of "poetry"), "Elvis's Twin Sister" (a nun in a convent thinking of Heartbreak Hotel), "Mrs. Tiresias" (whose husband went out a man and came home female), and "Queen Herod" (with three female Magi). Her first collection Standing Female Nude includes the title poem written from the perspective of an artist's model. "Education for Leisure" was censored in schools for knife-crime references. "Valentine" offers an onion as a love token. "Last Post" inverts Wilfred Owen's "Dulce et Decorum Est" to eulogize WWI veterans. Rapture won the T.S. Eliot Prize. She also wrote political poetry including "Campaign" about Brexit and "The Assassination of Margaret Thatcher" adaptation of Everyman. She was a member of the "New Generation" school succeeding the Martian poets and had a decade-long relationship with Liverpool Poet Adrian Henri.
