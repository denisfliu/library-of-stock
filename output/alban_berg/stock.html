Austrian composer (1885–1935) and member of the Second Viennese School alongside Arnold Schoenberg (his teacher) and Anton Webern. Best known for the operas Wozzeck and the unfinished Lulu, and for the Lyric Suite for string quartet. His twelve-tone Violin Concerto, dedicated 'to the memory of an angel' (Manon Gropius), incorporates a Carinthian folk song and Bach's chorale 'Es ist genug.' Often associated with encoding personal secrets in his music, including references to his love affair with Hanna Fuchs-Robettin.
General / Biographical

Austrian composer (1885–1935); student of Arnold Schoenberg and colleague of Anton Webern in the Second Viennese School. His music employs the twelve-tone technique (serialism). His Opus 1 is a piano sonata in B minor, using developing variation. A riot called the Skandalkonzert occurred during performance of his Altenberg-Lieder. He also wrote the concert aria 'Der Wein' for solo soprano with text from a translation of Les fleurs du mal.

Summary of Facts

Alban Berg (1885–1935) was an Austrian composer and a central member of the Second Viennese School, alongside his teacher Arnold Schoenberg and colleague Anton Webern. He studied with Schoenberg and absorbed his twelve-tone technique, which he applied throughout his mature work while consistently maintaining expressive, even Romantic, qualities that distinguish him from his colleagues.

Berg is best known for two operas. Wozzeck (his first opera) is based on Georg Büchner's play and follows a soldier tormented by his superiors. Its three acts are respectively structured as a set of character pieces, a five-movement symphony, and six inventions — an extraordinary formal architecture overlaid on a dramatic narrative. The opening of Wozzeck is structured as a Baroque suite to portray a captain's incomprehension of how the poor live. A fast, out-of-tune polka on an onstage piano introduces a bar scene. The aria 'Wir arme Leut' introduces the opera's main leitmotif. The protagonist eventually murders his wife Marie (who had been raped by the Drum Major), then drowns himself while trying to retrieve a knife thrown into a pond. His second opera, Lulu, remained unfinished at his death; Schoenberg declined to complete it. Lulu is formally innovative: each character has their own characteristic tone row derived from a single master tone row based on a diatonic hexachord. The opera introduced the vibraphone to the orchestra. Its prologue features a ringmaster calling Lulu his 'prized snake.' A palindromic silent film plays during an interlude depicting Lulu's arrest, trial, jailing, cholera, and hospitalization. The same baritone plays both Dr. Schon (Lulu's patron and eventual husband) and Jack the Ripper, who ultimately murders both Lulu and the lesbian Countess Geschwitz. Lulu's character Alwa is often considered a self-portrait of the composer.

Berg's Violin Concerto (1935), his last completed major work, was commissioned by American violinist Louis Krasner and dedicated 'to the memory of an angel,' referring to the death of Manon Gropius — daughter of architect Walter Gropius and Alma Mahler. The tone row's first nine notes form a chain of ascending thirds; the last three form ascending whole steps; the first three arpeggiate a G-minor triad, giving the work both twelve-tone and tonal qualities. The first movement includes a quotation of a Carinthian folk song. The soloist enters with a four-note arpeggio on open strings after the harp quietly arpeggiate perfect fifths. The second movement culminates in variations on Bach's chorale 'Es ist genug,' whose melody fits the final notes of the tone row perfectly. Like Berg's Lyric Suite, the concerto is thought to contain encoded references to his love affair with Hanna Fuchs-Robettin.

The Lyric Suite for string quartet was called a 'latent opera' by theorist Theodor Adorno. It uses an 'A-B-H-F' motif encoding the names of Berg and Hanna Fuchs-Robettin (H = B-natural in German notation). Movements carry emotional tempo markings — the fifth is marked 'presto delirando' and 'tenebroso'; the sixth is 'largo desolato' and quotes Tristan und Isolde in reference to the love affair. The work was inspired by an Alexander von Zemlinsky symphony.

Among Berg's other works: his Opus 1 is a piano sonata in B minor (one movement, uses developing variation); the Altenberg-Lieder provoked a riot called the Skandalkonzert at its premiere; and 'Der Wein' is a concert aria for soprano with text drawn from a translation of Baudelaire's Les fleurs du mal.
