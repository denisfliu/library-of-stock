Tossups

T1 2022 Penn Bowl (2022) Diff 8 · Literature
After discussing the “neuroscience of Hamlet,” Angus Fletcher’s Wonderworks reports how audience members chanted along to a soliloquy written by this author and originally delivered by the mononymous actor Montdory; that soliloquy begins “My father or my betrothed? Love or honor?” This author adapted a play by Juan Ruiz de Alarcón into a farce in which Dorante pretends to be a military hero to court Clarice, whose name he stupidly mixes up with her friend Lucerne. This author of The Liar wrote a play in which the title character kills (*) Don Gomès in a duel before also besting Don Rodrigo for the hand of Chimène (“shee-MEN”). This author famously disregarded the classical unities and upset Cardinal Richelieu in that play, in which the title exiled Spanish soldier fights the Moors. For 10 points, name this 17th-century French playwright who wrote the tragicomedy Le Cid.
ANSWER: Pierre Corneille (“cor-NAY”)
T2 2022 MUSES III (2022) Diff 10 · Literature
In an early comedy by this author, the protagonist suffers delusions of hell after forging love letters in order to prevent the marriage of his beloved and his friend Tircis. The protagonist of a play by this author accidentally confesses his feelings for his true love to the princess (+) Rosine before being reprimanded by his true love and then killed by Prince Florilame. The name of the main character switches to Calisto and Theogenes between acts in a 2002 adaptation of one of this author’s plays by Tony Kushner. That comedy ends with a cave-inhabiting (*) magician revealing that characters such as Clindor and Isabelle were actually just actors in a play-within-a-play. This author of The Comic Illusion briefly withdrew from public life after Cardinal Richelieu said that one of his plays too strongly violated the Aristotelian unities. For 10 points, name this French playwright of Le Cid.
ANSWER: Pierre Corneille (The play in the first line is Mélite.)

That play was targeted in a (*) pamphlet campaign that led Jean Chapelain (“shap-LANN”) to condemn this author of The Comic Illusion for violating the three unities. T5 2012 ACF Regionals (2012) Diff 8 · Literature
At the end of one of this man’s plays, a character is shocked to see his son killed, only to learn that his son has become an actor and that he has actually been watching a play. In that work, the magician Alcandre supplies a device that allows Pridamant to view the actions of his son Clindor from afar; that play was adapted into English by Tony Kushner. This author of The Comic Illusion produced a verse translation of The Imitation of Christ and wrote Three Discourses on Dramatic Poetry, in which he argued against an unthinking adherence to the Aristotelian unities, prompted by Cardinal Richelieu’s plan to shatter the popularity of his most famous work. In that play, a champion carrying a bloody sword informs Chimène that her lover Don Rodrigue, the title Moor-fighting hero, had spared him. For 10 points, name this French playwright and rival of Jean Racine who wrote Le Cid.
