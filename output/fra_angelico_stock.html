Fra Angelico

← Home · View source questions · Make cards
Italian Renaissance painter and Dominican friar. Best known for his Annunciation fresco (Gabriel with multicolored wings approaching Mary in an enclosed garden/portico) at the San Marco monastery in Florence, and the San Marco Altarpiece. Also painted frescoes in each cell of the convent and decorated the Niccoline Chapel at the Vatican. Collaborated with Benozzo Gozzoli and Luca Signorelli.
The Annunciation (San Marco)

Fra Angelico's most famous work. Gabriel with multicolored wings approaches a sitting Virgin Mary in a portico/enclosed garden (hortus conclusus). In one version, Gabriel and Mary have arms crossed in identical positions. Among the first Western paintings to demonstrate linear perspective. Black lines mysteriously present between arcs of the loggia.

                                  Summary of Facts

                                  Fra Angelico (born Guido di Pietro) was an Italian Renaissance painter and Dominican friar who combined Gothic and Renaissance styles. He is best known for decorating his own monastery of San Marco in Florence, where he painted frescoes in each cell of a complex rebuilt by Michelozzo di Bartolomeo.

                                  His most famous work is the Annunciation at San Marco, in which the angel Gabriel with multicolored wings approaches a sitting Virgin Mary in a portico or enclosed garden (hortus conclusus style). In one version, Gabriel and Mary have their arms crossed in identical positions. The most famous version sits at the top of the entrance staircase. These Annunciations were among the first Western paintings to demonstrate linear perspective. One version features golden rays emanating from God's hands and blue pendentives, and another has the word of God literally emerging from Gabriel's mouth. A less brightly-colored version is named for Cortona. Black lines are mysteriously present between arcs of the loggia.

                                  The San Marco Altarpiece features the infant Jesus holding a black orb above a small Crucifixion image, with Saints Cosmas and Damian kneeling on an Anatolian carpet featuring Cancer and Pisces (symbolizing the Council of Florence and Medici propaganda). It includes nine predella panels of Cosmas and Damian stories, and the Annalena Altarpiece is often considered the first sacra conversazione.

                                  Other San Marco frescoes include a Crucifixion above portrait medallions of Dominican friars, a Mockery of Christ (disembodied hand brutalizing a blindfolded Jesus with a stick), and a Transfiguration (Moses and Elias under Jesus's outstretched arms, described as 'very, very yellow'). His St. Lawrence frescoes in the Niccoline Chapel show Lawrence in a flame-pattern cloak. He painted a Last Judgment with opened tombs at the bottom center, a Deposition with landscape divided by three Gothic arches, a Coronation of the Virgin at the Uffizi, and The Vision of Ezekiel (circular, with Creation and Gospel of John text). He collaborated with Luca Signorelli and his student Benozzo Gozzoli on Judgment Day frescoes at the Orvieto Cathedral. His tomb is in the Basilica of Santa Maria sopra Minerva in Rome.
