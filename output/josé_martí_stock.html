Summary of Facts

José Martí (1853-1895) was a Cuban poet, essayist, and revolutionary, a key figure in Cuban independence. He was killed fighting the Spanish at the Battle of Dos Ríos.

His Versos Sencillos (Simple Verses) includes poems adapted by Pete Seeger into the song 'Guantanamera.' 'A Sincere Man Am I' describes being 'from the land where palm trees grow.' 'I Cultivate a White Rose' gives the title flower to both 'the true friend' and 'the cruel person' in 'July as in January.' 'The Girl from Guatemala' is a 'flowered tale' about a woman who 'died of love.'

His essay 'Our America' (Nuestra América) argues against US dominance in Latin America. He created the newspaper Patria, wrote the Manifesto of Montecristi, was an ally of Máximo Gómez, and helped kickstart Modernismo alongside Rubén Darío.
