Bonuses

B1 2021 PACE NSC (2021) Diff 5 · Fine Arts
This director’s film Woman in the Moon contains the first onscreen countdown to a rocket launch. For 10 points each:
[10] Identify this German director who allegedly turned down an offer to head the Nazi’s film division. This director of Metropolis later criticized the Nazi regime in Hangmen Also Die!
ANSWER: Fritz Lang [or Friedrich Christian Anton Lang]
[10] The advent of this technology allowed Lang to depict the protagonist of M whistling “In the Hall of the Mountain King.” This technology was popularized in the US by The Jazz Singer.
ANSWER: synchronized sound [or sync sound; or sound film; accept talkies or talking pictures; accept more specific technologies such as Vitaphone]
[10] Late in his life, Lang went back to West Germany to make a final film about the “thousand eyes” of this character. This character’s “testament” is detailed in a 1933 thriller in which he outlines his criminal plans from a mental asylum.
ANSWER: Dr. Mabuse (“ma-BOO-zuh”)
B2 2021 Illinois Open (2021) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
This character breathes through a punctured wine bottle while hiding in a basement cistern near the end of a film titled for his battle of wits with his nemesis Inspector Juve. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this criminal mastermind, the star of five internationally-popular French silent films directed by Louis Feuillade from 1913-14.
ANSWER: Fantômas
[10] A fan club called the Society of the Friends of Fantômas was co-founded by two early proponents of this artistic movement. A razor slices a woman’s eyeball in a seminal film from this movement, Un Chien Andalou.
ANSWER: Surrealism [the Society of the Friends of Fantômas was founded by Guillaume Apollinaire and Max Jacob]
[10] This director drew from Juve contre Fantômas to create a sequence in which the title character shuffles a deck of cards to choose a disguise to perpetrate a stock market panic, in the opening sequence of his first film about the criminal psychologist Dr. Mabuse.
ANSWER: Fritz Lang [or Friedrich Christian Anton Lang]
