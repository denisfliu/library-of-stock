Summary of Facts

Yinka Shonibare is a British-Nigerian artist and member of the Young British Artists, most commonly identified by his signature use of headless mannequins dressed in colorful Dutch wax (Ankara) fabric. He is paralyzed on his left side and uses an electric wheelchair.

His most frequently clued works are The Swing (After Fragonard), a sculptural recreation of Fragonard's painting using a headless mannequin in Ankara fabric, and Nelson's Ship in a Bottle, a 1:30 scale replica of the HMS Victory with textile sails, commissioned for the Fourth Plinth of Trafalgar Square. The sails symbolize African identity and independence.

Diary of a Victorian Dandy is a series of photographs in which Shonibare inserts himself as a Black dandy in elaborate Victorian settings, based on Hogarth's A Rake's Progress. He buys his Dutch wax fabric at Brixton Market.

Other clued works include Line Painting (patterned circles with African textile patterns), The American Library (2018, names of successful immigrants on 3,200 book spines), a recreation of Gainsborough's Mr. and Mrs. Andrews, a recreation of Caravaggio's Medusa using a Black model with snakes made of Ankara cloth, Gallantry and Criminal Conversation (headless mannequins in period costumes), and a 2023 sculpture Hibiscus Rising in Aire Park described as 'Our Angel of the North.' He was the audience favorite for the 2004 Turner Prize but did not win.
