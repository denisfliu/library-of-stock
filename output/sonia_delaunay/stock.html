Prose of the Trans-Siberian and of Little Jehanne of France (1913, with Blaise Cendrars)

Summary of Facts

Sonia Delaunay (1885-1979), born Sarah Stern in Ukraine, was a French artist and fashion designer who co-founded the art movement Orphism with her husband Robert Delaunay. In quizbowl, she is most frequently identified by her illustrated artists' book collaboration with Blaise Cendrars, Prose of the Trans-Siberian and of Little Jehanne of France (1913), described as the first "simultaneous book" with floods of color swirling across pages. She published enough copies that they could be stacked to reach the height of the Eiffel Tower.

She applied the theory of simultaneisme (simultaneous contrasting colors) to fashion, creating "dress-poems" embroidered with avant-garde poetry by Vicente Huidobro and Tristan Tzara. She designed ballet costumes for Diaghilev's production of Cleopatre, placing a rainbow on the waist and using concentric circles and zig-zags to emphasize movement. She also designed cardboard trapezoid costumes for Tristan Tzara's 1923 Dada play The Gas Heart, in which actors could not escape a riot due to the restrictive costumes. Her other works include the painting Coccinelle, featuring semicircles and arches of varying colors.
