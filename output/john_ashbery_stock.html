American New York School poet. Best known for "Self-Portrait in a Convex Mirror" (ekphrasis on Parmigianino painting), the collections Some Trees (Yale Younger Poets Prize, selected by Auden) and The Tennis Court Oath. Known for experimental, difficult poetry. Also wrote Girls on the Run (inspired by Henry Darger) and the book-length poems Flow Chart and Three Poems.

Summary of Facts

John Ashbery is an American poet of the New York School, alongside Frank O'Hara, Kenneth Koch, and James Schuyler. His most famous work is "Self-Portrait in a Convex Mirror," a long ekphrastic poem meditating on Parmigianino's self-portrait that describes "the right hand / Bigger than the head, thrust at the viewer" and notes "the glass chose to reflect only what he saw." The poem asserts "The soul establishes itself," contains "an affirmation that doesn't affirm anything," and ends "Here and there in cold pockets of remembrance, whispers out of time." It quotes Vasari and inspired a Pulitzer-winning piece for string orchestra by Roger Reynolds.

His first collection, Some Trees (1956), won the Yale Younger Poets Prize when W.H. Auden controversially selected it despite admitting he did not understand a word of the manuscript. The title poem contains the lines "you and I / Are suddenly what the trees try / To tell us we are: / That their merely being there / Means something." His second collection, The Tennis Court Oath, contains the long experimental poem "Europe" divided into 111 numbered sections.

Girls on the Run is a book-length ekphrastic poem inspired by outsider artist Henry Darger, featuring the Vivians. "And Ut Pictura Poesis Is Her Name," titled for a dictum from Horace's Ars Poetica, describes poetry as generated by "the extreme austerity of an almost empty mind / Colliding with the lush, Rousseau-like foliage of its desire to communicate." "View of Delft" is an ekphrasis on a Vermeer painting.

Ashbery named W.H. Auden as his greatest influence. He claimed to hate all French poets except Raymond Roussel and co-edited the Roussel-inspired literary journal Locus Solus with Koch and Schuyler. He translated Rimbaud's Illuminations. Ben Lerner's novel Leaving the Atocha Station is titled for an Ashbery poem, and its protagonist Adam Gordon carries Ashbery's Collected Poems. Ashbery's first poem, "The Battle," was written at age 8 after seeing a film of A Midsummer Night's Dream. "The History of My Life" draws from the death of his younger brother Richard. He died in 2017 after publishing Commotion of the Birds.
