The Rover

Behn's most famous play, subtitled 'The Banish'd Cavaliers,' based on Thomas Killigrew's Thomaso. Set during Carnival in Naples. Florinda marries Colonel Belville. Hellena, bound for a nunnery, falls for the licentious Captain Willmore ('there's no sinner like a young saint'). The courtesan Angellica Bianca holds Willmore at gunpoint but calls off his murder. Lucetta seduces Blunt and drops him through a trapdoor. The play may have been performed by Elizabeth Barry and Nell Gwynn. A 'fake' marriage ceremony turns out legal in The Town Fop.

Oroonoko

Behn's most famous prose work, sometimes called the earliest British novel. The title character, a prince from Coramantien, is enslaved and taken to Suriname. He is reunited with his lover Imoinda but leads a failed slave revolt against deputy governor Byam. After killing Imoinda (cutting off her head), Oroonoko is disemboweled and dismembered. The characters are given Christian names Caesar and Clemene. Behn may have gathered material while serving as a spy in Suriname. Charles Gildon invented a biography of Behn claiming she had an affair with Oroonoko. Thomas Southerne adapted it for the stage.

The Emperor of the Moon

Dr. Baliardo, an amateur astronomer, wants to marry his daughters (and/or niece) to alien suitors — the Prince of Thunderland and the Emperor of the Moon. His daughter Elaria and Bellemante are forbidden human suitors. Men disguise themselves as aliens to trick Baliardo. The play ends with a giant zodiac dance.

"The Disappointment"

A poem about the impotent shepherd Lysander's failed sexual encounter with Cloris. 'Th' Insensible fell weeping in his Hands.' Lysander curses 'his birth, his fate, his stars.' Cloris finds 'beneath the verdant leaves a snake.' Originally misattributed to the Earl of Rochester. Republished in 'Poems upon Several Occasions.'

General / Biographical

Behn was the premier female playwright of the Restoration. Virginia Woolf wrote in A Room of One's Own that 'all women ought to let flowers fall upon' her tomb (buried in Westminster Abbey's East Cloister, not Poets' Corner). She was a spy for Charles II using codenames 'Agent 160' and 'Astrea' (the Greek goddess name). Member of the 'fair triumvirate of wit' with Eliza Haywood and Delarivier Manley. Wrote Aesop's fables in rhyming quatrains for Barlow's 1687 edition. Henry Purcell wrote incidental music for her Abdelazer (second movement later used as theme for Britten's Young Person's Guide to the Orchestra). Other works: The Dutch Lover (Haunce van Ezel, 'Epistle to the Reader'), The Feign'd Curtizans, The False Count (chimney-sweep Guiliom), The Roundheads, Love-Letters Between a Nobleman and His Sister, The Fair Jilt (said to be first English novel by a woman), Agnes of Castro, Oenone to Paris (paraphrasing Ovid's Heroides).

Summary of Facts

Aphra Behn is the premier female playwright of the English Restoration, known primarily for The Rover and Oroonoko. The Rover, subtitled 'The Banish'd Cavaliers,' is set during Carnival in Naples and based on Thomas Killigrew's Thomaso. It follows the licentious Captain Willmore and Hellena, who is bound for a convent. The courtesan Angellica Bianca holds Willmore at gunpoint but relents. Lucetta drops Blunt through a trapdoor.

Oroonoko, sometimes called the earliest British novel, tells of a Coramantien prince enslaved in Suriname who kills his lover Imoinda and leads a failed slave revolt before being dismembered. The Emperor of the Moon features Dr. Baliardo trying to marry his daughters to aliens. 'The Disappointment' depicts the shepherd Lysander's impotence with Cloris, originally misattributed to the Earl of Rochester.

Virginia Woolf wrote in A Room of One's Own that women should let flowers fall on Behn's tomb (Westminster Abbey's East Cloister). Behn spied for Charles II under the codenames Astrea and Agent 160. She was part of the 'fair triumvirate of wit' with Haywood and Manley. Purcell's incidental music for her Abdelazer provided the theme for Britten's Young Person's Guide to the Orchestra.
