English composer (1913–1976), co-founder of the Aldeburgh Festival. Best known for the operas Peter Grimes, The Turn of the Screw, Billy Budd, and the War Requiem. His longtime partner, tenor Peter Pears, premiered most of his tenor roles. Other important works include The Young Person's Guide to the Orchestra (based on Purcell's Abdelazer) and A Ceremony of Carols. A key figure in 20th-century British music, he also revived interest in Purcell's operas. Arvo Pärt composed a Cantus in Memoriam Benjamin Britten after his death.
General / Biographical

English composer (1913–1976), born in Suffolk. His longtime partner, tenor Peter Pears, premiered most of his tenor roles; together they co-founded the Aldeburgh Festival (at the Snape Maltings Concert Hall). He befriended W. H. Auden while both worked on GPO Film Unit documentaries; Auden wrote the libretto for his operetta Paul Bunyan. He was commissioned by the Japanese government to write a piece for the 2600-year anniversary of the Mikado (Sinfonia da Requiem). He extensively revised Purcell's Dido and Aeneas with Imogen Holst, reviving Purcell's operas for the public. Arvo Pärt composed Cantus in Memoriam Benjamin Britten after his death. His style inspired Alexandre Desplat's score for Wes Anderson's Moonrise Kingdom.

Peter Grimes

Opera based on George Crabbe's poem 'The Borough.' The title character, a fisherman who is an outcast of his community, is blamed for the deaths of two apprentices. Ellen Orford defends him and sings the 'Embroidery Aria' after Balstrode shows her a bloodied jersey belonging to John (the apprentice who fell off a cliff). The protagonist sings 'What harbor shelters peace' and 'Now the Great Bear and Pleiades.' At the opera's end, Captain Balstrode advises Peter to sail out to sea and drown himself. Four Sea Interludes ('Dawn,' 'Sunday Morning,' 'Moonlight,' 'Storm') are excerpted for concert performance. The four is the fourth a passacaglia. A Ned Keene leads the pub in a round during a storm. War Requiem

Commissioned for the consecration of the rebuilt Coventry Cathedral (bombed in WWII). Interweaves traditional Latin Mass text with poems by Wilfred Owen sung by the tenor and baritone soloists. Unified by a C–F-sharp tritone motif. Premiered in 1962 with Peter Pears and Dietrich Fischer-Dieskau as soloists; Heather Harper replaced Galina Vishnevskaya as the soprano ten days before the premiere. Features a boys' choir performing offstage accompanied by portable organ. Concludes with an eight-part choral canon 'in paradisum deducant.' The tenor sings 'Strange Meeting' (Wilfred Owen poem), addressing another person with the words 'strange friend.' Wikipedia

Summary of Facts

Benjamin Britten (1913–1976) was England's leading 20th-century composer and a central figure in the revival of British opera. His longtime romantic partner was tenor Peter Pears, who premiered most of his tenor roles; together they co-founded the Aldeburgh Festival, held at the Snape Maltings Concert Hall. He befriended W. H. Auden while working on GPO Film Unit documentaries; Auden later wrote the libretto for his operetta Paul Bunyan. He was commissioned by the Japanese government to write a piece for the 2600-year anniversary of the Mikado, producing the Sinfonia da Requiem. He also extensively revised Purcell's Dido and Aeneas with Imogen Holst, widely credited with reviving Purcell's operas for modern audiences. After Britten's death, Arvo Pärt composed Cantus in Memoriam Benjamin Britten — a mensuration canon for string orchestra opening with tubular bells followed by descending A-minor scales at varying speeds.

Britten's most performed opera is Peter Grimes (1945), based on George Crabbe's poem 'The Borough.' The title character, a fisherman ostracized by the Borough community, is blamed for the deaths of two apprentices. Ellen Orford sings the 'Embroidery Aria' after Balstrode shows her a bloodied jersey belonging to the apprentice John, who fell from a cliff. Peter sings 'Now the Great Bear and Pleiades' and eventually sails out to drown himself on Balstrode's advice. Ned Keene leads the pub in a round during a storm. An ominous passacaglia in Act II plays while a non-speaking boy is led to Peter's hut. In his final monologue, Britten has the tuba imitate a foghorn by playing two notes a semitone apart. Four Sea Interludes ('Dawn,' 'Sunday Morning,' 'Moonlight,' 'Storm') have been excerpted for concert performance.

The War Requiem (1962) was commissioned for the consecration of the rebuilt Coventry Cathedral. It interweaves traditional Latin Mass text with nine poems by Wilfred Owen (including 'Strange Meeting' and 'Futility') sung by the tenor and baritone soloists, and is unified throughout by a C–F-sharp tritone motif. The premiere featured Pears and Dietrich Fischer-Dieskau; Heather Harper replaced Galina Vishnevskaya as soprano ten days before the premiere. A boys' choir performs offstage accompanied by portable organ. The work concludes with an eight-part choral canon 'in paradisum deducant.'

The Turn of the Screw is a chamber opera based on Henry James, in which each of 16 scenes is preceded by a variation on a twelve-tone theme (beginning A-D-B-E) that first appears during a coach journey to a country house. The ghosts Peter Quint and Miss Jessel sing 'the ceremony of innocence is drowned' while haunting the Governess and the children Miles and Flora. Miles sings the trancelike Latin word 'Malo' and a list of masculine Latin nouns with homosexual innuendos.

The Young Person's Guide to the Orchestra is an educational work commissioned for a documentary (directed by Muir Mathieson, featuring Malcolm Sargent), subtitled 'Variations and Fugue on a Theme of Purcell,' based on the Rondeau from Purcell's Abdelazer. Variations E through H feature string instruments. A Ceremony of Carols is scored for treble choir and harp; it opens with the processional 'Hodie Christus natus est' and includes movements like 'Adam lay i-bounden' and 'Balulalow.'

Other important works include: Simple Symphony (for string orchestra, movements 'Boisterous Bourree' and 'Playful Pizzicato,' based on youthful piano pieces); Billy Budd (opera, stammering sailor murders master-at-arms Claggart); Albert Herring (comic opera, protagonist declared May King since there are no virgins for May Queen; crushed wreath found in a well); Gloriana (opera about Elizabeth I and the Earl of Essex, Courtly Dances frequently excerpted); Noye's Fludde (opera for young performers based on a medieval mystery play, rain depicted by striking mugs with a wooden spoon); Serenade for Tenor, Horn, and Strings (for Pears, Prologue and Epilogue for solo horn, settings of Blake and Tennyson); and the church parables Curlew River, The Burning Fiery Furnace, and The Prodigal Son (based on Japanese noh, librettist William Plomer).
