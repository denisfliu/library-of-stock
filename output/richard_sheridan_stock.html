The School for Scandal

A comedy of manners about the Surface brothers, Joseph and Charles, who are visited by their uncle Sir Oliver Surface in disguise. Oliver poses as the moneylender 'Mr. Premium' (with the help of Moses) and as the poor 'Mr. Stanley' to test his nephews. Joseph Surface is the hypocritical villain, while Charles is the good-natured spendthrift who sells family portraits at auction but refuses to sell Oliver's picture for a hundred pounds. The famous 'screen scene' has a folding screen pulled down to reveal Lady Teazle and her affair with Joseph Surface. The gossipmongers Lady Sneerwell and Snake drive the plot. Sir Peter Teazle's affairs are meddled with. This play was adapted from John Vanbrugh's The Relapse in a sanitized version called A Trip to Scarborough.

The Rivals

A comedy set in Bath in which Captain Jack Absolute pretends to be 'Ensign Beverley' to woo Lydia Languish, whose guardian Mrs. Malaprop constantly misuses words (calling someone 'the very pineapple of politeness' instead of 'pinnacle' and describing someone as 'headstrong as an allegory on the banks of the Nile' instead of 'alligator'). Sir Lucius O'Trigger almost duels Jack Absolute over a woman until he realizes he was actually sending love letters to Mrs. Malaprop (thinking she was 'Delia'). The maid Lucy earns tips delivering love notes but misleads Sir Lucius about 'Delia's' identity. The play also features Bob Acres. Mrs. Malaprop's guardian recommends Fordyce's Sermons over Ovid.

Summary of Facts

Richard Brinsley Sheridan was an Irish playwright and politician, best known for two comedies of manners: The School for Scandal and The Rivals.

The School for Scandal centers on the Surface brothers, Joseph (hypocritical villain) and Charles (good-natured spendthrift), tested by their uncle Sir Oliver Surface in disguise. Oliver poses as the moneylender 'Mr. Premium' with Moses's help and as the poor 'Mr. Stanley.' Charles sells family portraits at auction but refuses to sell Oliver's picture. The famous 'screen scene' reveals Lady Teazle's affair with Joseph when a folding screen is pulled down. The gossipmongers Lady Sneerwell and Snake drive the intrigue around Sir Peter Teazle's affairs.

The Rivals is set in Bath and features Captain Jack Absolute pretending to be 'Ensign Beverley' to woo Lydia Languish. Her guardian Mrs. Malaprop constantly misuses words — calling someone 'the very pineapple of politeness' (for pinnacle) and describing someone as 'headstrong as an allegory on the banks of the Nile' (for alligator) — giving us the term 'malapropism.' Sir Lucius O'Trigger almost duels Jack until realizing he was sending love letters to Mrs. Malaprop, mistaking her for 'Delia.' The cunning maid Lucy earns tips delivering love notes.

The Critic is a satirical play featuring a rehearsal of Mr. Puff's play The Spanish Armada, attended by Mr. Sneer and Mr. Dangle, with a vain writer based on Richard Cumberland. Sheridan also adapted John Vanbrugh's The Relapse as A Trip to Scarborough. He came from a literary family: his mother Frances Sheridan wrote The Discovery.
