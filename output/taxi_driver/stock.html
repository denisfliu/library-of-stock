Summary of Facts

Taxi Driver (1976) is a landmark film directed by Martin Scorsese, starring Robert De Niro as Travis Bickle — an insomniac Vietnam veteran who works the night shift as a New York City taxi driver. Repulsed by the city's corruption and vice, Travis becomes dangerously obsessed with cleaning up the streets. He becomes romantically fixated on Betsy (Cybill Shepherd), a beautiful campaign volunteer for presidential candidate Senator Palantine; Travis says of her, 'She appeared like an angel, out of this filthy mess,' in a slow-motion shot of her walking in a white dress. When he takes her on a disastrous date to a porn theater, she refuses to see him again. His subsequent phone apology is filmed with the camera panning right to an empty hallway, holding there as we hear The Conversation — one of the film's most striking shots. A fellow driver named Wizard tells Travis: 'You get a job, you become the job.'

Travis's disgust with society intensifies. He attempts to assassinate Senator Palantine at a rally, but flees when spotted by Secret Service. He then turns his violent impulse toward rescuing the teenage prostitute Iris (Jodie Foster) from her pimp Sport, culminating in a bloody shootout at a brothel. The film's ending — in which Travis is celebrated as a hero — is famously theorized to be his dying fantasy. The climactic scene includes Travis using a rearview mirror and his bloody hand to mime shooting himself in the head. Earlier, Betsy tells Travis he reminds her of 'that song by Kris Kristofferson' because he's a 'walking contradiction.'

Taxi Driver won the Palme d'Or at the 1976 Cannes Film Festival and was nominated for four Oscars. Its influence on cinema has been enormous: Francis Ford Coppola's The Conversation drew on similar themes, and the film is frequently cited in discussions of New Hollywood alienation filmmaking.
