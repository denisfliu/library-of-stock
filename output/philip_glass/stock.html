American minimalist composer (born 1937), the most-performed living American composer. Known for repetitive arpeggiated structures and additive rhythmic processes. Student of Nadia Boulanger. Created the Philip Glass Ensemble. Major works include the Portrait Trilogy operas (Einstein on the Beach, Satyagraha, Akhnaten), the film score Koyaanisqatsi, the symphonies Low and Heroes (after David Bowie albums), and the album Glassworks.
General / Biographical

American minimalist composer, widely considered the most-performed living American composer. Student of Nadia Boulanger and Vincent Persichetti (who also taught him at Juilliard). Juilliard classmate of Steve Reich. Leads the Philip Glass Ensemble, for which he composed Music in Twelve Parts. Worked closely with the Mabou Mines theater company. Uses a characteristic ensemble of four saxophones, two flutes, three electric organs, and female voice. Einstein on the Beach

Five-hour opera with no conventional plot, created in collaboration with director Robert Wilson. First in Glass's Portrait Trilogy. Divided into three acts — 'Train,' 'Trial,' and 'Field/Spaceship' — separated by five 'Knee Plays' (intermezzos in which solfege syllables are chanted and numbers counted). A solo violinist is dressed as the title figure (Einstein). The audience is welcome to leave and return at any time. Includes text by Christopher Knowles ('Prematurely Air-Conditioned Supermarket' by Lucinda Childs, read from a bed) and choreography by Lucinda Childs. A bus driver narrates a story of love at the end of one Knee Play. Wikipedia

Akhnaten

Third opera in the Portrait Trilogy, about the monotheist pharaoh Akhenaten. Title role is a countertenor (who thus sings higher than his wife Nefertiti, a contralto). Celesta doubles the synthesizer; notably lacks violins, giving strings a stronger tone. An offstage chorus sings Psalm 104. The Hymn to the Sun ('Thou dost appear beautiful') in Act II is always performed in the audience's native language. A tour guide declares 'there is nothing left' of Akhenaten's temples in Act III. Phelim McDermott's staging uses juggling background performers. Title character is frequently doubled by the trumpet. Wikipedia

Koyaanisqatsi (Film Score)

Score for Godfrey Reggio's 1982 experimental film. The title is a Hopi word meaning 'life out of balance.' Bass vocalists chant the five-syllable title repeatedly in an ominous descending four-note organ ostinato. The track 'Pruit Igoe' plays during the destruction of the Pruitt–Igoe housing complex. String Quartets

Summary of Facts

Philip Glass (born 1937) is an American minimalist composer widely recognized as the most-performed living American composer. He studied with Nadia Boulanger in Paris and with Vincent Persichetti at Juilliard, where he was a classmate of Steve Reich. He leads the Philip Glass Ensemble, for which he composed Music in Twelve Parts — a three-hour work originally misunderstood to have twelve movements when Glass actually meant twelve musical lines. Glass has long association with the Mabou Mines theater company (for whom he wrote Music for Voices) and collaborated extensively with the Kronos Quartet, which recorded his string quartets and various film scores.

Glass's most celebrated achievement is his 'Portrait Trilogy' of operas about three world-historical figures: Einstein on the Beach (1976, Albert Einstein), Satyagraha (1979, Gandhi), and Akhnaten (1983, the Egyptian pharaoh Akhenaten). Einstein on the Beach, created in collaboration with director Robert Wilson, is a five-hour opera with no conventional plot. Audiences are welcome to leave and return at will. It is divided into three acts — 'Train,' 'Trial,' and 'Field/Spaceship' — separated by five 'Knee Plays' in which chorus members count numbers and chant solfege syllables. A solo violinist is dressed as Einstein throughout. Text contributions came from Christopher Knowles and Lucinda Childs, including the section 'Prematurely Air-Conditioned Supermarket' read by Childs from a bed. The opera opens with a white square highlighting two figures seated at desks. A Los Angeles Opera production staged dancers in suspenders on scaffolding in front of LED circles. Satyagraha is sung entirely in Sanskrit, and its third act contains the 'Newcastle March'; Miss Schlesen and Kallenbach sing a duet about 'how the saints attained success.' Akhnaten features a countertenor in the title role, meaning Akhenaten sings higher notes than his contralto wife Nefertiti. The opera lacks violins (the celesta doubles the synthesizer), its offstage chorus sings Psalm 104, and the 'Hymn to the Sun' ('Thou dost appear beautiful') from Act II is always performed in the audience's native language. The libretto uses Hebrew, Akkadian, and ancient Egyptian. A tour guide in Act III declares 'there is nothing left' of Akhenaten's temples. Phelim McDermott's celebrated staging uses juggling performers in the background.

Glass's film scores are equally prominent in quizbowl. Koyaanisqatsi (1982), directed by Godfrey Reggio, opens with an ominous descending four-note organ ostinato while bass vocalists repeatedly chant the five-syllable Hopi title ('life out of balance'). The track 'Pruit Igoe' accompanies the demolition of the Pruitt–Igoe housing complex. It is the first in a Reggio trilogy. Glass also scored Martin Scorsese's Kundun, received an Academy Award nomination for The Hours, and collaborated with Errol Morris on A Brief History of Time and The Thin Blue Line.

Glass composed two celebrated symphonies based on David Bowie's Berlin Trilogy albums: the Low Symphony and the Heroes Symphony. Symphony No. 12, based on Bowie's Lodger, premiered in 2019. Glass has composed nine string quartets; No. 2 ('Company') was incidental music for a Mabou Mines production of Samuel Beckett's Company; Glass calls the novella a 'prose poem.' No. 3 ('Mishima') was extracted from his Mishima: A Life in Four Chapters soundtrack. No. 4 was commissioned by Geoffrey Hendricks, premiered at the Hauser Gallery, and was written in memory of the artist Brian Buczak. The most recent (No. 9, 2022) was inspired by King Lear. The Kronos Quartet has recorded many of them.

Other important works: Glassworks (1982) is a six-movement album including 'Floe,' 'Island,' 'Rubric,' and 'Facades,' with a special 'Sony Walkman mix'; Songs from Liquid Days set lyrics by Suzanne Vega (in the movement 'Freezing') accompanied by a string quartet; the piano album Solo Piano includes five 'Metamorphosis' pieces (Pearl Jam traditionally opens concerts with 'Metamorphosis Two') and Mad Rush (inspired by the Dalai Lama's visit to New York); 1000 Airplanes on the Roof is a one-person opera about alien abduction, created with playwright David Henry Hwang; The Perfect American is an opera about Walt Disney on his deathbed; The Trial (2014) adapted Kafka with a libretto by Christopher Hampton; and the Concerto Fantasy for Two Timpanists requires the players to use their hands for higher pitches. The play Philip Glass Buys a Loaf of Bread by David Ives (in the anthology All in the Timing) parodies Glass's style with lines like 'Go! Go! Go! Time! Time! Time!'
