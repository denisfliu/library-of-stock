American author of French Creole heritage who wrote about Louisiana life. Best known for the novella The Awakening (about Edna Pontellier) and short stories including 'Desiree's Baby,' 'The Story of an Hour,' 'A Pair of Silk Stockings,' and 'The Storm.' Her works are considered early feminist literature exploring female independence and sexuality, as well as racial issues in Creole Louisiana.
The Awakening

Chopin's most famous work, a novella about Edna Pontellier, who discovers her own sexuality and identity while vacationing on Grand Isle in Louisiana. Edna has an affair with Robert Lebrun, who leaves a note reading 'Good-by - because I love you' in the 'pigeon house' she moves into after leaving her husband Leonce. The novel begins with a parrot squawking 'Allez vous-en!' and ends with Edna drowning herself in the Gulf of Mexico. A crippled bird flutters overhead at the end, mirroring the parrot from the start. Edna breaks a glass vase, stomps on her wedding ring, keeps a picture of a 'great tragedian' on her desk, and befriends the old pianist Mademoiselle Reisz. Edna's father is a former colonel in the Confederate army.

"Desiree's Baby"

A story about Desiree, an adopted foundling raised by the Valmondes, who marries Armand Aubigny, owner of the L'Abri estate. When their baby appears to have dark skin, resembling the 'quadroon' boy fanning him with peacock feathers, Armand expels Desiree and the baby. Desiree disappears into the bayou. At the end, Armand discovers a letter from his mother revealing that it is he, not Desiree, who has Black ancestry. Armand had earlier burned a willow cradle and read the letter. Madame Valmonde visits and is astonished by the child; the turbaned wet nurse Zandrine attends the baby. Desiree is compared to La Blanche.

"The Story of an Hour"

"A Pair of Silk Stockings"

A story about Mrs. Sommers, who unexpectedly comes into possession of fifteen dollars she had intended to spend on clothes for her children. Instead, she goes on a shopping spree for herself, buying kid gloves, stockings, and attending a matinee. She tips a waiter who bows 'as before a princess of royal blood' and shares a box of candy. At the end, she wishes the cable car would 'never stop' and 'go on and on with her forever.'

Other Works

Chopin also wrote the novel At Fault, in which the sawmill manager David pursues the Creole widow Therese Lafirme; Fanny drowns while scavenging for alcohol. The short story 'The Storm' depicts a sexual encounter between Alcee and Calixta, a sequel to 'At the 'Cadian Ball.' The collection A Night in Acadie includes 'Regret' (a spinster cries after caring for four children) and 'A Respectable Woman' (Mrs. Baroda is attracted to Gouvernail). The collection Bayou Folk includes 'Desiree's Baby' and 'The Gentleman of Bayou Teche.' Emily Toth called Chopin 'a rogue in porcelain,' drawing on Per Seyersted's influential monograph.

Summary of Facts

Kate Chopin (born Katherine O'Flaherty) was an American author of French Creole heritage whose works explore female independence, sexuality, and racial issues in Louisiana. She is considered an early feminist writer.

Chopin's most famous work is The Awakening, a novella about Edna Pontellier, who discovers her own sexuality while vacationing on Grand Isle. Edna has an affair with Robert Lebrun, who leaves a note reading 'Good-by - because I love you' in the 'pigeon house' she moves into. The novel begins with a parrot squawking 'Allez vous-en!' and ends with Edna drowning herself in the Gulf of Mexico. A crippled bird flutters overhead at the end, mirroring the opening. Edna breaks a glass vase, stomps on her wedding ring, and befriends the pianist Mademoiselle Reisz. Her husband Leonce and the recurring character Gouvernail also appear.

'Desiree's Baby' is Chopin's most frequently clued short story, about Armand Aubigny who expels his adopted wife Desiree and their baby when the child appears to have dark skin, resembling a 'quadroon' boy fanning him with peacock feathers. Desiree disappears into the bayou. The twist ending reveals that Armand himself, not Desiree, has Black ancestry, discovered through a letter from his mother. 'The Story of an Hour' is a very short story in which Louise Mallard whispers 'free, free, free!' after hearing her husband died in a railroad accident, then dies of heart disease when he returns alive -- 'of the joy that kills.' 'A Pair of Silk Stockings' follows Mrs. Sommers, who spends fifteen dollars on herself instead of her children.

Chopin's other works include At Fault (her first novel, about David and Therese Lafirme), 'The Storm' (a sequel to 'At the 'Cadian Ball' depicting a sexual encounter between Alcee and Calixta), and stories collected in A Night in Acadie and Bayou Folk. Emily Toth called Chopin 'a rogue in porcelain.' Chopin's works are set in the Creole and Acadian communities of Louisiana, and she is frequently clued alongside other Southern and feminist authors.
