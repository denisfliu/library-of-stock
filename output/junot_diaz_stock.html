Dominican-American author known for his recurring narrator Yunior de las Casas. Best known for the Pulitzer-winning novel The Brief Wondrous Life of Oscar Wao, about an overweight Dominican sci-fi fanatic at Rutgers who is cursed by a fuku originating from the Trujillo regime. His short story collections Drown and This is How You Lose Her also feature Yunior. Known for second-person narration, Spanglish, and stories about Dominican immigrant life. Also wrote the essay 'MFA vs. POC' critiquing the whiteness of MFA programs.
The Brief Wondrous Life of Oscar Wao

Diaz's Pulitzer-winning novel, mostly narrated by Yunior de las Casas. Oscar is an overweight Dominican sci-fi nerd/fanatic at Rutgers trying to lose his virginity. His family is haunted by a fuku curse inflicted by the dictator Rafael Trujillo (whom Yunior compares to Sauron). Oscar is beaten by Solomon Grundy and Gorilla Grod in a canefield but saved by a golden mongoose. Oscar's mother Belicia (Beli) sees a faceless man waving at her and a goat carcass while being driven from an outing with 'the Gangster.' Two cops beat Beli hard enough to kill her unborn fetus, leaving her in a canefield where she is also rescued by a golden mongoose. Socorro jumps in front of a train, leading Beli to be raised by La Inca. Both Beli and Oscar are left to die in canefields. The story of 'Poor Abelard,' Beli's cancer-stricken father, is included. The novel begins by explaining the zafa, a counterspell to the fuku curse. Oscar's nickname resembles 'Oscar Wilde' in a Dominican accent. Lola is Oscar's sister. Oscar falls in love with Ybon and is killed. Wikipedia

This is How You Lose Her

A short story collection narrated by Yunior. Contains 'The Cheater's Guide to Love' (the final story), in which Yunior tries to put his life back together after his fiancee discovers he cheated on her for six years; a Kenyan man marries a girl who sends her ex-boyfriend emails of fifty mistresses; five sections labeled by years after the breakup; ends with 'the half-life of love is forever.' Also contains 'How to Date a Brown Girl (Black Girl, White Girl, or Halfie)' — dating advice in second person, ending with 'put the government cheese back in its place before your mom kills you.' 'Alma' features the title girlfriend discovering cheating with an Indian girl named Laxmi. 'The Pura Principle': Yunior's brother Rafa gets cancer and marries the title undocumented girl. 'Miss Lora': teenage narrator has affair with a muscular female teacher while discussing 'The Day After.' Starts with 'The Sun, the Moon, The Stars' about Yunior's struggles with Magdalena. General / Biographical

Dominican-American author. Recurring character Yunior de las Casas narrates across multiple works. Known for second-person narration and Spanglish. Creative writing professor at MIT. Wrote the essay 'MFA vs. POC' critiquing white-dominated MFA programs (in 2014 New Yorker). In 2018, Zinzi Clemmons accused him of sexual harassment; he stepped down as chair of the Pulitzer Prize Board. Characters include a man who calls his mother's prayer group 'The Four Horsefaces of the Apocalypse' and a girl who writes 'tarde venientibus ossa' ('to the latecomers are left the bones').

Summary of Facts

Junot Diaz is a Dominican-American author whose works revolve around the recurring narrator Yunior de las Casas and themes of Dominican immigrant life, masculinity, and the legacy of the Trujillo dictatorship.

His most important work is The Brief Wondrous Life of Oscar Wao, a Pulitzer-winning novel mostly narrated by Yunior. Oscar is an overweight Dominican sci-fi nerd at Rutgers trying to lose his virginity, whose family is haunted by a fuku curse inflicted by dictator Rafael Trujillo (whom Yunior compares to Sauron). Oscar is beaten by Solomon Grundy and Gorilla Grod in a canefield but saved by a golden mongoose. His mother Belicia sees a faceless man and a goat carcass while being driven from an outing with 'the Gangster'; cops beat her hard enough to kill her unborn fetus, leaving her in a canefield where she is also rescued by a golden mongoose. Socorro jumps in front of a train, leading Beli to be raised by La Inca. The novel begins by explaining the zafa, a counterspell to the fuku. Oscar falls in love with Ybon and is killed. The story of 'Poor Abelard,' Beli's father, is included.

This is How You Lose Her is a short story collection featuring Yunior. 'The Cheater's Guide to Love' follows Yunior after his fiancee discovers six years of infidelity through emails of fifty mistresses; it ends with 'the half-life of love is forever.' 'How to Date a Brown Girl' gives dating advice in second person, ending with 'put the government cheese back in its place.' 'Miss Lora' describes a teenager's affair with a muscular female teacher. 'Alma' features cheating discovered with Indian girl Laxmi. 'The Pura Principle' has brother Rafa getting cancer.

Drown is his first collection, opening with 'Ysrael' about a boy whose face was eaten by a pig. Another story features a father forbidding eating to prevent vomiting in a Volkswagen.

Biographically, Diaz is known for second-person narration and Spanglish. He teaches at MIT and wrote the essay 'MFA vs. POC' critiquing white-dominated programs. In 2018, Zinzi Clemmons accused him of sexual harassment, leading him to step down as Pulitzer Prize Board chair.
