American composer and lyricist, widely considered the most important figure in 20th-century musical theater. Known for writing both music and lyrics to Sweeney Todd, Company, Into the Woods, Sunday in the Park with George, Follies, Merrily We Roll Along, and A Little Night Music, as well as lyrics for West Side Story (composed by Leonard Bernstein). Protege of Oscar Hammerstein and student of Milton Babbitt. His book Finishing the Hat collects annotated lyrics. Frequent collaborators include Hal Prince, James Lapine, Bernadette Peters, and orchestrator Jonathan Tunick. Died in 2021.
Sweeney Todd (1979)

Sondheim's stage adaptation of a Victorian penny-dreadful about the "Demon Barber of Fleet Street." Features the darkly comic song "A Little Priest" in which Mrs. Lovett and Sweeney imagine the taste of pies made from various professions ("Shepherd's pie peppered / With actual shepherd on top"). The musical begins with an ominous organ solo cut off by a shrill whistle. Anthony Hope sings "Johanna" ("I feel you, and one day, I'll steal you") to Judge Turpin's ward. Features a Stravinsky-inspired "madness motif" repeating the eighth-note pattern D, C, D, C, E-flat, C, F, C. Wikipedia

Company (1970)

A concept musical about the unmarried 35-year-old Bobby who observes the dysfunctional marriages of his friends during his birthday party. Bobby sings "Being Alive" ("Somebody, hold me too close / Somebody, hurt me too deep"). Amy panics on the morning of her wedding, lambasting "a prehistoric ritual where everybody promises fidelity forever" in a rapid patter song. The "personable" / "coercin' a bull" rhyme appears here. Marianne Elliott staged a genderbent revival. The protagonist's name is repeated by a group at the opening. Wikipedia

A Little Night Music (1973)

Sondheim's adaptation of Ingmar Bergman's film Smiles of a Summer Night. Features the song "Send in the Clowns," sung by Desiree Armfeldt ("Isn't it rich? / Are we a pair? / Me here at last on the ground and you in midair"). Bernadette Peters succeeded Catherine Zeta-Jones in the role of Desiree in the Broadway revival. The melody of "Send in the Clowns" was written with many pauses to fit Glynis Johns's voice. Ravel inspired the many "Night Waltzes" orchestrated by Jonathan Tunick. Wikipedia

Sunday in the Park with George (1984)

A musical about French pointillist painter Georges Seurat in the months leading up to his masterpiece A Sunday Afternoon on the Island of La Grande Jatte. Features the songs "Finishing the Hat," "Color and Light" (in which Seurat sings "Red red red red Red red orange"), "Move On," and "No Life." The mistress Dot sings "a trickle of sweat, the back of the head." Seurat's work is displayed in 1984 with Chromolume #7. Mandy Patinkin debuted the role; Bernadette Peters played Dot and Marie. Written with collaborator James Lapine. Wikipedia

Merrily We Roll Along (1981)

Sondheim's infamous flop that uses reverse chronology to follow the disintegrating friendship of Mary Flynn, Charley Kringas, and Franklin Shepard from adulthood back to adolescence. Gussie has an affair with composer Frank Shepard. The chorus sings about "see[ing] the pretty countryside" and "bursting with dreams" during scene changes. Jonathan Groff starred as Frank in the 2023 revival alongside Daniel Radcliffe and Lindsay Mendez. Sondheim worked with Hal Prince until this show's failure. He was working on another reverse chronology musical, All Together Now, before his death. Wikipedia

Follies (1971)

A musical about the 30th reunion of a vaudeville act, exploring the loveless marriage of Phyllis Rogers and Ben Stone. Sally Durant's torch song "Losing My Mind" reveals her lifelong obsession with Ben. Other songs include "Could I Leave You?," "Loveland" (which mocks the characters), and "I'm Still Here" (in which the singer says she's "been through Herbert and J. Edgar Hoover"). Wikipedia

Summary of Facts

Stephen Sondheim is the single most frequently asked-about composer in quizbowl Fine Arts musical theater questions. Clues overwhelmingly identify him through his best-known musicals: Sweeney Todd (the "Demon Barber of Fleet Street," "A Little Priest," the ominous organ opening), Company (Bobby's 35th birthday, "Being Alive," Amy's wedding patter), and A Little Night Music ("Send in the Clowns," the Bergman adaptation, Desiree Armfeldt). His role as lyricist of West Side Story (with Leonard Bernstein) is the single most common giveaway clue.

At the middle difficulty level, clues draw on Sunday in the Park with George (Seurat, "Finishing the Hat," "Color and Light"), Into the Woods (fairy tale mashup, "Agony"), Merrily We Roll Along (reverse chronology, his biggest flop, the 2023 revival with Jonathan Groff), and Follies ("Losing My Mind," "I'm Still Here"). His book Finishing the Hat is referenced frequently as a source of lyrical commentary.

Hard/power clues focus on musical-technical details (the Stravinsky-inspired madness motif in Sweeney Todd, the descending minor third in Company, Ravel's influence on A Little Night Music's waltzes), biographical details (student of Milton Babbitt, protege of Oscar Hammerstein, three artistic principles), and obscure facts (co-writing The Last of Sheila with Anthony Perkins, the Glass Onion cameo, the Vertigo opinion in Finale by D.T. Max, Here We Are with David Ives). His collaborators — Hal Prince, James Lapine, Bernadette Peters, Jonathan Tunick — appear at various difficulty levels.
