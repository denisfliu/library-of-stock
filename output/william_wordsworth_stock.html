Other Works and General

Wordsworth co-authored Lyrical Ballads (1798) with Coleridge, which included a famous Preface defining poetry as 'the spontaneous overflow of powerful feelings.' He was Poet Laureate (1843-1850). He wrote 'Ode: Intimations of Immortality' ('The Child is father of the Man'; 'trailing clouds of glory'). Jonathan Bate's Romantic Ecology is a foundational ecocriticism work about Wordsworth. He wrote 'The Solitary Reaper,' 'Michael,' 'Resolution and Independence' (the Leech-Gatherer), and 'We Are Seven.' He lived in the Lake District and was one of the 'Lake Poets.' His sister Dorothy was an important companion and diarist. He wrote 'The World Is Too Much with Us' ('Getting and spending, we lay waste our powers').

Summary of Facts

William Wordsworth was an English Romantic poet who served as Poet Laureate from 1843 to 1850. With Samuel Taylor Coleridge, he co-authored Lyrical Ballads (1798), whose famous Preface defined poetry as 'the spontaneous overflow of powerful feelings.' He is one of the most frequently clued Romantic poets.

The Prelude, subtitled 'Growth of a Poet's Mind,' is Wordsworth's major autobiographical poem in blank verse, published posthumously. It recounts childhood experiences including skating, boat-stealing, and 'spots of time,' and includes the ascent of Mount Snowdon and reflections on the French Revolution.

'Lines Composed a Few Miles above Tintern Abbey' describes the speaker returning after five years to the banks of the Wye, finding 'a sense sublime / Of something far more deeply interfused.' It is addressed to his sister Dorothy.

'I Wandered Lonely as a Cloud' describes encountering 'a host of golden daffodils' beside a lake; later 'they flash upon that inward eye / Which is the bliss of solitude.' 'London, 1802' addresses Milton: 'Milton! thou shouldst be living at this hour' because England 'is a fen / Of stagnant waters.'

'Ode: Intimations of Immortality' contains 'The Child is father of the Man' and 'trailing clouds of glory.' 'The World Is Too Much with Us' laments 'Getting and spending, we lay waste our powers.' Other poems include 'The Solitary Reaper,' 'Michael,' and 'Resolution and Independence' (the Leech-Gatherer). Jonathan Bate's Romantic Ecology is a foundational ecocriticism work about Wordsworth. He lived in the Lake District and was one of the 'Lake Poets' with Coleridge and Southey. His sister Dorothy was an important companion and diarist.
