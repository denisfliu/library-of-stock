T1 2025 LITANY (2025) Diff 8 · Literature
In one poem, one of these people declares “your soft hand is of a woman itself” before exclaiming “you smile?” that's what “[these people] call our harmony.” The question “why can’t [one of these people] lift each foot in turn” appears in another poem where one of these people claims his business is to do one action to the “souls of men” and not flesh. The claim (*) “man’s reach should exceed his grasp, or what’s a heaven for?” appears in a poem titled for one of these people that is addressed to Lucrezia. The Duke of Ferrara describes a creation by one of these people named Fra Pandolf in another poem. Fra Lippo Lippi and Andrea del Sarto held, for 10 points, what occupation during the Renaissance?
ANSWER: painter [accept artist or “Fra Lippo Lippi” or “Andrea del Sarto”]
T2 2019 Chicago Open (2019) Diff 10 · Literature
This character laments that he “was tempted and complied / and built this house and sinned,” before telling a woman “you loved me quite enough, it seems tonight. This must suffice me here.” This character confesses that because “less is more,” a “truer light of God” burns in his rivals’ “vexed beating stuffed and stopped-up brain.” He compares himself to a “weak-eyed bat no sun should tempt out of the grange whose four walls make his world” while pleading for more time with his (*) wife, who is being called away by “the Cousin’s whistle.” This character tells his wife Lucrezia that had she “given [him] soul,” they “might have risen to Rafael.” While criticizing his own passionless precision, he argues “Ah, but a man’s reach should exceed his grasp, or what’s a heaven for?” For 10 points, name this speaker of a dramatic monologue by Robert Browning, an artist known as the “faultless painter.”
ANSWER: Andrea del Sarto [accept either; prompt on del Sarto, I guess]
T3 2016 Chicago Open (2016) Diff 10 · Literature
This poem’s speaker complains about those of turbulent temperament by saying: “The sudden blood of these men! at a word — / Praise them, it boils, or blame them, it boils too.” The speaker of this poem recalls “kingly days” when “Too live the life grew, golden and not grey,” and repeatedly expresses a desire to retreat to “sober pleasant Fiesole.” This poem praises the “...perfect brow / And perfect eyes, and more than perfect mouth” of a woman, but laments that she cannot inspire beyond her beauty, saying “Had you, with these the same, but brought a (*) mind!” This poem’s narrator meditates on the success of rivals who access “a heaven that’s shut to [him],” and in whom “there burns a truer light.” This poem’s final line of “Again, the Cousin’s whistle!” references the ongoing infidelity of its speaker’s wife, Lucrezia. For 10 points, name this Robert Browning poem about an Italian painter of “faultless” but soulless works.
ANSWER: “Andrea del Sarto”
T4 2016 ACF Nationals (2016) Diff 9 · Fine Arts
This artist included a giraffe in a fresco which he made for the Medici Villa at Poggia a Caiano called The Triumph of Caesar, which was finished by his fellow Florentine artist Alessandro Allori. A man with well-defined buttocks drops a severed head in one of this artist’s grisaille frescoes on the life of John the Baptist that he made for the Cloister of the Scalzco. His version of the Last Supper contains a representation of the Trinity above two servants who stand on a balcony overlooking the central scene. Putti hold slabs from Mary’s tomb as they surround a rising Mary in a painting this artist created that was partly based on his Passerini Assumption. The most famous work of this artist of the Panciatichi Assumption was originally supposed to contain St. Bonaventure instead of St. Francis. In that painting, two angels wrap their arms around the legs of the title figure, who holds Jesus and stands on a pedestal decorated with some mythological monsters. For 10 points, name this “faultless painter” of Madonna of the Harpies.
ANSWER: Andrea del Sarto [or Andrea d'Agnolo di Francesco di Luca di Paolo del Migliore; or Andrea Lanfranchi; or Andrea Vannucchi; prompt on "Andrea"]
T5 2015 George Oppen (2015) Diff 9 · Fine Arts
A painting that this man placed over the doorway leading to a church's north transept takes its nickname from the sack that Joseph is leaning on. Two servants converse over a balcony railing, and Judas is unusually part of the main group, in the well-preserved Last Supper this artist painted on the refectory wall in San Salvi. This artist modeled St. John after a terracotta sculpture by his studio-mate Jacopo Sansovino in one of his paintings. This artist's largest undertaking was a grisaille fresco series in the Chiostro dello Scalzo, depicting the life of John the Baptist. Several angels hover over a canopy in this artist's painting Birth of the Virgin, in which one of the women attending to St. Anne is modeled after his wife (*) Lucrezia del Fede. Two angels cling to Mary's legs in this artist's most famous painting, in which she holds Christ while seated atop a polygonal pedestal decorated with reliefs of mythological monsters. For 10 points, name this "faultless painter" of the Madonna of the Harpies, named for being the son of a tailor.
ANSWER: Andrea del Sarto [resignedly accept Andrea d'Agnolo di Francesco di Luca di Paolo del Migliore]
T6 2014 ACF Nationals (2014) Diff 9 · Fine Arts
This artist painted an unusual lunette-shaped Annunciation scene with an illuminated lilac background that was changed into a rectangle by adding night-blue drapery to the corners. This artist notably juxtaposed a vignette in the lef background of St. Elizabeth leading the child John the Baptist into the hills against the main scene of a Madonna where Jesus reaches down to an angel while standing on the title steps. This artist included the panels Baptism of the People and Birth of the Baptist in a series of grisaille works showing scenes from the life of St. John the Baptist for the Cloister dello Scalzo. This student of Piero de Cosimo painted the Madonna della Scala and showed Joseph holding an open book while resting his arm on the namesake sack in his unusual interpretation of the “Rest During the Flight into Egypt” painted in a lunette. This painter of the Madonna del Sacco taught Pontormo and in his most famous work depicted Francis and John the Evangelist on either side of a stone pedestal that is carved with the title mythological monsters. For 10 points, name this Florentine painter of Madonna of the Harpies, known as “without errors.”
ANSWER: Andrea del Sarto
T7 2012 Oxford Open (2012) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
Irene Parent Duclos’s copy of this artist’s painting of Christ reaching toward an angel with a book is the only work by a woman in the Accademia; that painting, which depicted a rest en route to Egypt, was produced for the Santissima Annunziata. St. Francis holds a cross and St. John book while standing next to the title figure, who balances on a pedestal decorated sharp-taloned winged maidens, in a painting that led Vasari to call this man “the painter without errors.” For 10 points, name this artist of Madonna del Sacco and Madonna of the Harpies.
ANSWER: Andrea del Sarto

B1 2024 NASAT (2024) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
In a painting by this artist, the title figure caresses the baby Jesus and has her legs grasped by two of the title figures. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this painter of the Madonna of the Harpies, who may have used his wife as the model for his Madonna del Sacco.
ANSWER: Andrea del Sarto [or Andrea del Sarto; or Andrea d'Agnolo]
[10] Del Sarto's Madonna del Sacco is a fresco completed in one of these spaces, which are named for their shape and commonly found above archways. If found above a large door, these spaces are called tympanums.
ANSWER: lunettes
[10] Del Sarto created Passerini and Panciatichi versions of this Biblical scene, in which the Virgin Mary is taken to Heaven.
ANSWER: Assumption of the Virgin
B2 2024 ACF Winter (2024) Diff 7 · Literature
In a review of Prufrock and Other Observations, Ezra Pound claimed, “The most interesting poems in Victorian English are” those found in Men and Women, a collection by an author with this surname. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this surname of an author who is told, “Hang it all… there can be but the one ‘Sordello’” in The Cantos. An author with this surname wrote about doing an action as men “strive for right” and “turn from praise.”
ANSWER: Browning [accept Robert Browning or Elizabeth Barrett Browning] (The poem is “How do I love thee? Let me count the ways” by Elizabeth Barrett Browning.)
[10] Along with ballads, Pound translated poems in this form by Guido Cavalcanti. Elizabeth Barrett Browning wrote a collection titled for poems in this 14-line form “from the Portuguese.”
ANSWER: sonnets [accept Sonnets from the Portuguese or the Sonnets and Ballate of Guido Cavalcanti]
[10] A Pound poem about a person with this profession ends, “Show us there’s chance at least of winning through.”Robert Browning wrote, “But do not let us quarrel any more” to open a poem about a person with this profession.
ANSWER: artist [or painter] (The poems are “To Whistler, American” and “Andrea del Sarto.”)
B3 2023 ACF Regionals (2023) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
Jane Fortune’s book on the “Forgotten Artists of Florence,” Invisible Women, devotes a chapter to the all-women workshop of Sister Plautilla Nelli. For 10 points each:
[10] Nelli’s 21-foot version of this scene was restored by Fortune’s Advancing Women Artists group, which raised funds by allowing donors to “adopt” each of the 12 apostles who sit at a table with Jesus.
ANSWER: Last Supper
[10] Nelli and other nuns were encouraged to paint by this man, whose portraitist Fra Bartolomeo was often copied by Nelli. This friar inspired Sandro Botticelli’s shift to religious subjects in paintings like The Mystical Nativity.
ANSWER: Girolamo Savonarola
[10] Many of Nelli’s restored works reside at a San Salvi museum that houses this artist’s Last Supper. The AWA also restored Irene Parenti Duclos’s copy of this artist’s lunette fresco Madonna del Sacco, for which his wife may have modeled.
ANSWER: Andrea del Sarto [or Andrea del Sarto; or Andrea d’Agnolo; accept Museum of the Last Supper of Andrea del Sarto]
B4 2020 Longhorn Invitational Tournament (2020) Diff 7 · Fine Arts
In a work created for the Medici Villa in Poggia, a man can be seen carrying a cage of exotic birds in the foreground while two soldiers lean on a marble statue of this military leader and stare at a giraffe. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this leader who was depicted riding in an ornate chariot in a cycle of nine paintings by Andrea Mantegna celebrating his military victories in Gaul.
ANSWER: Gaius Julius Caesar [accept the Triumphs of Caesar] (The first version is by Andrea del Sarto)
[10] Mantegna’s Triumphs of Caesar was painted for Mantua’s Ducal Palace. That building is probably best known for a different cycle of frescoes Mantegna created for this room, in which Mantegna painted a sky-blue oculus in the di sotto in su style. Either the Italian or English translation is okay.
ANSWER: Camera Degli Sposi [accept “Wedding Room”; prompt on partial]
[10] Mantegna was also commissioned to paint multiple scenes, such as his Parnassas and the Triumph of the Virtues, for the studiolo of a noblewoman from this Mantuan family within the Ducal Palace’s “Grotto Wing”.
ANSWER: the d’Este family [accept Isabella d’Este]
B5 2017 NASAT (2017) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
This artist's fondness for heavily applying red chalk to his drawings led one critic to describe his Madonna and Child with Saint John as being "as scarlet as a slaughterhouse floor." For 10 points each:
[10] Identify this artist, celebrated as being a "Faultless Painter" in a Robert Browning poem. He painted The Madonna of the Harpies, and laid the title figures out in a diamond pattern in his Medici Holy Family.
ANSWER: Andrea del Sarto [or Andrea d'Agnolo]
[10] This animal-loving saint holds a cross on the left-hand side of del Sarto's Madonna of the Harpies. A common way to depict him in Renaissance art was to have gold lines tracing directly from Jesus's wounds to his own.
ANSWER: Saint Francis of Assisi
[10] Surprisingly, del Sarto never had a major exhibition in the United States until 2015, when this New York museum put on such a show. This museum owns Jan Vermeer's Officer and Laughing Girl and Carel Fabritius's The Goldfinch, the subject of a Donna Tartt novel.
ANSWER: The Frick Collection
B6 2017 Early Fall Tournament (EFT) (2017) Diff 7 · Fine Arts
A poem by Robert Browning about this artist notes that, even though his paintings were considered flawless or “senza errori” during his life, they lack a deeper character, a position echoed by Vasari. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this artist of The Madonna of the Harpies, whose surname indicates that he was a tailor’s son.
ANSWER: Andrea del Sarto [prompt on Andrea]
[10] Sarto made a more technically nice copy of Raphael’s group portrait of one of these people after Raphael’s death. “Screaming” holders of this title by Francis Bacon were based on a Velasquez portrait.
ANSWER: Pope [or Papa]
[10] Andrea del Sarto’s more ambitious projects include a series of works executed in this technique in the Chiostro dello Scalzo. The outsides of Netherlandish triptychs were often painted using this technique, such as the outside of Bosch’s The Last Judgment and van Eyck’s Ghent Altarpiece.
ANSWER: grisaille [griz-EYE] [prompt on answers like gray or black-and-white]

This person sits high atop a chariot in the final painting of a series in the Ducal Palace of Mantua. For 10 points each:
