American essayist and novelist from California. Known for essay collections Slouching Towards Bethlehem and The White Album, the memoir The Year of Magical Thinking about her husband John Gregory Dunne's death, and the novel Play It As It Lays. A key figure of New Journalism who wrote about California counterculture, Hollywood, and grief.
Slouching Towards Bethlehem

Didion's first major essay collection, divided into sections including 'Life Styles in the Golden Land' and 'Seven Places of the Mind.' The title essay, named after a Yeats quote, describes the Haight-Ashbury district of San Francisco in 1967, opening with 'The center was not holding.' In that essay, Didion meets a five-year-old girl given acid and peyote ('High Kindergarten'), follows drug-dealer Deadeye on Haight Street, and cannot find Chester Anderson after being accused of 'media poisoning.' 'Some Dreamers of the Golden Dream' covers Lucille Miller's conviction for murdering her husband in a burning car in San Bernardino. 'Goodbye to All That' describes her disillusionment with New York City and notes 'it is distinctly possible to stay too long at the Fair.' 'On Keeping a Notebook' explains that writing down anecdotes helps reconnect with past selves. 'John Wayne: A Love Song' recalls visiting the set of The Sons of Elder Katie. 'Notes from a Native Daughter' asks 'In what way does the Holy Land resemble the Sacramento Valley?'

The White Album

Didion's second major essay collection, whose title essay opens with the famous line 'We tell ourselves stories in order to live.' That essay describes a recording session of The Doors (called 'the Norman Mailers of the Top Forty' and 'missionaries of apocalyptic sex'), conversations with Manson follower Linda Kasabian, and descriptions of Black Panther meetings. Didion buys Linda Kasabian a dress to wear to her trial. She visits Eldridge Cleaver's apartment where he mocks 'Guardian dinners.' The essay 'In Bed' describes her chronic migraines. 'Many Mansions' discusses Ronald Reagan's mansion. The collection was titled after a Beatles album. Didion discussed the murder of actor Ramon Navarro and the influence of Roger Corman's biker pictures.

The Year of Magical Thinking

A grief memoir about the death of Didion's husband, the writer John Gregory Dunne, who died suddenly while their daughter Quintana Roo lay unconscious in a hospital. The book features the recurring sentence 'You sit down to dinner and life as you know it ends' and emphasizes how 'life changes fast.' The third chapter ends with Didion wondering how her husband could come back if he had no shoes (she is unable to give away his shoes). She recalls the 'vortex effect' of memories. The book discusses W.H. Auden's 'Funeral Blues.' Didion quotes 'I tell you that I shall not live two days' from Sir Gawain in the Vulgate Mort Artu.

Play It As It Lays

A novel about Maria Wyeth, a failed/institutionalized actress in Hollywood. The novel begins with the question 'What makes Iago evil?' and Maria's father taught her that overturning a rock reveals a rattlesnake. Maria compulsively drives the freeway going nowhere. She writes 'NOTHING APPLIES' on her psychological test and ends with 'I know what nothing means, and keep on playing.' BZ commits suicide, leading to Maria's institutionalization. Maria gets a ride to an abortion clinic from a 'man in white duck pants' who talks about his Cadillac. Carter is Maria's ex-husband. Didion and her husband adapted the novel into a screenplay.

Blue Nights

A 2011 memoir about the death of Didion's daughter Quintana Roo. The book opens with a girl who has 'quicksilver changes of mood' marrying a musician named Gerry Michael. Didion states 'When we talk about mortality we are talking about our children.' Didion recalls arguing with Quintana about reading Auden's 'Funeral Blues.'

Other Works and Journalism

Didion defined writing as 'a hostile act' in a talk whose title was 'stolen' from George Orwell's 'Why I Write.' She wrote Salvador, about her 1982 trip to El Salvador where 'to disappear' had become a transitive verb in Spanish. She argued for the innocence of the Central Park Five in a New York Review of Books piece. She complained about the 'democratic process' being limited to professionals on Sunday shows in a piece on the 1988 presidential campaign. She co-wrote screenplays with her husband including The Panic in Needle Park (Al Pacino's breakthrough) and A Star is Born (1976). Harrison Ford attended her Easter parties while working as a carpenter. The Center Will Not Hold is a documentary by her nephew Griffin Dunne. She recalled seeing the nuclear reactor underneath UC Berkeley. The essay 'On Self-Respect' is also frequently mentioned.

Summary of Facts

Joan Didion was an American essayist and novelist from California, a key figure of New Journalism. Her work chronicles California counterculture, Hollywood, grief, and American political life.

Didion's most frequently clued work is the essay collection Slouching Towards Bethlehem, named after a Yeats quote. The title essay describes the Haight-Ashbury district of San Francisco in 1967, opening with 'The center was not holding.' Didion meets a five-year-old given acid and peyote ('High Kindergarten'), follows drug-dealer Deadeye, and cannot find Chester Anderson. Other key essays include 'Goodbye to All That' (disillusionment with New York: 'it is distinctly possible to stay too long at the Fair'), 'Some Dreamers of the Golden Dream' (Lucille Miller's murder conviction in San Bernardino), 'John Wayne: A Love Song,' and 'Notes from a Native Daughter.'

The White Album opens with the iconic line 'We tell ourselves stories in order to live.' The title essay describes The Doors ('the Norman Mailers of the Top Forty'), conversations with Manson follower Linda Kasabian, and Black Panther meetings. Didion visits Eldridge Cleaver's apartment. The collection was named after the Beatles album.

The Year of Magical Thinking is Didion's grief memoir about the sudden death of her husband John Gregory Dunne while their daughter Quintana Roo was hospitalized. The recurring line 'You sit down to dinner and life as you know it ends' anchors the book. Didion is unable to give away her husband's shoes. Blue Nights, a second memoir, chronicles Quintana Roo's death.

Play It As It Lays follows the institutionalized Hollywood actress Maria Wyeth, who compulsively drives the freeway. The novel begins with 'What makes Iago evil?' and ends with 'I know what nothing means, and keep on playing.' BZ's suicide leads to Maria's institutionalization.

Didion also wrote Salvador (about El Salvador in 1982), co-wrote screenplays with her husband (The Panic in Needle Park, A Star is Born), and defined writing as 'a hostile act' in a talk borrowing Orwell's title 'Why I Write.' Harrison Ford attended her Easter parties as a carpenter, and her nephew Griffin Dunne directed the documentary The Center Will Not Hold.
