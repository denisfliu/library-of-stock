English architect (1573–1652), credited with introducing Palladian ideals to Britain. Best known for the Banqueting House at Whitehall (whose ceiling Rubens painted with the Apotheosis of James I) and the Queen's House in Greenwich (the first consciously classical building in Britain). Also designed the Covent Garden piazza and St. Paul's Church, Covent Garden. Earlier career as a set and costume designer for Ben Jonson's court masques.
Banqueting House at Whitehall

Built in 1622 as part of the Palace of Whitehall; it is the only remaining part of that destroyed complex. Interior uses a double-cube design. Rubens painted the nine-panel ceiling depicting the Apotheosis of James I (who had commissioned the mural, promising to knight Rubens). The building stopped being used for masques when smoke was found to be damaging the ceiling. James I originally wanted it to be part of 'the biggest palace in Christendom.' Wikipedia

Queen's House, Greenwich

Designed first for Anne of Denmark (consort of James I, first queen consort of both England and Scotland), then completed for Queen Henrietta Maria. The Queen's House was the first consciously classical building in Britain and contains the Tulip Staircase — the first self-supporting spiral staircase in England. The two-part design of Greenwich Hospital was split to allow an unimpeded view from this building. Wikipedia

Summary of Facts

Inigo Jones (1573–1652) was the pioneering English architect credited with introducing Palladian ideals to British architecture. His most famous building is the Banqueting House at Whitehall (1622), the only remaining part of the Palace of Whitehall, which burned down in 1689. Its interior uses a double-cube design, and Rubens painted the nine-panel ceiling depicting the Apotheosis of James I — a work commissioned by the king in exchange for a knighthood. The building stopped being used for masques when smoke was found to be damaging the ceiling.

Jones designed the Queen's House at Greenwich, originally commissioned by Anne of Denmark (James I's queen consort and the first queen consort of both England and Scotland), making it the first consciously classical building in Britain. The Queen's House contains the Tulip Staircase, the first self-supporting spiral staircase in England. The two-part design of the later Greenwich Hospital was split to preserve the view from the Queen's House.

His Covent Garden development transplanted the Italian piazza form to England, creating London's first modern public square. The rectangular design was inspired by the Place Royale in Paris. St. Paul's Church, Covent Garden—one end of the piazza—features four Tuscan columns supporting a portico.

Before his architecture career, Jones served as a stage and costume designer for Ben Jonson's court masques, including The Masque of Blackness and The Hue and Cry After Cupid, and introduced Italian theatrical innovations to England. He later oversaw the restoration of Old St. Paul's Cathedral. He published a reconstruction of Stonehenge claiming it was a Roman site with a hexagonal structure at its center. With Salomon de Caus, he designed a garden for Frederick V of Bohemia described as 'the eighth wonder of the world.' Colen Campbell's Vitruvius Britannicus praises Jones's Palladian designs.
