     Leadin: A bearded man in a red and blue robe hanging on a ladder points in anguish at Jesus in this artist's version of the Deposition, now in the Volterra. FTPE:
  Part 1 (ans: Rosso Fiorentino [or Giovanni Battista di Jacopo or Il Rosso]):
    Name this artist, who painted the title guy knocking out a bunch of other nude men in his 1523 work, Moses Defending the Daughters of Jethro.
  Part 2 (ans: The Dead Christ With Angels [or Dead Christ Supported By Angels; accept equivalents involving Dead Christ and Angels]):
    The title nude figure with a bent torso sits next to several curly haired figures in Fiorentino's version of this scene. Manet accidentally painted the spear wound on the wrong side of the body in his painting of this name, whose central figure reclines backwards and wears a white loin cloth.
  Part 3 (ans: Andrea Mantegna):
    A more famous foreshortened Dead Christ, without angels, was painted by this artist of a bombed fresco cycle in the Ovetari Chapel and the nine-part series Triumphs of Caesar.

[B20] 2012 ACF Nationals (2012) | Diff: 9 | Fine Arts
     Leadin: The center panel of this altarpiece shows Madonna enthroned with a child, surrounded by saints on the left and right panels. For 10 points each:
  Part 1 (ans: San Zeno Altarpiece):
    Name this triptych in which Benedict, Lawrence, Gregory, and John the Baptist are situated on the right panel while John the Evangelist, Paul, Peter, and the titular saint of Verona are situated on the left panel.
  Part 2 (ans: Andrea Mantegna):
    The San Zeno Altarpiece was painted by this artist, who used di sotto in su in his painting Saint James led to his Execution, and created the engraving Battle of the Sea Monsters. He also painted a series depicting the Triumphs of Caesar.
  Part 3 (ans: Saint Sebastian):
    Mantegna may be best known for a painting depicting this saint being tied to a post and shot with arrows following Diocletian's persecution of Christians.
