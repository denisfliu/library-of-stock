Summary of Facts

Guido Reni was an Italian Baroque painter from Bologna, a student of Annibale Carracci and member of the Bolognese School. His masterpiece is the ceiling fresco Aurora at the Casino dell'Aurora (Palazzo Pallavicini Rospigliosi in Rome), showing the Hours circling around Apollo's chariot as the goddess Aurora floats ahead bringing the dawn, commissioned by Cardinal Scipio Borghese.

His portrait of Beatrice Cenci shows a young woman in a white turban and robes looking over her shoulder. He allegedly painted it after visiting Beatrice in prison before her execution. The portrait inspired Percy Shelley's play The Cenci and is sometimes attributed to Elisabetta Sirani instead. A copy appears in Aunt Ruth's apartment in David Lynch's Mulholland Drive.

Reni's bright style stands in contrast to Caravaggio's tenebrism. He painted Saint Michael slaying a monster with the face of Pope Innocent X. A self-portrait hangs in the Uffizi, and he painted St. Dominic's Glory for the Basilica of San Domenico. King Wladislaw IV of Poland commissioned his Rape of Europa.
