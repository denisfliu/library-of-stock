Italian Baroque painter from Bologna, student of Annibale Carracci. Most commonly identified by the ceiling fresco Aurora at the Casino dell'Aurora and his seven homoerotic depictions of Saint Sebastian (which inspired Mishima's Confessions of a Mask). Also known for the portrait of Beatrice Cenci, Saint Michael defeating Satan, Atalanta and Hippomenes, and the Massacre of the Innocents. Had a gambling addiction late in life.

Summary of Facts

Guido Reni (1575-1642) was an Italian Baroque painter from Bologna, a student of Annibale Carracci and a leading figure of the Bolognese School alongside Elisabetta Sirani, Francesco Albani, and Domenichino. His bright style stands in contrast to Caravaggio's tenebrism.

His masterpiece is the ceiling fresco Aurora at the Casino dell'Aurora (Palazzo Pallavicini Rospigliosi in Rome), showing the Hours circling around Apollo's chariot as the goddess Aurora showers the ground with rose petals and floats ahead bringing the dawn. Commissioned by Cardinal Scipio Borghese, it is a classic example of the quadro riportato technique (a fresco meant to resemble a framed easel painting), painted about 20 years after Annibale Carracci's Loves of the Gods. A torch-bearing putto identified as Phosphorus appears in the work, and the billowing robes of the Hours were inspired by a second-century Roman marble relief of dancers in Borghese's collection. Guercino painted a competing Aurora fresco in response at the Villa Ludovisi.

Reni painted seven renditions of Saint Sebastian, starting the trend of homoeroticizing the subject. The figure's pose is based on Michelangelo's Rebellious Slave and is shown in contrapposto. These paintings had lasting cultural influence: Yukio Mishima's protagonist Kochan in Confessions of a Mask has a sexual awakening while looking at Reni's Saint Sebastian, and the painting partially inspired Oscar Wilde's pseudonym 'Sebastian Melmoth.'

His portrait of Beatrice Cenci shows a young woman in a white turban and robes looking over her shoulder. He allegedly painted it after visiting Beatrice in prison before her execution. The portrait inspired Percy Shelley's play The Cenci (set during the pontificate of Clement VIII) and is sometimes attributed to Elisabetta Sirani. A copy appears in Aunt Ruth's apartment in David Lynch's Mulholland Drive.

Reni's Saint Michael the Archangel Defeats Satan depicts the archangel crushing a devil with the face of Pope Innocent X, who had slandered Reni. The painting was commissioned by the Barberini family and is displayed at the entrance to Santa Maria della Concezione dei Cappuccini, a church featuring a crypt decorated with Capuchin monks' bones.

Other notable works include Atalanta and Hippomenes (title figures in crossed diagonal poses), the Massacre of the Innocents (in Bologna's Pinacoteca Nazionale), Saint Cecilia (white turban, gazing upwards, in the Uffizi), Nessus and Deianeira (Hercules killing Nessus in the background), and The Rape of Europa (commissioned by King Wladyslaw IV of Poland). A self-portrait hangs in the Uffizi, and he painted St. Dominic's Glory for the Basilica of San Domenico.

Late in his career, Reni had a gambling addiction and produced rushed works to compensate. He turned down a commission to paint the Chapel of San Gennaro in Naples after an assassin was sent by a 'Cabal' of painters who may also have poisoned Domenichino. Elisabetta Sirani was praised as 'the reincarnation of Guido Reni' in Bologna, and some of Lavinia Fontana's works were once wrongly attributed to him.
