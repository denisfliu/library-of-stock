T1 2025 LITANY (2025) Diff 8 · Literature
Per the title, this author is mimicked in a poem that tells of when “Morning from her orient chamber came.” James Thomson’s Castle of Indolence and the earliest known poem by John Keats are both labeled an imitation of this author, whose portrayals of characters who resist strong temptation led a later poet to call this “sage and serious” author a ”better teacher than Scotus or Aquinas” in the tract Areopagitica [“ah-REE-oh-pah-GIT-ica”]. Keats’s Eve of St. Agnes and Byron’s (*) Childe Harold’s Pilgrimage use the alexandrine-terminated ABABBCC form introduced by this author in a poem often prefaced with his “Letter to Raleigh.” He used an interlocking rhyme scheme in the three quatrains of his namesake sonnet form. For 10 points, name this poet of Amoretti and the epic The Faerie Queene.
ANSWER: Edmund Spenser [accept “An Imitation of Spenser”; accept Spenserian stanza; accept Spenserian sonnet]

A poem by this author beginning (*) “One day I wrote her name upon the strand” is dedicated to Elizabeth Boyle. The shepherd Colin Clout appears in several works by this author of the sonnet cycle Amoretti who also created the female knight Britomart and the Redcrosse Knight. For 10 points, name this Elizabethan poet of The Faerie Queene.
ANSWER: Edmund Spenser(The first poem is “Of this worlds Theatre in which we stay” from Amoretti. The collection in the third sentence is The Shepheardes Calender.)
