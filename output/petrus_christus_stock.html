Summary of Facts

Petrus Christus was an Early Netherlandish painter who was the leading artist in Bruges after Jan van Eyck's death, alongside Hans Memling. His Portrait of a Carthusian is famous for a trompe l'oeil fly painted on the frame, below a fork-bearded monk against a red background. A Goldsmith in His Shop shows a man (possibly St. Eligius) weighing a wedding ring for a wealthy couple, with a convex mirror showing two men and a shelf with coral. It influenced Quentin Massys.

Christus pioneered geometric perspective and the vanishing point in Northern European painting. His Madonna of the Dry Tree features 15 gilt letters 'A' (Ave Maria) hanging from thorny branches. His Portrait of a Young Girl is side-eyed and influenced by Rogier van der Weyden. He possibly inherited Van Eyck's workshop. His Death of the Virgin shows the title figure's soul carried by miniature angels. His Head of Christ at the Met adds blood dripping from the Crown of Thorns to a lost Van Eyck composition.
