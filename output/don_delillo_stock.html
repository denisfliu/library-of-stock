White Noise

DeLillo's breakthrough novel, set at the College-on-the-Hill. Jack Gladney, who invented the academic field of Hitler Studies but doesn't know German, narrates. His wife Babette secretly takes the experimental drug Dylar (obtained through an affair with Willie Mink / Mr. Gray) to combat her fear of death. The novel's second part centers on the 'Airborne Toxic Event' involving the chemical Nyodene D. Jack's friend Murray Jay Siskind gives a simultaneous lecture on Elvis's mother while Jack lectures on Hitler's mother. Murray takes Jack to see the 'most photographed barn in America.' The company SIMUVAC uses real disasters to prepare for simulations. Jack eventually shoots Willie Mink, and the novel ends with Wilder Gladney crossing a highway on a tricycle. Jack's son Heinrich debates whether it's raining.

Libra

DeLillo's historical novel depicting the life of Lee Harvey Oswald leading up to the JFK assassination. The archivist Nicholas Branch reconstructs the central event. DeLillo invents the characters Win Everett and T.J. Mackey. The novel compares the Warren Commission report to Joyce's Ulysses and includes lyrical passages about the U-2 plane and Oswald's time in 'the brig' at Atsugi, Japan.

General / Biographical

DeLillo is consistently identified as a postmodern American author, though he rejected the label 'postmodern novelist.' Harold Bloom listed him alongside Pynchon, Roth, and McCarthy as one of the four best living American writers. He is associated with Colm Toibin (who sent him a picture of Borges) and the critic Fredric Jameson discussed his work.

Summary of Facts

Don DeLillo is one of the most prominent postmodern American novelists in quizbowl, though he himself rejected the label. Harold Bloom placed him alongside Pynchon, Roth, and McCarthy as one of the four best living American writers.

White Noise is DeLillo's most frequently clued work. Jack Gladney, who invented the academic field of Hitler Studies at the College-on-the-Hill but cannot speak German, narrates the novel. His wife Babette takes the experimental drug Dylar — obtained through an affair with Willie Mink (Mr. Gray) — to combat her fear of death. The drug causes users to confuse words with things. The novel's second section centers on the 'Airborne Toxic Event' caused by the chemical Nyodene D. Jack's friend Murray Jay Siskind delivers a simultaneous lecture on Elvis's mother while Jack lectures on Hitler's mother, and they visit 'the most photographed barn in America.' The company SIMUVAC uses real disasters to rehearse for simulations, reflecting Baudrillard's influence. Jack eventually shoots Willie Mink and takes him to a hospital run by nuns. The novel ends with the toddler Wilder crossing a highway on a tricycle.

Underworld is DeLillo's massive Cold War novel. Its prologue, originally published as 'Pafko at the Wall,' describes the 1951 'Shot Heard Round the World' game. Bobby Thompson's baseball is caught by Cotter Martin, stolen by his father Manx, and eventually bought by the waste management executive Nick Shay. J. Edgar Hoover attends the game and is fascinated by Bruegel's Triumph of Death. Klara Sax, the artist, had an affair with teenage Nick Shay. The novel describes the Northeast power outage of 1965 and features the graffiti artist Moonman 157.

Libra fictionalizes the life of Lee Harvey Oswald through the archivist Nicholas Branch's reconstruction, and features invented characters Win Everett and T.J. Mackey. Mao II follows the reclusive author Bill Gray who tries to rescue a Swiss poet from Beirut terrorists; it opens with a Unification Church mass wedding at Yankee Stadium. Falling Man addresses 9/11 through Keith Neudecker. Cosmopolis follows billionaire Eric Packer crossing New York in a limo for a haircut. Point Omega depicts a filmmaker documenting an Iraq War architect alongside Psycho slowed to 24 hours. The Angel Esmeralda collection includes stories about nuns, orbital spies, and art museum obsession.
