Summary of Facts

Jacob Epstein was an American-born British sculptor active in the early-to-mid 20th century, associated with Vorticism and modernist sculpture. He is most commonly identified as the sculptor of Rock Drill and the tomb of Oscar Wilde.

Rock Drill is his most frequently clued work, a Vorticist sculpture that originally depicted an angular, mechanical figure mounted on an actual industrial rock drill. After World War I, Epstein removed the arms and lower portion, casting just the torso in metal. The sculpture's robotic appearance has been compared to General Grievous and the battle droids from Star Wars. The work also shares its name (Torso in Metal) with a section of Ezra Pound's The Cantos.

His tomb for Oscar Wilde at Pere Lachaise Cemetery features an Assyrian-inspired flying winged sphinx. The monument originally had notably large testicles, which Robert Ross covered with a plaque, causing Epstein to refuse to attend the unveiling. A screen was later installed to prevent people from kissing the monument.

Epstein created eighteen realistically rendered nude sculptures for the BMA building on the Strand, commissioned by architect Charles Holden. These sparked immediate protests, and many were damaged when the government of Southern Rhodesia took over the building. His monumental alabaster Jacob and the Angel was acquired by Tussaud's Waxworks rather than the Tate, to his dismay.

Other clued works include St. Michael and the Devil and Ecce Homo at Coventry Cathedral; Genesis (a nude pregnant woman in alabaster); The Rush of Green in Hyde Park; The Risen Christ (adapted from a portrait of his friend Bernard van Dieren); Day and Night; and the Rima the Bird Girl memorial, which Arthur Conan Doyle petitioned to remove.

Epstein was the father-in-law of Lucian Freud. He testified on behalf of Brancusi's Bird in Space during the famous customs trial. He sculpted a bronze portrait bust of Paul Robeson, during which Robeson sang lullabies to Epstein's daughter Peggy Jean. He wrote the autobiography Let There Be Sculpture.
