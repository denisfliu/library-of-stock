Tossups

T1 2024 Chicago Open (2024) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
This artist adapted a portrait of his friend Bernard van Dieren into an anti-war depiction of The Risen Christ that was free from religious symbols. This artist complained when his alabaster depiction of Jacob and the Angel was acquired by Tussaud’s Waxworks rather than the Tate. Arthur Conan Doyle launched a petition to remove a memorial that this artist created depicting a bare-chested version of Rima the Bird Girl. This artist sparked protests with eighteen nudes he sculpted for niches on the (*) BMA building on the Strand. This father-in-law of Lucian Freud refused to attend the unveiling of one of his works after Robert Ross had a plaque placed over a set of very large testicles. General Grievous resembles a figure that this sculptor placed atop an industrial tool. This artist sculpted a winged sphinx for the tomb of Oscar Wilde. For 10 points, name this American-British sculptor of Rock Drill.
ANSWER: Jacob Epstein
T2 2023 ARCHIVE (2023) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
Prior to this artist's Genesis show at Leicester Galleries in 1931, vandals damaged some of his publicly displayed sculptures by firing at them. After the government of Southern Rhodesia took over the building of the British Medical Association, many of this sculptor's 18 realistically rendered nude men for the building's niches were damaged. Paul (+) Robeson sang lullabies to this artist's daughter Peggy Jean while this artist was sculpting a bronze portrait bust of Robeson. A year after completing one sculpture, this artist removed its arms and most of the bottom part to more fully expose a mechanical device it was built upon; that work by this artist may have inspired the battle (*) droids in the Star Wars prequels. Following World War I, this artist cast just the torso of that Vorticist sculpture in metal. A flying, demonic angel that once had some notably large testicles zigzags across the façade of this sculptor's tomb for Oscar Wilde. For 10 points, name this British sculptor of Rock Drill.
ANSWER: Jacob Epstein
T3 2021 NASAT (2021) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
A sculptor from this country commissioned Mexican workers to produce terracotta figurines for his work Field; that artist is best known for a sculpture of a reddish humanoid figure with outstretched wings called The Angel of the North. Torso in Metal is part of a sculpture from this country that shares its name with a section of Ezra Pound's The Cantos. Exhibitions titled Sensation, Freeze, and Beautiful Inside My Head Forever were held by artists from this country. Charles Saatchi patronized many artists from this home country of Antony Gormley. An artist from this country covered a skull in diamonds in For the Love of God and preserved a shark in formaldehyde for another work. For 10 points, name this country, the home of Damien Hirst.
ANSWER: United Kingdom [accept England or Great Britain] (the unmentioned artists are Antony Gormley and Jacob Epstein)
T4 2018 Oxford Open (2018) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
A work of this artist purchased by Lord Harewood [har-wood] from a Brighton exhibition depicts a huge naked biblical figure in alabaster staring blankly and directly upwards. Another monumental sculpture created from the same block of alabaster by this man is housed in the Tate and depicts a fatigued biblical namesake supported by an angel. An angular figure in the form of a readymade by this artist was separated from the industrial machinery on which it had once sat and exhibited as a solo torso. A screen was installed to prevent people kissing a funerary monument, created by this artist, of an Assyrian inspired flying sphinx. For 10 points, name this sculptor of Jacob and the Angel and Rock Drill who created the tomb of Oscar Wilde.
ANSWER: Sir Jacob Epstein
T5 2010 VCU Open (Saturday) (2010) Diff 9 · Fine Arts
One sculpture by this artist depicts a sad-looking figure with his knees near his chest, while another of his works depicts a reclining pregnant woman with her hand on her stomach. In addition to Elemental and Genesis, he also depicted a winged-man standing over a figure with horns in St. Michael and the Devil, which is located at the Coventry Cathedral with his sculpture Ecce Homo. This author of Let There Be Sculpture caused controversy with his two sculptures representing the life cycle entitled Day and Night; those works were commissioned by architect Charles Holden, who had earlier commissioned this man to create eighteen sculptures for the headquarters of the British Medical Association on the Strand. He may be best known for a sculpture of a sphinx on the tomb of Oscar Wilde or for a sculpture influenced by the Vorticism movement that represents a bleak future dominated by machinery. For 10 points, name this 20th century American-born British sculptor who created the Rock Drill.
ANSWER: Jacob Epstein
