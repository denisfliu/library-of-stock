Summary of Facts

Pieter Bruegel the Elder was a Flemish Renaissance painter renowned for peasant genre scenes and landscapes. He is most commonly identified by Landscape with the Fall of Icarus and Hunters in the Snow.

Landscape with the Fall of Icarus shows a ploughman ignoring Icarus's legs sticking out of the sea as a ship sails past. The existing version may be a copy. It inspired W.H. Auden's 'Musee des Beaux Arts' and Wislawa Szymborska's poetry.

Hunters in the Snow is part of his Labors of the Months series (five of six survive). Three hunters with dogs trudge through a winter landscape with skaters on a frozen lake. One carries a dead fox. Other surviving panels include The Gloomy Day, Haymaking, The Harvesters, and The Return of the Herd.

The Peasant Wedding shows a feast in a barn with soup carried on a door, a boy in a red cap, and a paper crown. Netherlandish Proverbs (The Blue Cloak) illustrates 100+ Dutch idioms.

Other major works include The Tower of Babel (Nimrod, Colosseum-based), Mad Meg (woman leading army to Hell), The Triumph of Death (skeletons), The Fall of the Rebel Angels (Bosch-influenced), The Blind Leading the Blind, Children's Games, The Misanthrope, The Fight Between Carnival and Lent, and Two Monkeys. He collaborated with publisher Hieronymus Cock and was the father of painters 'Velvet' (Jan) and 'Hell' (Pieter the Younger).
