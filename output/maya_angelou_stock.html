African-American poet and memoirist known for the autobiography I Know Why the Caged Bird Sings (titled after a Paul Laurence Dunbar poem) and for reading 'On the Pulse of Morning' at Bill Clinton's 1993 inauguration. Also known for the poems 'Still I Rise' and 'Phenomenal Woman.'
I Know Why the Caged Bird Sings

Angelou's most famous autobiography, set mostly in Stamps, Arkansas. She describes going mute after being raped by Mr. Freeman at the age of eight. The title comes from Paul Laurence Dunbar's poem 'Sympathy.' Key episodes include Henry Reed's valedictorian speech ('To Be or Not to Be') followed by singing 'Lift Ev'ry Voice and Sing,' residents crowding Momma's store to hear a Joe Louis boxing match on the radio, and Angelou moving in with Daddy Clidell and Vivian Baxter Johnson before conducting a San Francisco streetcar and becoming pregnant in high school.

Still I Rise

A defiant poem opening 'You may write me down in history / With your bitter, twisted lies.' The speaker asks 'Does my haughtiness offend you?' and 'Does my sassiness upset you?' She says 'I am the hope and the dream of the slave' and 'just like moons and like suns / with the certainty of tides... I' will rise.

General / Biographical

Born in St. Louis as Marguerite Annie Johnson. She was a former calypso dancer and legally adopted her stage name. She wrote multiple autobiographies including All God's Children Need Traveling Shoes (about time as university administrator in Ghana/Accra), and Mom & Me & Mom (seventh autobiography, published at 85). She wrote the screenplay for Georgia, Georgia. Poetry collections include Just Give Me a Cool Drink of Water 'fore I Diiie and I Shall Not Be Moved. She was a member of the Harlem Writers Guild. She played The Queen in a 1961 production of Genet's The Blacks and was nominated for a Tony for Look Away.

Summary of Facts

Maya Angelou is an African-American poet and memoirist best known for I Know Why the Caged Bird Sings, an autobiography whose title comes from Paul Laurence Dunbar's poem 'Sympathy.' The memoir is mostly set in Stamps, Arkansas, where Angelou describes going mute after being raped by Mr. Freeman at age eight. Other episodes include Henry Reed's valedictorian speech followed by 'Lift Ev'ry Voice and Sing,' and residents crowding Momma's store to hear a Joe Louis boxing match.

Her poem 'On the Pulse of Morning,' read at Bill Clinton's 1993 inauguration, opens 'A Rock, a River, a Tree' and urges 'say simply / very simply / good morning.' 'Still I Rise' opens 'You may write me down in history / With your bitter, twisted lies' and asks 'Does my haughtiness offend you?' 'Phenomenal Woman' celebrates feminine power.

Angelou's other autobiographies include All God's Children Need Traveling Shoes (about her time in Ghana as a university administrator) and Mom & Me & Mom. Born as Marguerite Annie Johnson in St. Louis, she was a former calypso dancer and member of the Harlem Writers Guild. She wrote the screenplay for Georgia, Georgia and played The Queen in a 1961 production of Genet's The Blacks.
