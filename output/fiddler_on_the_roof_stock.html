Fiddler on the Roof

← Home · View source questions · Make cards
A 1964 musical with music by Jerry Bock, lyrics by Sheldon Harnick, and book by Joseph Stein. Based on Sholem Aleichem's Yiddish-language stories of Tevye the Dairyman/Milkman. Set in the Jewish shtetl of Anatevka in the Pale of Settlement of Imperial Russia around 1905. The plot follows Tevye, who tries to maintain Jewish traditions as his daughters marry for love rather than by arrangement. Features a klezmer-influenced score. Choreography by Jerome Robbins. Won nine Tony Awards. A 1971 film adaptation starred Topol as Tevye, with John Williams winning his first Oscar for adapting the score and Isaac Stern performing violin solos. Zero Mostel originated the role of Tevye on Broadway.
Songs: "If I Were a Rich Man"

Tevye's signature solo number in which he fantasizes about being wealthy. He sings 'all day long I'd biddy biddy bum' and imagines having 'a proper double chin' on his wife and 'a staircase going nowhere just for show.' The song's nonsense syllables were meant to evoke Hasidic chanting. Performers often ignore the scripted onomatopoeias, instead imitating raucous animal calls. Has been the subject of its own standalone tossup. Wikipedia

The Bottle Dance / Wedding Scene

A famous choreographic set piece by Jerome Robbins at Tzeitel and Motel's wedding, in which dancers balance bottles on their hats/heads to slowly accelerating music. Robbins visited Orthodox Jewish weddings to get inspiration for this scene. A clarinet solo accompanies the scene. The wedding celebration also involves characters dancing with Russian soldiers, and eventually a pogrom interrupts. Wikipedia

Characters and Plot

Tevye the Milkman/Dairyman is the protagonist, married to Golde, with daughters including Tzeitel, Hodel, and Chava. The story follows Tevye's attempts to maintain Jewish Orthodox traditions as his daughters choose love matches. Tzeitel marries Motel the tailor (rejecting the butcher Lazar Wolf). Tevye recounts a dream about Golde's grandmother that is interrupted by the ghost Frumah Sarah. The family is eventually expelled from Anatevka after a pogrom. Wikipedia

Performers and Productions

Zero Mostel originated Tevye on Broadway; his other credits include Pseudolus in A Funny Thing Happened on the Way to the Forum and Max Bialystock in The Producers. He was blacklisted after appearing before HUAC. Israeli actor Topol (Chaim Topol) played Tevye in two West End productions and the 1971 film. Welsh bass-baritone Bryn Terfel played Tevye in a 2015 West End revival. Wikipedia

Summary of Facts

Fiddler on the Roof is a staple of quizbowl musical theater, appearing both as a direct answerline and as one of the most common identifiers for Jerome Robbins, Sholem Aleichem, klezmer music, and Yiddish language/culture. The 1964 musical by Jerry Bock and Sheldon Harnick is set in the Jewish shtetl of Anatevka in the Pale of Settlement.

The most frequently clued elements are the songs 'Tradition' (the opening number with the horse/mule argument), 'If I Were a Rich Man' (Tevye's 'biddy biddy bum' fantasy), and 'Sunrise, Sunset' (the wedding song with 'is there a canopy in store for me?'). The Bottle Dance — Jerome Robbins's choreographic showpiece at the wedding, with dancers balancing bottles on their heads — is consistently clued, both in direct questions and in Robbins-focused bonuses.

At easier difficulties, the musical is identified by its setting (Anatevka, Russian shtetl, Pale of Settlement), its protagonist (Tevye the Milkman), and its source material (Sholem Aleichem's Yiddish stories). At harder difficulties, clues target specific plot details like Frumah Sarah's ghostly interruption, specific performers (Zero Mostel, Topol, Bryn Terfel), and the 1971 film's John Williams Oscar and Isaac Stern solos.

The musical is one of the most common identifiers for Jerome Robbins (alongside West Side Story and Fancy Free), and frequently appears in bonuses about klezmer music, Yiddish literature, and Jewish culture in America. Marc Chagall's paintings of a green-faced fiddler inspired the musical's title imagery. 'If I Were a Rich Man' has appeared as its own standalone tossup answer.
