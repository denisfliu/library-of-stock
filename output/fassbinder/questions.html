Tossups

T1 2019 Reel Knowledge (2019) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
A film by this director ends with two men writhing on the ground over a song he co-wrote titled “Too Much Tenderness”. The lesbian relationship in the fictional play Maloja Snake in Oliver Assayas’ film Clouds of Sils Maria is inspired by a film by this director, which is mostly set in a bedroom dominated by a reproduction of Pousin’s Midas and Bacchus. After a woman enters a bar in the background behind a row of tables, this director’s camera pushes in on the (*) title character at the counter with a friend as they stare down at her. This director’s melodramatic period began with a film about fruit peddler called The Merchant of Four Seasons. A “film in 13 parts and an epilogue” first seen as a TV series by this director begins in 1928 when Franz is released from jail after killing his wife, while in another of his films an elderly woman falling in love with the title Morrocan migrant worker. For 10 points, identify this bisexual German director who had already made 40 films by the time he died at age 37, including Berlin Alexanderplatz, and Ali: Fear Eats the Soul.
ANSWER: Rainer Werner Fassbinder [The first two clues are The American Soldier and The Bitter Tears of Petra von Kant]
T2 2016 ARTSEE (2016) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
At the opening of one of this director’s films, the title character tells the old woman he’s dancing with, “Think much, cry much.” In a film by this direction, the phrase “the world as will and idea” is used to activate a terrorist cell secretly funded by an industrialist who hopes their activities will increase demand for his security systems. The title character leaves his lover for the bartender Barbara after Emmi tells him she will not cook couscous for him any longer in a film by this man that ends with the title character in the hospital with a (*) stomach ulcer. The Blue Angel inspired this director’s film about the corruption of the building commissioner von Bohm by the title prostitute, Lola; that film is included with another movie by this man that ends with a gas explosion in his BRD Trilogy. This director of The Third Generation and Ali: Fear Eats the Soul also adapted and directed a 15-hour long miniseries based on Alfred Doblin’s Berlin Alexanderplatz. For 10 points, name this West German director of The Marriage of Maria Braun.
ANSWER: Rainer Werner Fassbinder
