Tossups

T1 2017 Eisenhower Memorial Tournament (EMT) (2017) Diff 7 · Other Academic
One building designed by this architect includes two “ghost structures” in the shape of homes that were created from steel tubes. This architect also designed a house with a massive window resembling a sailboat and a large number nine next to its front door. In addition to designing Philadelphia’s Franklin Court, this architect of the Lieb House also designed the massive, fluted limestone façade for a certain museum. This architect created a house with an exposed chimney, a wrap-around staircase, and a gently sloping roof for his mother. This architect of the (*) Vanna House also designed the Seattle Art Museum and the Sainsbury Wing of the National Gallery. For 10 points, name this postmodern architect, the author of the book Learning from Las Vegas, who was also known for quipping that “less is a bore”.
ANSWER: Robert Venturi [accept mentions of his wife Denise Scott Brown as well, as long as Venturi is given]

Bonuses

B1 2024 Booster Shot (2024) Diff 7 · Fine Arts
Buildings whose shape represents their purpose are termed “ducks” and contrasted with “decorated” architecture in a book titled for this city. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this city, the subject of a critique on modernist architecture titled Learning from [it]. The world’s strongest light beam is located in a pyramid-shaped building in this city.
ANSWER: Las Vegas [accept Learning from Las Vegas; accept Paradise]
[10] Learning From Las Vegas was written by Steve Izenour and these two postmodernist architects. A firm named for this husband-and-wife pair designed the current building of the Seattle Art Museum.
ANSWER: Robert Venturi AND Denise Scott Brown [accept Venturi, Scott Brown, and Associates]
[10] Venturi’s quip that “less is a bore” plays on this other architect’s maxim that “less is more.” This last director of the Bauhaus designed the Seagram Building.
ANSWER: Ludwig Mies van der Rohe [accept Ludwig Mies van der Rohe]
B2 2020 IKEA (2020) Diff 7 · Fine Arts
When her husband was awarded the 1991 Pritzker Prize, this architect did not attend the award ceremony in protest. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this architect who collaborated with her husband on projects like the Sainsbury Wing. She discussed sexism in architecture in her essay "Room at the Top?"
ANSWER: Denise Scott Brown [prompt on Brown]
[10] Scott Brown collaborated with her husband, Robert Venturi, on an art museum in this city. A spiral-shaped sculpture made of hundreds of guitars is a focal point of a Frank Gehry-designed museum in this city.
ANSWER: Seattle (The second museum is Frank Gehry's Museum of Pop Culture.)
[10] Scott Brown compared a "duck" with a "decorated shed" in a book co-authored with Venturi and Steven Izenour titled Learning from [this city], whose Strip includes casinos like Bellagio.
ANSWER: Las Vegas [accept Paradise]

In the 1960s, this architect ran several studios approaching urban architecture as a combination of form, forces, and function. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this designer of campus plans for several colleges, such as Williams, Bryn Mawr, and the University of Michigan. She published the essay "Room at the Top?" examining sexism in architecture, two years before boycotting a 1991 ceremony.
