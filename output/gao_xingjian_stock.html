Summary of Facts

Gao Xingjian (born 1940) is a Chinese-French author who won the 2000 Nobel Prize in Literature. His play Bus Stop features characters like Hothead, Glasses, and the Carpenter discussing why they're waiting at a bus stop, using 'polyphonic dialogue.' It was banned in China. Soul Mountain was written during a journey through remote southwestern China, alternating 'I' and 'you' narratives.
