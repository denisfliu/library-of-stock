Summary of Facts

Charles Perrault was a 17th-century French author best known for his collection of fairy tales, Tales from Past Times (also called Tales of Mother Goose or Stories of Fairy Tales from Past Times with Morals). He adopted the persona of Mother Goose for this collection, which includes popular versions of Cinderella, Sleeping Beauty, Puss in Boots, and Bluebeard. The name 'Prince Charming' was inspired by a passage from his version of Sleeping Beauty. Angela Carter was commissioned to translate his fairy tales into English during the 1970s. Many 'anti-tales' (fairy tales with tragic endings) were adapted from his stories.

Perrault also played a key role in the Quarrel of the Ancients and the Moderns. He outraged members of the Academie Francaise by reading his poem The Age of Louis the Great, which contained a couplet praising the modern Age of Louis over the ancient age of Augustus. This controversy inspired Jonathan Swift's The Battle of the Books.
