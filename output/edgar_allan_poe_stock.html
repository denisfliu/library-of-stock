Edgar Allan Poe

American poet, short story writer, and critic. Most commonly identified by 'The Raven' ('Nevermore,' bust of Pallas, Lenore), 'Annabel Lee' ('kingdom by the sea'), 'To Helen' ('Nicean barks of yore'), 'The Fall of the House of Usher' (Roderick, Madeline, tarn), 'The Tell-Tale Heart' ('vulture eye'), 'The Cask of Amontillado' (Montresor, Fortunato), 'The Black Cat' (Pluto), 'The Gold-Bug' (William Legrand), and 'The Masque of the Red Death' (Prince Prospero). Known for critical essays 'The Philosophy of Composition' and 'The Poetic Principle.' Baudelaire translated him; Mallarme wrote 'The Tomb of Edgar Poe.' Rival Rufus Wilmot Griswold wrote savage obituary.
Poetry (The Raven, Annabel Lee, To Helen, etc.)

Poe's poetry is among the most commonly clued in quizbowl. 'The Raven' features a narrator disturbed by 'silken, sad, uncertain rustling of each purple curtain' before a raven perches 'upon a bust of Pallas' and repeats 'Nevermore,' mentioning 'Night's Plutonian shore' and asking 'Is there balm in Gilead?' 'Annabel Lee' repeats 'in a kingdom by the sea' about a maiden chilled and killed by jealous angels. 'To Helen' compares beauty to 'Nicean barks of yore' and 'the glory that was Greece / And the grandeur that was Rome.' Other poems include 'The Bells' (tintinnabulation), 'The City in the Sea' ('Death has reared himself a throne'), 'A Dream Within a Dream' ('grains of the golden sand'), 'Ulalume' ('Astarte's bediamonded crescent'), 'The Conqueror Worm' ('the play is the tragedy, Man'), 'Lenore' ('Ah, broken is the golden bowl'), and 'Dream-Land.'

Short Stories

Poe's short stories span gothic horror, detective fiction, and adventure. 'The Fall of the House of Usher': narrator reads The Mad Trist to Roderick; Madeline (thought dead) appears in doorway; house slides into tarn; 'The Haunted Palace' embedded poem; catalepsy diagnosis. 'The Tell-Tale Heart': narrator obsesses over old man's pale blue 'vulture eye'; hides body under floorboards; 'why will you say I am mad?' 'The Cask of Amontillado': Montresor walls up Fortunato; 'For the love of God!' 'The Black Cat': Pluto; narrator gouges cat's eye; white mark shaped like gallows; wife murdered with axe. 'The Gold-Bug': William Legrand finds scarabaeus with death's-head; 'Bishop's Hostel' = Bessop's Castle; cryptography. 'The Masque of the Red Death': Prince Prospero's masked ball; seven rooms; ebony clock. 'Ligeia': title woman's eyes described via Glanvill; 'The Conqueror Worm'; resurrects through Lady Rowena. The Narrative of Arthur Gordon Pym: only novel; 'Tekeli-li'; Dirk Peters; island of black natives. Others: 'A Descent into the Maelstrom,' 'The Pit and the Pendulum' (General Lasalle rescues), 'The Murders in the Rue Morgue' (first detective story), 'Hop-Frog,' 'Metzengerstein,' 'The Man of the Crowd,' 'The Premature Burial,' 'The Imp of the Perverse.'

Critical Essays and Biographical

Poe is also known for his critical essays. 'The Philosophy of Composition' explains his process of writing 'The Raven' and declares 'the death of a beautiful woman is unquestionably the most poetical topic in the world.' 'The Poetic Principle' argues a 'long poem' is a 'flat contradiction in terms' and opposes the 'heresy of the Didactic.' He was editor of the Southern Literary Messenger, earned the nickname 'the tomahawk man' for brutal reviews, and feuded with Longfellow over plagiarism. His rival Rufus Wilmot Griswold wrote a savage obituary and became his literary executor. Baudelaire spent decades translating Poe into French; Mallarme wrote 'The Tomb of Edgar Poe.' Died 1849 in Baltimore under mysterious circumstances; 'cooping' theory. A 'Poe Toaster' leaves roses on his grave.

Summary of Facts

Edgar Allan Poe is among the most frequently clued American authors in quizbowl, appearing across all difficulty levels with a massive range of poems, stories, and critical essays. His poetry is dominated by 'The Raven' (bust of Pallas, 'Nevermore,' Night's Plutonian shore), 'Annabel Lee' ('kingdom by the sea'), and 'To Helen' ('Nicean barks of yore'). Other frequently clued poems include 'The Bells' (tintinnabulation), 'The City in the Sea' ('Death has reared himself a throne'), 'A Dream Within a Dream,' 'Ulalume,' and 'The Conqueror Worm.'

Poe's short stories span gothic horror and detective fiction. 'The Fall of the House of Usher' (narrator reads The Mad Trist, Madeline rises from death, house slides into tarn) and 'The Tell-Tale Heart' (vulture eye, body under floorboards) are the most common giveaways. 'The Cask of Amontillado' (Montresor walls up Fortunato), 'The Black Cat' (Pluto, gallows mark), 'The Gold-Bug' (cryptography, Legrand), and 'The Masque of the Red Death' (Prince Prospero, seven rooms, ebony clock) all appear regularly. 'Ligeia' features the Glanvill quote and resurrection through Lady Rowena. The Narrative of Arthur Gordon Pym is his only novel, identified through 'Tekeli-li' and Dirk Peters.

Poe's critical essays are significant: 'The Philosophy of Composition' declares the death of a beautiful woman the most poetical topic and explains writing 'The Raven.' 'The Poetic Principle' opposes the 'heresy of the Didactic.' His influence on French literature is enormous — Baudelaire spent decades translating him, and Mallarme wrote 'The Tomb of Edgar Poe.' His death in 1849 in Baltimore under mysterious circumstances (cooping theory) and his rivalry with Griswold are frequently clued biographical details.
