Questions: Jacques Rivette

2 tossups · 4 bonuses

Tossups

T1 2023 On Cinema at the Buzzer (2023) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
This critic said that Paul Verhoeven’s films are all about “surviving in a world populated by assholes” in an interview in which he called Showgirls “one of the great American films of the last few years.” This critic wrote that he had the “most profound contempt” for the man who tilted the camera up to view Emmanuelle Riva’s hand as her character dies against a barbed wire fence in the film Kapò. After leading a putsch against Eric Rohmer (“ay-reek ROH-mer”) to become head editor of (*) Cahiers du cinéma (“kah-YAY doo see-nay-MAH”), this critic took the magazine in a Marxist direction. This author of “The Genius of Howard Hawks” directed a film in which candies mysteriously appear in the mouths of two women who explore a mysterious abandoned house in Paris. For 10 points, name this director of Out 1 and Celine and Julie Go Boating.
ANSWER: Jacques Rivette
T2 2019 Reel Knowledge (2019) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
A film by this director was first publicly shown in the U.S. at the Museum of the Moving Image in 2006, 35 years after its first showing. A film by this director used scenes with 16mm footage to show the footage made by a TV crew of a theater company preparing a production, and 35mm film to show the crew themselves shooting the footage; that film was his first to feature extensive improvisation and an over 3 hour run time. A colour film by this director uses black and white stills along with a black and white segment from the previous episode to connect with the next episode. That film, whose first episode is titled (*) “From Lili to Thomas”, is a thirteen-hour film inspired by Balzac’s Human Comedy. In a film by this director, one title character is reading a book of magic when another begins dropping objects on the ground, before visiting a theater called the “House of Fiction” that shows the same play everyday. For 10 points, identify this French New Wave director behind L’amour fou, Out 1, and Celine and Julie Go Boating.
ANSWER: Jacques Rivette

Bonuses

B1 2024 IQBT Undergraduate Championship (2024) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
This director described his “taste for quotations” in an interview with Cahiers du Cinéma (“kah-YAY doo see-nay-MAH”). For 10 points each:
[10] Name this director, who had Michel Subor’s (“mee-SHELL soo-BOR’s”) Bruno loosely quote the end of The Lady from Shanghai in Le Petit Soldat (“luh puh-TEE sol-DAH”). He directed Breathless.
ANSWER: Jean-Luc Godard (“goh-DAR”)
[10] A Michel Mourlet (“moor-LAY”) quote is misattributed to André Bazin (“bah-ZEN”) in Godard’s Contempt, which is about making an adaptation of this literary work. A John Goodman-played Bible salesman robs the central group and kills their frog in another adaptation of this work.
ANSWER: The Odyssey [or Odýsseia] (The unnamed film is O Brother, Where Art Thou)
[10] A quote often misattributed to Godard linking camera movements to morality was actually said by Luc Moullet (“moo-LAY”)) and used by this director to criticize Gillo (“JEEL-loh”) Pontecorvo’s Kapò (“kah-POH”). He directed Céline and Julie Go Boating.
ANSWER: Jacques Rivette
B2 2024 ARCADIA (2024) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
The stars of one of this director’s films, Juliet Berto and Dominique Labourier, moved in together during production and would recall their dreams to each other to get into character. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this director of a film in which two women repeatedly enter an enigmatic house and suck on hard candies to remember what they saw there, Celine and Julie go Boating.
ANSWER: Jacques Rivette
[10] Berto, who portrayed Celine, also appeared in Weekend and other films by this French New Wave director of Breathless.
ANSWER: Jean-Luc Godard
[10] Rivette’s Celine is the namesake of Celine Song, the Korean-Canadian director of this 2023 film about two childhood sweethearts in Korea who reconnect in New York City as adults.
ANSWER: Past Lives
B3 2022 WORKSHOP (2022) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
Creepy stop motion-animated creatures made of animal bones appear in a film adaptation of Alice in Wonderland from this country. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this country where a film about girls called Marie I and Marie II, Daisies, was shot during its “New Wave.” The director of One Flew Over the Cuckoo's Nest and Amadeus, Miloš Forman, was from this country.
ANSWER: Czechoslovakia [or Czechia or the Czech Republic or Česká republika; or Československo; accept Czechoslovak New Wave or Czech New Wave] (Alice was directed by Jan Švankmajer; Daisies was directed by Věra Chytilová)
[10] This French director referenced Alice in Wonderland with a scene in which one of the title women follows the other around Paris, attempting to return items that the latter has dropped, in his film Céline and Julie Go Boating.
ANSWER: Jacques Rivette
[10] Theories often connect Alice in Wonderland to the work of this American director, who included the fake sitcom Rabbits in the film Lost Empire and mentioned a “cottontail rabbit” in the first episode of Twin Peaks.
ANSWER: David Lynch [or David Keith Lynch]
B4 2013 Arrabal (2013) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
This film opens with one title character dropping her scarf in a Parisian park, à la the White Rabbit from Alice in Wonderland, and the other title character picking it up and chasing after her—including when she hops a tram—for fifteen minutes. For 10 points each:
[10] Identify this phantasmagorical film by the director of Paris Belongs to Us that follows a librarian and magician as they attempt to unravel the mystery behind the haunted house at 7 bis, rue du Nadir-aux-Pommes, which originally left them with no memory of their visit and a hard candy in their mouth.
ANSWER: Céline and Julie Go Boating [or Céline et Julie vont en bateau]
[10] Céline and Julie Go Boating is a film by this French nouvelle vague director who is also behind the stunning 13-hour epic Out 1: Noli me tangere.
ANSWER: Jacques Rivette
[10] This superb Rivette film depicts the destruction of the relationship between Sébastien, who is directing a rehearsal of Racine's Andromaque, and his wife, Claire, cast in the leading role of Hermione, who cannot stand the presence of a TV crew filming rehearsals.
ANSWER: L'amour fou
