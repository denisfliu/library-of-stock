English essayist and novelist, real name Eric Arthur Blair. Known for 1984, Animal Farm, 'Shooting an Elephant,' 'Politics and the English Language,' and Homage to Catalonia.
Essays (collected)

Orwell's essays are the most frequently clued aspect of his work. 'Shooting an Elephant' describes killing an elephant 'solely to avoid looking a fool' while stationed as a colonial police officer in Moulmein, Burma, using a .44 Winchester; 'when the white man turns tyrant it is his own freedom that he destroys.' 'Politics and the English Language' rails against 'dying metaphors,' 'pretentious diction,' 'meaningless words,' 'operators/verbal false limbs'; satirically translates Ecclesiastes into bad modern English; provides 6 rules for writing. 'Inside the Whale' defends Henry Miller's Tropic of Cancer using a biblical metaphor (Jonah). 'Such, Such Were the Joys' describes childhood at St. Cyprian's under 'Sambo.' Other essays: 'Good Bad Books' (Uncle Tom's Cabin), 'The Decline of the English Murder,' 'Lear, Tolstoy, and the Fool,' 'Why I Write' (four motives including 'sheer egoism'), 'The Art of Donald McGill' (dirty postcards), 'Boys' Weeklies,' 'The Lion and the Unicorn' ('a family with the wrong members in control'), 'Notes on Nationalism.' He called Kipling a 'good bad poet.'

1984

Dystopian novel about Winston Smith in Oceania. Julia sends him a note saying 'I love you.' Winston is betrayed by Mr. Charrington and tortured by O'Brien in the Ministry of Truth with rats in Room 101. Emmanuel Goldstein authored The Theory and Practice of Oligarchical Collectivism. The Tehran Conference inspired dividing the world into Eurasia, Eastasia, and Oceania. Coined doublethink, Newspeak, Big Brother, thoughtcrime. 'Freedom is the freedom to say that two plus two equals four' (echoing Zamyatin's We). 'If you want a vision of the future, imagine a boot stamping on a human face forever.' Orwell's wife Eileen's poem 'End of the Century, 1984' may have inspired the title. Sandra Newman wrote a feminist retelling.

Animal Farm

Allegorical novella of the Russian Revolution. An anthem for everyone is replaced with a congratulatory song for one being. Mr. Frederick blows up the restored windmill. Napoleon represents Stalin, Snowball represents Trotsky. The Battle of the Cowshed allegorizes the Russian Civil War. Snowball is blamed for trying to sabotage the windmill. Peter Smollett convinced Jonathan Cape to refuse publication.

Homage to Catalonia

Memoir of fighting for the POUM faction of the Republican army in the Spanish Civil War. Shot through the neck by a sniper. Recounts three days atop the Poliorama theater defending POUM HQ during the May Days in Barcelona. Failed to free Georges Kopp. 'Buttered toast!' shouted through a megaphone. Lamented the tendency to say 'manana.'

Summary of Facts

George Orwell (Eric Arthur Blair) is one of the most clued English authors in quizbowl, primarily through his essays. 'Shooting an Elephant' is the most frequent giveaway, describing his killing of an elephant 'solely to avoid looking a fool' while stationed as a colonial police officer in Moulmein, Burma. 'Politics and the English Language' attacks dying metaphors, pretentious diction, and meaningless words, famously translating Ecclesiastes into deliberately bad modern prose. 'Inside the Whale' defends Henry Miller's Tropic of Cancer using the Jonah metaphor. Other major essays include 'Such, Such Were the Joys' (childhood at St. Cyprian's), 'Good Bad Books,' 'The Decline of the English Murder,' 'Lear, Tolstoy, and the Fool,' and 'Why I Write.'

1984 follows Winston Smith in Oceania, tortured with rats in Room 101 by O'Brien after being betrayed by Mr. Charrington. It coined doublethink, Newspeak, and Big Brother. Animal Farm allegorizes the Russian Revolution with Napoleon and Snowball. Homage to Catalonia recounts his time fighting for POUM in the Spanish Civil War, where he was shot in the neck. Keep the Aspidistra Flying features Gordon Comstock at the New Albion agency writing 'London Pleasures.' The Road to Wigan Pier documents coal miners' conditions. Down and Out in Paris and London is his first book.
