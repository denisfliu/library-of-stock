Filipino national hero, novelist, poet, and ophthalmologist. Best known for the novels Noli Me Tangere and its sequel El Filibusterismo, both written in Spanish, which exposed the abuses of Spanish colonial rule and the Catholic Church in the Philippines. Executed by firing squad on December 30, 1896. Wrote 'Mi Ultimo Adios' the night before his execution. His correspondent Ferdinand Blumentritt is known for their letters. Benedict Anderson invoked his idea of the 'devil of comparisons.' Unamuno compared him to a 'Tagalog Hamlet.' Maria Clara, inspired by his lover Leonor Rivera, became an idealized Filipino woman. Founded La Liga Filipina.
Noli Me Tangere

Rizal's first and most famous novel, titled after the Latin words Jesus spoke to Mary Magdalene ('Touch me not'). The dedication vows to use the ancient method of placing the sick on temple steps to treat the country's 'social cancer.' The mestizo Crisostomo Ibarra returns to the Philippines to start a school and marry Maria Clara, the presumed daughter of Captain Tiago. The evil friar Padre Damaso is revealed to be Maria Clara's real biological father. Damaso schemes against Ibarra, who is excommunicated and jailed for a revolt he did not lead. A 'yellowish individual' is crushed by the stone he drops from a crane in a failed assassination attempt at the school dedication. The friars Salvi and Damaso scheme against Ibarra. Sisa goes insane and dies after her sons Crispin and Basilio are accused of stealing from a convent. The revolutionary Elias dies at the end. Maria Clara becomes a nun (and Father Salvi uses her for sex). Wikipedia

El Filibusterismo

Sequel to Noli Me Tangere. Crisostomo Ibarra returns to the Philippines disguised as the jeweler Simoun (with blue goggles), seeking revenge. He plans to use a pomegranate-shaped lamp packed with nitroglycerin to bomb a wedding feast. He laces Captain Tiago's house with explosives and leaves a warning reading 'Mene, Thecel, Pares' (alluding to Belshazzar's feast). In some editions, Ibarra is said to have returned from fighting for Cuba's independence from Spain. Wikipedia

Mi Ultimo Adios (My Last Farewell)

Rizal's most famous poem, written the night before his execution. Legendarily hidden inside an alcohol stove and smuggled out of his prison cell. The untitled poem was given its name by friends and family. Speaker calls his country 'my adored Land, region of the sun caressed' and proclaims 'to die is to rest.' Hopes a bird landing on his marker will sing 'a song of peace.' Goes 'where there are no slaves, tyrants, or hangmen / Where faith does not kill, where the one who reigns is God.' Charles Derbyshire did a popular English translation. Inscribed on the bronze monument Motto Stella, designed by Richard Kissling. Read to the U.S. Congress by Henry Cooper. Indonesian soldiers recited it during the National Revolution. Wikipedia

General / Biographical

Jose Protasio Rizal Mercado y Alonso Realonda (1861-1896), Filipino national hero, novelist, poet, and ophthalmologist. Executed by firing squad on December 30, 1896. Wrote in Spanish, not Tagalog. Conversant in 22 languages. Founded La Liga Filipina. His correspondent Ferdinand Blumentritt is famous for their letters. Benedict Anderson invoked his idea of the 'devil of comparisons.' Unamuno compared him to a 'Tagalog Hamlet.' Maria Clara was inspired by his lover Leonor Rivera. Supported the Katipunan revolutionary movement. The Propaganda Movement and La Solidaridad published his novels. Ambeth Ocampo discovered his unfinished novel Makamisa. A namesake law mandates Philippine schools teach about him. Popularized the folktale 'The Monkey and the Turtle.' Also wrote 'To the Philippine Youth' ('the fair hope of my fatherland'). Falsely attributed as writer of 'Sa Aking Mga Kabata' (about using the Tagalog language).

Summary of Facts

Jose Rizal (1861-1896) is the national hero of the Philippines and the most frequently asked-about Filipino author in quizbowl. His two novels, both written in Spanish, form the core of nearly every question about him. Noli Me Tangere (titled after Jesus's words to Mary Magdalene) centers on the mestizo Crisostomo Ibarra, who returns to the Philippines and clashes with the corrupt friar Padre Damaso. Maria Clara, Captain Tiago's presumed daughter, is actually Damaso's illegitimate child. Ibarra is falsely accused of leading a revolt. A 'yellowish individual' is crushed by his own stone during the school dedication assassination attempt. The novel's dedication vows to treat the country's 'social cancer.' In the sequel El Filibusterismo, Ibarra returns disguised as the jeweler Simoun wearing blue goggles, and plans to bomb a wedding feast using a pomegranate-shaped lamp packed with nitroglycerin. He leaves a 'Mene, Thecel, Pares' warning alluding to Belshazzar's feast. 'Mi Ultimo Adios' (My Last Farewell), written the night before his execution by firing squad and legendarily smuggled out in an alcohol stove, proclaims 'to die is to rest' and was later read to the U.S. Congress by Henry Cooper. Biographically, Rizal was an ophthalmologist who wrote in Spanish and was conversant in 22 languages. Ferdinand Blumentritt is famous for their correspondence. Benedict Anderson invoked his idea of the 'devil of comparisons.' Unamuno compared him to a 'Tagalog Hamlet.' He founded La Liga Filipina, supported the Katipunan, and his lover Leonor Rivera inspired Maria Clara.
