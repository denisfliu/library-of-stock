Summary of Facts

Leonardo da Vinci (1452-1519) was an Italian Renaissance polymath, painter, and inventor. His Mona Lisa, a portrait of a member of the Giocondo family using his signature sfumato technique, is the most famous painting in the world and draws millions to its room at the Louvre. The Virgin of the Rocks exists in two versions (Louvre and National Gallery London), showing John the Baptist as a child kneeling beside a blue-cloaked Mary in a grotto.

The Vitruvian Man draws an idealized human figure inside a square and circle, inspired by Vitruvius's De architectura. The Battle of Anghiari is a lost painting of four soldiers fighting for a standard, possibly painted over by Vasari's Battle of Marciano (identified by the phrase 'Cerca Trova'). Lady with an Ermine depicts Cecilia Gallerani holding an ermine symbolizing her pregnancy. His portrait of Ginevra de' Benci is the only Leonardo publicly viewable in the Americas.

His notebooks, over 7,000 pages written in left-handed mirror script, include the Codex Leicester (owned by Bill Gates since 1994) and were inherited by Francesco Melzi. He was a student of Verrocchio and teacher of Salai. He was modeled as Plato in Raphael's School of Athens. Ingres depicted his death attended by King Francis I.
