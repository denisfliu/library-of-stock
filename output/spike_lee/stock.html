Do the Right Thing (1989)

Set in the Bedford-Stuyvesant neighborhood of Brooklyn on the hottest day of the summer. Spike Lee plays Mookie, a delivery driver for Sal's Pizzeria. Radio Raheem carries a boombox playing 'Fight the Power' by Public Enemy. After Sal destroys Raheem's boombox with a baseball bat, leading to Raheem's death, Mookie throws a trash can through the pizzeria's window, sparking a riot. Features a montage of racial stereotype insults directly into camera. Ends with a photo of MLK and Malcolm X shaking hands. Summary of Facts

Spike Lee (legal name Shelton Jackson Lee) is an African American director who calls all his films 'joints.' He is most famous for Do the Right Thing (1989), set in Bedford-Stuyvesant, Brooklyn, on the hottest day of summer. Lee plays Mookie, a delivery driver for Sal's Pizzeria. The film's central conflict develops as Radio Raheem constantly plays 'Fight the Power' by Public Enemy on his boombox; when Sal destroys the boombox with a baseball bat, a confrontation leads to Raheem's death by police. Mookie then throws a trash can through the pizzeria window, igniting a riot. The film features a famous montage of characters from different ethnic groups delivering racial stereotype insults directly into the camera. Radio Raheem's iconic knuckle rings reading 'LOVE' and 'HATE' — designed by costume designer Ruth E. Carter — are used in a 'right hand/left hand' speech homaging The Night of the Hunter. Dutch angles portray the tension between Sal and Radio Raheem. The film ends with a still of Smiley's photo of MLK and Malcolm X shaking hands. Cinematographer Ernest Dickerson used a heat bar under the camera to simulate the intense summer heat. Smiley (described as mentally disabled) is shown throughout the film trying to sell this photo; at the end he finally places it on Sal's Wall of Fame, which had previously only featured Italian-Americans.

Malcolm X (1992) is a biopic starring Denzel Washington and Angela Bassett, with costumes by Ruth E. Carter. BlacKkKlansman (2018) follows Black detective Ron Stallworth, who impersonates a white nationalist over the phone to infiltrate a KKK chapter while a white detective goes in person; Harry Belafonte has a cameo in his final film role, delivering a monologue about The Birth of a Nation and lynching. BlacKkKlansman was scored by Terence Blanchard, who also scored Da 5 Bloods (2020), about five Black Vietnam veterans returning to find gold buried in the 1960s and lost after a napalm strike. 25th Hour (2002) features two friends discussing their friendship before a window overlooking Ground Zero, and a protagonist who reads 'Fuck You' on his father's bar wall and delivers a long racial monologue with a city-wide montage. Mo' Better Blues (1990) drew accusations of anti-Semitism from the ADL for portraying two club owners as 'Shylocks'; the main character buys two women the same dress, which they both wear to the same club. She's Gotta Have It (1986) was Lee's debut — Nola Darling cycles between three men and claims monogamy is slavery; it was remade as a Netflix series in 2017.

Stylistically, Lee is known for popularizing the double dolly zoom (derived from Melvin Van Peebles' Sweet Sweetback) to represent 'black suspicion of the white gaze.' He frequently uses unusual camera orientations (rolling upside down, rotating). He refers to all his films as 'joints.' His frequent collaborators include cinematographer Ernest Dickerson, costume designer Ruth E. Carter, and composer Terence Blanchard. He directed the concert film American Utopia (2020), featuring David Byrne, which opens with an overhead shot of Byrne holding a model of a human brain. He also directed the documentary When the Levees Broke: A Requiem in Four Acts about Hurricane Katrina's devastation of New Orleans. He remade Oldboy and Akira Kurosawa's High and Low. He created the story mode of NBA 2K16.
