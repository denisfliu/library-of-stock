Tossups

T1 2023 On Cinema at the Buzzer (2023) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
This person talks about how adults lose their brain plasticity and reach a “plateau of stupidity” at the beginning of a film that opens with an overhead shot of this person holding a model of a human brain. In a review of another film, Pauline Kael wrote that “there’s something unknowable and almost autistic” about this person, who she calls a “stupefying performer” who “bobs his head like a chicken.” At the beginning of that 1984 film, the camera holds tight on this person’s white sneakers as he walks down a (*) stage and places a cassette player on the ground. Classical Japanese theater inspired the ridiculously large suit worn by this man during the song “Girlfriend Is Better” in a Jonathan Demme film. For 10 points, name this singer who appears in the concert film Stop Making Sense.
ANSWER: David Byrne (The first clue is about Spike Lee’s concert film American Utopia.)
T2 2022 ACF Fall (2022) Diff 6 · Pop Culture
In one of this director’s films, a character rubs ice cubes over his girlfriend’s body after complaining that he wants his son to speak English, saying “it’s bad enough his name is Hector.” In a film by this director, a character is asked if they are “circumstanced” by Felix Kendrickson during a polygraph test, after which a police officer throws a rock through a kitchen window as a distraction. In one of his films, this director played Mookie, who throws a trash can through the window of Sal’s Pizzeria after the death of Radio Raheem. In a 2018 film by this director, Ron Stallworth impersonates a white nationalist over the phone to join a chapter of the Ku Klux Klan. For 10 points, name this director of the “joints” BlacKkKlansman and Do the Right Thing.
ANSWER: Spike Lee [or Shelton Jackson Lee]
T3 2019 Reel Knowledge (2019) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
In one film, this director’s camera rolls upside down as an upset man reads a newspaper on the street, and in another his camera rotates right as a man holding a trumpet mimes playing it with his other hand. The cinematographer for a film by this director, Ernest Dickerson, lit a can of fuel below the camera to make the shot look like a hot summer day. In a film by this director, two friends of the protagonist discuss the future of their friendship while standing by a window overlooking (*) Ground Zero. The protagonist of that film delivers a long monologue, accompanied by a montage where different ethnic groups in a city look into the camera, after he reads the words “Fuck You” written on the wall of his father’s bar. In a similar sequence, this director’s camera zooms in on characters standing in the street making long-winded racial stereotype insults directly into the camera. That sequence takes place after Mookie, played by this director, asks a co-worker about his favourite celebrities, who are all white like the pictures on the wall. For 10 points, identify this director of the 25th Hour, and a joint about Sal’s Pizzeria, Do the Right Thing.

T4 2016 MYSTERIUM (2016) Diff 7 · Fine Arts

In that movie by this director, Mister Señor Love Daddy repeatedly says “and that’s the truth, Ruth!” and announces how (*) hot it is.
