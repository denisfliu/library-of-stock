The Death of Artemio Cruz

As Cruz lies on his deathbed, his wife Catalina and daughter Teresa wait to learn the location of his will, while his secretary Padilla plays tapes of his business deals.

Summary of Facts

Carlos Fuentes (1928-2012) was a Mexican novelist and a key figure in the Latin American literary Boom alongside Garcia Marquez, Vargas Llosa, and Cortazar. He was a contemporary of Octavio Paz.

The Death of Artemio Cruz, dedicated to sociologist C. Wright Mills, uses three alternating narrative voices -- first person (I), second person (you/tu), and third person (he) -- inspired partly by Citizen Kane. The dying businessman Artemio Cruz, whose wife Catalina and daughter Teresa wait by his bedside hoping to learn the location of his will, reflects on his life while his secretary Padilla plays tapes of his business deals. Cruz betrayed his cellmate Gonzalo Bernal (who was executed by firing squad), then married Bernal's sister Catalina and inherited Don Gamaliel Bernal's estate. His son Lorenzo was sent to manage estates in Cocuya and later died fighting Franco's forces in Spain. Cruz's childhood friend was Lunero; after accidentally shooting his uncle, Cruz fled to Veracruz.

Aura is a novella written entirely in second person, in which the young historian Felipe Montero is hired by the 109-year-old Senora Consuelo to catalogue the memoirs of her dead husband (whose papers reveal reactionary opinions on Republicanism, Boulanger, and Dreyfus, and notes on Consuelo's experiments with magical herbs). The green-eyed title character turns out to be a magically created younger version of Consuelo. A rabbit named Saga and cats live in the rat-infested, perpetually dark house. Montero transforms into General Llorente while Aura becomes old Consuelo.

The Old Gringo fictionalizes Ambrose Bierce's disappearance in Mexico during the Mexican Revolution. The title character, nicknamed the 'Indiana General' (also partly based on William Benton), carries a copy of Don Quixote and a Colt .44, and proves his marksmanship by shooting a hole through a silver coin. He joins Tomas Arroyo's forces, falls in love with American governess Harriet Winslow (who came to tutor the Miranda children), and is killed after burning false land-granting documents signed by the King of Spain. Arroyo is then executed by Pancho Villa. The gringo's corpse is dug up and shot by firing squad to prevent a diplomatic incident; Winslow has him buried in Arlington Cemetery. Soldiers leave a ballroom of mirrors intact after gazing at their reflections. Harriet's father had disappeared during the American intervention in Cuba. Jane Fonda and Gregory Peck starred in the film adaptation.

Terra Nostra is a massive 800-page novel divided into 'Old World,' 'New World,' and 'Next World.' Characters like the student Ludovico and peasant girl/witch Celestina are reborn across historical eras, first appearing during Philip II's building of El Escorial. The expansive cast includes Don Quixote and Hieronymus Bosch. The Garden of Earthly Delights appears in a mirror in El Senor's palace, symbolizing the novel's three-part structure. The novel opens in a future Paris where the Arc de Triomphe is made of sand and the Eiffel Tower is a zoo. Its title means 'our earth.'

Where the Air is Clear (La region mas transparente) is Fuentes's 1958 debut novel, set in post-revolutionary Mexico City. It centers on the enigmatic Ixca Cienfuegos and follows business tycoon Federico Robles, who married Norma Larragoiti. The title comes from Alfonso Reyes's Vision de Anahuac.

Christopher Unborn borrows its structure from Tristram Shandy and is narrated by an unborn fetus. Angel and Angeles scheme to have a child born at midnight on October 12, 1992 -- the 500th anniversary of Columbus's discovery of the Americas -- to win a government prize.

Other works include 'Chac Mool,' a short story from the collection Burnt Water in which Filiberto buys a chac-mool (Mesoamerican reclining statue) that comes to life, floods his home, and kills him; Nietzsche on His Balcony, Fuentes's last novel in which Nietzsche is reincarnated once a year for 24 hours; and The Hydra Head. Fuentes also wrote the introduction to Frida Kahlo's diary.
