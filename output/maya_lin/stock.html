Chinese-American architect and artist, best known for designing the Vietnam Veterans Memorial in Washington, D.C. as a 21-year-old Yale undergraduate. Her design of two black granite walls in a V-shape inscribed with 58,000+ names beat Frederick Hart's entry in a 1981 competition. Also known for land art (Wave Field series), the Civil Rights Memorial in Montgomery, Alabama, the Confluence Project along the Columbia River, and environmental works like What Is Missing? and Ghost Forest.
Vietnam Veterans Memorial (1982)

Two walls of polished black granite (gabbro) arranged in a V-shape on the National Mall, inscribed with 58,000+ names of soldiers killed in the Vietnam War. Designed when Lin was a 21-year-old Yale undergraduate. Frederick Hart's Three Soldiers statue and Glenna Goodacre's Women's Memorial were later added nearby. Wikipedia

Summary of Facts

Maya Lin is one of the most frequently clued architects in quiz bowl, appearing as both an answerline and a common mention in questions about Washington D.C., land art, the Vietnam War, and Yale University. Her career is defined by the Vietnam Veterans Memorial (1982), which she designed while still an undergraduate at Yale. The memorial consists of two walls of polished black granite arranged in a V-shape, inscribed with over 58,000 names of deceased soldiers. Visitors rub names onto paper. The design was controversial — it beat Frederick Hart's submission in a 1981 competition, and Hart's bronze statue Three Soldiers was later placed nearby as a compromise. A Women's counterpart by Glenna Goodacre was also added. Lin described her concept as 'taking a knife and cutting into the earth.' The material (black granite or gabbro) is itself clued in bonus parts, with some architects calling it 'too feminine.'

Lin's second most-clued work is the Wave Field series (also called Wavefields or Earth Drawings), undulating landscapes inspired by the Serpent Mound and Adena/Hopewell mounds of her home state of Ohio. Installations exist at the University of Michigan (Ann Arbor), Miami, and Storm King Art Center. Her Eleven Minute Line, an earthwork in Sweden, is also part of this series.

The Civil Rights Memorial in Montgomery, Alabama features a conical black granite table inscribed with names of 41 civil rights activists and a Martin Luther King Jr. quote, using the same material as the Vietnam Veterans Memorial. The Women's Table at Yale depicts a spiral of numbers representing rising female enrollment, inscribed on black granite.

The Confluence Project consists of seven art installations along the Columbia River, exploring Native American history and the Lewis and Clark Expedition. Her environmental project What Is Missing? documents endangered and extinct species and includes Ghost Forest (49 dead cedar trees installed in Madison Square Park in 2021). Other notable works include Folding of the Chesapeake (54,000 glass marbles at the Renwick Gallery), Groundswell (recycled safety glass mounds at the Wexner Center), Listening Cone (bronze and redwood megaphone-shaped sculpture), and Silver River (84-foot recycled silver sculpture in Las Vegas).

Lin is consistently identified as Chinese-American, a Yale alumna, and as having won the Vietnam Memorial commission at age 21. Ross Perot infamously called her an 'egg roll' after learning her ethnicity. She is clued in land art questions alongside Robert Smithson's Spiral Jetty and Michael Heizer's Double Negative.
