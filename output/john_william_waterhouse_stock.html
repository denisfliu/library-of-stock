Summary of Facts

John William Waterhouse was a Pre-Raphaelite-adjacent painter primarily known for literary and mythological subjects. His Lady of Shalott depicts a red-haired woman in a white dress sitting on a quilt while floating in a boat. His Ophelia shows the Shakespeare character sitting on a tree branch over a lilypad-covered river (distinct from Millais's version). He painted Gather Ye Rosebuds While Ye May (women in pink and blue), and La Belle Dame sans Merci (maroon-clad woman wrapping a scarf around a man).
