General / Biographical

Summary of Facts

Pablo Picasso was a Spanish artist who co-founded Cubism with Georges Braque, going through analytic and synthetic phases. He is most commonly identified by Guernica and Les Demoiselles d'Avignon.

Guernica (1937) is an anti-war mural depicting the bombing of a Basque town, featuring a screaming woman with a dead baby, a bull, and a flower by a dead man's sword. Dora Maar documented its creation. Les Demoiselles d'Avignon shows five nude prostitutes with faces resembling African masks, painted at Le Bateau-Lavoir and inspired by an African exhibit at the Palais du Trocadero.

The Old Guitarist is a Blue Period work of a blind musician informed by El Greco. Still Life with Chair Caning was the first intentional collage. Portrait of Gertrude Stein required 80-90 sittings and its face was modeled on Iberian sculpture. The Weeping Woman depicts lover Dora Maar. Le Reve depicts Marie-Therese Walter (Steve Wynn put his elbow through it).

His sculptures include Bull's Head (bicycle parts), Guitar (sheet metal), and the Chicago sculpture in Daley Plaza (inspired by an Afghan hound). He created 58 variations of Las Meninas, 15 versions of Women of Algiers (Version O sold for $179M), Three Musicians, Family of Saltimbanques (inspired Rilke's 5th Duino Elegy), and Massacre in Korea. He designed costumes for the Ballets Russes and used doves as peace symbols for the 1949 World Peace Conference.
