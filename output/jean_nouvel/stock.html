Jean Nouvel is a French architect and 2008 Pritzker Prize winner. He is best known for the Torre Agbar (Torre Glòries) in Barcelona, the Arab World Institute in Paris, and the Louvre Abu Dhabi. Other major works include the Guthrie Theater in Minneapolis, the Musée du quai Branly in Paris, the Copenhagen Concert Hall, and the Opéra de Lyon. He is known for facades that use light and perforated screens, and for his concept of finding 'singularities' in design.
Torre Agbar (Torre Glòries), Barcelona

Bullet-shaped skyscraper in Barcelona, Spain, originally built for the Agbar water company. Nouvel designed it to mimic a waterspout or erupting geyser, inspired by the nearby Montserrat mountain. It has 4,400–4,500 pixelated square LED-covered windows; its floors are oval/elliptical in plan. Nouvel himself described the shape as phallic. Temperature sensors regulate motorized external blinds.

Arab World Institute (Institut du Monde Arabe), Paris

Paris building featuring a high-tech metallic brise soleil on its south facade with 240 motor-controlled apertures called 'irises' that open and close like camera shutters to regulate the amount of light entering. The iris design was inspired by the mashrabiya screens of Islamic architecture. This building established Nouvel's reputation and is his most iconic early work; it typically appears as the giveaway in Nouvel tossups.

Louvre Abu Dhabi

Branch of the Louvre on Saadiyat Island, Abu Dhabi, UAE. Features an eight-layered 'floating dome' with a perforated, web-like pattern that allows sunlight to stream through as if filtered through date palm fronds, creating a 'rain of light' effect. The dome has 8,000 medallions. Described as the first universal art museum to open in the 21st century. Leonardo's Salvator Mundi was briefly slated for display here.

Summary of Facts

Jean Nouvel is a French architect born in 1945 who won the Pritzker Prize in 2008. He is associated with the High-Tech and postmodern architecture movements, and has described his design method as finding 'singularities' in objects — a concept he developed in a book co-written with a philosopher.

His most iconic building is the Torre Agbar (also called the Torre Glòries) in Barcelona — a bullet-shaped, phallic tower meant to evoke an erupting geyser or waterspout inspired by the nearby Montserrat mountain. It has 4,400–4,500 pixelated square windows and LEDs on its exterior; its floors take an oval/elliptical shape. Nouvel himself likened the building to a phallus. Temperature sensors regulate its motorized blinds.

His earliest landmark is the Arab World Institute (Institut du Monde Arabe) in Paris, where he designed a high-tech metallic brise soleil facade with motorized 'irises' (240 motor-controlled apertures) that open and close to regulate light, inspired by the mashrabiya of Islamic architecture. This building established his reputation for technically sophisticated facades.

The Louvre Abu Dhabi on Saadiyat Island, UAE, features an eight-layered, web-patterned 'floating dome' designed to allow sunlight to stream through as if filtered through date palm fronds, creating a 'rain of light' effect. The building was described as the first universal art museum to open in the 21st century. Leonardo's Salvator Mundi was briefly slated for display here.

The Musée du quai Branly in Paris (designed with Patrick Blanc's 'green wall' of living plants) was built without barriers and railings so visitors could move freely between different exhibits of world art. It houses collections of non-Western art and culture.

The Guthrie Theater in Minneapolis features a cantilevered lobby called the 'Endless Bridge' that juts out toward the Mississippi River. The building has a dark-glass exterior.

The Copenhagen Concert Hall (DR Koncerthuset) is an illuminated blue cube — described as the most expensive concert hall ever built. It drew inspiration from Hans Scharoun's 'vineyard' design for Berlin's Philharmonie.

The Opéra de Lyon is covered by a new semi-cylindrical glass and steel double vault that Nouvel added to the historic opera house. The opera is named after Nouvel.

Nouvel also placed a massive rusty cube called 'Monolith' on a Swiss lake for the 2002 Swiss Expo. He designed the National Museum of Qatar in Doha (interlocking discs inspired by the desert rose crystal), the Leeum Samsung Museum of Art in Seoul (alongside Mario Botta and Rem Koolhaas), the Larry Gagosian gallery in Le Bourget, Paris, and an office building in Nicosia with balconies of various heights suggesting the structure is 'breathing.' He was among the voices advocating for traditional hand tools to be used in the reconstruction of Notre-Dame de Paris, and he turned down the Dancing House in Prague (which was then designed by Frank Gehry with Vlado Milunic).
