Peruvian Nobel laureate novelist and one of the central figures of the Latin American Boom. His most frequently clued works are Aunt Julia and the Scriptwriter (whose even chapters consist of increasingly deranged radio serials by Pedro Camacho), The Time of the Hero (set at the Leoncio Prado Military Academy), The Feast of the Goat (about Dominican dictator Rafael Trujillo), The Green House (about a brothel in the desert near Piura), Conversation in the Cathedral, and The War of the End of the World. Ran for the presidency of Peru in 1990. Also known for his literary feud with Gabriel Garcia Marquez and his critical study of Flaubert, The Perpetual Orgy.
Aunt Julia and the Scriptwriter

Vargas Llosa's most frequently clued novel. A semi-autobiographical story about law student Mario's romance with an older divorcee, Aunt Julia. The even-numbered chapters consist of radio soap operas (radionovelas) written by the Bolivian scriptwriter Pedro Camacho for Radio Panamericana, which grow increasingly surreal and jumbled as Camacho descends into madness. The Time of the Hero

Vargas Llosa's first novel (1963), originally titled La Ciudad y los Perros (The City and the Dogs). Set at the Leoncio Prado Military Academy in Lima, it follows cadets including the Jaguar, the Slave, and the Poet (Alberto). The Jaguar leads a gang called 'The Circle' that steals exam answers. The Slave is killed, and the Poet (Alberto) is exposed for selling pornographic stories. The novel was so controversial that copies were burned at the military academy. Wikipedia

Summary of Facts

Mario Vargas Llosa is a Peruvian Nobel Prize-winning novelist and one of the central figures of the Latin American literary Boom. His most frequently asked-about work in quizbowl is Aunt Julia and the Scriptwriter, a semi-autobiographical novel in which the young law student Mario falls in love with and marries an older divorced relative, Aunt Julia. The novel's distinctive structure alternates between Mario's narration and the increasingly unhinged radio soap operas (radionovelas) written by the Bolivian scriptwriter Pedro Camacho for Radio Panamericana. Camacho's serials feature recurring absurdities: characters described as being in the 'prime of life' at fifty, the repeated name Ezequiel Delfin due to his poor memory, anti-Argentine bigotry, a Jehovah's Witness who threatens self-castration, and eventually all characters dying in a fire as Camacho descends into madness and is committed to an asylum.

His first novel, The Time of the Hero (originally La Ciudad y los Perros / The City and the Dogs), is set at the Leoncio Prado Military Academy and follows cadets including the Jaguar (who leads 'The Circle' gang and kills the Slave), the Slave, and the Poet (Alberto, who writes pornography for classmates). Faulkner inspired its nonlinear structure.

The Feast of the Goat portrays Dominican dictator Rafael Trujillo's regime through three intertwined narratives: Trujillo brooding over his impotence on the night of his assassination, four assassins plotting the killing, and Urania Cabral returning decades later to confront her paralyzed father Agustin ('Egghead'), who allowed Trujillo to rape her for political gain. Characters include Lieutenant Amadito and Colonel Johnny Abbes.

The Green House is set near Piura in northern Peru and centers on a brothel built in the desert by the blind harp player Don Anselmo, later rebuilt by his daughter Chunga. Key characters include the prostitute 'Wildflower' (Bonifacia), a foundling whose eyes and tongue were eaten by vultures, and the giant Bolas.

Conversation in the Cathedral opens with the iconic line 'At what precise moment had Peru fucked itself up?' and follows journalist Santiago Zavala meeting his father's chauffeur Ambrosio at a dog pound and talking in the title bar. Santiago discovers his father Fermin's secret gay affairs (known as 'Gold Ball').

The War of the End of the World fictionalizes the Canudos rebellion in Brazil, inspired by Euclides da Cunha's Os Sertoes. It features a near-sighted journalist (based on da Cunha), the religious leader the Counselor, and the red-headed Scottish phrenologist Galileo Gall.

The Bad Girl reimagines Madame Bovary through a title character (real name Otilia) who adopts many identities — Comrade Arlette, Kuriko, Mme. Arnoux — while always loved by Ricardo.

Biographically, Vargas Llosa ran for the Peruvian presidency in 1990, had a famous feud with Gabriel Garcia Marquez (punching him at a 1976 movie premiere in Mexico City, shouting 'That's for what you did to Patricia'), and was represented by the literary agent Carmen Balcells. His ex-wife published a memoir titled 'What Little [Vargas Llosa] Didn't Say.' His critical works include The Perpetual Orgy (on Flaubert), 'The Temptation of the Impossible' (on Victor Hugo), and Letters to a Young Novelist.
