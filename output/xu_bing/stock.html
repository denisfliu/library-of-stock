Chinese contemporary artist known for his installations exploring language and text. Best known for A Book from the Sky (Tianshu), an installation of hand-carved nonsense characters resembling Chinese script displayed on scrolls hanging from the ceiling. Also known for Ghosts Pounding the Wall (stone rubbings of the Great Wall), Background Story (LED and plant debris installations), and the film Dragonfly Eyes (made entirely from surveillance camera footage). Frequently paired with Ai Weiwei in questions about contemporary Chinese art.

Summary of Facts

Xu Bing is a Chinese contemporary artist whose work centers on language, text, and cultural meaning. His most frequently asked-about work by far is A Book from the Sky (Tianshu, 1987-1991), an installation featuring approximately 4,000 hand-carved woodblock characters that closely resemble Chinese script but are entirely meaningless. The installation includes books and three large scrolls hung from the ceiling, and was originally titled 'Mirror to Analyze the World.' This work appears in nearly every question referencing Xu Bing. His other notable works include Ghosts Pounding the Wall, a large stone rubbing made at the Great Wall of China with dirt dumped on it, created for the Elvehjem Museum; Background Story, a series using LED lights and plant debris to recreate classical Chinese landscape paintings; and Dragonfly Eyes, a feature-length film assembled entirely from surveillance camera footage. In quiz bowl, Xu Bing is almost always associated with contemporary Chinese art and is very frequently paired with Ai Weiwei as the two most commonly referenced contemporary Chinese artists.
