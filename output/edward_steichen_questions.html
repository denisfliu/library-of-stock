Questions: Edward Steichen

2 tossups · 10 bonuses

Tossups

T1 2025 NAFTA (2025) Diff 9 · Fine Arts
At age 14, Stephen Shore cold-called this photographer and sold three photos to begin his career. This avid horticulturist took the photo Heavy Roses and exhibited many new breeds of delphinium. This photographer likened an actress to a leopardess behind shrubbery in a photo of her face with a piece of black lace suspended in front of it. This photographer won an Oscar for directing the World War II documentary The (*) Fighting Lady. Inspired by both Whistler’s Nocturnes and an earlier photo of the same subject, this photographer used gum bichromate in his image of the Flatiron Building. This native Luxembourger shot The Pond—Moonlight and chose 503 photos from 68 countries for a 1955 event at the MoMA. For 10 points, Alfred Stieglitz co-founded the Photo-Secessionist gallery 291 with what curator of the exhibition The Family of Man?
ANSWER: Edward Steichen [or Éduard Jean Steichen] (The actress in the third sentence is Gloria Swanson.)
T2 2024 PLAYTIME! (2024) Diff 7 · Fine Arts
It’s not Timothy, but an artist with this first name worked with Alexander Upshaw on the western images Canyon De Chelly and Old Eagle. Another artist with this first name depicted a nude Charis Wilson sitting on a blanket in Santa Monica. An artist with this first name was funded by J. P. Morgan to create the photobook The North American Indian and had the surname Curtis. Another artist with this first name pioneered adding color with layers of (*) gum bichromate print, and curated the MoMA exhibition The Family of Man. This is the first name of a Pictorialist who photographed the “still-lifes” Nautilus and Pepper No. 30, as well as another who created The Pond-Moonlight and The Flatiron. For 10 points, give this first name of photographers surnamed Weston and Steichen.
ANSWER: Edward [accept Edward Steichen or Edward Jean Steichen; accept Edward Weston or Edward Henry Weston; accept Edward S. Curtis or Edward Sheriff Curtis]

Bonuses

B1 2023 MRNA (2023) Diff 7 · Fine Arts
With Alfred Stieglitz, this photographer co-founded the Little Galleries of the Photo-Secession, later known as Gallery 291. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this photographer who captured the Flatiron Building at night behind some tree branches and curated the exhibit The Family of Man.
ANSWER: Edward Steichen (sty-KEN)
[10] Steichen used gum bichromate to color The Pond–Moonlight, exemplifying this style popular among Photo-Secessionists that, unlike straight photography, involves manipulating photos for aesthetic effect.
ANSWER: pictorialism [or pictorialist photography]
[10] Steichen also pioneered the use of photography for magazines in this industry. Steichen was chief photographer for Vogue and Vanity Fair, which focus on this industry.
ANSWER: fashion industry [or clothing industry; or apparel industry]
B2 2022 NASAT (2022) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
This artist, whose 1911 pictures of Paul Poiret's gowns for Art et Decoration magazine were the first modern fashion photographs, was chief photographer for Vogue and Vanity Fair in much of the 20s and 30s. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this curator of the MoMA's The Family of Man exhibit, whose commercial turn was bemoaned by fellow Photo-Secessionist Alfred Stieglitz.
ANSWER: Edward Steichen
[10] Steichen's photograph of Gloria Swanson behind a veil does not show this trademark facial feature of hers. A celebrated Vogue cover only shows Jean Patchett's eye, lips, and one of these moles whose two-word name reflects their attractive status.
ANSWER: beauty marks [or beauty spots]
[10] Swanson was a client of this Polish-Jewish popularizer of the noun "make-up," who used a "beauty micrometer" to analyze faces. His namesake company moved a drawn "X" from inside its logo to the end.
ANSWER: Max Factor [or Maksymilian Faktorowicz]
