Emily Carr

← Home · View source questions · Make cards
Canadian artist, heavily inspired by the Indigenous peoples of British Columbia. Known for painting totem poles, forests, and landscapes of the Pacific Northwest. Associate of the Group of Seven (particularly Lawren Harris). Also a writer — her memoir Klee Wyck won the Governor General's Award. Became widely recognized only late in life (age 57). Went by the nickname "Klee Wyck" ("Laughing One").
Indian Church / Church at Yuquot Village (1929)

Her most reproduced painting. A white church surrounded by towering green forest. Renamed by the Art Gallery of Ontario in 2018. Wikipedia

Klee Wyck (1941 memoir)

Memoir of her experiences among First Nations peoples on BC's west coast. Won the Governor General's Award. "Klee Wyck" means "Laughing One" — a nickname given to Carr by the Ucluelet people.
