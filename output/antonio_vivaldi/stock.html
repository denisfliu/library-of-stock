Italian Baroque composer and violinist (1745–1799), nicknamed the 'Red Priest.' Taught violin and composition at the Ospedale della Pieta, an all-girls orphanage in Venice. Composed over 500 violin concertos; his works are catalogued in the Ryom-Verzeichnis (RV) system. Best known for The Four Seasons (from The Contest Between Harmony and Invention), L'estro armonico, and the Gloria in D major.
The Four Seasons

Four violin concertos (Spring, Summer, Autumn, Winter) published as the first four concertos of The Contest Between Harmony and Invention (Il cimento dell'armonia e dell'inventione), Op. 8. Each concerto is accompanied by a sonnet (written by Vivaldi himself) depicting seasonal scenes: buzzing flies, barking dogs, thunderstorms, cuckoos, hailstorms, and shepherd's dogs. Winter (RV 297) opens with trills on consecutive eighth notes B-flat, A-flat, D-flat. In Summer, rapid ascending minor scales and tremolos depict thunderstorms. Autumn's last movement imitates bagpipe drones with sustained notes in the low strings. Spring uses violin pizzicatos to suggest rain. Famously used in the film Portrait of a Lady on Fire (camera zooms in on a tearful Heloise during the final scene). Max Richter made a minimalist 'recomposition' premiered by Daniel Hope in 2012. L'estro armonico

Opus 3 — a set of twelve concertos for string instruments, each scored for four violins, two violas, cello, and continuo. The most frequently cited early Vivaldi collection. Bach transcribed three of these concertos for solo harpsichord and two for organ; the four-harpsichord concerto by Bach transcribes the 10th piece. Johann Ernst of Saxe-Weimar brought L'estro armonico from Venice to Weimar, inspiring Bach's Weimar keyboard transcriptions. Turlough O'Carolan allegedly performed the fifth piece perfectly on the spot without having heard it before. A siciliana appears in one Bach-transcribed concerto from this collection. Wikipedia

Gloria in D Major (RV 589)

Vivaldi's most popular choral work; rediscovered by Alfredo Casella who organized a 1939 'Vivaldi Week' in its honor. Opens with a jaunty D major rendering of 'Gloria in excelsis Deo' before an 'Et in terra pax' section in B minor; ends with the 'Cum Sancto Spiritu' fugue. The sixth movement ('Domine deus, rex coelestis') is scored for soprano, continuo, and oboe or violin alone. Written for the Ospedale della Pieta; two settings survive, catalogued as RV 588 and RV 589; RV 589 reworks material from Giovanni Maria Ruggieri's similar piece. Wikipedia

Juditha Triumphans

Vivaldi's only surviving oratorio (RV 644). Written while at the Ospedale della Pieta; all roles are sung by women (the figlie di coro, who performed behind a metal grate). Allegorizes a military victory by Johann Matthias von der Schulenburg at Corfu. The alto sings the role of Holofernes. Wikipedia

Mandolin Concerto in C major / Lute Concerto in D major

The Mandolin Concerto in C major (RV 425) is frequently paired with The Four Seasons or with four concerti grossi. A visiting poet wrote of a mandolin prodigy at the Ospedale della Pieta featured in RV 425. The Lute Concerto in D major (RV 93) is perhaps the most famous concerto for lute; main theme built from the descending motif F-sharp, E, long D; today often performed on guitar. Vivaldi also wrote a Double Concerto for Two Mandolins in G major. The Lute Concerto is paired with a Mandolin Concerto in C major — see RV 537 (Short Double Trumpet Concerto in C, one of the earliest solo brass pieces).

General / Biographical

Antonio Vivaldi (1678–1741), Italian Baroque composer, violinist, and teacher. Nicknamed 'the Red Priest' (il prete rosso) for his red hair after being ordained; was excused from celebrating mass due to asthma. Taught violin and composition at the Ospedale della Pieta, an all-girls orphanage in Venice (the figlie di coro performed from behind a metal grate). Claimed to have written 94 operas, fewer than half surviving; operas include Griselda, Motezuma, Dorilla in Tempe, Bajazet (pasticcio). His rival was Domenico Zipoli; his longtime collaborator was contralto Anna Giro. Works catalogued in the Ryom-Verzeichnis (RV) system by Peter Ryom. His name was used in a partial anagram in Benedetto Marcello's satirical pamphlet Il teatro alla moda (published as 'Aldiviva Licante'). Fritz Kreisler wrote a Violin Concerto in C major falsely attributed to Vivaldi. Ezra Pound and Olga Rudge helped popularize his music at the Concerti Tigulliani. Also used in Portrait of a Lady on Fire ('Summer') and The Favourite. Wikipedia

Summary of Facts

Antonio Vivaldi (1678–1741) was an Italian Baroque composer, violinist, and teacher, nicknamed 'the Red Priest' (il prete rosso) for his red hair. Born in Venice, he was ordained as a priest but was quickly excused from celebrating mass due to asthma. He spent much of his career as a violin teacher and later director at the Ospedale della Pieta, an all-girls orphanage in Venice whose ensembles — the figlie di coro — performed from behind a metal grate and achieved international fame.

Vivaldi composed prolifically: over 500 violin concertos, some 46 operas (fewer than half surviving), sacred music, and chamber works. His works are catalogued in the Ryom-Verzeichnis (RV) system by musicologist Peter Ryom. He is best known for The Four Seasons (Le quattro stagioni), four programmatic violin concertos published as the first four entries of his Op. 8 collection, The Contest Between Harmony and Invention (Il cimento dell'armonia e dell'inventione). Each concerto is accompanied by a sonnet (written by Vivaldi) depicting seasonal scenes — buzzing flies, barking dogs, thunderstorms, cuckoos, hailstorms, shepherd's dogs, and the drones of bagpipes. Winter (RV 297) opens with trills on consecutive eighth notes B-flat, A-flat, D-flat.

His Op. 3, L'estro armonico (twelve string concertos), was brought from Venice to Weimar by Johann Ernst of Saxe-Weimar and transcribed for keyboard by J. S. Bach (including a Concerto for Four Harpsichords transcribing the 10th concerto). Vivaldi's most popular choral work, the Gloria in D major (RV 589), was rediscovered and popularized by Alfredo Casella's 1939 'Vivaldi Week' in his honor; it opens with a jaunty D major rendering of 'Gloria in excelsis Deo' and ends with the 'Cum Sancto Spiritu' fugue. His only surviving oratorio, Juditha Triumphans (RV 644), allegorizes a military victory at Corfu; all roles are sung by women.

Vivaldi also composed the Mandolin Concerto in C major (RV 425) and the Lute Concerto in D major (RV 93, whose descending F-sharp–E–long-D theme is often played today on guitar). His name was used as a partial anagram ('Aldiviva Licante') in Benedetto Marcello's satirical pamphlet Il teatro alla moda. Fritz Kreisler falsely attributed a C major Violin Concerto to him. Ezra Pound and Olga Rudge helped revive interest in his music at the Concerti Tigulliani. His music appears in Portrait of a Lady on Fire ('Summer') and The Favourite.
