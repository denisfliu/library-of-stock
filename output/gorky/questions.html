B18 2013 ACF Nationals (2013) Diff 9 · Fine Arts
Identify the following about works in the recently opened Crystal Bridges Museum of Art, for 10 points each.
[10] The museum’s patron, Alice Walton, controversially bought this Asher Durand masterpiece from the New York Public Library. Thomas Cole and William Cullen Bryant stand on a cliff in this painting.
ANSWER: Kindred Spirits
[10] Eyeball-esque figures abound in this artist’s Composition (Still Life), now owned by the museum. This teacher of Mark Rothko painted a portrait of Willem de Kooning titled Portrait of Master Bill.
ANSWER: Arshile Gorky [or Vosdanig Manoug Atoian]
[10] An open door separates Robert Louis Stevenson from his wife, who sits reclining on a chair, in a portrait in Crystal Bridges by this artist. He painted Frederick Law Olmsted holding a walking stick in the woods and a seated John D. Rockefeller.
ANSWER: John Singer Sargent
B19 2012 ACF Regionals (2012) Diff 8 · Literature
This author wrote a story about the bloodthirsty serial killer Adam Sokolovich, who murders the prostitute Korol’kova. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this Nobel laureate who wrote “Looped Ears” and a story whose title character suffers a heart attack at a hotel in Capri and has his coffin brought home on the luxury ship Atlantis, titled “The Gentleman from San Francisco.”
ANSWER: Ivan Alekseyevich Bunin
[10] Bunin considered his story “Looped Ears” to be a parody of this Dostoyevsky novel without all of the spiritual angst. In it, Raskolnikov initially justifies his murder of a pawnbroker by regarding her as a parasite on society.
ANSWER: Crime and Punishment [or Pryestupleniye i nakazaniye]
[10] This close friend of Bunin wrote about Pelagea’s involvement in her son’s revolutionary group in Mother and shacked a thief, prostitute, gambler, ex-artist, and ex-aristocrat together in a lodging house in his most famous work.
ANSWER: Maxim Gorky [or Alexei Maximovich Peshkov]
