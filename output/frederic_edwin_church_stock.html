Frederic Edwin Church

← Home · View source questions · Make cards
American landscape painter, member of the Hudson River School. Best known for The Heart of the Andes and Cotopaxi, inspired by Alexander von Humboldt's Kosmos and trips to Ecuador. Also painted Twilight in the Wilderness, Aurora Borealis, and El Khasne Petra. Lived at Olana, a Persian-themed mansion.
The Heart of the Andes (1859)

Massive landscape of a sweeping mountain view with a waterfall and Mount Chimborazo. Two people stop at a rustic cross on a cliffside path. Inspired by Alexander von Humboldt's Kosmos and funded by Cyrus Field. Flora from five different bio-regions referenced from the Naturgemälde map. Premiered to 12,000+ people who used opera glasses to view it in a darkened room; framed with curtains like a window.

                          Summary of Facts

                          Frederic Edwin Church was an American landscape painter and member of the Hudson River School. His art was heavily inspired by Alexander von Humboldt's Kosmos, which motivated him to travel to South America (particularly Ecuador) with funding from Cyrus Field.

                          His most famous painting, The Heart of the Andes (1859), is a massive landscape featuring a sweeping mountain view with Mount Chimborazo, a waterfall, and two people stopping at a rustic cross on a cliffside path. It includes flora from five different bio-regions referenced from Humboldt's Naturgemälde map. It premiered to over 12,000 people who used opera glasses in a darkened room and was framed with curtains like a window. Cotopaxi (1862) depicts the erupting Cotopaxi volcano with smoke and ash, with the 1862 version dominated by red and pink tones.

                          Twilight in the Wilderness shows bright red clouds reflected in a river (at the Cleveland Museum of Art). El Khasne, Petra looks through the Siq onto the facade of 'the treasury' and hangs in the sitting room at Olana, Church's Persian-themed mansion designed with Calvert Vaux. Aurora Borealis depicts a ship under an arc of light, based on descriptions from an arctic expedition by Church's student Isaac Hayes.

                          Other works include Our Banner in the Sky (an evening sky as an ethereal American flag supporting Union forces in the Civil War), The Icebergs (remains of a shipwreck), El Rio de Luz (cross of sunlight dominating center-right), Cross in the Wilderness, The Parthenon, and The Falls of Tequendama. Church also created a large 'picturesque' landscape best observed from the 'Viewshed' near the Calvert Vaux-designed 'Cosy Cottage' at Olana.
