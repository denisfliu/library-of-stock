Albert Camus

← Home · View source questions · Make cards
French-Algerian author and philosopher. Most commonly identified by The Stranger (L'Étranger), The Plague (La Peste), The Myth of Sisyphus, The Fall, The Rebel, and the short story 'The Guest' from the collection Exile and the Kingdom. Also wrote plays including Caligula, The Just Assassins, and The Misunderstanding. Central to his philosophy: the absurd, and the conclusion that 'one must imagine Sisyphus happy.'

Summary of Facts

Albert Camus was a French-Algerian author and philosopher central to existentialism and absurdism. His most famous works are The Stranger, The Plague, The Myth of Sisyphus, The Fall, and The Rebel.

The Stranger (L'Étranger) opens 'Maman died today' and follows the apathetic Meursault, who helps his neighbor Raymond write a letter, then shoots an Arab on a beach. Salamano mistreats his dog. In prison, Meursault rejects a chaplain's graces and opens himself to 'the gentle indifference of the world.' The novella contains a newspaper clipping about the plot of The Misunderstanding.

The Plague describes Dr. Bernard Rieux fighting a plague in quarantined Oran, Algeria. Father Paneloux preaches against the disease. Only two people are called 'saints': an old asthmatic Spaniard and a man who tears up paper for cats.

The Myth of Sisyphus asks 'does the realization of the absurd require suicide?' and answers no — 'one must imagine Sisyphus happy.' It uses the conqueror, the actor, and the seducer/Don Juan as examples of men living the absurd life.

The Fall features Jean-Baptiste Clamence, a 'judge-penitent' at Amsterdam's Mexico-City Bar who stole The Just Judges panel from the Ghent Altarpiece. The Rebel argues the French Revolution's terror was caused by a misguided quest for 'the end of history.'

Exile and the Kingdom contains 'The Guest' (schoolmaster Daru finds 'You handed over our brother' on his chalkboard after freeing a prisoner), 'The Growing Stone' (D'Arrast helps a sailor in Brazil), 'The Renegade' (torture in Taghaza), and 'Jonas' (canvas reading 'solitary' or 'solidary').

Plays include Caligula (orchestrates his own death; 'men die and are not happy'), The Just Assassins (Kaliayev's assassination of Grand Duke Sergei Alexandrovich), and The Misunderstanding (Martha and mother accidentally kill their son Jan).
