Summary of Facts

David Hockney (born 1937) is a British artist who has spent much of his career in California. His painting A Bigger Splash (1967) depicts a backyard pool and was the subject of a 1974 Jack Hazan biopic and a 2015 Luca Guadagnino film. Mr and Mrs Clark and Percy (1970-71) shows a white cat atop a man slouched in a chair, with the couple on opposite sides of shutters.

He is known for 'joiners' — photo collages made from multiple Polaroids, including Pearblossom Highway. His book Secret Knowledge analyzes old masters' use of optical aids and was featured in Teller's documentary Tim's Vermeer. He designed a stained glass window for Westminster Abbey using an iPad and created a purple-dominated Grand Canyon landscape using 21 panels. He was a late member of the Pop Art movement.
