Anthropology questions in this unit cluster heavily around a small canon of theorists and their signature fieldwork. Claude Levi-Strauss and Bronislaw Malinowski are the two most-asked answers, followed closely by Ruth Benedict, Clifford Geertz, Franz Boas, and Margaret Mead. Boas's Columbia circle — Benedict, Mead, Kroeber, and later Zora Neale Hurston and Gene Weltfish — dominates the American side of the canon, while Malinowski, Radcliffe-Brown, Evans-Pritchard, and Edmund Leach anchor a distinct British tradition built on intensive fieldwork among the Trobriand islanders, the Nuer, and other African and Melanesian peoples. Marcel Mauss and Levi-Strauss represent a separate French line running through kinship, exchange, and structuralism.

A second major cluster consists of the works and concepts these theorists produced: Patterns of Culture, The Chrysanthemum and the Sword, Coming of Age in Samoa, Argonauts of the Western Pacific, and The Golden Bough recur as titles, alongside common-link concept answerlines such as culture, magic, kinship, marriage, taboo, potlatch, gift giving, and the Kula ring. Symbolic and interpretive approaches, represented by Geertz's "thick description" and Balinese cockfight essay and Mary Douglas's work on taboo and pollution, form a recognizable sub-cluster alongside the culture-and-personality tradition of Benedict, Mead, and Bateson.

A smaller but consistent presence is biological and evolutionary anthropology, covering Olduvai Gorge, Neanderthals, Homo erectus, and primatologists Jane Goodall and Dian Fossey, as well as materialist and political-economic anthropology represented by Marvin Harris, Marshall Sahlins, David Graeber, and Napoleon Chagnon's studies of the Yanomamo. Contemporary theory appears through Arjun Appadurai, Bruno Latour, Erving Goffman, and Michael Taussig. Geographic and ethnic-group answerlines — the United States, Indonesia, Brazil, Haiti, the Kalahari Desert, Australian Aborigines, and the Inuit — round out the canon as the peoples and places most often tied to these theorists' fieldwork.
