# Boasian Foundations and Cultural Evolutionism
> The late-19th- and early-20th-century founders who established cultural relativism against earlier unilinear evolutionist schemes.

# British Social Anthropology and Functionalist Fieldwork
> Functionalist and structural-functionalist theorists who pioneered long-term participant-observation fieldwork in Melanesia and Africa.

# American Culture-and-Personality School
> Boas's students who linked culture to individual psychology and national character through fieldwork in Oceania and East Asia.

# French Structuralism and Exchange Theory
> French thinkers who modeled kinship, myth, and gift exchange as expressions of an underlying, universal structure.

# Symbolic and Interpretive Anthropology
> Approaches treating culture as a web of symbols, rituals, and meanings to be interpreted rather than causally explained.

# Materialist and Political-Economic Anthropology
> Theorists explaining custom through ecology, economy, and power, and later critiques of capitalism, debt, and violence.

# Biological and Evolutionary Anthropology
> The study of human physical evolution, extinct hominins, and living primates through fossils, tools, and field observation.

# Contemporary and Postmodern Anthropology
> Later-20th- and 21st-century theorists addressing globalization, science studies, and the performance of everyday social life.

# Ethnographic Regions and Peoples
> Places and peoples that recur across the unit as canonical fieldwork sites and geographic answerlines.
