behavioral economics	Behavioral Economics	Field combining psychology and economics to study cognitive biases and bounded rationality, pioneered by Kahneman, Tversky, and Thaler.
bias	Behavioral Economics	Systematic deviation from rational judgment, such as overconfidence or anchoring, studied extensively in behavioral economics.
Black-Scholes model	Financial Economics	Formula for pricing European options based on volatility, strike price, and time to expiration, developed by Black, Scholes, and Merton.
climate change	Macroeconomic Theory & Business Cycles	Global-warming economics, including carbon pricing and discounting future damages, associated with William Nordhaus's integrated assessment models.
commons	Market Failure & Public Economics	Shared resources vulnerable to overuse absent clear property rights, as in Garrett Hardin's tragedy-of-the-commons framing.
creative destruction	Macroeconomic Theory & Business Cycles	Schumpeter's term for innovation-driven displacement of old firms, products, and jobs by new ones, fueling long-run growth.
deadweight loss	Market Failure & Public Economics	Loss of economic efficiency when equilibrium is not achieved, caused by taxes, price controls, or monopoly pricing.
difference-in-differences	Labor, Welfare & Inequality Economics	Econometric technique comparing changes over time between treatment and control groups to estimate causal effects, as in minimum-wage studies.
economic rent	Classical & Neoclassical Foundations	Payment to a factor of production above what is needed to keep it in its current use; central to Ricardian rent theory.
firms	Market Structure & Industrial Organization	Business organizations whose boundaries Coase explained via transaction costs; theory of the firm studies their production decisions.
foreign exchange rate	International Trade & Development Economics	Price of one currency in terms of another, determined by trade flows, interest-rate differentials, and purchasing power parity.
foreign exchange rates	International Trade & Development Economics	Currency prices that adjust to trade and capital flows; fixed, floating, and managed regimes shape international monetary policy.
globalization	International Trade & Development Economics	Increasing integration of world trade, capital, and labor markets, debated for effects on inequality and national sovereignty.
income inequality	Labor, Welfare & Inequality Economics	Unequal distribution of income across a population, measured by the Gini coefficient and analyzed by Piketty and Kuznets.
information asymmetry	Game Theory & Information Economics	Situation where one party has more or better information than another, causing adverse selection and moral hazard.
interest rates	Macroeconomic Theory & Business Cycles	Cost of borrowing set partly by central-bank policy; influences investment, saving, and exchange rates.
James Tobin	Macroeconomic Theory & Business Cycles	Nobel economist known for Tobin's q investment theory and the proposed Tobin tax on international currency transactions.
Jeffrey Sachs	International Trade & Development Economics	Development economist known for shock-therapy transition advice to post-Soviet economies and work on ending extreme poverty.
Joan Robinson	Market Structure & Industrial Organization	British economist who pioneered the theory of imperfect competition and introduced the term monopsony for single-buyer markets.
Karl Marx	Classical & Neoclassical Foundations	German philosopher-economist whose Das Kapital critiqued capitalism via the labor theory of value and theory of exploitation.
opportunity cost	Consumer & Producer Theory	Value of the next-best alternative forgone when making a choice; central to marginal decision-making in economics.
price elasticity of demand	Consumer & Producer Theory	Measure of how much quantity demanded changes in response to a price change, distinguishing elastic from inelastic goods.
rational	Behavioral Economics	Standard neoclassical assumption of consistent, utility-maximizing behavior, challenged by behavioral economics' findings of systematic bias.
rational expectations	Macroeconomic Theory & Business Cycles	New classical assumption that agents form forecasts using all available information, underlying Robert Lucas's critique of policy models.
rent	Consumer & Producer Theory	Payment for the use of a factor of production, notably land, distinguished from economic rent as surplus above opportunity cost.
risk aversion	Financial Economics	Preference for certain outcomes over uncertain ones of equal expected value, formalized via concave utility functions in finance.
Sharpe ratio	Financial Economics	Measure of risk-adjusted investment return, dividing excess return over the risk-free rate by volatility; devised by William Sharpe.
Slutsky equation	Consumer & Producer Theory	Decomposes a price change's effect on demand into substitution and income effects; named for Eugen Slutsky.
Solow-Swan model	Macroeconomic Theory & Business Cycles	Neoclassical growth model where capital accumulation, labor, and exogenous technology determine long-run output per worker.
welfare economics	Labor, Welfare & Inequality Economics	Branch studying how resource allocations affect social well-being, using Pareto efficiency and compensation criteria; Sen and Pigou are key figures.
William Nordhaus	Macroeconomic Theory & Business Cycles	Nobel economist who integrated climate change into long-run growth models, pioneering environmental macroeconomics.
bounded rationality	Behavioral Economics	Herbert Simon's concept that decision-makers face cognitive and informational limits, leading them to satisfice rather than optimize.
contracts	Game Theory & Information Economics	Agreements structuring economic exchange under incomplete information; contract theory studies incentives and principal-agent problems.
Daniel Kahneman	Behavioral Economics	Psychologist who won the economics Nobel for Prospect Theory and cognitive-bias research with Amos Tversky; wrote Thinking, Fast and Slow.
David Card	Labor, Welfare & Inequality Economics	Labor economist awarded the Nobel for natural-experiment studies of minimum wage and immigration, notably with Alan Krueger in New Jersey.
discount rate	Macroeconomic Theory & Business Cycles	Rate used to convert future values to present value, and the rate the Federal Reserve charges banks for direct loans.
economic inequality	Labor, Welfare & Inequality Economics	Disparities in income and wealth across individuals or groups, measured via the Gini coefficient and studied by Piketty.
elasticity	Consumer & Producer Theory	General measure of responsiveness of one variable to another, such as quantity demanded to price or income.
elasticity of substitution	Consumer & Producer Theory	Measure of how easily one input or good can be substituted for another as relative prices change.
exchange rates	International Trade & Development Economics	Relative currency values that adjust to trade flows and interest-rate differentials, central to open-economy macroeconomics.
Franco Modigliani	Macroeconomic Theory & Business Cycles	Nobel economist known for the life-cycle hypothesis of saving and, with Miller, the Modigliani-Miller theorem on capital structure.
frictional unemployment	Macroeconomic Theory & Business Cycles	Short-term joblessness from workers transitioning between jobs or entering the labor force, distinct from structural or cyclical unemployment.
games	Game Theory & Information Economics	Formal strategic-interaction structures with players, strategies, and payoffs analyzed in game theory, from zero-sum to cooperative games.
Gary Becker	Labor, Welfare & Inequality Economics	Chicago economist who extended economic analysis to human capital, discrimination, crime, and the family.
GDP	Macroeconomic Theory & Business Cycles	Gross Domestic Product: total market value of goods and services produced in an economy, the standard measure of output.
Gini coefficient	Labor, Welfare & Inequality Economics	Statistical measure of income or wealth inequality ranging from 0, perfect equality, to 1, perfect inequality.
government spending	Macroeconomic Theory & Business Cycles	Fiscal-policy component of aggregate demand; Keynesians advocate raising it during recessions to stimulate output and employment.
Heckscher-Ohlin model	International Trade & Development Economics	Trade theory explaining that countries export goods intensive in their abundant factors of production, whether land, labor, or capital.
housing	Macroeconomic Theory & Business Cycles	Asset and market whose booms and busts, as in 2008, transmit through mortgage credit into broader business cycles.
institutions	Market Failure & Public Economics	Formal and informal rules such as property rights, law, and norms shaping economic behavior, central to New Institutional Economics.
instrumental variables	Labor, Welfare & Inequality Economics	Econometric technique using a variable correlated with treatment but not the outcome directly, to estimate causal effects.
liquidity	Financial Economics	Ease of converting an asset to cash without loss of value; liquidity crises can trigger bank runs and crashes.
mechanism design	Game Theory & Information Economics	Reverse game theory: designing rules or institutions, like auctions, so self-interested play yields a desired social outcome.
microfinance	International Trade & Development Economics	Small loans to poor entrepreneurs lacking collateral, popularized by Muhammad Yunus's Grameen Bank as a development tool.
monetary policy	Macroeconomic Theory & Business Cycles	Central-bank actions adjusting interest rates and the money supply to manage inflation and employment.
Paul Anthony Samuelson	Macroeconomic Theory & Business Cycles	Full name of Paul Samuelson, founder of the neoclassical synthesis and author of the textbook Economics.
Phillips curve	Macroeconomic Theory & Business Cycles	Empirical relationship suggesting a tradeoff between inflation and unemployment, later modified to include expectations.
production possibilities frontier	Consumer & Producer Theory	Curve showing maximum combinations of two goods an economy can produce given fixed resources, illustrating opportunity cost.
socialism	Classical & Neoclassical Foundations	Economic system of collective or state ownership of the means of production, contrasted with capitalism in Marxist and classical debate.
Thorstein Veblen	Classical & Neoclassical Foundations	Institutional economist who coined conspicuous consumption in The Theory of the Leisure Class, critiquing status-driven spending.
Tit-for-Tat	Game Theory & Information Economics	Simple, successful strategy in repeated prisoner's-dilemma tournaments: cooperate first, then mirror the opponent's previous move.
tragedy of the commons	Market Failure & Public Economics	Garrett Hardin's concept that shared, unregulated resources get overexploited because individual users bear only part of the cost.
utility functions	Consumer & Producer Theory	Mathematical representations of consumer preferences ranking bundles of goods, used to derive demand curves.
accounting	Macroeconomic Theory & Business Cycles	National-income accounting framework, including GDP and GNP, used to measure an economy's aggregate output and flows.
agriculture	International Trade & Development Economics	Sector central to development economics, including farm subsidies, commodity markets, and rural poverty in developing economies.
Albert Otto Hirschman	International Trade & Development Economics	Development economist known for exit, voice, and loyalty and the hiding hand concept of unplanned project benefits.
Amartya	Labor, Welfare & Inequality Economics	Likely shorthand for Amartya Sen, the Indian welfare economist known for the capability approach and famine studies.
An Essay on the Principle of Population	Classical & Neoclassical Foundations	Thomas Malthus's 1798 work arguing population grows faster than food supply, leading to recurring subsistence crises.
Antoine Augustin Cournot	Market Structure & Industrial Organization	19th-century French mathematician whose Cournot model describes quantity-setting duopolists reaching a stable equilibrium.
Arthur Cecil Pigou	Market Failure & Public Economics	British economist who developed welfare economics and proposed Pigouvian taxes to correct negative externalities like pollution.
Austria	Macroeconomic Theory & Business Cycles	Home of the Austrian School of economics, including Menger, Mises, Hayek, and Schumpeter, known for subjective value and business-cycle theory.
bank runs	Financial Economics	Situation where depositors withdraw funds en masse fearing insolvency, potentially causing a solvent bank to fail; motivates deposit insurance.
barter system	Classical & Neoclassical Foundations	Direct exchange of goods without money, the classical starting point for theories explaining money's origin as a medium of exchange.
Battle of the Sexes	Game Theory & Information Economics	Coordination game where two players prefer to coordinate but disagree on which of two outcomes to coordinate on.
beta	Financial Economics	Measure of an asset's sensitivity to overall market risk, used in the Capital Asset Pricing Model to determine expected return.
Black-Scholes-Merton model	Financial Economics	Full name of the Black-Scholes option-pricing model, extended by Robert Merton, for which Scholes and Merton won the Nobel.
bonds	Financial Economics	Fixed-income debt securities paying periodic interest, or coupons; prices move inversely to interest rates.
Claudia Goldin	Labor, Welfare & Inequality Economics	Nobel labor economist known for research on the gender pay gap and women's changing role in the U.S. labor force.
Coase theorem	Market Failure & Public Economics	Ronald Coase's proposition that with well-defined property rights and no transaction costs, parties bargain to an efficient outcome regardless of initial allocation.
constant returns to scale	Consumer & Producer Theory	Production function property where output scales exactly proportionally with all inputs, so doubling capital and labor doubles output.
