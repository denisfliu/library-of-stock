# Classical Antiquity
> Greek and Roman poets, dramatists, and epics that form the bedrock of the Western literary tradition.
Antigone | Sophocles's tragedy of a heroine who defies Creon's edict to bury her brother Polynices.
Sappho | Greek lyric poet of Lesbos famed for intense personal odes to female beloveds.
Orpheus | Mythic musician who descended to the underworld for Eurydice; a common link across many operas and poems.
Greece | Home of Sappho, Sophocles, and Aristophanes, and, in modern form, Cavafy and Kazantzakis.
Virgil | Roman epic poet; wrote the Aeneid and guided Dante through Inferno and Purgatorio.
Aristophanes | Greek Old Comedy playwright; wrote Lysistrata and The Clouds.
Greek | Language of Homer, Sappho, and the Athenian tragedians.
Horace | Roman lyric poet of the Odes and Ars Poetica, an Augustan contemporary of Virgil.
Menander | Greek New Comedy playwright whose domestic plots influenced Roman comedy.
Ovid | Roman poet of the Metamorphoses, a compendium of mythic transformations.
Gaius Valerius Catullus | Roman neoteric poet of passionate lyrics to his beloved Lesbia.
Latin | Language of Virgil, Ovid, Catullus, and Horace.
Euripides | Greek tragedian known for psychologically complex heroines; wrote Medea and The Trojan Women.
Medea | Euripides's tragic sorceress who murders her children to punish Jason's betrayal.
Orestes | Mythic son of Agamemnon who avenges his father's murder, dramatized across Greek tragedy.
Pindar | Greek lyric poet renowned for victory odes honoring athletic champions.
Lucian of Samosata | Greek satirist of the Roman era known for fantastical dialogues and True History.
Sulpicia | Rare female Roman elegiac poet whose verses survive within Tibullus's corpus.
Aesop | Legendary Greek fabulist credited with animal fables teaching moral lessons.
Alexandria | Ancient Egyptian city and setting of Cavafy's modern Greek poetry.
comedy | Dramatic genre pioneered by Aristophanes and Menander in ancient Greece.
Juvenal | Roman satirist known for his biting Satires attacking Roman vice.
Lysistrata | Aristophanes's comedy in which women withhold sex to force an end to the Peloponnesian War.
odes | Poetic form of celebratory verse perfected by Pindar and Horace.
Odysseus | Homer's cunning hero of the Odyssey, wandering home to Ithaca after the Trojan War.
Oedipus | Sophocles's tragic king who unknowingly kills his father and marries his mother.
Sophocles | Greek tragedian; wrote Oedipus Rex and Antigone.
Aeschylus | Earliest of the great Greek tragedians; wrote the Oresteia trilogy.
Catullus | Roman neoteric poet of passionate lyrics to his beloved Lesbia.
clouds | Personified chorus and title of Aristophanes's comedy satirizing Socrates.

Electra | Mythic daughter of Agamemnon who helps avenge his murder, dramatized across Greek tragedy.

Marcus Tullius Cicero | Roman orator-statesman known for his rhetorical treatises and philosophical dialogues.

Roland | Hero of the Song of Roland, Charlemagne's knight betrayed by Ganelon at Roncevaux.
