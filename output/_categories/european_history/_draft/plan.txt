# Antiquity, Late Antiquity, and Byzantium
> Classical Greece and Rome, Rome's transformation into a Christian empire, and the Byzantine Empire down to the fall of Constantinople in 1453.

# Early Medieval Europe
> Germanic successor kingdoms, the Frankish Merovingian and Carolingian dynasties, Anglo-Saxon England, the Viking Age, and the earliest Slavic and Rus' states, roughly 400-1000.

# High and Late Medieval Europe
> The Crusades, the Holy Roman Empire and papacy, the Hundred Years' War and Wars of the Roses, the Iberian Reconquista, the Mongols, the Black Death, and medieval Central and Eastern Europe, roughly 1000-1500.

# Renaissance and the Age of Exploration
> The Italian city-states and humanism, the invention of printing, and the Portuguese and Spanish voyages of discovery, roughly 1400-1600.

# Reformation and the Wars of Religion
> Luther, Calvin, and Zwingli's challenge to Rome, the Catholic Counter-Reformation, the French Wars of Religion, the Thirty Years' War, and Tudor-Stuart England, roughly 1500-1660.

# Absolutism and the Early Modern State
> Louis XIV's France, the rise of Russia, Prussia, and the Habsburg and Ottoman empires, the English constitutional struggles, the Dutch Golden Age, and the Enlightenment, roughly 1650-1789.

# Revolution and Napoleonic Europe
> The French Revolution, the Napoleonic Wars, and the Congress of Vienna's settlement, 1789-1815.

# Nationalism and Reform in Nineteenth-Century Europe
> Italian and German unification, the revolutions of 1848, Balkan nationalism and Ottoman decline, British and Irish political reform, and the Industrial Revolution, 1815-1914.

# World War I and Revolutionary Europe
> The First World War, the Russian Revolution and founding of the Soviet Union, and the postwar peace settlement, 1914-1920s.

# Interwar Crisis and World War II
> The Weimar Republic, the rise of fascism and interwar dictatorships, the Second World War, and the Holocaust, 1920s-1945.

# Cold War and Contemporary Europe
> The Cold War division of the continent, decolonization, European integration, the 1989 revolutions and Soviet collapse, the Yugoslav wars, and post-Cold War politics, 1945-present.

# Economy, Society, and Culture
> Trade goods, social institutions, everyday life, disease, and material culture recurring across many eras rather than anchored to one.
