European History questions concentrate heavily on national and regional overviews, with Hungary, Portugal, Switzerland, France, Greece, Sweden, Italy, Poland, and Romania among the single most-asked answerlines, alongside major cities like Constantinople, Florence, Venice, Prague, and Vienna. Medieval and early modern material forms a dense core: Charlemagne and the Carolingians, the Byzantine Empire, the Crusades, the Hundred Years' War, and the Thirty Years' War recur alongside towering monarchs such as Charles V, Louis XIV, Catherine the Great, and Peter the Great, whose reigns anchor discussion of dynastic rivalry, absolutism, and state-building from the Habsburgs, Ottomans, and Romanovs to the Vasas and Hohenzollerns.

Modern political history supplies a second major cluster. The French Revolution and Napoleon Bonaparte give way to the nationalist movements that unified Italy and Germany and unraveled the Ottoman and Austro-Hungarian empires, while the long arc of British and Irish history runs from the Glorious Revolution through Chartism, Irish Home Rule, and the Troubles. The twentieth century is dominated by the two World Wars and the totalitarian movements that framed them, Stalin, Hitler, and Mussolini foremost among them, followed by the Cold War's division of the continent, its 1989 revolutions, and the later wars of Yugoslav dissolution.

A substantial share of answerlines fall outside strict chronology: dynasties and royal houses (Habsburg, Bourbon, Plantagenet, Romanov) recur across centuries, as do generic regnal names shared by many rulers, and a long tail of lower-frequency answerlines covering economic and social history, guilds, trade goods like wool, silk, and wine, universities, feudal institutions, and everyday practices such as witch trials and dueling, that surface across many individual topics rather than anchoring their own.
