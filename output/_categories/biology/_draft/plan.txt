# Molecular Biology
> DNA, RNA, and the processes and laboratory techniques that read, copy, and manipulate genetic material.

# Cell Biology
> Organelles, cellular structures, and the processes that keep eukaryotic and prokaryotic cells running.

# Biochemistry & Metabolism
> Proteins, small molecules, enzymes, and the metabolic pathways that build and break down cellular fuel.

# Genetics
> Mendelian inheritance, mutation, genetic disorders, and the model organisms used to study heredity.

# Evolutionary Biology
> Mechanisms and evidence of evolution, from natural selection and speciation to phylogenetics and molecular evolution.

# Ecology
> Populations, communities, and ecosystems, including symbiosis, competition, and conservation concepts.

# Microorganisms & Fungi
> Bacteria, archaea, protists, fungi, and viruses, along with the infectious diseases and lab techniques built around them.

# Immunology
> The cells, molecules, and diseases of the immune system, from antibodies to autoimmune disorders.

# Human Physiology & Anatomy
> Organs, organ systems, and the physiological processes of the human body, healthy and diseased.

# Plant Biology
> Plant anatomy, hormones, and physiological processes distinct to photosynthetic organisms.

# Animal Diversity & Behavior
> Non-human animal taxa, their anatomy, and behavioral or ecological adaptations.

# Scientists & History of Biology
> Researchers, historic experiments, and discoveries that shaped the field.
