Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart	Classical Era	Austrian child prodigy and leading Classical composer of Don Giovanni, The Magic Flute, and 41 symphonies.
Arnold Schoenberg	Twentieth-Century Modernism	Austrian founder of the Second Viennese School; developed atonal expressionism and twelve-tone technique, taught Berg and Webern.
clarinet	Instruments, Forms, and Musical Institutions	Single-reed woodwind instrument; featured in Mozart's Clarinet Concerto, Copland's Clarinet Concerto, and klezmer and jazz.
Ludwig van Beethoven	Classical Era	German composer bridging Classical and Romantic eras; nine symphonies, the opera Fidelio, and 32 piano sonatas.
Robert Schumann	Romanticism	German Romantic composer of piano cycles like Carnaval and Kinderszenen and song cycles; married pianist Clara Schumann.
Johann Sebastian Bach	Baroque	German Baroque master of counterpoint; wrote the Brandenburg Concertos, Well-Tempered Clavier, and Mass in B minor.
Franz Liszt	Romanticism	Hungarian virtuoso pianist-composer of the Hungarian Rhapsodies and symphonic poems; pioneered thematic transformation.
Maurice Ravel	Impressionism and the Ballets Russes	French composer of Bolero and Daphnis et Chloe, a master orchestrator often paired with Debussy in musical Impressionism.
Franz Schubert	Romanticism	Austrian composer of over 600 lieder and the "Unfinished" and "Great" symphonies; died young at 31.
trumpet	Instruments, Forms, and Musical Institutions	Brass instrument used in Baroque concertos and jazz; played by Louis Armstrong, Dizzy Gillespie, and Miles Davis.
flute	Instruments, Forms, and Musical Institutions	Transverse woodwind instrument central to orchestral and chamber writing since the Baroque, as in Bach's flute sonatas.
Gustav Mahler	Romanticism	Austrian late-Romantic composer of nine numbered symphonies and song cycles like Das Lied von der Erde; also a leading conductor.
piano	Instruments, Forms, and Musical Institutions	Hammered keyboard instrument central to solo, chamber, and concerto repertoire since the Classical era.
Bela Bartok	National Schools and Folk Traditions	Hungarian composer and ethnomusicologist who folded collected folk melodies into modernist works like the Concerto for Orchestra.
Benjamin Britten	National Schools and Folk Traditions	20th-century English composer of Peter Grimes, the War Requiem, and The Young Person's Guide to the Orchestra; founded the Aldeburgh Festival.
Frederic Chopin	Romanticism	Polish-French Romantic composer almost exclusively for solo piano; wrote nocturnes, mazurkas, polonaises, and etudes.
George Frideric Handel	Baroque	German-born English Baroque composer of Messiah, Water Music, and numerous opera serias and oratorios.
Antonio Vivaldi	Baroque	Italian Baroque violinist-composer famed for The Four Seasons and hundreds of concertos; taught at the Ospedale della Pieta.
Franz Joseph Haydn	Classical Era	Austrian "father of the symphony and string quartet"; longtime Esterhazy court composer, wrote the oratorio The Creation.
Hector Berlioz	Romanticism	French Romantic composer of the programmatic Symphonie Fantastique, known for innovative orchestration and the idee fixe device.
Ralph Vaughan Williams	National Schools and Folk Traditions	English composer who collected folk songs; wrote pastoral symphonies and Fantasia on a Theme by Thomas Tallis.
Sergei Rachmaninoff	Romanticism	Late-Romantic Russian composer-pianist known for lush piano concertos and the Rhapsody on a Theme of Paganini.
Claude Debussy	Impressionism and the Ballets Russes	French Impressionist composer of Prelude to the Afternoon of a Faun and La Mer; pioneered whole-tone harmony and tone color.
cello	Instruments, Forms, and Musical Institutions	Bowed string instrument tuned below the viola; central to string quartets and concertos by Elgar and Dvorak.
harpsichord	Instruments, Forms, and Musical Institutions	Plucked-string keyboard instrument predating the piano; central to Baroque continuo playing and Scarlatti's sonatas.
Jean Sibelius	National Schools and Folk Traditions	Finnish nationalist composer of seven symphonies and the tone poem Finlandia; became a symbol of Finnish independence.
viola	Instruments, Forms, and Musical Institutions	Bowed string instrument slightly larger than the violin, tuned a fifth lower; alto voice of the string family.
Igor Stravinsky	Impressionism and the Ballets Russes	Russian composer of The Rite of Spring, The Firebird, and Petrushka for Diaghilev's Ballets Russes.
Leonard Bernstein	Jazz and American Vernacular Music	American conductor-composer of the musical West Side Story; longtime New York Philharmonic director and Young People's Concerts host.
violin	Instruments, Forms, and Musical Institutions	Highest-pitched bowed string instrument; central to concertos by Beethoven, Brahms, Tchaikovsky, and Sibelius.
harp	Instruments, Forms, and Musical Institutions	Plucked string instrument with pedals for chromatic pitches; featured in orchestral, chamber, and Celtic traditions.
Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky	National Schools and Folk Traditions	Russian Romantic composer of the ballets Swan Lake and The Nutcracker, the 1812 Overture, and six symphonies.
string quartet	Instruments, Forms, and Musical Institutions	Chamber ensemble of two violins, viola, and cello; also the genre pioneered by Haydn as a core Classical form.
Aaron Copland	Jazz and American Vernacular Music	American composer of the ballets Appalachian Spring and Rodeo, blending folk idioms into an accessible "Americana" style.
Camille Saint-Saens	Romanticism	French Romantic composer of Carnival of the Animals, the Organ Symphony, and the opera Samson and Delilah.
Felix Mendelssohn	Romanticism	German early-Romantic composer of the Italian and Scottish symphonies, Elijah, and the Midsummer Night's Dream incidental music.
Niccolo Paganini	Romanticism	Italian virtuoso violinist-composer famed for dazzling technique in his 24 Caprices; inspired Liszt's pianistic virtuosity.
Charles Ives	Twentieth-Century Modernism	American modernist known for polytonal, collage-like works like The Unanswered Question and Three Places in New England.
Dmitri Shostakovich	Twentieth-Century Modernism	Soviet composer of 15 symphonies and string quartets, often read as coded commentary on Stalinist repression.
oboe	Instruments, Forms, and Musical Institutions	Double-reed woodwind that tunes the orchestra; features in Baroque concertos and Tchaikovsky's ballets.
Richard Strauss	Romanticism	German late-Romantic composer of the tone poems Also sprach Zarathustra and Don Juan and the operas Salome and Der Rosenkavalier.
Anton Bruckner	Romanticism	Austrian late-Romantic symphonist known for massive, devout symphonies influenced by Wagner's harmony.
Claudio Monteverdi	Opera	Early Baroque composer of L'Orfeo (1607), among the first operas still regularly performed; bridged Renaissance madrigal and Baroque monody.
Philip Glass	Minimalism and Contemporary Classical	American minimalist composer of Einstein on the Beach and Akhnaten, built on repetitive, additive rhythmic structures.
Antonin Dvorak	National Schools and Folk Traditions	Czech nationalist composer of the New World Symphony and Slavonic Dances, drawing on Czech and American folk idioms.
France	Instruments, Forms, and Musical Institutions	Common-link country for French musical traditions, from Lully and Rameau's Baroque opera to Debussy and Ravel's Impressionism.
Olivier Messiaen	Twentieth-Century Modernism	French modernist inspired by birdsong and Catholic mysticism; wrote the Quartet for the End of Time and Turangalila-Symphonie.
Paul Hindemith	Twentieth-Century Modernism	German modernist composer and violist known for a Neoclassical style; wrote the opera Mathis der Maler.
Erik Satie	Impressionism and the Ballets Russes	French eccentric composer of the piano Gymnopedies; precursor to Impressionism and ambient "furniture music."
guitar	Instruments, Forms, and Musical Institutions	Plucked string instrument, classical or electric; central to Spanish and Latin American music and jazz.
Arcangelo Corelli	Baroque	Italian Baroque violinist-composer who standardized the concerto grosso and trio sonata forms.
drums	Instruments, Forms, and Musical Institutions	Percussion instrument family central to jazz rhythm sections, also including orchestral timpani.
French horn	Instruments, Forms, and Musical Institutions	Coiled brass instrument with a mellow tone; prominent in Romantic symphonic writing, notably Brahms and Strauss.
John Cage	Twentieth-Century Modernism	American experimental composer known for chance operation, the prepared piano, and the silent piece 4'33".
minimalism	Minimalism and Contemporary Classical	Late-20th-century movement built on repetitive, slowly shifting patterns, pioneered by Reich, Glass, and Riley.
Spain	Instruments, Forms, and Musical Institutions	Common-link country for Iberian composers like Falla and Albeniz and forms like flamenco and the fandango.
Gustav Holst	National Schools and Folk Traditions	English composer best known for the orchestral suite The Planets; friend and contemporary of Vaughan Williams.
bassoon	Instruments, Forms, and Musical Institutions	Double-reed woodwind with a low range, providing the bass voice of the woodwind section.
etudes	Instruments, Forms, and Musical Institutions	Technical study pieces for solo instrument, most famously Chopin's piano etudes exploring specific difficulties.
Henry Purcell	Opera	English Baroque composer of the opera Dido and Aeneas, noted for its lament "When I am laid in earth."
Miles Davis	Jazz and American Vernacular Music	American jazz trumpeter and bandleader central to cool jazz and fusion; led the sessions for Kind of Blue.
Samuel Barber	Twentieth-Century Modernism	American composer known for the elegiac Adagio for Strings, originally the slow movement of a string quartet.
Steve Reich	Minimalism and Contemporary Classical	American minimalist composer of Music for 18 Musicians and Different Trains, known for phasing techniques.
Thomas Tallis	Medieval and Renaissance	English Renaissance composer of Tudor sacred music, including the 40-voice motet Spem in Alium.
Edvard Grieg	National Schools and Folk Traditions	Norwegian nationalist composer of the Piano Concerto in A minor and the Peer Gynt suites.
pipe organ	Instruments, Forms, and Musical Institutions	Wind keyboard instrument played via pipes; central to church music and Bach's organ works.
timpani	Instruments, Forms, and Musical Institutions	Tuned kettle drums, standard percussion of the orchestra since the Baroque, capable of pitch changes via pedals.
Joseph Haydn	Classical Era	Austrian Classical composer, mentor to Mozart and teacher of Beethoven; prolific symphonist and string quartet pioneer.
Leos Janacek	National Schools and Folk Traditions	Czech nationalist composer known for speech-melody-influenced operas like Jenufa and the orchestral Sinfonietta.
madrigals	Medieval and Renaissance	Secular vocal part-songs of the Renaissance, especially Italian and English; Monteverdi's books span the Renaissance-Baroque transition.
Poland	Instruments, Forms, and Musical Institutions	Common-link country tied to Chopin's mazurkas and polonaises and 20th-century composers like Penderecki and Lutoslawski.
Sergei Prokofiev	Twentieth-Century Modernism	Soviet composer of Peter and the Wolf, Romeo and Juliet, and the satirical Classical Symphony.
Carl Maria von Weber	Romanticism	Early German Romantic composer whose opera Der Freischutz helped launch German Romantic opera.
George Gershwin	Jazz and American Vernacular Music	American composer who fused jazz idioms with concert music in Rhapsody in Blue and the opera Porgy and Bess.
preludes	Instruments, Forms, and Musical Institutions	Short standalone pieces, often paired with fugues or collected in cycles like Chopin's and Debussy's Preludes.
waltzes	Instruments, Forms, and Musical Institutions	Triple-meter dance form popularized by the Strauss family and used by Chopin and Brahms in concert works.
Italy	Instruments, Forms, and Musical Institutions	Common-link country central to opera's birth and Baroque violin tradition, from Monteverdi and Vivaldi to Verdi and Puccini.
Ottorino Respighi	Romanticism	Italian composer of the orchestral tone poems Pines of Rome and Fountains of Rome, known for lush, colorful orchestration.
string quartets	Instruments, Forms, and Musical Institutions	Chamber genre for two violins, viola, and cello; a core medium for compositional development across nearly every era.
