# The Ancient Near East & Middle East
> Mesopotamian cities and empires, Persian dynasties, Phoenician traders, and the rise of Islam across the Levant and Arabia.

# Ancient Egypt & the Nile
> Pharaohs, pyramids, and the Nile Valley civilization from unification under Narmer through Ptolemaic rule.

# Ancient Greece & the Aegean
> City-states, historians, and conquerors of the Greek world, from Sparta and Athens through Alexander's Macedonian empire.

# Ancient Rome & the Mediterranean
> Rome's rise from republic to empire, its provinces and rivals across the Mediterranean, and the historians who chronicled it.

# East Asia
> Chinese dynasties from Shang through the twentieth century, plus Tibet and the Silk Road networks linking China to the wider world.

# South & Southeast Asia
> Indus Valley cities, Mauryan and Buddhist India, Sri Lanka, and the colonial and postcolonial states of the region.

# Sub-Saharan & African History
> Kingdoms, migrations, and colonial-era nations of Africa outside the Nile Valley, from the Garamantes to modern South Africa and Nigeria.

# Pre-Columbian & Latin American History
> Mesoamerican and Andean civilizations before Columbus, plus the colonial and modern history of Latin America and the Caribbean.

# Oceania & the Pacific
> Indigenous and colonial history of Australia, New Zealand, and the Pacific islands, including Maori-British relations and Antarctic exploration.

# Canada & Northern North America
> Canadian confederation, its provinces and prime ministers, and the indigenous peoples of the Arctic north.

# Modern Europe & the World Wars
> Nineteenth- and twentieth-century European states, the two World Wars, and the rise and fall of the Soviet Union.

# Trade, Technology & Material Culture
> Common-link commodities, materials, and archaeological objects, from metals and crops to artifacts, traded and used across many civilizations.
