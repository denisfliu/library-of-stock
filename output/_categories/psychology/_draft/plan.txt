# Founding Schools: Structuralism & Functionalism
> The late-19th-century origins of experimental psychology in Wundt's introspective structuralism and James's pragmatist functionalism.

# Gestalt Psychology & Perception
> The German school studying how the mind organizes sensory wholes, alongside classic perceptual phenomena and illusions.

# Psychoanalysis & the Freudian Tradition
> Freud's theory of the unconscious and the analysts, concepts, and splinter schools it spawned.

# Behaviorism & Learning
> Conditioning-based accounts of learning built on animal experiments, from Pavlov's heirs through Watson, Thorndike, Skinner, and Bandura.

# Developmental Psychology
> Stage theories and experiments tracing cognitive, social, and moral growth from infancy through adolescence.

# Cognitive Psychology, Memory & Decision-Making
> Information-processing approaches to attention, memory, and judgment, including the heuristics-and-biases program.

# Social Psychology & Landmark Experiments
> Classic obedience, conformity, and group-behavior studies, plus the attribution and prejudice research they inspired.

# Personality & Psychological Testing
> Trait theories, intelligence testing, and the projective and self-report instruments used to measure individual differences.

# Motivation, Emotion & Humanistic Psychology
> Maslow, Rogers, and the study of drives, feelings, and self-actualization as an alternative to behaviorism and psychoanalysis.

# Clinical, Abnormal & Health Psychology
> Mental disorders, their diagnostic classification, and the therapeutic approaches developed to treat them.
