Aristophanes	Greek Comedy	Athenian Old Comedy playwright whose only complete surviving plays include Lysistrata, The Clouds, The Frogs, The Wasps, and Peace.
The Golden Ass	Roman Prose and Satire	Apuleius's Latin novel, the only ancient Roman novel to survive complete; follows Lucius transformed into a donkey by magic.
Gaius Valerius Catullus	Roman Poetry	Roman Neoteric poet known for passionate, obscene verse addressed to his lover Lesbia and translations of Sappho.
Medea	Greek Tragedy	Euripides's sorceress who murders her own children to punish husband Jason for abandoning her for Glauce.
Sappho of Lesbos	Greek Lyric Poetry	Archaic Greek poetess from Lesbos famed for intense personal lyrics of desire, often addressed to other women.
Sappho	Greek Lyric Poetry	Archaic Greek lyric poet from Lesbos whose fragmentary verses on love and longing shaped later lyric poetry.
Aeschylus	Greek Tragedy	Earliest of the three great Athenian tragedians; wrote the Oresteia trilogy, The Persians, and Seven Against Thebes.
Agamemnon	Greek Tragedy	Mycenaean king who led the Greeks at Troy, murdered by wife Clytemnestra upon his return; Aeschylus's play opens the Oresteia.
Antigone	Greek Tragedy	Sophocles's heroine who defies Creon's edict by burying her brother Polynices, leading to her own death by suicide.
Miles Gloriosus	Roman Comedy	Plautus's comedy about a vain soldier, Pyrgopolynices, outwitted by his clever slave Palaestrio over a captured mistress.
Orestes	Greek Tragedy	Son of Agamemnon and Clytemnestra who avenges his father's murder by killing his mother, then is pursued by the Furies.
Peace	Greek Comedy	Aristophanes's comedy in which farmer Trygaeus flies to Olympus on a giant dung beetle to rescue the goddess Peace.
Pindar	Greek Lyric Poetry	Greek lyric poet renowned for victory odes celebrating athletic triumphs at Olympia and other Panhellenic games.
Terence	Roman Comedy	Roman comic playwright, a former slave from Carthage, who adapted Menander's Greek New Comedy into plays like The Brothers.
The Clouds	Greek Comedy	Aristophanes's comedy satirizing Socrates and sophistic education, as Strepsiades sends his son Pheidippides to study at the Thinkery.
The Frogs	Greek Comedy	Aristophanes's comedy where Dionysus travels to the Underworld with slave Xanthias to judge a contest between Aeschylus and Euripides.
The Persians	Greek Tragedy	Aeschylus's earliest surviving tragedy, dramatizing the Persian court's reaction to defeat at Salamis; the only extant historical Greek tragedy.
Titus Maccius Plautus	Roman Comedy	Roman comic playwright who adapted Greek New Comedy, writing Miles Gloriosus, Menaechmi, Pseudolus, and Amphitryon.
catharsis	Literary Terms and Criticism	Aristotelian concept from the Poetics describing tragedy's purgation or purification of the audience's pity and fear.
Euripides	Greek Tragedy	Youngest of the three great tragedians, known for psychologically complex women and religious skepticism; wrote Medea and The Bacchae.
Lysistrata	Greek Comedy	Aristophanes's comedy in which Athenian women withhold sex from their husbands to force an end to the Peloponnesian War.
Ovid	Roman Poetry	Augustan Roman poet of the Metamorphoses and Ars Amatoria, exiled by Augustus to Tomis on the Black Sea.
Publius Ovidius Naso	Roman Poetry	Full Roman name of Ovid, Augustan poet of the Metamorphoses and Ars Amatoria, later exiled to Tomis.
Publius Papinius Statius	Roman Poetry	Flavian-era Roman epic poet who wrote the Thebaid, on the war of the Seven Against Thebes, and the Silvae.
Satyricon	Roman Prose and Satire	Petronius's satirical Latin prose novel following Encolpius's misadventures, including the lavish banquet of the freedman Trimalchio.
Seneca the Younger	Roman Tragedy and Philosophy	Roman Stoic philosopher and tutor to Nero who wrote bloody Latin tragedies like Medea and Thyestes alongside philosophical letters.
Sextus Propertius	Roman Poetry	Augustan Roman elegiac poet whose love poems address his mistress Cynthia; part of Maecenas's literary circle.
The Bacchae	Greek Tragedy	Euripides's final tragedy, in which Dionysus punishes Pentheus for denying his divinity and is torn apart by frenzied maenads.
the Wasps	Greek Comedy	Aristophanes's comedy satirizing Athenian litigiousness through Philocleon's obsession with jury service, restrained by his son Bdelycleon.
Xanthias	Greek Comedy	Slave of Dionysus in Aristophanes's The Frogs, comically switching roles and costumes with his master.
