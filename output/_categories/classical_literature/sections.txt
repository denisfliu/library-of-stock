# Greek Tragedy
> Athenian dramas of myth and suffering staged at civic festivals, from Aeschylus's Oresteia through Sophocles and Euripides.
Medea | Euripides's sorceress who murders her own children to punish husband Jason for abandoning her for Glauce.
Aeschylus | Earliest of the three great Athenian tragedians; wrote the Oresteia trilogy, The Persians, and Seven Against Thebes.
Agamemnon | Mycenaean king who led the Greeks at Troy, murdered by wife Clytemnestra upon his return; Aeschylus's play opens the Oresteia.
Antigone | Sophocles's heroine who defies Creon's edict by burying her brother Polynices, leading to her own death by suicide.
Orestes | Son of Agamemnon and Clytemnestra who avenges his father's murder by killing his mother, then is pursued by the Furies.
The Persians | Aeschylus's earliest surviving tragedy, dramatizing the Persian court's reaction to defeat at Salamis; the only extant historical Greek tragedy.
Euripides | Youngest of the three great tragedians, known for psychologically complex women and religious skepticism; wrote Medea and The Bacchae.
The Bacchae | Euripides's final tragedy, in which Dionysus punishes Pentheus for denying his divinity and is torn apart by frenzied maenads.

# Greek Comedy
> Aristophanes's surviving Old Comedy plays, blending topical satire, fantastical plots, and choral spectacle in fifth-century Athens.
Aristophanes | Athenian Old Comedy playwright whose only complete surviving plays include Lysistrata, The Clouds, The Frogs, The Wasps, and Peace.
Peace | Aristophanes's comedy in which farmer Trygaeus flies to Olympus on a giant dung beetle to rescue the goddess Peace.
The Clouds | Aristophanes's comedy satirizing Socrates and sophistic education, as Strepsiades sends his son Pheidippides to study at the Thinkery.
The Frogs | Aristophanes's comedy where Dionysus travels to the Underworld with slave Xanthias to judge a contest between Aeschylus and Euripides.
Lysistrata | Aristophanes's comedy in which Athenian women withhold sex from their husbands to force an end to the Peloponnesian War.
the Wasps | Aristophanes's comedy satirizing Athenian litigiousness through Philocleon's obsession with jury service, restrained by his son Bdelycleon.
Xanthias | Slave of Dionysus in Aristophanes's The Frogs, comically switching roles and costumes with his master.

# Greek Lyric Poetry
> Personal and occasional Greek verse sung to music, spanning Sappho's intimate lyrics and Pindar's public victory odes.
Sappho of Lesbos | Archaic Greek poetess from Lesbos famed for intense personal lyrics of desire, often addressed to other women.
Sappho | Archaic Greek lyric poet from Lesbos whose fragmentary verses on love and longing shaped later lyric poetry.
Pindar | Greek lyric poet renowned for victory odes celebrating athletic triumphs at Olympia and other Panhellenic games.

# Roman Comedy
> Latin adaptations of Greek New Comedy for the Roman stage, built on stock characters, disguise, and clever slaves.
Miles Gloriosus | Plautus's comedy about a vain soldier, Pyrgopolynices, outwitted by his clever slave Palaestrio over a captured mistress.
Terence | Roman comic playwright, a former slave from Carthage, who adapted Menander's Greek New Comedy into plays like The Brothers.
Titus Maccius Plautus | Roman comic playwright who adapted Greek New Comedy, writing Miles Gloriosus, Menaechmi, Pseudolus, and Amphitryon.

# Roman Poetry
> The Augustan-era flowering of Latin verse in elegy, epic, and lyric, from Catullus's invective to Ovid's mythological epic.
Gaius Valerius Catullus | Roman Neoteric poet known for passionate, obscene verse addressed to his lover Lesbia and translations of Sappho.
Ovid | Augustan Roman poet of the Metamorphoses and Ars Amatoria, exiled by Augustus to Tomis on the Black Sea.
Publius Ovidius Naso | Full Roman name of Ovid, Augustan poet of the Metamorphoses and Ars Amatoria, later exiled to Tomis.
Publius Papinius Statius | Flavian-era Roman epic poet who wrote the Thebaid, on the war of the Seven Against Thebes, and the Silvae.
Sextus Propertius | Augustan Roman elegiac poet whose love poems address his mistress Cynthia; part of Maecenas's literary circle.

# Roman Prose and Satire
> Latin narrative prose that satirized contemporary Roman society through picaresque and fantastical plots.
The Golden Ass | Apuleius's Latin novel, the only ancient Roman novel to survive complete; follows Lucius transformed into a donkey by magic.
Satyricon | Petronius's satirical Latin prose novel following Encolpius's misadventures, including the lavish banquet of the freedman Trimalchio.

# Roman Tragedy and Philosophy
> Seneca's Stoic philosophy and blood-soaked Latin tragedies reworking Greek myth for a Roman audience.
Seneca the Younger | Roman Stoic philosopher and tutor to Nero who wrote bloody Latin tragedies like Medea and Thyestes alongside philosophical letters.

# Literary Terms and Criticism
> Concepts from ancient literary theory used to analyze the effects and structure of classical drama and poetry.
catharsis | Aristotelian concept from the Poetics describing tragedy's purgation or purification of the audience's pity and fear.
