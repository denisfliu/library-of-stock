# Hinduism
> Vedic and Dharmic tradition of South Asia spanning scripture, gods, and the karma-samsara-moksha framework.
Shiva | Hindu god of destruction and transformation, depicted with a trident, third eye, and cosmic Nataraja dance.
Diwali | Hindu festival of lights celebrating the return of Rama and Sita, marked by oil lamps and fireworks.
Holi | Hindu spring festival of colors commemorating Krishna and the burning of the demoness Holika.
yoga | Hindu system of physical and meditative disciplines aimed at union with the divine and liberation.
Durga | Hindu warrior goddess who slew the buffalo demon Mahishasura; celebrated in Durga Puja and Navaratri.
Krishna | Avatar of Vishnu and central figure of the Bhagavad Gita, revered as a divine cowherd and hero of the Mahabharata.
Ganesha | Elephant-headed Hindu god of beginnings and remover of obstacles, son of Shiva and Parvati.
Hinduism | Dharmic religion of South Asia encompassing Vedic scripture, karma, reincarnation, and gods like Vishnu and Shiva.
Upanishads | Philosophical Hindu texts concluding the Vedas, developing the concepts of atman, Brahman, and moksha.
dharma | Concept of cosmic law, duty, and righteous conduct central to Hindu and Buddhist thought.
samsara | Cycle of death and rebirth in Hindu, Buddhist, and Jain thought, escaped through liberation.
brahmins | Priestly caste atop the Hindu varna system, traditionally responsible for ritual and teaching.
Vishnu | Hindu preserver god who descends in avatars including Krishna and Rama to restore cosmic order.
Lakshmi | Hindu goddess of wealth and fortune, consort of Vishnu, celebrated at Diwali.
Maya | Vedantic concept of illusion veiling ultimate reality behind the phenomenal world.
Swami Vivekananda | Hindu reformer who introduced Vedanta to the West at the 1893 Parliament of World Religions.
cows | Sacred animals in Hinduism, protected as symbols of nonviolence and abundance.
karma | Law of moral cause and effect across Hindu, Buddhist, and Jain thought shaping rebirth.
Puja | Hindu ritual worship of a deity through offerings, prayers, and rites performed at home or temple shrines.
Bhagavad-Gita = The Bhagavad Gita | Hindu scripture within the Mahabharata in which Krishna counsels the warrior Arjuna on duty and devotion before battle.
Cremation | Funerary rite central to Hindu tradition, ideally performed beside a sacred river like the Ganges.
Kali | Fierce Hindu goddess of destruction and time, depicted dark-skinned with a necklace of skulls.
tantra | Esoteric ritual tradition in Hinduism and Vajrayana Buddhism using mantras, mandalas, and yogic practice.
Varanasi | Hindu holy city on the Ganges where pilgrims bathe and cremate the dead for spiritual liberation.
Vedas = The Vedas | Oldest and most authoritative Hindu scriptures, comprising hymns, rituals, and philosophy.
guru | Spiritual teacher who guides disciples toward enlightenment in Hindu and Sikh tradition.
mantras | Sacred syllables or phrases repeated in Hindu and Buddhist meditation and ritual, such as Om.
soma | Sacred ritual drink and its deified plant described in the Rig Veda.
atman | Hindu concept of the individual soul or self, ultimately identical with Brahman.
Ayurveda | Traditional Indian system of medicine rooted in Hindu philosophy, balancing the body's doshas.
Brahma | Hindu creator god of the Trimurti, alongside Vishnu and Shiva, often depicted with four heads.
Gayatri Mantra | Revered Vedic hymn addressed to the solar deity Savitr, recited during morning devotion.
Hanuman | Monkey god of devotion and strength who aided Rama in the Ramayana.
India | Birthplace of Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism, and Sikhism.
Indra | Vedic king of the gods associated with thunder, storms, and the slaying of the serpent Vritra.
lotus | Flower symbolizing purity and spiritual awakening in Hindu and Buddhist iconography.
Mount Meru | Cosmic mountain at the center of the universe in Hindu and Buddhist cosmology.
murti | Consecrated image or statue of a deity used as a focus of worship in Hindu temples.
Nepal | Himalayan nation combining majority Hindu practice with Buddhism, birthplace of the historical Buddha.
om | Sacred syllable representing the primordial sound of the universe in Hindu and Buddhist chanting.
pujas | Plural of puja, Hindu ritual worship offerings made to deities at home shrines or temples.
Rama | Avatar of Vishnu and hero of the Ramayana, who rescues his wife Sita from the demon Ravana.
Rig Veda | Oldest of the four Vedas, a collection of hymns to Vedic deities like Indra and Agni.
Saraswati | Hindu goddess of knowledge, music, and the arts, consort of Brahma.
threads | Sacred thread worn by twice-born Hindu males after an initiation rite (upanayana).
Vaishnavism | Hindu tradition centered on devotion to Vishnu and his avatars, especially Krishna and Rama.
aarti | Hindu ritual of waving lit lamps before a deity's image accompanied by devotional song.
Adi Shankara | 8th-century philosopher who systematized Advaita Vedanta's nondualist teachings.
Ganges River | Sacred Hindu river where pilgrims bathe for purification and ashes of the dead are scattered.
Hindu weddings | Ceremonies featuring the sacred fire and the saptapadi, seven vow-bound steps taken by the couple.
kama | One of Hinduism's four aims of life, referring to sensory and aesthetic pleasure.
Kumbh Mela | Massive periodic Hindu pilgrimage festival where devotees bathe at sacred river confluences.
lotuses | Flowers symbolizing purity and spiritual awakening in Hindu and Buddhist iconography.
Parvati | Hindu mother goddess, consort of Shiva and mother of Ganesha, embodying marital devotion.
reincarnation | Belief in rebirth of the soul across lifetimes shaped by karma, central to Hindu, Buddhist, and Jain thought.
Rigveda | Oldest of the four Vedas, a collection of hymns to Vedic deities like Indra and Agni.
Sanskrit | Classical liturgical language of Hindu scripture including the Vedas and Upanishads.
Shakti | Divine feminine cosmic energy personified in goddesses like Durga, Kali, and Parvati.
Surya | Hindu sun god, charioteer of the skies, invoked in the Gayatri Mantra.
wheels | Symbol of dharma and the cycle of samsara, as on the Buddhist dharmachakra and the Indian flag's Ashoka Chakra.
yugas | Four cyclical world ages in Hindu cosmology, descending from the golden Satya Yuga to the current dark Kali Yuga.
Agni | Vedic god of fire who carries sacrificial offerings to the other gods.
Bengal | South Asian region known for devotion to the goddess Durga and Kali.
Bhagavata Purana | Hindu text detailing Krishna's life and devotional (bhakti) theology.
bhakti | Hindu path of loving devotion to a personal deity as a means to liberation.
bhakti yoga | Hindu spiritual discipline of devotional surrender to a chosen deity, praised in the Bhagavad Gita.
Brahman | Ultimate, formless reality underlying the universe in Hindu Vedantic philosophy.
circumambulation | Ritual walking around a sacred object or shrine, practiced at Hindu temples and the Islamic Kaaba alike.
Garuda | Divine eagle-like bird who serves as Vishnu's mount in Hindu mythology.
Hare Krishnas | Members of ISKCON, the International Society for Krishna Consciousness, devoted to chanting Krishna's names.
Hindu temples | Structures housing consecrated murti images, sites of puja and darshan with the divine.
lingam | Abstract phallic emblem representing Shiva, central to his temple worship.
Mahabharata | Epic Hindu poem containing the Bhagavad Gita, recounting the Kurukshetra war between Pandavas and Kauravas.
moksha | Liberation from the cycle of rebirth (samsara), the ultimate goal of Hindu spiritual life.
Raksha Bandhan | Hindu festival celebrating the bond between brothers and sisters, marked by tying a protective thread.
yajna | Vedic fire sacrifice ritual performed with mantras and offerings to the gods.
Yama | Hindu and Buddhist god of death who judges souls in the afterlife.
Ashrama | Four traditional stages of Hindu life: student, householder, forest-dweller, and renunciant.
ashrams | Hindu spiritual hermitages where students live and study under a guru.
Bali | Indonesian island that remains predominantly Hindu amid the region's Muslim majority.
Brahmin | Priestly caste atop the Hindu varna system, traditionally responsible for ritual and teaching.
breathing | Regulated through pranayama in yogic practice as a means of spiritual and bodily control.
chakras | Energy centers along the body used in yogic and tantric spiritual practice.
Dalits | Group historically outside the Hindu caste system, once called "untouchables."
elephant | Sacred animal embodied by the god Ganesha and honored in Hindu temple processions.
Hindu funerals | Rites centered on cremation, ideally beside a sacred river, to release the soul for rebirth.
Kalika | Fierce form of the goddess Kali associated with time and destruction.
Maharishi Mahesh Yogi | Indian guru who popularized Transcendental Meditation in the West, notably teaching the Beatles.
Navaratri | Nine-night Hindu festival honoring the goddess Durga, culminating in Dussehra.
Purusha | Cosmic primeval being in Vedic thought whose sacrificial dismemberment created the universe and caste system.
rishis | Ancient Vedic seers credited with composing the hymns of the Rig Veda.

Tamils | South Indian and Sri Lankan ethnic group with a strong Shaivite Hindu devotional tradition.

Vedanta | Philosophical school interpreting the Upanishads, including Advaita's nondualism, foundational to modern Hindu thought.
wheel | Symbol of dharma and the cycle of samsara, as on the Buddhist dharmachakra and the Indian flag's Ashoka Chakra.

Tibet | Himalayan region where Tibetan Buddhism developed under figures like the Dalai Lama alongside native Bon influence.
Zen Buddhism | Japanese meditative school emphasizing koans and seated meditation; traces its lineage to Bodhidharma.
Tibetan Buddhism | Vajrayana school led by the Dalai Lama, known for tantric practice, mandalas, and reincarnated lamas.
meditation | Contemplative practice central to Buddhism, also present in Hindu and other traditions' spiritual discipline.
koans | Paradoxical riddles used in Zen Buddhism to provoke sudden insight beyond rational thought.
Lotus Sutra | Influential Mahayana scripture teaching universal Buddha-nature, foundational to Tiantai and Nichiren Buddhism.
Pure Land Buddhism | Mahayana school devoted to Amitabha Buddha, seeking rebirth in his Western Pure Land through faith.
nirvana | Buddhist state of liberation from suffering and the cycle of rebirth by extinguishing craving.
Pali | Middle Indic language of the Theravada Buddhist canon, the Pali Canon or Tripitaka.
Bodhidharma | Semi-legendary Indian monk credited with bringing Chan/Zen Buddhism to China.
bodhisattvas | Mahayana Buddhist beings who postpone their own nirvana to help others attain enlightenment.
Theravada Buddhism | "Way of the Elders" school dominant in Southeast Asia, adhering closely to the Pali Canon.
Dalai Lama | Tibetan Buddhist spiritual leader believed to be a reincarnated bodhisattva of compassion.
Buddhism | Dharmic religion founded by Siddhartha Gautama teaching the Four Noble Truths and the path to nirvana.
Guanyin | East Asian bodhisattva of mercy and compassion, a feminine form of Avalokitesvara.
Heart Sutra | Short, influential Mahayana Buddhist text teaching the emptiness (sunyata) of all phenomena.
Mahayana Buddhism | "Great Vehicle" school emphasizing the bodhisattva path, dominant in East Asia.
Diamond Sutra | Mahayana Buddhist scripture on the perfection of wisdom and non-attachment; contains the oldest dated printed book.
Pure Land = The Pure Land | Amitabha Buddha's paradisal realm sought through faith in Pure Land Buddhist practice.
sangha | Community of Buddhist monks, nuns, and practitioners; one of the Three Jewels alongside Buddha and dharma.
Amitabha | Buddha of infinite light presiding over the Western Pure Land in Mahayana Buddhism.
arhats | Theravada Buddhist practitioners who have attained enlightenment and liberation from rebirth.
Bon | Indigenous Tibetan religion predating Buddhism, later absorbed into Tibetan Buddhist practice.
Buddhist monks | Ordained male renunciants who follow the Vinaya and form the core of the sangha.
mandalas | Geometric spiritual diagrams representing the cosmos, used in Vajrayana Buddhist and Hindu ritual.
Myanmar | Southeast Asian nation where Theravada Buddhism is the dominant religion.

sutras | Buddhist scriptural discourses attributed to the Buddha, such as the Lotus and Heart Sutras.
Buddha = The Buddha | Title of Siddhartha Gautama, the enlightened teacher whose life and teachings founded Buddhism.
Four Noble Truths | Buddha's core teaching on suffering, its cause, its cessation, and the path to end it.
Mahayana | "Great Vehicle" branch of Buddhism emphasizing universal liberation through the bodhisattva path.
Nichiren | 13th-century Japanese monk who founded a school centered on chanting devotion to the Lotus Sutra.
Sri Lanka | Island nation where Theravada Buddhism is the majority religion, alongside Hindu, Muslim, and Christian minorities.

Amitabha Buddha | Buddha of infinite light presiding over the Western Pure Land in Mahayana Buddhism.

dukkha | Buddhist concept of suffering or unsatisfactoriness, the first of the Four Noble Truths.

mandala | Geometric spiritual diagram representing the cosmos, used in Vajrayana Buddhist and Hindu ritual.

pure lands | Paradisal Buddha-fields, especially Amitabha's Western Pure Land, sought through devotional rebirth.

Vajrayana Buddhism | "Diamond Vehicle" tantric school dominant in Tibet, using mantras, mandalas, and esoteric ritual.
14th Dalai Lama | Tenzin Gyatso, current and exiled Dalai Lama, Nobel Peace laureate and global face of Tibetan Buddhism.
Avalokitesvara | Mahayana bodhisattva of compassion, worshipped in East Asia in the feminine form Guanyin.
bodhisattva | Mahayana Buddhist being who postpones their own nirvana to help others attain enlightenment.

Buddhist sutras | Scriptural discourses attributed to the Buddha, such as the Lotus and Heart Sutras.
Burma | Southeast Asian nation, also called Myanmar, where Theravada Buddhism is the dominant religion.
diamond | Symbol of indestructible clarity in Vajrayana ("Diamond Vehicle") Buddhism and the Diamond Sutra.

# Jainism
> Ancient Indian ascetic tradition of nonviolence, the Tirthankaras, and the Digambara-Svetambara split.
Jainism | Ancient Indian ascetic tradition founded by Mahavira, emphasizing ahimsa; split into Digambara and Svetambara sects.
ahimsa | Principle of nonviolence central to Jainism and also embraced by Hinduism and Buddhism.
Mahavira | 24th and final Tirthankara, traditional reformer-founder of Jainism in 6th-century BCE India.
Tirthankaras | Twenty-four Jain spiritual teachers who achieve liberation and show others the path; Mahavira is the last.
Rishabha | First Tirthankara of Jainism, traditionally credited with founding civilization's arts and crafts.
jiva | Jain and Hindu term for the individual soul bound by karma until liberated.
Paryushana | Annual Jain festival of fasting, repentance, and forgiveness.
Svetambara | "White-clad" Jain sect that permits monastics to wear simple robes, unlike the nude Digambara.
aparigraha | Jain vow of non-possession and non-attachment to material things.
Digambara | "Sky-clad" Jain sect whose monks renounce clothing entirely as a sign of total non-attachment.
Jain monks | Ascetic renunciants who practice extreme nonviolence, some going nude as Digambara.
Jain temples | Ornate structures housing images of the Tirthankaras, sites of Jain worship and pilgrimage.

Guru Gobind Singh | Tenth Sikh Guru who founded the Khalsa order and declared the Guru Granth Sahib the eternal Guru.
Guru Nanak | Founder of Sikhism in 15th-century Punjab, teaching one God and the equality of all people.
Khalsa | Sikh order of initiated warrior-saints founded by Guru Gobind Singh, bound by the Five Ks.
Adi Granth | Alternate name for the Guru Granth Sahib, the central Sikh scripture.
Guru Granth Sahib | Central Sikh scripture, treated as the eternal living Guru after Guru Gobind Singh.
gurus | The line of ten spiritual leaders beginning with Guru Nanak who shaped Sikh teaching.
Amrit Sanchar | Sikh initiation ceremony inducting members into the Khalsa with sweetened water stirred by a sword.
Amritsar | Punjabi city home to the Golden Temple, Sikhism's holiest shrine.
Golden Temple | Harmandir Sahib in Amritsar, Sikhism's holiest shrine, surrounded by a sacred pool.
Guru Nanak Dev Ji | Founder of Sikhism in 15th-century Punjab, teaching one God and the equality of all people.
Mul Mantar | Opening verse of the Guru Granth Sahib, summarizing core Sikh belief in one God.
Sikh gurus | The line of ten spiritual leaders beginning with Guru Nanak who shaped Sikh teaching.
Guru Nanak Dev | Founder of Sikhism in 15th-century Punjab, teaching one God and the equality of all people.
Langar | Free communal kitchen and meal served at Sikh gurdwaras to all visitors regardless of background.
Nihang | Order of armed Sikh warrior-ascetics known for distinctive blue robes and tall turbans.
Sri Guru Granth Sahib | Central Sikh scripture, treated as the eternal living Guru after Guru Gobind Singh.

Zoroastrianism | Ancient Persian faith founded by Zoroaster worshipping Ahura Mazda through fire temples; dualism with the evil Ahriman.
fire | Central symbol of purity maintained in Zoroastrian fire temples.
Ahura Mazda | Supreme god of Zoroastrianism, source of goodness and light opposing the destructive Ahriman.
fire temples | Zoroastrian places of worship maintaining a sacred perpetual flame.
Zoroaster | Persian prophet who founded Zoroastrianism upon receiving revelation of Ahura Mazda.
Gathas | Oldest hymns of the Zoroastrian Avesta, traditionally composed by Zoroaster himself.
Ahriman | Destructive spirit opposing Ahura Mazda in Zoroastrian dualism.

Anahita | Ancient Iranian goddess of water and fertility honored in Zoroastrian tradition.

Parsis | Zoroastrian community descended from Persians who fled to India to escape Islamic persecution.
