# Hinduism
> Vedic and Dharmic tradition of South Asia spanning scripture, gods, and the karma-samsara-moksha framework.

# Buddhism
> Teachings of the Buddha and their Theravada, Mahayana, and Vajrayana developments across Asia.

# Jainism
> Ancient Indian ascetic tradition of nonviolence, the Tirthankaras, and the Digambara-Svetambara split.

# Sikhism
> Punjabi monotheistic faith of the ten Gurus, the Guru Granth Sahib, and the Khalsa.

# Zoroastrianism
> Ancient Persian dualistic faith of Zoroaster, Ahura Mazda, and sacred fire.

# Judaism
> Hebrew Bible, Talmud, festivals, and figures of the Jewish tradition from the patriarchs onward.

# Christianity
> New Testament, church history, denominations, and figures from Jesus through the Reformation and beyond.

# Islam
> Qur'an, hadith, the Five Pillars, and the Sunni-Shi'a traditions founded by Muhammad.

# East Asian Traditions
> Shinto, Confucianism, and Taoism, and their syncretic overlap across China, Japan, Korea, and Vietnam.

# New Religious Movements and Baha'i
> 19th- and 20th-century faiths and restorationist movements, from Baha'i and Mormonism to Scientology.

# African, Diaspora, and Indigenous Traditions
> Yoruba-derived and syncretic New World religions, plus shamanic and indigenous belief systems worldwide.

# Comparative Concepts and General Identifiers
> Cross-tradition motifs, symbols, numerology, and terms that recur across multiple religions.
