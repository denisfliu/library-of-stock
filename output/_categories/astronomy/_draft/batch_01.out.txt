quasars	Active Galaxies & High-Energy Phenomena	Extremely luminous active galactic nuclei powered by supermassive black holes accreting matter; among the most distant observable objects.
Jupiter	Solar System	Largest planet in the solar system, a gas giant with the Great Red Spot storm and the four large Galilean moons.
luminosity	Physical Concepts	Total energy a star radiates per unit time; combines with temperature via the Stefan-Boltzmann law to place stars on the HR diagram.
star formation	Stars & Stellar Evolution	Process by which stars condense from collapsing clouds of gas and dust within molecular clouds.
neutron stars	Stellar Remnants & Compact Objects	Extremely dense remnants of core-collapse supernovae, composed almost entirely of neutrons; observed as pulsars when rotating.
Comets	Solar System	Icy small bodies that develop comas and tails on approaching the Sun, originating in the Kuiper belt or Oort cloud.
cosmic microwave background radiation	Cosmology	Relic thermal radiation from about 380,000 years after the Big Bang, key evidence for the hot Big Bang model.
gamma ray bursts	Active Galaxies & High-Energy Phenomena	Extremely energetic explosions, the brightest electromagnetic events known, linked to collapsing massive stars or neutron star mergers.
Uranus	Solar System	Ice giant planet with an extreme roughly 98-degree axial tilt, discovered by William Herschel in 1781.
Dark Matter	Cosmology	Unseen mass inferred from galactic rotation curves and gravitational lensing that does not interact via electromagnetism.
Edwin Hubble	Astronomers & Physicists	American astronomer who showed galaxies lie beyond the Milky Way and discovered the universe's expansion via Hubble's Law.
elliptical galaxies	Galaxies & Large-Scale Structure	Smooth, ellipsoidal galaxies with little gas or ongoing star formation, composed largely of older stars.
asteroids	Solar System	Small rocky bodies orbiting the Sun, concentrated mainly in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter.
cosmic inflation	Cosmology	Theorized period of exponential expansion in the universe's first fraction of a second, explaining its flatness and uniformity.
gravitational lensing	Cosmology	Bending of light by massive objects predicted by general relativity, used to detect dark matter and distant galaxies.
gravitational waves	Physical Concepts	Ripples in spacetime from accelerating massive objects like merging black holes, first directly detected by LIGO in 2015.
pulsars	Stellar Remnants & Compact Objects	Rapidly rotating neutron stars emitting beams of radiation observed as regular pulses.
supermassive black holes	Active Galaxies & High-Energy Phenomena	Black holes of millions to billions of solar masses found at the centers of most galaxies, powering active galactic nuclei.
Venus	Solar System	Second planet from the Sun, with a thick CO2 atmosphere causing a runaway greenhouse effect and surface temperatures over 460 C.
exoplanets	Exoplanets & Astrobiology	Planets orbiting stars other than the Sun, detected via transit photometry, radial velocity, and direct imaging.
Mars	Solar System	Fourth planet from the Sun, the "Red Planet," home to the solar system's largest volcano, Olympus Mons.
metallicity	Physical Concepts	Proportion of a star's or galaxy's mass composed of elements heavier than hydrogen and helium.
Neptune	Solar System	Outermost ice giant planet, discovered via its gravitational effect on Uranus; orbited by the moon Triton.
radio waves	Observational Astronomy & Instruments	Long-wavelength electromagnetic radiation used via radio telescopes to study pulsars, the CMB, and galactic structure.
sunspots	Solar System	Temporary dark regions on the Sun's photosphere caused by magnetic flux concentrations, following an 11-year cycle.
black holes	Stellar Remnants & Compact Objects	Regions of spacetime with gravity so strong not even light can escape, formed from collapsed massive stars or larger processes.
Crab Nebula	Stellar Remnants & Compact Objects	Supernova remnant from a star that exploded in 1054 CE, observed by Chinese astronomers; houses the Crab Pulsar.
helium	Physical Concepts	Second most abundant element in the universe, produced in Big Bang nucleosynthesis and by stellar fusion.
supernovae	Stellar Remnants & Compact Objects	Explosive deaths of massive stars or accreting white dwarfs, briefly outshining entire galaxies and seeding heavy elements.
Titan	Solar System	Saturn's largest moon, with a thick nitrogen atmosphere and lakes of liquid methane and ethane.
blue	Physical Concepts	Color associated with hot, high-temperature stars per Wien's law and blackbody radiation.
distance	Physical Concepts	Fundamental astronomical measurement determined via parallax, standard candles, or redshift.
Enceladus	Solar System	Saturn's icy moon with active water-ice geysers erupting from its south pole, suggesting a subsurface ocean.
hydrogen	Physical Concepts	Most abundant element in the universe, fuel for stellar fusion via the proton-proton chain and CNO cycle.
spiral galaxies	Galaxies & Large-Scale Structure	Galaxies with a flattened, rotating disk and spiral arms of gas, dust, and young stars, like the Milky Way.
stellar wind	Stars & Stellar Evolution	Outflow of charged particles from a star's outer layers, driving mass loss especially in giant and massive stars.
temperature	Physical Concepts	Determines a star's color and spectral classification; central axis of the Hertzsprung-Russell diagram.
cosmic dust	Physical Concepts	Fine particles of solid material in interstellar space, contributing to the extinction and reddening of starlight.
Magellanic Clouds	Galaxies & Large-Scale Structure	Two irregular dwarf galaxies orbiting the Milky Way, visible from the Southern Hemisphere.
main sequence	Stars & Stellar Evolution	Stage of stable hydrogen-fusing stars forming a diagonal band on the Hertzsprung-Russell diagram.
mass	Physical Concepts	Fundamental property determining a star's fate, luminosity, and lifetime.
nucleosynthesis	Stars & Stellar Evolution	Process by which elements are formed via nuclear fusion in stars or in the early universe.
reionization	Cosmology	Epoch when ultraviolet radiation from the first stars and galaxies ionized neutral hydrogen throughout the universe.
Taurus	Stars & Stellar Evolution	Zodiac constellation containing the Pleiades and Hyades star clusters, the bright star Aldebaran, and the Crab Nebula.
Triton	Solar System	Neptune's largest moon, likely a captured Kuiper belt object with a retrograde orbit and cryovolcanic activity.
white dwarf	Stellar Remnants & Compact Objects	Dense remnant core of a low-to-medium mass star left after it sheds its outer layers as a planetary nebula.
Wolf-Rayet stars	Stars & Stellar Evolution	Massive, hot, evolved stars losing mass rapidly via strong stellar winds, precursors to some supernovae.
21-centimeter line	Observational Astronomy & Instruments	Radio emission line from neutral hydrogen's hyperfine transition, used to map galactic structure and the early universe.
accretion	Stars & Stellar Evolution	Process by which matter falls onto a star, black hole, or protoplanetary disk, releasing gravitational energy.
Big Bang	Cosmology	Event marking the origin of the universe's expansion from a hot, dense state roughly 13.8 billion years ago.
Ceres	Solar System	Largest object in the asteroid belt, classified as a dwarf planet.
corona	Solar System	The Sun's outermost atmospheric layer, visible during total solar eclipses and far hotter than the photosphere.
gamma rays	Physical Concepts	Highest-energy electromagnetic radiation, produced in processes like supernovae, pulsars, and gamma-ray bursts.
globular clusters	Galaxies & Large-Scale Structure	Dense, roughly spherical collections of old stars orbiting in a galaxy's halo.
hydrostatic equilibrium	Stars & Stellar Evolution	Balance between inward gravity and outward pressure that maintains a star's stable structure.
James Webb Space Telescope	Observational Astronomy & Instruments	Infrared space telescope launched in 2021, successor to Hubble, studying early galaxies and exoplanet atmospheres.
Kuiper belt	Solar System	Disk of icy bodies beyond Neptune's orbit, home to Pluto and the source of many short-period comets.
Lambda-CDM model	Cosmology	Standard cosmological model combining a cosmological constant (dark energy) with cold dark matter to explain cosmic structure and expansion.
Saturn	Solar System	Ringed gas giant planet, second largest in the solar system, with the moons Titan and Enceladus.
Type Ia supernovae	Stellar Remnants & Compact Objects	Thermonuclear explosions of white dwarfs exceeding the Chandrasekhar limit, used as standard candles to measure cosmic distances.
Vera Rubin	Astronomers & Physicists	American astronomer whose galaxy rotation curve observations provided key evidence for dark matter.
white dwarfs	Stellar Remnants & Compact Objects	Dense remnant cores of low-to-medium mass stars supported by electron degeneracy pressure.
X-rays	Observational Astronomy & Instruments	High-energy electromagnetic radiation used to study black holes, neutron stars, and hot gas in galaxy clusters.
accretion disks	Stars & Stellar Evolution	Rotating disks of matter spiraling inward onto a star, black hole, or protoplanet, releasing energy through friction.
Active Galactic Nuclei	Active Galaxies & High-Energy Phenomena	Compact, extremely luminous regions at galactic centers powered by accretion onto a supermassive black hole.
Andromeda Galaxy	Galaxies & Large-Scale Structure	Nearest large spiral galaxy to the Milky Way, on a collision course with it in several billion years.
blazars	Active Galaxies & High-Energy Phenomena	Active galactic nuclei with a relativistic jet pointed nearly directly at Earth, showing rapid variability.
carbon	Physical Concepts	Key element produced via the triple-alpha process in stellar cores, essential for organic chemistry.
Cepheid variables	Stars & Stellar Evolution	Pulsating stars whose period directly relates to luminosity, used as standard candles for measuring cosmic distances.
Charon	Solar System	Pluto's largest moon, massive enough relative to Pluto that the pair orbit a common barycenter outside Pluto.
CMBR	Cosmology	Abbreviation for the cosmic microwave background radiation, relic heat from the early universe.
convection	Stars & Stellar Evolution	Energy transport mechanism via bulk motion of plasma, occurring in stellar envelopes and cores.
Doppler effect	Physical Concepts	Shift in observed wavelength due to relative motion, used to measure radial velocities and redshift.
Gaia	Observational Astronomy & Instruments	European Space Agency mission mapping precise positions, distances, and motions of over a billion stars.
galaxies	Galaxies & Large-Scale Structure	Massive gravitationally bound systems of stars, gas, and dark matter, ranging from dwarf to giant elliptical and spiral forms.
galaxy clusters	Galaxies & Large-Scale Structure	Largest gravitationally bound structures in the universe, containing hundreds to thousands of galaxies bound by dark matter.
galaxy mergers	Galaxies & Large-Scale Structure	Collisions and combinations of galaxies over cosmic time, often triggering starbursts and reshaping galactic structure.
infrared	Observational Astronomy & Instruments	Electromagnetic radiation used to observe cool objects, dust-obscured star formation, and distant redshifted galaxies.
instability strip	Stars & Stellar Evolution	Region of the Hertzsprung-Russell diagram where stars pulsate, including Cepheids and RR Lyrae variables.
interstellar medium	Physical Concepts	Matter, gas and dust, that fills the space between stars within a galaxy.
