Film questions in quizbowl distribute across a canon of national movements and their defining directors. The French New Wave (Godard, Truffaut, Rivette, Varda) and Italian Neorealism (De Sica, Rossellini, later Fellini and Antonioni) anchor postwar European cinema, while a broader European art-cinema cluster covers Bergman, Bunuel, Dreyer, Fassbinder, Herzog, Tarkovsky, and Kieslowski across Sweden, Spain, Germany, and Eastern Europe. Classic Hollywood supplies its own dense cluster — Hitchcock, Welles, Wilder, Capra — organized around film noir and the studio-era star system, with Citizen Kane, Casablanca, and Vertigo recurring as both standalone answers and supporting clues.

American cinema after the studio era splits into two clusters: New Hollywood auteurs (Scorsese, Kubrick, Coppola) who reshaped genre filmmaking in the late 1960s and 1970s with The Godfather, Taxi Driver, and Apocalypse Now, and an independent/experimental strand running from Cassavetes and Maya Deren through Lynch, Tarantino, and Spike Lee to more recent figures like Sofia Coppola and Paul Thomas Anderson. East Asian cinema forms another major cluster, led by Kurosawa's samurai films, Ozu's family dramas, Wong Kar-wai's romantic style, and the Korean New Wave (Bong Joon-ho, Park Chan-wook).

A smaller but consistent share of questions covers world cinema beyond these poles — Satyajit Ray and Indian Parallel Cinema, Iranian art-house directors like Kiarostami, African cinema through Ousmane Sembene, and scattered national new waves (Taiwanese, Romanian, Czechoslovak). Genre film — westerns, movie musicals, epics, and horror — appears as its own recurring cluster independent of national movement, alongside film theory and technique (montage, auteur theory, the male gaze, the Cannes and Venice festivals). Countries and cities function as organizing answerlines in their own right, gathering clues about the directors and movements associated with them rather than any single film.
