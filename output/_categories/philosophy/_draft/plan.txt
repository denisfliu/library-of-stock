# Ancient Greek Philosophy
> The Pre-Socratics, Socrates, Plato, Aristotle, and the Hellenistic schools that first framed metaphysics, ethics, and logic.

# Medieval & Islamic-Jewish Philosophy
> Patristic and Scholastic thought alongside the Islamic and Jewish philosophers who preserved and extended the classical inheritance.

# Renaissance & Early Modern Rationalism
> Continental rationalists and Renaissance political thinkers who grounded knowledge in reason and mathematical method.

# British Empiricism & Enlightenment
> Empiricist epistemology and Enlightenment social-contract theory from Britain and the continent.

# German Idealism & 19th-Century Philosophy
> Kant through Hegel, Schopenhauer, Kierkegaard, Marx, and Nietzsche, and the systems and reactions that followed the critical turn.

# Ethics & Political Philosophy
> Utilitarian, deontological, and contractarian theories of right action, justice, and the state, ancient to contemporary.

# Analytic Philosophy, Language & Pragmatism
> The Anglo-American tradition from Frege and Russell through logical positivism, ordinary language philosophy, formal semantics, and pragmatism.

# Philosophy of Mind, Epistemology & Science
> Theories of knowledge, mind, and scientific method, from Gettier problems to functionalism and the philosophy of science.

# Phenomenology & Existentialism
> Consciousness, being, and lived experience as examined by Husserl, Heidegger, Sartre, and their existentialist successors.

# Post-structuralism & Critical Theory
> Twentieth-century French and German thought critiquing power, language, and ideology, from the Frankfurt School to deconstruction.

# Non-Western Philosophy
> Chinese, Indian, Japanese, and African philosophical traditions and their major schools and thinkers.
