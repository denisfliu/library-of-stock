Immanuel Kant	German Idealism & 19th-Century Philosophy	Prussian Enlightenment philosopher; categorical imperative and transcendental idealism in the Critique of Pure Reason; founder of German Idealism.
David Hume	British Empiricism & Enlightenment	Scottish empiricist skeptical of induction and necessary causation; reason is "slave of the passions"; formulated the is-ought problem.
Martin Heidegger	Phenomenology & Existentialism	German phenomenologist of Being and Time; analyzed Dasein's being-toward-death; influenced existentialism and hermeneutics.
Rene Descartes	Renaissance & Early Modern Rationalism	French rationalist; "cogito ergo sum," mind-body dualism, and methodical doubt in the Meditations; father of modern philosophy.
Ludwig Wittgenstein	Analytic Philosophy, Language & Pragmatism	Austrian-British philosopher of the Tractatus and later Philosophical Investigations; language games and the private language argument.
Aristotle	Ancient Greek Philosophy	Student of Plato who founded the Peripatetic school and formal logic; wrote the Nicomachean Ethics and Politics; theory of four causes.
Bertrand Russell	Analytic Philosophy, Language & Pragmatism	British logician; co-authored Principia Mathematica with Whitehead; theory of descriptions developed in "On Denoting."
John Locke	British Empiricism & Enlightenment	English empiricist; tabula rasa in An Essay Concerning Human Understanding; natural rights and social contract theory.
Georg Wilhelm Friedrich Hegel	German Idealism & 19th-Century Philosophy	German idealist of dialectical logic (thesis-antithesis-synthesis) and the master-slave dialectic; wrote The Phenomenology of Spirit.
Jacques Derrida	Post-structuralism & Critical Theory	French philosopher who founded deconstruction; critiqued logocentrism and coined differance in Of Grammatology.
Utilitarianism	Ethics & Political Philosophy	Consequentialist theory holding the right act maximizes aggregate happiness; developed by Jeremy Bentham and John Stuart Mill.
Arthur Schopenhauer	German Idealism & 19th-Century Philosophy	Pessimist German philosopher of The World as Will and Representation; drew on Kant and Buddhism; influenced Nietzsche.
Michel Foucault	Post-structuralism & Critical Theory	French theorist of power and knowledge; Discipline and Punish and The History of Sexuality; panopticon and biopower.
Baruch Spinoza	Renaissance & Early Modern Rationalism	Dutch rationalist; pantheist monism argued geometrically in the Ethics; conatus and "deus sive natura."
Friedrich Nietzsche	German Idealism & 19th-Century Philosophy	German philosopher of will to power and eternal recurrence; declared "God is dead"; the Ubermensch in Thus Spoke Zarathustra.
truth	Philosophy of Mind, Epistemology & Science	Core epistemological concept; correspondence, coherence, and pragmatist theories dispute what makes a proposition true.
free will	Philosophy of Mind, Epistemology & Science	Debate over whether choices are undetermined; compatibilist and libertarian positions oppose hard determinism.
knowledge	Philosophy of Mind, Epistemology & Science	Traditionally defined as justified true belief; central topic of epistemology, complicated by Gettier problems.
Stoicism	Ancient Greek Philosophy	Hellenistic school founded by Zeno of Citium teaching virtue and acceptance of fate; later exemplified by Seneca, Epictetus, and Marcus Aurelius.
John Stuart Mill	Ethics & Political Philosophy	English utilitarian; On Liberty defends individual freedom via the harm principle; refined Bentham's hedonic calculus.
Willard Van Orman Quine	Analytic Philosophy, Language & Pragmatism	American analytic philosopher; "Two Dogmas of Empiricism" attacked the analytic-synthetic distinction; naturalized epistemology.
William James	Analytic Philosophy, Language & Pragmatism	American pragmatist and psychologist; Pragmatism and The Varieties of Religious Experience; the "will to believe."
Gottlob Frege	Analytic Philosophy, Language & Pragmatism	German logician; founder of modern logic and philosophy of language; sense/reference distinction in "On Sense and Reference."
Karl Marx	German Idealism & 19th-Century Philosophy	German philosopher and economist; historical materialism and class struggle in The Communist Manifesto and Capital.
Plato	Ancient Greek Philosophy	Student of Socrates; theory of Forms, the philosopher-kings of the Republic, and the allegory of the cave.
causation	Philosophy of Mind, Epistemology & Science	Metaphysical relation Hume reduced to mere constant conjunction; central to philosophy of science and metaphysics.
Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz	Renaissance & Early Modern Rationalism	German rationalist; monads, pre-established harmony, and the principle of sufficient reason; co-invented calculus.
Hannah Arendt	Ethics & Political Philosophy	German-American political theorist; The Origins of Totalitarianism and the "banality of evil" in Eichmann in Jerusalem.
John Dewey	Analytic Philosophy, Language & Pragmatism	American pragmatist; instrumentalism and influential work on education and democracy.
John Rawls	Ethics & Political Philosophy	American political philosopher; A Theory of Justice's veil of ignorance and original position define justice as fairness.
William of Ockham	Medieval & Islamic-Jewish Philosophy	English scholastic nominalist; Ockham's razor favors the simplest sufficient explanation.
aesthetics	German Idealism & 19th-Century Philosophy	Study of beauty and art systematized by Kant's Critique of Judgment, which distinguishes the beautiful from the sublime.
George Berkeley	British Empiricism & Enlightenment	Irish empiricist and idealist bishop; "esse est percipi" denies matter exists apart from perception.
history	German Idealism & 19th-Century Philosophy	Philosophy of history examines historical meaning and progress, notably Hegel's dialectical unfolding of Spirit through time.
Peter Singer	Ethics & Political Philosophy	Australian utilitarian ethicist; Animal Liberation and effective altruism; arguments against speciesism.
Simone de Beauvoir	Phenomenology & Existentialism	French existentialist; The Second Sex argues "one is not born, but becomes, a woman."
Thomas Hobbes	British Empiricism & Enlightenment	English philosopher; Leviathan describes escaping the state of nature's "war of all against all" via social contract.
time	Philosophy of Mind, Epistemology & Science	Metaphysical topic covering McTaggart's A- and B-series, presentism versus eternalism, and time's relation to change.
consciousness	Philosophy of Mind, Epistemology & Science	Central problem of philosophy of mind; qualia and the "hard problem" of subjective experience.
ethics	Ethics & Political Philosophy	Branch of philosophy studying right action; deontological, consequentialist, and virtue-based approaches.
Jean-Jacques Rousseau	British Empiricism & Enlightenment	Genevan Enlightenment thinker; The Social Contract's general will and the natural goodness corrupted by society.
metaphysics	Philosophy of Mind, Epistemology & Science	Branch of philosophy studying the fundamental nature of reality, being, and existence.
Neoplatonism	Medieval & Islamic-Jewish Philosophy	Late-antique school founded by Plotinus positing emanation from the One; influenced Christian and Islamic thought.
Avicenna	Medieval & Islamic-Jewish Philosophy	Persian polymath (Ibn Sina); flying man argument and the essence-existence distinction; influenced scholasticism.
skepticism	Philosophy of Mind, Epistemology & Science	Epistemological position doubting the possibility of certain knowledge; from ancient Pyrrhonism to Cartesian doubt.
atoms	Ancient Greek Philosophy	Indivisible material particles posited by Democritus and Leucippus, later adopted in Epicurus's atomism.
Robert Nozick	Ethics & Political Philosophy	American libertarian political philosopher; Anarchy, State, and Utopia defends the minimal state against Rawls.
Socrates	Ancient Greek Philosophy	Athenian philosopher who left no writings; the elenchus method; tried and executed on charges of impiety.
Thomas Nagel	Philosophy of Mind, Epistemology & Science	American philosopher of mind; "What Is It Like to Be a Bat?" argues subjective experience resists physicalist reduction.
Charles Sanders Peirce	Analytic Philosophy, Language & Pragmatism	American founder of pragmatism; the pragmatic maxim in "How to Make Our Ideas Clear"; pioneer of semiotics.
Jean-Paul Sartre	Phenomenology & Existentialism	French existentialist; Being and Nothingness holds "existence precedes essence"; radical freedom and bad faith.
justice	Ethics & Political Philosophy	Central political concept concerning fair distribution and desert; from Plato's Republic to Rawls's justice as fairness.
phenomenology	Phenomenology & Existentialism	Method founded by Husserl studying the structures of consciousness through bracketing, or epoche.
pragmatism	Analytic Philosophy, Language & Pragmatism	American movement holding truth is what "works" practically; founded by Peirce, developed by James and Dewey.
Saul Kripke	Analytic Philosophy, Language & Pragmatism	American analytic philosopher; Naming and Necessity argues names are rigid designators, defends a posteriori necessity.
Edmund Husserl	Phenomenology & Existentialism	German founder of phenomenology; the intentionality of consciousness and the transcendental ego.
Antonio Gramsci	Post-structuralism & Critical Theory	Italian Marxist theorist; concept of cultural hegemony developed in the Prison Notebooks.
Blaise Pascal	Renaissance & Early Modern Rationalism	French mathematician-philosopher; Pascal's wager and the Pensees; "the heart has its reasons."
Confucius	Non-Western Philosophy	Chinese sage of ren and li recorded in the Analects; foundational to Confucianism and filial piety.
Daniel Dennett	Philosophy of Mind, Epistemology & Science	American philosopher of mind; Consciousness Explained's multiple drafts model and the intentional stance.
idealism	German Idealism & 19th-Century Philosophy	View that reality is fundamentally mind-dependent; Berkeley's subjective form, Kant's transcendental form, Hegel's absolute form.
logic	Analytic Philosophy, Language & Pragmatism	Formal study of valid inference; syllogistic from Aristotle, symbolic logic from Frege and Russell.
love	Ancient Greek Philosophy	Examined as eros in Plato's Symposium, where Diotima's ladder ascends from bodies to the Form of Beauty.
Parmenides	Ancient Greek Philosophy	Pre-Socratic of Elea; argued being is one and unchanging, and that change is mere illusion.
Philosophical Investigations	Analytic Philosophy, Language & Pragmatism	Wittgenstein's posthumous later work rejecting the Tractatus's picture theory for language games and meaning-as-use.
atheism	Philosophy of Mind, Epistemology & Science	Denial of God's existence, debated via the problem of evil and against classical theistic proofs.
Critique of Pure Reason	German Idealism & 19th-Century Philosophy	Kant's 1781 work reconciling rationalism and empiricism through transcendental idealism and synthetic a priori knowledge.
John Duns Scotus	Medieval & Islamic-Jewish Philosophy	Scottish scholastic "Subtle Doctor"; haecceity (individuating "thisness") and the univocity of being.
Mary Wollstonecraft	British Empiricism & Enlightenment	English Enlightenment feminist; A Vindication of the Rights of Woman argues for women's education and rationality.
possible worlds	Philosophy of Mind, Epistemology & Science	Modal-logic device for analyzing necessity and possibility; David Lewis's modal realism treats them as concretely existing.
Thomas Aquinas	Medieval & Islamic-Jewish Philosophy	Dominican scholastic; synthesized Aristotle with Christian theology in the Summa Theologica; the Five Ways and natural law.
categorical imperative	German Idealism & 19th-Century Philosophy	Kant's supreme moral principle: act only on maxims one could will as universal law, treating persons as ends.
Epicurus	Ancient Greek Philosophy	Founder of Epicureanism; pleasure as absence of pain, atomism, and dispelling fear of death and the gods.
Gilles Deleuze	Post-structuralism & Critical Theory	French philosopher of Difference and Repetition and (with Guattari) A Thousand Plateaus; the "body without organs."
Averroes	Medieval & Islamic-Jewish Philosophy	Andalusian Islamic philosopher (Ibn Rushd); commentaries on Aristotle defending philosophy's compatibility with faith.
cynicism	Ancient Greek Philosophy	Hellenistic school founded by Antisthenes, exemplified by Diogenes of Sinope's ascetic rejection of convention.
epistemology	Philosophy of Mind, Epistemology & Science	Branch of philosophy studying the nature, sources, and limits of knowledge and justified belief.
induction	Philosophy of Mind, Epistemology & Science	Inference from particular observations to general conclusions; Hume's problem questions its rational justification.
Jeremy Bentham	Ethics & Political Philosophy	English founder of modern utilitarianism; felicific calculus and the "greatest happiness principle"; designed the panopticon.
Martha Nussbaum	Ethics & Political Philosophy	American philosopher; capabilities approach to justice developed with Amartya Sen; work on emotion and virtue ethics.
