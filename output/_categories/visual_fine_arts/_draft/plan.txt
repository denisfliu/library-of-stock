# Ancient, Medieval, and Byzantine Art
> Greek and Roman sculpture, Byzantine icons, and early manuscript illumination that establish the classical and devotional foundations of Western art.

# Italian Renaissance and Mannerism
> Florence, Rome, and Venice from Giotto through Michelangelo and Titian, plus the elongated, artificial style that followed as Mannerism.

# Northern Renaissance and Netherlandish Painting
> Flemish and Dutch-influenced panel painters north of the Alps, from Van Eyck's oil technique to Bruegel's peasant scenes and altarpieces.

# Baroque and Rococo
> The dramatic light and movement of 17th-century Baroque painting and sculpture, followed by Rococo's lighter, ornamental 18th-century successor.

# Neoclassicism, Romanticism, and Nineteenth-Century Landscape
> The classical revival and emotional, sublime landscapes and history painting spanning the late 18th and 19th centuries, including the Hudson River School.

# Realism, Impressionism, and Post-Impressionism
> Painters who broke from academic convention to depict everyday life and fleeting light, from Courbet's Realism through Impressionism and Post-Impressionism.

# Cubism, Expressionism, and Early Modernism
> The fractured forms and expressive color of early 20th-century modernism, spanning Cubism, Fauvism, Expressionism, and Futurism.

# Surrealism and Dada
> Dreamlike imagery and anti-rational provocation in the interwar avant-garde, from Dada's absurdist gestures to Surrealism's exploration of the unconscious.

# Abstract Expressionism, Pop Art, and Contemporary Art
> Postwar American gesture painting and the pop, minimalist, and conceptual movements that followed into today's contemporary art world.

# Global Traditions and Muralism
> Artistic traditions outside the European mainstream and socially engaged movements, including Japanese ukiyo-e, Mexican muralism, and modern global art.

# Sculpture, Architecture, and Photography
> Sculptors, architects, and photographers considered by medium rather than painterly movement, from Bernini's marble to modern documentary photography.

# Materials, Motifs, and Common-Link Subjects
> Recurring materials, colors, motifs, and subjects, along with the cities, countries, and museums associated with art, that anchor common-link questions.
