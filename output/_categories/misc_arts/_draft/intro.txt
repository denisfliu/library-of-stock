Misc Arts questions gather disciplines that fall outside painting, sculpture, and the other named Fine Arts units — chiefly performance art, fashion, world music, and decorative craft. Performance and conceptual art form the largest cluster, led by Marina Abramovic's endurance pieces and the Fluxus collective (Yoko Ono, Joseph Beuys), alongside conceptual photographers and text artists like Cindy Sherman and Barbara Kruger who use the body or staged image to interrogate identity and consumerism.

Fashion supplies a second major cluster, spanning historic couturiers (Christian Dior's New Look, Elsa Schiaparelli's Surrealist collaborations) through contemporary designers (Alexander McQueen, Versace, Comme des Garcons) and the accessories and garments associated with them. World musical instruments and traditions — gamelan, sitar, didgeridoo — appear alongside general musical vocabulary like rhythm and drums, testing familiarity with non-Western musical cultures.

A smaller cluster covers decorative and functional crafts across civilizations: pottery, carpets, masks, silk, and terracotta recur as answerlines tied to specific cultural traditions rather than named individual artists. Public and installation art, represented by Ai Weiwei and Anish Kapoor, and countries, cities, and institutions — China, Japan, France, Tate Modern — round out the unit, each gathering clues about the traditions and artists associated with them.
