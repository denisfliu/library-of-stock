# Performance and Conceptual Art
> Body, action, and idea-driven art that treats the artist or concept itself as medium.
Marina Abramovic | Serbian performance artist known for endurance pieces testing physical and psychological limits, including The Artist Is Present.
Barbara Kruger | American conceptual artist known for bold text-over-image works critiquing consumerism and power, such as "Your body is a battleground."
Fluxus | International 1960s art collective blending performance, music, and visual art in playful, anti-commercial "events."
Yoko Ono | Japanese-American Fluxus-associated conceptual and performance artist; created Cut Piece and married John Lennon.
Andy Warhol | Pop artist who also produced experimental films and staged multimedia performances at his Factory studio.
performance art | Art form using the artist's body and live action as medium, exemplified by Marina Abramovic and the Fluxus artists.
Cabaret | Live entertainment tradition combining music, comedy, and drag, historically centered in Weimar-era Berlin clubs.
Cindy Sherman | American conceptual photographer known for staged self-portraits critiquing media representations of women.
drag | Performance tradition of exaggerated gender presentation, historically tied to cabaret and ballroom culture.
Joseph Beuys | German Fluxus-associated artist known for performance pieces using felt, fat, and symbolic materials, including I Like America and America Likes Me.

# Fashion and Design
> Couture houses, designers, and garments treated as art objects.
Yves Saint-Laurent | French fashion designer who founded his own house and popularized le smoking, the women's tuxedo suit.
Alexander McQueen | British fashion designer known for dramatic, theatrical runway shows and provocative avant-garde tailoring.
handbags | Fashion accessory produced as a signature item by luxury houses like Hermes and Chanel.
Versace | Italian fashion house founded by Gianni Versace, known for bold prints and glamorous celebrity dressing.
Christian Dior | French designer whose 1947 "New Look" redefined postwar women's fashion with a cinched waist and full skirt.
Comme des Garcons | Japanese fashion house led by designer Rei Kawakubo, known for deconstructed, avant-garde silhouettes.
Elsa Schiaparelli | Italian-French designer known for Surrealist-influenced fashion collaborations with Salvador Dali, including the Lobster Dress.
shoes | Footwear elevated to art object by designers like Manolo Blahnik and Christian Louboutin.

# World Musical Instruments and Traditions
> Instruments and rhythmic traditions from non-Western musical cultures.
gamelan | Indonesian ensemble of tuned percussion instruments, including metallophones and gongs, central to Javanese and Balinese music.
rhythm | Fundamental musical element organizing time, central to percussion-driven traditions like gamelan and drumming.
didgeridoo | Long wind instrument of Aboriginal Australian origin, producing a continuous drone through circular breathing.
drums | Percussion instruments central to rhythmic traditions across nearly every world culture.
sitar | Long-necked plucked string instrument central to Hindustani classical music, popularized in the West by Ravi Shankar.

# Ceramics, Textiles, and Decorative Crafts
> Functional and ceremonial objects made from clay, fiber, and glass across cultures.
chairs | Furniture form frequently designed by artists and architects as functional sculpture.
pottery | Ceramic vessels shaped from clay, one of humanity's oldest decorative and functional art forms.
carpets | Woven textile floor coverings, especially prized in Persian and Islamic decorative art traditions.
glass | Decorative and functional material shaped through blowing and casting, prominent in studio art glass traditions like Chihuly's.
masks | Ceremonial and decorative objects worn across cultures, from African ritual masks to Venetian carnival masks.
silk | Luxury textile fiber historically traded along the Silk Road and central to Chinese decorative craft.
Terracotta | Fired clay material used for sculpture and pottery, notably China's life-sized Terracotta Army.

# Public and Installation Art
> Large-scale sculpture and unsanctioned art made for public and civic space.
Anish Kapoor | British-Indian sculptor known for large-scale reflective public works like Cloud Gate ("The Bean") in Chicago.
Ai Weiwei | Chinese conceptual artist and activist known for large-scale installations like Sunflower Seeds and outspoken government criticism.
graffiti | Unsanctioned public wall art tradition that evolved into recognized street art through figures like Banksy and Jean-Michel Basquiat.

# Countries, Cities, and Institutions
> Nations, cities, and museums that organize clues across art forms and traditions.
Japan | Home to traditional arts like ikebana and the postwar Gutai performance art group.
China | Home to the terracotta warrior sculptures and contemporary conceptual artist Ai Weiwei.
New York City | Hub of performance and conceptual art movements, home to Andy Warhol's Factory and countless galleries.
Germany | Home to Fluxus artist Joseph Beuys and a major postwar contemporary art scene.
France | Home to fashion houses Dior and Saint Laurent, centered in Paris.
Italy | Home to fashion houses Versace and Prada and a long design and craft tradition.
Arabic | Language and script frequently incorporated into calligraphy and decorative Islamic art.
Argentina | Home to tango culture and a distinct Latin American contemporary art scene.
Cuba | Home to a distinct Afro-Caribbean art and music tradition shaped by its revolutionary history.
Hawaii | Home to hula dance and a distinct Polynesian craft and decorative art tradition.
India | Home to the sitar tradition and a long history of textile and carpet-weaving crafts.
People's Republic of China | Home to the terracotta warrior sculptures and contemporary conceptual artist Ai Weiwei.
Tate Modern | London museum of international modern and contemporary art housed in a former power station.
