Jazz questions in quizbowl trace the genre's stylistic evolution roughly chronologically. Early jazz and swing supply bandleaders like Duke Ellington, Louis Armstrong, and Count Basie, whose orchestras defined the music's first commercial peak. Bebop follows with Charlie Parker, Dizzy Gillespie, Thelonious Monk, and Bud Powell, who traded swing's danceable riffs for fast, harmonically dense improvisation centered on clubs like Birdland. Miles Davis anchors the next stretch of the canon almost single-handedly, appearing across cool jazz (Kind of Blue) and fusion (Bitches Brew) alike, while John Coltrane, Sonny Rollins, and McCoy Tyner represent the hard bop and post-bop saxophone tradition that followed bebop.

A smaller but consistent cluster covers jazz's departures from the mainstream lineage: Ornette Coleman and Charles Mingus for free jazz and genre-blending composition, and Herbie Hancock and Chick Corea for the electric fusion era. Jazz vocalists — Ella Fitzgerald, Nina Simone, and the blues tradition behind Bessie Smith — form their own recurring cluster, often tested through signature songs like "Strange Fruit" rather than through instrumental technique.

Instruments and general jazz vocabulary make up a large share of the distribution: piano, saxophone, trumpet, trombone, guitar, drums, and double bass are tested both as answerlines in their own right and as connective tissue between performers. Countries and regions outside the United States — Brazil and bossa nova, Cuba and Afro-Cuban rhythm, France and Gypsy jazz, Nigeria and Afrobeat — round out the canon, reflecting jazz's absorption of, and export to, musical traditions worldwide.
