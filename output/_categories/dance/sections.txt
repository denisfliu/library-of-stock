# Classical and Romantic Ballet
> The 19th-century story-ballet canon and the composers who scored it.
Swan Lake | Tchaikovsky ballet of Odette and her evil double Odile, cursed by the sorcerer Rothbart; choreographed by Petipa and Ivanov.
Marius Petipa | French-Russian choreographer of the Imperial Ballet who codified the classical repertoire: Swan Lake, Sleeping Beauty, and Nutcracker.
The Nutcracker | Tchaikovsky's Christmas ballet of Clara, the Sugar Plum Fairy, and the Nutcracker Prince; choreographed by Petipa and Ivanov.
Giselle | Romantic ballet of a peasant girl who dies of heartbreak and becomes a Wili; choreographed by Coralli and Perrot.
The Sleeping Beauty | Tchaikovsky ballet of Princess Aurora, cursed by Carabosse and awakened by a prince's kiss; choreographed by Petipa.
Coppelia | Delibes's comic ballet about a lifelike dancing doll built by the toymaker Dr. Coppelius.
La Bayadere | Petipa's Orientalist ballet set in India, famous for its "Kingdom of the Shades" corps de ballet scene.
Leo Delibes | French Romantic composer of the ballets Coppelia and Sylvia.
Don Quixote | Minkus's comic ballet loosely based on Cervantes, famous for its show-stopping Kitri and Basilio grand pas de deux.
Giselle, ou les Wilis | Romantic ballet of a peasant girl who dies of heartbreak and becomes a Wili; choreographed by Coralli and Perrot.
the Sugar Plum Fairy = Sugar Plum Fairy | The Nutcracker's regal fairy ruler of the Land of Sweets, famous for her celesta-scored variation.
Marius | Refers to Marius Petipa, French-Russian choreographer who codified the classical ballet repertoire.

# The Ballets Russes Era
> Diaghilev's early-20th-century company and the composers, dancers, and scandals it produced.
The Rite of Spring | Stravinsky's 1913 ballet of pagan sacrifice whose premiere provoked a riot; choreographed by Nijinsky for the Ballets Russes.
Vaslav Nijinsky | Legendary Ballets Russes dancer-choreographer of The Rite of Spring and Afternoon of a Faun; Diaghilev's star performer.
Michel Fokine | Choreographer for Diaghilev's Ballets Russes who created The Firebird and The Dying Swan.
The Afternoon of a Faun | Debussy-scored Ballets Russes work choreographed and danced by Nijinsky, controversial for its sensual final gesture.
The Dying Swan | Fokine's short solo set to Saint-Saens's music, famously performed by Anna Pavlova.
The Firebird | Stravinsky's first Ballets Russes score, based on Russian folklore about a magical bird; choreographed by Fokine.
Igor Stravinsky | Russian composer of the Ballets Russes scores The Firebird, Petrushka, and The Rite of Spring.
Leonide Massine | Ballets Russes dancer-choreographer who created Parade and later worked in symphonic ballet.
Parade | Ballets Russes work with music by Satie, scenario by Cocteau, sets by Picasso, and choreography by Massine.
Sergei Diaghilev | Russian impresario who founded the Ballets Russes, commissioning Stravinsky and employing Nijinsky and Fokine.

# 20th-Century Ballet Companies and Choreographers
> Balanchine, Ashton, Robbins, and the national companies that shaped modern ballet repertoire.
George Balanchine | Russian-American choreographer who co-founded New York City Ballet and pioneered neoclassical, plotless ballet.
Romeo and Juliet | Prokofiev's ballet adaptation of Shakespeare's tragedy, a staple 20th-century repertory work choreographed by Lavrovsky and others.
Frederick Ashton | British choreographer and founding figure of the Royal Ballet, known for narrative works like Cinderella.
The Royal Ballet | Britain's national ballet company, based at Covent Garden, shaped by founding choreographer Frederick Ashton.
Agnes de Mille | American choreographer who integrated ballet into Broadway with Oklahoma!'s dream ballet; also choreographed Rodeo.
Jerome Robbins | American choreographer at New York City Ballet and on Broadway, choreographing West Side Story.
Joffrey Ballet | American ballet company founded by Robert Joffrey, known for reviving The Rite of Spring's original choreography.
Maria Tallchief | First major Native American prima ballerina, a leading dancer for Balanchine's New York City Ballet.
Rodeo | Agnes de Mille's Copland-scored ballet about a tomboyish cowgirl finding romance at a ranch.

# Modern Dance Pioneers
> The early modern dance rebellion against ballet convention, led by Graham and Cunningham.
Martha Graham | American modern dance pioneer who developed the contraction-and-release technique bearing her name; choreographed Appalachian Spring.
Merce Cunningham | American modern dance choreographer who used chance procedures and long collaborated with composer John Cage.
Appalachian Spring | Martha Graham's modern dance work set to Aaron Copland's score, depicting American pioneer life.

# Concert Dance and Companies
> Postwar modern and contemporary dance companies built around a single choreographer's vision.
Alvin Ailey | American choreographer who founded the Alvin Ailey American Dance Theater; known for the modern dance suite Revelations.
Revelations | Alvin Ailey's signature modern dance suite set to spirituals and blues, exploring African-American religious experience.
Twyla Tharp | American choreographer blending ballet and modern dance with popular music; choreographed Movin' Out.
Katherine Dunham | American dancer-anthropologist who fused Caribbean and African dance forms into modern dance technique.
Alvin Ailey Jr. | American choreographer who founded the Alvin Ailey American Dance Theater; known for the modern dance suite Revelations.

# Ballet Stars and Technique
> Celebrated dancers and the vocabulary of positions, steps, and training methods they embody.
en pointe | Ballet technique of dancing on the tips of the toes, enabled by reinforced pointe shoes.
arabesque | Ballet position with one leg extended straight behind the body while balancing on the other.
Misty Copeland | American Ballet Theatre principal, the first Black woman to hold that rank in the company's history.
Odile | Swan Lake's Black Swan, the sorcerer Rothbart's daughter who deceives Prince Siegfried; famous for her 32 fouettes.
pas de deux | Ballet duet, typically for a ballerina and male partner, forming the centerpiece of classical story ballets.
Rudolf Nureyev | Soviet-born dancer who defected to the West in 1961; renowned partner of Margot Fonteyn.
Agrippina Vaganova | Russian dancer and pedagogue whose codified training method, the Vaganova method, is taught worldwide.
Anna Pavlova | Russian ballerina famed for her performance of The Dying Swan and worldwide touring company.
jumps | Elevation movements like the grand jete and entrechat that showcase a dancer's technique.
Margot Fonteyn | British prima ballerina and longtime Royal Ballet star, celebrated for her partnership with Rudolf Nureyev.
Mikhail Baryshnikov | Soviet-born dancer who defected in 1974; noted for American Ballet Theatre roles and later modern dance work.

# Musical Theater and Popular Dance
> Dance on the Broadway stage and in Hollywood film musicals.
Bob Fosse | American choreographer of angular, jazz-hands style; choreographed Chicago and Cabaret.
Fred Astaire | American film dancer known for elegant tap and ballroom routines, frequent partner of Ginger Rogers.
tap dancing | American dance style using metal-tipped shoes to create rhythmic percussion, popularized by Fred Astaire.

# Social, Folk, and World Dance
> Vernacular partnered and community dance traditions outside the concert stage.
tango | Sensual partnered dance originating in Argentina and Uruguay's Rio de la Plata region.
belly dance | Middle Eastern dance style emphasizing hip and torso articulation, associated with Egyptian and Turkish traditions.
breakdancing | Acrobatic street dance style originating in the 1970s New York hip-hop scene.
flamenco | Passionate Andalusian dance style combining percussive footwork, guitar, and cante singing.
lindy hop | Energetic partnered swing dance that emerged in 1920s-30s Harlem alongside big-band jazz.
Square dance | American folk dance for four couples in a square formation, guided by a caller.

# Countries, Terms, and Motifs
> Nations, companies, and general vocabulary that recur across dance styles and eras.
New York City | Home to George Balanchine's New York City Ballet and American Ballet Theatre.
Russia | Home to the Imperial Ballet, the Ballets Russes, and choreographers Petipa, Fokine, and Balanchine.
United States of America | Home to American Ballet Theatre, New York City Ballet, and modern dance pioneers Graham and Cunningham.
white | Color associated with the traditional Romantic tutu and Odette's virtuous swan in Swan Lake.
ballet | General Western theatrical dance form built on codified positions and technique, developed from French and Russian court traditions.
Denmark | Home to the Royal Danish Ballet and the Bournonville technique.
Dominican Republic | Birthplace of merengue and bachata, popular partnered social dances.
drums | Percussion instruments central to many folk and world dance traditions' rhythmic accompaniment.
France | Birthplace of codified ballet technique under Louis XIV and home to the Paris Opera Ballet.
Ireland | Home of Irish step dance, popularized internationally by Riverdance.
Spain | Home to flamenco, the passionate Andalusian dance tradition.
