Dance questions in quizbowl center overwhelmingly on classical ballet, both the 19th-century Russian Imperial repertoire and the choreographers who built it. Swan Lake, The Nutcracker, The Sleeping Beauty, Giselle, Coppelia, and La Bayadere anchor the canon, with Marius Petipa as the dominant choreographer and Tchaikovsky and Delibes as the recurring composers. A second major cluster covers Sergei Diaghilev's Ballets Russes, whose commissions (Stravinsky's The Firebird, Petrushka, and The Rite of Spring) and dancers (Nijinsky, Fokine, Massine) reshaped early-20th-century dance and supply some of the unit's densest cross-references between music and dance.

American dance forms the next largest cluster, split between ballet institution-builders (Balanchine and New York City Ballet, Jerome Robbins, Agnes de Mille) and modern dance founders who rejected ballet's vocabulary entirely — Martha Graham and Merce Cunningham foremost among them, with Alvin Ailey's Revelations and Twyla Tharp's crossover work extending the modern lineage into the postwar era. Individual star dancers recur as answerlines independent of any single company: Nureyev, Baryshnikov, Fonteyn, Pavlova, and Misty Copeland are tested on biography and technique (arabesque, pas de deux, en pointe, fouettes) as often as on any specific ballet.

A smaller but consistent share of questions covers dance outside the ballet-modern axis: musical theater and film choreography (Bob Fosse, Fred Astaire, tap dancing), and social and folk traditions from around the world (tango, flamenco, belly dance, lindy hop, breakdancing, square dance). Countries and cities appear as organizing answerlines in their own right — Russia, France, Denmark, and New York City each gather clues about the companies and traditions associated with them rather than a single work or person.
