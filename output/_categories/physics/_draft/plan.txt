# Classical Mechanics
> Newtonian dynamics, Lagrangian and Hamiltonian formalism, oscillators, rigid-body motion, and orbital mechanics.

# Electromagnetism
> Electric and magnetic fields, circuits, and the unification of electricity and magnetism into electromagnetic waves.

# Thermodynamics and Statistical Mechanics
> Heat, entropy, and the statistical treatment of large systems of particles, from ideal gases to phase transitions.

# Fluid Mechanics
> Flow of liquids and gases, viscosity, turbulence, and the equations governing continuous media.

# Optics and Waves
> The behavior of light and other waves: reflection, refraction, diffraction, interference, and wave phenomena in general.

# Quantum Mechanics
> The mathematical formalism and physical phenomena of the quantum world, from wavefunctions to entanglement.

# Atomic, Nuclear, and Particle Physics
> The structure of atoms and nuclei, radioactive decay, and the fundamental particles and forces of the Standard Model.

# Condensed Matter and Materials Physics
> The collective behavior of solids and liquids: conductivity, magnetism, superconductivity, and semiconductor devices.

# Relativity, Gravitation, and Cosmology
> Einstein's special and general relativity, black holes, gravitational waves, and the large-scale structure and history of the universe.
