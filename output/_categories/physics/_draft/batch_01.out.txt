entropy	Thermodynamics and Statistical Mechanics	Measure of disorder or number of microstates; never decreases in an isolated system per the second law of thermodynamics.
angular momentum	Classical Mechanics	Conserved rotational quantity, product of moment of inertia and angular velocity; quantized in atomic and particle physics.
neutrinos	Atomic, Nuclear, and Particle Physics	Nearly massless, weakly-interacting leptons produced in beta decay; come in three flavors that oscillate between states.
magnetic field	Electromagnetism	Vector field produced by moving charges or intrinsic spin; exerts force on moving charges via the Lorentz force law.
quantum tunneling	Quantum Mechanics	Passage of particles through classically forbidden potential barriers; explains alpha decay and scanning tunneling microscopy.
index of refraction	Optics and Waves	Ratio of light's speed in vacuum to its speed in a medium; governs Snell's law and total internal reflection.
viscosity	Fluid Mechanics	Measure of a fluid's resistance to shear flow; appears in the Navier-Stokes equations and Reynolds number.
diffraction	Optics and Waves	Bending and spreading of waves around obstacles or through apertures; basis of diffraction gratings and double-slit patterns.
quantum entanglement	Quantum Mechanics	Correlation between particles' quantum states regardless of separation; tested by Bell's theorem and used in quantum computing.
spin	Quantum Mechanics	Intrinsic angular momentum of particles with no classical analog; determines whether particles are fermions or bosons.
time	Relativity, Gravitation, and Cosmology	Quantity treated as relative rather than absolute in special relativity, tied to entropy's arrow via the second law.
General Relativity	Relativity, Gravitation, and Cosmology	Einstein's geometric theory of gravity as curvature of spacetime; predicts black holes and gravitational waves.
linear momentum	Classical Mechanics	Product of mass and velocity, conserved absent external forces; central to collision and rocket-propulsion problems.
photons	Quantum Mechanics	Massless quanta of the electromagnetic field exhibiting wave-particle duality; central to the photoelectric effect and lasers.
polarization	Optics and Waves	Orientation of a transverse wave's oscillation, especially light's electric field; set by Brewster's angle and polarizing filters.
pressure	Thermodynamics and Statistical Mechanics	Force per unit area exerted by a fluid or gas; appears in the ideal gas law and Bernoulli's principle.
temperature	Thermodynamics and Statistical Mechanics	Measure of average kinetic energy of particles in a system; defines thermal equilibrium via the zeroth law.
lasers	Optics and Waves	Devices producing coherent light via stimulated emission and population inversion; used in interferometry and optical tweezers.
mass	Classical Mechanics	Measure of an object's inertia and gravitational coupling; equivalence of inertial and gravitational mass underlies general relativity.
superconductivity	Condensed Matter and Materials Physics	Zero electrical resistance below a critical temperature; explained by BCS theory and Cooper pairs.
moment of inertia	Classical Mechanics	Rotational analog of mass, quantifying resistance to angular acceleration; depends on mass distribution about the axis.
phase transitions	Thermodynamics and Statistical Mechanics	Changes between states or orderings of matter, such as melting or the Ising model's ferromagnetic transition, marked by critical points.
Reynolds number	Fluid Mechanics	Dimensionless ratio of inertial to viscous forces predicting laminar versus turbulent flow; named for Osborne Reynolds.
scattering	Atomic, Nuclear, and Particle Physics	General process where particles or waves are deflected by interactions, as in Compton, Rutherford, and Rayleigh scattering.
Enrico Fermi	Atomic, Nuclear, and Particle Physics	Italian-American physicist who built the first nuclear reactor and developed Fermi-Dirac statistics; namesake of fermions.
ferromagnetism	Condensed Matter and Materials Physics	Spontaneous alignment of magnetic moments below the Curie temperature, as in iron; modeled by the Ising model.
Ludwig Boltzmann	Thermodynamics and Statistical Mechanics	Austrian physicist who founded statistical mechanics, linking entropy to microstate counting via S = k log W.
phonons	Condensed Matter and Materials Physics	Quantized lattice vibrations in solids that carry heat and sound; analogous to photons for the electromagnetic field.
fermions	Quantum Mechanics	Particles with half-integer spin obeying the Pauli exclusion principle, including electrons, quarks, and protons.
Lagrangian	Classical Mechanics	Function equal to kinetic minus potential energy whose extremization via least action yields equations of motion.
quantum harmonic oscillator	Quantum Mechanics	Quantum system with evenly spaced energy levels, solved via ladder operators; models molecular vibrations and phonons.
second law of thermodynamics	Thermodynamics and Statistical Mechanics	States that entropy of an isolated system never decreases; establishes the thermodynamic arrow of time.
stress	Fluid Mechanics	Force per unit area within a material, related to strain by Young's modulus and other elastic moduli.
torque	Classical Mechanics	Rotational analog of force, equal to the cross product of position and force; causes angular acceleration.
action	Classical Mechanics	Quantity with units of energy times time, extremized in the principle of least action to derive Lagrangian mechanics.
plasmas	Fluid Mechanics	Ionized gas of free electrons and ions exhibiting collective behavior; confined in tokamaks for fusion research.
Hamiltonian	Classical Mechanics	Function representing total energy as position and momentum, generating equations of motion via Hamilton's equations.
impedance	Electromagnetism	Complex measure of opposition to alternating current, combining resistance and reactance; central to circuit analysis.
magnetic monopoles	Electromagnetism	Hypothetical isolated north or south magnetic poles, not yet observed; would modify Maxwell's equations if found.
Schrodinger equation	Quantum Mechanics	Fundamental equation describing how a quantum wavefunction evolves in time; solved for the hydrogen atom and harmonic oscillator.
special relativity	Relativity, Gravitation, and Cosmology	Einstein's 1905 theory positing constant light speed and relativity of simultaneity; yields time dilation and length contraction.
chaos	Classical Mechanics	Sensitive dependence on initial conditions in nonlinear dynamical systems; characterized by Lyapunov exponents and the KAM theorem.
electric field	Electromagnetism	Vector field describing force per unit charge around charged objects; governed by Gauss's law and Coulomb's law.
frequency	Optics and Waves	Number of oscillations per unit time of a wave; inversely related to wavelength and period.
Hooke's law	Classical Mechanics	States that a spring's restoring force is proportional to its displacement; basis for simple harmonic motion.
kinetic energy	Classical Mechanics	Energy of motion, equal to one-half mass times velocity squared; interconverts with potential energy.
Navier-Stokes equations	Fluid Mechanics	Partial differential equations describing motion of viscous fluids; a Millennium Prize problem concerns their solutions' existence.
partition function	Thermodynamics and Statistical Mechanics	Statistical mechanics quantity summing over microstates' Boltzmann factors; generates free energy and other thermodynamic quantities.
Paul Dirac	Quantum Mechanics	British physicist who formulated the relativistic Dirac equation predicting antimatter and the positron.
semiconductors	Condensed Matter and Materials Physics	Materials with conductivity between conductors and insulators, doped into n-type and p-type regions; basis of diodes and transistors.
Albert Einstein	Relativity, Gravitation, and Cosmology	German-born physicist who developed special and general relativity and explained the photoelectric effect and Brownian motion.
diffusion	Thermodynamics and Statistical Mechanics	Net movement of particles from high to low concentration, driven by random thermal motion as in Brownian motion.
diodes	Condensed Matter and Materials Physics	Semiconductor devices allowing current flow in one direction via a p-n junction; used as rectifiers and LEDs.
plasma	Fluid Mechanics	Ionized state of matter with free-moving charges, the fourth state of matter; found in stars and fusion reactors.
precession	Classical Mechanics	Slow rotation of a spinning object's axis due to torque, as seen in gyroscopes and Larmor precession of spins.
Richard Feynman	Atomic, Nuclear, and Particle Physics	American physicist who developed quantum electrodynamics and Feynman diagrams; co-won the 1965 Nobel Prize.
Brownian motion	Thermodynamics and Statistical Mechanics	Random jittering of particles suspended in fluid caused by molecular collisions; explained by Einstein using statistical mechanics.
convection	Thermodynamics and Statistical Mechanics	Heat transfer via bulk fluid motion, distinct from conduction and radiation; drives atmospheric and oceanic circulation.
electrons	Atomic, Nuclear, and Particle Physics	Negatively charged elementary leptons that orbit atomic nuclei; carriers of electric current in conductors.
momentum	Classical Mechanics	Product of mass and velocity, conserved in closed systems; generalized to four-momentum in relativity.
nuclear fusion	Atomic, Nuclear, and Particle Physics	Combining of light nuclei into heavier ones releasing energy, as powers the Sun; pursued via tokamaks on Earth.
speed of light	Relativity, Gravitation, and Cosmology	Universal constant c that is invariant in all reference frames per special relativity; upper limit on information transfer.
damping	Classical Mechanics	Dissipation of oscillatory energy, characterized as underdamped, critically damped, or overdamped by the damping ratio.
Feynman diagrams	Atomic, Nuclear, and Particle Physics	Pictorial representations of particle interactions in quantum field theory, used to compute scattering amplitudes.
Heisenberg uncertainty principle	Quantum Mechanics	States that position and momentum, or energy and time, cannot both be precisely known; formulated by Werner Heisenberg.
neutrons	Atomic, Nuclear, and Particle Physics	Neutral nucleons composed of quarks, found in atomic nuclei alongside protons; free neutrons decay via beta decay.
power	Classical Mechanics	Rate of doing work or transferring energy, measured in watts; equals force times velocity.
reflection	Optics and Waves	Bouncing of waves off a surface, obeying the law of equal angles of incidence and reflection.
Charles-Augustin de Coulomb	Electromagnetism	French physicist who formulated Coulomb's law describing the force between point charges.
cosmological constant	Relativity, Gravitation, and Cosmology	Term in Einstein's field equations representing vacuum energy density, linked to dark energy and cosmic acceleration.
density	Fluid Mechanics	Mass per unit volume of a substance, key to buoyancy, Bernoulli's principle, and equation-of-state calculations.
Doppler effect	Optics and Waves	Shift in observed frequency of waves from a moving source, causing redshift in receding astronomical objects.
gluons	Atomic, Nuclear, and Particle Physics	Massless gauge bosons that mediate the strong force between quarks, carrying color charge in quantum chromodynamics.
perturbation theory	Quantum Mechanics	Method of approximating solutions to complex quantum systems by starting from a solvable system and adding small corrections.
rotation	Classical Mechanics	Motion of a body about an axis, described by angular velocity, torque, and moment of inertia.
strain	Fluid Mechanics	Deformation of a material relative to its original dimensions, related to stress via Young's modulus.
turbulence	Fluid Mechanics	Chaotic, irregular fluid flow at high Reynolds numbers, characterized by eddies and vortices.
capacitors	Electromagnetism	Circuit components storing energy in an electric field between two conductive plates; capacitance depends on geometry and dielectric.
Fourier transform	Quantum Mechanics	Mathematical operation decomposing a function into constituent frequencies; connects position and momentum representations.
heat capacity	Thermodynamics and Statistical Mechanics	Heat needed to raise a substance's temperature by one degree; specific heat capacity is heat capacity per unit mass.
