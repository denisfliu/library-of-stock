# Sondheim's Musicals
> The concept musicals and dense wordplay of Broadway's most acclaimed composer-lyricist.
Stephen Sondheim | Prolific Broadway composer-lyricist known for complex harmony and wordplay; wrote Company, Into the Woods, Sweeney Todd, and Sunday in the Park with George.
Into the Woods | Sondheim and James Lapine's musical weaving together fairy tales like Cinderella, Little Red Riding Hood, and Jack and the Beanstalk.
Company | Sondheim's concept musical about a single man, Bobby, examining marriage through vignettes with his married friends.

# Bob Fosse's Musicals
> The angular choreography and cynical showmanship of Fosse's signature shows.
Cabaret | Kander and Ebb musical set in Weimar-era Berlin's Kit Kat Klub, narrated by the sardonic Emcee amid the Nazis' rise.
Chicago | Kander and Ebb musical satirizing celebrity and the justice system through murderesses Roxie Hart and Velma Kelly; choreographed by Bob Fosse.
Pippin | Stephen Schwartz musical about Charlemagne's son seeking meaning in life, staged with Fosse's signature choreography and a manipulative Leading Player.

# Andrew Lloyd Webber and British Musicals
> The pop-inflected megamusicals that dominated the West End and Broadway from the 1970s on.
Andrew Lloyd Webber | British composer of pop-influenced megamusicals including Jesus Christ Superstar, Cats, and The Phantom of the Opera.
Jesus Christ Superstar | Lloyd Webber and Tim Rice's rock opera retelling the last week of Jesus's life from Judas's perspective.

# Contemporary and Hip-Hop Musicals
> 21st-century Broadway blending hip-hop, pop, and traditional musical theater form.
Lin-Manuel Miranda | Composer-lyricist-performer who wrote and starred in Hamilton and In the Heights, blending hip-hop with Broadway tradition.
Phillipa Soo | Actress who originated the role of Eliza Hamilton in Lin-Manuel Miranda's Hamilton.

# Golden Age and Dance-Driven Broadway
> Mid-century book musicals built around integrated dance and choreography.
West Side Story | Bernstein and Sondheim musical relocating Romeo and Juliet to rival New York gangs the Jets and Sharks; choreographed by Jerome Robbins.
A Chorus Line | Michael Bennett's musical following Broadway dancers auditioning and sharing personal stories at a single callback.
