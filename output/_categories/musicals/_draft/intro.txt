Musicals questions cluster heavily around a small set of dominant creators. Stephen Sondheim anchors the unit both as a standalone answerline and through individual shows like Into the Woods and Company, prized for their conceptual structure and intricate lyrics. Bob Fosse's directing and choreography connect three separate hit shows — Cabaret, Chicago, and Pippin — each tested independently but sharing his signature angular, sexually charged dance vocabulary. Andrew Lloyd Webber represents the British megamusical tradition, with Jesus Christ Superstar as his recurring rock-opera answerline.

A smaller cluster covers contemporary Broadway, led by Lin-Manuel Miranda's Hamilton and its cast (including Phillipa Soo), reflecting the genre's more recent hip-hop and pop-influenced turn. Golden Age and dance-driven Broadway rounds out the canon through West Side Story and A Chorus Line, both prized for integrating choreography into narrative rather than treating dance as separate spectacle.
