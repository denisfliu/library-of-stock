# Pictorialism and the Photo-Secession
> Early-20th-century efforts to establish photography as a fine art rather than mere documentation.
Alfred Stieglitz | American photographer who founded the Photo-Secession movement and Camera Work journal; married to painter Georgia O'Keeffe.
Edward Steichen | Photographer who helped found the Photo-Secession with Stieglitz; later directed MoMA's photography department and curated The Family of Man.
Paul Strand | Photographer who moved from Stieglitz's circle toward sharply focused "straight photography."

# Documentary and Depression-Era Photography
> Photographers who chronicled American poverty and rural life, especially under the Farm Security Administration.
Dorothea Lange | FSA photographer famed for "Migrant Mother," documenting Depression-era poverty and Japanese American internment.
Great Depression | 1930s economic crisis documented extensively by Farm Security Administration photographers like Lange, Evans, and Bourke-White.
Walker Evans | FSA photographer known for stark, formally precise images of rural poverty, notably in Let Us Now Praise Famous Men.
Jacob Riis | Danish-American photojournalist whose How the Other Half Lives exposed New York tenement poverty using early flash photography.
Migrant Mother | Dorothea Lange's iconic FSA photograph of a careworn mother, Florence Owens Thompson, and her children.

# Photojournalism and War Photography
> Photographers who documented conflict and world events for the press.
Margaret Bourke-White | First female war photojournalist for Life magazine; photographed Soviet industry, the Depression, and the WWII liberation of concentration camps.
Henri Cartier-Bresson | French photographer who coined the "decisive moment" concept; co-founded the Magnum Photos cooperative.
Robert Capa | Hungarian-American war photographer who covered the Spanish Civil War and D-Day; co-founded Magnum Photos.
Magnum Photos | Photographer-owned cooperative agency founded by Capa, Cartier-Bresson, and others to retain rights over their images.
World War II | Conflict documented by photojournalists including Robert Capa and Margaret Bourke-White.

# Surrealist and Avant-Garde Photography
> Experimental technique and imagery drawing on Dada and Surrealist art movements.
Man Ray | American Dadaist and Surrealist photographer known for cameraless "rayographs" and the solarization technique.
Brassai | Hungarian-French photographer known for atmospheric nighttime images of 1930s Paris, published as Paris de nuit.

# Street Photography and Portraiture
> Candid and posed images of people, from Victorian portraiture to unvarnished street observation.
Julia Margaret Cameron | 19th-century British portrait photographer known for soft-focus images of Victorian luminaries and allegorical scenes.
Diane Arbus | American photographer known for unflinching portraits of marginalized and unconventional subjects.
Weegee | American tabloid photographer known for stark flash images of New York crime scenes and nightlife.

# Fashion and Celebrity Photography
> Studio and editorial photography built around style, magazines, and public figures.
Annie Leibovitz | Contemporary portrait photographer known for staged celebrity images for Rolling Stone and Vanity Fair.
Vogue | Influential fashion magazine that showcased major photographers including Steichen and Leibovitz.

# Places, Institutions, and Motifs
> Cities, landmarks, and general vocabulary that recur across photographic movements.
Germany | Home to the Bauhaus's experimental photography program and New Objectivity photographers like August Sander.
New York City | Home to Stieglitz's 291 gallery, the Photo-Secession, and countless street photographers.
Paris | Hub of early street photography, home to Brassai's nighttime images and Man Ray's Surrealist circle.
Central Park | Frequently photographed New York City landmark designed by Frederick Law Olmsted.
photography | General medium of capturing images through light-sensitive processes, spanning documentary, art, and journalistic traditions.
Pre-Raphaelite Brotherhood | 19th-century British art movement whose aesthetic influenced early portrait photographers like Julia Margaret Cameron.
Vietnam | Subject of extensive war photojournalism, including Nick Ut's and Eddie Adams's iconic Vietnam War images.
Yosemite National Park | Landscape frequently photographed by Ansel Adams, known for his dramatic black-and-white images.
