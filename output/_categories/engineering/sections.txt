# Civil & Structural Engineering
> Bridges, buildings, and the structural principles that keep them standing.
bridges | Structures spanning gaps like valleys or waterways, classified by design (beam, arch, suspension, cable-stayed) and analyzed for load-bearing capacity.
stability | Property of a structure or system to return to equilibrium after a disturbance, a key consideration in structural design.

# Mechanical Engineering
> Machines and the mechanical components that convert and transmit force and motion.
pistons | Cylindrical components that move within a cylinder to convert pressure into linear motion, central to internal combustion engines.
screws | Simple machines with a helical ridge, or thread, that convert rotational motion into linear force, used as fasteners.

# Materials & Manufacturing
> Construction materials and the processes used to shape and fabricate them.
3D printers | Additive manufacturing devices that build objects layer by layer from digital models, using materials like plastic filament or resin.
concrete | Composite construction material of cement, aggregate, and water, reinforced with steel rebar for added tensile strength.
extrusion | Manufacturing process forcing material through a shaped die to create objects with a fixed cross-sectional profile.

# Electrical & Electronics Engineering
> Circuits, signals, and the electrical systems that carry and process them.
frequency | Rate of oscillation of a periodic signal, measured in hertz, central to circuit design and signal processing.

# Energy Engineering
> Systems for generating and converting power, including renewable energy technology.
wind turbines | Devices converting kinetic wind energy into electricity via rotating blades connected to a generator.
