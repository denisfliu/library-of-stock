Opera in quizbowl covers the full spectrum from Monteverdi's Baroque revolution to Glass's modernist minimalism. Questions ask about composers (Verdi, Puccini, Mozart, Wagner), individual operas and their plots (La Traviata, Carmen, Don Giovanni), famous arias ("Vesti la giubba"), singers (Maria Callas), and operatic concepts (verismo, the castrato tradition). The canon heavily weights four major traditions: Italian Romantic opera (Rossini through Puccini); French Grand Opera spectacle (Meyerbeer, Bizet); German Romantic music drama (Wagner); and 20th-century innovations (Britten, Glass, Adams).

Italian opera dominates by volume: Verdi and Puccini together account for roughly a third of all questions, with their signature works—La Traviata, Rigoletto, Tosca, Turandot—appearing with remarkable frequency. The verismo style (raw contemporary emotion replacing historical grandeur) emerged from late-19th-century Italy and appears as both a compositional movement and a direct answerline. Mozart ranks highest among Classical composers, though his three Italian opera collaborations with Da Ponte (Don Giovanni, The Marriage of Figaro, Così fan tutte) are answered more often than his German Singspiele.

The remaining repertoire clusters by nation and tradition. Wagner and Strauss dominate German opera questions, with Wagner's leitmotif technique and philosophical music drama influencing all modern composers. French opera centers on Bizet's Carmen (perhaps opera's most frequently answered single work) and the spectacle-driven Grand Opera of Meyerbeer. Russian opera, represented primarily by Mussorgsky's Boris Godunov, often surfaces alongside European Romantic tradition. American operatic voices—Gershwin's Porgy and Bess and Weill's The Threepenny Opera—appear sporadically but mark the genre's 20th-century global expansion.
