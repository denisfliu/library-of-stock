# Baroque & Classical Opera (1600-1800)
> Opera's earliest century, from Monteverdi's sung drama through Handel's opera seria to Mozart's synthesis of comedy and myth.

Claudio Monteverdi | Early Baroque composer of L'Orfeo (1607), among the first operas still regularly performed; helped establish recitative-based drama.
Henry Purcell | English Baroque composer whose sole full-length opera, written for a girls' school, drew on Virgil's Aeneid.
- Dido and Aeneas | Short opera of the Carthaginian queen abandoned by Aeneas; her lament "When I am laid in earth" closes it.
George Frideric Handel | Baroque composer of Italian opera seria, including Giulio Cesare and Rinaldo, written for star castrato singers.
Jean-Philippe Rameau | French Baroque composer-theorist whose tragedies en musique, built on Lully's model, added richer harmony and orchestration.
Christoph Willibald Gluck = Christoph Willibald | Reform-era composer of Orfeo ed Euridice; stripped away vocal ornamentation to prioritize dramatic clarity over display.
Orpheus | Common-link answerline spanning Monteverdi's L'Orfeo, Gluck's Orfeo ed Euridice, and Offenbach's Orpheus in the Underworld.
Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart | Classical composer of Don Giovanni, The Marriage of Figaro, and The Magic Flute, spanning opera seria and comedy.
- Don Giovanni | Opera of the seducer dragged to hell by the murdered Commendatore's statue; includes "Là ci darem la mano."
- The Magic Flute | Singspiel of Tamino's fairy-tale trials and Masonic symbolism; the Queen of the Night's aria demands an extreme high F.
- The Marriage of Figaro | Comedy of the servants Figaro and Susanna outwitting the philandering Count; sequel to Rossini's Barber of Seville.
- The Abduction from the Seraglio | Singspiel of a rescue from a Turkish harem; spoken German dialogue alternates with virtuosic arias.
- Cosi fan tutte -> Così fan tutte | Two soldiers, urged by a cynical philosopher, test their fiancées' fidelity by wooing each other's partner in disguise.
- Idomeneo, re di Creta | Early opera seria in which the Cretan king must sacrifice his son to fulfill a vow to Neptune.
Count Almaviva | Philandering Count of Mozart's Marriage of Figaro, seen earlier as a young suitor of Rosina in Rossini's prequel Barber of Seville.
Figaro | Beaumarchais's clever barber-valet, hero of both Rossini's Barber of Seville and its Mozart-composed sequel The Marriage of Figaro.
Singspiel | German-language genre alternating spoken dialogue with song, as in Mozart's Abduction from the Seraglio and The Magic Flute.

# Bel Canto & Verdi (Italian Romantic Opera)
> Italy's early-to-mid Romantic century, bel canto's florid vocal display giving way to Verdi's tighter musical drama.

Gioachino Rossini = Gioachino Antonio Rossini | Early Romantic composer of The Barber of Seville and other bel canto comedies; known for rapid-fire patter arias.
- The Barber of Seville | Comedy of the barber Figaro aiding Count Almaviva's courtship of Rosina; precedes Marriage of Figaro's plot.
Gaetano Donizetti | Prolific bel canto composer of Lucia di Lammermoor; also wrote comedies such as The Elixir of Love and Don Pasquale.
- Lucia di Lammermoor | Opera of a bride forced into marriage who murders her husband and descends into madness in the Mad Scene.
- La fille du regiment -> La fille du régiment | Comedy of Marie, a vivandière raised by a French army regiment; the tenor's aria demands nine high Cs.
- Don Pasquale | Comedy in which an elderly bachelor's sham marriage to the scheming young widow Norina teaches him a humbling lesson.
Vincenzo Bellini | Bel canto composer of Norma; wrote long, ornamented melodic lines for dramatic sopranos.
- Norma | Opera of a Druid priestess torn between duty and a forbidden Roman proconsul; "Casta diva" is her signature aria.
- I Puritani | Final opera, set amid the English Civil War; the heroine Elvira's mad scene echoes Lucia's, though this one ends happily.
Giuseppe Verdi = Giuseppe Fortunino Francesco Verdi | Mid-Romantic composer of Rigoletto, La Traviata, and Aida; tightened Italian opera's drama and psychological focus.
- La Traviata | Opera of the courtesan Violetta, who sacrifices love for her lover's family honor and dies of consumption.
- Rigoletto | Opera of a court jester whose curse dooms his daughter Gilda, killed in his place.
- "La Donna e mobile" -> "La donna è mobile" | Rigoletto's aria for the philandering Duke of Mantua, cynically insisting that women are the fickle ones.
- Aida | Opera of a captured Nubian princess loved by an Egyptian general; both are entombed alive at its close.
- Il trovatore | Rival half-brothers, the troubadour Manrico and Count di Luna, both love Leonora; features the Anvil Chorus and a gypsy's revenge.
- Otello | Late tragedy after Shakespeare; the Moorish general is manipulated by the ensign Iago into murdering his wife Desdemona.
- Don Carlos | Historical opera of the Spanish prince, his father Philip II, and the auto-da-fé staged by the Grand Inquisitor.
- Falstaff | Final opera, a comedy after Shakespeare's Merry Wives of Windsor centered on the fat knight's schemes and eventual humiliation.
- Nabucco | Early opera on the Babylonian captivity of the Jews; its chorus "Va, pensiero" became an Italian nationalist anthem.

# Verismo & Puccini (Italian Opera, 1890s-1920s)
> Late 19th-century Italy turns from Romantic grandeur to gritty realism, with Puccini as its dominant voice.

verismo | Late 19th-century Italian realist movement depicting ordinary, often violent lives; exemplified by Cavalleria Rusticana and I Pagliacci.
Giacomo Puccini | Verismo-era composer of La bohème, Tosca, and Madama Butterfly; Turandot left unfinished at his death.
- Turandot | Unfinished opera of an icy Chinese princess who poses riddles to suitors; contains the aria "Nessun dorma."
- "Nessun dorma" | Prince Calaf's aria from Turandot, vowing that no one shall sleep until he wins Turandot's hand by dawn.
- La boheme -> La bohème | Opera of poor Parisian artists; the seamstress Mimì dies of consumption after reuniting with Rodolfo.
- Tosca = Floria Tosca | Opera of the singer Tosca, who kills the police chief Scarpia then leaps to her death after her lover's execution.
- Madame Butterfly = butterfly -> Madama Butterfly | Opera of Cio-Cio-San, a Nagasaki geisha abandoned by the American officer Pinkerton; ends in her suicide.
- Manon Lescaut | Early opera after Prévost's novel; the heroine Manon dies in a Louisiana desert after her lover Des Grieux's ruin.
- The Girl of the Golden West | Opera set in a California Gold Rush mining camp; the sheriff Rance loses saloon-keeper Minnie to the outlaw Ramerrez.
Cavalleria Rusticana | Mascagni's one-act verismo opera of Sicilian village honor and a fatal duel; frequently double-billed with I Pagliacci.
I Pagliacci | Leoncavallo's verismo opera in which the clown Canio murders his unfaithful wife Nedda onstage during a performance.
- "Vesti la giubba" | Canio's aria, sung while the clown weeps donning his costume before performing despite his heartbreak.
Andrea Chenier -> Andrea Chénier | Giordano's opera set in the French Revolution; the poet Andrea Chénier and Maddalena choose the guillotine together.
La Gioconda | Ponchielli's opera of a Venetian street singer's self-sacrifice for her rival in love; its ballet "Dance of the Hours" is widely excerpted.
La Wally | Catalani's opera of a stubborn Tyrolean woman whose farewell aria "Ebben? Ne andrò lontana" precedes a fatal avalanche.

# French Opera (19th Century)
> French opera balances Grand Opera spectacle with lighter lyric and opéra comique traditions.

Giacomo Meyerbeer | Grand Opera composer of Les Huguenots; built spectacle from massed chorus, ballet, and historical pageantry.
- Les Huguenots | Five-act Grand Opera set amid the St. Bartholomew's Day Massacre of French Protestants.
Georges Bizet | Composer of Carmen, his only lasting success; died months after its lukewarm premiere.
- Carmen | Opera of the Seville cigarette girl who leaves Don José for Escamillo; Habanera, Toreador Song.
- The Pearl Fishers | Earlier opera set among Ceylonese pearl divers; friends Zurga and Nadir both love the priestess Leïla, "Au fond du temple saint."
Jules Massenet | Lyric composer of Manon and Werther, alongside the frequently cited Thaïs; known for sensuous orchestration.
- Thais -> Thaïs | Opera of a courtesan converted to piety by the monk Athanaël, who instead falls in love with her; contains the orchestral "Méditation."
Jacques Offenbach | Composer of French operetta and opéra bouffe; his sole true opera adapted E.T.A. Hoffmann's fantastic tales.
- The Tales of Hoffmann | The poet Hoffmann recounts three failed loves involving a mechanical doll, a courtesan, and a consumptive singer.
Faust | Gounod's opera after Goethe; the scholar Faust sells his soul to Méphistophélès to seduce Marguerite, who is ultimately redeemed.
Romeo and Juliet | Gounod's other enduring opera, after Shakespeare; adds a final duet in which the lovers briefly wake before dying.

# German & Austro-Hungarian Opera (Wagner and Successors)
> German-language opera runs from Mozart's successors through Wagner's music dramas to 20th-century atonality.

Richard Wagner | Composer-theorist of Tristan und Isolde, Parsifal, and the Ring cycle; pioneered leitmotif and continuous "music drama."
- Tristan und Isolde = Tristan and Isolde | Opera of an adulterous love potion and its unresolved "Tristan chord," a landmark of chromatic harmony.
- Parsifal | Final opera; the "pure fool" Parsifal redeems the Grail knights and heals the wounded king Amfortas.
- Lohengrin | Opera of a mysterious knight who rescues Elsa but must leave when she asks his forbidden name; source of the Bridal Chorus.
- Das Rheingold | Opening opera of the Ring cycle; the dwarf Alberich forges a world-dominating ring after renouncing love.
- Siegfried | Third Ring opera; the fearless hero reforges his father's sword, slays the dragon Fafner, and wakes the sleeping Brünnhilde.
- Bayreuth | Bavarian town where Wagner built his own festival theater, designed for the Ring cycle's premiere and still hosting his works.
Richard Strauss = Richard | Late-Romantic composer of Salome and Elektra's dissonant intensity, followed by the lyrical Der Rosenkavalier.
- Salome | Opera in which the princess demands John the Baptist's head after her Dance of the Seven Veils.
- Der Rosenkavalier | Comedy of the aging Marschallin, who gives up her young lover Octavian to the younger Sophie.
- Ariadne auf Naxos | Opera-within-an-opera in which a serious mythological drama and a comic troupe are forced to perform simultaneously.
Fidelio | Beethoven's only opera; Leonore disguises herself as a man to free her imprisoned husband Florestan from a political jail.
Die Fledermaus | Johann Strauss II's Viennese operetta of a masked-ball revenge prank and mistaken identities.
Alban Berg | Second Viennese School composer whose atonal, expressionist operas set socially critical texts.
- Wozzeck | Atonal opera in which an abused soldier murders his lover Marie, then drowns retrieving the knife afterward.
soldiers | Common link: Zimmermann's nearly unstageable Die Soldaten and the disguised soldiers testing their fiancées in Così fan tutte.
The Threepenny Opera | Weill and Brecht's satire of the criminal Mackie Messer ("Mack the Knife"); blends jazz-inflected songs with Marxist critique.
Vienna | Austrian capital central to German-language opera: premiere city of Mozart's and Beethoven's operas and home to Strauss II's operettas.

# Russian & Eastern European Opera
> A smaller but growing cluster of national schools east of the German tradition.

Modest Mussorgsky | Russian nationalist composer whose stark, folk-inflected harmony shaped his sole completed opera on a guilt-haunted tsar.
- Boris Godunov | Russian historical opera on the guilt-haunted tsar following the death of the child heir Dmitri.
Bedrich Smetana -> Bedřich Smetana | Czech nationalist composer often called the father of Czech opera; best known for the comedy The Bartered Bride.
Rusalka | Dvořák's opera of a water nymph who trades her voice for legs to love a mortal prince; her aria is the "Song to the Moon."
The Love for Three Oranges | Prokofiev's satirical fairy-tale opera in which a melancholic prince must recover three enchanted oranges, each holding a princess.
Hungary | Country common link: Bartók's symbolist Duke Bluebeard's Castle and Ligeti's absurdist Le Grand Macabre anchor its questions.

# English-Language & American Opera
> Victorian operetta through 20th-century minimalism, unified by English-language libretti.

Arthur Sullivan | Victorian composer, with librettist W. S. Gilbert, of comic operettas built on witty wordplay and tuneful patter songs.
- The Pirates of Penzance; or, The Slave of Duty | Comedy of Frederic, apprenticed to a band of tender-hearted pirates until his 21st birthday, misdated by a leap-year technicality.
- Major-General Stanley | Character whose rapid-fire "modern major-general" song catalogues his encyclopedic, impractical learning.
Porgy and Bess | Gershwin's opera set in Catfish Row, Charleston; the disabled Porgy and Bess's romance amid Sportin' Life's temptations; "Summertime."
Benjamin Britten | 20th-century British composer of Peter Grimes and Billy Budd; explored outsiders through orchestral sea interludes.
- Peter Grimes | Opera of an outcast Suffolk fisherman blamed for his apprentices' deaths; framed by the orchestral Sea Interludes.
Amahl and the Night Visitors | Menotti's opera, written for television, in which a disabled boy and his mother host the three Magi en route to Bethlehem.
Philip Glass | Minimalist composer of Einstein on the Beach; built from repetitive structures in collaboration with director Robert Wilson.
- Einstein on the Beach | Four-and-a-half-hour opera with Robert Wilson; abstract imagery and repetition replace conventional plot and dialogue.
John Adams = John Coolidge Adams | Minimalist composer after Glass; Nixon in China and Doctor Atomic dramatize recent history in a documentary style.
- Doctor Atomic | Opera on physicist J. Robert Oppenheimer and the tense final hours before the first atomic bomb test at Trinity.
- Nixon in China | Opera dramatizing President Nixon's 1972 diplomatic visit, mixing ceremony with the leaders' interior monologues.
Thomas Ades -> Thomas Adès | Contemporary British composer known for dense orchestration in operas including Powder Her Face and The Tempest.

# Singers, Arias & Vocal Terms
> Cutting across composers and works: the voice types, techniques, and performers that define opera singing.

castrato | Castrated male singer retaining a high voice; dominated Baroque and Classical opera seria roles, including many of Handel's, until 19th-century decline.
soprano | Highest standard female voice type, typically cast as opera's heroines and love interests, from Mozart's Countess to Puccini's Mimì.
tenor | Standard high male voice type, typically cast as opera's romantic leads, from Rodolfo in La bohème to the Duke in Rigoletto.
coloratura soprano | Soprano subtype specializing in rapid runs, trills, and high extension, as required by the Queen of the Night and Lucia's Mad Scene.
patter song | Comic aria delivered in rapid-fire syllables, a bel canto device of Rossini's later perfected by Gilbert and Sullivan's operettas.
sleepwalking | Plot device in which a guilt-stricken character reveals hidden crimes while asleep, as in Verdi's Macbeth and Bellini's La sonnambula.
Maria Callas | Mid-20th-century soprano known for dramatic intensity in bel canto revival roles such as Bellini's Norma.
