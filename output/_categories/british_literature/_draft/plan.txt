# Old English & Anglo-Saxon Literature
> Alliterative verse and prose in the earliest form of English, running from Caedmon through the Norman Conquest of 1066.

# Middle English Literature
> Vernacular poetry and prose written after 1066 under Norman and continental influence, culminating in Chaucer's generation.

# Elizabethan & Jacobean Literature
> Poetry and drama of Shakespeare's era and the reigns of Elizabeth I and James I, from sonnet sequences to revenge tragedy.

# Metaphysical & Civil War-Era Poetry
> Seventeenth-century poets known for wit and conceits, alongside Milton and the literature surrounding the English Civil War.

# Restoration & Augustan Literature
> Comedy of manners, satire, and the rise of the novel from the 1660 Restoration through the mid-eighteenth century.

# Romanticism
> Poets and novelists of imagination, nature, and revolutionary feeling, writing from the 1780s through the 1830s.

# Victorian Literature
> The novel's golden age and the poetry of an industrializing empire, spanning Dickens and the Brontes to the fin de siecle.

# Modernism & the World Wars
> Experimental fiction and poetry answering fragmentation, war, and new psychology in the early twentieth century.

# Mid-20th-Century Fiction, Poetry & Theatre
> Postwar writing spanning dystopian satire, the Movement, Absurdist and kitchen-sink drama, and Catholic novelists.

# Contemporary & Postcolonial Literature
> Living and recent authors addressing empire's aftermath, migration, and memory from the late twentieth century onward.

# Common Themes & Cross-Category References
> Recurring motifs, generic terms, and non-British figures or works that surface as comparanda in British literature questions.
