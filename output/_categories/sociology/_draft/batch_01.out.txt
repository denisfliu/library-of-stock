Erving Goffman	Symbolic Interactionism & Micro-Sociology	Canadian-American sociologist of dramaturgical theory; wrote The Presentation of Self in Everyday Life and Stigma.
Emile Durkheim	Classical Sociology	French founder of sociology; wrote The Division of Labor in Society and Suicide; coined anomie and social facts.
Pierre Bourdieu	Social Capital & Networks	French sociologist of habitus and cultural, social, and symbolic capital; wrote Distinction on taste and class.
social networks	Social Capital & Networks	Ties linking individuals or groups, studied via network analysis, weak-tie theory, and social capital research.
Talcott Parsons	Structural Functionalism & Middle-Range Theory	American sociologist who imported Weber to the US; wrote The Structure of Social Action; developed structural functionalism.
cities	Urban Sociology & the Chicago School	Central subject of urban sociology, from the Chicago School's ecological models to Jane Jacobs's street-level critiques.
Georg Simmel	Classical Sociology	German formal sociologist of social interaction and money; wrote "The Metropolis and Mental Life" and The Philosophy of Money.
C. Wright Mills	Conflict, Power & Critical Theory	American sociologist who wrote The Power Elite and The Sociological Imagination, critiquing concentrated elite power.
intersectionality	Race, Class & Gender	Concept coined by Kimberle Crenshaw describing overlapping systems of race, gender, and class oppression.
Jane Jacobs	Urban Sociology & the Chicago School	Urbanist who wrote The Death and Life of Great American Cities, championing sidewalks and mixed-use neighborhoods.
Robert King Merton	Structural Functionalism & Middle-Range Theory	American sociologist who coined "self-fulfilling prophecy," Strain Theory, and middle-range theory.
crime	Urban Sociology & the Chicago School	Studied via Strain Theory, Broken Windows theory, and Chicago School ecological approaches to deviance.
Chicago	Urban Sociology & the Chicago School	Home of the Chicago School of urban sociology, centered at the University of Chicago's sociology department.
gender	Race, Class & Gender	Social category theorized by Judith Butler as performative; central to feminist sociology and studies of masculinity.
social capital	Social Capital & Networks	Resources embedded in social networks, theorized by Bourdieu and popularized by Robert Putnam's Bowling Alone.
weak ties	Social Capital & Networks	Mark Granovetter's concept that loose acquaintances, not close ties, best transmit new information like job leads.
capital	Social Capital & Networks	In Bourdieu's framework, includes economic, cultural, social, and symbolic forms convertible into social advantage.
deviance	Urban Sociology & the Chicago School	Rule-breaking behavior explained by Strain Theory, labeling theory, and Chicago School ecological approaches.
Karl Marx	Classical Sociology	German philosopher-economist (b. 1818) of historical materialism and class conflict; co-wrote The Communist Manifesto with Engels.
Strain Theory	Structural Functionalism & Middle-Range Theory	Robert Merton's theory that deviance arises from a gap between culturally valued goals and legitimate means.
symbolic interactionism	Symbolic Interactionism & Micro-Sociology	Micro-sociological tradition, rooted in George Herbert Mead, holding that meaning is created through social interaction.
Thorstein Veblen	Classical Sociology	American economist-sociologist who coined "conspicuous consumption" in The Theory of the Leisure Class.
W. E. B. Du Bois	Race, Class & Gender	Pioneering Black sociologist who wrote The Souls of Black Folk and The Philadelphia Negro; coined "double consciousness."
Broken Windows theory	Urban Sociology & the Chicago School	Wilson and Kelling's theory that visible disorder like broken windows invites further crime and decay.
David Harvey	Urban Sociology & the Chicago School	Marxist geographer of the urban process, spatial fixes, and "the right to the city."
Jurgen Habermas	Conflict, Power & Critical Theory	German Frankfurt School theorist (b. 1929) of the public sphere and communicative action.
Marcel Mauss	Classical Sociology	French sociologist and Durkheim's nephew; wrote The Gift, analyzing reciprocity and exchange in archaic societies.
anomie	Classical Sociology	Durkheim's concept of normlessness arising from breakdown of social regulation, linked to his study of suicide.
Anthony Giddens	Culture, Media & Modernity	British sociologist of structuration theory and "runaway world" globalization; advised Tony Blair's Third Way.
Auguste Comte	Classical Sociology	French founder who coined the term "sociology" and proposed positivism and the law of three stages.
habitus	Social Capital & Networks	Bourdieu's concept of internalized dispositions shaping perception and behavior, shaped by one's social position.
masculinity	Race, Class & Gender	Gendered identity studied through concepts like Raewyn Connell's "hegemonic masculinity."
Patricia Hill Collins	Race, Class & Gender	Black feminist sociologist who wrote Black Feminist Thought, developing the matrix of domination framework.
poverty	Race, Class & Gender	Studied through structural inequality, William Julius Wilson's urban poverty research, and welfare-state analysis.
suicide	Classical Sociology	Subject of Durkheim's landmark study distinguishing egoistic, altruistic, and anomic types by social integration.
Alexis de Tocqueville	Classical Sociology	French thinker who wrote Democracy in America, analyzing civic associations and the tyranny of the majority.
Barbara Ehrenreich	Race, Class & Gender	American writer-sociologist who wrote Nickel and Dimed, an undercover account of low-wage work in America.
bell hooks	Race, Class & Gender	Black feminist scholar (Gloria Watkins) who wrote Ain't I a Woman, theorizing intersecting race, class, and gender oppression.
crowds	Classical Sociology	Studied in classical collective-behavior theory examining how individuals act differently in mass gatherings.
democracy	Classical Sociology	Analyzed by Tocqueville's Democracy in America as sustained by civic associations and equality of conditions.
division of labor	Classical Sociology	Subject of Durkheim's The Division of Labor in Society, contrasting mechanical and organic solidarity.
George Herbert Mead	Symbolic Interactionism & Micro-Sociology	American philosopher-sociologist who founded symbolic interactionism, theorizing the social self through "I" and "me."
Harriet Martineau	Classical Sociology	British writer often called sociology's first female founder; translated Comte and wrote Society in America.
Henri Lefebvre	Urban Sociology & the Chicago School	French Marxist theorist of The Production of Space and "the right to the city."
Immanuel Wallerstein	Nations, States & Global Systems	American sociologist who developed world-systems theory, dividing the global economy into core, periphery, and semi-periphery.
marriage	Race, Class & Gender	Studied as a social institution shaped by gender roles, class, and changing family structures.
Norbert Elias	Culture, Media & Modernity	German sociologist who wrote The Civilizing Process, tracing long-term shifts in manners and self-restraint.
race	Race, Class & Gender	Social category central to Du Bois's, Wilson's, and intersectionality scholars' analyses of inequality.
religion	Classical Sociology	Analyzed by Durkheim's Elementary Forms of Religious Life and Weber's Protestant Ethic and the Spirit of Capitalism.
sidewalks	Urban Sociology & the Chicago School	Focus of Jane Jacobs's argument that pedestrian street life produces urban safety and community.
the power elite	Conflict, Power & Critical Theory	C. Wright Mills's concept of a unified elite spanning corporate, political, and military leadership.
violence	Conflict, Power & Critical Theory	Analyzed through Weber's monopoly of legitimate violence and Bourdieu's concept of symbolic violence.
Arlie Russell Hochschild	Race, Class & Gender	American sociologist who coined "emotional labor" in The Managed Heart, studying feeling in service work.
bureaucracy	Classical Sociology	Weber's model of rational-legal authority, characterized by hierarchy, rules, and impersonal administration.
Charles Wright Mills	Conflict, Power & Critical Theory	Same sociologist as C. Wright Mills, author of The Power Elite and White Collar.
city	Urban Sociology & the Chicago School	Object of study for the Chicago School's ecological models and later critical urban theorists like Harvey and Lefebvre.
culture	Culture, Media & Modernity	Shared beliefs, symbols, and practices studied across sociology, from Bourdieu's cultural capital to cultural studies.
ethnography	Symbolic Interactionism & Micro-Sociology	Qualitative fieldwork method exemplified by Goffman's asylum studies and Laud Humphreys's Tearoom Trade.
Harold Garfinkel	Symbolic Interactionism & Micro-Sociology	American sociologist who founded ethnomethodology, studying how people produce shared social order through everyday practices.
hegemony	Conflict, Power & Critical Theory	Antonio Gramsci's concept of ruling-class dominance maintained through cultural consent rather than force alone.
immigration	Urban Sociology & the Chicago School	Studied by the Chicago School through assimilation and ethnic-enclave models in American cities.
Italy	Nations, States & Global Systems	National context for studies of civil society and social capital, as in Putnam's Making Democracy Work.
Judith Butler	Race, Class & Gender	Philosopher-theorist of gender performativity, arguing gender is constituted through repeated acts, as in Gender Trouble.
Lewis Mumford	Urban Sociology & the Chicago School	American urbanist and historian who wrote The City in History, critiquing sprawling suburban development.
middle-range theory	Structural Functionalism & Middle-Range Theory	Robert Merton's approach bridging abstract grand theory and narrow empirical description.
Middletown	Urban Sociology & the Chicago School	Robert and Helen Lynd's landmark community study of Muncie, Indiana, as a typical American town.
nations	Nations, States & Global Systems	Political communities analyzed through Benedict Anderson's "imagined communities" and theories of nationalism.
Robert K. Merton	Structural Functionalism & Middle-Range Theory	Same sociologist as Robert King Merton, known for Strain Theory and middle-range theory.
Robert Putnam	Social Capital & Networks	American political scientist who wrote Bowling Alone, arguing American social capital and civic engagement declined.
roles	Symbolic Interactionism & Micro-Sociology	Expected behaviors attached to social positions, central to Goffman's dramaturgical theory and role theory.

social facts	Classical Sociology	Durkheim's term for external, coercive social phenomena like law and morality that constrain individual behavior.
structural functionalism	Structural Functionalism & Middle-Range Theory	Parsons-led paradigm analyzing society as an interconnected system of institutions serving functional needs.

suburbs	Urban Sociology & the Chicago School	Residential form critiqued by Lewis Mumford and studied as a counterpoint to Jane Jacobs's dense cities.
symbolic	Symbolic Interactionism & Micro-Sociology	Common-link term spanning symbolic interactionism, Bourdieu's symbolic capital, and symbolic violence.

television	Culture, Media & Modernity	Mass medium analyzed in studies of media effects, consumer culture, and McLuhan's media theory.
