# Classical Sociology
> The discipline's 19th- and early-20th-century founders and the core concepts they coined to explain modern social order.

# Symbolic Interactionism & Micro-Sociology
> Micro-level approaches treating everyday interaction, role-play, and shared meaning as the basic unit of social life.

# Structural Functionalism & Middle-Range Theory
> Mid-20th-century American theory explaining social institutions by the functions they perform for the wider system.

# Conflict, Power & Critical Theory
> Approaches centered on power, domination, and ideology, spanning Marxist-inflected conflict theory, the Frankfurt School, and Foucault.

# Urban Sociology & the Chicago School
> The study of cities, neighborhoods, and urban life, anchored by the Chicago School's ethnographic tradition and its later critics.

# Race, Class & Gender
> Scholarship on racial, economic, and gender inequality, including foundational Black sociology and second- and third-wave feminist theory.

# Social Capital & Networks
> Theories of social ties, trust, and the convertible resources embedded in networks and communities.

# Nations, States & Global Systems
> Macro-historical sociology of nation-states, nationalism, revolutions, and the modern global economy.

# Culture, Media & Modernity
> Theories of mass culture, media, and the changing character of the self and society under modern conditions.
