methane	Climate & Paleoclimate	Potent greenhouse gas released from wetlands, livestock, and permafrost thaw, far stronger than CO2 over short timescales.
Milankovitch cycles	Climate & Paleoclimate	Periodic variations in Earth's orbit and axial tilt that influence long-term climate patterns and glacial cycles.

oceanic currents	Hydrology & Oceanography	Large-scale movements of seawater driven by wind, density differences, and Earth's rotation.
orthoclase	Mineralogy & Petrology	Potassium-rich feldspar mineral common in granite and a member of the Bowen's reaction series.
outer core	Plate Tectonics & Earth's Interior	Liquid iron-nickel layer of Earth's interior whose convective flow generates the planet's magnetic field.

pillow lava	Volcanism	Rounded, bulbous lava formations produced by underwater volcanic eruptions.

pyroclastic flow	Volcanism	Fast-moving, superheated current of gas and volcanic matter, one of the deadliest volcanic hazards.
pyroxenes	Mineralogy & Petrology	Group of dark, mafic silicate minerals common in basalt and other igneous rocks.
radiative forcing	Climate & Paleoclimate	Measure of the change in energy balance of Earth's atmosphere caused by factors like greenhouse gases.

remote sensing	Scientists & Foundational Concepts	Technique of gathering data about Earth's surface using satellite or airborne sensors rather than direct contact.
river deltas	Hydrology & Oceanography	Landforms formed where a river deposits sediment as it enters a slower-moving body of water.

sandstone	Mineralogy & Petrology	Sedimentary rock composed of cemented sand-sized mineral grains, primarily quartz.
sea surface temperature	Hydrology & Oceanography	Temperature of the ocean's upper layer, a key indicator for phenomena like El Nino and hurricane formation.
sediment	Geomorphology & Surface Processes	Solid material transported and deposited by water, wind, or ice, the raw material of sedimentary rock.

seismic wave velocity	Plate Tectonics & Earth's Interior	Speed at which seismic waves travel through Earth's layers, used to map internal structure.
seismic waves	Plate Tectonics & Earth's Interior	Vibrations generated by earthquakes or explosions that travel through Earth, including P, S, and surface waves.
soils	Geomorphology & Surface Processes	Weathered surface layers of mineral and organic material supporting plant life, classified by horizons.
stromatolites	Geologic Time & Historical Geology	Layered structures built by ancient microbial mats, among the oldest fossil evidence of life on Earth.

sulfur	Mineralogy & Petrology	Yellow, non-metallic element found near volcanic vents and hot springs, forming its own native mineral.

tectonic faults	Plate Tectonics & Earth's Interior	Fractures in rock along which displacement has occurred, sites of earthquake generation.

tornado	Atmospheric Science & Meteorology	Violently rotating column of air extending from a thunderstorm to the ground.
tropical cyclones	Atmospheric Science & Meteorology	Large rotating storm systems fueled by warm ocean water, called hurricanes or typhoons regionally.
uniformitarianism	Scientists & Foundational Concepts	Principle that geologic processes observed today have operated similarly throughout Earth's history.
vadose zone	Hydrology & Oceanography	Unsaturated subsurface zone between the ground surface and the water table.

volcanoes	Volcanism	Openings in Earth's crust through which magma, gas, and ash erupt, building characteristic landforms.
