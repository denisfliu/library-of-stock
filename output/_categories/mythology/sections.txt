# Greek and Roman Mythology
> The largest and most heavily tested cluster: the Olympian pantheon, the Trojan War cycle, the labors of Heracles, and the founding legends of Rome.
Heracles | Greek hero of the Twelve Labors ordered by Eurystheus; son of Zeus and Alcmene; wielded a club and lion pelt.
Apollo | Greek god of prophecy, music, and archery; twin of Artemis; associated with Delphi and the lyre.
Artemis | Greek virgin goddess of the hunt and the moon; twin of Apollo; punished Actaeon for seeing her bathe.
Theseus | Athenian hero who slew the Minotaur in the Labyrinth with help from Ariadne's thread.
Achilles | Greek hero of the Trojan War, invulnerable but for his heel; slew Hector to avenge Patroclus.
Aphrodite | Greek goddess of love and beauty, born from sea foam; wife of Hephaestus, lover of Ares.
Cadmus | Founder of Thebes who slew a dragon and sowed its teeth, producing the Spartoi warriors.
Demeter | Greek goddess of the harvest whose grief over Persephone's abduction by Hades causes winter.
Dionysus | Greek god of wine and ecstatic ritual; twice-born son of Zeus; associated with the Bacchae and maenads.
Poseidon | Greek god of the sea and earthquakes, wielder of the trident; rival of Athena for patronage of Athens.
Orpheus | Legendary musician who descended to the underworld to retrieve Eurydice but lost her by looking back.
Aeneas | Trojan hero who fled burning Troy and founded the line leading to Rome, per Virgil's Aeneid.
Hermes | Greek messenger god, guide of souls, and patron of thieves and travelers; born on Mount Cyllene.
Cybele | Phrygian mother goddess adopted into Greco-Roman cult, associated with lions and the priest Attis.
Janus | Roman two-faced god of beginnings, doorways, and transitions; namesake of January.
Pan | Greek goat-legged god of shepherds and wild places, inventor of the pan-pipes; pursued the nymph Syrinx.
Perseus | Greek hero who beheaded Medusa and rescued Andromeda from a sea monster.
Asclepius | Greek god of medicine bearing a snake-entwined staff; resurrected the dead and was killed by Zeus for it.
Circe | Sorceress who transformed Odysseus's men into swine on her island Aeaea.
Odysseus | Cunning Greek hero of the Odyssey whose ten-year journey home included Circe, Polyphemus, and the Sirens.
Zeus | King of the Greek gods, wielder of the thunderbolt, notorious for shapeshifting affairs.
Athena | Greek goddess of wisdom and war born fully armed from Zeus's head; patron of Athens.
Hecate | Greek goddess of magic, crossroads, and the night, often depicted with three forms or torches.
Pelops | Grandson of Zeus who won Hippodamia in a chariot race after his father Tantalus served him to the gods.
Persephone | Greek goddess abducted by Hades to the underworld, whose return with the seasons explains spring.
Amazons = the Amazons | Legendary tribe of warrior women fought by Heracles and Theseus, led by Penthesilea and Hippolyta.
Thebes | City founded by Cadmus, site of the Oedipus myth and the Seven Against Thebes.
Diomedes | Greek hero of the Trojan War who wounded Aphrodite and Ares in battle.
Hephaestus | Greek lame smith god of fire and craft; husband of Aphrodite, forger of divine weapons.

Crete | Island home of King Minos, the Labyrinth, and the Minotaur.
Hesiod | Greek poet whose Theogony and Works and Days systematized the gods' genealogy and the ages of man.
Medea | Colchian sorceress who helped Jason win the Golden Fleece, later killing her own children in revenge.

Ares | Greek god of war, son of Zeus and Hera, lover of Aphrodite, generally disliked among the gods.
Delphi | Site of Apollo's oracle where the Pythia delivered prophecies from a sacred chasm.
