The Misc Science unit's recorded canon is extremely small, consisting only of statistical methods that cut across the standard science subcategories rather than belonging to any one of them. ANOVA, the test comparing means across multiple groups, is the more frequent of the two answerlines, followed by maximum likelihood estimation, a general-purpose technique for fitting statistical models to data.

Both topics reflect the unit's role as a home for quantitative and methodological questions that arise across biology, physics, chemistry, and the social sciences alike, rather than a coherent subject area of its own.
