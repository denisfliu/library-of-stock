[B1] 2019 ACF Regionals (2019) | Diff: 8 | Fine Arts
     Leadin: Answer the following about the peripatetic German painter Moritz Rugendas, for 10 points each.
  Part 1 (ans: Brazil [or Federative Republic of Brazil; or Republica Federativa do Brasil; accept Picturesque Voyage to Brazil or Voyage Pittoresque dans le Bresil]):
    Rugendas's depiction of a so-called Virgin Forest in this modern-day country's province of Rio de Janeiro became the archetypal European image of a tropical forest.
  Part 2 (ans: picturesque [or pittoresque; accept Picturesque Voyage to Brazil]):
    With the help of Victor Aime Huber ("ay-MAY yoo-BAIR"), Rugendas published that image in a set of landscapes titled for this type of voyage to Brazil. William Gilpin popularized this term in the English art world of the late 18th century.
  Part 3 (ans: Frederic Edwin Church):
    Rugendas was inspired by the travels of Alexander Humboldt, as was this American member of the Hudson River School, who depicted a landscape traversed by Humboldt in his painting Heart of the Andes.

[B2] 2015 Penn Bowl (2015) | Diff: 8 | Fine Arts
     Leadin: For 10 points each, answer these questions about exoticism in Western art:
  Part 1 (ans: Paul Gauguin):
    This artist of The Yellow Christ and Where Do We Come From? What Are We? Where Are We Going? is well known for his vibrant, simplistic depictions of life in Tahiti and Martinique.
  Part 2 (ans: Frederic Edwin Church):
    This member of the Hudson River School is known for his grand, dramatic landscape art. He painted multiple views of the volcano Cotopaxi (koh-tuh-PAK-see), including an 1862 version dominated by red and pink.
  Part 3 (ans: The Heart of the Andes):
    This large 1859 painting shows a sweeping mountain landscape and a waterfall feeding into a pool. Over 12,000 people paid 25 cents to attend its first exhibition, where it was framed with curtains like a window.
