Canadian author, poet, and critic. Best known for The Handmaid's Tale (Offred in the Republic of Gilead) and its sequel The Testaments. Also wrote Alias Grace, The Blind Assassin, Oryx and Crake (and the MaddAddam trilogy), The Edible Woman, Cat's Eye, The Robber Bride, The Penelopiad, and poetry including The Circle Game and '[you fit into me]'. Critic who wrote Survival (about Canadian literature) and Payback.
The Handmaid's Tale

Atwood's most famous novel, set in the totalitarian Republic of Gilead (established by the Sons of Jacob after overthrowing the US government). Women are sorted into categories: Econowives, Marthas, Aunts, and Handmaids. The protagonist Offred ('of Fred') is a Handmaid forced to bear children for the Commander and his wife, former televangelist Serena Joy. Offred plays a forbidden game of Scrabble with the Commander, visits Moira at Jezebel's (a nightclub for businessmen), and begins a sexual relationship with the chauffeur Nick (arranged by Serena Joy to increase chances of pregnancy). Unwomen are exiled to the Colonies to clean toxic waste. Offred witnesses a Particicution. She escapes in a black van that Nick claims belongs to the resistance. The novel refers to forcibly relocated black people as 'Children of Ham.' It ends with notes by Professor James Pieixoto about Offred's tapes. Won the Governor General's Award in 1985.

The Testaments

The 2019 sequel to The Handmaid's Tale, narrated by Agnes, Nicole (Baby Nicole), and Aunt Lydia (revealed to be a former schoolteacher and family court judge). Agnes Jemima becomes a Supplicant to escape marriage after Tabitha's death. She attends Rubies Premarital Preparatory with Shunammite and the daughter of the dentist who molested her, Mr. Grove. Agnes and Nicole escape to Canada aboard the 'Nellie J. Banks' disguised as Pearl Girls. Becka dies in a cistern to help save Nicole. Daisy learns she is Baby Nicole on her sixteenth birthday. Aunt Lydia describes her work in the Mayday resistance, orchestrating a plot to smuggle damaging information about Gilead to Canada. A car bomb is detonated outside a thrift store. The novel depicts the fall of Gilead. Also ends with notes by Professor James Pieixoto.

Alias Grace

A novel fictionalizing the real-life case of Grace Marks, who was convicted of the murders of Thomas Kinnear and Nancy Montgomery. The doctor Simon Jordan is hired to interview Grace in hopes she will be proven hysteric. Grace claims to have been possessed by Mary Whitney (who died after an abortion). The novel was based on an anecdote told by Susanna Moodie, whose diaries also inspired Atwood's poetry collection The Journals of Susanna Moodie. A peddler disguises himself as a 'neuro-hypnotist' and gives Grace a button.

Oryx and Crake / MaddAddam Trilogy

A dystopian/speculative fiction trilogy: Oryx and Crake (2003), The Year of the Flood (2009), and MaddAddam (2013). In Oryx and Crake, the Snowman (previously Jimmy) is the last original human in a world devastated by a deliberate pandemic. His friend Crake slits the throat of Oryx in an airlock, and Jimmy shoots Crake. Both title characters take their names from the online game Extinctathon (led by the mysterious 'MaddAddam'). Jimmy watched his mother being executed in prison. The world features genetically engineered hybrid animals like rakunks. Jimmy's lab partner hacks Uncle Pete's computer to watch porn and play Extinctathon. Atwood refused the label of science fiction, since the book has no 'talking squids in outer space.' MaddAddam (narrated by Toby and Zeb) is the final novel.

The Penelopiad

A novella giving voice to Penelope from the Odyssey. Twelve hanged maids interrupt Penelope's narrative using forms like a court trial and a jump rope rhyme. It kicked off the Canongate Myth Series. Atwood questioned whether calling this work feminist was appropriate in a 2018 op-ed 'Am I a Bad Feminist.'

Poetry

Atwood is also a poet. Her collection The Circle Game won the 1966 Governor General's Award. Its title poem describes 'children on the lawn / joined hand to hand / go round and round' and ends with 'I want the circle / broken.' The short poem '[you fit into me]' reads: 'You fit into me / like a hook into an eye / a fish hook / an open eye.' 'Backdrop addresses cowboy' compares cowboys to 'innocent as a bathtub / full of bullets.' 'Marrying the hangman' is a notable poem. 'Helen of Troy Does Countertop Dancing' imagines Helen as a stripper who declares 'This is a torch song. Touch me and you'll burn.' 'Spelling' observes 'A child is not a poem / a poem is not a child.' 'Happy Endings' features passages A through F answering 'what happens next' after John and Mary meet. The Journals of Susanna Moodie was inspired by the 19th-century writer's diaries.

Other Works and General

Atwood also wrote Hag-Seed (reworking of The Tempest for the Hogarth Shakespeare project), 'The Female Body' (essay about the title object as a plastic doll), Payback: Debt and the Shadow Side of Wealth (nonfiction about debt), Survival (a controversial history of Canadian literature in which she failed to read any native poetry, inspired by Northrop Frye's 'garrison mentality'). She won the Booker Prize for The Blind Assassin in 2000 and the Giller Prize. She also wrote 'Bluebeard's Egg' (about Sally and her 'heart man' husband Ed). She invented the LongPen. Atwood co-shared the 2000 Booker Prize and wrote about Macbeth in a novel (Hag-Seed). The Heart Goes Last (2015) features Charmaine and Stan in the Consilience experiment.

Summary of Facts

Margaret Atwood (Margaret Eleanor Atwood) is a Canadian author, poet, and critic, one of the most frequently clued literary figures in quizbowl. She is identified by her Canadian nationality and association with The Handmaid's Tale in virtually every tossup.

The Handmaid's Tale is her most clued work, set in the totalitarian Republic of Gilead where women are categorized as Econowives, Marthas, Aunts, and Handmaids. Offred ('of Fred') is forced to bear children for the Commander, whose wife, former televangelist Serena Joy, arranges for Offred to sleep with the chauffeur Nick. Key clues include the forbidden Scrabble games with the Commander, visiting Moira at Jezebel's, the Particicution, Unwomen exiled to the Colonies, and Offred's escape in a black van. The novel ends with notes by Professor James Pieixoto.

The Testaments (2019 sequel) is narrated by Agnes, Nicole, and Aunt Lydia. Agnes attends Rubies Premarital Preparatory and escapes to Canada with Nicole aboard the 'Nellie J. Banks' disguised as Pearl Girls. Aunt Lydia orchestrates the Mayday resistance. Becka dies in a cistern. The novel ends with the fall of Gilead.

Alias Grace fictionalizes Grace Marks's murders of Thomas Kinnear and Nancy Montgomery, with Simon Jordan investigating. Grace claims possession by Mary Whitney. The Edible Woman follows Marian MacAlpin, who stops eating and bakes a woman-shaped cake. Oryx and Crake introduces Jimmy/Snowman as the last human after Crake's deliberate pandemic, with rakunks and the game Extinctathon. The Blind Assassin (Booker Prize) features Iris and Laura Chase and a nested science fiction narrative about blind assassins on Zycron. Cat's Eye follows painter Elaine Risley. The Robber Bride centers on Tony, Roz, and Charis versus the mysterious Zenia. The Penelopiad gives voice to Penelope's twelve hanged maids.

Atwood's poetry includes The Circle Game (Governor General's Award), '[you fit into me],' 'Helen of Troy Does Countertop Dancing,' and 'Happy Endings.' She wrote the critical work Survival (inspired by Northrop Frye's 'garrison mentality'), Hag-Seed (a Tempest reworking), and Payback (about debt).
