Orpheus in the Underworld (Orphée aux enfers)

Offenbach's comic operetta (1858), a satirical parody of Gluck's opera Orpheus and Eurydice. In this version, Eurydice actually hates Orpheus and runs away to elope with Jupiter instead. Public Opinion is a mezzo-soprano role who forces Orpheus to rescue Eurydice. The Act II finale 'Infernal Galop' (Galop Infernal) became famous as the can-can tune, danced by the Greek gods in the underworld. Jupiter appears in the 'Fly Duet' ('Duo de la mouche') disguised as a fly. Libretto by Ludovic Halévy (Fromental Halévy's nephew). The US Marines' Hymn is based on the 'Gendarmes' Duet' from Genevieve de Brabant. Wikipedia

The Tales of Hoffmann (Les contes d'Hoffmann)

Offenbach's final opera, left unfinished at his death and completed by Ernest Guiraud. Based on three tales by E.T.A. Hoffmann. The opera has three acts named after the protagonist's three loves: Olympia, Antonia, and Giulietta, plus a Prologue and Epilogue set in Luther's Tavern. Hoffmann recounts his loves while drinking with his friend Nicklausse. Giulietta steals Hoffmann's reflection. Dr. Miracle causes Antonia to sing herself to death. A 1951 Powell and Pressburger film adaptation starred Robert Rounseville and Moira Shearer. Wikipedia

General / Biographical

Jacques Offenbach (1819–1880), German-born French composer. His operettas and operas were hugely popular in Second Empire France. Many title roles were created by soprano Hortense Schneider. Manuel Rosenthal arranged his pieces for the ballet Gaîté Parisienne. He also wrote a Grand Concerto for cello nicknamed 'Concerto Militaire.' Other operettas include La Vie Parisienne, La Périchole, La Grande-Duchesse de Gerolstein (where Fritz receives a sabre), and La Belle Hélène. Josephine Baker starred in a 1934 revival of his opera La Créole. Karl Kraus frequently performed his operetta about a spoiled title character who makes Fritz a general on a whim. Franz von Suppé modeled his operettas on Offenbach's style.

Summary of Facts

Jacques Offenbach (1819–1880) was a German-born French composer who became the dominant figure of Second Empire French operetta. He is best known for Orpheus in the Underworld (Orphée aux enfers, 1858) and his final opera The Tales of Hoffmann (Les contes d'Hoffmann), left unfinished at his death and completed by Ernest Guiraud.

Orpheus in the Underworld is a satirical operetta that parodies Gluck's Orpheus and Eurydice. In this version, Eurydice hates Orpheus and runs away to elope with Jupiter instead; she is eventually lost when Jupiter's thunderbolt shocks Orpheus into looking back at her. The mezzo-soprano role Public Opinion forces Orpheus to attempt the rescue. Jupiter appears in the 'Fly Duet' ('Duo de la mouche'), disguised as a fly and making only buzzing noises while courting Eurydice. The libretto was written by Ludovic Halévy, whose uncle Fromental Halévy wrote the opera The Jewess; the Offenbach-Halévy partnership was long and productive. The Act II 'Infernal Galop' (Galop Infernal) became famous as the definitive can-can tune, with the Greek gods dancing the can-can in the underworld. Saint-Saëns parodied it by slowing it down enormously for the 'Tortoises' movement of Carnival of the Animals. The US Marines' Hymn is based on the 'Gendarmes' Duet' from another Offenbach operetta, Geneviève de Brabant.

The Tales of Hoffmann has a Prologue and Epilogue set in Luther's Tavern, where Hoffmann recounts his three great loves. Act 1 (Olympia) is based on E.T.A. Hoffmann's 'The Sandman': Hoffmann falls in love with Spalanzani's mechanical doll Olympia, who sings the coloratura 'Doll Song' ('Les oiseaux dans la charmille' — 'The birds in the arbor'), periodically slowing to a halt and needing to be re-wound. Act 2 (Antonia) features Dr. Miracle causing Antonia's death by encouraging her to sing. Act 3 (Giulietta) is set in Venice: Giulietta seduces Hoffmann to steal his reflection. The act features the famous Venetian barcarolle 'Belle nuit, o nuit d'amour,' sung by Giulietta and Nicklausse. A 1951 film adaptation by Powell and Pressburger (their last major film) starred Robert Rounseville and Moira Shearer.

Offenbach's other works include La Vie Parisienne, La Périchole, and La Grande-Duchesse de Gerolstein (in which the title character gives Fritz a sabre). Josephine Baker starred in a 1934 revival of his opera La Créole. Manuel Rosenthal arranged various Offenbach pieces for the ballet Gaîté Parisienne (the overture drawn from La Vie Parisienne). Many of Offenbach's title roles were created by soprano Hortense Schneider. The overture to The Tales of Hoffmann is normally performed in an arrangement made by Carl Bier for the Vienna premiere.
