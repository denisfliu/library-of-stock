General / Biographical

Cindy Sherman (born Cynthia Morris Sherman, 1954) is an American conceptual photographer associated with the Pictures Generation. Chuck Close called her 'perhaps the most important artist' of the 1980s. She is a MacArthur Fellow. She had a brief relationship with Robert Longo, who included her in his Men in the Cities series. She graduated from Buffalo State College. She created the Bus Riders series and recently posted self-portrait work on Instagram using Facetune. A 2016 Broad Museum exhibition titled 'The Imitation of Life' caused controversy when photos of Sherman in blackface surfaced. Suzy Lake is cited as a mutual influence; Sherman invited Lake to a 1975 exhibition at Hallwalls. Wikipedia

Summary of Facts

Cindy Sherman (born Cynthia Morris Sherman, 1954) is an American conceptual photographer associated with the Pictures Generation. Chuck Close called her 'perhaps the most important artist of the 1980s,' and she is a MacArthur Fellow.

Her best-known work is the Untitled Film Stills (1977–1980) — 69 black-and-white self-portraits in which Sherman poses as unnamed women in stereotypical female roles evocative of 1950s–60s Hollywood B-movies. Characters bear nicknames like 'The Hitchhiker' (Sherman next to a suitcase on an Arizona roadside) and 'City Girl' (Untitled #21, looking out at skyscrapers). All images are untitled. The photo Untitled #96 — Sherman lying on a kitchen floor in orange, clutching a crumpled newspaper ad — was for a time the second most expensive photograph ever sold at auction (before Andreas Gursky's Rhine II). Laura Mulvey's 1991 essay 'A Phantasmagoria of the Female Body' in New Left Review helped popularize the series. James Franco controversially attempted to reshoot the series with himself.

Sherman's Centerfolds (1981) subverted the magazine centerfold format with color photographs of women in distressed or pensive poses. The Sex Pictures (1992) used medical dolls, prosthetic limbs, and mannequin parts arranged in sexually explicit positions to critique pornography — created partly in the context of debates over NEA funding of Mapplethorpe and Andres Serrano. The History Portraits series imitated Old Master paintings through costume and prosthetics, including a reworking of Raphael's Fornarina (No. 205) and an imitation of Caravaggio's Young Sick Bacchus with fake grapes. The Clowns series showed Sherman in clown outfits against digitally manipulated backgrounds, with multiple clowns superimposed.

Sherman graduated from Buffalo State College and had a brief relationship with Robert Longo, who included her in his Men in the Cities series. In 2016, a Broad Museum exhibition titled 'The Imitation of Life' caused controversy when photographs of Sherman in blackface surfaced. Her recent work includes Instagram self-portraits manipulated with Facetune, created for W magazine. Suzy Lake is cited as a mutual influence; Sherman invited Lake to a 1975 exhibition at Hallwalls.
