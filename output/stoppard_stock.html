Arcadia

Thomasina says 'Mountains are not pyramids and trees are not cones' and 'pooh to Hobbes.' She devises an equation describing a rabbit eating its own babies. She dies in a fire on her 17th birthday. Hannah Jarvis discovers the 'hermit of Sidley Park' was Septimus. Lord Byron is mentioned but never appears. A tortoise named Plautus/Lightning is on the table. Ezra Chater is a terrible poet who died of a monkey bite. The play ends with two characters waltzing.

Leopoldstadt

Stoppard's 2020 play spanning five acts from 1899 to 1955, depicting the Merz family in Vienna's Jewish quarter. Ludwig Jakobovicz plays cat's cradle with Leo Rosenbaum and Nathan Fischbein. Rosa responds 'Auschwitz' to a list of names at the end. Based on Stoppard's own grandparents, who were Holocaust victims. Leo changes his name to Leo Chamberlain and forgets his Jewish past.

Summary of Facts

Tom Stoppard (born 1937 as Tomas Straussler) is a Czech-born British playwright and screenwriter known for intellectually playful, structurally inventive drama.

Rosencrantz and Guildenstern Are Dead follows two minor Hamlet characters who open the play observing a coin land on heads 92 times consecutively. They encounter The Player and his troupe and discuss death as 'the absence of presence.' The film adaptation stars Tim Roth and Gary Oldman.

Arcadia alternates between 1809 and the present at Sidley Park. Septimus Hodge tutors the precocious Thomasina Coverly, who says 'pooh to Hobbes' and devises equations about entropy. Hannah Jarvis discovers the 'hermit of Sidley Park' was Septimus. Ezra Chater is a terrible poet who died of a monkey bite. A tortoise named Plautus or Lightning sits on the table.

Travesties is set in 1917 Zurich where Henry Carr encounters James Joyce, Vladimir Lenin, and Tristan Tzara, twisting the plot of The Importance of Being Earnest. Leopoldstadt spans 1899-1955 depicting a Viennese Jewish family, ending with Rosa responding 'Auschwitz' to a list of names.

Stoppard is also a prolific screenwriter of Shakespeare in Love, Brazil, Indiana Jones and the Last Crusade, and Empire of the Sun. His other plays include The Real Thing, The Invention of Love, Jumpers, The Real Inspector Hound, and The Coast of Utopia trilogy.
