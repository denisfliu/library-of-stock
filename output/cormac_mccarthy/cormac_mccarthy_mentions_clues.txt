     Leadin: This Nobel Prize-winning poet described the eyes of the title animal as "astonished without knowledge" in "The Alpaca." For 10 points each:
  Part 1 (ans: Gabriela Mistral [or Lucila Godoy Alcayaga]):
    Name this Latin American poet, who penned such works as Tala and Sonnets of Death. She opines about the spiritual nature of art in her "Decalogue of the Artist," found in the collection Desolacion.
  Part 2 (ans: Chile):
    Gabriela Mistral was a poet from this nation. Another writer from this country, Roberto Bolano, wrote a lengthy novel about the search efforts undertaken to find Benno von Archimboldi in 2666.
  Part 3 (ans: Mexico):
    Bolano's novels 2666 and The Savage Detectives are set in this Latin American nation. Cormac McCarthy's Border Trilogy often features Americans crossing into this nation on horseback.
