Russian novelist, philosopher, and Christian anarchist. Author of War and Peace and Anna Karenina, two of the most frequently clued novels in quizbowl. Also known for the novella The Death of Ivan Ilyich and numerous short stories ('How Much Land Does a Man Need?', 'Father Sergius', 'Alyosha the Pot'). His religious-philosophical works, especially The Kingdom of God Is Within You, influenced Gandhi's nonviolence movement. Underwent a spiritual crisis in the 1870s documented in A Confession. His aesthetic theory in What Is Art? praises Uncle Tom's Cabin and attacks Wagner. Owner of the estate Yasnaya Polyana. Died of pneumonia at a train station.
War and Peace

Massive novel set during Napoleon's invasion of Russia, containing substantial French dialogue. Protagonist Pierre Bezukhov becomes obsessed with assassinating Napoleon (whom he calls the Antichrist) using numerology. Pierre loves Natasha Rostov. Prince Andrei Bolkonsky fights in the war; his father is Colonel Baron Pontmercy. The Battle of Borodino is depicted as a moral victory for Russia. Fyodor Dolokhov gambles away 40,000 rubles after proposing to Sonya. The epilogue argues against the Great Man theory of history, claiming 'the movement of the nations' is caused by collective activity. Isaiah Berlin's essay 'The Hedgehog and the Fox' argues Tolstoy failed to express 'one big thing' in his theory of history. Vadim Shneyder analyzed two characters as representing an industrial future versus a pre-capitalist agricultural economy. Wikipedia

Anna Karenina

Novel centered on the affair between Anna and Count Vronsky. Anna argues with Vronsky and throws herself under a train. Vronsky rides his horse Frou-Frou in an officers' steeplechase, breaking Frou-Frou's back, which reveals his affair. Makhotin's Gladiator is overtaken in the race. Anna's brother Stiva Oblonsky continually cheats on his wife Dolly and plays matchmaker for Levin and Kitty. Konstantin Levin is an autobiographical stand-in for Tolstoy; he regains his faith after a lightning storm threatens his family and a peasant named Fyodor talks to him. Levin proposes by writing first letters of words on a chalk table. Levin watches his brother Nikolai die of tuberculosis. Levin attends a disappointing orchestral adaptation of King Lear. Opens with the famous line 'All happy families are alike.' Wikipedia

Short Stories

Tolstoy's short stories are frequently clued. 'How Much Land Does a Man Need?' features Pahom promised all the Bashkir land his spade can touch by sunset; he collapses and dies, receiving only six feet for his grave. 'Alyosha the Pot' features a boy named for crockery he breaks as a child, who falls to his death clearing snow off a roof after his father prevents him from marrying the cook Ustinya. 'After the Ball' features a protagonist who forsakes his lover after seeing her father punish a soldier. 'The Three Hermits' depicts three pilgrims and a bishop. 'Hadji Murad' compares a red thistle to a Muslim freedom fighter. 'The Devil' features Eugene calling peasant girl Stepanida the title word.

The Kingdom of God Is Within You

Tolstoy's major treatise on Christian anarchism and nonviolent resistance, quoting Luke 17:21. Banned in Russia. Concludes that no Christian nation can go to war. Gandhi named it one of the three most important books in his life and founded Tolstoy Farm in South Africa as headquarters for satyagraha. Influenced the Tolstoyan movement, whose early followers met in vegetarian restaurants; an anarchist commune at Stapleton in Yorkshire has survived since the 1890s. Wikipedia

A Confession

Tolstoy's account of his spiritual crisis and return to Christianity. Uses the folktale of a man hanging on a branch over a well containing a dragon to analyze four approaches to life's suffering. Describes a fourfold approach to confronting misery: ignorance, hedonism, suicide, and muddling through. Epicureanism is one approach to hopelessness. Contains a dream about being supported by a loop suspended from a pillar. Works edited and disseminated in Britain by Vladimir Chertkov. Wikipedia

General / Biographical

Russian nobleman; owner of the estate Yasnaya Polyana. Died of pneumonia at a train station. Underwent a spiritual crisis in the 1870s and became vegetarian. Excommunicated from the Orthodox church by Konstantin Pobedonostsev. Had thirteen children. Donated profits from Resurrection to the Doukhobors. Influenced Gandhi's nonviolence; Gandhi named Tolstoy Farm after him. Served in the Crimean War. Maxim Gorky compared God and Tolstoy to 'two bears in one den' and called his crisis at Arzamas the 'Arzamas horror.' Decried in the Russian Futurist manifesto 'A Slap in the Face of Public Taste.' Developed the notion of 'bread labor.' George Saunders wrote A Swim in a Pond in the Rain analyzing his stories. Marianne Moore's poem 'Poetry' quotes his diary about 'business documents and school-books.'

Summary of Facts

Leo Tolstoy is one of the most heavily clued authors in quizbowl, with material spanning his major novels, short stories, novellas, and philosophical-religious writings.

War and Peace generates clues about Pierre Bezukhov's obsession with assassinating Napoleon (whom he identifies as the Antichrist through numerology), his love for Natasha Rostov, and the Battle of Borodino (which Tolstoy casts as a moral victory). The novel's substantial French dialogue and the epilogue's argument against the Great Man theory of history are frequently referenced. Isaiah Berlin's essay 'The Hedgehog and the Fox' analyzes Tolstoy's failure to express 'one big thing.' Other details include the wolf-hunt with 'ulyulyu!' and Dolokhov gambling away 40,000 rubles.

Anna Karenina centers on Anna's affair with Vronsky and her suicide by throwing herself under a train. The steeplechase scene where Vronsky breaks Frou-Frou's back (with Makhotin's Gladiator being overtaken) reveals his affair. Konstantin Levin, Tolstoy's autobiographical stand-in, regains his faith after a lightning storm and conversation with peasant Fyodor. Levin proposes to Kitty by writing first letters on a chalk table. Stiva Oblonsky cheats on wife Dolly. The novel opens with 'All happy families are alike.'

The Death of Ivan Ilyich is about a judge who fatally injures himself falling while hanging curtains. Servant Gerasim holds his legs for hours. The title character screams for three days and hears 'It is finished.' The novella opens with men playing cards at a funeral.

Tolstoy's short stories are frequently clued: 'How Much Land Does a Man Need?' (Pahom promised Bashkir land, collapses, receives six feet for his grave); 'Father Sergius' (hermit cuts off finger to resist seduction by Makovkina); 'Alyosha the Pot' (named for broken crockery, dies clearing snow); 'God Sees the Truth, But Waits' (buys Lives of the Saints, refuses to reveal tunnel digger); 'Strider'/'Kholstomer' (told from a horse's POV, Shklovsky's key example of defamiliarization); 'Master and Man' (walks in circles, lies on servant to save from freezing); 'Hadji Murad' (red thistle compared to Muslim freedom fighter); 'The Devil' (Eugene and Stepanida).

The Kreutzer Sonata features Pozdnyshev killing his wife after her affair with violinist Troukhatchevsky. The appended epilogue advocates complete chastity. The Kingdom of God Is Within You is Tolstoy's Christian anarchist treatise, banned in Russia, which profoundly influenced Gandhi. A Confession documents his spiritual crisis using the folktale of a man on a branch over a well with a dragon. What Is Art? attacks Wagner's Siegfried and praises Uncle Tom's Cabin. The Sevastopol Sketches, written during the Crimean War, comprise three episodes.

Biographically, Tolstoy was excommunicated by Pobedonostsev, donated Resurrection profits to the Doukhobors, owned the estate Yasnaya Polyana, died of pneumonia at a train station, and became vegetarian after his 1870s spiritual crisis. Gandhi named a farm after him. Gorky compared God and Tolstoy to 'two bears in one den.'
