Summary of Facts

Andrea Mantegna was an Italian Renaissance painter, court artist to the Gonzaga family in Mantua, and brother-in-law of Giovanni Bellini. He is most commonly identified by The Dead Christ (extreme foreshortening) and the Camera degli Sposi.

The Lamentation over the Dead Christ shows an extremely foreshortened body on a marble slab with prominent nail wounds on the feet, with Mary, St. John, and Mary Magdalene weeping at the left. It is housed in Milan's Brera Gallery.

The Camera degli Sposi is a frescoed room in the Ducal Palace of Mantua. Its ceiling uses the di sotto in su technique depicting an oculus opening to a blue sky surrounded by putti, a peacock, and a turbaned man. The Court Scene shows patron Ludovico Gonzaga and his family, including a dwarf.

The Triumphs of Caesar is a nine-panel series depicting Caesar's victory procession with SPQR banners, slaves, and spoils, now at Hampton Court Palace. His three versions of St. Sebastian (Vienna, Venice, Louvre) show the saint tied to a column pierced by arrows, one with a rider/Death figure in clouds.

The San Zeno Altarpiece is a triptych with four Corinthian columns dividing scenes connected by garlands, with the Crucifixion as the central predella and pseudo-Kufic script on Mary's halo.

Other important works include Parnassus (Mars and Venus on a natural arch, for Isabella d'Este's studiolo), Triumph of the Virtues, St. James Led to His Execution (destroyed in 1944 bombing of Ovetari Chapel), engravings like Battle of the Sea Gods, Adoration of the Magi, Judith and Holofernes, and Agony in the Garden.
