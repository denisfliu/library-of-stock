Summary of Facts

Lucian Freud (1922–2011) was a German-born British figurative painter and grandson of Sigmund Freud. He is the defining artist of the School of London, working in thick impasto to create unsparing nude portraits. His most celebrated work, Benefits Supervisor Sleeping (1995), depicts Job Centre supervisor Sue Tilley — 'Big Sue,' weighing 127 kg — lying naked on a broken-down couch; it sold for $33.6 million in 2008. Girl with a White Dog (1951–52) shows his first wife Kitty Garman (eldest daughter of sculptor Jacob Epstein) with one breast escaping a yellow robe and a bull-terrier resting on her legs. Girl with a Kitten shows Kitty staring into the distance while apparently strangling a tabby cat; a 1949 portrait of her emphasizes wide eyes in front of peeling green shutters. After Cezanne was painted on an unusual hexagonal canvas and riffs on Cezanne's Afternoon in Naples. His early painting The Painter's Room contains a zebra head — an actual stinky zebra head he kept in his room while living with artist John Craxton. He also painted The Painter Surprised by a Naked Admirer. Freud was a close friend and rival of Francis Bacon, who painted Three Studies of Lucian Freud (sold for a record $142 million in 2013). In 2002 Freud painted a nude portrait of pregnant Kate Moss, and also tattooed two small swallows on her back. Martin Gayford's memoir Man with a Blue Scarf recounts sitting for a portrait by Freud. He spent 4,000 hours painting his mother, and his London flat interiors include Interior at Paddington and Large Interior, W11. Freud married Kitty Garman and later Lady Caroline Blackwood.
