Summary of Facts

Jacopo Pontormo was an Italian Mannerist painter from Florence. His most famous work is the Deposition from the Cross (c. 1528) in the Capponi Chapel (designed by Brunelleschi), depicting a crowd of entangled figures in soft blue and pink cloaks surrounding Christ's serpentine body. A crouching curly-haired figure in the foreground supports himself on tiptoe. The chapel also contains his Annunciation wall fresco with Mary in a jubilant contrapposto pose.

His Visitation includes two maids huddling around Elizabeth and Mary, with a composition that copies Dürer's Four Witches. Bill Viola recreated it as a video piece. The Vertumnus and Pomona lunette at the Medici villa at Poggio a Caiano shows both figures as rustic peasants with a sickle and fruit basket.

Pontormo's adopted son Bronzino served as court painter to Cosimo de' Medici. According to Vasari, Pontormo was tremendously afraid of death and avoided large gatherings, eventually working himself to death on a Last Judgement. His Joseph in Egypt series shows a child and father ascending a spiral staircase. His Portrait of a Halberdier is claimed to depict young Cosimo de' Medici.
