T1 2020 FLopen (2020) Diff 9 · Fine Arts
This movement’s practitioner Gerald Laing created a portrait in which a subject’s facial features are highlighted by a black circle. An artist from this movement included her motif of a blossoming rose in the painting It’s a Man’s World and showed a women’s bare bottom underneath a proscenium arch in her painting Bum. Pauline Boty belonged to this movement, which also produced an image of a hand holding a gun pointed toward a woman posing above a (*) warplane. A Lawrence Alloway essay coined the name for this movement, which included former Independent Group members Billy Apple and Eduardo Paolozzi. A work from this movement features a marquee advertising The Jazz Singer and a bodybuilder in a living room holding a sucker. For 10 points, Richard Hamilton was a British practitioner of what movement that derived images from advertising and mass culture?
ANSWER: British PopArt [prompt on Independent Group until mentioned] (The Laing work is Brigitte Bardot, and the work featuring the gun and the warplane is Eduardo Paolozzi’s I Was a Rich Man’s Plaything.)

T1 2025 Spec Script (2025) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
This thinker usually ate pork pies while watching movies in the front row, according to the text of Eduardo Paolozzi’s painting series As Is When depicting this thinker “admiring” Betty Hutton and Betty Grable. Ethan Coen’s senior thesis at Princeton was on this thinker. In a 1993 film, this thinker illustrates why he can’t “understand” lions by sketching a “very pleasant pineapple.” In Terry Eagleton’s script for that film, this thinker is asked “how many toes do philosophers have?” by a green Martian and refuses to admit that there “isn’t a (*) rhinoceros in [a] room.” This thinker loved Tom Mix westerns and watched a movie nearly every day, according to memoirs by Norman Malcolm. Black backdrops are used throughout a Derek Jarman film that fictionalizes this queer thinker’s tryst with the lover of his patron John Maynard Keynes. For 10 points, name this thinker who often sprinted to the cinema after lecturing at Cambridge, where he had studied under Bertrand Russell.
ANSWER: Ludwig Wittgenstein [or Ludwig Josef Johann Wittgenstein]

T3 2019 RULFO (2019) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
Manet’s The Balcony inspired a painting from this movement that depicts a woman with pink socks standing on a table while a girl in a pin-filled dress sits in the foreground. In another painting from this movement, a robot holds an unconscious woman in a yellow dress on the outside of a “fun house” designed by John Voelcker. Phrases like “If this be sin” and “Woman of the streets” appear in a work from this movement in which part of its name appears in a cloud of smoke fired from a gun. At the lecture (*) Bunk! , Eduardo Paolozzi presented a series of early collages from this movement such as I was a Rich Man’s Plaything. In another collage from this movement, a picture of the earth makes up the ceiling of a house in which a nude woman sits with a lampshade on her head and a muscleman holds an over-sized lollipop. For 10 points, name this movement whose exhibition This is Tomorrow displayed works by Richard Hamilton.
ANSWER: British Pop Art (accept Independent Group )
T4 2018 Scattergories (2018) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
A term coined by the architect James Wines puns on this word. The coinage of a term including this word is variously attributed to John McHale and Lawrence Alloway. In an artwork which also includes a picture of a warplane with the words “Keep ‘Em Flying!”, this word appears in a cloud of smoke underneath the words “Intimate Confessions.” That artwork is I Was a Rich Man’s Plaything by (*) Eduardo Paolozzi. The letter L is added to this word in a derisive term for unattractive public art. This word appears on an object held by a bodybuilder in Richard Hamilton’s Just What Is It That Makes Today’s Homes So Different, So Appealing? For 10 point, name this word which refers to an art movement exemplified by Andy Warhol’s Campbell’s Soup Cans.
ANSWER: pop
T5 2018 Delta Burke (2018) Diff 6 · Fine Arts
The origin of this art movement is often traced to a collective of artists including Alison and Peter Smithson and Eduardo Paolozzi who called themselves the Independent Group. One of the first works of this movement, which includes a nude woman wearing a lampshade as well as a bodybuilder, was created by Richard Hamilton. The artist who used Benday dots to create works like Whaam! and a painting featuring the word balloon, “I’d Rather Sink Than Call Brad for Help” is part of this movement. For 10 points, Roy Lichtenstein was part of what art movement along with the man who painted Campbell Soup Cans on canvas, Andy Warhol?
ANSWER: Pop Art
T6 2017 Jordaens Visual Arts (2017) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
Along with a can of lemon juice, this figure appears in front of a woman in a gold swimsuit in Eduardo Paolozzi’s Real Gold. A black box shaped like this figure was used as an exhibition space for a collection of found object in a “museum” displayed with the Ray Gun Wing by Claes Oldenburg. In 2012, Damien Hirst created a painting of this figure composed of a series of perfect circles. A painting titled for this figure appears alongside a poster reading “See that baldheaded guy over there? That’s Curly Grogan. He and his mob run half the rackets in this town!” in a depiction of the (*) artist’s studio. In that painting, this character holds a fishing rod and smirks as another character leans over a wharf and yells “I’ve caught a big one!” For 10 points, name this character who is told to “look” in the title of that painting by Roy Lichtenstein, who first appeared in Steamboat Willie and features in the “Sorcerer’s Apprentice” section of Fantasia.
ANSWER: Mickey Mouse
T7 2013 Michigan Fall Tournament (2013) Diff 7 · Fine Arts
One work of art in this movement includes two people in a yellow and blue silhouette and uses those two colors in an oversize rendering of an American flag atop a skyscraper. That artist of As Is When touched off this movement with his lecture “Bunk” and created I Was a Rich Man’s Plaything. Eduardo Paolozzi’s later works are associated with this movement, one member of which used the This Is Tomorrow exhibition to display a piece that includes a Romance poster and a nude bodybuilder holding a tootsie pop. Another member of this movement showed a woman’s face surrounded by waves as she proclaims that she’d rather sink than call Brad for help. The best known member of this movement created the Marilyn Diptych and painted a lot of Campbell’s soup cans. For 10 points, name this movement which included Richard Hamilton, Roy Lichtenstein, and Andy Warhol.
ANSWER: pop art

B2 2023 Halloween Hippopotamus Invitational (2023) Diff 7 · Fine Arts
Art. What is it good for? The point of art has always been contentious, and never more so as technology and culture developed. For 10 points each,
[10] Name this art movement that emerged in the late 1950s, which was spearheaded by the likes of Eduardo Paolozzi and Richard Hamilton. It often uses kitschy cultural references in ironic ways.
ANSWER: Pop art
[10] Name this pop art artist known mostly for his paintings of desserts, which are akin to still-lifes for the consumerist age.
ANSWER: Wayne Thiebaud (“tee-bo”)
[10] Thiebaud didn’t only paint desserts. Name this painting of his that depicts a man in a business suit dangling not far off the ground from a tree branch with his briefcase thrown on the grass below and a city skyline in the background. This painting is meant to represent the need to escape urban life.
ANSWER: Man in Tree
