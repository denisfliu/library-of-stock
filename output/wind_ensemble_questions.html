Questions: Wind Ensemble

6 tossups · 3 bonuses

Tossups

T1 2024 ACF Nationals (2024) Diff 9 · Fine Arts
An unaccompanied horn solo opens a 1993 piece in this genre based on hymns and Bach chorales that reminded the composer of Abraham Lincoln. Three movements inspired by the Odyssey, such as “Immortal thread, so weak,” make up a piece in this genre titled Wine-Dark Sea by John Mackey. Give Us This Day is a “short” piece in this genre by David Maslanka. A fugue marked “Rather broad” is the third movement of a B-flat-major piece in this genre by Paul Hindemith. It’s not a requiem, but an optional chorus appears in the martial third movement “Apotheosis” of a piece in this genre written for the tenth anniversary of the July Revolution. A “grande” piece in this genre subtitled “funèbre et triomphale” (“few-NEB ray tree-awn-FALL”) was written by Hector Berlioz. For 10 points, name this genre of large-scale music for an orchestra without strings.
ANSWER: symphony for concert band [or symphony for wind ensemble; or symphony for wind band; prompt on band or wind ensemble by asking “what kind of piece?”; prompt on symphony or short symphony by asking “for what type of ensemble?”; reject “symphony for brass band”]
T2 2023 NASAT (2023) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
For one of these ensembles, the Leeds Music Corporation commissioned Darius Milhaud's Suite Française. William Schuman's George Washington Bridge and Chester Overture were written for this ensemble. The time signatures 1.5/4 and 2.5/4 appear in the "Lord Melbourne" movement of a piece for this ensemble that opens with a "Lisbon" movement. Frederick Fennell founded one of these ensembles at the Eastman School of Music that performs pieces by John Barnes Chance and Frank Ticheli. Colonial Song and Irish Tune are commonly played pieces for this ensemble by the composer of Lincolnshire Posy, Percy Grainger. The March from Hindemith's (HIN-duh-mithz) Symphonic Metamorphosis is often adapted for these ensembles by swapping violin parts for flutes and clarinets. For 10 points, name these ensembles consisting of woodwinds, brass, and percussion.
ANSWER: concert band [or wind ensemble; or wind band; or symphonic band; or military band; reject "big band"]
T3 2022 DECAF (2022) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
Note to players: composer and type of piece required. An ascending call of F, B-flat, E-flat, long F opens the third movement of one of these works that also contains “Scherzo” and “Nocturne” movements. In the first movement of one of these pieces, the last note of the first theme is sustained over a walk down a major scale in parallel triads, transitioning to a soloist playing the second theme starting (+) dotted F, short A, C, C, B-flat, G, F. Two ascending major walks from F to C open one of these pieces, while another opens with the notes short E-flat, F, short high C, B-flat, short G, short E-flat, F, low B-flat in the low (*) brass, a progression that repeats throughout that movement. One of these pieces calls for an anvil in its third movement “Song of the Blacksmith,” which precedes the “Fantasia on the Dargason.” For 10 points, name these pieces by a composer who taught at St. Paul’s Girls’ School that include a “second in F” and “first in E-flat.”
ANSWER: suites for military band by Gustav (Theodore von) Holst [accept equivalents for military band like wind band or concert band or symphonic band or wind ensemble or winds or brass band; prompt on Holst suites with “for what ensemble?”; prompt on Holst pieces for band with “in what genre?”] (The lead-in is Moorside Suite.)
T4 2021 NASAT (2021) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
A piece for this ensemble features a rhythmic Khoomar section and concludes with the movement Lorva Horovel. A Child's Garden of Dreams was composed by David Maslanka for this ensemble, for which Alfred Reed wrote his Armenian Dances. A piece for this ensemble calls for an anvil in its "Song of the Blacksmith" movement before its rapid "Fantasia on the Dargason." A chaconne opens Gustav Holst's first suite for this ensemble. This ensemble is scored for pieces like "The Thunderer" and "Semper Fidelis," which were composed by a leader of one of these ensembles named John Philip Sousa. "Stars and Stripes Forever" is commonly played by a military example of, for 10 points, what ensemble consisting of woodwinds, brass, and percussion?
ANSWER: concert band [or symphonic band; or military band; or marching band; or wind band; or wind ensemble]
T5 2017 NASAT (2017) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
This ensemble plays Johan de Meij's (YO-han de MAY's) "Lord of the Rings" Symphony. A six-movement piece for this ensemble includes movements called "Lord Melbourne" and "Rufford Park Poachers." Ralph Vaughan Williams quoted the folk songs "Dives and Lazarus" and "Seventeen Come Sunday" in the first movement of a suite for this ensemble. Percy Grainger composed Lincolnshire Posy for this ensemble. Gustav Holst originally composed his "Fantasia on the Dargason" for the second of his two suites for this ensemble, which are in E-flat and F. John Philip Sousa led this type of ensemble, for which he composed marches like "The Stars and Stripes Forever." For 10 points, name this type of large ensemble that generally does not have a string section.
ANSWER: concert band [or military band; or symphonic band; or marching band; or wind band; or wind ensemble]
T6 2017 Eisenhower Memorial Tournament (EMT) (2017) Diff 7 · Fine Arts
John Mackey’s Wine-Dark Sea is a piece for this instrumentation. Donald Hunsberger commissioned a piece in this genre that uses crotales, vibraphone, and chimes to create sounds that “hang in the air,” that piece is …and the mountains rising nowhere. This genre does not usually include singers, but one piece in this genre begins and ends with a soprano singing “I am an angel of light,” that piece is Angels in the Architecture by Frank Ticheli. Paul Hindemith’s only work for this instrumentation was his Symphony In B-Flat, and Georg Friedrich Handel wrote a suite originally in this genre to accompany (*) fireworks. One suite for this instrumentation is based on six folk tunes, including “Rufford Park Poacher.” This instrumentation usually consists of woodwind, brass, and percussion instruments. For 10 points, name this genre that includes Percy Grainger’s Molly on the Shore and Lincolnshire Posy.
ANSWER: wind ensemble [accept band, concert band, symphonic band, wind band, anti-prompt on “military band,” DO NOT accept or prompt on “marching band”]

Bonuses

B1 2025 ACF Nationals (2025) Diff 9 · Fine Arts
Answer the following about the English Folk Song Suite, for 10 points each.
[10] The suite is by this English composer of The Lark Ascending, whose nine symphonies include A Sea Symphony, A London Symphony, and Sinfonia Antartica.
ANSWER: Ralph Vaughan Williams [reject “Williams”]
[10] The English Folk Song Suite was originally written for this kind of ensemble. Another piece for this kind of ensemble ends with the movement “The Lost Lady Found.”
ANSWER: concert band [or military band; accept wind ensemble or wind orchestra or wind band; prompt on winds; reject “orchestra” or “brass band”] (The latter work is Percy Grainger’s Lincolnshire Posy.)
[10] The English Folk Song Suite was originally published by this British firm, whose general manager, Ernst Roth, was released from internment partly due to Vaughan Williams’s intervention. In the 20th century, this firm was the main publisher of Benjamin Britten’s operas and Stravinsky’s ballets.
ANSWER: Boosey & Hawkes Company

[10] As a composer of American patriotic music, Gould was heavily influenced by this “March King,” who titles a Gould album that opens with this composer’s march “The Stars and Stripes Forever.”

[10] Both Gould and Sousa wrote extensively for this ensemble, one of which Sousa spent decades directing. Conductors like Frederick Fennell gained fame for interpreting landmark works for this ensemble such as Lincolnshire Posy.
ANSWER: wind band [or military band or concert band or symphonic band or wind ensemble or wind symphony or winds]
