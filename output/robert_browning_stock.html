Summary of Facts

Robert Browning is a Victorian English poet, primarily identified through his dramatic monologues. His most frequently clued poem is "My Last Duchess," in which the Duke of Ferrara describes a painting by Fra Pandolf of his deceased wife, who had a heart "too soon made glad." The poem ends with the Duke showing the emissary a bronze statue of Neptune cast by Claus of Innsbruck. "Porphyria's Lover" features a speaker who strangles his beloved with her yellow hair and notes that God "has not said a word." Pippa Passes is a verse drama set in Asolo containing the famous line "God's in his heaven, all's right with the world," and was notably parodied in Nabokov's Pale Fire.

Browning's "Soliloquy of the Spanish Cloister" opens with "Gr-r-r" and features a monk plotting against Brother Lawrence who drinks orange juice in three sips to frustrate the Arian. "Rabbi Ben Ezra" begins "Grow old along with me! The best is yet to be" and responds to the Rubaiyat of Omar Khayyam. "Andrea del Sarto" contains the line "a man's reach should exceed his grasp."

The Ring and the Book is an epic based on a 1698 Roman murder trial. "Childe Roland to the Dark Tower Came" takes its title from King Lear and ends with the slug-horn being blown. Sordello is a notoriously difficult poem about a troubadour that prompted Ezra Pound to write "Hang it all, Robert Browning, there can be but one Sordello" in Canto II.

Browning was married to Elizabeth Barrett Browning and lived with her at the Casa Guidi in Italy. He inspired Ezra Pound and is the basis for Randolph Henry Ash in A.S. Byatt's Possession. His collection Men and Women contains many of his best-known monologues. Other notable poems include "The Lost Leader" (attacking Wordsworth), "The Bishop Orders His Tomb," "Two in the Campagna," "A Toccata of Galuppi's," and "Caliban Upon Setebos."
