Summary of Facts

Domenico Ghirlandaio was a Florentine Renaissance painter of the late 15th century, best known as the teacher of Michelangelo — a fact well-documented despite Ascanio Condivi's propaganda claiming Michelangelo was self-taught.

His most frequently clued work is the Portrait of an Old Man and His Grandson, which depicts a man with rhinophyma (a bulbous nose deformity) gazing at a young boy. The painting uses three-quarters perspective and the old man is possibly modeled on Francesco Sassetti. Its attribution was challenged by Adolfo Venturi, who credited it to Ghirlandaio's brother Davide.

Ghirlandaio painted extensive fresco cycles in the Tornabuoni Chapel in Santa Maria Novella, commissioned by Giovanni Tornabuoni, depicting 8 scenes from the life of Mary facing 8 scenes from the life of John the Baptist. These include The Nativity of Mary and the Birth of the Virgin (set in a 15th-century Florentine bedroom with a frieze of jocular putti). Some work on these frescoes may have been done by the then-13-year-old Michelangelo.

For the Sassetti Chapel, he painted frescoes about the life of St. Francis, including The Confirmation of Franciscan Rule (showing Lorenzo de' Medici emerging from a staircase) and inserted himself as a shepherd observing the Adoration. The chapel's altarpiece, the Adoration of the Shepherds, shows Jesus in front of a grey sarcophagus with visitors walking along a narrow cliffside trail. His Adoration of the Magi version contains a sarcophagus inscribed with a prophecy by the augur Fulvius.

His St. Jerome in His Study was painted for the Church of Ognissanti and shares similarities with van Eyck's version; it was commissioned alongside Botticelli's Saint Augustine in his Study. He also painted a Last Supper in the Ognissanti, The Vocation of the Apostles for the Sistine Chapel, and helped paint the Stories of Moses and Jesus on its walls.
