A Suitable Boy

Seth's massive novel (approximately 1,400-1,500 pages), set in the fictional Indian city of Brahmpur in the aftermath of Indian independence and partition. Rupa Mehra searches for a suitable husband for her daughter Lata, whose suitors include the Muslim cricketer Kabir, the Oxford-educated poet Amit (a Chatterji), and the shoe manufacturer Haresh. Lata ultimately marries Haresh; Kabir does not attend the wedding. The novel's table of contents consists of 19 rhyming couplets. Subplots include a Hindu-Muslim riot caused by building a Hindu temple near a mosque, the courtesan Saeeda Bai revealing that Tasneem is her daughter (causing Maan to stab Firoz), and the child math prodigy Bhaskar befriending Dr. Durrani. Contains instructions for shoemaking. The planned sequel, A Suitable Girl, was delayed after Seth's breakup with violinist Philippe Honore; Seth returned his advance to Penguin. Wikipedia

Set in San Francisco, it follows yuppie programmer/software engineer John Brown, who declares 'The Dow-Jones of my heart is depressed' and places (or has placed for him) a lonelyhearts classified ad. He meets the lawyer Liz Dorati through the ad. Other characters include the sculptor/drummer Janet Hayakawa (of the band Liquid Sheep) and Phil. Features an anti-nuclear protest at Lungless Labs and a subplot about a cat named Charlemagne being sent to 'Psycho-Kitty.' Associated with the New Narrative poetry movement. Wikipedia

Summary of Facts

Vikram Seth is an Indian author best known for two novels: A Suitable Boy and The Golden Gate. A Suitable Boy is a massive novel (approximately 1,400-1,500 pages) set in the fictional Indian city of Brahmpur in the aftermath of Indian independence and partition. Its central plot follows Rupa Mehra's search for a suitable husband for her daughter Lata. Lata's suitors include the Muslim cricketer Kabir (who plays Malvolio in a production of Twelfth Night), the Oxford-educated poet Amit Chatterji, and the shoe manufacturer Haresh. Lata ultimately marries Haresh, and Kabir does not attend the wedding. The novel's table of contents consists of 19 rhyming couplets summarizing each part. Subplots include a Hindu-Muslim riot caused by the decision to build a temple near a mosque, a violent scene at a Ramlila procession, the courtesan Saeeda Bai revealing Tasneem is her daughter (leading Maan to stab Firoz), and the child math prodigy Bhaskar befriending Dr. Durrani. The lapdog Cuddles enjoys biting characters, and the novel includes instructions for shoemaking. A character writes a wedding proposal through the acrostic 'A Modest Proposal.' The planned sequel, A Suitable Girl, was delayed by Seth's breakup with violinist Philippe Honore, and Seth returned his advance to Penguin.

The Golden Gate (1986) is a verse novel written entirely in 590 Onegin stanzas (the stanza form from Pushkin's Eugene Onegin) in iambic tetrameter. It follows San Francisco yuppies, centering on the programmer/software engineer John Brown, who declares 'The Dow-Jones of my heart is depressed.' His ex-girlfriend (or the drummer Janet Hayakawa of the band Liquid Sheep) places a classified ad for him, through which he meets the lawyer Liz Dorati. The novel features an anti-nuclear protest at Lungless Labs (where Phil and Liz attend Father O'Hare's demonstrations), and a subplot about the despised cat Charlemagne being sent to 'Psycho-Kitty' after urinating on a pillow. The novel is associated with the New Narrative poetry movement.

An Equal Music is about violinist Michael Holme, a member of the Maggiore Quartet, who rekindles his romance with Julia McNicholl, a professional pianist who is married and going deaf. They spot each other on opposite buses on Oxford Street in London. Michael had previously had an affair with Julia after playing Beethoven's Piano Sonata No. 5 with her. The novel was dedicated to Philippe Honore.

Seth's other works include Three Chinese Poets (translations including Du Fu and Wang Wei's 'The Deer Park'), Two Lives (nonfiction about his great-aunt Hennerle Gerda Caro), The Rivered Earth (inspired by purchasing poet George Herbert's home), and the poetry collection All You Who Sleep Tonight.
