Italian bel canto opera composer (1801–1835). Best known for Norma (1831) and La sonnambula (1831). Also composed I puritani (1835, his final opera) and I Capuleti e i Montecchi. A rival of Donizetti, he was beloved by Wagner for his long melodic lines. His frequent librettist was Felice Romani. He died at age 33.
Norma

Bellini's most famous opera (1831), set in Roman-occupied Gaul. The title character is a Druid priestess named Norma who has a secret affair with the Roman proconsul Pollione. Norma cuts mistletoe and sings 'Casta diva' in Act 1. The opera ends with Norma confessing her sins and both she and Pollione being burnt alive. The mezzo-soprano role Adalgisa is Pollione's other love interest, who eventually sides with Norma. The 'Guerra, guerra!' chorus urges the Celts to war against the Romans. Norma and Pollione also sing the duet 'In mia man alfin tu sei.' Wikipedia

La sonnambula

Bellini's 1831 opera about Amina, a Swiss village girl who sleepwalks. The village phantom turns out to be Amina sleepwalking across a rickety mill bridge. Her fiancé Elvino mistakenly thinks she is having an affair when she ends up in Count Rodolfo's room. In her famous Act III aria 'Ah! non credea mirarti,' she laments the withering of flowers while sleepwalking across the bridge — an oboe interjects. The first line of this aria is inscribed on Bellini's tomb. Lisa (the innkeeper) encourages Elvino's suspicions. A 2009 Met production controversially set the opera in a modern rehearsal hall. General / Biographical

Vincenzo Bellini (1801–1835), Italian bel canto opera composer. Died at age 33 — his tomb is inscribed with a line from La sonnambula. His frequent librettist was Felice Romani. He was a rival of Donizetti (Donizetti wrote 30 operas before having success, while Bellini found early success). Beloved by Wagner for his long melodic lines. His opera I Capuleti e i Montecchi is based on Romeo and Juliet. His early opera Il pirata featured Giovanni Rubini and was his first collaboration with Felice Romani. Maria Callas's signature Bellini roles include Norma and Imogene (from Il pirata). Joan Sutherland ('La Stupenda') was a prime 20th-century interpreter. The bel canto style exemplified by Bellini and Donizetti stressed precise execution of tempo, dynamic, and ornamentation.

Summary of Facts

Vincenzo Bellini (1801–1835) was an Italian bel canto opera composer who died at the age of 33. His tomb is inscribed with the first line of Amina's 'flower aria' from La sonnambula: 'Ah! non credea mirarti.' His frequent librettist was Felice Romani, and he was admired by Wagner for his long melodic lines. His primary rival was Gaetano Donizetti; together they exemplified the bel canto style, which stressed precise execution of tempo, dynamic, and ornamentation.

Bellini's most famous opera is Norma (1831), set in Roman-occupied Gaul. Norma is a Druid priestess who has secretly had children with the Roman proconsul Pollione. She raises mistletoe to the moon and sings the famous cavatina 'Casta diva' ('Chaste goddess') in Act 1 — an F major, 12/8 aria that begins with a sustained messa di voce on A4 for seven-and-a-half beats and concludes with a two-octave coloratura passage on 'ah, si.' The mezzo-soprano role Adalgisa is Pollione's other love interest; he urges her to come to Rome in the duet 'Vieni in Roma.' The chorus 'Guerra, guerra!' incites the Celts to war against the Romans. Norma and Pollione sing the duet 'In mia man alfin tu sei,' in which he offers to die rather than let her sacrifice their children. Norma ultimately confesses her sins, and both she and Pollione are burned alive. Norma also sings 'Ah! Bello a me ritorna' wishing for peace with Rome before 'Casta diva.'

La sonnambula (1831) concerns Amina, a Swiss village girl who sleepwalks. The village 'phantom' is revealed to be Amina walking in her sleep across a rickety mill bridge. Her fiancé Elvino (encouraged by the innkeeper Lisa) mistakenly believes Amina is having an affair with Count Rodolfo when she ends up in his room and takes back his engagement ring. In the climactic 'flower aria,' 'Ah! non credea mirarti / si presto estinto, o fiore,' Amina laments withered flowers while sleepwalking — an oboe interjects. This aria's first line is inscribed on Bellini's tomb. A 2009 Met production directed controversially placed the opera in a modern New York rehearsal hall, prompting boos from American audiences.

I puritani (1835) was Bellini's final opera, set during the English Civil War. It centers on Elvira's love for the Royalist lord Arturo. A 'March of the Pilgrims' appears. The opera was premiered by the famous 'Puritans' quartet: Lablache, Rubini, Tamburini, and Giulia Grisi. The libretto was by Carlo Pepoli — Bellini famously told him: 'Carve into your head in adamantine letters... people must weep, shudder, die through singing.' The tenor role Arturo sings to an extraordinary F5 in the aria 'Credeasi, misera,' one of the highest notes in the standard tenor repertoire.

Bellini's other operas include Il pirata, his first collaboration with Felice Romani, in which the shipwrecked count Gualtiero kills Ernesto in a duel over Imogene (a signature Maria Callas role); and I Capuleti e i Montecchi, based on Romeo and Juliet. Maria Callas and Joan Sutherland ('La Stupenda,' who recorded with her husband Richard Bonynge) were the prime 20th-century interpreters of Bellini's operas.
