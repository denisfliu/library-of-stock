Italian bel canto opera composer (1801-1835). Best known for Norma (1831) and La sonnambula (1831). Also composed I puritani (1835, his final opera) and I Capuleti e i Montecchi. A rival of Donizetti, he was beloved by Wagner for his long melodic lines. His frequent librettist was Felice Romani. Maria Callas, Joan Sutherland, and Beverly Sills led the mid-20th century bel canto revival featuring his works. He died at age 33.
Norma

Bellini's most famous opera (1831), set in Roman-occupied Gaul. The title character is a Druid priestess named Norma who has a secret affair with the Roman proconsul Pollione. Norma cuts mistletoe and sings 'Casta diva' in Act 1. The opera ends with Norma confessing her sins and both she and Pollione being burnt alive. The mezzo-soprano role Adalgisa is Pollione's other love interest, who eventually sides with Norma. The 'Guerra, guerra!' chorus urges the Celts to war against the Romans. Norma and Pollione also sing the duet 'In mia man alfin tu sei.' Norma's father Oroveso is given care of her sons at the end. Pollione sings 'Meco all'altar di Venere' recalling a dream where Norma sends lightning at his beloved. Norma contemplates killing her children with a knife in Act 2 ('Teneri, teneri figli'). Giuditta Pasta premiered the role and requested the famous aria 'Casta diva' be transposed from G major down to F major. Rosa Ponselle caused a sensation in the Met's 1927 revival. Maria Callas performed 'Casta diva' 89 times in concert during the postwar bel canto revival, and infamously cancelled after only Act 1 at the 1958 Rome Opera House. Jenny Lind caught her big break during a Berlin production of Norma. Wikipedia

"Casta diva"

Norma's Act 1 cavatina praying to the moon goddess ('Chaste goddess'). She raises mistletoe to the moon. The aria is in F major, 12/8 time. It begins with the soprano sustaining a messa di voce A4 for seven-and-a-half beats. It concludes with a two-octave coloratura passage on the words 'ah, si,' between statements of 'che regnar tu fai nel ciel.' Originally written in G major; Giuditta Pasta requested it be transposed to F major at the premiere, a tradition upheld by modern productions. Maria Callas performed it 89 times in concert during the postwar bel canto revival. Montserrat Caballe's 1974 outdoor performance at the Roman Theatre of Orange was legendary. Wikipedia

La sonnambula

Bellini's 1831 opera about Amina, a Swiss village girl who sleepwalks. The village phantom turns out to be Amina sleepwalking across a rickety mill bridge. Her fiance Elvino mistakenly thinks she is having an affair when she ends up in Count Rodolfo's room. In her famous Act III aria 'Ah! non credea mirarti,' she laments the withering of flowers while sleepwalking across the bridge -- an oboe interjects. The first line of this aria is inscribed on Bellini's tomb. Lisa (the innkeeper) encourages Elvino's suspicions. A 2009 Met production controversially set the opera in a modern rehearsal hall. A landmark 1955 La Scala production conducted by Leonard Bernstein and directed by Luchino Visconti, headlined by Maria Callas, is credited with purging the excessively flashy soprano antics that had become traditional earlier in the century. I puritani

Bellini's final opera (1835), set during the English Civil War. Based on the Ancelot and Saintine play 'Roundheads and Cavaliers.' Centers on Elvira's love for the Royalist lord Arturo. The opera ends with Arturo marrying Elvira after a general pardon is declared by Oliver Cromwell. Features a 'March of the Pilgrims.' Premiered by the 'Puritans' quartet of Lablache, Rubini, Tamburini, and Giulia Grisi. Libretto by Carlo Pepoli. The tenor role Arturo sings an F5 in the ensemble aria 'Credeasi, misera,' one of the highest notes in standard tenor repertoire. Bellini told Pepoli: 'Carve into your head in adamantine letters... people must weep, shudder, die through singing.' Elvira's mad scene aria 'Qui la voce...Vien, diletto' is a highlight. Act 2 ends with the bass-baritone duet 'Suoni la tromba.' Enrichetta escapes using a borrowed wedding veil. Six variations on a theme from this opera form the basis for the Liszt-led collaborative composition Hexameron. The final sequence of Werner Herzog's Fitzcarraldo includes a shipboard performance of this opera. Rossini suggested adding an additional act. Luigi Bassi wrote a clarinet fantasy based on its themes, and Bottesini based a Duo Concertante on it. Maria Callas shocked the opera world in 1949 by singing Elvira days after performing as Brunnhilde in Die Walkure. Wikipedia

General / Biographical

Vincenzo Bellini (1801-1835), Italian bel canto opera composer. Died at age 33 -- his tomb is inscribed with a line from La sonnambula. His frequent librettist was Felice Romani. He was a rival of Donizetti (Donizetti wrote 30 operas before having success, while Bellini found early success). Beloved by Wagner for his long melodic lines. His opera I Capuleti e i Montecchi is based on Romeo and Juliet. His early opera Il pirata featured Giovanni Rubini and was his first collaboration with Felice Romani. Maria Callas's signature Bellini roles include Norma and Imogene (from Il pirata). Joan Sutherland ('La Stupenda') was a prime 20th-century interpreter. Beverly Sills also contributed to the bel canto revival alongside Callas and Sutherland. The bel canto style exemplified by Bellini and Donizetti stressed precise execution of tempo, dynamic, and ornamentation. Jenny Lind caught her big break during a Berlin production of Bellini's Norma. Callas shocked the opera world in 1949 by singing Elvira in I puritani just days after performing as Brunnhilde in Die Walkure.

Summary of Facts

Vincenzo Bellini (1801-1835) was an Italian bel canto opera composer who died at the age of 33. His tomb is inscribed with the first line of Amina's 'flower aria' from La sonnambula: 'Ah! non credea mirarti.' His frequent librettist was Felice Romani, and he was admired by Wagner for his long melodic lines. His primary rival was Gaetano Donizetti -- while Donizetti wrote 30 operas before achieving success with Anna Bolena, Bellini found fame at a young age. Together they exemplified the bel canto style, which stressed precise execution of tempo, dynamic, and ornamentation. Maria Callas, Joan Sutherland ('La Stupenda'), and Beverly Sills led the mid-20th century bel canto revival featuring Bellini's works.

Bellini's most famous opera is Norma (1831), set in Roman-occupied Gaul. Norma is a Druid priestess who has secretly had children with the Roman proconsul Pollione. She raises mistletoe to the moon and sings the famous cavatina 'Casta diva' ('Chaste goddess') in Act 1 -- an F major, 12/8 aria that begins with a sustained messa di voce on A4 for seven-and-a-half beats and concludes with a two-octave coloratura passage on 'ah, si.' The aria was originally in G major but was transposed to F major at the request of Giuditta Pasta, who premiered the role, a tradition maintained ever since. The mezzo-soprano role Adalgisa is Pollione's other love interest; he urges her to come to Rome in the duet 'Vieni in Roma.' Norma and Adalgisa promise eternal friendship in the duet 'Si fino all'ore estreme,' and Adalgisa confesses her secret lover in 'Sola, furtiva, al tempio.' Pollione recalls a dream where Norma sends lightning to smite his beloved at the altar in 'Meco all'altar di Venere.' In Act 2, Norma contemplates killing her children with a knife ('Teneri, teneri figli'), sending them away with servant Clotilde. Her father Oroveso is ultimately given care of her sons. The chorus 'Guerra, guerra!' incites the Celts to war after one character strikes a shield three times. Norma and Pollione sing the duet 'In mia man alfin tu sei,' in which he offers to die rather than let her sacrifice their children. The opera opens with the march and chorus 'Dell'aura tua profetica.' Some performances eliminate the massive gong strikes near the end of the trio 'Oh! Di qual sei tu vittima.' Norma ultimately confesses her sins, and both she and Pollione are burned alive. Maria Callas performed 'Casta diva' 89 times in concert, and infamously cancelled after Act 1 at the 1958 Rome Opera House. Rosa Ponselle caused a sensation in the Met's 1927 revival. Montserrat Caballe's 1974 outdoor performance of 'Casta diva' at the Roman Theatre of Orange was legendary. Jenny Lind caught her big break during a Berlin production of Norma.

La sonnambula (1831) concerns Amina, a Swiss village girl who sleepwalks. The village 'phantom' is revealed to be Amina walking in her sleep across a rickety mill bridge. Her fiance Elvino (encouraged by the innkeeper Lisa) mistakenly believes Amina is having an affair with Count Rodolfo when she ends up in his room and takes back his engagement ring. In the climactic 'flower aria,' 'Ah! non credea mirarti / si presto estinto, o fiore,' Amina laments withered flowers while sleepwalking -- an oboe interjects. A mezzo-soprano version of the sleepwalking scene includes a high D-flat on the word 'andiam.' Luisa Tetrazzini's 1911 recording features cello obbligato under her cadenza. This aria's first line is inscribed on Bellini's tomb. A landmark 1955 La Scala production, conducted by Leonard Bernstein and directed by Luchino Visconti with Maria Callas in the title role, is credited with purging the excessively flashy soprano antics that had become traditional. A 2009 Met production controversially set the opera in a modern New York rehearsal hall, prompting boos.

I puritani (1835) was Bellini's final opera, set during the English Civil War and based on Ancelot and Saintine's play 'Roundheads and Cavaliers.' It centers on Elvira's love for the Royalist lord Arturo and ends with their marriage after Oliver Cromwell declares a general pardon. Enrichetta escapes using a borrowed wedding veil. The opera features Elvira's mad scene aria 'Qui la voce...Vien, diletto' and the description of her madness in 'Cinta di fiori.' The Act 2 bass-baritone duet 'Suoni la tromba' and the aria 'A te, o cara, amor talora' are also notable. The tenor role Arturo sings an extraordinary F5 in 'Credeasi, misera,' one of the highest notes in standard tenor repertoire. The libretto was by Carlo Pepoli -- Bellini famously told him: 'Carve into your head in adamantine letters... people must weep, shudder, die through singing.' The opera was premiered by the 'Puritans' quartet: Lablache, Rubini, Tamburini, and Giulia Grisi. Rossini suggested adding an additional act. Six variations on a theme from this opera form the Franz Liszt-led collaborative composition Hexameron. The final sequence of Werner Herzog's Fitzcarraldo features a shipboard performance of I puritani in full costume. Luigi Bassi wrote a clarinet fantasy based on its themes, and Bottesini based a Duo Concertante on it. Maria Callas shocked the opera world in 1949 by singing Elvira just days after performing as Brunnhilde in Die Walkure.

Bellini's other operas include Il pirata, his first collaboration with Felice Romani, in which the shipwrecked count Gualtiero kills Ernesto in a duel over Imogene (a signature Maria Callas role); and I Capuleti e i Montecchi, based on Romeo and Juliet.
