General / Biographical

Pirandello is identified as an Italian Nobel Prize-winning playwright and absurdist. He is Sicilian. He was a precursor to the Theatre of the Absurd. Dario Fo was the first Italian to win the Nobel Prize since Pirandello. His essay 'On Humor' examined Manzoni's The Betrothed. His muse was Marta Abba.

Summary of Facts

Luigi Pirandello is an Italian Nobel Prize-winning playwright and novelist best known for his metatheatrical play Six Characters in Search of an Author, his play Henry IV about a man who believes himself to be a Holy Roman Emperor, and his novel The Late Mattia Pascal.

Six Characters in Search of an Author is Pirandello's most frequently clued work by a wide margin. Six characters -- The Father, the mother, the Son, the Step-Daughter, the Boy, and the Child -- interrupt a rehearsal of Pirandello's own play Mixing It Up (also called The Rules of the Game or Il giuoco delle parti). The Father claims to be possessed by a 'Demon of Experiment' and begs the Stage Manager to let his family tell their story. The hanging of coats and hats on a rack summons Madame Pace, who runs a brothel/fashion house where the Step-Daughter works as a prostitute. The Father offers a hundred lire in a 'pale blue envelope' and flirts with his former step-daughter in the brothel. The Step-Daughter performs the cabaret number 'Prenez garde a Tchou-Tchin-Tchou.' A girl dressed in white with a black sash clings to a woman whose 'wax-like' face is covered by a widow's veil. The play ends when the Child drowns in a fountain and the Boy shoots himself, leading the Stage Manager to exclaim 'I've lost a whole day over these people.' The Leading Man complains about wearing a chef's hat. The curtain is meant to be left up between acts. The Prompter reads out 'When the curtain rises, Leo Gala is busy beating an egg in a cup.' The play is part of Pirandello's 'theatre in the theatre' trilogy, along with Each in His Own Way and Tonight We Improvise.

Henry IV (Enrico IV) is about a nobleman who falls off his horse during a costume pageant/masquerade and comes to believe he is the Holy Roman Emperor Henry IV. Count di Nolli pays four valets to serve him in a residence at Goslar for eleven years. A portrait of Matilda dressed as a famous countess hangs in his 'throne room.' A doctor attempts to 'cure' him by replacing two Portraits, the only modern objects visible. The title character stabs Tito Belcredi (whom he mistakes for Peter Damian) after the attempted cure. He chides servants for not living in a dream of history. He relates his situation to a sleeping Irish priest being touched by a flower. He develops a taste for 'playing the madman' after twenty years.

The Late Mattia Pascal is a novel about a man who wins at Monte Carlo and then learns he has been declared dead (his family mistakes another corpse for him). He adopts the name Adriano Meis and starts a new life. The librarian Don Eligio encourages him to write his story. The title character complains about Copernicus. He eventually abandons his new identity by faking his death but is unable to return to his old life.

Right You Are (If You Think So) features Laudisi investigating conflicting claims by Lady Frola and Mr. Ponza about whether Ponza's second wife is actually Frola's daughter. Laudisi looks in the mirror and asks 'Who is insane among us?' One character claims she is 'a daughter and a second wife, but nobody to herself.'

Tonight We Improvise features Doctor Hinkfuss, who fails to provide a script and leads actors in improvised performance of the novella Leonora, Addio! Pirandello's muse Marta Abba adapted it. An aviation officer leaves his wife, causing her to die while singing opera. At the end, Hinkfuss apologizes.

Other works include Liola (a rural comedy about a rake, the first play in his collection Naked Masks), Each in His Own Way (in which Francesco and Doro nearly duel over their differing interpretations of the love affairs of Delia Morello), The Man with a Flower in His Mouth (a one-act dialogue on Christmas Eve between a man who missed his train and a man dying of facial epithelioma -- the first TV drama produced by the BBC in 1930), The Old and the Young (a historical novel set in Sicily after the Garibaldi uprising of 1860), and 'The Dissolution of the League' (about Mafia cattle thievery). His essay 'On Humor' examined Don Abbondio in Manzoni's The Betrothed.

Pirandello is a Sicilian author. Dario Fo was the first Italian to win the Nobel Prize since Pirandello.
