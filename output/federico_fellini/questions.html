Tossups

T1 2025 MRNA (2025) Diff 7 · Fine Arts
In a film by this director, the camera moves through a beachside “Miss Mermaid” pageant to introduce five unemployed young men. In a film by this director, the camera follows a woman laying planks around her flooded apartment. A woman nicknamed “Sardine” dances a rumba for a group of boys on a beach in a film by this director before two priests barge in. At the end of a film by this director, the protagonist wanders onto a beach where a sea (*) monster has washed up the morning after a raging party. A film by this director ends with a filmmaker joining a group dancing on a beachside film set. In another film by this director, a celebrity reporter romps through the Trevi fountain with an American actress. For 10 points, name this director who portrayed Italy’s seasides in 8 ½ and La Dolce Vita.
ANSWER: Federico Fellini (the first film is I Vitelloni)
T2 2025 Absolute Cinema (2025) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
In a 1962 anthology that emulates the style of a Renaissance author, hallucinations of this actress haunt a doctor after he sees a billboard in which her provocative pose is accompanied by the caption “drink more milk!” This actress, who cameoed as herself in the films The Clowns and Intervista, allegedly once used a bow and arrow to wound the arm of a photographer who followed her to her villa. In her best-known film, this actress says, “there are three things I like most: (*) love, love, and love.” This actress meows along with a cat she carries on her head in the lead-up to a scene in which dawn breaks just as she pours water on the male lead’s head as a sort of “anointment.” In that 1960 film, this former model plays Sylvia Rank, who wears a sleeveless black dress while frolicking in the Trevi Fountain. For 10 points, name this blonde Swedish star of Fellini’s La Dolce Vita.
ANSWER: Anita Ekberg [or Kerstin Anita Marianne Ekberg] (The first line describes Fellini’s segment of Boccaccio ’70.)
T3 2023 On Cinema at the Buzzer (2023) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
While making a film at this studio, Maurice Kruger has a heart attack, so the washed-up star Jack Andrus takes over as director, in the film Two Weeks in Another Town. This studio’s grounds are now home to the prop for a giant head of Venus that sinks into a canal in a scene from a 1976 film starring Donald Sutherland. The motto “Cinema is the most powerful weapon” adorned this studio when it was inaugurated in 1937 by the politician who co-founded it. Andrew Marton used an arena built on this studio’s backlot to shoot the (*) chariot race in a William Wyler film. The Barefoot Contessa, Quo Vadis, Ben-Hur and Cleopatra were among the international productions filmed at this studio during the “Hollywood on the Tiber” period. For 10 points, name this enormous studio in Rome.
ANSWER: Cinecittà (“chee-nay-chee-TAH”) [or Cinema City] (The 1976 film is Fellini’s Casanova.)

B3 2022 CMST (2022) Diff 9 · Fine Arts

ANSWER: Cabiria [accept Nights of Cabiria or Le notti di Cabiria]
[10] Pastrone’s strongman character Maciste influenced the brutish circus performer Zampanò in La Strada, a film by this director of Nights of Cabiria and 8 1/2.

ANSWER: Hell [or the Underworld; or Hades; accept Maciste in Hell or Maciste all’inferno; prompt on the afterlife] (The Cocteau film is Orpheus.)
B4 2021 The World As It Is (2021) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
