Summary of Facts

Alfred Sisley was a British-born Impressionist painter who worked primarily in France. He is most commonly identified by his Flood at Port-Marly (also called The Boat During the Flood, Port-Marly), which depicts water-covered streets in the French village during a flood. He created a series of paintings of the Thames, including his Hampton Court series. He painted near the Forest of Fontainebleau and is associated with the Barbizon School of pre-Impressionists. His earliest surviving work is Lane Near a Small Town. Other known works include Bridge at Villeneuve-la-Garenne and View of the Thames. He is one of the less frequently clued Impressionists in quizbowl, appearing mostly as bonus answers.
