Rem Koolhaas (full name Remment Lucas Koolhaas) is a Dutch architect who co-founded the Office of Metropolitan Architecture (OMA) with Elia Zenghelis. He is known for landmark buildings including the Seattle Central Library, the CCTV Headquarters in Beijing, the Casa da Musica in Porto, the Qatar National Library, and De Rotterdam. He is also a prominent architectural theorist, authoring 'Delirious New York' and 'S,M,L,XL', and won the 2000 Pritzker Prize.
Seattle Central Library

A glass-and-steel library in Seattle, Washington. One of Koolhaas's most cited works, appearing in nearly every quizbowl clue about him. It features the Charles Simonyi Mixing Center and the 'Living Room' on the third floor. Structural engineers Magnusson Klemencic Associates worked on the project.

A monumental architectural theory book co-designed with Bruce Mau. Arranges OMA's projects chronologically within size categories (Small, Medium, Large, Extra-Large). Summary of Facts

Rem Koolhaas (full name Remment Lucas Koolhaas) is a Dutch architect who co-founded the Office of Metropolitan Architecture (OMA), based in Rotterdam, with Elia Zenghelis. He is also a professor at Harvard. Koolhaas was a teacher of Zaha Hadid during her studies in London.

Koolhaas's most frequently cited building is the Seattle Central Library, a glass-and-steel structure that includes features such as the Charles Simonyi Mixing Center and the 'Living Room' on the third floor. Structural engineers Magnusson Klemencic Associates worked on the project. His second most-cited work is the CCTV Headquarters in Beijing, designed with Ole Scheeren, featuring two slanted towers connected by a 75-meter perpendicular cantilevered section; the building is popularly nicknamed for its resemblance to a pair of shorts. OMA also designed the Shenzhen Stock Exchange building in China.

Koolhaas designed the Casa da Musica in Porto (also called Casa de Musica), the Qatar National Library in Education City, Doha (completed 2017), which resembles two pieces of paper being separated and folded, and De Rotterdam, described as the largest building in the Netherlands. He designed the McCormick Tribune Campus Center at the Illinois Institute of Technology, topped by a huge tubular metal train tunnel. He also designed the Guggenheim Hermitage Museum in Las Vegas, the RAK Convention Center in the UAE, and OMA worked on the Serpentine Galleries pavilion in London in 2006. OMA collaborated on the Fondazione Prada in Milan and a 'Transformer' building for Prada in Seoul.

As a theorist, Koolhaas wrote 'Delirious New York,' whose cover features his wife Madelon Vriesendorp's painting 'Flagrant Delit,' showing two distorted skyscrapers in bed next to the Statue of Liberty's torch. The book called Manhattan the 'twentieth century's Rosetta Stone' and praises the 'chance-like' nature of city living. He also introduced the term 'Manhattanism' to describe an ideology embracing urban congestion. His monumental book 'S,M,L,XL,' co-designed with Bruce Mau, arranges OMA's projects in order of size and contains essays such as 'Islam after Einstein,' the poem 'Learning Japanese,' and a masterplan essay for Yokohama described as 'programmatic lava.' He also wrote the essay 'Junkspace.' Koolhaas won the 2000 Pritzker Prize. His 2002 proposed redesign of the European Union flag, never adopted, is referred to as the 'Barcode' design.
