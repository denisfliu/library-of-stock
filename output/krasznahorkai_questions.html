Questions: Laszlo Krasznahorkai

1 tossups · 9 bonuses

Tossups

T1 2021 ACF Nationals (2021) Diff 9 · Literature
This author wrote about a game warden who decides to hunt humans after a conversion experience in the second of a pair of linked novellas about hunting. A Kyoto heron spearing for fish and a Louvre guard watching the Venus de Milo are subjects of two of the disparate chapters on aesthetics that make up another novel by this author. Mr. Eszter tries to supplant the “artificial” tuning system used in Bach’s Well-Tempered Clavier in another novel by this author, in which a traveling circus comes to a small town with the world’s largest dead whale. This author of The Last Wolf and Herman, as well as Seiobo There Below, collaborated on a seven-hour-long, extremely slow adaptation of one of his novels by Béla Tarr. Susan Sontag used the phrase “contemporary master of the apocalypse” to describe, for 10 points, what Hungarian author of The Melancholy of Resistance and Satantango?
ANSWER: Laszlo Krasznahorkai

B1 2026 ACF Regionals (2026) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
After Pandora’s Box, G. W. Pabst’s second collaboration with Louise Brooks was a 1929 film titled for one of these objects in which the innocent girl Thymian falls into prostitution. For 10 points each:
[10] Paul Schrader’s primary influences for the film First Reformed were Ingmar Bergman’s Winter Light and a Robert Bresson film titled for what sort of object belonging to a country priest?
ANSWER: diary [or diaries; accept Diary of a Lost Girl or Diary of a Country Priest; accept Tagebuch einer Verlorenen or Journal d’un curé de campagne]
[10] A director from this country made Diary for My Children as part of her Diary Trilogy. A pair of married directors from this country collaborated with a novelist on the films Werckmeister Harmonies and Sátántangó.
ANSWER: Hungary [or Magyarorszag] (The first director is Márta Mészáros. Béla Tarr and Ágnes Hranitzky collaborated with the novelist László Krasznahorkai on the latter films.)
[10] This director’s first film, Glumov’s Diary, uses several visual gags to portray the book’s contents. This Soviet pioneer of montage directed Battleship Potemkin.
ANSWER: Sergei Eisenstein
B2 2024 Chicago Open (2024) Diff 10 · Literature
In Michael Hamburger’s translation, a poem titled for this event begins, “From pointed pates hats fly into the blue.” For 10 points each:
[10] Name this event that titles a Jakob van Hoddis piece reputed to be the “first expressionist poem.” An old man who is busy binding tomatoes is the only person to notice this event in a Czesław Miłosz “song” titled for it.
ANSWER: end of the world [accept apocalypse; accept Weltende or “A Song on the End of the World”]
[10] This language was used in apocalyptic poems like “A Ball at the Opera” by the Skamandrite poet Julian Tuwim and for most of Czesław Miłosz’s poetry.
ANSWER: Polish [or polski]
[10] The title of this 2014 collection subverts its author’s reputation as a “master of the apocalypse.” After “79 paragraphs on blank pages,” this collection ends by resolving to “leave everything here” in the prose poem “I Don’t Need Anything from Here.”
ANSWER: The World Goes On [or Megy a világ] (by László Krasznahorkai)
B3 2024 ARGOS (2024) Diff 9 · Literature
The narrator of a novel by this author praises the artistic isolation of a Sienese painter while looking at depictions of torture in the Louvre on the eve of the Sudetenland annexation. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this author who included meditations on the revolutionary aspects of artworks in his novel The Aesthetics of Resistance. A play by this author is set in an asylum run by Coulmier.
ANSWER: Peter Weiss [or Peter Ulrich Weiss] (The play is Marat/Sade.)
[10] In this novel, the apprentice Aulista questions if his master Perugino has lost interest in painting after he delays the completion of his Tezi Altarpiece. This novel’s interconnected chapters on aesthetics are numbered according to the Fibonacci sequence.
ANSWER: Seiobo There Below [or Seiobo járt odalent] (by László Krasznahorkai)
[10] Gaspard Winckler attempts to forge a portrait by Antonello da Messina in Le Condottière, a posthumously published novel by Georges Perec, a member of this primarily French constrained writing movement.
ANSWER: Oulipo [or Workshop of Potential Literature; or Ouvroir de littérature potentielle]
B4 2022 Penn Bowl (2022) Diff 8 · Literature
Max Neumann created the surreal illustrations for a series of prose pieces by this author that describes a horrible beast enclosed in a suffocatingly small space. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this author who wrote the collections Animalinside and The World Goes On. The conman Irimiás convinces the inhabitants of a post-communist collective farm to abscond to a nearby crumbling mansion in this man’s 1985 debut novel.
ANSWER: László Krasznahorkai (The novel is Sátántangó.)
[10] Colm Tóibín’s introduction to Animalinside compares Krasznahorkai to this author, who himself wrote about terrible creatures as a symbol of alienation in his story “The Burrow” and his novella The Metamorphosis.
ANSWER: Franz Kafka
[10] Tóibín’s introduction also compares Krasznahorkai with this author, who wrote about the title character becoming a manservant in Mr. Knott’s house in his novel Watt.
ANSWER: Samuel Beckett

B6 2019 ACF Nationals (2019) Diff 9 · Literature
A pregnant woman walks through Rome to a Bach concert in a novel with this characteristic, F.C. Delius’s Portrait of the Mother as a Young Woman. For 10 points each:
[10] Identify this stylistic quirk shared by Bohumil Hrabal’s novel Dancing Lessons for the Advanced of Age and by approximately the last twenty pages of another novel, in which the narrator recalls being proposed to in Gibraltar.
ANSWER: they’re written in one sentence [or obvious equivalents; prompt on written without paragraph breaks or similar]
[10] A man tells a bartender about his experiences reporting on the killing of the title animal in the Spanish region of Extremadura in this author’s single-sentence novella The Last Wolf. The World Goes On is a collection of stories by this winner of the 2015 Man Booker International.
ANSWER: László Krasznahorkai
[10] The sentence “And they marched all night” follows a 39,995 word sentence in The Gates of Paradise by this country’s author Jerzy Andrzejewski, who appears as “Alpha” in Czeslaw Milosz’s The Captive Mind.
ANSWER: Poland
B7 2018 Cambridge Open (2018) Diff 8 · Literature
In one novel, this author reflects on two uses of the word “no” in his life; one when his wife suggests they have children, and one when he is asked by a doctor whether he has children. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this author who wrote about refusing to bring new life into a world that allowed the Holocaust in Kaddish for an Unborn Child, and of a child’s experience in Buchenwald in Fateless.
ANSWER: Imre Kertész [KER-TESH]
[10] This author from the same country as Kertész writes about a circus that only has a whale in The Melancholy of Resistance, and how farmers escape from their masters in an authoritarian society in Satantango.
ANSWER: László Krasznahorkai [KRASS-NA-WHORE-KYE]
[10] Kertész and Krasznahorkai are both from this Central European nation, whose other contemporary authors include Péter Nádas and whose national epic is The Siege of Sziget.
ANSWER: Hungary [accept Magyarország]
B8 2016 ACF Nationals (2016) Diff 9 · Literature
A novel in this language is numbered according to the Fibonacci sequence, and features the title goddess descending into a noh actor, as well as a description of the eyes of a statue of the Amida Buddha. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this language, which was used to write Seiobo There Below. It also was used to write a novel in which Korin’s “life changes forever” after he finds a manuscript about four adventurers, War and War.
ANSWER: Hungarian [or Magyar]
[10] Seiobo There Below and War and War are by this “Master of the Apocalypse,” who allegorized fascism’s rise in a novel about a town’s fascination with a circus exhibiting a whale carcass, The Melancholy of Resistance.
ANSWER: László Krasznahorkai
[10] The epigraph to War and War enigmatically notes that “[this place] is sad.” The third part of Dante’s The Divine Comedy describes this place, including its Empyrean.
ANSWER: Heaven [or Paradise or Paradiso]
B9 2013 Arrabal (2013) Diff 10 · Literature
According to Susan Sontag, this writer’s A Book of Memories was “the greatest novel written in our time, and one of the great books of the century.” For 10 points each:

[10] This countryman of Peter Nadas wrote of Georgy Korin, who finds an unknown manuscript and is overwhelmed by its beauty, in his novel War and War, and Mrs. Eszter’s scheming to get herself appointed head of the town committee on moral renewal in The Melancholy of Resistance.
ANSWER: Laszlo Krasznahorkai
[10] Sure, whatever, they’re Hungarian, just like this author of Fatelessness and Kaddish for a Child Not Born.
