Contemporary British author of Jamaican and English descent, best known for White Teeth (2000), On Beauty (2005), NW (2012), and Swing Time (2016). James Wood coined the term 'hysterical realism' in response to White Teeth. Her essay collections include Feel Free and Changing My Mind. Also wrote the play The Wife of Willesden (adapting Chaucer), the novel The Fraud (2023), and The Autograph Man. Mother is Yvonne Bailey, featured in New Daughters of Africa.
White Teeth (2000)

Smith's debut and best-known novel, set in London, about the friendship between Archie Jones (English, WWII veteran) and Samad Iqbal (Bangladeshi immigrant). Opens with Archie's attempted suicide by gassing himself in his car, interrupted by a butcher. Archie marries Clara Bowden. Samad's twins Magid and Millat are raised in Willesden; Millat joins the Islamic fundamentalist group KEVIN, while Joshua Chalfen joins the animal rights group FATE. Both disrupt Marcus Chalfen's FutureMouse genetic engineering conference at the novel's end. Dr. Perret/Dr. Sick, a Nazi scientist, is revealed to be alive. Samad is obsessed with ancestor Mangal Pandey. Hortense Bowden, a Jehovah's Witness, was born during the Kingston earthquake. Features 'Neena the Niece-of-Shame.' James Wood coined 'hysterical realism' to criticize this novel. Wikipedia

On Beauty (2005)

Loosely structured after E. M. Forster's Howards End. Chronicles the rivalry between art historians Howard Belsey and Monty Kipps (a black conservative). The Belsey family lives on the outskirts of Boston. A painting of Maitresse Erzulie (a Hector Hippolyte painting) is a major plot point. Howard's wife Kiki was left the painting in a will. Claire Malcolm teaches Zora at a poetry class that visits a spoken word venue called The Bus Stop. Howard's motto is 'To the pure all things are pure.' Takes its title from an Elaine Scarry essay. Opens with an email from Jerome to Harold. A character puts on a faux-Brooklyn accent and joins Haitian street peddlers. Wikipedia

Summary of Facts

Zadie Smith is a contemporary British author of Jamaican and English descent, one of the most heavily clued living novelists in quizbowl. Her debut novel White Teeth (2000) dominates her clue profile, appearing in virtually every tossup and most bonuses. White Teeth follows the friendship between Archie Jones and Bangladeshi immigrant Samad Iqbal in London's Willesden area. The novel opens with Archie's attempted suicide by gassing himself in his car, interrupted by a butcher. Samad's twin sons Magid and Millat diverge — Millat joins the Islamic fundamentalist group KEVIN while Joshua Chalfen joins the animal rights group FATE, both disrupting Marcus Chalfen's FutureMouse genetic engineering conference at the novel's climax. Dr. Perret/Dr. Sick, a Nazi scientist, is revealed to be alive. Samad is obsessed with his ancestor Mangal Pandey, wanting a portrait hung at O'Connell's Pub. Irie cannot identify her baby's father because she had sex with the twin brothers twenty minutes apart. James Wood coined 'hysterical realism' to describe this novel. Other characters include Hortense Bowden (Jehovah's Witness born during the Kingston earthquake), the lesbian shoe-mender 'Neena the Niece-of-Shame,' and Horst Ibelgaufts.

On Beauty (2005), loosely structured after Forster's Howards End, centers on the academic rivalry between Rembrandt scholars Howard Belsey and the black conservative Monty Kipps. A Hector Hippolyte painting of Maitresse Erzulie is a major plot point. Claire Malcolm teaches Zora at a poetry workshop. The title comes from an Elaine Scarry essay.

NW (2012) follows four northwest London inhabitants: Felix (stabbed to death for asking a man to move his feet), Keisha/Natalie (secretly arranges sex online; stopped from jumping off a bridge by Nathan), Leah Hanwell, and Nathan. Swing Time (2016) features a girl with flat feet taking tap lessons with the prodigy Tracey, later working for the pop diva Aimee.

Smith's essay collections are also clued. Feel Free (2018) includes 'Joy,' 'Generation Why?' (on Facebook/Brexit), and imagines Justin Bieber meeting Martin Buber. Changing My Mind contains 'Two Directions for the Novel.' 'The Instrumentalist' on Tar was a 2024 Pulitzer finalist. Other works include The Fraud (2023, fictionalizing the Tichborne case), The Wife of Willesden (adapting Chaucer's Wife of Bath as a Jamaican woman named Alvita), The Embassy of Cambodia (unpaid maid Fatou), and The Autograph Man (Jewish-Chinese protagonist Alex-Li).
