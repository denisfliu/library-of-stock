Dubliners

A collection of fifteen stories arranged from young to old protagonists. 'Araby': narrator fails to buy a gift for Mangan's sister at the title bazaar, sees himself as 'a creature driven and derided by vanity.' 'Eveline': title character clutches an iron railing, decides against immigrating to Buenos Aires with Frank. 'The Dead': Gabriel Conroy learns about Gretta's deceased former lover Michael Furey after Bartell D'Arcy sings 'The Lass of Aughrim'; ends with snow falling 'upon all the living and the dead.' 'A Painful Case': Mr. Duffy reads of Emily Sinico's death by train. 'Two Gallants': Lenehan dines on peas and ginger beer. 'Clay': spinster from Lamplight laundry touches a wet lump while blindfolded. 'A Mother': amateur impresario with a 'game leg.' 'An Encounter': boys ditch school, meet a man who rants about whipping boys. 'The Sisters': Father Flynn's death. 'A Little Cloud': Little Chandler's failed literary ambitions.

Ulysses

Set nearly entirely on June 16, 1904 (Bloomsday, inspired by Joyce's first date with Nora Barnacle). Opens with 'stately, plump Buck Mulligan.' Stephen Dedalus cries 'Non serviam!' and strikes a chandelier with his ashplant after seeing his mother's ghost in the 'Circe' chapter (set in Nighttown brothel owned by Bella Cohen). 'Oxen of the Sun' recapitulates the history of English writing as Mina Purefoy gives birth. Stephen calls God 'a shout in the street' and history 'a nightmare from which I am trying to awake.' Molly Bloom's soliloquy ends 'yes I said yes I will Yes.' The 'ineluctable modality of the visible.' Stephen corrects a dean's use of 'funnel' to 'tundish.' A commemorative coin added the word 'that' erroneously. Rebecca West wrote the novel was 'ugly and incompetent' but 'a work of art.' The Ulysses obscenity trials: first trial convicted Anderson and Heap of The Little Review for the 'Nausicaa' episode; Judge Woolsey's opinion about 'old Saxon words' was published in later editions. Tom Stoppard's Travesties involves Joyce and Henry Carr feuding over a staging of Wilde's Earnest. Richard Ellmann wrote the definitive biography.

Finnegans Wake

Begins mid-sentence with 'riverrun, past Eve and Adam's'; last line forms a sentence with the first. Hundred-letter 'thunder words.' Anna Livia Plurabelle (ALP) and H.C.E. (Humphrey Chimpden Earwicker). Coined the word 'quark.' Marshall McLuhan's War and Peace in the Global Village structured around the thunder words. Samuel Beckett's first essay, 'Dante... Bruno. Vico.. Joyce,' was written for the Exagmination of Work in Progress. Excerpts published in the journal transition.

A Portrait of the Artist as a Young Man

Autobiographical novel about Stephen Dedalus who attends Jesuit schools (Clongowes Wood and Belvedere). Father Arnall's terrifying hell sermon traumatizes Stephen. Stephen corrects a dean's use of 'funnel' to 'tundish.' He vows to forge 'the uncreated conscience of my race.' Inspired Clarice Lispector's Near to the Wild Heart.

General / Biographical

Joyce's wife Nora Barnacle inspired Gretta Conroy and the character who works at Finn's Hotel. Their first date (June 16) became Bloomsday. Joyce's infamously filthy love letters to Nora sold for 240,000 pounds at Sotheby's in 2004. Daughter Lucia Joyce danced at Paris's inaugural international dance festival, wore a fish-scale costume, was institutionalized in 1936. Joyce's play Exiles involves Richard Rowan and Robert Hand in Dublin; inspired by his affair with Nora in Trieste. Pomes Penyeach collection includes 'Bahnhofstrasse.' Joyce wrote for/about the journal transition. Tom Stoppard's Travesties depicts Joyce feuding with Henry Carr. In Flann O'Brien's The Dalkey Archive, Joyce fakes his death and works as a bartender.

Summary of Facts

James Joyce is an Irish modernist author whose works revolutionized 20th-century literature. Dubliners, his short story collection, is arranged from young to old protagonists. 'Araby' ends with the narrator seeing himself as 'a creature driven and derided by vanity.' 'The Dead' concludes with Gabriel Conroy contemplating Michael Furey's spirit as snow falls, after Bartell D'Arcy sings 'The Lass of Aughrim.' 'Eveline' clutches an iron railing. 'A Painful Case' centers on Mr. Duffy reading of Emily Sinico's death.

Ulysses is set on June 16, 1904 (Bloomsday, named for Joyce's first date with Nora Barnacle). It opens with Buck Mulligan and ends with Molly Bloom's 'yes I said yes I will Yes.' The 'Circe' chapter takes place in Nighttown where Stephen sees his mother's ghost. 'Oxen of the Sun' recapitulates English prose history. Stephen calls God 'a shout in the street.' The novel's obscenity trials included conviction of Anderson and Heap for the 'Nausicaa' episode and Judge Woolsey's landmark ruling.

Finnegans Wake begins with 'riverrun' and has a circular structure. It coined 'quark' and features hundred-letter thunder words. A Portrait of the Artist follows Stephen Dedalus through Jesuit schools with Father Arnall's hell sermon.

Joyce's love letters to Nora sold for 240,000 pounds. His daughter Lucia was a dancer institutionalized in 1936. His play Exiles features Richard Rowan. Stoppard's Travesties depicts Joyce feuding with Henry Carr. Richard Ellmann wrote the definitive biography.
