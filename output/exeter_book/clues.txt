     Leadin: The speaker of a poem included in this codex describes "the citadels crumbled" and "the work of giants ruined" in a poem that focuses on a "well-crafted ancient-work." For 10 points each:
  Part 1 (ans: Exeter Book):
    Name this codex of Old English poems including "The Seafarer." "Master artisans" lie buried for "a hundred generations" in "The Ruin," a poem from this codex.
  Part 2 (ans: elegy [or elegies]):
    "The Ruin" belongs to this genre of Old English poems. "The Wife's Lament" and "The Husband's Message,"both of which are in the Exeter Book, are other examples of these poetic laments for the dead.
  Part 3 (ans: J. R. R. Tolkien [or John Ronald Reuel Tolkien]):
    Old English words used in "The Ruin," including enta and orþonc ("or-thonk"), inspired character and place names used by this author. This author likened Beowulf to an elegy in the essay "The Monsters and the Critics."

[B2] 2018 Oxford Open (2018) | Diff: 8 | Literature
     Leadin: This language was used to write a poem which, translated, begins: 'This masonry is wondrous; fates broke it / courtyard pavements were smashed; the work of giants is decaying.' For 10 points each:
  Part 1 (ans: Anglo-Saxon (or Old English)):
    The title character of another poem in this language reminisces that 'Blithe heart cannot know, / through its happiness, what hardships they suffer / who drive the foam-furrow furthest from land.'
  Part 2 (ans: Exeter Book):
    This collection of Anglo-Saxon verse, the largest still in existence, contains The Wanderer, The Ruin, and The Seafarer, as well as 95 shorter riddles.
  Part 3 (ans: Middle Earth (Hail Earandel brightest of angels/ over Middle Earth sent to men - Eala Earendel engla beorhtast / Ofer middangeard monnum sended.)):
    This fictional world takes its name from a line in the poem Christ I, which is included in the Exeter Book. The line in question is preceded by one that declares 'Hail Earendel brightest of Angels'. This was also the name JRR Tolkien used for the central continent where most of the action of the Lord of Rings takes place.
