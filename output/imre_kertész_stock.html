Summary of Facts

Imre Kertész (1929-2016) was a Hungarian Nobel Prize-winning author who drew on his own Holocaust experiences at Auschwitz and Buchenwald. Fatelessness is a semi-autobiographical novel in which Gyuri Köves learns to survive the Zeitz camp from Bandi Citrom. Kaddish for a Child Not Born centers on B.'s refusal to bring a child into a world that allowed the Holocaust, recalled through the word 'no.' He also wrote Detective Story (about a former secret policeman) and Liquidation.
