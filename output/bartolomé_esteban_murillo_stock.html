General / Biographical

Early career influenced by Zurbaran (the 'Spanish Caravaggio'). Died after falling from scaffolding at a church in Cadiz while painting Mystic Marriage of St. Catherine. Summary of Facts

Bartolome Esteban Murillo (1617-1682) was a Spanish Baroque painter from Seville, a contemporary of Velazquez. His paintings are categorized into three styles based on degree of tenebrism: 'cool,' 'warm,' and 'vaporous' (estilo vaporoso), with the vaporous style representing his mature work with soft, diffuse tones.

He is best known for his nearly twenty versions of the Immaculate Conception, which feature Mary in a blue cloak surrounded by cherubs with putti blending into the background like haze. The Immaculate Conception of Los Venerables was possibly the most expensive painting in the world when the Louvre purchased it from Marshal Soult's estate. 'The Colossal' version unusually features a full moon at the bottom with cherubs struggling to hold up a cloud. Unlike other painters' versions, his omit references to the Marian litanies.

His genre scenes of the street children and beggars of Seville are equally famous. The Young Beggar and Boys Eating Grapes and Melon depict poor children in vivid detail. Two Women at a Window (A Girl and Her Duena) shows a flirtatious woman lifting her veil next to a somber bespectacled duena, depicting Galician-born prostitutes in Madrid.

Murillo's early career was influenced by Zurbaran (the 'Spanish Caravaggio'), and like Velazquez, he lightened his use of tenebrism later in his career. He created the most widely circulated portrait of St. Isidore of Seville (patron saint of the Internet) for Seville Cathedral. Other works include Vision of St. Anthony, Saints Justa and Rufina, Young Man with a Basket of Fruit, and the Heavenly and Earthly Trinities. A portrait attributed to him features concentric circles representing the heliocentric model, linked to the Galileo legend of 'Eppur si muove.' He died after falling from scaffolding while painting the Mystic Marriage of St. Catherine for a Capuchin church in Cadiz.
