B1 2023 MRNA (2023) Diff 7 · Literature
Much of the literature circulated in this manner was written on East German Erika typewriters, as the Soviet Union kept typographic samples of every individual domestic printing device. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this form of dissident literature in the Eastern bloc in which illegal manuscripts passed from person to person. Doctor Zhivago was the first full-length novel to circulate in this form.
ANSWER: samizdat
[10] This Mikhail Bulgakov novel circulated for decades as samizdat before a complete version was published in 1973. This novel, in which the devil visits Moscow, is the source of the quote “manuscripts don’t burn.”
ANSWER: The Master and Margarita [or Мастер и Маргарита]
[10] This Hungarian author of Abigél spent the 1950s teaching at a girl’s school after being labeled an enemy of the party. Bernard Rix’s translation of this author’s novel The Door was republished in 2015 to widespread acclaim.
ANSWER: Magda Szabó
B2 2022 Chicago Open (2022) Diff 10 · Literature
Zimbabwean translator Len Rix won the 2018 PEN Translation prize for his translation of this novel. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this novel in which a group of children play the game Cherry Tree before the onset of World War II. Later in life, Irén and Bálint marry, while Henriette Weld, who was shot by invading Nazis, occupies a shadowy afterlife and observes her friends as a ghost.
ANSWER: Katalin Street [or Katalin utca]
[10] This author of Katalin Street wrote a cheerier young adult adventure novel, Abigail, and a novel about a writer’s complex friendship with her unconventional housekeeper Emerence, The Door.
ANSWER: Magda Szabó (“SAH-bo”) [or Szabó Magda]
[10] Magda Szabó, like László Krasznahorkai (“KRASS-nuh-HOR-kye”) and Fatelessness author Imre Kertész (“KER-tayss”), wrote primarily in this European language.
ANSWER: Hungarian [or magyar nyelv]

B1 2023 PACE NSC (2023) Diff 5 · Literature
Answer the following about winners of the PEN Translation Prize, for 10 points each.
[10] In 1998, the PEN Translation Prize was awarded to Peter Constantine for his translation of Six Early Stories by this German author of The Magic Mountain.
ANSWER: Thomas Mann [or Paul Thomas Mann]
[10] The last winner of the PEN Prize for verse translation was Dennis Tedlock for his rendering of this text transcribed by Father Ximénez (“hih-MEN-eth”), which includes the creation myth of the Mayan people.
ANSWER: Popol Vuh [or Popol Wuj or Pop Wuj or Popul Vuh: The Mayan Book of the Dawn of Life]
[10] Other winners of the prize include Tim Wilkinson and Len Rix, who translated novels in this language titled Fatelessness and Katalin Street. Another novelist used this language for The Melancholy of Resistance and Sátántangó.
ANSWER: Hungarian [or magyar nyelv] (The original authors are respectively Imre Kertész, Magda Szabó, and László Krasnahorkai.)
