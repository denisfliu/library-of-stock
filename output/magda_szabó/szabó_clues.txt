[B1] 2023 PACE NSC (2023) | Diff: 5 | Literature
     Leadin: Answer the following about winners of the PEN Translation Prize, for 10 points each.
  Part 1 (ans: Thomas Mann [or Paul Thomas Mann]):
    In 1998, the PEN Translation Prize was awarded to Peter Constantine for his translation of Six Early Stories by this German author of The Magic Mountain.
  Part 2 (ans: Popol Vuh [or Popol Wuj or Pop Wuj or Popul Vuh: The Mayan Book of the Dawn of Life]):
    The last winner of the PEN Prize for verse translation was Dennis Tedlock for his rendering of this text transcribed by Father Ximenez ("hih-MEN-eth"), which includes the creation myth of the Mayan people.
  Part 3 (ans: Hungarian [or magyar nyelv] (The original authors are respectively Imre Kertesz, Magda Szabo, and Laszlo Krasnahorkai.)):
    Other winners of the prize include Tim Wilkinson and Len Rix, who translated novels in this language titled Fatelessness and Katalin Street. Another novelist used this language for The Melancholy of Resistance and Satantango.

[B2] 2023 MRNA (2023) | Diff: 7 | Literature
     Leadin: Much of the literature circulated in this manner was written on East German Erika typewriters, as the Soviet Union kept typographic samples of every individual domestic printing device. For 10 points each:
  Part 1 (ans: samizdat):
    Name this form of dissident literature in the Eastern bloc in which illegal manuscripts passed from person to person. Doctor Zhivago was the first full-length novel to circulate in this form.
  Part 2 (ans: The Master and Margarita [or Мастер и Маргарита]):
    This Mikhail Bulgakov novel circulated for decades as samizdat before a complete version was published in 1973. This novel, in which the devil visits Moscow, is the source of the quote "manuscripts don't burn."
  Part 3 (ans: Magda Szabo):
    This Hungarian author of Abigel spent the 1950s teaching at a girl's school after being labeled an enemy of the party. Bernard Rix's translation of this author's novel The Door was republished in 2015 to widespread acclaim.

[B3] 2022 Chicago Open (2022) | Diff: 10 | Literature
     Leadin: Zimbabwean translator Len Rix won the 2018 PEN Translation prize for his translation of this novel. For 10 points each:
  Part 1 (ans: Katalin Street [or Katalin utca]):
    Name this novel in which a group of children play the game Cherry Tree before the onset of World War II. Later in life, Iren and Balint marry, while Henriette Weld, who was shot by invading Nazis, occupies a shadowy afterlife and observes her friends as a ghost.
  Part 2 (ans: Magda Szabo ("SAH-bo") [or Szabo Magda]):
    This author of Katalin Street wrote a cheerier young adult adventure novel, Abigail, and a novel about a writer's complex friendship with her unconventional housekeeper Emerence, The Door.
  Part 3 (ans: Hungarian [or magyar nyelv]):
    Magda Szabo, like Laszlo Krasznahorkai ("KRASS-nuh-HOR-kye") and Fatelessness author Imre Kertesz ("KER-tayss"), wrote primarily in this European language.
