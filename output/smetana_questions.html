Tossups

T1 2024 PLAYTIME! (2024) Diff 7 · Fine Arts
In 2024, this composer’s 200th birthday was celebrated in an annual spring music festival that always begins on May 12th with his most famous orchestral work. A viola solo on a descending short-long-short motif over violin tremolos opens an E minor chamber piece by this composer that features a second movement polka. Another piece by this composer begins with two harps imitating the bard Lumír and uses the motif [read slowly] “B-flat, E-flat, D, B-flat” to depict a (*) “High Castle.” This non-Beethoven composer imitated his hearing loss with a sustained high E at the end of a string quartet that premiered with Dvořák as violist. Two flutes play swirling figures to represent a river in a set of six tone poems depicting the homeland of this composer of From My Life. For 10 points, name this Czech composer whose symphonic cycle Má vlast includes “The Moldau.”
ANSWER: Bedřich Smetana (The first line refers to the Prague Spring Festival. Moldau is the German name for the Vltava river.)
T2 2024 IQBT Undergraduate Championship (2024) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
In a tone poem by this composer, the G-minor opening section is followed by a fugato (“foo-GAH-toh”) in A minor and 3/4 time that begins with the muted first violins playing a descending D6, G-sharp5 tritone. In a tone poem by this composer, the key signature switches for the first time from one sharp to four flats for an episode in which a flute duet plays over high, muted strings, titled “Moonlight. Dance of the Nymphs.” Another tone poem by him opens with two harps, one strumming, one playing cadenzas, depicting a mythical (*) bard. A tone poem by him adapts the Italian melody “La Mantovana” (“lah mahn-toh-VAH-nah”), which was later used for the Israeli national anthem, and depicts a river flowing by a castle called Vyšehrad (“vee-SHEH-rahd”). For 10 points, name this Czech composer whose cycle of six tone poems, Má Vlast (“mah vlahst”), includes “The Moldau” (“MOLE-dow”).
ANSWER: Bedřich Smetana
T3 2023 Chicago Open (2023) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
This city is the epithet of a Breton choirmaster whose motets were incompletely edited by George Nugent. A Jewish violinist from this city wrote 33 polyphonic settings of Hebrew liturgical music, punningly titled “Songs of Solomon.” L’Ottuso Accademico defends a compositional style first used in this city in the book On the Imperfections of Modern Music. Samuel Cohen set a poem by Naftali Herz Imber to the tune of a madrigal named for this city. While working in this city, a composer was criticized by Giovanni (*) Artusi for a style of song in which lyrics govern music. “Hatikvah” and Ma Vlást separately adapted the tune of a madrigal named for this birthplace of seconda pratica. Before moving to Venice from this city, one composer wrote three of his nine madrigal books and the oldest regularly performed opera. For 10 points, name this city where the early phase of Claudio Monteverdi’s career was patronized by the Gonzagas.
ANSWER: Mantua [or Mantova; accept “La Mantovana” or “Il Ballo di Mantova” or “Mantuan Dance”; prompt on “Fuggi, Fuggi” by asking “what is that madrigal’s alternate title?”] (The first clue refers to Jacquet of Mantua. The second clue refers to Salomone Rossi. “Hatikvah” is not directly based on “Vltava;” Cohen used the Romanian adaptation of “La Mantovana,” “Carul cu boi,” while Smetana used its Czech adaptation, “Kočka leze dírou.”)
T4 2022 DECAF (2022) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
In a letter explicating this piece to its composer’s friend, the number of performers is said to be purposefully set to discuss “what so obviously troubles me.” This piece’s second movement contains a “solo quasi Tromba” on the C string, followed immediately by a transposition to the (+) G string. The third movement of this piece opens with an exposed cello solo descending from an F above the staff to a sequence of trills below the staff. After an opening sforzando staccato chord followed by eighth note oscillations, this piece introduces a melody consisting of descending repetitions of the rhythm pickup eighth note, half note, low quarter note in the solo (*) viola. In the second section of this piece’s final movement, harsh, quiet tremolos underlie a long harmonic high E in the first violin representing its composer’s deafness. For 10 points, name this E minor chamber piece by the composer of Má vlast.
ANSWER: Bedřich Smetana’s String Quartet No. 1 [or “From My Life” or „Z mého života” or “Aus meinem Leben” or JB 1:105 or T 106; accept Smetana’s String Quartet in E minor before the last line and prompt after]
T5 2018 ACF Regionals (2018) Diff 8 · Fine Arts

In a piece by this composer, the theme [read slowly] B, E, F-sharp, G, A, B, B appears after an opening in which two flutes play undulating E minor sixteenth note figures to represent waters; that piece appears in a cycle after one depicting the castle Vyšehrad (“VISH-eh-raht”). T6 2012 ACF Nationals (2012) Diff 9 · Fine Arts
This composer dedicated his piano piece “Faded Happiness,” the first of his six Reves, to a former student in the Thun family. In the second movement of this composer’s first string quartet, the viola is instructed to play a polka like a trumpet. He composed the concert etude On the Seashore, and wrote his Piano Concerto in G Minor to mourn the death of his daughter Bedriska. This composer wrote a string quartet in which a violin plays a long harmonic E after the rest of the quartet suddenly stops and plays a low tremolo in a section representing his loss of hearing. His symphonic poems include Sarka, as well as a piece which begins with two flutes representing streams that converge to the namesake river. For 10 points, name this Czech composer of the string quartet “From My Life,” who included his symphonic poems “Vysehrad” and “The Moldau” in Ma Vlast.
ANSWER: Bedrich Smetana

B1 2025 IQBT Undergraduate Championship (2025) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
Over the course of 1874, this composer went from full hearing to total deafness. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this Czech nationalist composer of The Bartered Bride. During his decade of deafness, he wrote his tone poem cycle Má vlast, which contains the “The Moldau.”
ANSWER: Bedřich Smetana
[10] Smetana depicted his deafness and subsequent tinnitus with a high E in the finale of this autobiographical chamber work.
ANSWER: String Quartet No. 1 in E minor [or “From My Life” quartet; or Z mého života]
[10] The last word of the tempo indication for the second movement of the “From My Life” quartet advertises that the movement is in this genre. Smetana wrote a set of three “poetic” pieces in this genre for solo piano.
ANSWER: polka [or polky; accept “Allegro moderato à la polka”; accept Three Poetic Polkas; accept Polkas poétiques; accept Poetické polky]
B2 2025 ACF Nationals (2025) Diff 9 · Fine Arts
After hearing this opera during its 1916 run in Prague, a Czech author went on to translate most of the composer’s operas into German, finally granting them international success. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this opera. The Kostelnička (“KOSS-tell-NEECH-kuh”) murders the baby of this opera’s title character, who marries Laca (“LAH-tsuh”) after he slashes her face with a knife to make her too ugly for Števa to marry.
ANSWER: Jenůfa (“yeh-NOO-fuh”) [or Její pastorkyňa; or Her Stepdaughter]
[10] Jenůfa was the first of many Janáček (“yuh-NAH-check”) operas translated by this Czech author, Janáček’s main advocate. He later wrote Die Musik Israels, the first comprehensive study of the music of Israel, where he settled after fleeing Prague in 1939.
ANSWER: Max Brod
[10] Brod praised this earlier Czech composer for mixing nationalism and cosmopolitanism. Brod translated this composer’s opera The Bartered Bride.
ANSWER: Bedřich Smetana
