T1 2024 Notes on the Underground (2024) Diff 10 · Literature
The feminine king of Thailand switches bodies with his masculine concubine in a story by this author that ponders whether souls are sexed. In a story by this author, a lecturer details his experiment introducing democracy to a colony of spiders. The narrator of another of this author’s stories has his wardrobe roasted by the ancient Greek Alcibadides after somehow summoning him to the present day while reading Plutarch. This author’s story “Life!” consists of a dialogue between Prometheus and the Wandering Jew. A composer of insipid polkas like “Fine Words Butter No Parsnips” dreams of being a composer of great serious works in this author’s story “Fame,” which is considered part of his early (*) Romantic period instead of his more widely read Fantastical period. An overzealous doctor created by this author commits nearly everyone in his town to an asylum in the novella The Alienist. The line “I have never quite understood a conversation that I had with a lady many years ago, when I was seventeen and she was thirty” opens this author’s story “Midnight Mass.” For 10 points, name this 19th-century author who set most of his stories in and around Rio de Janeiro.
ANSWER: Joaquim Maria Machado de Assis
