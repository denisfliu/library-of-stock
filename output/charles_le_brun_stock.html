Summary of Facts

Charles Le Brun was a 17th-century French painter who served as 'First Painter to the King' under Louis XIV and originated the 'Louis XIV style.' He designed and decorated the Hall of Mirrors at Versailles, with the central ceiling panel The King Governs Alone, and completed the Salons of War and Peace with Jules Hardouin-Mansart.

His History of Alexander series includes The Tent of Darius (queens of Persia groveling before Alexander) and Alexander and Porus. These works earned him the Chief Painter post. He founded the French Academy with Jean-Baptiste Colbert's support and headed the Gobelins tapestry manufactory. He decorated Vaux-le-Vicomte for Nicolas Fouquet. His Chancellor Séguier on Horseback shows servants holding blue parasols flanking the mounted patron.

Le Brun created physiognomy lectures with engravings of human faces distorted to resemble animals, which inspired the expressions in Copley's Watson and the Shark. He was a distant relative of Elisabeth Vigée Le Brun. His lost Sacrifice of Polyxena was found in the Coco Chanel suite at the Paris Ritz in 2013.
