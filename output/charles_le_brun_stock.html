Summary of Facts

Charles Le Brun (1619-1690) was a 17th-century French painter who served as 'First Painter to the King' under Louis XIV and originated the 'Louis XIV style.' He founded the French Academy (Academie Royale de Peinture et de Sculpture) with the support of Jean-Baptiste Colbert and led the Poussiniste faction in the Quarrel of the Ancients and Moderns, championing drawing over color against the Rubenistes led by Roger de Piles.

His most significant work was the decoration of the Hall of Mirrors at Versailles, where the central ceiling panel is The King Governs Alone and Crossing the Rhine also adorns the ceiling. He created over 30 works for the hall and completed the Salons of War and Peace with Jules Hardouin-Mansart. He also designed the Ambassador Staircase and decorated seven rooms named for Roman deities at Versailles. One ceiling painting shows Louis XIV carrying a massive golden tiller and gazing at his reflection in Minerva's shield.

His History of Alexander series, commissioned by Louis XIV, includes The Tent of Darius (also called The Family of Darius Before Alexander, hung in the Mars Room), where the queens of Persia grovel and Sisygambis mistakenly kneels to Hephaestion. Entry of Alexander into Babylon shows Alexander on a golden chariot pulled by elephants. Alexander and Porus depicts an angry shirtless Indian king carried before Alexander on a white horse. These works earned Le Brun the Chief Painter post.

Le Brun headed the Gobelins tapestry manufactory, reshaping it from the workshop of Comans and de la Planche. He decorated Vaux-le-Vicomte for Nicolas Fouquet. His Chancellor Seguier on Horseback shows servants holding blue parasols flanking the mounted patron. His physiognomy treatise Les expressions des passions de l'ame produced engravings of human faces distorted to resemble animals, which inspired the facial expressions in Copley's Watson and the Shark and Joshua Reynolds's portraits. He painted Louis XIV Visiting the Gobelins Factory. He was a distant relative of Elisabeth Vigee Le Brun. His lost Sacrifice of Polyxena was found in the Coco Chanel suite at the Paris Ritz in 2013.
