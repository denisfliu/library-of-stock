[BM1] 2023 British Novice (2023) | Diff: 6 | Fine Arts
     Leadin: Depictions of this figure in art are likely influenced by a Donatello statue of her which is one of the earliest surviving Renaissance sculptures to be made in the round. For 10 points each:
  Part 1 (ans: Judith):
    Name this figure depicted by artists like Lucas Cranach the Elder alongside the head of the Roman general Holofernes ("hollow-FERN-eez"), whom she decapitated in her namesake book of the Bible.
  Part 2 (ans: Artemisia Gentileschi):
    This Baroque painter's notoriously bloody depiction of Judith shows her in the act of beheading Holofernes, who this artist is thought to have depicted as her rapist, Agostino Tassi.
  Part 3 (ans: Caravaggio [or Michelangelo Merisi]):
    Gentileschi's depiction of Judith Beheading Holofernes draws heavily from a painting of the same scene by this other artist, one of her primary influences. This master of chiaroscuro ("kee-AHRO-skoor-oh") fled Rome for Naples to avoid a death sentence for killing a man in a street fight.

[BM2] 2023 ACF Fall (2023) | Diff: 6 | Fine Arts
     Leadin: While working in this artist's Nuremberg workshop, Hans Baldung is thought to have been given the nickname "Grien" for his profane depictions of witchcraft. For 10 points each:
  Part 1 (ans: Albrecht Durer):
    Name this German artist who included a shriveled, snake-infested horseman and goat-headed devil in his "master print" of a knight.
  Part 2 (ans: Erasmus [or Desiderius Erasmus Roterodamus; or Erasmus of Rotterdam]):
    Durer's Knight, Death and the Devil is sometimes interpreted as a response to a soldier's handbook by this author. Before moving to England, Hans Holbein the Younger painted an acclaimed portrait of this author.
  Part 3 (ans: the Bible [accept Septuagint or New Testament]):
    In another of his "master prints," Durer depicted the theologian St. Jerome translating this text into the Vulgate. Lucas Cranach the Elder illustrated Martin Luther's translation of this text in Wittenberg.
