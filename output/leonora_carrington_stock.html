Summary of Facts

Leonora Carrington was a British-born Surrealist painter and author who did most of her work in Mexico. Her self-portrait Inn of the Dawn Horse (1937–1938) shows her sitting in a blue chair reaching toward a lactating hyena, with a rocking horse on the wall and a white horse galloping through the window symbolizing her freedom. The Giantess (The Guardian of the Egg) is a tempera painting of a massive golden-haired woman with geese flying from her white cape — three small women in it may symbolize Carrington, Remedios Varo, and Kati Horna.

Her Portrait of Max Ernst depicts her lover in a frozen landscape wearing a red coat with a mermaid tail beside a white horse. How Doth the Little Crocodile, inspired by Lewis Carroll, exists as both a painting and bronze sculpture showing crocodiles in galaxies. The Magical World of the Maya is a mural for the National Museum of Anthropology.

Carrington suffered a mental breakdown in Spain after Ernst's arrest and was institutionalized, experiences she documented in the memoir Down Below. She wrote The Hearing Trumpet, a surrealist novel about Marian Leatherby using the title device to eavesdrop. She grew up at the Gothic mansion Crookhey Hall, calling her father 'Lord Candlestick.' In Mexico City, she was close friends with Remedios Varo and Kati Horna ('three witches'). She illustrated Elena Poniatowska's Lilus Kikus. The 2022 Venice Biennale's main exhibition 'The Milk of Dreams' honored her.
