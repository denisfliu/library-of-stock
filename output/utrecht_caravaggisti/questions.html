Questions: Utrecht Caravaggisti

1 tossup · 0 bonuses

Tossups

T1 2016 CLEAR (2016) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
One artist of this school painted a work in which a pale hand points down to a tool basket and a crown of thorns in the lower left corner, as three distraught women see him placed in a tomb; that work, The Deposition, is found in Rome’s San Pietro in Montorio. Another artist of this school painted a work in which the view of a woman’s candle is blocked by a guard’s arm, while the woman holds the robe of the title man; the use of small visible light sources, as in The Denial of St. Peter and Christ Before the High Priest earned that artist the name “Gherardo delle Notti”. This school was noted for depictions of prostitutes, including one of a smiling, bare-chested woman, grabbed by an old man, the Scene of Venal Love, and Dirck van Baburen’s The Procuress. Another member of this school painted several musicians, as in his Singing Lute Player or Flute Player. For 10 points, Gerrit van Honthorst and Hendrik ter Brugghen were members of what school named for a Dutch city and the painter of The Calling of St. Matthew.
ANSWER: Utrecht Caravaggisti [accept Dutch Caravaggisti before “a Dutch city”; prompt on “Utrecht School” or “Caravaggisti”]

Tossups

T1 2022 NASAT (2022) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
Elton John helped rediscover paintings of male nudes in this genre like Autumn Blue that were painted in Falmouth, Cornwall by the gay artist Henry Scott Tuke. A painting in this genre by Simon de Vlieger mirrors the composition of an earlier canvas since they both drew inspiration from a print by Maertan de Vos. Albert Pinkham Ryder's Constance is a painting in this genre of which Ivan Aivazovsky was a romantic master. The Utrecht School influenced Aelbert Cuyp's golden lighting in paintings in this genre like The Maas at Dordrecht. The only work in this genre by Rembrandt was stolen from the Isabella Stewart Gardner Museum in 1990. Poetry by Thomas Campbell accompanied a painting in this genre of an object "tugged to her last berth." For 10 points, name this genre of paintings like J.M.W. Turner's The Fighting Temeraire which depicts ships in the ocean.
ANSWER: seascape [or marine painting; or maritime painting; or naval painting; accept other clear equivalents; prompt on ship paintings; prompt on landscapes]
