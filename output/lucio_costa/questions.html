Answer some questions about lesser-known architects. For 10 points each:

ANSWER: FazlurSainzRahman Khan
[10] This other architect designed a museum using materials from the ruins of the Church of Sao Miguel Arcanjo. He may be better known for co-designing a city whose center contains the Monumental Axis.
ANSWER: Lucio Costa

B2 2018 IMSAnity (2018) Diff 5 · Fine Arts
Several architects worked extensively in Brazil. For 10 points each:
[10] Brazil’s largest city, Sao Paulo, was planned by this Swiss-born architect whose other South American designs include the Curutchet House. He is better known for houses like the Villa Savoye and the book Toward an Architecture.
ANSWER: Le Corbusier (or Charles-Édouard Jeanneret)
[10] Sao Paulo’s Edificio Copan residential complex was designed by this man, who incorporated several blue shells into his Church of Saint Francis of Assisi. His best-known city plan features buildings like the Palace of Dawn and a hyperboloid-shaped cathedral.
ANSWER: Oscar Niemeyer
[10] This other Brazilian architect designed Rio’s Gustavo Capanema Palace with Niemeyer and Roberto Burle Marx. While Niemeyer designed Brasilia’s civic buildings, this man developed a “pilot plan” to lay out the city.
ANSWER: Lucio Costa
B3 2012 Michigan KABO (2012) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
For 10 points each, name these people who were involved in the design of Brasilia:
[10] This man who submitted Plan 32 for the design of the U.N. Headquarters used sixteen hyperboloid columns that support a glass roof in his Cathedral for Brasilia, and he also designed the National Congress building there. One of his first projects was designing the suburb of Pampulha.
ANSWER: Oscar Niemeyer
[10] The layout of Brasilia in the shape of a cross or an airplane was thought of by this architect who used his design for the Parque Guinle for many of Brasilia’s apartment towers.
ANSWER: Lucio Costa
[10] This designer of the Plaza of the Three Powers in Brasilia is best known for orchestrating the city’s gardens and foliage patterns. He had worked as a landscape artist with Costa in his work on the Schwartz House.
ANSWER: Roberto Burle Marx
