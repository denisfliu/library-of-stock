German photographer (born 1955), student of Bernd and Hilla Becher. Known for mural-sized, large-format photographs often taken from high vantage points, extensively digitally edited to create repetitive patterns and surreal, expanded landscapes. His photograph Rhein II (a near-abstract image of the Rhine River between bands of grass and sky) is the most expensive photograph ever sold ($4.3 million in 2011). Also known for 99 Cent (and 99 Cent II Diptychon), showing aisles of a discount supermarket, and the Pyongyang series. Associated with the Düsseldorf School.

99 Cent and 99 Cent II Diptychon (1999, 2001)

The diptych was the fourth most expensive photograph ever sold. Both are mural-sized. Wikipedia

Stock Exchange Series and Other Works

Series of photographs depicting stock exchanges in Tokyo, Chicago, Hong Kong, Kuwait, New York, and other cities from aerial/high vantage points, using camera blur to capture movement. Chicago Board of Trade II is one example. Other works include: Bahrain I (Formula One racetrack in the desert); a photograph of the Super-Kamiokande neutrino observatory (showing golden spheres/phototubes); and a photograph of an Italian beach with hundreds of umbrellas grouped in colorful sections. General / Biographical

German photographer (born 1955 in Leipzig). Student of Bernd and Hilla Becher (the Düsseldorf School). Known for large-format, mural-sized photographs extensively digitally edited. Photographs often taken from high vantage points showing repetitive patterns and crowds. Other works include Politik II (showing 13 politicians in conversation under a clock from an Ed Ruscha painting), a hotel interior combining three-floor shots into a rippling yellow light pattern, and a photograph of an Italian beach. Summary of Facts

Andreas Gursky (born 1955) is a German photographer and student of Bernd and Hilla Becher, associated with the Düsseldorf School. He is known for large-format, mural-sized photographs extensively digitally edited to create repetitive patterns, surreal compositions, and expanded perspectives, often taken from high vantage points.

His most famous work, Rhein II (1999), is a near-abstract image of the Rhine River showing two bands of green grass separated by the river under a gray sky — factories were digitally removed from the horizon. It sold for $4.3 million in 2011, the most expensive photograph ever sold at auction. His 99 Cent (1999) shows the brightly colored, neatly organized shelves of a California discount supermarket, with the title word appearing on the wall alongside 'thanks,' 'only,' and 'nothing over.' The two-panel 99 Cent II Diptychon (2001) is a hyper-saturated version and was the fourth most expensive photograph ever sold.

Gursky's Pyongyang series (2007) documents the Arirang Festival in North Korea, showing thousands of matching cheerleaders with paneled depictions of flowers and doves behind them. He made a series of aerial photographs of stock exchanges (Tokyo, Chicago, Hong Kong, Kuwait, New York) using blur to capture movement; Chicago Board of Trade II is one example. His Bahrain I shows the Sakhir Grand Prix racetrack as a black tangle in the desert landscape. He also photographed the Super-Kamiokande neutrino observatory, showing golden spheres (phototubes) reflected in the water with two humans on boats. Politik II depicts 13 politicians in conversation under a clock from an Ed Ruscha painting.
