A 1978 musical by Andrew Lloyd Webber and Tim Rice based on the life of Eva Peron, the First Lady of Argentina. Features iconic songs including "Don't Cry for Me, Argentina" (sung from the balcony of the Casa Rosada), "Oh What a Circus" (sung by Che Guevara at her funeral, featuring the Salve Regina), "Rainbow Tour" (about her mixed reception visiting Spain and Italy), and "You Must Love Me" (written for the 1996 Madonna film adaptation). Patti LuPone won her first Best Actress Tony for the role. The musical has been compared to Here Lies Love (about Imelda Marcos) for its portrayal of a controversial female politician.
Songs and Scenes

The musical's major songs and their contexts as they appear in quizbowl clues. "Don't Cry for Me, Argentina" is the most iconic, sung from the balcony of the Casa Rosada with the line "the truth is I never left you." "Oh What a Circus" opens the show and is sung during Eva's funeral; it was originally performed by the character Che Guevara. "Rainbow Tour" describes Eva's visits to Spain and Italy, with men hiding behind raised newspapers singing about mixed results. "You Must Love Me" was written specifically for the 1996 film adaptation starring Madonna. Historical Context (Eva Peron)

Historical details about Eva Peron that frequently appear in clues about both the musical and the historical figure, providing useful context. Eva coined the term 'descamisados' (shirtless ones) for her husband Juan Peron's supporters. She secured women's suffrage in Argentina. She was encouraged to run for Vice President at the 'Cabildo Abierto' rally but declined. Her body was embalmed by Dr. Pedro Ara and secretly buried in Milan after the Revolucion Libertadora. She met Pope Pius XII during the Rainbow Tour and was pelted with tomatoes after meeting Franco.
