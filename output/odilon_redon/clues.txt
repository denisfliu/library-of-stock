[B1] 2016 Early Fall Tournament (EFT) (2016) | Diff: 7 | Fine Arts
     Leadin: This painter was one of the jurors in charge of awarding the Prix Blumenthal, a grant given to young up-and-coming French artists. For 10 points each:
  Part 1 (ans: Paul Signac [or Paul Victor Jules Signac]):
    Name this artist, who painted art critic Felix Feneon in front of a swirling background of different colors and patterns.
  Part 2 (ans: pointillism [prompt on Neo-Impressionism]):
    Signac was a practitioner of this technique, which uses smaller brushstrokes than the similar Divisionism. Georges Seurat utilized it in his A Sunday Afternoon on the Island of Le Grande Jatte.
  Part 3 (ans: Odilon Redon [or Bertrand-Jean Redon]):
    Along with Seurat and Signac, this French symbolist helped found the Salon des Independants. His works include Eye Balloon and a charcoal work depicting a giant floating head supported by a single wing, Guardian Spirit of the Waters.

[B2] 2015 ACF Nationals (2015) | Diff: 9 | Fine Arts
     Leadin: A critic called this artist the "Prince of Mysterious Dreams." For 10 points each:
  Part 1 (ans: Odilon Redon [or Bertrand-John Redon]):
    Name this painter of Japanese-influenced scenes, such as Vase with Japanese Warrior. In The Eye like a Strange Balloon Moves towards Infinity, from his lithographic series To Edgar Poe, he depicted an eyeball embedded in a hot-air balloon.
  Part 2 (ans: symbolism):
    Odilon Redon, along with Gustave Moreau and Fernand Khnopff, were members of this school of art, which emphasized depicting the dream world.
  Part 3 (ans: Rodolphe Bresdin 9):
    This draftsman and teacher of Odilon Redon was a major influence on the Symbolists. He often made scenes depicting tormented landscapes, such as The Comedy of Death, which features birds with rat heads and a terrified Jesus Christ fleeing away into the sky as skeletons menacingly laugh at him.
