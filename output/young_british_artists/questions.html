Questions: Young British Artists

5 tossups · 9 bonuses

Tossups

T1 2025 ACF Fall (2025) Diff 6 · Fine Arts
An artist from this country included 13 paintings of rhesus macaques in The Upper Room and used elephant dung in a controversial depiction of a bare-breasted Mary. The Chinese performance duo Mad for Real jumped on an artwork from this country consisting of a disheveled bed. One artist from this country covered a cast of a skull in diamonds in For the Love of God. Sculptors decorate a “fourth plinth” in this country near a column named for a naval admiral. A member of a group of “Young Artists” from this country has encased animals like cows and sharks in formaldehyde. For 10 points, Tracey Emin and Damien Hirst are from what country where art is displayed at the Tate Modern and the King’s Gallery at Buckingham?
ANSWER: United Kingdom [or UK; or Great Britain; or GB; or United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland; accept Young British Artists; accept England; reject “Scotland” or “Wales” or “Northern Ireland”] (The artist clued in the first line is Chris Ofili. The column is Nelson’s Column.)
T2 2023 IQBT Undergraduate Championship (2023) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
An artist from this movement created a series of food-themed self-portraits, including Eating a Banana and Self-Portrait with Fried Eggs. Another artist from this movement scrawled a warning in mirrored text about “speak[ing] in…sameness” over a nude self-portrait entitled Propped. Jay Jopling founded the “White Cube” gallery to host shows by artists from this movement. This movement’s (*) Sensation exhibition included an open tent into which the artist had stitched the names of her grandmother and boyfriends, among others. This movement includes the artist of My Bed and an artist who displayed a dead shark in formaldehyde. For 10 points, name this movement comprising Tracey Emin, Damien Hirst, and other contemporary provocateurs from the United Kingdom.
ANSWER: YBA [or Young British Artists]
T3 2023 Black History Month Packet (2023) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
One artist from this movement included his Modern Magic series among many African-inspired handcrafted quilts, sculptures, and paintings for his first solo exhibition, Restitution of the Mind and Soul. That artist from this movement created a colorful bookcase of American “activists,” and suspended a shoe made of Dutch wax with an invisible wire in two other works. Years after building the house of another artist from this movement, David Adjaye built a walnut-paneled room for an installation of 13 paintings by that artist depicting (*) rhesus macaque monkeys. Another artist from this movement created a large model of Nelson’s Ship in a Bottle for the Fourth Plinth of Trafalgar Square, while a more famous work by him depicts a woman wearing fake Chanel fabric in a headless version of The Swing that used Ankara fabric. An artist from this movement who created The Upper Room and No Woman No Cry was attacked by Rudy Giuliani for using elephant dung in a depiction of a Black Madonna. For 10 points, name this art movement whose members include Yinka Shonibare and Chris Ofili, the latter of whom feuded with Stuckists following a scandal involving the Tate Gallery.
ANSWER: Young British Artists [or YBA; accept Brit artists]
T4 2019 ILLIAC (2019) Diff 6 · Fine Arts
An artist from this country painted a black-and-white checkerboard pattern collapsing towards the center in Movement in Squares. Another artist from this country placed 102 names on the inside of a blue tent. Billy Childish created the Stuckist movement to oppose one artistic movement from this country. One artist from this country put (*) her bed on display and created the tent artwork Everyone I Have Ever Slept With. Another artist from this country placed a shark in formaldehyde in The Physical Impossibility of Death in the Mind of Someone Living. Tracey Emin and Damien Hirst are from, for 10 points, what country home to the Tate Gallery in London?
ANSWER: United Kingdom [accept England or Britain; accept Young British Artists] (Movement in Squares is by Bridget Riley)
T5 2013 VCU Closed (2013) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
One member of this art movement painted two stacked red circles on a pink background in his work Snowman. Another member of this movement had a work where ten blue-overalled workers and large grinding machines destroyed every one of his possessions in a pseudo-assembly line process. In addition to Gary Hume and Michael Landy, this art movement had an artist with a work that contained close-ups of female genitalia collaged with (*) elephant dung in his The Holy Virgin Mary, which famously offended Rudy Guiliani. Another work from this movement was jumped on by the performance artists Yuan Chai and Jian Jun X and contained bloody underwear and used condoms. For 10 points, name this art movement whose notable works include Tracy Emin's My Bed and Damien Hirst's The Physical Impossibility of Death in the Mind of Someone Living.
ANSWER: Young British Artists [or YBA; prompt on Britart; prompt on Brit artists; prompt on Young Brits]

Bonuses

B1 2025 PACE NSC (2025) Diff 5 · Fine Arts
Fiona Rae and Sarah Lucas were included in this movement's 1988 exhibit Freeze. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this art movement opposed by Billy Childish, a painter whose name appears appliquéd onto a blue tent in an artwork depicting everyone an artist from this movement had slept with.
ANSWER: Young British Artists [or YBAs; or Britartists]
[10] Several YBA artists met at the Freeze exhibition curated by this artist, who began burning thousands of his polka dot paintings in 2022 in an NFT project. This artist preserved a shark in formaldehyde.
ANSWER: Damien Hirst [or Damien Steven Hirst]
[10] The YBA Jenny Saville is known for her large depictions of obese women in this genre. The Guerilla Girls protested paintings at the Met in this genre, which overwhelmingly depict women without any clothes.
ANSWER: nudes [accept nude paintings or nude portraits; prompt on paintings of naked people or paintings of unclothed people; prompt on portraits]
B2 2025 NAFTA (2025) Diff 9 · Fine Arts
Answer the following about Tish Murtha, who photographed working class people in Newcastle from the late 1970s until her death in 2013, for 10 points each.
[10] Murtha’s best-known series depicts, and is titled for, the “Unemployment” of people with this trait. Tracey Emin and Damien Hirst belonged to a group of “British Artists” named for this trait that arose in the 1980s.
ANSWER: young [or youthful; accept Young British Artists]
[10] In a photo by Murtha featuring the puppet Randolf, a child does this action as others watch outside a building. The photo Behind the Gare Saint-Lazare captures the “decisive moment” of a man doing this action.
ANSWER: jumping [accept jumping out a window or jumping into a pool/puddle; accept leaping]
[10] Murtha also published a collection depicting “Juvenile” groups named for this activity. Art Kane captured 57 practitioners of this activity in a photograph partly titled for a “Great Day.”
ANSWER: jazz music [accept Juvenile Jazz Bands; prompt on playing music or marching bands or performing] (Kane’s photograph is A Great Day in Harlem.)
B3 2023 Penn Bowl (2023) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
Four members of this movement, Andre Breton, Jacques Herolt, Yves Tanguy, and Victor Brauner created a drawing of a woman's face joined with two goosenecks, a fist, and a naked butt. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this movement, whose members played exquisite corpse, in which each person draws on a paper with the others’ parts hidden. Breton called this movement “pure psychic automatism.”
ANSWER: surrealism
[10] This artist created a series of untitled paintings created exquisite corpse-like for his 2022 exhibit Exquisite Corpse. He painted a Black man with a gap-toothed smile in Portrait of the Artist as a Shadow of his Former Self.
ANSWER: Kerry James Marshall
[10] Two members of this movement, the Chapman Brothers, each drew ten heads and ten torsos for their Exquisite Corpse series. An artist from this movement stitched the names of everyone she slept with on a tent.
ANSWER: Young British Artists [or YBA]
