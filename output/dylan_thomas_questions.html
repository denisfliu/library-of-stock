T1 2025 LITANY (2025) Diff 8 · Literature
The speaker uses this adjective to describe fire after declaring, “it was air and playing, lovely and watery” in a poem that later applies this adjective and “whinnying” to a stable from which “spellbound horses” walk “on to the fields of praise.” This is the only adjective in the lengthy title of an entry in 18 Poems whose stanzas each end with a statement beginning, “And I am dumb to tell...” This word is applied to a (*) bay where good men’s “frail deeds might have danced.” In the last entry in Deaths and Entrances, this adjective and “carefree” describe a speaker who was “famous among the barns”; later, this word and “dying” describe how “Time held me” at the end of “Fern Hill.” For 10 points, Dylan Thomas often used what color for its traditional associations with youth and nature?
ANSWER: green [reject putative synonyms; specifically, accept “fire green as grass” or “whinnying green stable” or “The force that through the green fuse drives the flower” or “green bay” or “green and carefree” or “green and dying”] (all clues are from Dylan Thomas poems)
