British-Nigerian artist and member of the Young British Artists (YBAs). Best known for incorporating elephant dung into his mixed-media paintings, most notoriously The Holy Virgin Mary (1996), which sparked a controversy with Rudy Giuliani when exhibited at Brooklyn Museum's Sensation show. Won the Turner Prize in 1998, becoming the first Black artist to do so. Lives and works in Port of Spain, Trinidad. Other major works include No Woman No Cry (a tribute to Stephen Lawrence) and The Upper Room (an installation of 13 paintings of rhesus macaques representing the Last Supper).
The Holy Virgin Mary (1996)

Mixed-media painting of a Black Madonna on a gold background, incorporating elephant dung (forming one breast and used as supports) and collaged images of buttocks cut from pornographic magazines, which serve as cherubs. Exhibited at the Sensation show at the Brooklyn Museum in 1999, where it drew fierce condemnation from Rudy Giuliani and was vandalized by Dennis Heiner, who smeared white paint on it. Wikipedia

No Woman No Cry (1998)

A tribute to Stephen Lawrence, a Black British teenager murdered in a racially motivated crime. Depicts Doreen Lawrence (Stephen's mother) as a weeping woman in a red dress, with a pendant made of elephant dung. Collaged images of Stephen Lawrence appear in the painting. Title borrowed from Bob Marley. Described as a 'modern Pieta.' Wikipedia

Summary of Facts

Chris Ofili is a British-Nigerian painter and mixed-media artist born in Manchester in 1968. He is a prominent member of the Young British Artists (YBAs) and is best known for incorporating elephant dung into his paintings, which he collects from the London Zoo and uses both as a material within the canvas and as physical supports for his works.

Ofili's most famous and controversial work is The Holy Virgin Mary (1996), a mixed-media depiction of a Black Madonna with one breast formed from elephant dung and surrounded by collaged images of buttocks from pornographic magazines standing in for cherubs. When exhibited at the Sensation show at the Brooklyn Museum in 1999, New York Mayor Rudy Giuliani denounced it as blasphemous and threatened to cut the museum's funding. Dennis Heiner vandalized the painting by smearing white paint on it.

His painting No Woman No Cry (1998), named after the Bob Marley song, is a tribute to Stephen Lawrence, a Black British teenager murdered in a racially motivated attack. It depicts Lawrence's mother Doreen as a weeping woman in a red dress, with collaged images of Stephen visible in the painting and a necklace pendant made of elephant dung. It has been described as a 'modern Pieta.'

The Upper Room (1999-2002) is an installation of 13 paintings of rhesus macaque monkeys representing Jesus and his 12 apostles at the Last Supper. Each painting incorporates elephant dung. The installation is displayed in a walnut-paneled room designed by architect David Adjaye, and its purchase by the Tate Gallery was controversial because Ofili served on the Tate's Board of Trustees at the time.

Ofili won the Turner Prize in 1998, becoming the first Black artist to do so. He developed his distinctive painting style while studying cave paintings in Zimbabwe. He now lives and works in Port of Spain, Trinidad, where he moved to join his friend, the painter Peter Doig. His Trinidad period has been marked by 'blue' studies inspired by the Jab Jab participants of the Trinidad and Tobago Carnival. Other works include the Captain Shit superhero series, the sculpture Shithead, the Afro Muses watercolor series, a reworking of the Union Jack in Pan-African colors, and the Requiem mural at Tate Britain commemorating the Grenfell Tower fire.
