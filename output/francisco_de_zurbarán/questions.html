T1 2024 NASAT (2024) Diff 8 · Fine Arts
An artist from this country painted Bruno of Cologne witnessing meat turning into ash in front of Carthusian monks. Another painter from this country depicted a bearded woman breastfeeding her baby next to a stone slab. Strings suspending food in windowsills appear in a genre of paintings from this country such as Quince, Cabbage, Melon and Cucumber. Another artist from this country painted The Young Beggar and Children Eating Grapes and a Melon. Cupid holds a pink ribbon and a mirror in front of the title goddess in a painting from this country. A girl steps on a dog on the left of a group portrait from this country around the Infanta Margarita. The Rokeby Venus and Las Meninas were painted in, for 10 points, what home country of Diego Velázquez?
ANSWER: Spain [The opening clues refer to Francisco de Zurbarán, Jusepe de Ribera, bodegón paintings by Juan Sánchez Cotán, and Bartolomé Esteban Murillo, respectively.]
T2 2021 NASAT (2021) Diff 8 · Fine Arts

A painter from this country depicted a pure white lamb with its feet bound in Agnus Dei and painted a saint wearing plain white robes while chained to a barely-visible tree trunk in The Martyrdom of Saint Serapion. An artist from this country reused the model from his Christ in the House of Martha and Mary for a painting of an old woman frying eggs. That artist from this country showed Cupid holding a mirror to the title Rokeby Venus. For 10 points, name this home country of Diego Velazquez.
ANSWER: Spain [accept Kingdom of Spain or Reino de España] (the other unmentioned painters are Jusepe de Ribera and Francisco de Zurbarán, respectively)
T3 2013 Chicago Open (2013) Diff 10 · Fine Arts

This artist also painted a hooded monk with ripped robe clutching an upside-down (*) skull up to his chest in a painting of a saint “in meditation.” The title saint of a painting by this artist wears a badge of the Mercedarians and slumps his head to the right as he dies with both of his arms tied to a tree by ropes. B5 2020 Longhorn Invitational Tournament (2020) Diff 7 · Fine Arts
The introduction of this type of work to Spanish painting is credited to the Toledo-born painter Blas de Prado after his travels to the Court of Morocco. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this type of work. Austere, realistic works of this type in their bodegón genre were created by de Prado’s student, Juan Sanchez Cotán, and included common pantry items among other elements like fruits and bowls.
ANSWER: still-life
[10] This later Spanish painter created many still-lives influenced by the earlier works of Cotán. The title Mercedarian Saint slumps his head and dies with arms bound to a tree by ropes in his Martyrdom of Saint Serapion.

B6 2018 ACF Nationals (2018) Diff 9 · Fine Arts

ANSWER: Breda [accept Siege of Breda or The Surrender of Breda or La rendición de Breda]
