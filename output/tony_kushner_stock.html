American playwright best known for Angels in America: A Gay Fantasia on National Themes, a two-part play (Millennium Approaches and Perestroika) about the AIDS crisis in 1980s New York. Also wrote A Bright Room Called Day, Homebody/Kabul, and The Intelligent Homosexual's Guide to Capitalism and Socialism with a Key to the Scriptures. Wrote screenplays for Lincoln and West Side Story (2021).
Angels in America

A two-part 'Gay Fantasia on National Themes' set in 1980s New York City, split into Millennium Approaches and Perestroika. Louis Ironson abandons his AIDS-stricken lover Prior Walter for Joe Pitt, a closeted Mormon. Prior has visions of angels who address him as 'prophet' and deliver the message to 'stop moving,' which was supposedly delivered after the 1906 San Francisco earthquake. The angel explains that mankind's progression has caused heaven to deteriorate and that God abandoned heaven on the day of that earthquake. Roy Cohn, a power broker who claims labels reflect one's 'clout,' wishes to be reborn as an octopus and offers Joe a job in the Justice Department. The ghost of Ethel Rosenberg visits Cohn on his deathbed and sings him a lullaby as he dies of AIDS. Louis says a Kaddish over Cohn in Perestroika. Joe's wife Harper is addicted to Valium and hallucinates visits to Antarctica with her imaginary travel agent Mr. Lies. The same actor portrays Mr. Lies and Belize (a nurse), and another actor portrays both Joe's mother Hannah and Rabbi Chemelwitz. A speech criticizing the 'Beautiful Theory' asks about 'the Great Question before us' and is delivered by the world's oldest living Bolshevik. Two of Prior's ancestors describe dying in different British plagues.

The Intelligent Homosexual's Guide

A play whose title blends the titles of books by George Bernard Shaw and Mary Baker Eddy, often shortened to iHo. It centers on the suicidal communist and retired longshoreman Gus Marcantonio, whose children Pill, Empty, and V try to convince him not to commit suicide. Set in New York City.

Other Works and General

Kushner wrote Homebody/Kabul, in which a woman called Homebody dies in Kabul and a woman fantasizes about sleeping with a hat salesman missing three fingers. He wrote screenplays for Lincoln and the 2021 West Side Story, and adapted the Czech opera Brundibar into a children's book illustrated by Maurice Sendak. He collaborated with Eric Bogosian on Love's Fires (dramatic sketches based on Shakespeare's sonnets). He is one of the only playwrights nominated for an EGOT. He also wrote the afterword to Moises Kaufman's Gross Indecency about Oscar Wilde's trials, and collaborated with Ariel Dorfman on a theatrical version of Widows.

Summary of Facts

Tony Kushner is an American playwright best known for Angels in America: A Gay Fantasia on National Themes, a two-part play split into Millennium Approaches and Perestroika, set in 1980s New York City. Louis Ironson abandons his AIDS-stricken lover Prior Walter, who is visited by angels addressing him as 'prophet.' The angels deliver the message to 'stop moving,' supposedly sent after the 1906 San Francisco earthquake, when God abandoned heaven. Prior's ancestors describe dying in different British plagues. The power broker Roy Cohn declares 'I wish I was an octopus,' claims labels reflect 'clout,' and offers a Justice Department job to the closeted Mormon Joe Pitt. The ghost of Ethel Rosenberg visits Cohn on his deathbed, singing him a lullaby as he dies of AIDS, and Louis says a Kaddish over him. Joe's wife Harper is addicted to Valium and hallucinates visits to Antarctica with the imaginary travel agent Mr. Lies. Stage directions require the same actor to portray Mr. Lies and the nurse Belize, and another to portray Hannah and Rabbi Chemelwitz. The world's oldest living Bolshevik delivers a speech criticizing the 'Beautiful Theory.'

A Bright Room Called Day is set in Agnes Eggling's apartment and cuts between the fall of the Weimar Republic and 1980s New York. In a revision, Xillah with an 'X' only interacts with Zillah with a 'Z.'

The Intelligent Homosexual's Guide to Capitalism and Socialism with a Key to the Scriptures (often shortened to iHo) blends titles by G.B. Shaw and Mary Baker Eddy. It centers on the suicidal communist and retired longshoreman Gus Marcantonio, whose children are named Pill, Empty, and V.

Kushner also wrote Homebody/Kabul, in which a woman dies in Kabul and another fantasizes about a hat salesman missing three fingers. He wrote screenplays for Lincoln and the 2021 West Side Story, adapted the Czech opera Brundibar with Maurice Sendak, and collaborated with Eric Bogosian on Love's Fires. He wrote the afterword to Moises Kaufman's Gross Indecency about Oscar Wilde's trials.
