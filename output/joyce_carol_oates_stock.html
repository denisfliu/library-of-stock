Also wrote We Were the Mulvaneys, Bellefleur, Black Water, Foxfire, and A Garden of Earthly Delights. Notorious for her controversial tweets.
"Where Are You Going, Where Have You Been?"

Oates's most anthologized short story, dedicated to Bob Dylan and inspired by a series of killings in Tucson, Arizona. Fifteen-year-old Connie is visited at her home by the devilish Arnold Friend, who drives a gold jalopy painted with phrases like 'DONE BY CRAZY WOMAN DRIVER' and 'MAN THE FLYING SAUCERS,' with the numbers 33, 19, and 17 inscribed on it (a 'secret code' never explained). Arnold tells Connie 'Gonna get you, baby' at a burger joint before returning in his car. He says 'This place you are now — inside your daddy's house — is nothing but a cardboard box I can knock down any time.' After listening to 'It's All Over Now, Baby Blue,' Oates was inspired to write the story. Adapted into the 1985 film Smooth Talk (Joyce Chopra directed; Laura Dern and Treat Williams starred).

Blonde

A historical fiction novel fictionalizing the life of Marilyn Monroe. Oates replaces real names with designations: 'R.F.' orders a hit on the protagonist (a Kennedy fictionalization), and ex-husbands are called 'The Ex-Athlete' and 'Playwright.' The protagonist's fetus talks to her near a rose bush in the film adaptation. Adapted into a graphic 2022 biopic starring Ana de Armas, directed by Andrew Dominik. Oates wrote 'Norma Jean is me' in a journal while working on it.

General / Biographical

Joyce Carol Oates is an extraordinarily prolific American author (63 novels, 47 short story collections as of 2025) and professor at Princeton. She is notorious for controversial tweets, including comments on ISIS ('is there nothing celebratory & joyous?'), criticisms of Spielberg's The Fabelmans, a single-sentence work about cat food in China, and Halloween decoration hot takes. She wrote a version of Chekhov's 'The Lady with the Pet Dog' from Anna Sergeyevna's perspective. She is a two-time O. Henry Award winner. The short story 'Landfill' was inspired by the death of John Fiocco. Wild Nights! fictionalized the last years of Poe, Dickinson, Twain, James, and Hemingway. What I Lived For takes its title from Thoreau's Walden. The Accursed is a gothic novel about vampires in Princeton.

Summary of Facts

Joyce Carol Oates is one of the most prolific American authors of the 20th and 21st centuries, with 63 novels and 47 short story collections. She teaches at Princeton and is notorious for controversial tweets about topics ranging from ISIS to Spielberg's The Fabelmans to Halloween decorations.

Her most anthologized work is the short story 'Where Are You Going, Where Have You Been?' about fifteen-year-old Connie, who is visited by the devilish Arnold Friend in his gold jalopy painted with 'DONE BY CRAZY WOMAN DRIVER' and 'MAN THE FLYING SAUCERS,' with the unexplained numbers 33, 19, and 17. The story was dedicated to Bob Dylan and inspired by the Tucson, Arizona serial killings. Arnold whispers 'Gonna get you, baby' at a burger joint before coercing Connie into his car. It was adapted into the 1985 film Smooth Talk with Laura Dern.

Blonde fictionalizes Marilyn Monroe's life using designations like 'The Ex-Athlete,' 'Playwright,' and 'R.F.' (a Kennedy). It was adapted into a 2022 film starring Ana de Armas. Oates's Wonderland Quartet of novels set in Eden County includes A Garden of Earthly Delights (Clara Walpole rising from poverty, titled after the Bosch triptych), Expensive People, them (the Wendall family in Detroit; Jules commits murder during the Detroit Race Riot), and Wonderland (Trick Monk befriends Jesse Vogel).

We Were the Mulvaneys depicts a family torn apart by Marianne's sexual assault. Bellefleur is a magical realist gothic novel in which Gideon crashes a bomb-laden plane into the family mansion. Black Water mirrors Chappaquiddick with 'The Senator' killing Kelly Kelleher. Foxfire follows Legs Sadovsky and the Wirtz sisters forming a female gang. Oates also wrote a version of Chekhov's 'The Lady with the Pet Dog' from Anna Sergeyevna's perspective, the collection Wild Nights!, and the Princeton gothic novel The Accursed.
