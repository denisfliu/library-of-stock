Production and Context

Directed by Vittorio de Sica, written by Cesare Zavattini. A masterpiece of Italian neorealism. The bicycle is a 'Fides' brand. An anti-commercialist parody by comedian Maurizio Nichetti is based on this film (a 1989 parody in which frequent commercial breaks and a power outage disrupt a director's reinterpretation). Influenced Indian Parallel Cinema, including Satyajit Ray's Apu Trilogy and Bimal Roy's Do Bigha Zamin.

Summary of Facts

Bicycle Thieves (Ladri di biciclette, 1948) is a landmark Italian neorealist film directed by Vittorio de Sica with a screenplay by Cesare Zavattini. The film follows Antonio Ricci, a poor Roman laborer who is offered work posting film posters — including of Rita Hayworth — on the condition that he has a bicycle. His wife Maria pawns her dowry bedsheets to redeem Antonio's bicycle from a pawn shop. The very next day, while Antonio is up a ladder posting bills, his bicycle (a 'Fides' brand) is stolen. Antonio and his young son Bruno spend the rest of the film searching Rome.

During a rainstorm, Antonio tails a suspicious old man to a church serving lunch. A suspect later fakes a seizure to avoid arrest. A fortune teller tells Antonio he will find the bicycle either quickly or not at all. A restaurant scene memorably intercuts between the poor father-son duo discussing their plight and a wealthy family eating lavishly. At the end of the film, Antonio is reduced to attempting to steal an unattended bicycle himself — in an inversion of the opening dynamic — and is caught and humiliated before Bruno. The two walk off into the crowd crying.

The film is widely called one of the greatest ever made and a masterpiece of Italian neorealism. De Sica's other key neorealist films include Sciuscia and Umberto D. A 1989 anti-commercialist parody by comedian Maurizio Nichetti (in which commercial breaks and a power outage disrupt a director's reinterpretation) is based on this film. Bicycle Thieves also influenced the Indian Parallel Cinema movement, including Satyajit Ray's Apu Trilogy and Bimal Roy's Do Bigha Zamin.
