     Leadin: In a short monograph on this photograph for Afterall Books's One Work series, David Campany argues that it was a product of its artist's move towards the idea of a singular, rather than serial, picture. For 10 points each:
  Part 1 (ans: Picture for Women):

    Jeff Wall's Picture for Women was chiefly inspired by a painting by this artist in which a bartender faces the viewers and places her hands next to a dish of oranges.
  Part 3 (ans: The Death of Sardanapalus ("sar-duh-NAP-puh-luss")):

     Leadin: Answer the following about the artistic inspirations for Jeff Wall's photographs, for 10 points each.
  Part 1 (ans: The Death of Sardanapalus [or La Mort de Sardanapale] (by Eugene Delacroix)):
    In a Wall photograph (emphasize) inspired by this painting, a woman's destroyed room contains a dresser with the drawers pulled out and an overturned mattress. Sculptural depictions of elephant heads protrude from the corners of the central object in this 19th-century painting.
  Part 2 (ans: A Bar at the Folies-Bergere [or Un bar aux Folies Bergere] (by Edouard Manet)):
    A man and woman stand in front of a mirror in Wall's Picture for Women, which was inspired by this painting. A bowl of oranges sits in the foreground of this painting, which depicts a prostitute wearing a pendant on a black necklace reflected by a mirror.
  Part 3 (ans: Hokusai [or Katsushika Hokusai]):
    Wall's photograph A Sudden Gust of Wind was inspired by this Japanese artist, who painted Thirty-Six Views of Mount Fuji.
