Soviet-Ukrainian composer and pianist (1937–2020) with a 21st-century cult following. Best known for writing classical music — especially piano sonatas, suites, etudes, and preludes — that infuses classical forms with written-out jazz idioms influenced by Art Tatum and Bill Evans. His 20 piano sonatas combine classical structure with jazz improvisation techniques. His Eight Concert Etudes (Op. 40) include a driving Toccatina in E minor as the third etude. He also wrote 24 preludes 'in jazz style' and a Suite 'in the Old Style' modeled after Bach. His Opus 41 Variations gives a swing treatment to The Rite of Spring's opening bassoon theme. Opus 26 is a variation for piano and orchestra named for sunrise.
General / Biographical

Soviet-Ukrainian composer and pianist (1937–2020). Infused classical forms with written-out jazz improvisation techniques inspired by Art Tatum and Bill Evans. Despite writing prolifically, he was virtually unknown in the West until the 21st century, when a cult following developed. Most of his compositional output was for piano. He wrote 20 piano sonatas, Eight Concert Etudes (Op. 40), 24 Preludes 'in jazz style,' a Suite 'in the Old Style,' and various concertos and character pieces.

Summary of Facts

Nikolai Kapustin (1937–2020) was a Soviet-Ukrainian composer and pianist who developed a significant 21st-century cult following despite being largely unknown in the West during his lifetime. His music is characterized by the fusion of classical forms with written-out jazz improvisation techniques — he did not improvise in performance but notated jazz-inflected passages in full, inspired particularly by jazz pianists Art Tatum and Bill Evans. Most of his compositional output was for piano, including 20 piano sonatas. His Eight Concert Etudes (Op. 40) include a driving Toccatina in E minor as the third etude. His Op. 41 Variations gives a swing treatment to The Rite of Spring's opening bassoon theme. He wrote 24 Preludes 'in jazz style' and a Suite 'in the Old Style' modeled after Bach's orchestral suites. His Opus 26 Variations for piano and orchestra is named for sunrise.
