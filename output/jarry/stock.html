Summary of Facts

Alfred Jarry was a French absurdist playwright and author, widely considered a forefather of Absurdism. He is overwhelmingly identified through his play Ubu Roi, a scatological send-up of Macbeth in which the cowardly title character usurps the throne of Poland and Goes to war with Russia. The play's premiere caused an uproar when the title character exclaimed "merde!" (with an extra R), leading Jarry to later stage it with marionettes. The title character frequently swears "by my green candle." W.B. Yeats, who attended the premiere, lamented "after us, the Savage God." Paul Sérusier and Pierre Bonnard helped design the set. The play ends with the title character fleeing to France to escape Bougrelas and the Tsar's army. Bougrelas and Bordure avenge the death of King Wenceslas of Poland by deposing the title character. Sequels titled Ubu Cuckolded and Ubu in Chains followed. Rachilde, a French Decadent author, was a close friend of Jarry and helped establish the myth about the riotous opening night.

Jarry also created a significant body of visual art. A woodcut by Jarry depicts one of his characters (Ubu) in a dunce cap with a spiral affixed to his bloated stomach. The Beraldi Album includes his sketch of two men clapping before a burning house. His work influenced several paintings: a painting reimagines Ubu as a monstrous red-bricked spinning top with a green scarf, titled with the word "imperator"; he influenced the inclusion of a doctor's peephole in the painting The Addition and of a wispy crown adorned with "IMRA" in Portrait of Madame B; a photograph inspired by Jarry depicting a close-up of an armadillo fetus was captured by Dora Maar; and a painting inspired by him features a thin metal scythe balancing on its tip in Sand.

Jarry invented the "science of imaginary solutions" called 'pataphysics, which he described as a field that "explains the universe supplementary to this one." His picaresque novel Exploits and Opinions of Dr. Faustroll features the laughing baboon Bosse-de-Nage as companion to a doctor of 'pataphysics. He also wrote The Passion Considered as an Uphill Bicycle Race.
