Orson Welles directed a 1974 documentary interviewing a Hungarian artist who created works with this characteristic. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this characteristic of paintings like The Supper at Emmaus by Han van Meegeren. Elmyr de Hory created artworks with this characteristic.
ANSWER: forgeries [or F for Fake; or answers indicating fake art]
[10] De Hory's forgeries first came to light after he attempted to sell an alleged painting by this artist to the Fogg Museum. This Fauvist artist's legitimate works include The Joy of Life and The Dance.
ANSWER: Henri Matisse [Henri Émile Benoît Matisse]

B3 2023 THERAPY (2023) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
The central figure in this work has a “24” embedded on his collar. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this artwork, representative of its artist’s distilled Crystal Cubism era. Its central poilu is depicted playing the title game against a blue background.
ANSWER: Soldier at a Game of Chess [by Jean Metzinger]
[10] Jean Metzinger collaborated with this artist and philosopher on the first theoretical treatise on Cubism. This artist’s La Chasse depicts a man with a golden hunting horn, with a group of hunting dogs to his right.
ANSWER: Albert Gleizes
[10] Metzinger was a co-founder of this artist collective associated with Cubism and Orphism. Josephin Peladan coined the primary name for this group due to his fascination with the quantity phi.
ANSWER: Section d’Or [or the Puteaux Group]
B4 2019 Chicago Open (2019) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
The Cézanne retrospective at the 1907 iteration of this exhibition helped to inspire Analytical Cubism. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this annual art exhibition spearheaded by Frantz Jourdain in 1903. The term “Fauvism” was coined in response to its 1905 iteration.
ANSWER: Société du Salon d’Automne [or Autumn Salon; do not accept or prompt on partial answer]
[10] In 1908, the Salon d’Automne infamously rejected this man’s painting Houses at l’Estaque, which led Louis Vauxcelles [voh-SELL] to coin the term “Cubism.”
ANSWER: Georges Braque [brahk]
[10] This artist chronicled the fortunes of the Salon d’Automne in his The Epic: From Immobile Form to Mobile Form. This painter of Portrait of Jacques Nayral co-wrote the manifesto Du “Cubisme”.
ANSWER: Albert Gleizes [glehz] (The other co-author was Jean Metzinger.)
B5 2017 Chicago Open (2017) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
This artwork’s background depicts red mountains behind a lake that has a white sailboat in it. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this proto-cubist painting showing three title birds--including a pink flamingo--around a pond. It was painted by Jean Metzinger.
ANSWER: Paysage coloré aux oiseaux aquatiques [or Colored Landscape with Aquatic Birds]
[10] Jean Metzinger may be better known for co-authoring On Cubism with this other painter. This advocate of “crystal cubism” painted works like The Hunt.
ANSWER: Albert Gleizes
[10] Before Metzinger’s cubist period, he went through a divisionist phase, which is a form of this style with larger brush strokes. This branch of neo-impressionism was used in an 1884 painting depicting a monkey on a leash.
ANSWER: pointillism [accept word forms]
B6 2015 VCU Open (2015) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
This artist co-wrote a manifesto of Cubism with Jean Metzinger. For 10 points each:
[10] Name this painter of a portrait of Jacques Nayral, the almost entirely grey Woman with Phlox, and a painting called La Chasse, or The Hunt.
ANSWER: Albert Gleizes
[10] Gleizes’ painting The Bathers was exhibited at the 1912 edition of this salon, founded in 1884 in opposition to the government-sponsored Salon. In 1910, a painting by a donkey named Lolo was exhibited here.
ANSWER: Salon des Indépendants [or: Société des Artistes Indépendants, Salon/Society of Independent Artists]
[10] This artist’s Bathers at Asnières was exhibited at the first Salon des Indépendants. This pointillist also painted A Sunday Afternoon on the Island of La Grande Jatte.
ANSWER: Georges-Pierre Seurat
