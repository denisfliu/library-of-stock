The Alchemist

Dapper is blindfolded and meets the 'Queen of Fairy' (Dol Common in disguise). The angry Boy Kastril tries to get his sister to marry a Spanish count. Dutch Anabaptists led by Ananias unknowingly buy goods from Mammon. Subtle flees at the end.

Volpone

Characters with animal names include Corvino (crow), Voltore (vulture), and Corbaccio (raven). Sir Politic Would-be has a get-rich-quick scheme; Peregrine humiliates him by making him hide in a wine butt pretending to be a tortoise. Volpone attempts to rape Corvino's wife Celia. Mosca is forced to become a galley slave. Bonario inherits Corbaccio's property.

Poetry

'To Celia' ('Song: To Celia') opens 'Drink to me only with thine eyes' and includes the image of a rosy wreath sent to the beloved that now smells of her. 'On My First Son' mourns a child who died at seven, called 'his best piece of poetry' and 'thou child of my right hand.' 'To Penshurst' is one of the earliest country house poems, praising Philip Sidney's estate as 'not built to envious show.' Eulogized Shakespeare in the First Folio with 'he was not of an age, but for all time!'

General / Biographical

English Renaissance playwright. Former bricklayer who killed Gabriel Spenser in a duel. Feuded with John Marston and Thomas Dekker in the 'War of the Theatres' (parodied Marston in Cynthia's Revels; Dekker parodied Jonson as Horace in Satiromastix). Hawthornden Manuscripts record his conversations with William Drummond. Wrote The Isle of Dogs with Thomas Nashe (banned). Presided over the Apollo Club; the Cavalier poets called themselves the 'Tribe of Ben' or 'Sons of Ben.' Also wrote Epicoene (Dauphine's uncle Morose marries a man disguised as a 'Silent Woman'), Every Man in His Humour (Dickens revived it), the Masque of Blackness, and 'A Fit of Rhyme against Rhyme.' Popularized comedy of humours.

Summary of Facts

Ben Jonson (1572-1637) was an English Renaissance playwright and poet, a towering figure in Jacobean drama. He is known for his satirical comedies and his influential poetry.

The Alchemist is set in Lovewit's house during a plague, where the butler Jeremy (disguised as 'Captain Face'), Subtle, and the prostitute Dol Common run scams. Sir Epicure Mammon seeks the philosopher's stone, and Dapper meets the 'Queen of Fairy' (Dol Common in disguise). Volpone is set in Venice where the cunning title character feigns illness to receive gifts from legacy hunters like Corvino, Voltore, and Corbaccio, assisted by his servant Mosca. Bartholomew Fair features puppets refuting cross-dressing accusations by lifting their clothes, and mocks Puritans like Zeal-of-the-Land Busy.

Jonson's poetry includes 'To Celia' ('Drink to me only with thine eyes'), 'On My First Son' (mourning a child called 'his best piece of poetry'), and 'To Penshurst' (an early country house poem). He eulogized Shakespeare in the First Folio with 'he was not of an age, but for all time!'

Jonson was a former bricklayer who killed Gabriel Spenser in a duel. He feuded with Marston and Dekker in the 'War of the Theatres.' The Hawthornden Manuscripts record his conversations with William Drummond. The Cavalier poets called themselves his 'Tribe' or 'Sons.' He also wrote Epicoene, Every Man in His Humour, and masques like the Masque of Blackness.
