Questions: František Kupka

2 tossups · 2 bonuses

Tossups

T1 2021 GORKY II (2021) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
After this artist enrolled in the Foreign Legion, his wife attempted to accompany him on a march to Picardy, but was arrested and sent back to Paris. This artist was inspired by blue and red stained glass to paint several rectilinear shapes in his Cathedral, part of a series he titled Vertical and Diagonal Planes. A painting by this artist depicts a scene of a lake melding with a piano being played in the lower half of the painting. This non-Kandinsky artist wrote of how different (+) colors provoke different emotions in his book Creation in the Plastic Arts. This artist slumps back in a chair, holds a book, and raises a cigarette in a self portrait aptly titled The Yellow Scale. This Theosophist depicted several multicolored (*) circular forms blending into each other in a work titled Study for Fugue in Two Colors, which was a part of the larger Discs of Newton series. For 10 points, identify this Czech artist who, along with Robert and Sonia Delaunay, pioneered Orphism.
ANSWER: František Kupka (accept Frank Kupka or François Kupka)
T2 2016 ARTSEE (2016) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
A red-haired girl wearing a sheer dress crouches on a rock as she looks towards the approaching title object in this artist’s The Wave. Giant lily pads rest atop a yellow surface in a painting by this artist that is dominated by two orbs, one of which contains a fetus. This artist’s The Yellow Scale is ostensibly a self-portrait reclining and smoking a cigarette, though the main subject is that title color, which dominates the canvas. This artist’s transition away from representational works, like The Beginning of Life, can be first seen in his painting (*) Piano Keyboard/Lake. Studies of the interaction between light and stained glass led to his work The Cathedral, while his development of new color wheels led to the series Discs of Newton. His work inspired Guillaume Apollinaire to coin the term “Orphism.” For 10 points, name this early abstract artist, an ethnic Czech.
ANSWER: Frantisek Kupka

Bonuses

B1 2025 NAFTA (2025) Diff 9 · Fine Arts
A woman’s face peers out from amidst rectangular patches of color in this artist’s portrait of his wife “among verticals.” For 10 points each:
[10] Name this artist who used blue parallelograms to evoke stained glass in his painting The Cathedral. Along with a husband-and-wife couple, this artist pioneered an offshoot of Cubism known for abstraction and bright colors.
ANSWER: František Kupka
[10] Kupka’s The Cathedral was inspired by his studies of this Gothic cathedral, whose stained glass works include The Blue Virgin as well as three rose windows.
ANSWER: Chartres Cathedral
[10] Kupka’s abstraction was partly motivated by his involvement in this spiritual movement, which also inspired Nicholas Roerich and Wassily Kandinsky and was founded by H.P. Blavatsky.
ANSWER: Theosophy [or the Theosophical Society]
B2 2013 Arrabal (2013) Diff 10 · Fine Arts
This artist used the discovery of the composition of light as the basis for his “Study for Fugue in Two Colors.” For 10 points each:
[10] Identify that artist, best known for his geometrical and color paintings such as the aforementioned Disks of Newton.
ANSWER: Frantisek Kupka
[10] Kupka was an erstwhile member of this artistic movement, which was exemplified by the work of Sonia and Robert Delaunay.
ANSWER: Orphism
[10] Kupka’s art was mostly non-representational; an exception was his painting Planes By Colors, which features a reclining one of these.
ANSWER: female nude [prompt on “person” or anything similar; accept synonyms like “naked”]
