# Mythology Category Supplement

## Sectioning for Deities and Figures

Organize a deity/hero around the distinct things clued about them:

- **Individual myths** — each named story mentioned 3+ times or clued with specific plot details gets its own section (e.g., "Contest with Arachne", "Theft of Mjolnir")
- **Epithets and aspects** — alternate names, regional forms, syncretic identifications
- **Family and relationships** — parentage, consorts, children, rivalries
- **Symbols and attributes** — animals, weapons, plants, iconography
- **Cult sites and worship** — temples, festivals, oracles, if clued
- "General / Giveaway Identifiers" for the standard closers

## Indicators

- `God:`, `Goddess:` for deities
- `Hero:` for mortal/semi-divine heroes
- `Myth:` for identifying a story or episode
- `Creature:` for monsters and beasts
- `Artifact:` for named objects (weapons, treasures)
- `Epithet:` for identifying a title or aspect

## Cards

- `Myth: what happens in the story` -> `Myth/Episode Name (figure)`
- `God: deeds and attributes described` -> `God Name`
- `Artifact: description and owner context` -> `Artifact Name`
- Never leak the answer on the front — mythological names recur inside their own clues constantly; scrub them.

## Metadata

- `group` (optional, NEW): the tradition — "Greek", "Norse", "Egyptian", "Hindu", "Mesopotamian", "Japanese", "Aztec", "Yoruba", etc.
- **No `year`** — myths don't have dates; leave it unset.
- `continent`/`country`: the tradition's homeland (e.g., Norse -> Europe, Norway/Iceland by judgment).
- `tags`: leave for genuine classifications; do NOT duplicate the tradition already captured in `group`.

## Cross-References

Mythology cross-references are **unusually dense** — every figure connects to parents, siblings, consorts, killers, and rivals who are likely topics themselves. **Link liberally**: family relations, participants in shared myths, and equivalent figures across traditions (Zeus/Jupiter) should all be hyperlinked when the target topic exists.

## Universal Reminders

- **No outside knowledge for clue content** — describe only what the clues say; outside knowledge is OK for hyperlinks and metadata.
- Myth/section descriptions are **mini-paragraphs**, not one-liners like "His most famous myth."

## Common Pitfalls

- Greek vs. Roman names: check the answerline — a "Zeus" clue set is not a "Jupiter" clue set, even if content overlaps.
- Figures also clued in Literature (Odysseus in the *Odyssey*) or Religion — include only questions categorized as Mythology.
- Shared epithets and conflated figures (Athena/Minerva, Freya/Frigg) require careful answerline checking.
